# Hydraulic Pressure Dropping

**Catalog ID:** 0254  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Hydraulic Pressure Dropping — progressive loss of hydraulic pressure that may follow a leak, pump failure, or system malfunction, warning the crew that powered services may degrade or fail. Directly relevant to H130 operations and ICAO exams: you must report the trend, the effect on controls or gear, and your landing plan before pressure reaches zero.

"Pressure dropping" is a status phrase. It tells ATC the situation is evolving — not yet necessarily a total hydraulic failure, but moving that way.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master trend reporting in English, then apply it to helicopter hydraulic caution scenarios where early diversion prevents a worse end state.

Understanding hydraulic pressure dropping in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Hydraulic Pressure Dropping is the observed decrease in hydraulic system pressure, indicating a developing malfunction such as a leak or pump problem, requiring monitoring, checklist actions, possible precautionary landing, and clear ATC updates on system trend, aircraft controllability, and intentions.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pressão hidráulica caindo / Hydraulic Pressure Dropping.

Diminuição observada da pressão do sistema hidráulico, indicando uma pane em desenvolvimento como vazamento ou problema de bomba, exigindo monitoramento, ações de checklist, possível pouso preventivo e atualizações claras ao ATC sobre a tendência do sistema, a controlabilidade da aeronave e as intenções.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When hydraulic pressure is dropping, the crew should:

- identify the affected system and apply QRH/AFM procedures
- monitor pressure trend and related quantity indications
- assess flight controls, gear, brakes, and other hydraulic services
- declare Pan Pan early for precautionary priority landing if trend continues
- report hydraulic pressure dropping, current controllability, and intentions
- for H130: treat decreasing hydraulic pressure as a time-limited decision event

Hydraulic pressure dropping reports commonly include:

- hydraulic pressure dropping
- controls normal / becoming heavy / degraded
- request priority landing / diversion
- request long runway / emergency services
- souls and fuel when asked

**Important:** Trend language matters. "Hydraulic pressure dropping" is better than waiting to say "hydraulic failure" after the system is already dead. Give ATC the movie, not only the final frame.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use hydraulic pressure trend reports to:

- understand that the emergency may worsen before landing
- prioritize the aircraft before total system loss
- prepare a longer runway and emergency services
- plan for possible gear or brake abnormalities
- reduce traffic complexity around the arrival

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: hydraulic pressure begins decreasing in cruise. Controls still normal. You decide not to wait.

You transmit:

Pan Pan Pan, Control, ANAC123, hydraulic pressure dropping, request diversion and priority vectors for landing.

Later update:

ANAC123, hydraulic pressure still dropping, flight controls currently normal, continuing priority approach.

If pressure collapses and controls degrade, upgrade to Mayday.

For H130, the same trend-and-divert discipline applies — do not ride a dying hydraulic system to destination convenience.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan hydraulic pressure dropping, say intentions.
2. Control: ANAC123, diversion approved, expect Approach on one two four decimal five.
3. Approach: ANAC123, priority handling approved, expect runway two seven, emergency services alerted.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.
5. Tower: ANAC123, report hydraulic status and braking capability on final.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Hydraulic pressure dropping is a gift — a warning before the failure finishes.

Use the gift. Divert. Declare. Do not protect the schedule while the gauge falls.

Your radio should carry the trend: dropping, still dropping, now lost. Controllers sequence differently when they know the clock is running.

In the H130, hydraulic pressure is not a passenger comfort system. It is how you fly. Early English saves late heroics.

High priority on the exam because examiners want to hear trend awareness, not only total-failure scripts.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Hydraulic pressure is dropping but flight controls still feel normal. What do you say to ATC, and why might you divert?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would apply the checklist, monitor the pressure trend, and advise ATC with Pan Pan that hydraulic pressure is dropping, that controls are currently normal, and that I request diversion and priority vectors for landing. I would divert because the system may continue to degrade and I want to land while controllability and braking are still predictable. I would provide souls and fuel when asked and upgrade to Mayday if pressure is lost or controls become degraded. This applies directly to helicopter hydraulic warnings as well.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Waiting for "hydraulic failure" before saying anything.  
  ✔ Report "hydraulic pressure dropping" as soon as the trend is clear.

- ❌ Saying "pressure going down" only, without intentions.  
  ✔ Always add diversion or priority landing request.

- ❌ Not updating ATC when the trend continues.  
  ✔ Give short updates: still dropping / now stable / pressure lost.

- ❌ Continuing to destination for convenience with a falling gauge.  
  ✔ Divert while you still have system margin.
