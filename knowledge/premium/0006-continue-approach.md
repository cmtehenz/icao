# Continue Approach

**Catalog ID:** 0006  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the most misunderstood instructions in aviation.

Many pilots believe that if Tower tells them to Continue Approach, they are automatically cleared to land.

That is incorrect.

A professional pilot understands the difference immediately.

## 📖 Meaning

Continue flying the approach toward the runway while awaiting further instructions.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Continue a aproximação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

A Continue Approach instruction tells the pilot to keep flying the current approach.

It is not a landing clearance.

The aircraft should continue descending and configuring for landing while remaining prepared to receive:

- Cleared to Land
- Go Around
- Additional sequencing instructions

The pilot must never assume landing authorization.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Continue Approach to:

- keep arriving traffic flowing
- allow another aircraft to vacate the runway
- wait for a vehicle to clear the runway
- complete runway inspections
- sequence multiple arrivals
- delay landing clearance until the runway becomes available

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're flying an H130 to Florianópolis International Airport.

Tower says:

"ANAC123, continue approach."

The runway is clearly visible.

Everything looks normal.

However, a business jet has just landed and is still slowing down on the runway.

Instead of clearing you to land immediately, Tower keeps you on the approach while waiting for the runway to become available.

A few moments later:

"ANAC123, runway one four, cleared to land."

Only now is the landing authorized.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. ANAC123, continue approach.
2. ANAC123, continue approach, traffic vacating the runway.
3. ANAC123, continue approach, expect late landing clearance.
4. ANAC123, continue approach, number two behind the Airbus A320.
5. ANAC123, continue approach, caution wake turbulence.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Continuing approach, ANAC123.
2. Continuing approach, number two, ANAC123.
3. Continuing approach behind the Airbus, ANAC123.
4. Continuing approach, expecting landing clearance, ANAC123.
5. Continuing approach, caution wake turbulence acknowledged, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Gustavo, imagine you're walking toward the gate at an airport.

The security guard says:

"Keep walking."

That doesn't mean you can enter.

It only means continue until I tell you otherwise.

Continue Approach works exactly the same way.

You're still approaching.

You're still descending.

You're still preparing to land.

But the runway does not belong to you yet.

Only one instruction transfers the runway to you:

Cleared to Land.

Until then, keep flying the approach professionally and always be ready to execute a Go Around if instructed.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between Continue Approach and Cleared to Land?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Continue Approach instructs the pilot to keep flying the approach while waiting for further instructions. Cleared to Land authorizes the aircraft to land on the assigned runway. The first is not a landing clearance.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Assuming Continue Approach means landing is approved.  
  ✔ Continue flying. Wait for: Cleared to Land.

- ❌ Stopping the landing checklist while waiting.  
  Continue normal approach procedures.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Continuing approach, ANAC123.

- ❌ Becoming fixated on the runway.  
  Continue monitoring the radio because ATC may issue Go Around, Continue Approach, or Cleared to Land at any moment.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Think of a traffic light turning yellow.

You're allowed to continue moving.

But you don't yet have complete freedom.

That's exactly what Continue Approach means.

You are still approaching.

The runway is not yet yours.
