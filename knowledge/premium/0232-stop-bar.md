# Stop Bar

**Catalog ID:** 0232  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Stop Bar — a row of red lights across the taxiway at a runway holding position, used to reinforce the hold-short point and, when illuminated, to prohibit aircraft and vehicles from proceeding beyond the bar onto the runway until the stop bar is switched off and ATC clearance is received.

Stop bars are a runway incursion defense. Red lights mean stop. Crossing an illuminated stop bar without clearance is a serious breach — even if you think you heard a lineup clearance. In low visibility, stop bars are critical. In good visibility, they still mean what they say.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations at equipped Brazilian airports, stop bars appear at runway holding points on instrument runways. Ground taxi and air-taxi crews must treat an illuminated stop bar as a hard stop. Academy discipline here prevents runway incursions during training tempo and busy tower sequences.

This lesson builds Part 2 proficiency in stop bar phraseology, compliance, and runway safety communications.

## 📖 Meaning

A Stop Bar is a row of red unidirectional lights installed across the taxiway at a runway holding position, which when illuminated requires aircraft and vehicles to stop and hold, and which is extinguished in conjunction with ATC clearance to proceed onto or across the runway.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Stop Bar / Barra de parada.

Fila de luzes vermelhas unidirecionais instaladas transversalmente na taxiway na posição de espera da pista, que quando acesas exigem que aeronaves e veículos parem e aguardem, e que são apagadas em conjunto com a autorização do ATC para prosseguir para a pista ou cruzá-la.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When approaching a stop bar, the crew should:

- identify stop bar locations on the airport diagram and during taxi brief
- stop short of an illuminated stop bar — never cross red stop bar lights
- wait for ATC clearance and for the stop bar to be switched off before proceeding
- advise ATC if the stop bar remains illuminated after a clearance to lineup or cross
- in low visibility, treat stop bar compliance as mandatory runway protection
- for helicopters, hold hover-taxi position short of the bar with stable control

Stop bar operations commonly include:

- low visibility takeoff and taxi procedures (LVP)
- night operations at instrument runway holding points
- ATC switching stop bars off when issuing lineup or crossing clearance
- pilot reports when stop bars fail to extinguish after clearance
- reinforcement of painted hold-short markings

**Important:** Clearance without extinguished stop bar is incomplete protection. If Tower clears you to line up but the stop bar stays red, hold position and report. Do not cross. In the H130, the same rule applies to air-taxi — red means stop, regardless of hover capability.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers and aerodromes use stop bars to:

- provide a clear visual hold-short signal at the runway entry
- reduce runway incursions, especially in low visibility
- synchronize physical lights with verbal clearances
- protect the runway from aircraft and vehicles that mishear clearances
- support low visibility procedures at major airports

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You taxi to the holding point for runway one seven Left at Guarulhos in the H130. Low visibility procedures are in force. The stop bar is illuminated — a line of red lights across the taxiway.

You stop. You do not edge forward. Tower: cleared to line up runway one seven Left. The stop bar extinguishes. You confirm the lights are off, then taxi onto the runway and line up.

On another day, Tower clears you to cross but the stop bar stays red. You hold and report: stop bar still illuminated. Tower corrects the lighting and re-clears you. You prevented an incursion by trusting the lights as well as the radio.

That is stop bar discipline: red means stop until both clearance and lights agree.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, hold at the stop bar, runway one seven Left.
2. Tower: ANAC123, stop bar extinguished, cleared to line up runway one seven Left.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cross runway two eight, stop bar off.
4. Ground: ANAC123, taxi to holding point, hold short, stop bar illuminated.
5. Tower: ANAC123, confirm holding short, stop bar remains on, standby for lineup.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Stop bar red is not a suggestion. It is a wall of light across the taxiway.

Painted hold-short lines can be missed in fog. Red stop bars are designed to be unmistakable. Cross them only when they are off and you have a clearance.

If the clearance comes and the lights stay on, you stop and tell Tower. Lighting and clearance must match. One without the other is a trap.

In the H130, hover-taxi makes it easy to creep forward without noticing. Set a hard mental gate: illuminated stop bar equals hold. No creeping.

Low visibility is when stop bars earn their keep. Good visibility is when discipline is proven. Same rule both days.

Runway safety is often a single decision at a holding point. Make the stop bar part of that decision every time.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You are cleared to line up but the stop bar remains illuminated. Describe what you do and what you say.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would not cross the stop bar. I would hold position short of the red lights and advise Tower that the stop bar is still illuminated. I would wait until the stop bar is extinguished and I have a clear lineup or crossing clearance before proceeding onto the runway. The lights and the clearance must both allow me to proceed.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Crossing an illuminated stop bar because Tower already said "line up."  
  ✔ Hold and report if the stop bar remains on. Do not cross red lights.

- ❌ Creeping past the stop bar while waiting for the lights to go out.  
  ✔ Stop fully short. Proceed only after lights off and clearance confirmed.

- ❌ Ignoring stop bars in good visibility as "only for LVP."  
  ✔ Illuminated stop bars apply whenever they are on.

- ❌ Not reporting a stop bar that fails to extinguish after clearance.  
  ✔ Report immediately so ATC can correct lighting and protect the runway.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Stop bars protect the runway with red light.

Illuminated means stop. Extinguished plus clearance means you may proceed.

If the radio and the lights disagree, believe the red lights and talk to Tower.

In the H130, no creeping. Hold short cleanly. Enter only when both signals agree.

That is runway incursion prevention at the holding point — simple, strict, non-negotiable.
