# Tow Truck Collision

**Catalog ID:** 0224  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Tow Truck Collision — the ground incident in which a tow truck / tug collides with an aircraft, vehicle, or structure on the apron or maneuvering area, and the radiotelephony used to report, contain, and respond to that event.

A tow truck collision is a high-priority ground emergency. It can disable an aircraft, spill fuel, injure personnel, and block taxiways or stands. ATC needs an immediate, clear report: what happened, where, whether there is fire, injuries, or fuel leak, and whether the movement area is blocked.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, you may be the aircraft involved, a nearby witness, or traffic that must hold while RFF and maintenance respond. Brazilian hubs — Guarulhos, Congonhas, Santos Dumont, Curitiba, Florianópolis — run dense tug traffic. Part 2 and Part 3 both test structured emergency ground English.

Understanding how to report a tow truck collision and how to keep rotors, passengers, and taxi traffic safe afterward is essential for ICAO proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Tow Truck Collision is an occurrence in which a tow truck or aircraft tug strikes an aircraft, vehicle, person, or object on the aerodrome; in radiotelephony it is reported as a ground incident requiring ATC notification, possible RFF response, and movement restrictions.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Tow truck collision / Colisão com caminhão de reboque (trator/tug).

Ocorrência em que um caminhão de reboque ou trator de aeronave colide com aeronave, veículo, pessoa ou objeto no aeródromo; na fraseologia, é reportada como incidente de solo exigindo aviso ao controle, possível resposta do SCI e restrições de movimento.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a tow truck collision occurs or is observed, the pilot should:

- stop taxi or shut down intentions that would worsen the scene
- report immediately: location, collision with tow truck, aircraft involved if known
- state fire, smoke, fuel leak, and injuries if observed — or say unknown
- hold other traffic clear by staying off blocked routes and following ATC
- for helicopters nearby, reduce downwash toward the incident and personnel
- request RFF if damage or leak suggests fire risk; do not assume someone else called

Tow truck collision scenarios commonly include:

- tug striking a parked aircraft wing, rotor, or fuselage
- tug-to-tug or tug-to-vehicle apron collisions
- pushback collision with ground equipment
- tow movement into a taxiing aircraft path
- post-collision blocking of a taxiway or stand taxilane

**Important:** If your H130 is struck, secure the aircraft per SOP, protect passengers, and keep radio concise. If you only witness it, report and stay clear — do not taxi through the scene to "help" without ATC and ground coordination.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers handle tow truck collision reports to:

- stop conflicting taxi traffic immediately
- activate RFF and airport authority response
- close stands or taxiways affected by wreckage or spill
- gather essential safety facts without long narratives
- restore movement only after the area is safe and clear

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are taxiing behind a freighter pushback at Guarulhos in the H130.

Ahead, a tow truck strikes a wingtip of a parked aircraft on the cargo apron. You stop.

You call Ground with position, tow truck collision, parked aircraft struck, no fire observed, taxiway partially blocked. Ground holds all traffic, rolls firefighters, and reroutes you via another taxiway.

You never entered the scene. Your report started the response.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, hold position, tow truck collision on the apron, RFF responding.
2. Tower: ANAC123, confirm your position, report if involved in the tow truck collision.
3. Ground: ANAC123, Alpha blocked due tow truck collision, taxi via Bravo.
4. Apron: ANAC123, remain clear of stand five, tow truck collision, fuel spill possible.
5. Ground: ANAC123, firefighters on the way, hold short of the cargo apron.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Ground collisions sound slow. They are not slow for the people inside them.

Your radio must be faster than your curiosity. Location. What hit what. Fire or leak. Blocked or not. Then stop talking and follow instructions.

If you are involved: checklist memory items, passengers, and short updates. If you are a witness: be the clean reporter ATC needs.

Part 3 answers should show priority order — safety of people, stop movement, inform ATC, avoid making FOD or downwash worse with the helicopter.

Brazilian aprons are crowded with tugs. See-and-avoid on the ground is as real as in the circuit. Give way early. Hold when unsure. Never squeeze between a tug and a fuselage.

High-priority ground English is emergency English without the drama.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

While taxiing you see a tow truck collide with a parked aircraft. What do you say and what do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would stop immediately and keep clear of the incident area. I would report to Ground with my position, that a tow truck collided with a parked aircraft, and whether I see fire, smoke, fuel leak, or injuries — or say if that is unknown. I would request that other traffic be held if the taxiway is blocked and I would wait for instructions. In a helicopter I would avoid directing downwash toward the scene and personnel. I would not enter the area to inspect the damage unless specifically coordinated.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Taxiing closer to "see how bad it is" before reporting.  
  ✔ Stop, report first, remain clear.

- ❌ Long story without location or fire/leak status.  
  ✔ Position + event + fire/leak/injuries + blockage.

- ❌ Hover-taxiing past the scene with strong downwash.  
  ✔ Hold or accept a wide reroute; protect responders.

- ❌ Assuming RFF was already called because "everyone saw it."  
  ✔ Report anyway; ATC coordinates the official response.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Tow truck collision is a ground emergency with apron coordinates.

Stop. Report location and essentials. Stay clear.

Do not become second traffic through the wreckage. Do not wash the scene with rotor blast.

Short radio. Long patience. Let RFF work.
