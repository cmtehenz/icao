# Number Two Behind Traffic

**Catalog ID:** 0032  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the second position in the landing sequence — a common instruction at busy airports.

Number Two Behind Traffic means you are second in line to land and must follow the aircraft ahead on final approach.

You are not cleared to land yet.

You must maintain visual contact with the traffic ahead and comply with any spacing or reporting instructions.

Professional pilots treat number two as a sequencing instruction that requires patience and situational awareness.

## 📖 Meaning

Number Two Behind Traffic indicates the aircraft is second in the landing sequence and must follow the specified traffic on final approach.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Número dois atrás do tráfego.

A aeronave é a segunda na sequência de pouso e deve seguir o tráfego indicado na final.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When instructed Number Two Behind Traffic, the pilot should:

- acquire and maintain visual contact with the traffic ahead
- follow at a safe distance on final approach
- comply with continue approach or report instructions
- not land until cleared to land by Tower
- advise ATC if visual contact with the traffic is lost

Number Two Behind Traffic is commonly issued with:

- follow the traffic on final
- continue approach
- report final or report base
- maintain present speed

**Important:** Being number two does not authorize landing. Wait for cleared to land after the traffic ahead has landed and vacated.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Tower uses Number Two Behind Traffic to:

- sequence multiple aircraft on the same approach
- maintain safe spacing between arrivals
- reduce controller workload in visual conditions
- prepare pilots for delayed landing clearance
- manage busy patterns at airports like Congonhas and Florianópolis

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are approaching Balneário Camboriú in your H130.

An Airbus A320 is on a two-mile final ahead of you.

Tower: ANAC123, number two, follow the traffic on a two-mile final. Report final runway one eight.

You reply:

Number two, following the traffic, will report final, ANAC123.

You maintain visual contact and a safe distance behind the Airbus.

When you report final, Tower clears you to land after the traffic ahead vacates the runway.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, number two, follow the traffic on final runway one eight.
2. Tower: ANAC123, number two behind the Boeing seven three seven, continue approach.
3. Tower: ANAC123, number two, follow the traffic, report final.
4. Tower: ANAC123, number two, maintain present speed, traffic two miles ahead.
5. Tower: ANAC123, extend downwind, you are number two, traffic on short final.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Number two, following the traffic on final, ANAC123.
2. Number two behind the Boeing seven three seven, continuing approach, ANAC123.
3. Number two, following the traffic, will report final, ANAC123.
4. Number two, maintaining present speed, ANAC123.
5. Extending downwind, number two, traffic in sight, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine a two-person line at a single checkout counter.

You are second.

You watch the person ahead finish before you step forward.

Number two behind traffic works the same way in the air.

You see the traffic ahead.

You follow at a safe distance.

You wait your turn.

Brazilian pilots sometimes get impatient on number two and descend too close to the traffic ahead.

Maintain spacing.

And remember — continue approach and number two are not cleared to land.

At Congonhas, with its short final and dense traffic, discipline on number two prevents go-arounds and conflicts.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you do if Tower says you are number two behind traffic on final?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would acknowledge number two, confirm the traffic is in sight, and follow the traffic on final at a safe distance. I would continue the approach and wait for cleared to land after the traffic ahead has landed and vacated the runway.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Landing without clearance because you are on final behind traffic.  
  ✔ Wait for cleared to land even when number two on short final.

- ❌ Losing sight of traffic without advising Tower.  
  ✔ Report immediately if visual contact is lost.

- ❌ Closing too close to the traffic ahead.  
  ✔ Maintain safe visual separation throughout the approach.

- ❌ Reading back only "Roger" without confirming number two.  
  ✔ Number two, following the traffic, ANAC123.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Number Two Behind Traffic on Final

**Pronunciation:** NUM-ber TOO bee-HIND TRAF-ik on FY-nul

**Word Stress**

- Number → first syllable
- Behind → second syllable (bee-HIND)
- Final → first syllable (FY-nul)

Practice:

Number two... behind traffic...

Together:

Number two, following the traffic on final, ANAC123.

Speak aircraft types clearly: Boeing seven three seven.

ATC says:

Tower: ANAC123, number two, follow the traffic on final. Report final runway one eight.

**Your first task**

Read back the instruction as a professional pilot.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

You are on short final, traffic ahead has landed but not yet vacated. Tower has not cleared you to land. What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I continue the approach and wait for cleared to land. I must not land until Tower issues landing clearance and the runway is available. If a safe landing is not assured, I will go around and advise Tower.
