# Taxi to Holding Point

**Catalog ID:** 0026  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson bridges taxi clearances and runway operations.

When Ground says Taxi to Holding Point, they are directing you to a specific position on the movement area — usually near the departure runway — where you will wait for further instructions from Tower.

This is not a takeoff clearance.

It is not permission to enter the runway.

Professional pilots understand that the holding point is a staging area where ground control hands you off to tower control.

Getting this right prevents runway incursions and keeps departure sequencing safe.

## 📖 Meaning

Taxi to Holding Point instructs the pilot to taxi to a designated holding position and stop, awaiting further clearance before entering the runway.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Táxi até o ponto de espera.

Siga a rota indicada e pare no ponto de espera designado, aguardando novas instruções.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Taxi to Holding Point, the pilot should:

- taxi only on the assigned route
- stop at the designated holding point
- contact Tower when instructed
- not enter the runway unless explicitly cleared
- read back the holding point designator and any hold short instructions

Taxi to Holding Point clearances commonly include:

- taxiway designators to reach the holding point
- hold short of the runway until tower clearance
- contact tower at the holding point
- runway assignment for departure

**Important:** Reaching the holding point does not authorize line up or takeoff. Wait for Tower instructions.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Ground Control uses Taxi to Holding Point to:

- position aircraft near the departure runway efficiently
- coordinate the handoff from Ground to Tower
- prevent premature runway entry
- sequence departures in busy traffic
- keep the movement area organized at complex airports

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are departing Congonhas Airport in your H130.

Ground has cleared you from the helipad:

Ground: ANAC123, taxi to holding point Alpha one via Bravo, hold short of runway one seven right.

You taxi carefully along Bravo, stop at holding point Alpha one, and switch to Tower frequency as instructed.

Tower later clears you to line up and wait.

Until that moment, you remain stopped at the holding point.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Think of the holding point as the waiting room before the runway.

Ground brings you to the door.

Tower decides when you may enter.

Many runway incursions happen because pilots treat the holding point as optional — rolling past it because the runway looks empty.

The holding point is a hard boundary until Tower clears you forward.

In Brazil, pilots sometimes confuse taxi to holding point with line up and wait.

They are different steps in the departure sequence.

At Congonhas, with its short runways and dense traffic, discipline at the holding point is not optional.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between taxi to holding point and line up and wait?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Taxi to holding point is a ground movement instruction to taxi to a designated position and stop before the runway. Line up and wait is a tower instruction to enter the runway and position for departure without takeoff clearance. The holding point is reached on the taxiway; line up means you are on the runway.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Entering the runway after reaching the holding point without tower clearance.  
  ✔ Stop at the holding point and wait for tower instructions.

- ❌ Confusing holding point with the runway threshold.  
  ✔ The holding point is on the taxiway, before the runway.

- ❌ Omitting hold short from the readback.  
  ✔ Always read back hold short instructions when included.

- ❌ Forgetting to contact tower when instructed.  
  ✔ Switch to tower frequency at the holding point as cleared.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

Ground: ANAC123, runway one eight, taxi to holding point Delta via Bravo, hold short of runway one eight, contact tower.

**Your first task**

Read back the clearance exactly as a professional pilot.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

You have reached holding point Delta. May you enter runway one eight to line up?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

No. I must hold short of runway one eight until Tower explicitly clears me to enter the runway. Reaching the holding point only positions me for the handoff to Tower.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Taxi to Holding Point is a simple instruction with serious consequences if misunderstood.

Ground brings you close to the runway.

Tower controls runway access.

Stop at the holding point.

Read back hold short instructions.

Contact Tower when told.

The best pilots are patient at the holding point — because the runway will still be there when Tower is ready.
