# Flight Attendant

**Catalog ID:** 0342  
**Category:** Crew  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Flight Attendant — the cabin crew member responsible for passenger safety, cabin service, and emergency procedures in the cabin, and a standard ICAO vocabulary item for crew coordination English.

Even when the H130 operates with limited or no dedicated cabin crew, ICAO Part 1 and Part 2 expect correct terms for airline-style crew roles. You must understand who the flight attendant is, what they report to the flight deck, and how to speak about cabin status in standard English.

For ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, learn the role language for exams and for multi-crew airline scenarios, then map cabin reports sensibly to helicopter operations when no flight attendant is carried.

Master: flight attendant, cabin report, passenger status, cabin secure.

## 📖 Meaning

Flight Attendant is a cabin crew member trained to ensure passenger safety and cabin emergency procedures, communicate cabin conditions to the flight crew, and assist passengers during normal and abnormal operations.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Comissário de voo / Flight Attendant.

Membro da tripulação de cabine treinado para garantir a segurança dos passageiros e os procedimentos de emergência na cabine, comunicar as condições da cabine à tripulação de voo e assistir os passageiros em operações normais e anormais.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When working with or referring to a flight attendant, the crew should:

- use the standard term flight attendant (not informal nicknames on the exam)
- expect cabin secure, passenger count, and abnormal cabin reports from that role
- brief flight attendants on expected turbulence, diversion, or emergency landing
- receive sterile-cockpit-relevant cabin calls only when safety requires
- acknowledge cabin reports clearly: roger, cabin secure / medical assistance required

Flight attendant coordination commonly includes:

- cabin secure / cabin not secure
- passenger medical event report
- brace for landing / evacuate commands support
- doors / exits status on airliners; door/egress status mapped for H130 when applicable

**Important:** On airliner exam items, flight attendants own the cabin. In single-pilot H130 operations without cabin crew, the pilot still needs the vocabulary to describe cabin roles and reports correctly.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers rarely address flight attendants directly, but crew-role clarity matters when:

- the flight crew reports cabin status based on attendant information
- medical or evacuation events originate from cabin reports
- souls on board and assistance needs are relayed from cabin to flight deck to ATC
- examiners test whether the pilot uses correct crew titles in English
- coordination language stays standard across operators

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Multi-crew exam scenario: before descent into Congonhas, the flight deck calls the cabin.

Flight attendant reports cabin secure, all passengers seated.

Later, the flight attendant reports a passenger medical event. The pilots translate that cabin report into a Pan Pan call and request medical assistance.

In an H130 without a flight attendant, the pilot or a designated passenger brief covers seat belts and egress — but the exam term remains flight attendant.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger medical emergency, say if cabin crew request additional assistance.
2. Tower: ANAC123, confirm souls on board as reported by cabin.
3. Approach: ANAC123, advise when cabin is secure for landing.
4. Control: ANAC123, say nature of cabin report.
5. Tower: ANAC123, medical services will coordinate with crew on arrival.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, cabin secure, ready for approach.
2. ANAC123, flight attendant reports passenger medical emergency, request priority landing.
3. ANAC123, cabin not secure, request delay vectors.
4. ANAC123, souls on board as reported by cabin crew, medical assistance required.
5. ANAC123, cabin secure, evacuating only if required after landing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Flight attendant is a professional title. Use it.

Brazilian Portuguese uses comissário or comissária. On the ICAO exam, prefer flight attendant. Cabin crew is the broader group term.

The flight attendant's value to the pilots is information: cabin secure, medical event, smoke in cabin, exits ready.

Do not argue with a cabin safety report on frequency. Resolve it in the aircraft, then tell ATC what operations need.

In H130 Brazilian ops you may not carry a flight attendant. Still learn the role — Part 1 vocabulary is tested regardless of your daily crewing.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What does a flight attendant do, and how might you report a cabin medical event based on their information?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A flight attendant is responsible for passenger safety and cabin emergency procedures and reports cabin conditions to the flight crew. If they report a passenger medical emergency, I would assess diversion needs and tell ATC: medical emergency on board, request priority landing and medical assistance, based on the cabin report.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "comissário" in an English ICAO answer.  
  ✔ Use flight attendant or cabin crew as appropriate.

- ❌ Ignoring a cabin secure negative report and continuing the approach.  
  ✔ Delay or vector until cabin is secure when safety requires.

- ❌ Speaking to ATC as if the flight attendant is the caller.  
  ✔ Flight crew makes the radio call; cabin provides the information.

- ❌ Using slang titles instead of standard role names.  
  ✔ Keep exam English standard: flight attendant.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

The flight attendant reports a passenger with severe chest pain on descent.

**Your first task**

What do you say to Approach?

**Your second task**

Approach asks if the cabin is secure for landing. Cabin says yes. What do you reply?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, medical emergency on board, flight attendant reports passenger severe chest pain, request priority landing and medical assistance. Second task: ANAC123, cabin secure, ready for landing. I would keep the flight attendant informed of landing time and medical services.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Flight attendant is cabin safety with a standard English name.

Know the role. Use the title. Translate cabin reports into clear ATC English when needed.

On airliners, they own the cabin. On many H130 flights, you may not have one — but the vocabulary still belongs in your ICAO toolkit.

Professional crews speak professional titles.
