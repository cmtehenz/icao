# Medical Emergency

**Catalog ID:** 0336  
**Category:** Passenger  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Medical Emergency — a passenger or crew medical event that requires immediate assistance, priority handling, and clear communication with ATC so ground medical services can meet the aircraft.

A medical emergency is not a diagnosis on frequency. It is a operational decision: how serious is the condition, do you divert, and what do you need on landing.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, medical emergencies may involve passengers on charter, EMS positioning, or training flights with observers. Cabin space is limited. Medical kits are limited. The radio call must be precise so ATC can coordinate ambulances and priority landing.

Understanding how to declare urgency or distress, request medical assistance, and report souls on board in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Medical Emergency is a sudden illness, injury, or life-threatening condition affecting a passenger or crew member that requires medical assistance and may require diversion, priority landing, and coordination with airport emergency medical services.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Emergência médica / Medical Emergency.

Doença, lesão ou condição potencialmente fatal súbita que afeta um passageiro ou membro da tripulação e exige assistência médica, podendo exigir desvio, pouso prioritário e coordenação com os serviços médicos de emergência do aeroporto.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a medical emergency occurs, the crew should:

- assess severity with cabin crew or available medical volunteers
- decide divert versus continue based on condition, fuel, and nearest suitable airport
- declare Pan Pan for urgency or Mayday if life is immediately threatened and aircraft handling is affected
- request medical assistance and priority landing
- report souls on board and nature of assistance required when asked

Medical emergency reporting commonly includes:

- medical emergency on board
- passenger unconscious / chest pain / severe bleeding
- request priority landing and medical assistance
- souls on board and estimated time of arrival

**Important:** Do not diagnose on frequency. Report the condition in plain operational English: passenger unconscious, passenger not breathing, severe chest pain. Let medical services on the ground make the clinical assessment.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to medical emergency reports to:

- provide priority handling and shortest routing to a suitable airport
- coordinate ambulances and airport medical services
- clear traffic that would delay the approach
- obtain souls on board and nature of assistance required
- alert tower and ground so the aircraft is met on landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are en route from Congonhas to Florianópolis in the H130 when cabin reports a passenger with severe chest pain, pale and sweating.

You assess: divert to Navegantes is the nearest suitable airport with medical services.

You transmit urgency and request priority.

Approach clears traffic and coordinates ambulances.

You land. Medical services meet the aircraft. The passenger is transferred. The flight continues only after the situation is secure.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say nature of medical emergency and souls on board.
2. Approach: ANAC123, divert to Navegantes, expect runway one seven, medical services alerted.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway one seven cleared to land, ambulances standing by.
4. Control: ANAC123, say if passenger is conscious and breathing.
5. Tower: ANAC123, vacate when able, medical team will meet you on the apron.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, medical emergency on board, passenger severe chest pain, request priority landing Navegantes.
2. ANAC123, diverting Navegantes, souls on board six, request medical assistance on landing.
3. ANAC123, passenger unconscious, breathing, request ambulances standing by.
4. Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, medical emergency, passenger not breathing, request immediate landing.
5. ANAC123, on final, medical emergency continuing, request medical team meet aircraft.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Medical emergency is a communication problem as much as a medical one.

ATC cannot treat the passenger. ATC can clear the sky and bring ambulances.

Your job: fly the aircraft, decide divert or continue, and speak clearly.

Brazilian pilots often say "passageiro passou mal" on frequency. Prefer medical emergency on board, then one short description: chest pain, unconscious, not breathing.

Pan Pan is the normal urgency call for a passenger medical event when the aircraft is controllable. Mayday if the situation threatens the flight itself — for example, pilot medical emergency or a condition that forces immediate landing with no options.

In the H130, space is tight. Brief cabin early. Know the nearest suitable airports with medical coverage along Brazilian coastal and inland routes.

Do not invent clinical terms. Do not delay the call while searching for a doctor on board. Declare, divert if needed, land, hand over to professionals.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

A passenger collapses with severe chest pain. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare Pan Pan, state my callsign, report medical emergency on board with passenger severe chest pain, request priority landing at the nearest suitable airport, and request medical assistance on landing. I would provide souls on board when ATC asks and update if the passenger becomes unconscious or stops breathing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "passageiro passou mal" without standard English.  
  ✔ Use medical emergency on board plus a short condition description.

- ❌ Diagnosing "heart attack" as a certainty on frequency.  
  ✔ Report symptoms: severe chest pain, unconscious, not breathing.

- ❌ Continuing to destination when a nearby airport has better medical support.  
  ✔ Divert when the condition is serious and time matters.

- ❌ Forgetting to request medical assistance on landing.  
  ✔ Request medical assistance — ATC coordinates ambulances.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are en route to Florianópolis when a passenger becomes unconscious. Navegantes is twenty minutes closer and has medical services.

**Your first task**

Transmit your urgency declaration and diversion request.

**Your second task**

Approach asks for souls on board and whether the passenger is breathing. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, medical emergency on board, passenger unconscious, diverting Navegantes, request priority landing and medical assistance. Second task: ANAC123, souls on board six, passenger unconscious but breathing, request ambulances standing by. I would update immediately if breathing stops.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Medical emergency is about time, clarity, and the right airport.

Fly the aircraft. Assess severity. Declare urgency. Divert when it saves time. Request medical assistance.

Do not diagnose. Do not delay. Do not use only Portuguese on an ICAO frequency.

In the H130, you may have fewer cabin resources — that makes the radio call more important, not less.

Speak like a professional. Get the passenger to help on the ground.
