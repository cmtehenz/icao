# Mayday

**Catalog ID:** 0052  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the most serious radio call in aviation.

Mayday is the international distress signal — used when an aircraft or occupants face grave and imminent danger and require immediate assistance.

Mayday overrides all other traffic.

It is not a general emergency word — it is reserved for distress only.

You must know when to use Mayday versus Pan Pan.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Mayday is the radiotelephony distress signal indicating grave and imminent danger requiring immediate assistance.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Mayday is appropriate, the pilot should:

- transmit Mayday three times at the start of the message
- state the station addressed and aircraft callsign
- describe the nature of the distress
- state intentions, position, and altitude
- provide souls on board and fuel remaining when requested

Mayday is used for distress situations including:

- engine failure in single-engine aircraft
- fire on board
- rapid decompression
- pilot incapacitation
- total loss of control
- any situation where safe flight is in serious doubt

**Important:** Mayday is for distress — grave and imminent danger. Do not use Mayday for urgency. Use Pan Pan Pan Pan for urgency. Say Mayday three times, clearly, at the beginning of the transmission.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Mayday declarations to:

- cease all non-emergency transmissions on the frequency
- clear all traffic from the distressed aircraft's route
- provide maximum assistance without delay
- coordinate emergency services and priority landing
- request souls on board, fuel remaining, and nature of emergency

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are departing Florianópolis in your H130, callsign ANAC123, at four hundred feet when the engine fails completely.

You enter autorotation and stabilize the aircraft.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Florianópolis Tower, ANAC123, engine failure, autorotating, one mile south of the airport.

Tower ceases all other traffic and coordinates emergency services.

You complete a controlled autorotation landing and report safe on the ground.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger Mayday, say souls on board and fuel remaining.
2. Tower: ANAC123, all traffic cleared from your approach, runway three zero cleared to land.
3. Approach: ANAC123, turn left heading two seven zero, descend at your discretion.
4. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services standing by.
5. Control: ANAC123, Mayday acknowledged, say nature of distress and intentions.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

When should you declare Mayday instead of Pan Pan?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare Mayday when the aircraft or occupants face grave and imminent danger and require immediate assistance. Examples include engine failure, fire on board, rapid decompression, and pilot incapacitation. I would use Pan Pan when the situation is urgent but does not involve grave and imminent danger, such as low fuel state or partial system failure with continued safe flight.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "emergência" or "socorro" instead of Mayday.  
  ✔ Use Mayday Mayday Mayday — the international distress signal.

- ❌ Using Mayday for non-distress situations like low fuel advisory.  
  ✔ Reserve Mayday for distress; use Pan Pan for urgency.

- ❌ Saying Mayday only once instead of three times.  
  ✔ Transmit Mayday three times at the start of the message.

- ❌ Declaring Mayday before securing the aircraft.  
  ✔ Aviate first — then declare Mayday from a stable aircraft.
