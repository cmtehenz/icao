# Say Indicated Airspeed

**Catalog ID:** 0318  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Say Indicated Airspeed — the ATC request for the crew to report current indicated airspeed (IAS). Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: read the ASI correctly and report speed clearly in knots.

Say indicated airspeed is Medium priority because controllers use speed reports for sequencing, spacing, and to detect abnormal flight conditions.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for airspeed reports under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including helicopter speed ranges that differ from jet traffic.

Understanding how to say indicated airspeed in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Say Indicated Airspeed is an ATC request for the pilot to report the aircraft's current indicated airspeed, normally in knots, so that ATC can manage sequencing, assess performance, or verify that the aircraft is flying a speed compatible with the traffic situation.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Informe a velocidade indicada / Say Indicated Airspeed.

Solicitação do controle para que o piloto informe a velocidade indicada atual da aeronave, normalmente em nós, para que o ATC gerencie o sequenciamento, avalie o desempenho ou verifique se a aeronave voa a uma velocidade compatível com a situação de tráfego.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC says say indicated airspeed, the crew should:

- read indicated airspeed from the ASI / PFD, not ground speed
- report in knots with a clear number: speed one two zero knots
- maintain the assigned speed if one has been given, or advise if unable
- for H130: expect lower IAS than surrounding fixed-wing traffic — report honestly
- distinguish IAS from ground speed and true airspeed when speaking
- advise ATC if speed must change for weather, configuration, or performance

Airspeed exchanges commonly include:

- say indicated airspeed / say airspeed
- speed [number] knots
- maintain [number] knots / unable speed
- reduce speed / increase speed
- ground speed [number] if specifically requested

**Important:** Unless ATC asks for ground speed, report indicated airspeed. Mixing the two confuses sequencing.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers request indicated airspeed to:

- sequence arrivals with compatible speed profiles
- check whether an aircraft can maintain spacing
- detect unexpectedly slow or fast flight
- issue speed control instructions that fit the aircraft
- coordinate mixed helicopter and airplane traffic in Brazilian terminal areas

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 on downwind with faster traffic on the approach.

Approach: ANAC123, say indicated airspeed.

You reply:

ANAC123, speed nine zero knots.

Approach may then say maintain present speed or adjust sequencing. Your honest IAS lets ATC plan around helicopter performance instead of assuming jet numbers.

If you are unable to fly a requested speed, say unable and give the speed you can maintain.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, say indicated airspeed.
2. Approach: ANAC123, say airspeed.
3. Tower: ANAC123, confirm speed.
4. Approach: ANAC123, maintain one zero zero knots.
5. Radar: ANAC123, say indicated airspeed for sequencing.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, speed nine zero knots.
2. ANAC123, indicated airspeed one zero zero knots.

4. ANAC123, unable one two zero knots, maintaining nine five.
5. ANAC123, reducing to eight zero knots.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Speed reports are instrument facts, spoken plainly.

Look at IAS. Say the number and knots. Do not convert to ground speed unless asked. Do not round so aggressively that ATC loses the truth.

In the H130 you will often be the slow traffic in the sequence. That is normal. Part 2 wants a clean speed call and an honest unable when the assigned speed is outside performance.

Speed control is a clearance when assigned. The report is information when requested.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC asks you to say indicated airspeed, then assigns a speed you cannot fly. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report current indicated airspeed in knots from the airspeed indicator. If ATC then assigns a speed outside H130 performance or configuration limits, I would advise unable and state the speed I can maintain. I would not report ground speed unless ATC specifically requests it.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting GPS ground speed when asked for indicated airspeed.  
  ✔ Read the ASI / indicated airspeed.

- ❌ Omitting "knots" or mumbling the digits.  
  ✔ Speak the number clearly with knots.

- ❌ Accepting an assigned speed the helicopter cannot maintain.  
  ✔ Say unable and give a workable speed.

- ❌ Confusing IAS with true airspeed in the report.  
  ✔ Default report is indicated airspeed unless specified.

**Your first task**

Your ASI shows ninety knots. What do you say?

**Your second task**

You cannot maintain one two zero knots. What do you say?

First task: ANAC123, speed nine zero knots. Second task: ANAC123, unable one two zero knots, maintaining nine zero — or the maximum you can safely fly. Report IAS, not ground speed, and never accept an impossible speed assignment silently.
