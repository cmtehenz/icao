# Drift Down Procedure

**Catalog ID:** 0177  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the Drift Down Procedure — the controlled descent performed when an aircraft is no longer able to maintain its current altitude due to engine failure, severe degradation of performance, or another emergency condition.

For multi-engine jets, drift down describes the descent from cruise altitude after an engine failure, gradually settling at a new, lower cruise altitude where the remaining thrust can sustain level flight.

For the H130 helicopter, the concept applies in a directly relevant form: any condition that removes power or significantly degrades performance — single-engine operation, hydraulic failure, rotor degradation — may require a controlled emergency descent to a lower, sustainable altitude where further flight or diversion is possible.

Knowing how to communicate a drift down situation to ATC in clear ICAO English is critical. Controllers cannot protect your airspace unless they know your limitations and your intentions.

## 📖 Meaning

Drift Down Procedure is the controlled descent an aircraft performs when performance degradation — most commonly engine failure — prevents it from maintaining the current cruise altitude, with the aircraft descending to the highest altitude where remaining performance allows level flight or controlled diversion.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Procedimento de descida por perda de desempenho.

Descida controlada realizada pela aeronave quando a degradação de desempenho — mais comumente falha de motor — impede a manutenção da altitude de cruzeiro atual, descendo a aeronave até a altitude mais elevada onde o desempenho remanescente permite voo nivelado ou desvio controlado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a drift down is required, the crew should:

- declare the nature of the emergency or performance degradation to ATC
- request block altitude or a specific lower altitude for the drift down
- advise ATC of minimum safe diversion altitude and destination intent
- maintain the best drift down airspeed for maximum range and performance
- monitor remaining performance and advise ATC of any further deterioration
- coordinate descent through other traffic with ATC assistance

In H130 helicopter operations, drift down considerations include:

- single-engine power limitation following OEI (One Engine Inoperative) event
- the maximum altitude sustainable on remaining engine under ISA conditions
- the need to descend to lower terrain-cleared altitude for diversion to nearest suitable landing area
- communications priority: declare, request block, advise intentions

**Important:** In the H130, OEI performance limits the sustainable altitude significantly. The pilot must know the aircraft's OEI performance ceiling for current weight and temperature before every flight. If that ceiling is below the current cruise altitude, drift down is immediate and mandatory.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers respond to drift down requests to:

- provide immediate altitude separation for descending aircraft
- clear other traffic from the descent path
- assign a block altitude to protect the aircraft during the performance-limited descent
- coordinate with adjacent sectors for the diversion routing
- provide priority handling and direct routing to the diversion airport

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are operating your H130 at flight level five five, cruising from Congonhas to Florianópolis, when your co-pilot calls an engine anomaly — power is degrading rapidly. You cannot sustain flight level five five.

You declare:

ANAC123, Mayday Mayday Mayday, engine malfunction, unable to maintain flight level five five, initiating drift down, request block altitude between flight level three zero and flight level five five.

Radar responds:

ANAC123, Mayday acknowledged, cleared block altitude flight level three zero to flight level five five, no traffic reported in descent corridor, advise current heading and intention.

You respond:

ANAC123, Mayday, heading one eight zero, intend to divert to Joinville, request direct routing, H130, one POB.

Radar coordinates your direct track and emergency services.

You drift down, stabilize at flight level three zero on remaining power, and track direct to Joinville.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Radar: ANAC123, cleared block altitude flight level three zero to flight level five five, no traffic, advise intentions.
2. Radar: ANAC123, identified, cleared direct Joinville, descend and maintain four thousand, emergency services alerted.

4. Radar: ANAC123, all traffic cleared, maintain own separation, advise souls on board and fuel remaining.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Drift down is a performance emergency.

It is not always dramatic. Sometimes it is quiet — a gradual loss of power you can feel before any warning light confirms it. The aircraft is just not climbing as it should. Or it cannot hold altitude.

In the H130, your OEI ceiling is the number you must know before every flight. Weight, temperature, altitude, humidity — they all affect that ceiling.

If you are above your OEI ceiling when the engine degrades — you are drifting down. The only question is whether you manage it or it manages you.

Manage it. Declare early. Tell ATC exactly what you need: block altitude, direct routing, distance to diversion.

Do not wait until you are in an uncontrolled descent to call Mayday.

The moment you recognize performance is insufficient to maintain altitude — declare. ATC can give you airspace immediately if you ask.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Describe the drift down procedure and what you should communicate to ATC when initiating it.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Drift down is the controlled descent performed when performance degradation prevents maintaining current cruise altitude. In the H130, this is most relevant during an OEI event, where the maximum sustainable altitude on the remaining engine may be significantly lower than cruise altitude. When initiating drift down, I would declare an emergency to ATC: Mayday, engine malfunction, unable to maintain current altitude, initiating drift down. I would request block altitude or a specific lower level, advise my heading and diversion intention, and provide souls on board and fuel remaining when requested. I would maintain the best drift down airspeed for maximum range and keep ATC informed of any further performance changes.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Delaying the Mayday call while troubleshooting, descending without informing ATC.  
  ✔ Declare immediately when you cannot maintain altitude. ATC cannot protect you without information.

- ❌ Requesting a fixed altitude without requesting block altitude during the descent.  
  ✔ During drift down you are passing through multiple levels. Request block altitude to protect the entire descent corridor.

- ❌ Not knowing the OEI performance ceiling before the flight.  
  ✔ Calculate and brief OEI ceiling during pre-flight for H130 operations.

- ❌ Omitting souls on board and fuel remaining from the emergency call.  
  ✔ Include these when declaring Mayday: two souls on board, fuel one hour remaining.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Drift down is controlled. That is its purpose.

The engine is failing. The altitude is going. You cannot stop either.

What you can control is the communication. The routing. The decision.

Declare early. Request block altitude. Track to the nearest suitable airport.

In the H130, know your OEI ceiling. Know it before every flight. Because when you need it, you will not have time to calculate it.

Performance limits your options. Communication preserves them.

Declare. Request. Navigate. Land safely.
