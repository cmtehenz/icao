# Confirm Fuel Remaining

**Catalog ID:** 0311  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Confirm Fuel Remaining — the ATC request for the crew to state how much usable fuel remains, usually in time or quantity as required by local procedure. Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: give an accurate fuel state without delay or confusion.

Confirm fuel remaining is Medium catalog priority but High operational importance whenever weather, delay, or diversion is in play.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for fuel remaining reports under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including time-based fuel endurance.

Understanding how to confirm fuel remaining in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Confirm Fuel Remaining is an ATC request for the pilot to report usable fuel remaining, commonly expressed as endurance in hours and minutes, and sometimes as quantity, so that ATC can assess diversion options, delay tolerance, and the need for priority handling.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Confirmar combustível remanescente / Confirm Fuel Remaining.

Solicitação do controle para que o piloto informe o combustível utilizável remanescente, comumente expresso como autonomia em horas e minutos, e às vezes como quantidade, para que o ATC avalie opções de desvio, tolerância a atrasos e a necessidade de prioridade.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC asks you to confirm fuel remaining, the crew should:

- read usable fuel endurance from the fuel totalizer or calculated remaining time
- report clearly in hours and minutes unless ATC asks for quantity
- distinguish normal fuel state from minimum fuel or mayday fuel emergency
- update the report if a long delay or diversion changes the picture
- for H130: know endurance at current power setting, not only departure fuel
- never invent a comfortable number to avoid ATC questions

Fuel-remaining exchanges commonly include:

- confirm fuel remaining / say fuel remaining
- fuel remaining [hours] [minutes]
- minimum fuel
- mayday mayday mayday, fuel
- endurance [time]

**Important:** Fuel remaining is a factual report. Minimum fuel and fuel emergency are separate declarations with specific meanings — do not mix them casually.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers request fuel remaining to:

- decide whether delay or holding is still safe
- prioritize aircraft approaching fuel criticality
- coordinate diversion to a closer suitable aerodrome
- compare your endurance with expected approach delay
- support search-and-rescue planning if the situation worsens

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 holding for Congonhas. Delay increases. Approach needs your endurance.

Approach: ANAC123, confirm fuel remaining.

You check calculated endurance and reply:

ANAC123, fuel remaining zero plus four five.

If delay continues and you will land with less than planned reserve, you may declare minimum fuel according to procedure. If a safe landing is in doubt, that becomes a fuel emergency — different words, different priority.

Accurate fuel remaining lets ATC help before the situation becomes critical.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, confirm fuel remaining.
2. Control: ANAC123, say fuel remaining in time.
3. Approach: ANAC123, confirm endurance.
4. Radar: ANAC123, advise if minimum fuel.
5. Tower: ANAC123, say fuel remaining on final.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Fuel remaining is a number you must be able to give in one clean sentence.

Hours and minutes. No story. No apology. If ATC asks, they are planning your options.

In the H130, fuel state changes with hover, headwind, and alternate selection. Keep a running mental endurance, especially in Brazilian terminal delays. Part 2 expects the report; Part 3 expects you to escalate correctly if that number becomes critical.

Do not say minimum fuel when you only mean "I would prefer a shorter delay." Use the precise declaration when the definition is met.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC asks you to confirm fuel remaining during a long hold. How do you answer, and how is that different from declaring minimum fuel?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report usable fuel remaining as endurance in hours and minutes based on current consumption. That is an information report only. Minimum fuel is a separate declaration used when any further delay may cause me to land with less than planned reserve. A fuel emergency is declared when a safe landing is in doubt. I would use the exact phrase that matches the situation, not a softer substitute.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting total fuel at departure instead of remaining now.  
  ✔ Give current usable endurance.

- ❌ Mixing liters, pounds, and time in one confused call.  
  ✔ Prefer hours and minutes unless ATC specifies quantity.

- ❌ Saying "minimum fuel" as a casual request for priority.  
  ✔ Use minimum fuel only when the definition applies.

- ❌ Delaying the report while recalculating for too long.  
  ✔ Give the best current figure promptly, then refine if needed.
