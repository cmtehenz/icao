# Work in Progress

**Catalog ID:** 0217  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Work in Progress — ATC and aerodrome advice that construction, maintenance, or temporary works are active on or near a taxiway, apron, runway strip, or other movement area.

Work in Progress (WIP) changes the picture you briefed from the charts. Painted lines may be displaced, shoulders may be soft, vehicles and workers may be present, and published taxi routes may be unavailable. The phrase is a hazard alert and often a route constraint.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations at Congonhas, Florianópolis, Curitiba, Santos Dumont, and Guarulhos, WIP near taxiways and aprons demands extra rotor clearance, reduced taxi speed, and strict compliance with revised routes. Hover taxi near works can throw debris and endanger personnel.

This lesson builds Part 2 proficiency in hearing WIP advisories, reading them back, and adjusting taxi plans without improvising through closed areas.

## 📖 Meaning

Work in Progress is an aerodrome condition in which construction, maintenance, or temporary works are active on or adjacent to the movement area; in radiotelephony it is an advisory or restriction warning pilots of workers, vehicles, displaced routes, or unavailable surfaces.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Work in progress / Obras em andamento (WIP).

Condição do aeródromo em que construção, manutenção ou trabalhos temporários estão ativos na área de movimento ou adjacente a ela; na fraseologia, é aviso ou restrição alertando sobre trabalhadores, veículos, rotas deslocadas ou superfícies indisponíveis.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When WIP is advised, the pilot should:

- read back the taxi route including the WIP caution when required
- reduce speed and increase lookout for vehicles and personnel
- follow only the cleared route — do not shortcut around barriers
- protect rotor clearance and avoid downwash over loose material
- advise ATC if the works make the cleared route unsafe or unclear
- expect possible hold-short points or temporary stop bars

Work in Progress commonly involves:

- taxiway resurfacing or edge work
- lighting and cable installation
- apron expansion or stand marking changes
- temporary fencing and vehicle access points
- NOTAM-published closures with real-time local updates

**Important:** WIP does not authorize you to invent an alternate taxi path. If Alpha is blocked by works and you need Bravo, request a revised clearance. In the H130, never hover over open trenches, cones, or loose gravel — FOD and personnel risk rise immediately.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers advise work in progress to:

- protect workers and vehicles on the aerodrome
- prevent aircraft from entering closed or unsafe surfaces
- update crews on conditions that may differ from older charts
- layer caution onto an otherwise valid taxi clearance
- coordinate temporary routes during construction phases

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are ready to taxi at Congonhas after startup in the H130.

Ground: ANAC123, taxi to holding point runway one seven Left via Alpha and Bravo, work in progress left of Alpha, taxi with caution.

You read back the full route and the WIP caution. You keep left-side clearance, slow the taxi, and watch for vehicles exiting the works area. You do not drift toward the cones to "save distance."

At the holding point you are ready, informed, and still on the cleared pavement.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Alpha, work in progress left of Alpha, taxi with caution.
2. Tower: ANAC123, hold short of Bravo, work in progress, vehicles crossing.
3. Ground: ANAC123, Alpha closed due work in progress, taxi via Charlie to the apron.
4. Tower: ANAC123, air-taxi to the helipad, work in progress east side, remain west.
5. Ground: ANAC123, continue taxi, work in progress near stand five, give way to vehicles.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

3. ANAC123, Alpha closed, taxi via Charlie to the apron.
4. ANAC123, air-taxi to the helipad, remain west of works.
5. ANAC123, continue taxi, give way to vehicles near stand five.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Work in progress is temporary — and temporary is where crews get complacent.

You briefed the airport yesterday. Today Alpha has cones. The chart in your head is already wrong.

Listen for WIP on first contact and on taxi clearance. Pair it with "taxi with caution" when both are given. The caution is the attitude; the WIP is the reason.

Brazilian aerodromes under renovation can change weekly. Congonhas and Guarulhos often run parallel works. Do not assume yesterday's route is today's route.

For helicopters, the danger is not only collision with a barrier. Downwash can lift gravel into the rotor system and toward workers. Stay on hard surface. Stay on the clearance.

If the works make your route ambiguous, stop and ask. Ambiguity on the ground is cheaper to fix by radio than by metal.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Ground clears you via Alpha with work in progress left of the taxiway. What do you do operationally and on the radio?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would read back the taxi clearance including the work-in-progress caution. I would reduce taxi speed, keep clear of the works side, and watch for vehicles and personnel. I would stay strictly on the cleared taxiway and not pass barriers or cones. If the works make the route unclear or unsafe for rotor clearance, I would stop and request an alternate route from Ground. I would not invent a shortcut around the construction area.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reading back the route but omitting the WIP caution when it was part of the instruction.  
  ✔ Include work in progress or taxi with caution when ATC included it.

- ❌ Crossing cones or temporary barriers to reach a familiar stand.  
  ✔ Request a revised clearance; never self-clear through works.

- ❌ Hover-taxiing close to gravel and open works with strong downwash.  
  ✔ Use ground taxi or a wider path; protect personnel and the rotor system.

- ❌ Assuming a NOTAM from last week still matches today's WIP location.  
  ✔ Treat real-time ATC advice as the current truth.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are taxiing at Florianópolis for departure in the H130.

**Your first task**

Ground says: "ANAC123, taxi to holding point runway one four via Alpha, work in progress right of Alpha, taxi with caution." What do you say?

**Your second task**

Halfway along Alpha, a works vehicle blocks your path with no gap. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, taxi to holding point runway one four via Alpha, work in progress, taxi with caution. Second task: ANAC123, holding on Alpha, vehicle blocking taxiway, request further instructions — to stop safely and let ATC coordinate rather than passing through the works without clearance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Work in progress means the airport is changing under your skids.

Charts lag. Radio does not.

Read it back. Slow down. Stay on the cleared pavement.

Do not invent a path around cones. Do not blast workers with downwash.

Ask early when the route becomes unclear.

That is how academy crews look professional on Brazilian aprons — and how they keep people on the ground safe.
