# Expect Hold

**Catalog ID:** 0108  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Expect Hold — the ATC advisory informing a pilot to anticipate a holding pattern before it is formally assigned.

Expect Hold is advance notice. It allows the pilot to plan fuel, speed, and configuration before reaching the holding fix.

In the H130, delays are common near Florianópolis, Curitiba, and Congonhas during peak hours, instrument approaches, and adverse weather.

You must acknowledge the advisory, calculate fuel impact, and prepare your hold entry without confusion.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and holding contingency planning.

## 📖 Meaning

Expect Hold is an ATC advisory warning the pilot that a holding clearance will likely be issued at or before a specified fix, allowing the crew to prepare for the anticipated delay.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Espere pela espera.

Aviso do controle de que uma autorização de espera provavelmente será emitida, permitindo ao piloto planejar o combustível e a configuração com antecedência.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Expect Hold is received, the pilot should:

- acknowledge the advisory
- calculate fuel remaining and endurance at holding speed
- plan the appropriate holding entry for the published fix
- reduce to holding speed early to extend endurance if needed
- request the expected further clearance time (EFC) if not given

Expect Hold planning commonly includes:

- fuel check and endurance calculation
- review of holding fix and pattern direction
- ATIS and weather updates during hold
- notification to passengers if delay is significant

**Important:** Expect Hold is an advisory, not a clearance. You may receive a different routing or approach before reaching the fix. Do not enter the hold until formally cleared.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC issues Expect Hold to:

- give advance notice of expected delays for fuel planning
- allow crews to prepare hold entries before reaching the fix
- reduce workload at the holding fix by pre-informing aircraft
- sequence multiple aircraft into holding stacks efficiently
- meet ICAO requirement to provide further clearance time

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Curitiba in your H130 at four thousand feet.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, expect hold at DANOL, EFC time zero nine four five.

You check your fuel, calculate endurance, and reduce speed slightly.

You acknowledge:

Expect hold DANOL, EFC zero nine four five, ANAC123.

At zero nine four five you receive the approach clearance and no hold was needed — but you were ready if it was.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, expect hold at DANOL, EFC time zero nine four five.
2. Approach: ANAC123, expect further clearance at time one zero one zero, plan hold at IDAMO.
3. Center: ANAC123, expect hold en route, advise fuel state.
4. Approach: ANAC123, expect thirty minute delay, expect hold at ORTUS.
5. Approach: ANAC123, cancel expect hold, cleared ILS runway one four.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Expect hold DANOL, EFC zero nine four five, ANAC123.
2. Expect further clearance one zero one zero, hold at IDAMO, ANAC123.
3. Advising fuel, ANAC123 endurance three hours ten minutes.
4. Expect hold ORTUS, thirty minutes, ANAC123.
5. Cancel expect hold, cleared ILS runway one four, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Expect hold is not a surprise — it is a gift.

ATC is giving you time to plan before the delay happens.

The sequence:

Hear expect hold. Check fuel. Calculate endurance. Identify the fix. Acknowledge with EFC.

Brazilian pilots sometimes miss the EFC time or forget to request it.

If ATC does not give an EFC with expect hold, ask: Request EFC time, ANAC123.

In the H130, holding speed is normally below ninety knots. Plan accordingly.

Know your minimum fuel and alternate fuel before entering a hold.

If fuel becomes critical during the hold, declare minimum fuel or mayday fuel early — not late.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What do you do when you receive an expect hold advisory and how do you plan for it?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

When I receive expect hold, I acknowledge with the fix and EFC time. I immediately check my fuel and calculate my endurance at holding speed. If no EFC is given, I request one. I review the holding fix, plan the entry, and reduce to holding speed before reaching the fix. I know that expect hold is advisory only — the actual hold clearance may not come if traffic resolves.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating expect hold as a formal hold clearance.  
  ✔ It is an advisory. Wait for the actual holding clearance.

- ❌ Not checking fuel after receiving expect hold.  
  ✔ Fuel check and endurance calculation are mandatory immediately.

- ❌ Forgetting to request EFC if ATC does not provide it.  
  ✔ Ask: Request EFC, ANAC123.

- ❌ Entering the hold at full cruise speed.  
  ✔ Reduce to holding speed before reaching the fix.
