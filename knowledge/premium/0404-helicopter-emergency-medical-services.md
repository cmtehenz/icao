# Helicopter Emergency Medical Services

**Catalog ID:** 0404  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Helicopter Emergency Medical Services — HEMS operations that transport medical teams and patients under time-critical conditions, often to unprepared sites, hospitals, or inter-facility transfers.

HEMS is core H130 mission work in Brazil: highway accidents, remote clinic transfers, and hospital rooftop or helipad arrivals in São Paulo, Rio, and coastal cities.

You must communicate priority, landing-site constraints, patient status at a high level, and coordination with ATC and ground emergency services in clear ICAO English for Parts 1, 2, and 4.

## 📖 Meaning

Helicopter Emergency Medical Services are specialized helicopter operations that provide rapid transport of medical personnel and patients to and from accident scenes, remote locations, or medical facilities, often requiring priority handling and landings at improvised or hospital sites.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Serviços médicos de emergência por helicóptero (HEMS) / aeromédico.

Operações especializadas de helicóptero que fornecem transporte rápido de equipes médicas e pacientes de/para locais de acidente, áreas remotas ou instalações médicas, frequentemente exigindo prioridade e pousos em locais improvisados ou hospitalares.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When conducting HEMS operations, the pilot should:

- state HEMS or medical priority early when requesting clearance
- brief landing-site hazards: wires, slope, dust, bystanders
- coordinate ETA with hospital or scene command when able
- request priority landing or hover for patient transfer as required
- keep medical details minimal on frequency — operational needs only

HEMS reporting commonly includes:

- HEMS flight, request priority
- landing at scene / hospital helipad
- short final, request traffic advice
- ready for departure, patient on board, request clearance

**Important:** Priority is requested, not assumed. Say HEMS and the operational need. Protect the landing site verbally if bystanders or vehicles threaten the approach.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers support HEMS operations by:

- granting priority sequencing when traffic permits
- providing traffic information near hospital helipads
- coordinating with tower and approach for scene flights
- expediting departures for time-critical transfers
- alerting other aircraft to low-level HEMS activity

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are airborne in your H130 from Congonhas on a HEMS launch to a highway accident on the Anchieta.

You transmit:

São Paulo Approach, ANAC123, HEMS flight, routing to accident site south of São Bernardo, request priority and traffic information, descending one thousand five hundred.

Approach provides traffic and clears you as requested. On scene, you report landing secured. After patient on board, you request priority to the hospital helipad.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger HEMS, priority approved as traffic permits, report on scene.
2. Tower: ANAC123, hospital helipad winds two three zero at eight, no reported traffic, report landing.
3. Approach: ANAC123, traffic twelve o'clock three miles opposite direction, advise in sight.
4. Tower: ANAC123, cleared for takeoff from scene, remain clear of Congonhas final.
5. Approach: ANAC123, cleared direct hospital, descend pilot's discretion, report pad in sight.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, HEMS, priority, will report on scene.
2. ANAC123, hospital helipad, will report landing.
3. ANAC123, traffic in sight, will maintain separation.
4. ANAC123, lifting from scene, remaining clear of final, request hospital.
5. ANAC123, pad in sight, descending for landing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

HEMS English is short, calm, and operational.

You are not a radio drama. You are a logistics chain with rotors.

Lead with HEMS. State where you are going. State what you need: priority, traffic, landing report. Do not broadcast patient diagnosis. ATC needs ETA, destination, and constraints — not clinical detail.

In the H130, scene landings in Brazil mean wires, spectators, and dust. If the site is unsafe, say unable to land, requesting alternate. That is professional, not failure.

Hospital pads in São Paulo and Rio sit inside dense traffic. Call early. Report pad in sight. Do not assume Tower sees you on a rooftop.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You are launched on a HEMS mission to a highway accident. What do you say to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would identify the flight as HEMS and state routing and needs: ANAC123, HEMS flight, routing to accident site south of São Bernardo, request priority and traffic information, descending one thousand five hundred. On scene I would report landing or hovering. After uplift I would request priority to the hospital helipad and report pad in sight.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Assuming priority without stating HEMS.  
  ✔ Say HEMS and request priority explicitly.

- ❌ Giving long medical details on ATC frequency.  
  ✔ Keep calls operational: destination, priority, landing status.

- ❌ Landing without reporting on scene in busy terminal airspace.  
  ✔ Report on scene and lifting so ATC can protect traffic.

- ❌ Accepting an unsafe roadside site to "save time."  
  ✔ If unsafe: unable to land, request alternate site.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, HEMS Flight, Request Priority

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, HEMZ FLITE, reh-KWEST pry-OR-ih-tee

**Word Stress**

- HEMS → HEMZ
- Flight → FLITE
- Priority → pry-OR-ih-tee (stress on second)

Practice:

HEMS flight... request priority... report on scene.

Together:

ANAC123, HEMS flight, request priority, routing to accident site, will report on scene.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**HEMS** — **H**ail the flight as HEMS, **E**xpedite with priority request, **M**inimize medical detail on freq, **S**ecure the site before landing.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

HEMS launch from Congonhas to a roadside accident, then transfer to a hospital pad.

**Your first task**

Initial call to Approach after departure.

**Your second task**

Patient on board, lifting from the scene, hospital pad is your destination. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, HEMS flight, airborne Congonhas, routing accident site São Bernardo, request priority and traffic information. Second task: ANAC123, lifting from scene, patient on board, request priority direct hospital helipad, will report pad in sight.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

HEMS is the H130 at its most purposeful.

Clear identity. Clear priority request. Clear site reports.

Protect the landing area. Protect the frequency from clutter. Protect the patient by flying a safe aircraft first.

Say HEMS. Fly the mission. Land only when the site is safe.
