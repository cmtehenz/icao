# Cyclic Trim Runaway

**Catalog ID:** 0451  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Cyclic Trim Runaway — uncommanded cyclic trim motor movement that drives the cyclic toward a stop or an unintended position, producing rapid pitch or roll changes.

Cyclic trim runaway is immediately dangerous. The pilot must oppose the motion, disable the trim per AFM procedure, and recover a controllable attitude before communicating.

For H130 helicopter operations, this is a critical flight control emergency. Declare Mayday, request immediate landing, and keep maneuvering to the minimum required for a safe approach.

Understanding how to report cyclic trim runaway in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Cyclic Trim Runaway is an uncommanded operation of the cyclic trim system that moves the cyclic control without pilot input, causing unintended pitch or roll attitude changes.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Runaway do trim do cíclico.

Operação não comandada do sistema de trim do cíclico que move o comando cíclico sem entrada do piloto, causando mudanças não intencionais de atitude de arfagem ou rolamento.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When cyclic trim runaway occurs, the pilot should:

- physically oppose the cyclic movement and hold attitude
- deactivate or isolate cyclic trim per AFM memory items
- re-trim manually if possible and assess remaining control forces
- land as soon as possible
- declare Mayday while or after recovering control

Cyclic trim runaway reporting commonly includes:

- cyclic trim runaway
- uncommanded cyclic movement, request immediate landing
- position and souls on board
- request straight-in approach and emergency services

**Important:** Do not let go of the cyclic to reach switches until you have a control strategy. Attitude first — always.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to cyclic trim runaway to:

- clear an immediate landing path
- eliminate complex vectors and altitude changes
- coordinate fire and rescue
- provide wind information early for a stable final
- keep communications short and clear

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are in cruise at one thousand eight hundred feet in your H130 toward Campinas.

The cyclic begins moving forward uncommanded. The nose drops. You pull against the runaway, hit the trim cut-out per procedure, and recover level flight with heavy forward pressure remaining.

You declare Mayday, report cyclic trim runaway, and request immediate landing at Campinas. Approach clears traffic. You fly a gentle straight-in and land.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Cyclic trim runaway tries to fly the helicopter without you.

Your job is to win that fight in the first seconds. Oppose. Cut out. Recover.

On the H130, know exactly where the trim release or cut-out is by touch. Looking for a switch while the nose is tucking is how attitudes get extreme.

After isolation, expect abnormal stick forces. Brief yourself for a flat, stable approach — no steep banks, no aggressive flare practice.

Mayday is appropriate. This is a flight control emergency, even if you "won" the initial fight.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Cyclic trim runs away nose-down. You isolate it and recover. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, cyclic trim runaway, trim isolated, request immediate landing nearest suitable. I would give position and souls on board when able.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Releasing the cyclic to search for the cut-out switch.  
  ✔ Hold attitude; activate cut-out without losing the cyclic.

- ❌ Declaring only a minor trim issue after a full runaway.  
  ✔ Use Mayday — cyclic runaway is critical.

- ❌ Flying a steep turn to a preferred runway.  
  ✔ Request straight-in; minimize maneuvering.

- ❌ Continuing cruise to save time after successful isolation.  
  ✔ Land as soon as possible for maintenance inspection.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Cyclic trim runaway is a contest for the disk.

Win it with your hands and the cut-out. Then Mayday. Then land.

On the H130, do not celebrate a recovered attitude as a solved problem. The trim system failed — end the flight on the ground.
