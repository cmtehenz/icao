# Precautionary Landing

**Catalog ID:** 0042  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers a critical distinction that many pilots confuse.

Precautionary Landing means landing when a potential emergency exists, but immediate danger is not yet confirmed.

This is less urgent than emergency landing — you are choosing to land to inspect a problem on the ground.

You may use Pan Pan or simply inform ATC of your intention without declaring full distress.

In your H130, a precautionary landing might follow unusual vibration, a doubtful indication, or a bird strike assessment with no confirmed damage.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Precautionary Landing is a landing made when a potential emergency exists but immediate danger to the aircraft or occupants is not yet confirmed.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pouso precautório.

Pouso realizado quando existe uma possível emergência, mas o perigo imediato ainda não está confirmado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Precautionary Landing is appropriate, the pilot should:

- inform ATC of the abnormal indication or concern
- state the intention to make a precautionary landing for inspection
- request vectors to a suitable airport without full emergency response if not required
- avoid declaring Mayday unless the situation escalates
- inspect the aircraft on the ground before continuing

Precautionary Landing reporting commonly includes:

- requesting precautionary landing at the nearest suitable airport
- precautionary landing inbound to inspect indications
- abnormal vibration, requesting precautionary landing
- no emergency declared, precautionary landing to verify gear indication

**Important:** Precautionary landing is not emergency landing. Use it when you need to land and inspect — not when immediate danger exists.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Precautionary Landing requests to:

- provide vectors to a suitable airport
- assign landing sequence without full emergency alert unless requested
- ask for the nature of the problem
- coordinate ground inspection support if requested
- maintain normal traffic flow when no distress is declared

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You completed a bird strike checklist in your H130 after departure from Belo Horizonte.

No engine damage confirmed, but you feel slight airframe vibration that was not present before.

You decide to land and inspect rather than continue to your destination.

You transmit:

Pan Pan Pan, Belo Horizonte Approach, ANAC123, abnormal vibration after bird strike assessment, requesting precautionary landing at Belo Horizonte to inspect the aircraft.

Approach asks you to say the nature of the problem and assigns vectors.

You land, inspect, find minor fan blade damage, and report to maintenance.

No Mayday was required — but the precautionary landing was the right decision.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger precautionary landing, say nature of the problem.
2. Approach: ANAC123, nearest airport twelve o'clock, one zero miles.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land runway three zero, emergency services not required unless requested.
4. Approach: ANAC123, maintain present altitude, say when ready for vectors.
5. Tower: ANAC123, report gear down and locked.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Precautionary landing is the concept many Brazilian pilots do not know in English.

They use emergency landing for every abnormal indication.

That is wrong — and it triggers full emergency response when you may not need it.

Precautionary landing means: I am not in immediate danger, but I want to land and check.

Emergency landing means: I am landing now because safety is at serious risk.

The distinction matters for ATC resource allocation and for your ICAO exam.

After a bird strike with no confirmed damage but lingering doubt — precautionary landing.

With doubtful gear indication but stable approach — precautionary landing.

With fire in the cabin — that is emergency landing, not precautionary.

In the H130, you have the flexibility to land at many suitable areas — but still use the correct phraseology.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between precautionary and emergency landing?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Emergency landing is when safety of the aircraft or occupants is at serious and immediate risk. Precautionary landing is when a potential problem exists but immediate danger is not confirmed. I use precautionary landing to inspect the aircraft on the ground, and emergency landing when I must land immediately due to distress.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Using emergency landing for every abnormal indication.  
  ✔ Use precautionary landing when you need to inspect, not when in immediate danger.

- ❌ Not knowing the precautionary landing concept in English.  
  ✔ Learn and use precautionary landing — it is an ICAO exam concept.

- ❌ Declaring Mayday for a precautionary landing.  
  ✔ Use Pan Pan or simply inform ATC — Mayday is for confirmed distress.

- ❌ Confusing precautionary landing with normal diversion.  
  ✔ Precautionary landing implies an abnormal condition requiring ground inspection.
