# Blocked Transmission

**Catalog ID:** 0305  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Blocked Transmission — a radio transmission that is stepped on, covered, or made unreadable because two stations transmit at the same time, or because a stuck microphone blocks the frequency. Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: recognize a blocked call, wait, retransmit, and help free a blocked frequency when a stuck mic is suspected.

Blocked transmission is High priority because critical clearances disappear under overlapping carriers, and a stuck microphone can silence an entire frequency.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for blocked transmissions, retransmits, and stuck-microphone advisories.

Understanding blocked transmission in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Blocked Transmission is a radiotelephony message that cannot be understood because another transmission overlaps it, or because a continuous carrier from a stuck microphone occupies the frequency, requiring stations to wait, retransmit, and, when appropriate, advise that the frequency is blocked.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Transmissão bloqueada / Blocked Transmission.

Mensagem radiotelefônica que não pode ser compreendida porque outra transmissão se sobrepõe a ela, ou porque uma portadora contínua de um microfone travado ocupa a frequência, exigindo que as estações aguardem, retransmitam e, quando apropriado, avisem que a frequência está bloqueada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a transmission is blocked, the crew should:

- recognize overlapping voices or a continuous carrier with no clear speech
- wait for a break, then retransmit your message clearly
- avoid keying over ATC or another aircraft — listen before transmit
- if a stuck microphone is suspected, advise: frequency blocked / possible stuck microphone
- check your own transmit switch and audio panel if others report a continuous block
- for H130: be especially careful in dense terminal frequencies and during high-workload hover or approach

Blocked transmission exchanges commonly include:

- blocked / transmission blocked
- say again, you were blocked
- frequency blocked / possible stuck mic
- retransmit your message
- check your microphone

**Important:** If your clearance was blocked, you do not have the clearance. Wait and ask for a repeat. If you may be the stuck mic, check your switch immediately.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers manage blocked transmissions to:

- recover clearances that were stepped on
- keep the frequency usable during stuck-mic events
- instruct aircraft to check microphones
- sequence retransmits so only one station speaks
- protect safety-critical instructions from being lost under overlap

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: busy São Paulo approach frequency. You call for descent; another aircraft keys at the same time.

Approach: ANAC123, you were blocked, say again.

You wait one second, then retransmit cleanly:

ANAC123, request descent.

Later, a continuous carrier blocks everyone. You hear:

All stations, frequency blocked, possible stuck microphone.

You immediately check your own push-to-talk and audio panel. When the frequency clears, you resume normal calls — shorter and with better listening discipline.

Blocked transmissions are a daily Part 2 reality in Brazilian terminal airspace.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, blocked, say again.
2. Approach: ANAC123, you were stepped on, retransmit.
3. Control: All stations, frequency blocked, check your microphones.
4. Tower: ANAC123, transmission blocked, say again intentions.
5. Ground: ANAC123, frequency clear now, go ahead.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Blocked transmission is almost always a listening failure before a talking failure.

Listen. Pause. Then key. If two of you talk, both messages die.

When ATC says you were blocked, do not argue — retransmit. When the frequency is blocked by a stuck mic, check yourself first. The stuck microphone is often the aircraft that does not know it is transmitting.

In the H130, cyclic and collective workload can leave a transmit switch trapped or a hand lingering on the PTT. Build a habit: after every call, confirm you are not still keyed.

Part 2 examiners watch whether you recover cleanly. Blocked is normal. Guessing the blocked clearance is not.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Your request to ATC is blocked by another aircraft, and later the frequency appears blocked by a stuck microphone. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

If my transmission was blocked, I would wait for a break and retransmit the message clearly, or request say again if ATC's reply was blocked. I would not assume the clearance was received. If the frequency is blocked by a continuous carrier, I would check my own microphone and audio panel immediately, remain silent if I am not the cause, and wait until the frequency is clear before calling again.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Immediately keying again on top of the overlapping call.  
  ✔ Wait for silence, then retransmit once.

- ❌ Assuming ATC heard your blocked request.  
  ✔ No acknowledgment means retransmit or confirm.

- ❌ Never checking your own PTT during a stuck-mic event.  
  ✔ Always verify you are not blocking the frequency.

- ❌ Giving a long retransmit on a congested frequency.  
  ✔ Short, clear, one idea per transmission.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**BLOCK** — **B**reathe and listen first, **L**et the overlap finish, **O**ffer one clean retransmit, **C**heck your own mic if stuck, **K**eep clearances unconfirmed until copied.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You request descent; Approach says you were blocked. Later a continuous carrier blocks the frequency.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit after being told you were blocked?

**Your second task**

What do you do and say when the frequency is blocked and a stuck mic is possible?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Wait briefly, then ANAC123, request descent — a clean retransmit. Second task: Check my push-to-talk and audio panel immediately; if clear on my side, remain silent until the frequency opens. If ATC asks stations to check microphones, I may transmit: ANAC123, microphone checked. I would not assume my earlier blocked request was approved.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Blocked transmission is High-priority Part 2 discipline for busy Brazilian frequencies.

Listen before you key. Retransmit when blocked. Check your mic when the frequency dies under a carrier.

In the H130, PTT discipline is part of airmanship. Clearances that were blocked do not exist until they are copied again.

Wait. Retransmit. Verify. Never guess.

That is the blocked transmission lesson — core Part 2 radio safety.
