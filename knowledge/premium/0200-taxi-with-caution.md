# Taxi with Caution

**Catalog ID:** 0200  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Taxi with Caution — an ATC instruction or advisory telling the pilot to taxi carefully because of a hazard, restriction, or condition that requires extra attention on the movement area.

"Taxi with caution" is not a clearance to ignore other instructions. It is a warning layered onto a taxi clearance: proceed, but be alert for the stated or implied hazard — vehicles, wet surface, construction, birds, jet blast, or limited wingtip clearance.

For H130 helicopter operations at Congonhas, Guarulhos, Santos Dumont, Curitiba, and Florianópolis, taxi may be ground taxi or hover taxi. The caution still applies: rotor clearance, FOD, vehicles, and other aircraft matter.

This lesson builds Part 2 proficiency in hearing, reading back, and acting on cautionary taxi instructions in DECEA/ICAO phraseology.

## 📖 Meaning

Taxi with Caution is an ATC instruction or advisory directing the pilot to proceed on the taxi clearance while exercising increased care due to a hazard, surface condition, vehicle, work in progress, or other risk on or near the taxi route.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Taxi with caution / Táxi com cautela.

Instrução ou aviso do controle para prosseguir no táxi autorizado exercendo atenção redobrada devido a perigo, condição de superfície, veículo, obras ou outro risco na rota de táxi ou próximo a ela.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When instructed to taxi with caution, the pilot should:

- read back the taxi clearance including the caution element when required
- reduce taxi speed and increase lookout
- identify the specific hazard if ATC stated one
- be prepared to stop if the hazard requires it
- advise ATC if unable to continue safely
- for helicopters, consider hover height, downwash, and rotor clearance relative to the hazard

Taxi with caution commonly relates to:

- vehicles or personnel on or near the taxiway
- construction and displaced taxi lines
- wet, slippery, or contaminated surfaces
- birds or wildlife
- opposite-direction or converging taxi traffic
- jet blast or rotor wash areas

**Important:** "Taxi with caution" does not cancel hold-short instructions, runway crossings, or route restrictions. Obey the full clearance. The caution is additional risk awareness, not a blank authorization.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers say taxi with caution to:

- alert crews to non-standard or temporary hazards
- reduce ground incidents without stopping all movement
- share responsibility for see-and-avoid on the maneuvering area
- highlight conditions that charts may not show in real time
- protect vehicles, workers, and other aircraft during simultaneous operations

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You have landed at Congonhas in the H130 and are cleared to taxi to the ramp.

Tower: ANAC123, taxi to the apron via Alpha, taxi with caution, vehicle on the left side of Alpha.

You read back the route and the caution. You slow the taxi, keep the vehicle in sight, and maintain rotor clearance. You do not assume the vehicle will move — you assume you must protect the aircraft.

At the apron you report ramp and shut down or park as cleared. The caution did its job: you arrived aware, not surprised.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, taxi via Alpha to the apron, taxi with caution, works in progress left of Alpha.
2. Ground: ANAC123, taxi to holding point runway one seven Right via Bravo, taxi with caution, vehicle ahead.
3. Tower: ANAC123, continue taxi, taxi with caution, wet surface.
4. Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Charlie, taxi with caution, opposite direction traffic.
5. Tower: ANAC123, air-taxi to the helipad, taxi with caution, personnel on the right.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Taxi with caution means: the clearance is still valid, but the environment is not routine.

Slow down. Look outside. Identify the hazard ATC named — or scan harder if they did not specify.

In helicopters, "caution" often means people, vehicles, and loose objects in the downwash zone. A caution that is minor for a light airplane can be serious for rotorcraft.

Never let a caution phrase replace a hold-short. If you were told to hold short, you hold short — cautiously or not.

Read back the caution when it is part of the instruction. It confirms you heard the risk, not only the route.

At busy Brazilian airports, ground hazards change by the hour. Treat every "taxi with caution" as real-time intelligence.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC clears you to taxi and adds "taxi with caution, vehicle on the taxiway." What does this mean and what do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

It means I am cleared to taxi on the assigned route, but I must proceed carefully because there is a vehicle hazard on or near the taxiway. I would reduce taxi speed, keep the vehicle in sight, maintain safe clearance, and be ready to stop if needed. I would still comply with all route and hold-short instructions. If I could not continue safely, I would stop and advise ATC.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reading back only the route and omitting "taxi with caution."  
  ✔ Include the caution in the readback when it is part of the instruction.

- ❌ Treating caution as permission to ignore hold-short points.  
  ✔ Caution adds awareness; it does not cancel restrictions.

- ❌ Continuing at normal taxi speed near the stated hazard.  
  ✔ Slow down, increase lookout, protect clearance.

- ❌ For helicopters, ignoring downwash effects near personnel/vehicles.  
  ✔ Adjust hover/taxi technique to protect people and equipment.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

At Guarulhos, Ground says: "ANAC123, taxi to the apron via Alpha and Bravo, taxi with caution, construction left of Bravo."

**Your first task**

Give the correct readback.

**Your second task**

You see workers closer to the taxi line than expected and are not sure about rotor clearance. What do you transmit?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, taxi to the apron via Alpha and Bravo, taxi with caution, construction left of Bravo. Second task: ANAC123, unable to continue on Bravo due to construction clearance, request alternate taxi route — or request to hold present position. I would not force the taxi past a clearance doubt.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Taxi with caution is ATC telling you the surface is not normal.

Hear it. Read it back. Slow down. Protect the aircraft and everyone around the rotors.

The route clearance still rules the path. The caution rules your attention.

In H130 operations at Brazilian airports, that extra attention prevents the most common ground mistakes: rushing, assuming, and missing the hazard that was already named.

Cleared to taxi. Cleared to be careful. Do both.
