# Maintain Present Speed

**Catalog ID:** 0018  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers an instruction that controllers use every day to sequence traffic safely.

Sometimes ATC does not want you to accelerate.

Sometimes ATC does not want you to slow down.

Instead, the controller simply says:

"Maintain Present Speed."

This instruction helps keep predictable spacing between aircraft.

Professional pilots understand that maintaining a stable speed is just as important as maintaining an assigned altitude or heading.

## 📖 Meaning

Maintain Present Speed instructs the pilot to continue flying at the aircraft's current indicated airspeed unless another speed is assigned.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Mantenha a velocidade atual.

Continue voando na velocidade indicada atual até receber nova instrução do controle.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Maintain Present Speed, the pilot should:

- maintain the current indicated airspeed
- avoid accelerating or decelerating unnecessarily
- continue complying with assigned headings and altitudes
- advise ATC if unable to comply

The instruction is intended to preserve traffic spacing and sequencing.

It does not modify:

- heading
- altitude
- route
- navigation clearance

Only the speed is affected.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Maintain Present Speed to:

- maintain spacing between aircraft
- sequence arrivals efficiently
- avoid overtaking slower traffic
- prevent compression of traffic on final
- reduce workload
- stabilize traffic flow

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're flying your H130 toward Florianópolis Airport.

Another helicopter is three miles ahead on the same approach.

Approach wants to keep the spacing constant.

The controller transmits:

Approach: ANAC123, maintain present speed.

You continue flying at your current approach speed.

A few minutes later, after the preceding helicopter lands, ATC says:

Approach: ANAC123, reduce speed at your discretion.

Only then do you adjust your speed.

Maintaining your original speed helped preserve safe separation.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, maintain present speed.
2. Approach: ANAC123, maintain present speed until five-mile final.
3. Center: ANAC123, maintain present speed due traffic.
4. Approach: ANAC123, maintain present speed, expect further clearance shortly.
5. Tower: ANAC123, maintain present speed, number two following traffic.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine you're driving on a highway.

The car in front of you is moving at exactly the right speed.

If you suddenly accelerate, you'll reduce the safety distance.

If you brake unnecessarily, you'll affect the traffic behind you.

The safest action is simply to keep the same speed.

That's exactly what ATC wants.

When the controller says:

Maintain Present Speed

they're not concerned with your aircraft's performance.

They're managing the entire traffic flow.

Professional pilots understand that speed is one of the controller's most valuable tools for maintaining safe separation.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Why would ATC instruct an aircraft to maintain its present speed?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

ATC may instruct an aircraft to maintain its present speed to preserve safe spacing between aircraft, improve traffic sequencing, and reduce the need for additional vectoring. The pilot should continue flying at the current speed until another clearance is received.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reducing speed because the airport is close.  
  ✔ Continue at the present speed until ATC changes the instruction.

- ❌ Increasing speed to "help" the controller.  
  ✔ Maintain the assigned speed unless instructed otherwise.

- ❌ Forgetting that helicopters may also receive speed instructions.  
  ✔ Although helicopters have different operating characteristics, speed control is still an important ATC tool.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Maintaining present speed, ANAC123.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Maintain Present Speed

**Pronunciation:** main-TAIN PREZ-ent SPEED

**Word Stress**

- Maintain → second syllable
- Present → first syllable
- Speed → strong final word

Practice:

Maintain... Present... Speed...

Now together:

Maintain Present Speed.

Then practice:

Maintaining present speed, ANAC123.

Keep speed short and clear—avoid stretching the vowel.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Imagine you're walking through a crowded airport terminal.

Everyone is moving smoothly.

If you suddenly speed up, you'll bump into someone.

If you suddenly stop, people behind you may collide with you.

The safest action is to continue at the same pace.

That's exactly what Maintain Present Speed means.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Today's lesson highlights another key principle of professional flying.

ATC doesn't manage only your aircraft.

The controller manages every aircraft in the airspace.

Sometimes the safest instruction is not to climb, descend, or turn.

Maintain your present speed.

Predictable pilots make traffic predictable—and predictable traffic is safe traffic.
