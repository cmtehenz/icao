# Line Up and Wait

**Catalog ID:** 0003  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the most safety-critical runway instructions you'll ever receive.

Many runway incursions and serious accidents have happened because pilots misunderstood the difference between:

- Line Up and Wait
- Cleared for Takeoff

Professional pilots never confuse these two clearances.

## Meaning

Enter the runway, align the aircraft with the runway centerline, and wait for takeoff clearance.

## Portuguese Meaning

Alinhe na pista e aguarde autorização para decolar.

## Operational Meaning

A Line Up and Wait (LUAW) clearance authorizes the aircraft to enter the active runway and position itself for departure.

It does NOT authorize the takeoff roll.

The aircraft must remain stopped on the runway centerline until ATC issues:

"Cleared for Takeoff."

## Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Line Up and Wait to:

- reduce runway occupancy time
- improve departure efficiency
- prepare aircraft for immediate departure
- sequence arriving and departing traffic
- minimize delays during busy operations

## Real Operational Context

You're flying your H130 at a controlled airport.

Ground has already transferred you to Tower.

Tower says:

"PT-ABC, runway one eight, line up and wait."

You taxi onto the runway.

Align with the centerline.

Complete your final checks.

But you do not begin the takeoff roll.

A Boeing 737 is still on short final.

Only after it lands and clears the runway will Tower say:

"PT-ABC, runway one eight, cleared for takeoff."

Only then do you begin your departure.

## Real ATC Phraseology

1. PT-ABC, runway one eight, line up and wait.
2. PT-ABC, line up and wait runway two seven.
3. PT-ABC, line up and wait behind landing traffic.
4. PT-ABC, runway one eight, line up and wait, traffic departing.
5. PT-ABC, line up and wait, expect immediate departure.

## Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Line up and wait runway one eight, PT-ABC.
2. Lining up and waiting runway two seven, PT-ABC.
3. Line up and wait behind landing traffic, PT-ABC.
4. Runway one eight, line up and wait, PT-ABC.
5. Line up and wait, expecting immediate departure, PT-ABC.

## Captain Delta Teaching

Gustavo, imagine you're first in line at a traffic light.

The light is still red.

You move your car up to the stop line because you're next.

But you don't drive through the intersection.

That's exactly what Line Up and Wait means.

ATC is positioning you so departure can happen quickly, but the runway is not yet yours.

Never assume that entering the runway means you're cleared to depart.

In aviation, one extra second of patience is much safer than one second of assumption.

## Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between "Line Up and Wait" and "Cleared for Takeoff"?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

"Line Up and Wait" allows the aircraft to enter the runway and prepare for departure, but the pilot must remain stopped. "Cleared for Takeoff" is the instruction that authorizes the aircraft to begin the takeoff roll.

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Believing Line Up and Wait authorizes takeoff.  
  ✔ It only authorizes runway entry.

- ❌ Beginning the takeoff roll after hearing: "...behind landing traffic..."  
  ✔ Wait for: Cleared for Takeoff.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Line up and wait runway one eight, PT-ABC.

- ❌ Forgetting the runway number.  
  Always include the assigned runway in your readback.

## Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Line Up and Wait

**Pronunciation:** LAIN up and WAIT

**Word Stress**

- Line → strong
- Wait → strong

Do not rush the phrase.

Practice:

Line... Up... And... Wait...

Now naturally:

Line up and wait.

Then inside a transmission:

PT-ABC, runway one eight, line up and wait.

## Memory Trick

Think of a sprinter at the Olympic Games.

The athlete is already in position.

Hands on the ground.

Feet in the blocks.

Ready to run.

But movement only begins when the starter says:

Go.

Line Up and Wait puts you in position.

Cleared for Takeoff is the "Go."
