# Tow Bar

**Catalog ID:** 0206  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** Low  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Tow Bar — the ground equipment used to connect a tug or tow vehicle to an aircraft so it can be moved on the apron or hangar area without using its own power.

A tow bar is not a flight procedure. It is ground-handling vocabulary that appears in ICAO English exams, ramp coordination, and maintenance conversations. Pilots must understand it, report when it is connected or disconnected, and communicate clearly when towing is in progress.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, the H130 is more often repositioned by hover taxi or ground handling with rotor blades secured. Tow bars are still used for hangar moves, maintenance repositioning, and fixed-wing traffic sharing the same Brazilian airport. Knowing the English keeps you professional on a mixed apron.

Understanding how to discuss tow bar status in ICAO-standard English — including advisories to Ground and coordination with ramp staff — is useful for Part 2 proficiency and ramp safety awareness.

## 📖 Meaning

A tow bar is a rigid or articulated metal bar that connects a ground tug to an aircraft's nose gear or designated tow point, allowing the aircraft to be pushed or pulled on the ground without engine power.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Barra de reboque / tow bar.

Barra rígida ou articulada que conecta um trator de pátio ao trem de nariz ou ponto de reboque da aeronave, permitindo empurrar ou puxar a aeronave no solo sem usar a potência dos motores.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a tow bar is involved in ground operations, the crew or ground staff should:

- confirm the correct tow bar type for the aircraft before connection
- ensure parking brake is set and rotors or propellers are secured as required before tow
- advise Ground when the aircraft is being towed and when the tow is complete
- keep the tow bar clear of taxi routes when not in use
- never start engines or engage rotors with a tow bar still connected
- report any tow bar damage or inability to disconnect before departure

Tow bar operations commonly include:

- connecting the tow bar at the parking stand for pushback or hangar move
- disconnecting and removing the tow bar before engine start
- advising ATC that the aircraft is under tow
- holding position until the tow bar is clear of the aircraft

**Important:** A connected tow bar means the aircraft is not free to taxi or start. Confirm visually and by radio that the tow bar is disconnected and clear before requesting startup or hover taxi. On a busy Brazilian apron, a forgotten tow bar is a ground incident waiting to happen.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers and ground coordination use tow bar awareness to:

- know which aircraft are under tow versus under their own power
- protect taxi routes from tug and tow-bar movements
- sequence pushbacks and hangar moves with active taxi traffic
- avoid clearing an aircraft to start or taxi while still connected
- coordinate maintenance repositioning on the movement area

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on the ramp at Florianópolis after an H130 training sortie. Maintenance needs to move a fixed-wing trainer from the hangar to stand four. The tug is connected with the tow bar.

Ground staff advise the academy operations desk that the aircraft is under tow across the apron.

You are preparing your H130 for the next lesson. Before you request startup, you look left and see the tow in progress across your planned hover-taxi path.

You hold. You wait until the tow is clear. Then you call Ground for startup and hover taxi to the helipad.

On the radio, when Ground needs the status of the towed aircraft, the tug crew reports that the tow bar is connected and the move is in progress. When the aircraft reaches stand four, they disconnect the tow bar and report ready for parking brake set.

You never start with a tow bar in the way. You never hover taxi across an active tow without clearance and visual confirmation.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, hold position, aircraft under tow crossing Alpha with tow bar connected.
2. Ground: ANAC123, tow bar disconnect complete, cleared to start engines.
3. Apron: ANAC123, confirm tow bar removed before startup.
4. Ground: ANAC123, aircraft towed to stand four, tow bar clear, taxiway Alpha available.
5. Ground: ANAC123, delay startup, tug and tow bar still on the apron near your position.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, holding position, traffic under tow in sight.
2. ANAC123, tow bar disconnect confirmed, cleared to start.
3. ANAC123, tow bar removed, ready for startup.
4. ANAC123, roger, Alpha available, request hover taxi to helipad.
5. ANAC123, delaying startup, standing by for tow bar clear.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Tow bar is simple vocabulary with serious consequences.

If the bar is connected, the aircraft is a towed object — not a taxiing aircraft. Engines off. Rotors stopped. Brakes as directed by the tug crew.

In the H130 world you may not use a tow bar every day. You will still share ramps with aircraft that do. You will hear Ground say an aircraft is under tow. You will be told to hold for a tug crossing your path.

Treat that like any surface conflict. Hold. Look. Confirm clear. Then move.

Before every startup, part of your scan is: chocks, covers, and tow bar — gone. If anything is still attached, you are not ready.

ICAO English exams love this word because it tests whether you understand ground-handling English, not only airborne phraseology. Know it. Use it correctly. Never rush past it.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You are ready to start engines on the apron. Ground staff signal that the tow bar is still connected to a neighboring aircraft blocking your path. Explain what you do and what you communicate.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would not start engines or request taxi until the tow is clear. I would hold position, keep the parking brake set, and advise Ground that I am ready but delayed because an aircraft under tow is blocking my path. I would wait for confirmation that the tow bar is disconnected and the tug is clear of the taxi route. Only then would I request startup and hover taxi clearance. Safety on the apron comes before schedule.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "barra de reboque" on the English frequency instead of tow bar.  
  ✔ Use ICAO English: tow bar connected / tow bar removed / under tow.

- ❌ Requesting startup while the tow bar is still attached or nearby.  
  ✔ Confirm tow bar disconnected and clear before requesting startup.

- ❌ Ignoring a tug under tow because "it is only ground equipment."  
  ✔ Treat towed aircraft as traffic. Hold and give way as instructed.

- ❌ Assuming hover taxi can pass over a tow in progress.  
  ✔ Hold short of the tow path until Ground confirms clear.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Tow Bar Removed, Ready for Startup

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, TOH BAR ree-MOOVD, REH-dee for START-up

**Word Stress**

- Tow → TOH
- Bar → BAR
- Removed → ree-MOOVD (stress on second syllable)
- Startup → START-up

Practice:

Tow bar removed... ready for startup.

Together:

ANAC123, tow bar removed, ready for startup, request hover taxi to the helipad.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**TOW BAR** — **T**ug only moves with bar connected, **O**ff engines while connected, **W**ait for disconnect before start, **B**rakes set during tow, **A**dvise Ground when under tow, **R**emove bar before any rotor turn.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are parked at Congonhas after an H130 training flight. A twin-engine trainer is being towed across taxiway Alpha toward the hangar. You need to hover taxi to the fuel pit.

**Your first task**

Ground says: "ANAC123, hold position, aircraft under tow crossing Alpha." What do you reply?

**Your second task**

The tow stops. Ground asks you to confirm you see the tow bar clear of Alpha before continuing. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, holding position, traffic under tow in sight. Second task: ANAC123, tow bar clear of Alpha, request hover taxi to the fuel pit. I would not move until I have visual confirmation that the tug, tow bar, and towed aircraft are clear of my route.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Tow bar is ground English that protects lives and metal.

Connected means not ready. Disconnected and clear means you may proceed — after clearance.

In the H130 academy you may tow less often than fixed-wing crews. You still operate on the same Brazilian aprons. You still hear the phrase. You still must hold for the tug.

Scan for the bar. Confirm it is gone. Then start. Then taxi.

That is professional ground discipline — and clean ICAO English.
