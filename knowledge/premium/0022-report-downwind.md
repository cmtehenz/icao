# Report Downwind

**Catalog ID:** 0022  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson focuses on one of the first reports in a standard traffic pattern.

When Tower instructs you to report downwind, they want to know when you are parallel to the landing runway, flying opposite to the landing direction.

This report helps ATC build the traffic sequence before base, final, and landing clearance.

For helicopter pilots, downwind reports are especially common during VFR operations at busy airports.

## 📖 Meaning

Report Downwind instructs the pilot to advise ATC when the aircraft is on the downwind leg of the traffic pattern for the assigned runway.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Report Downwind, the pilot should:

- join or continue the pattern as instructed
- report when established on the downwind leg
- specify left or right downwind when applicable
- include the runway and aircraft identification
- maintain pattern altitude unless otherwise instructed

Downwind reports are used:

- at towered airports during VFR arrivals
- when sequencing multiple aircraft in the circuit
- when Tower needs confirmation before issuing further instructions
- during helicopter operations that integrate with fixed-wing traffic

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers request Report Downwind to:

- establish traffic sequence early
- confirm pattern entry
- coordinate spacing before base and final
- manage mixed helicopter and fixed-wing traffic
- maintain runway and airspace awareness

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're inbound to Florianópolis International Airport in your H130.

Tower clears you to join the circuit:

Tower: ANAC123, enter left downwind runway one four, report downwind.

You read back and join at pattern altitude.

When abeam the threshold on the downwind leg, you report:

Pilot: Left downwind runway one four, ANAC123.

Tower now sequences you with other traffic and later clears you to continue the approach.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, report downwind runway one four.
2. Tower: ANAC123, enter right downwind runway two seven, report downwind.
3. Tower: ANAC123, report mid-field downwind.
4. Tower: ANAC123, report downwind, number three in sequence.
5. Tower: ANAC123, extend downwind, report abeam the tower.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Think of the traffic pattern like a racetrack around the airport.

Downwind is the long straight section where you run parallel to the runway, but in the opposite direction of landing.

When Tower says Report Downwind, they are asking:

"Tell me when you are on that straight section."

Why does it matter?

Because from downwind, ATC can see how the whole sequence will develop — who is first, who is second, and when to send you base or final.

Helicopter pilots must be especially disciplined here.

You may fly a slightly different path than fixed-wing traffic, but your radio reports must still be clear and standard.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Why does ATC ask pilots to report downwind during VFR operations?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Reporting downwind helps ATC confirm that the aircraft has entered the traffic pattern correctly. It allows the controller to sequence traffic and issue further instructions for base, final, and landing clearance.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting downwind before actually established on the leg.  
  ✔ Report when you are parallel to the runway on the downwind leg.

- ❌ Omitting left or right downwind when it matters.  
  ✔ Say left downwind or right downwind clearly.

- ❌ Forgetting the runway number.  
  ✔ Include the runway: Left downwind runway one four, ANAC123.

- ❌ Confusing downwind with base or final.  
  ✔ Downwind is parallel to the runway, opposite landing direction.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Report Downwind

**Pronunciation:** ri-PORT DOWN-wind

**Word Stress**

- Report → second syllable
- Downwind → first syllable on DOWN

Practice:

Report... Downwind...

Together:

Report Downwind.

Then inside a complete transmission:

Left downwind runway one four, ANAC123.

Keep downwind as one smooth word: DOWN-wind.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Downwind = you are **down** the runway direction, flying **wind** coming toward you (opposite landing).

When Tower wants that leg, they say Report Downwind.

Simple: parallel, opposite, report.

**Your first task**

What information should you include when you report downwind?

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Report Downwind is often the first checkpoint in a visual arrival.

It tells ATC you are in the pattern.

It starts the sequence.

From here, every next report — base, final, short final — builds the picture that keeps everyone separated.

Fly the pattern. Report at the right leg. Stay predictable on the radio.
