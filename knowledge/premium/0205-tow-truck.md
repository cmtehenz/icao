# Tow Truck

**Catalog ID:** 0205  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Tow Truck — the ground vehicle used to tow or push aircraft on the apron and, when authorized, on parts of the maneuvering area, and the ATC phraseology associated with tow operations.

Pilots encounter tow trucks as traffic to avoid, as assistance after a disabled aircraft event, or as the tug connected for pushback. Controllers may advise "tow truck ahead," approve tow movements, or coordinate holding for a tow in progress.

For H130 helicopter academy operations, you may rarely be towed, but you will share Congonhas, Guarulhos, Santos Dumont, Curitiba, and Florianópolis aprons with tow trucks serving other aircraft. Rotor clearance, downwash, and see-and-avoid around ground vehicles are part of safe taxi.

This lesson builds Part 2 communication for tow-truck advisories, requests, and readbacks.

## 📖 Meaning

Tow Truck is a ground support vehicle used to tow or push aircraft on the aerodrome; in radiotelephony it refers both to the vehicle itself and to ATC instructions or advisories related to tow movements and conflicts with taxiing aircraft.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Tow truck / Caminhão de reboque (tractor/tug).

Veículo de solo usado para rebocar ou empurrar aeronaves no aeródromo; na fraseologia, refere-se ao veículo e às instruções ou avisos do controle relacionados a movimentos de reboque e conflitos com aeronaves em táxi.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When tow truck activity affects you, the pilot should:

- acknowledge advisories such as tow truck ahead or give way to tow truck
- reduce taxi speed and maintain visual contact with the vehicle
- hold position if clearance is in doubt
- for disabled aircraft situations, coordinate with ATC before accepting tow assistance onto the maneuvering area
- for helicopters, protect rotor clearance and minimize downwash near the tow crew

Tow truck related communications include:

- taxi with caution, tow truck ahead
- give way to tow truck
- hold position, tow in progress
- request tow assistance (abnormal / disabled aircraft)
- tow truck clear of the taxiway

**Important:** A tow moving an aircraft may have limited ability to stop or maneuver quickly. Treat tow combinations as less flexible than a taxiing aircraft under its own power. When in doubt, hold and advise ATC.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers mention tow trucks to:

- prevent collisions between taxiing aircraft and ground vehicles
- sequence tow movements across active taxi routes
- protect maintenance and disabled-aircraft recovery operations
- attach caution or give-way instructions to taxi clearances
- keep pilots aware of non-radio or limited-radio ground traffic

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are taxiing to the ramp at Congonhas in the H130.

Ground: ANAC123, taxi to the ramp via Alpha, taxi with caution, tow truck ahead on Alpha.

You slow, see the tow truck pulling a light airplane toward a hangar, and hold short until the path is clear or ATC amends the route.

You: ANAC123, holding on Alpha, tow truck ahead.

Ground: ANAC123, hold position, tow truck will clear, then continue to the ramp.

When the tow is clear, you continue. No rush. No squeeze past the tug.

If you ever needed tow assistance after a ground incident, you would advise ATC of the situation and request coordination — not drag the helicopter across the maneuvering area without approval.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Alpha, taxi with caution, tow truck ahead.
2. Ground: ANAC123, give way to tow truck on your right.
3. Ground: ANAC123, hold position, tow in progress on Bravo.
4. Ground: ANAC123, tow truck clear, continue taxi to the ramp.
5. Ground: ANAC123, roger request for tow assistance, expect vehicle, hold position.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, taxi via Alpha, taxi with caution, tow truck ahead.
2. ANAC123, giving way to tow truck on the right.
3. ANAC123, holding position, tow in progress on Bravo.
4. ANAC123, tow truck clear, continuing taxi to the ramp.
5. ANAC123, holding position, expecting tow assistance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Tow trucks do not fly, but they create real traffic conflicts.

On the frequency, treat a tow advisory like any other hazard call: hear it, read it back, see it, then move.

In the H130, the danger is often clearance and downwash. A tow crew on foot beside a tug is vulnerable. If you must hover-taxi near them, rethink the plan — hold or request another route.

Do not pass a tow combination in a tight alley because "there is room on the chart." Charts do not measure your rotor disk against a moving fuselage.

If ATC says hold for tow in progress, hold. The tow may be committed to a path it cannot easily reverse.

Situational awareness on Brazilian aprons includes vehicles, not only aircraft.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC tells you to taxi with caution, tow truck ahead. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would read back the caution, reduce taxi speed, and locate the tow truck visually. I would maintain safe clearance and be prepared to hold if the path is blocked. If I could not continue safely, I would stop and advise ATC that I am holding due to the tow truck, then wait for further instructions or for the vehicle to clear.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing at normal speed after a tow truck caution.  
  ✔ Slow down, acquire visual contact, hold if needed.

- ❌ Squeezing past a tow and aircraft combination in a narrow taxi lane.  
  ✔ Hold and request alternate routing if clearance is doubtful.

- ❌ Ignoring tow advisories because "it is only a vehicle."  
  ✔ Tow combinations are traffic; treat them as conflict traffic.

- ❌ For helicopters, hover-taxiing close enough to blast the tow crew.  
  ✔ Hold or reroute; protect personnel from downwash.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

At Florianópolis, Ground says: "ANAC123, taxi to the ramp via Charlie, give way to tow truck on your left."

**Your first task**

Give the readback.

**Your second task**

The tow truck stops in a position that blocks safe rotor clearance. What do you transmit?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, taxi to the ramp via Charlie, giving way to tow truck on the left. Second task: ANAC123, holding on Charlie, unable to pass tow truck, request instructions. I would remain clear until ATC provides an alternate route or the tow truck clears.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Tow trucks are part of the airport traffic picture.

When ATC names one, make it part of your scan and your readback.

Hold when clearance is doubtful. Protect people from rotor wash. Never force a pass in a tight alley.

In H130 Brazilian operations, that discipline keeps apron movements uneventful.

See the tug. Respect the tow. Continue only when clear.
