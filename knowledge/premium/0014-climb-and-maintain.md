# Climb and Maintain

**Catalog ID:** 0014  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the most common ATC climb instructions issued after departure.

Many new pilots focus only on the altitude.

Professional pilots understand that this clearance contains two separate instructions:

Begin the climb immediately.  
Level off exactly at the assigned altitude.

Accuracy is essential. Stopping early or climbing above the assigned altitude can create a serious loss of separation.

## 📖 Meaning

Climb and Maintain instructs the pilot to climb immediately and maintain the assigned altitude until receiving further ATC instructions.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Suba e mantenha.

Inicie a subida imediatamente e nivele na altitude autorizada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Climb and Maintain, the pilot must:

- begin climbing without unnecessary delay
- stop exactly at the assigned altitude
- maintain that altitude until another clearance is issued
- continue complying with assigned headings and speeds

This instruction changes only the altitude.

It does not cancel:

- assigned heading
- radar vectors
- navigation instructions
- speed restrictions

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Climb and Maintain to:

- provide vertical separation
- clear aircraft above terrain
- organize departure traffic
- sequence arrivals and departures
- transition aircraft to en-route altitudes
- reduce controller workload

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're departing Florianópolis Airport on an IFR flight.

After takeoff, Departure contacts you:

Departure: ANAC123, climb and maintain four thousand feet.

You immediately begin climbing.

Your current heading remains unchanged.

At 4,000 feet, you smoothly level off.

A few minutes later:

Departure: ANAC123, climb and maintain seven thousand feet.

You continue the climb.

Throughout the departure, every altitude change is issued by ATC.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Departure: ANAC123, climb and maintain three thousand feet.
2. Departure: ANAC123, climb and maintain five thousand feet.
3. Departure: ANAC123, climb and maintain flight level one eight zero.
4. Departure: ANAC123, climb and maintain four thousand, fly heading zero niner zero.
5. Departure: ANAC123, climb and maintain six thousand, expect higher in ten minutes.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Climbing to three thousand, ANAC123.
2. Climb and maintain five thousand, ANAC123.
3. Flight level one eight zero, ANAC123.
4. Climb and maintain four thousand, heading zero niner zero, ANAC123.
5. Climbing six thousand, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine you're taking an elevator to the eighth floor.

You don't stop at the sixth floor because it looks close enough.

And you certainly don't continue to the tenth floor.

You stop exactly where you were instructed.

Altitude assignments work exactly the same way.

When ATC says:

Climb and maintain four thousand.

Your mission isn't simply to climb.

Your mission is to stop precisely at 4,000 feet.

Professional pilots know that altitude accuracy is one of the foundations of safe IFR flying.

Even a deviation of a few hundred feet can reduce the separation ATC is trying to maintain.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Why is it important to maintain the assigned altitude accurately?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Maintaining the assigned altitude ensures safe separation from other aircraft and allows ATC to manage traffic efficiently. Climbing above or below the assigned altitude may create a conflict with nearby traffic.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing the climb above the assigned altitude.  
  ✔ Level off exactly at the cleared altitude.

- ❌ Delaying the climb unnecessarily.  
  ✔ Begin climbing promptly after receiving the clearance.

- ❌ Thinking the heading also changes.  
  ✔ The instruction changes only the altitude unless ATC says otherwise.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Climb and maintain four thousand, ANAC123.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Climb and Maintain

**Pronunciation:** KLAIM and main-TAIN

**Word Stress**

- Climb → strong
- Maintain → stress the second syllable

Practice:

Climb... Maintain...

Together:

Climb and Maintain.

Now practice in a complete transmission:

Climb and maintain four thousand, ANAC123.

Speak the altitude clearly and avoid rushing the numbers.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Imagine filling a glass with water.

You pour until the water reaches a marked line.

You don't stop below it.

You don't overflow it.

The assigned altitude is that line.

Climb to it.

Maintain it.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

Departure: ANAC123, climb and maintain four thousand feet.

Pilot: _______________________

Departure: ANAC123, maintain heading zero niner zero.

**Your first task**

Read back both instructions exactly as a professional pilot.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

If ATC instructs you to "Climb and Maintain four thousand feet," should you continue climbing once you reach 4,000 feet? Explain why.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

No. I should level off at four thousand feet because ATC instructed me to maintain that altitude. I must remain at four thousand until receiving another altitude clearance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Today's lesson introduces one of the most fundamental principles of IFR flying:

An altitude clearance has two parts.

Climb to the assigned altitude.

Maintain it accurately.

Remember this sequence:

Takeoff → Climb and Maintain → Level Off → Await the Next Clearance

Precision is what separates professional pilots from average pilots.

In aviation, every hundred feet matters.
