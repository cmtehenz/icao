# APU Fire

**Catalog ID:** 0299  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers APU Fire — fire warning or confirmed fire associated with the auxiliary power unit, requiring immediate checklist actions, possible APU shutdown and fire extinguishing, and clear Mayday communication to ATC. The H130 may have limited or no APU compared with transport jets — still teach ICAO exam English for APU fire, then transfer the same urgency structure to any auxiliary-power or compartment fire scenario you may face.

APU fire is Critical because fire on or near the airframe demands immediate action and unambiguous radio calls.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for APU fire, then map the structure to helicopter fire emergencies under DECEA operations.

Understanding APU fire in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

APU Fire is a fire warning or confirmed fire involving the auxiliary power unit, requiring immediate crew actions per the fire checklist — typically APU shutdown, fire agent discharge if available, and prompt Mayday notification to ATC of the nature of the emergency and intentions for immediate landing or evacuation as required.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Fogo no APU / APU Fire.

Alerta de fogo ou fogo confirmado envolvendo a unidade auxiliar de potência (APU), exigindo ações imediatas da tripulação conforme o checklist de fogo — tipicamente desligamento do APU, descarga do agente extintor se disponível, e notificação Mayday rápida ao ATC sobre a natureza da emergência e as intenções de pouso imediato ou evacuação conforme necessário.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When an APU fire occurs, the crew should:

- accomplish the APU fire checklist immediately — shut down APU, discharge agent as published
- maintain aircraft control and prepare for immediate landing if airborne
- declare Mayday without delay
- report APU fire, actions taken, and intentions (immediate return, land, evacuate)
- request emergency services and priority landing
- for H130: transfer the same Mayday structure to auxiliary equipment or compartment fire even if no APU is installed

APU fire reporting commonly includes:

- APU fire / APU fire warning
- APU shut down / fire agent discharged
- request immediate priority landing / emergency services
- souls and fuel when asked
- advise if smoke, evacuation, or runway blocked after landing

**Important:** On the ICAO speaking test, use "APU fire" clearly. On the H130, APU may be limited or absent — retain exam vocabulary and transfer Critical fire-reporting logic to any similar emergency.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to APU fire reports to:

- provide immediate priority landing clearance
- alert airport emergency services for fire and rescue
- keep traffic clear of an aircraft with fire on board
- prepare for possible runway blockage or evacuation after landing
- coordinate ground response for smoke or residual fire risk

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Exam-style scenario: on the ground or shortly after departure, APU fire warning illuminates. Checklist completed — APU shut down, agent discharged. Warning persists or smoke is present.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Tower, ANAC123, APU fire, request immediate landing, emergency services.

If already airborne and returning:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Departure, ANAC123, APU fire, returning immediately, request priority vectors and emergency services.

For H130 reality, use the same Mayday skeleton for any confirmed fire involving auxiliary equipment or a compartment — name the fire, ask for landing and services now.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger Mayday APU fire, cleared to land any runway, emergency services rolling.
2. Departure: ANAC123, turn left heading two seven zero, descend three thousand, priority return approved.
3. Approach: ANAC123, runway one seven, wind calm, emergency services standing by.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.
5. Tower: ANAC123, after landing vacate if able, advise if evacuating on the runway.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

APU fire is not a discussion topic — it is a Mayday topic.

Fly or secure the aircraft. Kill the APU per checklist. Discharge agent if published. Then say Mayday, APU fire, request immediate landing and emergency services.

Do not downgrade fire language to "APU problem" or "small smoke." If the checklist is APU fire, the radio call is APU fire.

In the H130, you may never see a classic APU fire warning. That does not remove the exam requirement. Learn the phrase. Transfer the Critical logic to any fire that threatens the airframe.

Brazilian examiners expect Mayday discipline and precise fire vocabulary. Give them both.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You have an APU fire warning. What do you do, and what do you say to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would immediately apply the APU fire checklist — shut down the APU and discharge the fire agent as published — while maintaining control of the aircraft. I would declare Mayday, report APU fire, and request immediate priority landing with emergency services. I would provide souls and fuel when asked and advise if smoke continues or evacuation is required after landing. On a helicopter without a typical APU, I would use the same Mayday structure for an auxiliary or compartment fire.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Using Pan Pan for a confirmed APU fire.  
  ✔ Fire on the aircraft is Mayday unless procedure and situation clearly say otherwise.

- ❌ Saying only "fire" without locating APU.  
  ✔ Prefer "APU fire" so ATC and rescue know the zone.

- ❌ Delaying the radio call until after lengthy troubleshooting.  
  ✔ Checklist and Mayday in parallel — do not wait to "see if it goes out."

- ❌ Skipping APU fire study because H130 APU is limited.  
  ✔ Learn exam English; transfer fire Mayday logic to the helicopter.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, APU Fire, Request Immediate Landing

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, AY-PEE-YOO FY-er, reh-KWEST ih-MEE-dee-ut LAND-ing

**Word Stress**

- APU → AY-PEE-YOO (spell each letter clearly)
- Fire → FY-er
- Immediate → ih-MEE-dee-ut
- Landing → LAND-ing

Practice:

APU fire... request immediate landing, emergency services.

Together:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, APU fire, request immediate landing, emergency services.
