# Unruly Passenger

**Catalog ID:** 0332  
**Category:** Passenger  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Unruly Passenger — a passenger whose behavior threatens safety, order, or crew ability to operate the flight, including aggression, refusal to follow safety instructions, or interference with the crew.

Unruly Passenger is High priority because behavior problems can escalate into assault, cockpit interference, or unsafe cabin conditions. ICAO exams expect clear English: unruly passenger, security problem, request assistance on landing, and priority if the situation threatens safety.

For H130 operations, the cabin is small and there is usually no cabin crew. An unruly passenger is closer to the controls. Early firm management and early ATC notification matter more, not less.

Understanding how to communicate an unruly passenger event in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Unruly Passenger describes a passenger who fails to comply with safety instructions or behaves in a disruptive, aggressive, or threatening manner that may compromise flight safety and require crew action and ATC coordination for assistance on arrival.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Passageiro indisciplinado / Unruly Passenger.

Passageiro que não cumpre instruções de segurança ou se comporta de forma disruptiva, agressiva ou ameaçadora, podendo comprometer a segurança do voo e exigir ação da tripulação e coordenação com o controle para assistência na chegada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When dealing with an unruly passenger, the crew should:

- maintain aircraft control and do not let the conflict enter the flight controls area
- use clear verbal commands and available passenger help to de-escalate
- restrain only as a last resort within legal and operator policy limits
- advise ATC of an unruly passenger or security problem on board
- request police or security assistance on landing; request priority if safety is threatened

Unruly Passenger communication commonly includes:

- unruly passenger on board, request security on arrival
- passenger interfering with crew, request priority landing
- security problem on board
- passenger restrained, request police assistance

**Important:** On the H130, never leave the controls to fight a passenger in single-pilot flight. Divert early if behavior threatens the cockpit. Brazilian operators and police can meet helicopters on the apron — ask ATC for security assistance clearly.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to unruly passenger reports to:

- alert airport security and police
- provide priority handling when the pilot reports a safety threat
- coordinate a secure parking position or isolated stand if required
- request souls on board and nature of the security problem
- keep other traffic clear if an emergency landing is needed

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are approaching Guarulhos in the H130 with four passengers.

One passenger becomes aggressive, refuses to remain seated, and tries to move toward the front.

You keep control, order the passenger to sit, and have another passenger block the aisle area.

You transmit:

Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, unruly passenger on board, request priority landing and security assistance.

Approach gives you priority. Police meet the aircraft after landing. The situation never reached the controls because you acted early on frequency and in the cabin.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say nature of problem and souls on board.
2. Approach: ANAC123, priority approach, security alerted.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, police will meet you on the apron.
4. Approach: ANAC123, confirm passenger under control.
5. Ground: ANAC123, taxi to isolated stand, security standing by.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, unruly passenger on board, request priority landing and security assistance.
2. ANAC123, security problem on board, passenger aggressive, request police on arrival.
3. ANAC123, unruly passenger restrained, request security meet aircraft.
4. ANAC123, passenger interfering with crew, request immediate landing.
5. ANAC123, souls on board five, unruly passenger, security required.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Unruly is the standard ICAO exam word. Use it.

Source materials may say passenger fighting, misbehavior, or misconduct. On frequency, unruly passenger or security problem is cleaner.

Your goal is not to win an argument. Your goal is a safe aircraft and a secure landing.

In airliners, cabin crew manage the cabin. In the H130, proximity is the danger. One meter from an aggressive passenger to the cyclic is too close. Divert sooner than pride allows.

Tell ATC what you need: security, police, priority. Do not narrate every insult on frequency.

Firm. Short. Early.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What should you tell ATC when a passenger becomes aggressive and may threaten flight safety?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare Pan Pan if safety is threatened, state that there is an unruly passenger or security problem on board, and request priority landing and security or police assistance on arrival. I would keep control of the aircraft and prevent the passenger from interfering with the flight controls.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "passenger crazy" or slang.  
  ✔ Say unruly passenger or security problem on board.

- ❌ Waiting until the passenger reaches the controls before calling ATC.  
  ✔ Notify early and request security assistance.

- ❌ Leaving the controls to restrain the passenger in single-pilot ops.  
  ✔ Fly first; use other passengers if restraint is unavoidable and legal.

- ❌ Forgetting to request police on arrival.  
  ✔ Always request security or police meet the aircraft when behavior is aggressive.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

A passenger stands up, shouts, and moves toward the front of the H130 cabin. You are fifteen minutes from landing.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

What is your primary operational priority while others help restrain him?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, unruly passenger on board, request priority landing and security assistance. Second task: Maintain control of the helicopter and keep the passenger away from the flight controls; landing safely with police waiting is the goal.

Name it. Protect the controls. Request security.
