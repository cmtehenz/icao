# Generator Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0057  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers generator failure — a critical electrical systems event.

Generator Failure means the aircraft's primary electrical generator has stopped producing power — the aircraft must operate on battery or alternate power sources.

In the H130, the generator supplies electrical power for avionics, instruments, and communication equipment during flight.

Without generator output, battery endurance becomes your limiting factor.

You must aviate first, manage electrical load, then communicate with the correct urgency call.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Generator Failure is the loss of generator output, requiring operation on battery power with limited electrical endurance.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha do gerador.

Perda da produção de energia pelo gerador, exigindo operação com bateria e tempo elétrico limitado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Generator Failure occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the first priority
- identify the generator failure warning or annunciation
- switch to battery power and reduce non-essential load
- monitor battery voltage and remaining endurance
- declare Pan Pan or Mayday based on severity

Generator Failure reporting commonly includes:

- generator failure or generator out
- operating on battery power
- request vectors to nearest suitable airport
- souls on board and fuel remaining when requested

**Important:** Generator failure with adequate battery for a safe landing may warrant Pan Pan. Critically low battery or loss of essential avionics may require Mayday. Aviate first — then communicate clearly with standard phraseology.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Generator Failure declarations to:

- clear traffic from the aircraft's route
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable airport
- coordinate priority landing if battery endurance is critical
- request souls on board and fuel remaining
- alert airport authorities if distress is declared

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on a cross-country flight at three thousand feet in your H130 from Curitiba toward Londrina.

The generator failure light illuminates and electrical bus voltage drops.

You reduce non-essential load and maintain stable flight.

When stable, you transmit:

Pan Pan Pan Pan, Curitiba Approach, ANAC123, generator failure, operating on battery power, request vectors to Londrina.

Approach acknowledges and provides direct routing.

You land at Londrina before battery endurance becomes critical.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say battery endurance and souls on board.
2. Approach: ANAC123, turn left heading two four zero, descend two thousand five hundred, Londrina two zero miles.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway one eight cleared to land, wind calm.
4. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services notified, report final.
5. Departure: ANAC123, say nature of electrical problem and intentions.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Generator failure and alternator failure feel similar on the radio.

Both mean you are losing electrical endurance.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Aviate. Load-shed. Communicate.

Fly the aircraft first.

Turn off everything non-essential.

Know your battery time from the flight manual.

Then declare your urgency.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "gerador caiu" or "perdeu o gerador" on frequency.

Use generator failure or generator out — ICAO standard terms.

Do not confuse generator failure with total electrical failure.

Generator out on battery — Pan Pan.

Total electrical loss with no instruments — Mayday.

Train the assessment reflex so your radio call comes from a stable aircraft with a plan.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between generator failure and total electrical failure?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Generator failure means the generator has stopped producing power, but the aircraft may still operate on battery with limited endurance. Total electrical failure means all electrical power is lost, including battery, which is more serious. For generator failure, I would declare Pan Pan, report operating on battery power, and request vectors to the nearest suitable airport. I would upgrade to Mayday if battery endurance becomes critical or essential systems fail.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Using "gerador queimou" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use generator failure or generator out.

- ❌ Not distinguishing generator failure from total electrical failure.  
  ✔ Generator out on battery is urgency; total loss may be distress.

- ❌ Forgetting to report operating on battery power.  
  ✔ State battery operation — ATC needs to know your endurance.

- ❌ Delaying the call until radios fail.  
  ✔ Declare early while communication is still reliable.
