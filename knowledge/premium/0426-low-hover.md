# Low Hover

**Catalog ID:** 0426  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Low Hover — holding the helicopter in a stationary hover close to the surface, typically within ground effect, for landing, takeoff, or operational positioning.

Low hover is fundamental H130 work: helipad arrivals in São Paulo and Rio, offshore deck positioning, HEMS scene work, and confined-area operations across Brazilian terrain.

You must describe hover height, intentions, and hazards in clear ICAO English for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Low Hover is a helicopter flight condition in which the aircraft remains essentially stationary relative to the ground at a low height above the surface, usually within ground effect, while the pilot maintains precise control of height, heading, and position.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pairamento baixo / low hover.

Condição de voo de helicóptero em que a aeronave permanece essencialmente estacionária em relação ao solo a baixa altura, geralmente dentro do efeito solo, enquanto o piloto mantém controle preciso de altura, proa e posição.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When establishing or maintaining a low hover, the pilot should:

- confirm power margin and wind before committing to the hover
- set a clear hover height reference and hold it
- scan for dust, debris, wires, and bystanders
- advise ATC or ground of hover intentions when required
- transition smoothly to landing or departure without uncommanded drift

Low hover reporting commonly includes:

- established low hover
- hovering for landing / positioning
- unable to maintain hover, going around
- lifting from hover, request departure

**Important:** Low hover is not a parking place. Power, wind, and surface conditions change. If the hover becomes unstable or power-limited, depart or land — do not fight a deteriorating hover.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers work with low hover reports to:

- understand helicopter intentions near the field or helipad
- sequence fixed-wing traffic around hovering helicopters
- coordinate ground vehicles and personnel near the hover area
- support emergency or HEMS positioning near runways
- maintain situational awareness of aircraft that are airborne but not translating

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to a hospital helipad in your H130. Wind is light. You plan a low hover to confirm pad clear before touchdown.

On short final you establish a stable low hover over the pad threshold.

You transmit when required:

Approaching, established low hover, pad clear, landing.

A vehicle enters the pad. You hold the hover and advise:

Holding low hover, vehicle on pad, remaining clear.

When the pad is clear, you land from the hover.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, report established in low hover, pad traffic advised.
2. Tower: ANAC123, hold low hover, vehicle crossing, expect landing shortly.
3. Tower: ANAC123, pad clear, cleared to land from hover.
4. Tower: ANAC123, unable landing, remain in low hover or depart as required.
5. Tower: ANAC123, lift from hover, cleared departure northbound.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, wilco, will report established low hover.
2. ANAC123, holding low hover, standing by for pad clear.
3. ANAC123, pad clear, landing from hover.
4. ANAC123, remaining in low hover — or departing as required.
5. ANAC123, lifting from hover, departure northbound.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Low hover is where precision lives.

Height, heading, and position — all three. Lose one and the hover becomes a problem.

In the H130, ground effect helps at low hover, but dust, slope, and recirculation can steal that margin. Brief the surface. Brief the wind. Brief the escape path.

Part 3 loves: what do you do if power is insufficient to hold the hover? Answer: do not settle into an unrecoverable sink. Depart or land on a clear path. Communicate: unable to maintain hover, going around.

Brazilian city helipads are tight. Low hover buys you a look — not a long conversation. Decide quickly.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You establish a low hover over a helipad and see personnel still on the pad. What do you say and do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain a stable low hover clear of the personnel and transmit: holding low hover, personnel on pad, remaining clear. I would not land until the pad is clear. If the hover becomes unstable or fuel or power becomes a concern, I would depart and request another approach when the pad is reported clear.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Landing from an unstable low hover "because we were almost down."  
  ✔ Stabilize first. If unstable, go around.

- ❌ Not advising ATC when holding hover blocks the approach path.  
  ✔ Report holding low hover so traffic can be sequenced.

- ❌ Ignoring dust or recirculation that reduces visual references.  
  ✔ Depart early if visual cues degrade.

- ❌ Using long company chatter on tower frequency during hover.  
  ✔ Keep ATC calls short; use company frequency for detail.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Holding Low Hover, Pad Not Clear

**Pronunciation:** HOLD-ing LOH HUV-er, PAD not KLEER

**Word Stress**

- Hover → HUV-er (stress on first)
- Holding → HOLD-ing
- Clear → KLEER

Holding low hover... pad not clear... remaining clear.

Together:

Holding low hover, personnel on pad, remaining clear.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**HOVER** — **H**old height and heading, **O**bserve surface and wind, **V**erify power margin, **E**scape if unstable, **R**eport intentions briefly.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Inbound H130 to a city helipad. You establish a low hover. A vehicle enters the pad.

**Your first task**

What do you say to Tower?

**Your second task**

Pad clears. You are stable. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, holding low hover, vehicle on pad, remaining clear. Second task: ANAC123, pad clear, landing from hover — or, if clearance is required: ANAC123, pad clear, request landing from hover.
