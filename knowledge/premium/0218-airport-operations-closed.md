# Airport Operations Closed

**Catalog ID:** 0218  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Airport Operations Closed — the condition or advisory that the aerodrome, or a significant part of its operations, is not available for normal aircraft movements.

"Airport operations closed" is stronger than a single taxiway WIP notice. It may mean the airport is closed, the runway complex is unavailable, night operations have ended, or a temporary total stop of movements is in force. Pilots must not treat it as a soft suggestion.

For H130 helicopter training academy flights into Congonhas, Guarulhos, Santos Dumont, Curitiba, and Florianópolis, closure can follow weather below minima, an incident, VIP restriction, or published operating hours. Helicopters sometimes have more flexibility with alternate pads — but only with clearance and a legal destination. Closed means closed until ATC or the authority says otherwise.

This lesson builds Part 2 proficiency in understanding closure advisories, requesting intentions, and communicating diversion or delay plans clearly.

## 📖 Meaning

Airport Operations Closed means the aerodrome or its normal aircraft operations are unavailable for landing, takeoff, or ground movement as published or as advised by ATC/airport authority, requiring pilots to hold, divert, delay, or await reopening.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Airport operations closed / Operações do aeroporto encerradas / aeródromo fechado.

Significa que o aeródromo ou suas operações normais de aeronaves estão indisponíveis para pouso, decolagem ou movimento de solo conforme publicado ou avisado pelo controle/autoridade aeroportuária, exigindo espera, desvio, atraso ou aguardo da reabertura.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When airport operations are closed, the pilot should:

- acknowledge the advisory and stop any assumption of arrival or departure
- request expected reopening time if operationally useful
- plan fuel, alternate, and passenger implications immediately
- do not enter closed movement areas "just to park"
- coordinate with ATC for holding, diversion, or return to base
- for helicopters, confirm whether a specific helipad remains available — do not assume exception

Airport operations closed may result from:

- published operating hours ending
- weather or low visibility procedures stopping movements
- incident, wreckage, or emergency on the runway
- security or VIP temporary closure
- major infrastructure failure (lighting, power, RFF category)

**Important:** A closed airport is not a negotiation on the tower frequency. If you need an exception for an emergency, declare it. Training flights divert or delay. In the H130, having a nearby alternate heliport does not authorize landing on a closed runway without clearance.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers advise airport operations closed to:

- stop arrivals and departures when the aerodrome cannot accept traffic
- protect crews from planning into an unavailable destination
- coordinate holding stacks, diversions, and ground stops
- align radio messages with NOTAMs and airport authority decisions
- reduce frequency congestion from repeated individual refusals

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Florianópolis in the H130 after a cross-country training leg. Weather has deteriorated and an incident has stopped movements.

Approach: ANAC123, Florianópolis airport operations closed, expect further delay, advise intentions.

You acknowledge. You check fuel and alternates. You do not continue the approach hoping for a last-minute opening.

You reply with a clear intention: divert to Curitiba or hold only if fuel and ATC allow. The closed status forced an early, professional decision.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, airport operations closed, advise intentions.
2. Tower: ANAC123, negative, airport operations closed, remain clear of the control zone if unable to hold.
3. Ground: ANAC123, airport operations closed until further notice, expect no departure.
4. Approach: ANAC123, Florianópolis closed due incident, divert approved to Curitiba as requested.
5. Tower: ANAC123, airport reopening in three zero minutes, expect delay.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, airport operations closed, standing by with intentions.
2. ANAC123, roger, airport closed, remaining clear, request diversion.
3. ANAC123, airport operations closed, cancelling departure, request taxi to parking.
4. ANAC123, diverting to Curitiba, request vectors / flight plan change as required.
5. ANAC123, reopening in three zero minutes copied, request hold.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Closed is a hard word. Treat it as hard.

Students sometimes hear "closed" and still ask for a visual approach "to take a look." That wastes frequency time and reveals weak decision-making.

When operations are closed, your job is intentions: hold, divert, return, or shut down plans for departure. Say it cleanly. Give fuel state if relevant. Give souls on board if the situation is abnormal.

Brazilian coastal weather and busy terminals like Congonhas can flip from open to restricted quickly. Build alternates into every academy cross-country. The H130's range is finite — closure plus indecision equals fuel pressure.

If ATC says reopening in thirty minutes, that is information, not a clearance to arrive in twenty-nine. Plan margins. Fuel, alternate, and passenger plan come before hope.

When the field closes on a training day, the professional move is an early divert call — not a long silence while you wait for a miracle reopen.

Professional pilots respect closed. Amateurs argue with it.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You are inbound and ATC advises airport operations closed. What do you communicate and what do you decide?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would acknowledge that airport operations are closed and immediately review fuel, alternate aerodromes, and whether holding is realistic. I would advise ATC of my intentions clearly — for example request diversion to the alternate, or request holding only if fuel allows and a reopening time is known. I would not continue an approach into a closed airport for training purposes. If the situation became urgent due to fuel, I would declare the appropriate emergency and request priority assistance.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing the approach after hearing the airport is closed "to see the runway."  
  ✔ Acknowledge closure and state hold or divert intentions.

- ❌ Assuming helicopters are exempt from aerodrome closure.  
  ✔ Confirm any helipad exception explicitly; do not self-exempt.

- ❌ Delaying the diversion decision until fuel is critical.  
  ✔ Decide early while options and fuel margins still exist.

- ❌ Asking repeatedly for landing clearance instead of stating intentions.  
  ✔ One clear intention update beats multiple hopeful requests.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are twenty miles from Florianópolis in the H130 with fuel for forty minutes plus alternate to Curitiba.

**Your first task**

Approach says: "ANAC123, Florianópolis airport operations closed due incident, advise intentions." What do you say?

**Your second task**

Approach then offers holding for a possible reopening in one hour. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, request diversion to Curitiba. Second task: ANAC123, negative holding, fuel insufficient for one hour hold, continuing diversion to Curitiba — to refuse an unsafe hold and keep the plan matched to fuel reality. I would not accept holding that consumes the alternate reserve for a training flight.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Airport operations closed ends the arrival fantasy.

Acknowledge. Decide. Communicate intentions.

Hold only with fuel. Divert while options are easy. Never assume the H130 gets a free pass onto a closed field.

That discipline protects the academy schedule less than it protects the crew — and that is the right priority.
