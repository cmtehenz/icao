# Escape Slide Deployed

**Catalog ID:** 0287  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Escape Slide Deployed — the inflation and use of an aircraft escape slide for emergency evacuation, or an inadvertent slide deployment on the ground that creates a hazard and requires ATC and ground coordination. The H130 does not use airline-style escape slides; this lesson still teaches Medium-priority ICAO exam English and maps the same evacuation-communication logic to helicopter egress and ground hazards.

Escape slide deployed appears in ICAO speaking and radiotelephony scenarios for transport aircraft. Examiners expect you to report the event, state whether evacuation is in progress, and request emergency services as required.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master the standard phraseology for the exam, then transfer the structure to any evacuation or ground-obstruction report on the H130.

Understanding escape slide deployed in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Escape Slide Deployed means an aircraft escape slide has inflated — either intentionally for emergency evacuation or inadvertently on the ground — creating an immediate need to report the condition to ATC, protect persons and vehicles near the aircraft, and coordinate evacuation or slide disarming and recovery with emergency and ground services.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Escorrega de emergência acionado / Escape Slide Deployed.

Significa que um escorrega de evacuação da aeronave foi inflado — intencionalmente para evacuação de emergência ou inadvertidamente no solo — criando necessidade imediata de informar o ATC, proteger pessoas e veículos próximos à aeronave e coordenar a evacuação ou o desarmamento e a recuperação do escorrega com os serviços de emergência e de solo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When an escape slide is deployed, the crew should:

- determine whether deployment is intentional (evacuation) or inadvertent (ground hazard)
- for evacuation: command evacuate, declare as required, request firefighters
- for inadvertent deployment: stop movement if taxiing, advise ATC immediately
- request emergency services and keep the area clear of vehicles and persons
- report which door or side is affected when known
- for H130 training: retain exam vocabulary; transfer clear "evacuation / ground hazard" radio logic to helicopter egress

Escape slide reporting commonly includes:

- escape slide deployed / inadvertent slide deployment
- evacuating / not evacuating
- door or side affected
- request firefighters / remain clear
- souls and fuel when asked

**Important:** An inflated slide blocks taxiways and can injure ground personnel. Report it at once. Do not try to "fix it quietly" while vehicles approach the aircraft.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to escape slide reports to:

- stop conflicting ground traffic near the aircraft
- alert RFF for evacuation or slide recovery
- protect the runway or taxiway until the area is safe
- pass door/side information to emergency services
- coordinate towing and slide disarming with the operator

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Exam-style scenario: after a rejected take-off with smoke, you stop on the runway and order evacuation. Slides deploy.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Tower, ANAC123, evacuating on the runway, escape slides deployed, request firefighters.

Tower holds all traffic and confirms RFF rolling.

Alternate scenario: during pushback an inadvertent slide deploys at the gate.

You report:

ANAC123, inadvertent escape slide deployment, left forward door, request remain clear, request ground assistance.

For H130 reality, you will not deploy slides — but you will still report evacuation in progress or a ground hazard that blocks movement with the same clarity.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger evacuation, firefighters rolling, remain on frequency.
2. Tower: ANAC123, confirm escape slides deployed, say which side.
3. Ground: ANAC123, all traffic stopped, emergency services approaching from behind.
4. Ground: ANAC123, inadvertent slide noted, remain in position, assistance on the way.
5. Tower: ANAC123, say souls on board and if evacuation complete.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, wilco, remaining on frequency, firefighters rolling.
2. ANAC123, escape slides deployed both sides, evacuating.
3. ANAC123, roger, emergency services approaching from behind.
4. ANAC123, remaining in position, inadvertent slide left forward door.
5. ANAC123, souls eight, evacuation in progress / evacuation complete.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Escape slide deployed is two different problems with one radio rule: say what happened and what you are doing.

Intentional deployment means evacuation. Declare. Request firefighters. Report when complete.

Inadvertent deployment means a ground hazard. Stop if moving. Tell ATC which door. Keep people clear.

Do not mix the messages. "Slide deployed" alone is incomplete. Add evacuating or inadvertent — and the side if you know it.

For the ICAO exam, speak the airline scenario cleanly. For the H130, never invent slides that do not exist — transfer the communication pattern to real egress and ground-obstruction calls.

Clear. Specific. Immediate.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

After landing with a cabin fire indication you evacuate and the escape slides deploy. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare the emergency if not already declared, report that we are evacuating, state that escape slides are deployed, and request firefighters. I would remain on frequency, provide souls on board when asked, and advise when evacuation is complete or if anyone is injured.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "slide open" without evacuating or inadvertent.  
  ✔ State escape slide deployed and whether you are evacuating.

- ❌ Continuing to taxi with an inadvertent slide inflated.  
  ✔ Stop and advise ATC immediately; protect ground personnel.

- ❌ Not requesting firefighters during an evacuation.  
  ✔ Request emergency services as part of the first clear call.

- ❌ Treating the phrase as irrelevant to helicopters and skipping the lesson.  
  ✔ Learn exam English; transfer evacuation radio logic to the H130.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Smoke after landing. You stop on the runway and evacuate. Slides deploy.

**Your first task**

What is your initial call to Tower?

**Your second task**

Evacuation is complete. No fire visible. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday Mayday Mayday, Tower, ANAC123, evacuating on the runway, escape slides deployed, request firefighters. Second task: ANAC123, evacuation complete, no fire visible, remaining on runway. I would stay on frequency until RFF and ATC release the situation.
