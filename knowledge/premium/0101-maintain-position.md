# Maintain Position

**Catalog ID:** 0101  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers maintain position — the ATC instruction to hold the aircraft's current position on the ground and not move.

Maintain Position is a critical runway safety instruction. Misunderstanding or mishearing it can lead to runway incursion — one of the most dangerous events in aviation.

In the H130, maintain position is issued at busy airports like Congonhas, Curitiba, and Florianópolis when landing or departing traffic requires a hold on the ground.

You must readback maintain position with your callsign, stop immediately, and not move until ATC provides the next clearance.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 critical phraseology.

## 📖 Meaning

Maintain Position is an ATC ground instruction requiring the aircraft to stop and hold its current position on the taxiway or runway holding point, awaiting further clearance before proceeding.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Mantenha posição (maintain position).

Instrução do controle de tráfego aéreo para que a aeronave pare e mantenha sua posição atual no solo, aguardando autorização posterior antes de prosseguir.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Maintain Position is issued, the pilot should:

- stop the aircraft immediately at the current position
- readback the instruction with callsign
- hold until explicitly cleared to proceed
- do not enter the runway without a subsequent clearance
- confirm with ATC if the instruction is unclear or ambiguous

Maintain Position reporting commonly includes:

- readback maintain position with callsign
- request clarification if reason for hold is unclear
- advise ATC when ready to proceed
- query ATC after extended hold without further instruction

**Important:** Maintain Position means stop and stay. Do not interpret it as "slow down" or "almost there." Stop completely and hold until cleared.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC issues Maintain Position to:

- protect the runway from traffic conflicts
- sequence arriving and departing aircraft
- allow maintenance or runway inspection
- coordinate with vehicles on the movement area
- prevent runway incursions during high-traffic periods

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are taxiing to the runway at Curitiba in your H130.

A landing aircraft is on short final for the same runway.

Tower transmits:

ANAC123, maintain position, landing traffic.

You stop immediately at the holding point and readback:

Maintain position, ANAC123.

After the landing aircraft vacates, Tower clears you to line up.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, maintain position, landing traffic on final.
2. Tower: ANAC123, maintain position, runway inspection in progress.
3. Tower: ANAC123, maintain position, expect departure clearance in two minutes.
4. Tower: ANAC123, maintain position, vehicle crossing the runway.
5. Tower: ANAC123, maintain position, confirm ready for departure.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Maintain position, ANAC123.
2. Holding position, ANAC123, waiting.
3. Maintaining position, ANAC123, traffic on final copied.
4. ANAC123, maintaining position, advise when we can proceed.
5. Maintain position, ANAC123, ready for departure when cleared.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Maintain position — two words that save lives.

Stop. Hold. Do not move. Wait for the next clearance.

The sequence:

Hear maintain position. Stop. Readback. Hold. Wait for the next instruction.

Brazilian pilots sometimes slowly inch forward after a maintain position.

That is a runway incursion risk.

Stop means stop. Completely.

At Congonhas, runway incursion events have occurred due to partial readbacks and slow compliance.

Readback every maintain position with your callsign.

Never move until you have a positive clearance.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the correct readback when ATC issues maintain position?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

The correct readback is to repeat the instruction and add my callsign — maintain position, ANAC123. I would stop the aircraft immediately and hold until ATC issued the next clearance. If I was uncertain about the position or expected a long hold, I would advise ATC and confirm when I could proceed.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Replying only with "afirmativo" or "sim" to maintain position.  
  ✔ Readback the full instruction: maintain position, followed by your callsign.

- ❌ Slowly rolling forward after stopping.  
  ✔ Stop completely and hold until a positive clearance to proceed is given.

- ❌ Confusing maintain position with continue taxi.  
  ✔ Maintain position means stop and hold — not continue slowly.

- ❌ Not querying ATC after an unusually long hold.  
  ✔ If the hold is extended without explanation, ask: ANAC123, maintaining position, request clearance to proceed.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are taxiing to runway two seven at Congonhas in your H130. Just before the holding point, Tower issues: ANAC123, maintain position, landing traffic.

**Your first task**

What is your exact readback?

**Your second task**

After two minutes, no further instruction has come. What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

My readback would be: Maintain position, ANAC123. I would stop completely at the holding point. After two minutes without further instruction, I would transmit: Congonhas Tower, ANAC123, maintaining position, request clearance to proceed. I would not move until I received a positive clearance from ATC.
