# Electrical Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0047  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the most disruptive aircraft systems emergencies.

Electrical Failure means the aircraft has lost part or all of its electrical power — affecting avionics, instruments, lighting, and communication equipment.

In the H130, electrical power supports navigation, radios, and critical instruments.

A total electrical failure can leave you on battery power with limited capability.

You must aviate first, switch to alternate power if available, then communicate.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Electrical Failure is the partial or complete loss of aircraft electrical power, requiring alternate power sources and possible emergency procedures.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha elétrica.

Perda parcial ou total da energia elétrica da aeronave, exigindo fontes alternativas de energia.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Electrical Failure occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the first priority
- switch to battery power or alternate electrical source if available
- identify which systems remain operational
- declare Mayday for total failure or Pan Pan for partial failure
- inform ATC of electrical failure, intentions, and position

Electrical Failure reporting commonly includes:

- total electrical failure
- switching to battery power
- flying partial panel
- request vectors to nearest suitable airport

**Important:** Total electrical failure affecting safe flight is a distress situation. Partial failure with continued safe flight may warrant Pan Pan. Aviate first — then communicate clearly with standard phraseology.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Electrical Failure declarations to:

- clear traffic from the aircraft's route
- provide vectors using whatever communication remains
- coordinate priority landing and emergency services
- request souls on board and fuel remaining
- alert other aircraft and airport authorities

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on approach to Santos Dumont in your H130 at one thousand two hundred feet.

All electrical busses fail simultaneously.

Avionics go dark.

You switch to battery power and regain partial panel operation.

When stable, you transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Santos Dumont Tower, ANAC123, total electrical failure, switching to battery power, request immediate landing.

Tower clears all traffic and coordinates emergency services.

You complete a visual approach and report safe on the ground.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger Mayday, say souls on board and fuel remaining.
2. Tower: ANAC123, runway zero two cleared to land, wind zero three zero at one zero knots.
3. Approach: ANAC123, turn right heading three six zero, descend two thousand, say equipment operational.
4. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services standing by.
5. Departure: ANAC123, radar contact, say intentions and souls on board.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Electrical failure can happen suddenly or gradually.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Aviate. Alternate. Communicate.

Do not panic when instruments go dark.

Know your battery limitations.

Know your partial panel skills.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "falta de energia" or "painel apagou" on frequency.

Use total electrical failure or switching to battery power — ICAO standard terms.

For total failure affecting safe flight — Mayday.

For partial failure with continued capability — Pan Pan.

In the H130, battery time is limited.

Make your radio call count — state the problem once, clearly, with your intentions.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC after a total electrical failure on approach?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

After securing the aircraft and switching to battery power, I would declare Mayday Mayday Mayday, state my callsign, report total electrical failure, state that I am switching to battery power or flying partial panel, and request immediate landing. I would provide souls on board and fuel remaining when ATC requests.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "painel apagou" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use total electrical failure or partial electrical failure.

- ❌ Forgetting to mention switching to battery power.  
  ✔ Report switching to battery power — it tells ATC your capability.

- ❌ Declaring Mayday for a minor generator fluctuation.  
  ✔ Assess severity — Pan Pan for urgency, Mayday for distress.

- ❌ Omitting souls on board and fuel when ATC asks.  
  ✔ Provide souls on board and fuel remaining promptly.
