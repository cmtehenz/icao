# Icing Conditions

**Catalog ID:** 0090  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers icing conditions — when ice forms on the airframe and rotor system.

Icing Conditions exist when visible moisture is present and temperatures are at or below freezing, allowing ice accretion.

In the H130, icing can degrade rotor performance, increase weight, and affect visibility and antennas.

You must exit icing promptly, report icing to ATC, and divert or descend to warmer air when required.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Icing Conditions are meteorological conditions in which ice can form on the aircraft due to visible moisture and freezing temperatures.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Condições de gelo.

Condições meteorológicas em que gelo pode se formar na aeronave devido à umidade visível e temperaturas de congelamento.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Icing Conditions are encountered, the pilot should:

- exit icing as soon as practicable — climb, descend, or divert
- report icing intensity and type to ATC
- monitor rotor RPM, power required, and airframe ice
- avoid continued flight in known icing if the aircraft is not approved
- declare urgency if performance becomes critically degraded

Icing Conditions reporting commonly includes:

- light, moderate, or severe icing
- rime or clear ice if known
- request descent or climb to exit icing
- diverting due icing

**Important:** Many light helicopters are not certified for flight into known icing. Exit immediately and report.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Icing reports to:

- provide altitude changes to warmer or colder exit levels
- warn other aircraft with PIREPs
- coordinate diversions
- suggest routes clear of icing layers
- assist with priority handling if performance is degraded

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are IFR at five thousand feet toward Curitiba in your H130.

You enter cloud with a temperature of minus two degrees Celsius and notice ice forming on the windscreen and airframe.

You transmit:

Curitiba Approach, ANAC123, moderate icing at five thousand, request descent to three thousand to exit icing.

Approach clears the descent.

Ice stops accreting in warmer air and you continue with caution.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger icing, descend and maintain three thousand, say icing intensity.
2. Approach: ANAC123, previous aircraft reported light icing at five thousand.
3. Center: ANAC123, advise when clear of icing.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say air temperature and icing type if able.
5. Approach: ANAC123, diversion approved, say intentions.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Curitiba Approach, ANAC123, moderate icing at five thousand, request descent.
2. ANAC123, light rime icing, requesting climb to exit.
3. ANAC123, severe icing, diverting to nearest suitable airport.
4. Approach, ANAC123, clear of icing at three thousand.
5. Pan Pan Pan Pan, ANAC123, severe icing, performance decreasing, request priority landing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Ice is weight, drag, and lost performance.

Visible moisture. Freezing temperature. Ice forms.

The sequence:

Recognize. Exit. Report. Divert if needed.

Do not linger to "see if it gets worse."

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "formação de gelo" on frequency.

Use icing or moderate icing — ICAO standard terms with intensity.

Know the intensities:

Light — not a problem if exited promptly.

Moderate — rate of accretion hazardous if prolonged.

Severe — rate requires immediate exit.

In the H130, treat known icing as a no-go unless specifically approved.

Train early exit and clear icing PIREPs.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if you encountered icing in cloud?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report icing with intensity and altitude, and request climb or descent to exit the icing layer. If icing was severe or performance decreased, I would divert to the nearest suitable airport and declare Pan Pan if necessary. I would report when clear of icing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing in icing to complete the route.  
  ✔ Exit immediately — climb, descend, or divert.

- ❌ Saying "tem gelo" without intensity.  
  ✔ Use light, moderate, or severe icing.

- ❌ Not reporting when clear of icing.  
  ✔ Update ATC when conditions improve.

- ❌ Forgetting to state altitude.  
  ✔ Always include altitude with icing PIREPs.

At six thousand feet in cloud, ice forms on your H130 windscreen.

**Your first task**

Transmit your icing report and request.

Ice becomes severe and power required increases. What do you say?
