# Smoke Evacuation Procedure

**Catalog ID:** 0350  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Smoke Evacuation Procedure — the crew actions and phraseology used when smoke is present in the cockpit or cabin and must be removed or escaped from, including immediate landing decisions and coordination with ATC and fire services.

Smoke is a time-critical emergency. Vision, breathing, and systems can degrade quickly. ICAO English must be short: smoke in cockpit, smoke in cabin, request immediate landing, request fire services.

For H130 ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, smoke evacuation means ventilating or isolating sources per AFM, landing ASAP, and egressing if fire or dense smoke makes the aircraft unsurvivable on the ground. Do not invent airliner pack/recirc language the type does not use — know exam terms and map them to helicopter procedures.

Master: smoke evacuation, smoke in cockpit/cabin, immediate landing, fire services.

## 📖 Meaning

Smoke Evacuation Procedure is the set of memory and checklist actions used to clear or escape smoke from the cockpit or cabin, protect crew oxygen/breathing as equipped, land as soon as possible, and evacuate if smoke or fire remains a life threat after landing.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Procedimento de evacuação de fumaça / Smoke Evacuation Procedure.

Conjunto de ações de memória e checklist usadas para eliminar ou escapar da fumaça na cabine de comando ou de passageiros, proteger a respiração da tripulação conforme o equipamento, pousar o mais breve possível e evacuar se a fumaça ou o fogo continuar sendo ameaça à vida após o pouso.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When smoke requires an evacuation procedure mindset, the crew should:

- don oxygen / smoke goggles if equipped and required
- fly the aircraft; initiate diversion / immediate landing
- apply smoke/fumes checklist: source isolation, ventilation as per AFM
- declare Mayday for dense smoke or fire; Pan Pan may apply only if clearly limited and controlled
- advise ATC: smoke in cockpit/cabin, request immediate landing and fire services
- after landing, evacuate if smoke densifies or fire is suspected

Smoke evacuation reporting commonly includes:

- smoke in cockpit / smoke in cabin
- smoke evacuation procedure in progress
- request immediate landing / priority
- evacuating / request fire services

**Important:** "Smoke evacuation" on frequency means you are dealing with smoke and may evacuate people from the aircraft — not that you are only "venting air" while continuing normally.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to smoke reports to:

- provide immediate priority landing
- clear traffic from the approach and runway
- roll fire services to meet the aircraft
- obtain souls on board and whether evacuation is expected
- protect the evacuation area after landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

In cruise toward Curitiba, smoke appears in the H130 cabin — acrid, visibility dropping.

You declare Mayday, request immediate landing at the nearest suitable airport, and run the smoke checklist while flying the approach.

Tower clears you to land with fire services rolling.

After landing, smoke increases. You stop, secure, and evacuate. You advise ATC: evacuating, request fire services.

Procedure in the air. Egress on the ground if needed.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Mayday, nearest airport is ahead, expect straight-in, fire services alerted.
2. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, fire services rolling, advise if evacuating.
3. Tower: ANAC123, stop on the runway if required, evacuate at your discretion.
4. Ground: ANAC123, fire services at your position, say souls status.
5. Tower: ANAC123, confirm all souls clear of the aircraft.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Smoke is a clock. Do not negotiate with it.

Oxygen if equipped. Checklist. Land. Evacuate if the cabin remains unsurvivable.

Brazilian pilots may say only "fumaça." Prefer smoke in cockpit or smoke in cabin, then immediate landing / evacuating.

On airliner exams you may hear pack, recirculation, and outflow valve language. In the H130, use AFM smoke/fumes and ventilation steps that exist for the type. Do not invent systems.

Never taxi to remote parking with thickening smoke to "avoid blocking the runway" if people cannot breathe. Stop. Evacuate. Tell ATC.

Part 3 answers should show sequence: protect crew, fly, communicate, land, evacuate if required.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You have dense smoke in the cabin. What do you do and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would protect breathing with oxygen if equipped, fly the aircraft, apply the smoke checklist, and declare Mayday requesting immediate landing and fire services. After landing, if smoke continues or fire is suspected, I would stop, secure, evacuate, and report evacuating with a request for fire services, then advise when all souls are clear.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "fumaça" without location or intentions.  
  ✔ Say smoke in cockpit/cabin, request immediate landing.

- ❌ Continuing to destination with progressive dense smoke.  
  ✔ Land at the nearest suitable airport immediately.

- ❌ Delaying evacuation after landing while smoke thickens.  
  ✔ Stop, evacuate, update ATC.

- ❌ Using incorrect airliner system jargon on an H130 answer.  
  ✔ Use type-applicable AFM steps; know exam terms separately.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Dense smoke fills the cabin twenty miles from a suitable airport.

**Your first task**

Transmit your distress call.

**Your second task**

After landing, smoke worsens. What do you say as you evacuate?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, ANAC123, smoke in cabin, request immediate landing and fire services. Second task: ANAC123, stopping on the runway, evacuating, smoke continuing, request fire services. When able: ANAC123, all souls clear of the aircraft.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Smoke evacuation procedure is protect, land, and get out if the aircraft is no longer survivable.

Speak location of smoke. Request immediate landing. Roll fire services. Evacuate without delay when required.

In Brazilian H130 operations, keep actions type-real and English exam-ready.

Smoke does not wait for a perfect sentence — but a clear sentence brings the help that finishes the job.
