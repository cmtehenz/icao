# Radio Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0054  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers radio failure — when you cannot transmit or receive on assigned frequencies.

Radio Failure means communication equipment is inoperative — complete or partial.

You must know lost communication procedures: squawk 7600, follow last clearance, and land as soon as practicable.

If you regain partial communication, declare Pan Pan or Mayday as appropriate.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Radio Failure is the inoperative state of aircraft radio communication equipment, requiring lost communication procedures.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha de rádio.

Equipamento de comunicação por rádio inoperante, exigindo procedimentos de comunicação perdida.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Radio Failure occurs, the pilot should:

- attempt to restore communication on assigned and emergency frequencies
- squawk 7600 on the transponder
- follow the last received ATC clearance to the extent possible
- land at the nearest suitable airport if in VMC
- declare Pan Pan or Mayday if partial communication is restored and assistance is needed

Radio Failure reporting commonly includes:

- complete radio failure
- loss of radio contact
- squawking seven six zero zero
- transmission readable or not readable

**Important:** Squawk 7600 immediately when radio failure is confirmed. Follow ICAO lost communication procedures. If you regain transmit capability, inform ATC promptly with Pan Pan or Mayday as the situation requires.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Radio Failure indications to:

- identify the aircraft by transponder code 7600
- clear traffic from the aircraft's expected route
- monitor the aircraft visually and on radar
- anticipate aircraft actions per lost communication procedures
- coordinate with adjacent sectors for handoff

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on approach to Porto Alegre in your H130, callsign ANAC123, when both COM radios fail.

You attempt reset without success.

You squawk 7600 and continue the published approach per your last clearance.

You land on runway one one and taxi clear.

On the ground, you contact Tower by phone to report complete radio failure.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, radio check, how do you read?
2. Approach: ANAC123, if you read, squawk ident.
3. Tower: ANAC123, your transmission barely readable, say again.
4. Control: ANAC123, radar shows seven six zero zero, traffic cleared from your approach.
5. Tower: ANAC123, if you read, cleared to land runway one one.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What procedures do you follow when you experience complete radio failure?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would attempt to restore communication on assigned and emergency frequencies. If unsuccessful, I would squawk seven six zero zero on the transponder. I would follow my last received ATC clearance, comply with lost communication procedures, and land at the nearest suitable airport as soon as practicable. If I regain transmit capability, I would inform ATC immediately.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "sem rádio" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use complete radio failure or loss of radio contact.

- ❌ Forgetting to squawk 7600.  
  ✔ Squawk seven six zero zero immediately when radio failure is confirmed.

- ❌ Declaring Mayday for radio failure alone without an underlying emergency.  
  ✔ Follow lost comm procedures; use Pan Pan if partial comm is restored.

- ❌ Saying "squawk seventy-six hundred" instead of digit by digit.  
  ✔ Say squawking seven six zero zero — each digit separately.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Squawking Seven Six Zero Zero

**Pronunciation:** SKWAW-king SEV-en six ZEE-roh ZEE-roh

**Word Stress**

- Squawking → SKWAW-king
- Seven → SEV-en
- Zero → ZEE-roh (say each digit: seven, six, zero, zero)

Practice:

Seven... six... zero... zero...

Together:

ANAC123, complete radio failure, squawking seven six zero zero.

Speak each digit clearly — controllers listen for 7600.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**7600** — **7** attempt restore, **6** squawk seven six zero zero, **0** obey last clearance, **0** land when practicable.
