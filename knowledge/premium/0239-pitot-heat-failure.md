# Pitot Heat Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0239  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Pitot Heat Failure — loss of heating to the pitot system, which can allow ice to block the pitot tube in icing or moisture conditions and produce unreliable airspeed indications, requiring cross-check, AFM procedures, and clear ATC communication when airspeed is unreliable.

Pitot heat failure is not a minor caption. Airspeed is a primary control and performance reference. In cloud, rain, or icing, an unheated pitot can ice over quickly. Unreliable airspeed has caused serious accidents when crews chased false indications.

For H130 helicopter training academy IFR operations across Brazilian routes — Congonhas, Curitiba, Florianópolis — crews must recognize the failure, secure reliable references, exit icing if needed, and report the limitation in ICAO English for Part 2 and Part 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Pitot Heat Failure is a malfunction in which heating of the pitot probe is lost or inoperative, increasing the risk of pitot icing and erroneous airspeed indications in visible moisture or freezing conditions, and requiring abnormal procedures, attitude-based flight, and ATC notification when airspeed becomes unreliable or operational capability is limited.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha do aquecimento do pitot / Pitot Heat Failure.

Malfuncionamento em que o aquecimento da sonda pitot é perdido ou inoperante, aumentando o risco de gelo no pitot e indicações errôneas de velocidade em umidade visível ou condições de congelamento, e exigindo procedimentos anormais, voo baseado em atitude e notificação ao ATC quando a velocidade se torna não confiável ou a capacidade operacional fica limitada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When pitot heat fails, the crew should:

- confirm the failure and apply the AFM abnormal checklist
- monitor airspeed for sudden changes inconsistent with attitude and power
- avoid or exit icing and heavy moisture when pitot heat is inoperative
- fly known power and attitude if airspeed becomes unreliable
- advise ATC of pitot heat failure and unreliable airspeed if applicable
- request vectors, priority, or diversion as required for a safe landing

Pitot heat failure scenarios commonly include:

- caption or cold pitot discovered before entering cloud
- airspeed winding down or overreading due to blocked pitot in icing
- need to continue IFR with unreliable airspeed using backup references
- ATC requests for speed that you cannot accurately fly or report
- H130 IFR: limited redundancy — early diversion may be the professional choice

**Important:** If airspeed is unreliable, do not chase the airspeed indicator. Maintain attitude and power per procedure. Tell ATC you have unreliable airspeed and cannot accept speed assignments based on the failed indication. Request simple vectors and a precautionary landing if needed.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use pitot heat / unreliable airspeed reports to:

- avoid assigning specific indicated airspeeds the crew cannot reliably fly
- provide priority vectors and simplified clearances
- coordinate diversion to the nearest suitable airport
- increase separation when performance or speed control is uncertain
- request souls and fuel when the event becomes an emergency

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are climbing through five thousand feet in rain toward Curitiba. The pitot heat fails. Temperature is near freezing in the cloud layer ahead.

You apply the checklist and decide not to enter known icing without pitot heat.

You transmit:

ANAC123, pitot heat failure, request maintain present altitude, vectors to remain clear of icing, precautionary return to Guarulhos.

Approach: ANAC123, turn left heading two four zero, descend and maintain three thousand, expect vectors Guarulhos.

Later, airspeed begins to fluctuate. You update:

ANAC123, unreliable airspeed, flying attitude and power, request priority vectors for landing.

You stop chasing the needle. You fly the aircraft. You land with a known limitation.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Pitot heat keeps the airspeed system honest in moisture and ice. When it fails, honesty moves to attitude and power.

Do not wait for a dramatic airspeed failure before acting. If you must enter icing or heavy cloud without pitot heat, you are accepting a known risk. In the H130 training world, the better decision is often: stay clear, return, or divert.

If the airspeed lies, believe the instruments that still agree — attitude, power, altitude trend, and rotor/engine parameters as applicable. Chase the false airspeed and you invent a second emergency.

On the radio, be specific. "Instrument problem" is weak. "Pitot heat failure" or "unreliable airspeed" tells ATC what not to assign and how urgently to help.

Brazilian IFR weather can stack rain, cloud, and cold air on the same route. Say the limitation early. Request simple clearances. Land while the situation is still a system failure — not a loss of control story.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Pitot heat fails before you enter a cloud layer at freezing level. Airspeed is still normal. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would apply the AFM procedure and avoid entering icing or conditions likely to block the pitot. I would advise ATC of the pitot heat failure and request an altitude or routing to remain clear of icing, or a return or diversion if I cannot continue safely. If airspeed later became unreliable, I would fly attitude and power, report unreliable airspeed, and request priority vectors for landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Entering known icing after pitot heat failure because airspeed still looks normal.  
  ✔ Avoid icing; pitot blockage can develop quickly once moisture freezes.

- ❌ Chasing a decreasing airspeed with aggressive pitch changes.  
  ✔ Cross-check attitude and power; treat as unreliable airspeed per procedure.

- ❌ Accepting precise speed control instructions with a failed pitot system.  
  ✔ Advise unable assigned speed; request simplified clearances.

- ❌ Delaying the radio call until airspeed has already failed completely.  
  ✔ Report pitot heat failure early and state intentions.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Pitot heat failure is a quiet problem that becomes loud when the airspeed lies.

Avoid the ice. Fly attitude and power. Report the limitation. Divert while you still have options.

In the H130, redundancy is limited. Early decisions beat late heroics.

Tell ATC what failed and what you cannot do. Ask for simple vectors. Land.

That is how you keep airspeed failures from becoming control failures — in Brazilian IFR and in ICAO English.
