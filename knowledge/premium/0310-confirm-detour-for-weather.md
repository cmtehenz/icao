# Confirm Detour for Weather

**Catalog ID:** 0310  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Confirm Detour for Weather — the ATC request for the crew to confirm a weather-related path around convective cells, storms, or hazardous meteorological conditions. Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: request the detour, confirm direction, and report clear of weather.

Confirm detour for weather is High priority because convective weather is common in Brazil and silent weather turns create traffic conflicts.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for weather detours under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including left/right requests and return-to-route reports.

Understanding how to confirm a weather detour in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Confirm Detour for Weather is an ATC request for the pilot to verify or describe a temporary routing change made to avoid hazardous weather, including direction of turn, extent of the detour when known, and the intention to return to the cleared route when clear of the weather.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Confirmar desvio por meteorologia / Confirm Detour for Weather.

Solicitação do controle para que o piloto verifique ou descreva uma mudança temporária de rota para evitar meteorologia perigosa, incluindo direção da curva, extensão do desvio quando conhecida e a intenção de retornar à rota liberada quando estiver livre da meteorologia.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC asks you to confirm a weather detour, the crew should:

- confirm the detour and state left or right
- request a specific heading or degrees off course when asking first
- report approximate miles off track if useful
- advise when clear of weather and able to return
- for H130: respect performance limits — do not fly into known severe convection to "save" the route
- keep transmissions short on busy frequencies during widespread weather

Weather-detour exchanges commonly include:

- confirm detour for weather / detouring left/right due weather
- request weather deviation / request detour
- clear of weather / returning to course
- unable due weather
- request further left/right

**Important:** A weather detour is still a deviation from clearance until ATC approves or amends it. Ask early; do not wait until the cell is in the windscreen.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers confirm weather detours to:

- authorize path changes around storms while protecting separation
- coordinate multiple aircraft avoiding the same weather system
- plan when and how to bring you back to the airway or STAR
- avoid conflicting left and right deviations between aircraft
- maintain an accurate radar and procedural traffic picture

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 IFR from São Paulo toward Rio. A line of cells blocks the airway.

You call:

ANAC123, request detour thirty degrees left due weather.

Control approves. Later:

Control: ANAC123, confirm detour for weather.

You reply:

ANAC123, affirmative, detouring left due weather, fifteen miles left of track.

When the cells are behind you:

ANAC123, clear of weather, request return to route.

ATC issues a heading back to the airway. Weather avoided. Route restored. Frequency kept informed.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, confirm detour for weather.
2. Radar: ANAC123, weather deviation approved, left of course.
3. Control: ANAC123, say when clear of weather.
4. Approach: ANAC123, confirm further deviation required.
5. Radar: ANAC123, when able, return to route.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, affirmative, detouring left due weather.
2. ANAC123, request detour thirty degrees right due weather.
3. ANAC123, fifteen miles left of track, weather.
4. ANAC123, clear of weather, request return to route.
5. ANAC123, returning to course, resume own navigation.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Weather detours are negotiated, not improvised in silence.

Ask for the turn before you need the steep bank. Confirm when ATC asks. Report clear. In Brazilian summer convection, half the frequency may be detouring — short, standard words keep the picture clean.

The H130 can often work around cells at lower altitudes than jets, but the radio standard is the same. Left or right. Due weather. Clear of weather. Back on course.

Part 2 rewards that sequence.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Convective weather is ahead on your cleared route. How do you request a detour, and how do you confirm it when ATC asks?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would request a weather detour with direction and, if possible, degrees or heading, stating due weather. When ATC asks me to confirm the detour, I would affirm, state left or right of track, and give distance off course if known. When clear of the weather, I would report clear of weather and request return to route, then comply with the amended clearance.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Turning for weather without a request when two-way radio is available.  
  ✔ Request the detour as early as practical.

- ❌ Saying only "weather" without left or right.  
  ✔ Always include direction of the detour.

- ❌ Staying off route after the cells are gone.  
  ✔ Report clear of weather and return as cleared.

- ❌ Confusing detour with full diversion to another aerodrome.  
  ✔ Detour is temporary path around weather; diversion changes destination.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Cells block the airway. You detour left. Control asks you to confirm, then asks when clear.

**Your first task**

What do you say to confirm the weather detour?

**Your second task**

You are clear of the cells. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, affirmative, detouring left due weather — add miles off track if known. Second task: ANAC123, clear of weather, request return to route. Then fly the heading or clearance ATC issues to restore the route.
