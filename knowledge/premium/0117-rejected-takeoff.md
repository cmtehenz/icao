# Rejected Takeoff

**Catalog ID:** 0117  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the Rejected Takeoff — one of the most time-critical decisions in aviation.

A Rejected Takeoff, also called an RTO or Aborted Takeoff, is the decision to stop the aircraft during the takeoff roll before becoming airborne.

The decision happens in seconds.

The communication happens immediately after.

In the H130, a Rejected Takeoff may be caused by an abnormal indication, a bird strike, or an obstacle on the runway at Congonhas or Florianópolis.

You must know what to say, what to do, and why immediate ATC notification is essential.

## 📖 Meaning

A Rejected Takeoff is the deliberate decision to abort a takeoff during the ground roll, stopping the aircraft on the runway before it becomes airborne.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Decolagem interrompida (ou abortada).

Decisão deliberada de interromper a corrida de decolagem e parar a aeronave na pista antes de decolar.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

A Rejected Takeoff requires the pilot to:

- apply maximum braking or stopping procedure per aircraft type
- retard throttles immediately
- maintain runway centerline
- stop the aircraft safely on or near the runway
- notify ATC immediately after stopping

Rejected Takeoff notifications to ATC commonly include:

- runway occupied notification
- request for emergency services if required
- position on runway for situational awareness
- reason for rejection when able

**Important:** The decision to reject must be made early. The later the rejection, the higher the risk. After a defined speed (V1 in fixed-wing; critical decision point in helicopters), the calculus changes — but the communication remains mandatory.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers need immediate notification of a Rejected Takeoff to:

- clear the runway of arriving traffic
- activate emergency services if requested
- hold all other departures and arrivals
- sequence go-arounds for any aircraft on final
- coordinate with fire and rescue if required

Silence after a rejected takeoff creates confusion and risk.

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are departing Congonhas runway two eight left in your H130.

The takeoff roll has begun.

At approximately 40 knots, a caution light illuminates on your panel — an engine abnormality.

You immediately close the throttle and apply braking.

The aircraft decelerates rapidly.

You stop on the runway and transmit:

Congonhas Tower, ANAC123, rejected takeoff, runway two eight left, stopped, engine caution indication.

Tower responds:

ANAC123, roger, all traffic hold. Are you declaring an emergency?

You assess the situation:

Negative, no emergency at this time, request vacate runway.

Tower clears the runway and holds arrivals.

One transmission. Total clarity.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, understand rejected takeoff, confirm stopped?
2. Tower: ANAC123, all traffic holding, advise intentions.
3. Tower: ANAC123, are you declaring an emergency?
4. Tower: ANAC123, runway clear, vacate when able, advise when clear.
5. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services standing by, advise your status.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Congonhas Tower, ANAC123, rejected takeoff, runway two eight left, stopped.
2. ANAC123, rejected takeoff, engine indication, stopped on runway.
3. ANAC123, negative emergency at this time, request vacate runway.
4. ANAC123, declaring emergency, require emergency services.
5. ANAC123, stopped, clear of runway, will advise when able to taxi.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Three words. That is all it takes.

Rejected takeoff. ANAC123.

Then your position. Then your reason.

Many pilots worry about what to say after rejecting. The answer is simple: say what happened, where you are, and what you need.

Do not worry about sounding professional in that moment. You already are professional — because you stopped in time.

After a rejected takeoff, two things are happening simultaneously: you are managing the aircraft, and ATC needs information.

Give them the most important data first: you have stopped. The runway is occupied.

Everything else — the reason, your status, whether you need help — can follow.

Brazilian pilots sometimes freeze on the radio after an abnormal event.

Train this transmission before you ever need it.

Rejected takeoff, position, reason. Three elements. Ten seconds.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you say to ATC immediately after a rejected takeoff?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would immediately notify ATC of the rejected takeoff, my position on the runway, and the reason if known. I would say: Tower, ANAC123, rejected takeoff, runway two eight left, stopped, engine caution indication. I would then advise whether I required emergency services and await further instructions from ATC.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Remaining silent on the frequency after stopping on the runway.  
  ✔ Notify ATC immediately. The runway is occupied and arrivals may be on final.

- ❌ Saying "abortei a decolagem" on an English-speaking frequency.  
  ✔ Use rejected takeoff or stopped on runway.

- ❌ Waiting to complete a full technical assessment before calling ATC.  
  ✔ Call ATC first. Technical assessment follows.

- ❌ Vacating the runway without confirming with ATC.  
  ✔ Request clearance to vacate and confirm runway clear before moving.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are departing Florianópolis runway one four in your H130. During the takeoff roll at 35 knots, you experience an abnormal vibration. You reject the takeoff and stop on the runway.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit to ATC immediately after stopping?

**Your second task**

ATC asks if you are declaring an emergency. The vibration has stopped and the aircraft appears normal. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

After stopping, I would transmit: Florianópolis Tower, ANAC123, rejected takeoff, runway one four, stopped, abnormal vibration during roll. If ATC asked about emergency declaration and the aircraft appeared normal, I would say: Negative emergency at this time, ANAC123, request vacate runway, will advise. I would then inspect the aircraft before any further flight.
