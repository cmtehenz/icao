# Bird Strike

**Catalog ID:** 0037  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the most common wildlife hazards in aviation.

Bird Strike means collision between your aircraft and one or more birds.

The impact may cause engine damage, windscreen damage, or structural concerns — or it may cause no damage at all.

Your first report to ATC should be measured: assess the situation before declaring full distress.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 graded urgency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Bird Strike is a collision between an aircraft and one or more birds during flight.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Colisão com ave.

Impacto entre a aeronave e uma ou mais aves durante o voo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Bird Strike occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control
- assess engine parameters, vibration, and smell
- run the appropriate checklist
- report the bird strike to ATC with intentions
- declare Mayday only if damage is confirmed or safe flight is in doubt

Bird Strike reporting commonly includes:

- we may have had a bird strike after takeoff
- bird strike on departure, assessing the situation
- bird strike on final, going around
- checklist complete, no damage, continuing

**Important:** Graded urgency is essential. A possible bird strike does not always require Mayday. Assess first, then communicate with appropriate urgency.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Bird Strike reports to:

- request intentions: level off, return, or continue
- alert following traffic and coordinate runway inspection
- provide vectors for return or holding while assessment continues
- clear traffic if emergency landing is required
- document the event for airport wildlife management

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You have just departed Congonhas in your H130 on a charter flight to the coast.

At five hundred feet, you hear a loud thump on the right side.

Engine parameters look normal, but you feel slight vibration.

You level off and transmit:

Departure, ANAC123, we may have had a bird strike after takeoff. We are assessing the situation and would like to level off at three thousand feet while we run the checklist.

Departure acknowledges and asks you to report any abnormal indications.

After the checklist, vibration has stopped. You report no damage and request return to Congonhas for inspection.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Departure: ANAC123, roger bird strike, say intentions.
2. Tower: ANAC123, caution birds in the vicinity of the airport.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway inspection in progress.
4. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land runway three five left, wind two four zero at one zero.
5. Departure: ANAC123, report any abnormal indications.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Bird strike is where graded urgency matters most.

Brazilian pilots often jump to Mayday on the first thump.

The ICAO Delta model teaches cautious language first: we may have had a bird strike, assessing.

Mayday comes when damage is confirmed — engine failure, severe vibration, or windscreen penetration.

On approach, a bird strike on final means go around first, then report.

Congonhas and Santos Dumont have significant bird activity near water and green areas.

State your intentions clearly: level off, return, or continue.

Run the checklist before you make promises to ATC about landing.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you report to ATC immediately after a bird strike on takeoff?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report that we may have had a bird strike after takeoff, state that we are assessing the situation, request to level off while we run the checklist, and report any abnormal indications. I would declare Mayday only if damage is confirmed or safe flight is in doubt.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Declaring Mayday before completing initial assessment.  
  ✔ Use cautious language first: we may have had a bird strike, assessing.

- ❌ Saying we hit birds only in Portuguese on frequency.  
  ✔ Use bird strike — the ICAO standard term.

- ❌ Forgetting to state intentions: level off, return, or continue.  
  ✔ Always tell ATC what you plan to do next.

- ❌ Continuing approach without reporting bird strike on final.  
  ✔ Go around if needed, then report bird strike and intentions.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are departing Santos Dumont at four hundred feet in your H130 when you hear impact on the nose.

**Your first task**

After leveling off and beginning the checklist, transmit your initial report to Departure.

**Your second task**

Assess first. Run the checklist. Then communicate with the right urgency level.

State your intentions clearly on every transmission.
