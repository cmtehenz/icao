# Hot Air Balloon Traffic

**Catalog ID:** 0358  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Hot Air Balloon Traffic — the presence of free balloons in or near your route or aerodrome area, requiring traffic advisories, visual acquisition, and possible altitude or routing changes to maintain safe separation.

Balloons drift with the wind, have limited maneuverability, and often operate at sunrise in VMC. ICAO English must handle traffic calls cleanly: balloon traffic, clock position, altitude, and your intentions.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, balloon activity is common in parts of the South and Southeast in the early morning. Helicopters and balloons share low-level airspace — see and avoid plus clear radio coordination.

Understanding hot air balloon traffic phraseology is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Hot Air Balloon Traffic is the presence of one or more free hot air balloons as conflicting or nearby traffic, typically reported by ATC or observed visually, requiring pilots to acquire the traffic, maintain separation, and adjust route or altitude as needed because balloons have limited ability to maneuver.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Tráfego de balão de ar quente / Hot Air Balloon Traffic.

Presença de um ou mais balões livres de ar quente como tráfego conflitante ou próximo, tipicamente reportada pelo ATC ou observada visualmente, exigindo que os pilotos adquiram o tráfego, mantenham separação e ajustem rota ou altitude conforme necessário, porque balões têm capacidade limitada de manobra.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When hot air balloon traffic is a factor, the crew should:

- acknowledge ATC traffic advisory
- search visually along the reported bearing and altitude
- report traffic in sight or negative contact
- maneuver as required to maintain safe separation — balloons cannot easily clear you
- request altitude or routing change if visual contact is not obtained in time
- avoid flying through known balloon launch areas when NOTAMs or ATIS warn

Balloon traffic reporting commonly includes:

- balloon traffic in sight / negative contact
- request climb or deviation for balloon traffic
- balloon traffic left/right, same altitude
- clear of balloon traffic

**Important:** Balloons drift. A position reported two minutes ago may have moved with the wind. Keep looking and update ATC if you lose sight.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers issue balloon traffic advisories to:

- prevent midair conflict between powered aircraft and balloons
- pass clock position, distance, and altitude estimates
- coordinate altitude blocks when balloons are numerous
- alert departures and arrivals near launch fields
- receive pilot reports that refine balloon positions

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Departing a countryside field near São Paulo state in the H130 at sunrise, Tower advises hot air balloon traffic twelve o'clock, two miles, altitude three thousand.

You search, call traffic in sight, and offset right while climbing through the balloon's altitude band. Once clear, you report clear of balloon traffic and continue on course.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, traffic twelve o'clock, two miles, hot air balloon, altitude three thousand, advise in sight.
2. Approach: ANAC123, multiple balloons northeast of the field, advise.
3. Tower: ANAC123, balloon traffic moving east with the wind, report clear.
4. Approach: ANAC123, climb and maintain four thousand for balloon traffic.
5. Tower: ANAC123, negative radar on balloon, traffic advisory only, remain vigilant.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, looking for balloon traffic, twelve o'clock.
2. ANAC123, balloon traffic in sight, will maintain separation.
3. ANAC123, negative contact, request climb four thousand for balloon traffic.
4. ANAC123, clear of balloon traffic.
5. ANAC123, balloon traffic right two o'clock, same altitude, maneuvering left.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Balloon traffic is see-and-avoid with humility.

You can change heading and altitude quickly. The balloon mostly cannot.

Brazilian pilots may say "tem balão na frente." Prefer balloon traffic in sight or negative contact, request climb.

In the H130, low-level cruise puts you in the same band as many balloons. Slow down the scan at sunrise. Take the climb when negative contact persists.

High priority because closing speeds and limited balloon maneuverability leave little room for late discovery.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC reports hot air balloon traffic ahead. What do you say and do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would acknowledge, look for the balloon on the reported bearing, and call traffic in sight when acquired, maintaining safe separation. If I have negative contact, I would request climb, descent, or deviation until clear. I would report clear of balloon traffic when past the conflict.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "vi o balão" without standard traffic language.  
  ✔ Use balloon traffic in sight.

- ❌ Assuming the balloon will move out of the way.  
  ✔ You maneuver — balloons have limited control.

- ❌ Stopping the scan after one look.  
  ✔ Balloons drift; keep visual contact until clear.

- ❌ Not requesting altitude change when contact is negative.  
  ✔ Request climb or deviation early.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**BALLOON** — **B**earings from ATC, **A**cquire visually, **L**ateral or vertical fix, **L**ook again as it drifts, **O**ffer clear report, **O**ffset early if needed, **N**egative contact → request climb.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Tower: hot air balloon traffic twelve o'clock, one mile, same altitude.

**Your first task**

You do not see it yet. What do you say?

**Your second task**

You acquire the balloon and pass clear. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, negative contact, request climb or deviation for balloon traffic. Second task: ANAC123, balloon traffic in sight, clear of balloon traffic. I would not assume the balloon will clear itself.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Hot air balloon traffic is shared sky with unequal maneuverability.

Listen. Look. Separate. Report.

In Brazilian sunrise operations, expect balloons near popular launch areas.

See them early. Move the H130. Say it in standard English.
