# Report Final

**Catalog ID:** 0021  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the most common instructions during a visual approach.

When Tower says Report Final, the controller wants to know exactly when you are established on final approach.

This helps ATC sequence traffic, issue landing clearance at the right moment, and maintain safe spacing on the runway and in the pattern.

Reporting final is not the same as being cleared to land.

Professional pilots understand that difference immediately.

## 📖 Meaning

Report Final instructs the pilot to advise ATC when the aircraft is on final approach to the assigned runway.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Reporte final.

Informe ao controle quando a aeronave estiver estabelecida na perna final da pista designada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Report Final, the pilot should:

- continue the approach as cleared or as instructed
- report when established on final for the assigned runway
- include the runway and aircraft identification in the report
- remain prepared for landing clearance, go-around, or further instructions

A final report is commonly used:

- during VFR operations at towered airports
- during visual approaches
- when Tower is sequencing multiple aircraft
- when spacing must be confirmed before landing clearance

**Important:** Report Final does not authorize landing. The pilot must still receive Cleared to Land unless already cleared.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers use Report Final to:

- sequence arriving traffic safely
- confirm aircraft position before issuing landing clearance
- manage spacing between arrivals
- coordinate runway occupancy
- maintain situational awareness in the visual circuit

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're flying your H130 to Navegantes Airport on a clear afternoon.

Tower has already instructed you to join the left downwind for runway one eight.

After you report downwind, Tower says:

Tower: ANAC123, number two, follow the traffic on a two-mile final. Report final runway one eight.

You acknowledge and continue the approach.

When you turn final and the runway is clearly in sight, you report:

Pilot: Final runway one eight, ANAC123.

Tower then issues landing clearance when spacing is correct.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, report final runway one eight.
2. Tower: ANAC123, report final.
3. Tower: ANAC123, report long final runway three six.
4. Tower: ANAC123, report three-mile final runway two seven.
5. Tower: ANAC123, report final, number two behind the Airbus.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine you're waiting for a friend at a restaurant.

You don't need to know every step they took on the way.

You only need to know when they are about to arrive at the door.

That's what Report Final does for ATC.

The controller does not need to watch your entire approach.

They need one clear message at the right moment:

"I am now on final."

Until you report final, Tower may delay landing clearance because they cannot confirm your position in the sequence.

And remember — reporting final is like knocking on the door.

It does not mean you are allowed to enter.

Landing clearance is still required.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between Report Final and Cleared to Land?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Report Final instructs the pilot to advise ATC when established on final approach. Cleared to Land authorizes the aircraft to land on the assigned runway. Reporting final does not mean landing is approved.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Assuming Report Final means cleared to land.  
  ✔ Continue the approach and wait for explicit landing clearance.

- ❌ Reporting final too early, before established on final.  
  ✔ Report when you are actually on the final leg for the correct runway.

- ❌ Forgetting the runway number.  
  ✔ Include the runway whenever practical: Final runway one eight, ANAC123.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Better: Will report final runway one eight, ANAC123.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Report Final

**Pronunciation:** ri-PORT FY-nul

**Word Stress**

- Report → second syllable
- Final → first syllable

Practice:

Report... Final...

Together:

Report Final.

Then inside a complete transmission:

Final runway one eight, ANAC123.

Speak the runway number clearly and keep a steady pace.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Think of final approach like the last corridor before the runway.

Report Final is simply telling Tower:

"I have entered the corridor."

The door to land still requires Cleared to Land.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

Tower: ANAC123, number two, follow the traffic on final. Report final runway one eight.

**Your first task**

Read back the instruction exactly as a professional pilot.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

You have reported final but have not received landing clearance. What should you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I should continue the approach as instructed and remain prepared to land, but I must not land until I receive explicit landing clearance. I must also be ready for a go-around if clearance is not issued or if instructed.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Report Final is a small phrase with a large operational role.

It helps ATC see the traffic picture.

It helps you stay in the sequence.

And it reminds you that approach progress and landing authorization are not the same thing.

Report at the right time, with the right runway, and always stay ready for the next instruction.
