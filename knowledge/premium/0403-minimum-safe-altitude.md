# Minimum Safe Altitude

**Catalog ID:** 0403  
**Category:** Navigation  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Minimum Safe Altitude — the published or calculated altitude that provides terrain and obstacle clearance within a defined area, used for emergency drift-down, lost procedures, and situational awareness.

MSA is not optional knowledge. It is the floor beneath your IFR plan.

In the H130, MSA awareness is critical on Brazilian IFR routes across the Serra do Mar, Minas Gerais highlands, and coastal transitions where a descent below MSA without reference can be fatal. Part 2 and Part 4 require you to discuss and request altitudes with reference to MSA when needed.

## 📖 Meaning

Minimum Safe Altitude is the lowest altitude that provides a specified clearance above the highest terrain or obstacle within a defined sector or radius around a navigation facility or aerodrome, used for emergency and situational reference during instrument flight.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Altitude mínima de segurança (MSA).

Menor altitude que proporciona uma margem especificada acima do terreno ou obstáculo mais elevado dentro de um setor ou raio definido em torno de um auxílio à navegação ou aeródromo, usada como referência de emergência e consciência situacional em voo por instrumentos.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When using Minimum Safe Altitude, the crew should:

- brief sector MSAs during approach and en-route planning
- never descend below MSA without positive course guidance and a published procedure
- request climb to MSA if uncertain of position or terrain clearance
- use MSA as the emergency altitude if lost or experiencing navigation failure
- advise ATC when unable to accept an altitude below MSA for terrain reasons

MSA-related reporting commonly includes:

- unable lower, below MSA
- request climb to minimum safe altitude
- maintaining MSA due terrain
- position uncertain, climbing to MSA

**Important:** ATC minimum vectoring altitude and MSA are related but not identical. If a clearance would put you below a safe terrain altitude for your position, say unable and state terrain.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers work with Minimum Safe Altitude concepts to:

- assign altitudes that clear terrain in each sector
- provide minimum vectoring altitudes on radar
- understand pilot refusals based on terrain
- coordinate climbs when aircraft report position uncertainty
- protect IFR flights during navigation anomalies

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are IFR in your H130 toward Confins. Approach issues descend and maintain two thousand five hundred on a vector.

Your charted MSA for the sector is three thousand five hundred. You are not yet established on a published segment that allows lower.

You transmit:

Confins Approach, ANAC123, unable two thousand five hundred, below MSA, request three thousand five hundred.

Approach climbs you and issues a revised vector for the approach from a safe altitude.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, descend and maintain three thousand, minimum vectoring altitude.
2. Approach: ANAC123, roger unable, maintain three thousand five hundred, turn left heading zero one zero.
3. Control: ANAC123, say altitude, confirm at or above MSA.
4. Approach: ANAC123, when established on the localizer, cleared to descend with the procedure.
5. Control: ANAC123, if lost communications, maintain last assigned or MSA, whichever higher, per procedure.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, unable two thousand five hundred, below MSA, maintaining three thousand five hundred.
2. ANAC123, descending three thousand, minimum vectoring altitude.
3. ANAC123, left heading zero one zero, maintaining three thousand five hundred.
4. ANAC123, established localizer, descending with the procedure.
5. ANAC123, request climb to MSA, position uncertain.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

MSA is the altitude that keeps rock out of the windshield when the plan falls apart.

Brief it before top of descent. Know the highest sector on your arrival. In the H130, you can descend steeply — that capability is not permission to bust MSA.

"Unable" is professional English, not conflict. ANAC123, unable, below MSA, request higher. Controllers in Brazil understand terrain. They need you to say it.

Part 4 discussion often asks when you climb to MSA: navigation failure, position uncertain, or clearance conflict with terrain. Answer with the altitude and the reason.

Never let politeness put you below a safe altitude.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC clears you below the charted MSA while you are on random vectors. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would refuse the clearance if it does not provide terrain clearance for my position. I would say: ANAC123, unable two thousand five hundred, below MSA, request three thousand five hundred. I would maintain a safe altitude and accept a revised clearance that respects terrain until established on a published procedure allowing lower.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Accepting any descent clearance without checking MSA.  
  ✔ Cross-check sector MSA before leaving a safe altitude.

- ❌ Saying "I think it's low" without the word MSA.  
  ✔ Use: unable, below MSA, request higher.

- ❌ Descending below MSA because "we are visual with some lights."  
  ✔ Lights are not terrain clearance. Stay at MSA until the procedure allows lower.

- ❌ Confusing MSA with airport elevation.  
  ✔ MSA protects a sector — know which sector you are in.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

On vectors to Confins, Approach clears you to two thousand. Sector MSA is three thousand five hundred. You are not on a published approach segment.

**Your first task**

What do you say?

**Your second task**

Approach replies: "ANAC123, radar terrain clearance provided, descend two thousand." You still assess conflict with charted obstacles off the radar track. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, unable two thousand, below MSA, request three thousand five hundred. Second task: ANAC123, still unable two thousand due terrain, request maintain three thousand five hundred until established on the approach. Pilot final authority for terrain safety remains with you.
