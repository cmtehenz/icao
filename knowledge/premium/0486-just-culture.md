# Just Culture

**Catalog ID:** 0486  
**Category:** CRM  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Just Culture — an organizational safety climate in which people are encouraged to report errors and hazards without fear of unfair punishment, while still remaining accountable for willful misconduct or gross negligence.

Just Culture is not a free pass. It separates honest human error from deliberate violations so that learning can happen and safety can improve.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta training, Just Culture shapes how crews discuss mistakes after a flight, how they report near misses, and how they speak about safety events in Part 1 and Part 4 English without hiding the truth.

Understanding Just Culture in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 1 proficiency and Part 4 operational discussion.

## 📖 Meaning

Just Culture is a safety management principle that encourages open reporting of errors, hazards, and near misses by protecting reporters from punitive action for honest mistakes, while holding individuals accountable for reckless behavior, intentional non-compliance, or actions that knowingly endanger flight safety.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Cultura justa / Just Culture.

Princípio de gestão da segurança que incentiva o relato aberto de erros, perigos e quase acidentes, protegendo quem reporta de punição por erros honestos, mas mantendo a responsabilização por conduta imprudente, não conformidade intencional ou ações que coloquem a segurança do voo em risco de forma consciente.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When applying Just Culture on the line, the crew should:

- report errors and near misses promptly and factually
- describe what happened without blaming or covering up
- distinguish honest mistake from intentional violation
- support colleagues who report in good faith
- for H130: capture hover, sling, and confined-area near misses that never reach ATC
- use clear English when discussing events in debriefs and SMS reports

Just Culture discussions commonly include:

- voluntary safety report
- near miss / hazard report
- honest error versus willful violation
- no-blame reporting for unintentional mistakes
- accountability for reckless acts

**Important:** Just Culture protects honest reporting. It does not protect hiding a mistake, falsifying a log, or knowingly flying an unsafe aircraft.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers and ATS organizations apply Just Culture principles to:

- encourage pilots to report frequency congestion, unclear clearances, and near conflicts
- collect accurate safety data without fear-driven silence
- separate honest readback errors from deliberate non-compliance
- improve procedures based on real reports
- support SMS learning loops between operators and ATS units

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are flying an H130 from Congonhas to Santos. On departure you select the wrong VHF frequency for a few seconds, then correct it before missing a call.

In a punitive culture you might stay silent. In a Just Culture you note the event, brief the other pilot, and file a short voluntary report after landing: wrong frequency selected, corrected immediately, no ATC contact missed.

Later, Approach asks you to confirm a heading you misheard. You admit the error, request say again, and read back correctly. Honesty on frequency and honesty in the SMS report are the same habit.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, confirm heading two seven zero, previous readback incorrect.
2. Tower: ANAC123, say again intentions, last transmission unclear.
3. Control: ANAC123, report if unable to comply, no penalty for early advice.
4. Approach: ANAC123, traffic conflict resolved, request pilot report if desired.
5. Ground: ANAC123, confirm parking stand, previous call copied incorrectly.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, negative, say again heading, previous readback incorrect.
2. ANAC123, request say again intentions, last call unclear.
3. ANAC123, unable to comply as cleared, request alternative.
4. ANAC123, wilco, will file safety report after landing if required.
5. ANAC123, parking stand confirmed, previous call corrected.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Just Culture is courage with structure.

If you hide a mistake, the next crew inherits it. If you report an honest error, the system can fix the trap that caught you.

In the H130, many events never appear on the radio — a near wire strike in a confined area, a late go-around decision, a checklist item almost skipped. Those belong in the report system too.

Part 1 examiners listen for balanced language: you describe the error, the recovery, and the learning. You do not dramatize, and you do not minimize.

Accountability still exists. Flying drunk, ignoring a MEL, or lying on a report is not Just Culture. It is misconduct.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Explain what Just Culture means and how it affects how a pilot reports an honest operational error.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Just Culture means the organization encourages open reporting of honest mistakes and near misses without unfair punishment, while still holding people accountable for willful violations. If I make an honest error — for example selecting the wrong frequency and correcting it — I would report the facts clearly, describe the recovery, and support any SMS follow-up. I would not hide the event. The goal is learning and hazard removal, not blame for normal human error.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating Just Culture as “no consequences for anything.”  
  ✔ Honest errors are protected; reckless or intentional violations are not.

- ❌ Hiding near misses because “nothing happened.”  
  ✔ Near misses are the best learning data — report them.

- ❌ Writing emotional or vague reports instead of facts.  
  ✔ State what, when, where, and how you recovered — clearly in English.

- ❌ Blaming ATC or the other pilot to avoid self-reporting.  
  ✔ Report your own actions factually; systems learn from truth.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

After an H130 approach to Florianópolis you realize you almost descended below the published minimum before correcting. No ATC query was made. Your company has a Just Culture policy.

**Your first task**

Approach asks you to confirm you are established on the final approach course. You had a brief navigation doubt. What do you say on frequency?

**Your second task**

After landing, how do you describe the event in a voluntary safety report in clear ICAO English (no callsign needed in the report narrative)?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, affirmative, established final approach course — or, if still unsure: ANAC123, negative, request confirmation of final approach track. Second task: During final approach I had a brief navigation doubt and almost descended below published minima before correcting and continuing a stable approach. No ATC intervention occurred. I am filing a voluntary report so the hazard and my recovery can be reviewed under Just Culture.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Just Culture turns silence into safety data.

Report honest errors. Protect reporters. Punish only willful harm.

On the radio, admit what you did not copy. In the SMS, write what almost went wrong.

In the H130, the quiet near miss is often the most valuable report you will ever file.

Speak the truth. Learn from it. Fly the next sector safer.
