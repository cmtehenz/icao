# Night Vision Goggle Operations

**Catalog ID:** 0411  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Night Vision Goggle Operations — helicopter flight using NVGs to enhance visual cues in low-light conditions for HEMS, SAR, offshore, and special missions.

NVG operations are not simply "flying at night with goggles." They change scan technique, lighting discipline, and how you report weather, visibility, and emergencies to ATC.

For H130 helicopter operations in Brazil — coastal HEMS, Amazon night transfers, and offshore support — NVG English must be clear: goggle status, degraded vision, and intentions when the picture collapses.

Understanding how to discuss NVG operations in ICAO-standard English — including status reports, limitations, and emergency declarations — is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Night Vision Goggle Operations are helicopter flights conducted with night vision goggles that amplify available light to improve the pilot's visual reference in darkness, requiring specific lighting, scanning, and communication procedures when visual cues degrade or equipment fails.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Operações com óculos de visão noturna (NVG).

Voos de helicóptero realizados com óculos de visão noturna que amplificam a luz disponível para melhorar a referência visual do piloto no escuro, exigindo procedimentos específicos de iluminação, varredura e comunicação quando as pistas visuais se degradam ou o equipamento falha.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When conducting NVG operations, the crew should:

- brief NVG status, lighting configuration, and go/no-go weather before departure
- maintain cockpit lighting compatible with goggles
- report reduced visual reference early if the NVG picture degrades
- declare the emergency if NVG failure threatens safe flight
- state intentions: continue VFR, divert, or climb to IFR if qualified and cleared

NVG reporting commonly includes:

- NVG operations, night VFR
- NVG picture degraded, requesting vectors or diversion
- NVG failure, requesting climb / landing
- unable NVG approach, diverting

**Important:** NVGs improve the picture; they do not create weather. Fog, rain, and cultural lighting can wash out the image. Report the operational limitation, not just "dark."

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers support NVG operations by:

- providing traffic information for low-level night helicopter routes
- coordinating lighting and runway/helipad status
- offering vectors or diversion options when vision degrades
- prioritizing emergency landings after NVG failure
- keeping other traffic clear of low-level NVG corridors

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on a night HEMS flight in your H130 from Congonhas toward a highway scene on the Anchieta, NVGs on.

São Paulo Approach, night VFR, NVG operations, descending one thousand five hundred for scene.

Halfway there, heavy rain washes the NVG picture. You advise:

Request diversion to Congonhas, NVG picture degraded in rain, unable continue to scene.

Approach vectors you home. You land with a usable picture and a clear report.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger NVG operations, report on scene, traffic advised.
2. Approach: ANAC123, Congonhas winds calm, helipad lights on, report field in sight.
3. Approach: ANAC123, roger NVG degraded, cleared direct Congonhas, climb and maintain two thousand.
4. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land helipad, report NVG status on short final.
5. Approach: ANAC123, divert approved, say souls on board and fuel remaining.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, NVG operations, will report on scene.
2. ANAC123, Congonhas in sight, descending for helipad.
3. ANAC123, climbing two thousand, direct Congonhas, NVG degraded.
4. ANAC123, cleared to land, NVGs on, short final.
5. ANAC123, diverting Congonhas, souls four, fuel one hour two zero.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

NVG English is status English.

ATC cannot see your goggle picture. You must translate it: good, degraded, or failed — and what you need next.

In the H130 at night over Brazilian highways and coastline, cultural lighting and weather change the image minute by minute. Brief the abort plan before you launch.

Do not wait until you are IMC in goggles. Say the limitation early. Request climb, vectors, or diversion while you still have options.

Lighting discipline is part of the radio call culture: if you need pad lights adjusted, say so clearly. Ambiguous requests waste time when the picture is already poor.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You are on an NVG HEMS flight when rain degrades your goggle picture. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report the limitation and my intention immediately: NVG picture degraded in rain, unable continue to scene, request diversion to Congonhas and climb to two thousand. I would state souls and fuel if declaring urgency. I would not continue into conditions where the NVG image is unusable.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "visibility bad" without mentioning NVG status.  
  ✔ State NVG picture degraded or NVG failure so ATC understands the night capability loss.

- ❌ Continuing to the scene hoping the picture improves.  
  ✔ Divert or climb while options remain; report early.

- ❌ Forgetting to request lighting or traffic information at the diversion field.  
  ✔ Ask for helipad lights and traffic on short final.

- ❌ Mixing Portuguese weather slang on frequency.  
  ✔ Use ICAO English: rain, reduced visibility, NVG degraded, diverting.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** NVG Picture Degraded, Request Diversion

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, en-vee-JEE PIK-cher di-GRAY-did, reh-KWEST dye-VER-zhun

**Word Stress**

- Picture → PIK-cher
- Degraded → di-GRAY-did (stress on second)
- Diversion → dye-VER-zhun (stress on second)

Practice:

NVG picture degraded... request diversion.

Together:

NVG picture degraded in rain, request diversion to Congonhas.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**GOGGLE** — **G**ive NVG status on check-in, **O**bserve picture continuously, **G**o around or divert early, **G**et climb/vectors if needed, **L**ighting requests clear, **E**mergency declare if failed.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Night HEMS from Congonhas to a coastal scene. NVGs on. Rain intensifies; the picture collapses.

**Your first task**

What do you say to Approach when the NVG picture becomes unusable?

**Your second task**

Approach asks if you can continue VFR without NVGs. Ceiling is indefinite in rain. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, NVG picture unusable in rain, unable continue to scene, request diversion Congonhas, climb two thousand. Second task: ANAC123, unable continue VFR without NVGs, request IFR clearance or priority landing Congonhas — I would not accept a VFR continuation into IMC. Declare pan-pan if needed to secure priority.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

NVG operations succeed when the radio matches the picture.

Say NVG status. Say when it degrades. Say what you need — climb, vectors, diversion, landing.

In the H130 over Brazil at night, the goggles are a tool, not a guarantee.

Protect the mission by protecting the visual reference. Speak early. Divert while you still can.
