# Military Activity

**Catalog ID:** 0188  
**Category:** Navigation  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Military Activity — operations by military aircraft or units that affect civil traffic, including exercises, intercepts practice, formation flights, and temporary airspace activation that may require civil aircraft to reroute, hold, or accept altitude changes.

Military activity is not always confined to a published restricted area. It may be temporary, mobile, or announced by NOTAM and ATC advisory. Civil crews must understand the phraseology, comply with remain-clear or reroute instructions, and communicate clearly if weather or fuel state limits their options.

For H130 helicopter training academy flights in Brazil, military activity can affect coastal corridors, areas near major bases, and routes connecting Congonhas, Santos Dumont, Guarulhos, Curitiba, and Florianópolis. Coordination with ATC is the civil pilot's primary tool.

Understanding how to acknowledge military activity advisories, request alternative routing, and confirm clear of the activity supports Part 2 and Part 4 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Military Activity is any military flight operation or ground-based exercise that occupies or affects a volume of airspace used by civil aviation, requiring ATC to advise civil aircraft, impose routing or altitude restrictions, or activate temporary airspace measures to maintain separation and safety.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC advises military activity affecting your route, the crew should:

- acknowledge the advisory and the required action (remain clear, expect vectors, hold, altitude change)
- update the navigation plan for the revised routing or delay
- monitor for further instructions as the activity moves or ends
- advise ATC early if fuel, weather, or performance limits your ability to accept a long reroute
- report when established on the alternative route or when clear of the activity area if requested
- avoid speculative comments on the frequency about the nature of the military operation

Military activity scenarios commonly include:

- temporary exercise areas activated by NOTAM
- military aircraft operating in or near civil corridors
- ATC vectors around an active training block
- altitude blocks reserved for military use
- delayed clearances into terminal areas while activity is in progress

**Important:** Do not enter an area ATC has associated with military activity unless you receive an explicit clearance. In the H130, you may be offered lower altitudes or coastal offsets that jets cannot use — accept only what keeps you clear and legal. If you observe military traffic visually, maintain your own lookout and follow ATC instructions; do not maneuver toward the formation out of curiosity.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers work with military activity to:

- separate civil and military traffic in shared or adjacent airspace
- advise civil crews of hazards and expected reroutes
- coordinate with military controlling agencies
- restore normal routing when the activity is complete
- protect both the exercise and the civil flow into busy terminals

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Florianópolis from Curitiba. Approach advises military activity in progress west of the field and instructs you to expect vectors east of the normal arrival track.

You acknowledge. Approach issues a heading to remain clear and later sequences you for the approach from the east.

You never enter the activity area. You accept a slightly longer track. You land without conflict.

That is the civil side of military activity: hear the advisory, comply with the vector, keep the frequency professional, and let ATC manage the coordination.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, military activity in progress west of Florianópolis, expect vectors east of course.
2. Radar: ANAC123, remain clear of military activity, turn right heading zero nine zero.
3. Control: ANAC123, due military activity, unable direct, cleared via alternate route.
4. Approach: ANAC123, military activity terminated, resume normal routing, direct to the VOR.
5. Radar: ANAC123, hold at present position, military traffic crossing your route, expect further clearance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Military activity is ATC's problem to coordinate — and your problem to comply with cleanly.

When you hear the advisory, the correct response is acknowledgment and readiness for vectors or reroute. Do not ask for details that do not affect your clearance. Do not complain on frequency about the delay.

If the reroute burns fuel or pushes you toward weather, say that professionally: unable extended vectors due fuel, request shorter alternative or priority. That is Part 4 interaction done correctly — clear need, clear request.

In the H130 near Brazilian coastal and training areas, military activity may appear with little notice via ATC. Keep charts and NOTAMs current, but trust the live instruction over your assumption that the corridor is free.

When activity is terminated and normal routing resumes, read back and rejoin cleanly. Do not cut back through the former activity area until cleared.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC advises military activity on your route and issues a long vector that will significantly increase flight time. Your fuel is limited. Describe what you say and do.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would acknowledge the military activity and the vector, then advise ATC that I am unable to accept an extended reroute due to fuel state if that is the case. I would request a shorter alternative routing, an altitude that remains clear, or priority handling as appropriate. I would not enter the activity area. If no shorter route is available, I would divert to a suitable alternate while remaining clear of the military activity and coordinating the diversion with ATC.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Asking detailed questions about the military exercise on the civil frequency.  
  ✔ Acknowledge the advisory and comply; request only what you need for routing and fuel.

- ❌ Cutting through the activity area after it "looks quiet."  
  ✔ Remain clear until ATC terminates the restriction or clears you through.

- ❌ Accepting a fuel-critical reroute without advising ATC.  
  ✔ State fuel limitation early and request an alternative or diversion.

- ❌ Maneuvering toward observed military traffic for a better look.  
  ✔ Maintain separation mindset and follow ATC; lookout is for avoidance, not sightseeing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Military activity is a shared-sky reality in Brazilian operations.

Hear the advisory. Remain clear. Take the vector.

If fuel or weather makes the reroute unsafe, say so early and professionally. Request an alternative or divert.

Do not investigate the exercise. Do not cut corners when it "looks finished." Wait for ATC to terminate or clear you.

In the H130, flexibility helps — lower altitudes, shorter offsets — but only inside a legal clearance.

Comply cleanly. Communicate limits clearly. Resume normal routing when released.

That is how civil and military traffic share the same sky without sharing the same piece of air.
