# Severe Turbulence

**Catalog ID:** 0085  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers severe turbulence — when the aircraft is violently tossed and control becomes difficult.

Severe Turbulence causes large, abrupt changes in altitude and attitude, with the aircraft momentarily out of control at times.

In the H130, severe turbulence may occur near thunderstorms, mountain waves, and strong coastal wind gradients in Brazil.

You must secure the aircraft, reduce speed to turbulence penetration speed if applicable, and report to ATC immediately.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Severe Turbulence is turbulence that causes large, abrupt changes in altitude and attitude, making aircraft control difficult and causing occupants to be forced violently against restraints.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Turbulência severa.

Turbulência que causa grandes e abruptas mudanças de altitude e atitude, dificultando o controle da aeronave.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Severe Turbulence occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the first priority
- reduce to recommended turbulence speed if published
- avoid abrupt control inputs
- report severe turbulence to ATC with altitude and location
- request altitude or route change to exit the area
- declare Pan Pan or Mayday if structural damage or loss of control occurs

Severe Turbulence reporting commonly includes:

- severe turbulence at altitude
- request climb or descent to exit turbulence
- request weather deviation
- possible structural damage after severe turbulence

**Important:** Always report severe turbulence — ATC uses PIREPs to protect other aircraft. Aviate first, then communicate.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Severe Turbulence reports to:

- warn other aircraft with PIREPs
- offer altitude or route changes
- coordinate weather deviations
- clear traffic from the affected area if needed
- prepare for possible emergency declarations if damage occurs

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at four thousand feet near the Serra do Mar toward São Paulo in your H130.

Without warning, the aircraft is slammed by severe mountain-related turbulence.

You reduce speed, maintain attitude, and transmit:

São Paulo Approach, ANAC123, severe turbulence at four thousand feet, request climb to six thousand and weather deviation.

Approach clears the climb and provides vectors.

Turbulence decreases and you continue with caution.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger severe turbulence, climb and maintain six thousand, say ride conditions.
2. Approach: ANAC123, previous aircraft reported severe turbulence at four thousand, advise intentions.
3. Center: ANAC123, deviation approved, report when smooth.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say if able to maintain altitude.
5. Approach: ANAC123, thank you for the PIREP, traffic advised.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. São Paulo Approach, ANAC123, severe turbulence at four thousand, request climb.
2. ANAC123, severe turbulence, request weather deviation and lower altitude.
3. Pan Pan Pan Pan, ANAC123, severe turbulence, possible structural damage, request immediate landing.
4. ANAC123, severe turbulence, souls on board three, requesting vectors clear of weather.
5. Approach, ANAC123, moderate turbulence now, clear of severe, continuing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Severe turbulence is not uncomfortable — it is dangerous.

The sequence:

Fly attitude. Reduce speed. Report. Exit.

Do not chase altitude with abrupt cyclic.

Hold attitude and let the aircraft ride.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "turbulência forte" or "sacolejo demais" on frequency.

Use severe turbulence — ICAO standard intensity term.

Know the scale:

Light — slight strain.

Moderate — definite strain, loose objects move.

Severe — large abrupt changes, control difficult.

Extreme — aircraft practically impossible to control.

In the H130, report early so others avoid your altitude.

If damage is suspected after severe turbulence — land and inspect.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if you encountered severe turbulence?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain aircraft control, reduce to turbulence speed if applicable, and report severe turbulence with my altitude and position. I would request climb, descent, or weather deviation to exit the area. If structural damage or loss of control occurred, I would declare Pan Pan or Mayday and request immediate landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "turbulência forte" instead of the standard intensity.  
  ✔ Use severe turbulence — ICAO intensity terminology.

- ❌ Not reporting altitude and location with the PIREP.  
  ✔ State altitude and position so ATC can warn others.

- ❌ Making abrupt control inputs to chase altitude.  
  ✔ Maintain attitude — smooth inputs only.

- ❌ Continuing without requesting an exit from the area.  
  ✔ Request climb, descent, or deviation immediately.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**RIDE** — **R**educe speed, **I**nform ATC with altitude, **D**eviate or change level, **E**xit and update the PIREP.
