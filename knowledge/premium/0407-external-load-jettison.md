# External Load Jettison

**Catalog ID:** 0407  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers External Load Jettison — the emergency release of a sling or external load when the load threatens aircraft control, performance, or people on the ground.

Jettison is a critical H130 decision: save the aircraft and crew when the load becomes unmanageable, while communicating immediately so ATC and ground parties are warned.

Brazilian sling operations near roads, yards, and populated fringes make the radio call as important as the hook release. Part 2 and Part 3 demand clear urgency phraseology.

## 📖 Meaning

External Load Jettison is the intentional emergency release of an external sling or cargo-hook load when continued carriage endangers the helicopter, crew, or third parties, followed by immediate notification to ATC and ground personnel of the jettison and landing intentions.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Alívio / lançamento de emergência da carga externa (jettison).

Liberação intencional de emergência de uma carga em linga ou gancho quando a continuidade do transporte põe em risco o helicóptero, a tripulação ou terceiros, seguida de notificação imediata ao ATC e ao pessoal de solo sobre o jettison e as intenções de pouso.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When External Load Jettison is required, the pilot should:

- jettison when control, rotor RPM, or ground safety demands it — do not delay for a perfect radio call
- avoid releasing over people, vehicles, or sensitive areas if any safer alternative exists in the seconds available
- declare the emergency and state jettison immediately after release
- advise position of the dropped load if known
- request landing or return as required

Jettison reporting commonly includes:

- mayday or pan-pan, load jettisoned
- position of jettison
- returning to land / request priority
- souls on board, aircraft under control

**Important:** Aviate and release first if the aircraft is departing controlled flight. Speak in the next breath. A swinging load that will roll the helicopter leaves no time for a long briefing.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to External Load Jettison reports to:

- clear traffic from the area of the falling load
- alert ground emergency services if the load fell near people or roads
- provide priority landing for the helicopter
- mark the jettison location for follow-up
- coordinate with the operator and airport services

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are in a hover taxi with a sling load near a Campinas industrial site in your H130. The load snags, swings hard, and torque and attitude become critical.

You jettison over a clear paved yard.

You transmit:

Campinas Tower, ANAC123, mayday mayday mayday, external load jettisoned, north yard construction site, aircraft under control, request priority landing.

Tower clears the zone, alerts ground parties, and priorities your landing.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger mayday jettison, cleared to land, emergency services alerted.
2. Tower: ANAC123, confirm load jettisoned, say exact position.
3. Approach: ANAC123, roger pan-pan, priority approved, radar vectors to base.
4. Tower: ANAC123, all traffic advised, remain clear of jettison area.
5. Tower: ANAC123, when landed, report aircraft and crew status.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, mayday, load jettisoned, north yard, request priority landing.
2. ANAC123, affirmative jettisoned, position north construction yard.
3. ANAC123, pan-pan, load jettisoned, returning base, request vectors.
4. ANAC123, will remain clear, proceeding to land runway two five.
5. ANAC123, landed, crew safe, aircraft under control.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Jettison is not failure. Losing the aircraft for a bag of concrete is failure.

Brief the jettison decision before the lift: when, where, who calls. In the H130, know the hook release and the backup. Hands find it without looking.

The radio call must contain three facts: distress level, load jettisoned, position. Then intentions.

Brazilian sites often sit beside roads. If you can steer the release to empty pavement, do it. If the helicopter is rolling, release now and warn after.

Never be silent after a jettison. People below need the warning. ATC needs the traffic freeze.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Your sling load becomes uncontrollable and you jettison. What do you say to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

After releasing the load and ensuring the aircraft is flyable, I would declare and state the event: ANAC123, mayday mayday mayday, external load jettisoned, position north construction yard, aircraft under control, request priority landing. If the threat is lower but still urgent, pan-pan may be appropriate. I would give the jettison position so ground parties can be warned.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Delaying jettison to finish a radio sentence.  
  ✔ Release when required, then call.

- ❌ Saying only "I dropped the load" without position or urgency.  
  ✔ Use mayday/pan-pan, jettisoned, position, intentions.

- ❌ Jettisoning over a road when a clear area was available.  
  ✔ Steer to the safest release zone if control still allows.

- ❌ Continuing the mission without advising ATC.  
  ✔ Land and report. The event is an emergency until secured.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Uncontrollable sling swing over an industrial yard. You jettison onto empty pavement.

**Your first task**

Immediate radio call.

**Your second task**

Tower asks if you require emergency services. Crew is fine, aircraft controllable. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, mayday mayday mayday, external load jettisoned, north construction yard, aircraft under control, request priority landing. Second task: ANAC123, negative medical, request emergency services check the jettison site for hazards, landing now. Protect people on the ground even when the crew is safe.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

External load jettison trades cargo for control.

Decide early in the brief. Execute when required. Call with distress, jettison, and position.

In H130 Brazilian sling work, silence after release is negligence.

Release to save the aircraft. Speak to save everyone else. Land with priority. Debrief with honesty.
