# Crosswind Landing

**Catalog ID:** 0095  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers crosswind landing — landing with wind from the side of the runway or helipad.

Crosswind Landing requires technique to maintain alignment and avoid drift, especially near the ground.

In the H130, crosswinds are common at coastal airports like Navegantes and Florianópolis.

You must know your crosswind limits, request the most suitable runway when needed, and communicate clearly with ATC.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 operational communication.

## 📖 Meaning

A Crosswind Landing is a landing performed with a wind component from the side of the runway or landing area, requiring correction for drift.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pouso com vento cruzado.

Pouso realizado com componente de vento lateral em relação à pista ou área de pouso, exigindo correção de deriva.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a Crosswind Landing is required, the pilot should:

- calculate or estimate the crosswind component
- confirm it is within aircraft and personal limits
- request an into-wind runway or helipad when available
- use appropriate crab or slip technique to touchdown
- go around if the crosswind exceeds safe control

Crosswind Landing reporting commonly includes:

- request runway most into wind
- unable to accept crosswind, request other runway
- going around due crosswind
- wind check on final

**Important:** Know your maximum demonstrated or approved crosswind. Exceeding it is not professionalism — it is risk.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC supports Crosswind Landings by:

- providing current wind direction and speed
- assigning the most suitable runway when traffic permits
- approving runway changes for wind
- sequencing go-arounds when crosswind is excessive
- advising gusty crosswind conditions

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are approaching Navegantes in your H130.

Tower reports wind two zero zero at one eight knots for runway two nine — a significant crosswind.

You assess and transmit:

Navegantes Tower, ANAC123, request runway one eight, more into wind.

Tower reassigns runway one eight.

You land with a manageable headwind component instead of a strong crosswind.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, wind two zero zero at one eight knots, runway two nine, cleared to land.
2. Tower: ANAC123, runway one eight available, advise.
3. Tower: ANAC123, wind check, two one zero at two zero knots, gusting two five.
4. Tower: ANAC123, roger request runway change, cleared runway one eight.
5. Tower: ANAC123, crosswind landing in use, caution gusts.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Navegantes Tower, ANAC123, request runway one eight, more into wind.
2. ANAC123, unable to accept crosswind on runway two nine, request other runway.
3. ANAC123, going around due crosswind, request another approach.
4. Tower, ANAC123, wind check on final.
5. ANAC123, cleared to land runway one eight, wind copied.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Crosswind landing is math and technique.

Wind angle. Wind speed. Component. Limit.

The sequence:

Check wind. Compare to limits. Request better runway if needed. Land or go around.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "vento cruzado forte" on frequency.

Use crosswind or request runway more into wind — clear operational English.

In the H130, coastal airports often give you a choice — ask for it.

Do not accept a crosswind beyond your limit to "help the flow."

Train the request for an into-wind runway until it feels normal.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if the assigned runway had an excessive crosswind?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report unable to accept the crosswind on the assigned runway and request a runway more into wind. If no suitable runway was available and the crosswind exceeded limits, I would go around or divert to an alternate with a more favorable wind.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Accepting any runway without checking the crosswind component.  
  ✔ Calculate or estimate the component against your limits.

- ❌ Saying "pista ruim de vento" instead of a clear request.  
  ✔ Use request runway more into wind or unable to accept crosswind.

- ❌ Not going around when control margins disappear.  
  ✔ Go around — do not force a crosswind landing beyond limits.

- ❌ Forgetting to request a wind check on final.  
  ✔ Ask for wind check when gusts are reported.

Tower clears you to land runway two nine with wind two zero zero at two zero knots at Navegantes.

What do you request if this exceeds your crosswind comfort or limit?

**Your second task**

If no other runway is available and the crosswind is excessive, what do you do?

I would request a runway more into wind, for example runway one eight. If no suitable runway is available and the crosswind exceeds limits, I would go around or divert to an alternate with a more favorable wind.
