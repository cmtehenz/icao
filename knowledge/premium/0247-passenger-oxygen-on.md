# Passenger Oxygen On

**Catalog ID:** 0247  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Passenger Oxygen On — the confirmation that the passenger oxygen system is supplying oxygen after mask deployment or manual activation, typically during cabin altitude emergencies on pressurized transport aircraft. The H130 does not carry airline-style passenger oxygen drop systems; this lesson still teaches ICAO exam English and maps the same radio discipline to reporting oxygen status, cabin condition, and intentions.

"Passenger oxygen on" is a status phrase examiners and ATC expect after masks deploy. It tells the controller that the cabin is being protected while the crew manages descent and diversion.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master the phrase, then always be ready to transfer the template to crew oxygen status and any cabin environmental emergency you may face in helicopter operations.

Understanding how to report passenger oxygen status in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Passenger Oxygen On is the confirmation that the passenger oxygen system is activated and delivering oxygen to cabin occupants following mask deployment or system selection, usually associated with high cabin altitude, requiring continued emergency procedures, ATC updates on oxygen and cabin status, and priority handling as required.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Oxigênio dos passageiros ligado / Passenger Oxygen On.

Confirmação de que o sistema de oxigênio dos passageiros está ativado e fornecendo oxigênio aos ocupantes da cabine após a liberação das máscaras ou seleção do sistema, geralmente associado a altitude de cabine elevada, exigindo continuidade dos procedimentos de emergência, atualizações ao ATC sobre oxigênio e estado da cabine, e tratamento prioritário conforme necessário.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When passenger oxygen is on, the crew should:

- confirm passenger oxygen system activation per QRH/AFM
- ensure crew oxygen is also established and communication is clear
- continue emergency descent or level-off as required by cabin altitude
- update ATC: passenger oxygen on, cabin status, intentions
- request priority landing or diversion if not already approved
- for H130 training: practice status reporting for any oxygen or cabin environmental event

Passenger oxygen reporting commonly includes:

- passenger oxygen on / passenger oxygen deployed and flowing
- cabin altitude high / decreasing / under control
- continuing emergency descent
- request priority / diversion
- souls and fuel when asked

**Important:** "Passenger oxygen on" is a status update, not a complete emergency declaration by itself. Pair it with Mayday or Pan Pan context, descent status, and landing intentions. On the ICAO test, use the exact phrase; in H130 ops, transfer the status-reporting habit to relevant systems.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use passenger oxygen status reports to:

- confirm the cabin is receiving oxygen protection
- assess whether the emergency is stabilizing or worsening
- continue priority sequencing for emergency descent and landing
- coordinate medical support if hypoxia exposure is likely
- update emergency services on cabin condition before arrival

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Exam-style scenario: after oxygen masks deploy, you confirm the passenger oxygen system is supplying oxygen and continue the emergency descent.

You update ATC:

ANAC123, passenger oxygen on, cabin altitude decreasing, continuing emergency descent, request priority vectors.

Approach acknowledges and clears you for a straight-in. You later report cabin altitude under control and continue for landing with emergency services standing by.

For H130 reality, you may report crew oxygen or cabin condition instead — keep the clear status-update structure identical.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger, confirm passenger oxygen on and say cabin altitude status.
2. Control: ANAC123, continue emergency descent, report passenger oxygen and cabin under control.
3. Approach: ANAC123, priority handling approved, expect runway two seven straight-in.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.
5. Tower: ANAC123, medical services advised, report if any passengers require assistance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Passenger oxygen on is a status sentence. It tells ATC the cabin is protected while you fly the emergency.

After masks deploy, do not go silent. Confirm oxygen is flowing. Say passenger oxygen on. Say whether cabin altitude is high, decreasing, or under control. Say what you need next.

Silence after a Mayday is dangerous. Controllers plan around your updates. A short, clear oxygen status keeps the whole system working with you.

On the oral exam, examiners listen for exact vocabulary. On the line in an H130, they listen for the same discipline applied to whatever system is actually failing.

Status. Trend. Intention. That is professional emergency English.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

After oxygen masks deploy, how do you update ATC regarding passenger oxygen, and what other information should you include?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would confirm that the passenger oxygen system is activated and then update ATC that passenger oxygen is on. I would also report cabin altitude status — high, decreasing, or under control — and state whether we are continuing an emergency descent and requesting priority landing. I would provide souls and fuel when asked. On a helicopter without a passenger oxygen drop system, I would use the same clear status structure for crew oxygen or cabin environmental emergencies.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "oxygen of passengers is working" instead of "passenger oxygen on."  
  ✔ Use ICAO-standard "passenger oxygen on."

- ❌ Reporting oxygen status without cabin altitude trend.  
  ✔ Pair oxygen status with cabin altitude high / decreasing / under control.

- ❌ Treating "passenger oxygen on" as the end of communication.  
  ✔ Continue updates through descent and approach.

- ❌ Ignoring the phrase because the H130 has no passenger mask system.  
  ✔ Learn it for ICAO exams; transfer the status habit to real helicopter emergencies.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Passenger Oxygen On, Cabin Altitude Decreasing

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, PAS-en-jer OK-si-jen ON, KAB-in AL-ti-tood dee-KREE-sing

**Word Stress**

- Passenger → PAS-en-jer (stress on PAS)
- Oxygen → OK-si-jen
- Cabin → KAB-in
- Decreasing → dee-KREE-sing (stress on KREE)

Practice:

Passenger oxygen on... cabin altitude decreasing.

Together:

ANAC123, passenger oxygen on, cabin altitude decreasing, continuing emergency descent.

Masks have deployed. You confirm passenger oxygen is flowing. Cabin altitude is still high but starting to decrease.

What status update do you transmit?
