# TAF

**Catalog ID:** 0378  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers TAF — Terminal Aerodrome Forecast — the forecast of weather conditions expected at an aerodrome for a defined validity period, including wind, visibility, weather, and cloud, with change groups such as BECMG, TEMPO, and FM.

TAF is how you plan fuel, alternate, and departure time. For H130 Brazilian ops — afternoon TEMPO thunderstorms, morning fog BECMG, coastal wind shifts — misreading a TAF creates airborne surprises that Part 2 must then fix with holds and diversions.

Understanding TAF structure and discussing forecast weather in ICAO English supports Part 4 comprehension and Part 2 decision radio calls.

## 📖 Meaning

A TAF is a terminal aerodrome forecast that predicts wind, visibility, significant weather, and cloud conditions at an airport for a specified period, using coded change indicators to describe expected improvements, deteriorations, or temporary fluctuations during the validity window.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

TAF / Previsão de Tempo para Aeródromo.

Previsão terminal de aeródromo que prediz vento, visibilidade, tempo significativo e condições de nuvens em um aeroporto por um período especificado, usando indicadores de mudança codificados para descrever melhoras, pioras ou flutuações temporárias esperadas durante a validade.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When using a TAF, the crew should:

- check validity period against ETA and alternate ETAs
- decode base forecast and all FM / BECMG / TEMPO / PROB groups
- plan fuel and alternate for the worst TEMPO within the approach window
- delay departure if TEMPO thunderstorms or fog cover ETA with no legal alternate
- update with METAR in flight — TAF is forecast, METAR is truth
- explain diversion or delay to ATC using forecast language when helpful

TAF planning commonly includes:

- TEMPO TSRA at ETA — carry fuel for hold or leave earlier/later
- BECMG fog — plan arrival after improvement or divert
- FM wind shift — brief runway change
- PROB30 — treat as possible; do not ignore for helicopters with tight fuel

**Important:** TEMPO means temporary fluctuations, not "ignore." If TEMPO puts the airport below minima at your ETA, you need a plan — hold fuel, delay, or alternate. Brazilian TAFs often TEMPO thunderstorms in the wet season; brief them seriously for H130 fuel margins.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use TAF-informed expectations to:

- anticipate holding for forecast deterioration
- plan airport acceptance rates around TEMPO storms
- understand diversion requests before METAR falls
- advise expected improvement times when known
- coordinate flow when multiple aircraft share the same forecast problem

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

TAF for Congonhas shows TEMPO 1800/2100 visibility 1 000 meters in thunderstorms, ceiling 500 feet, around your ETA 19:00. You have limited hold fuel in the H130.

You delay departure ninety minutes, or you launch with a firm alternate and:

São Paulo Approach, ANAC123, requesting delay / hold due forecast TEMPO thunderstorms Congonhas, advise when weather improves.

If already inbound and storms arrive:

ANAC123, diverting Guarulhos due weather, request vectors.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

TAF is a story about the future airport. Read every chapter — especially TEMPO.

Pilots who only read the first line land in the TEMPO.

For the H130, fuel is the limiting chapter. You cannot hold like a jet. If the TAF TEMPO is below minima at ETA, either change ETA, carry a real alternate, or stay on the ground.

Part 4 exams ask BECMG vs TEMPO vs FM. Part 2 flights punish those who cannot apply them.

Brazilian tip: afternoon TAF TEMPO TS is normal in summer. Plan the day around it, not through it.

METAR updates the story in real time. When they disagree, fly the METAR and keep the alternate from the TAF.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is a TAF and how do TEMPO groups affect your planning?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A TAF is the forecast for an aerodrome over a validity period, including wind, visibility, weather, and clouds. TEMPO groups describe temporary fluctuations that may be better or worse than the base forecast. For planning I assume the TEMPO conditions can exist at my ETA, so if TEMPO is below landing minima I plan extra fuel for holding, a delayed departure, or a suitable alternate. In the H130 I am especially careful because holding fuel is limited. I always cross-check the TAF with the latest METAR before committing to an approach.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Ignoring TEMPO because it is "temporary."  
  ✔ Temporary can last long enough to miss your approach window — plan for it.

- ❌ Confusing BECMG (lasting change) with TEMPO (fluctuation).  
  ✔ BECMG = new lasting condition; TEMPO = temporary periods.

- ❌ No alternate when TAF is marginal at ETA.  
  ✔ Marginal TAF demands a legal, reachable alternate for the H130.

- ❌ Unable to explain TAF vs METAR in speaking tests.  
  ✔ TAF = forecast; METAR = observation now.

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, reh-KWEST-ing HOLD dyoo FOR-kast THUN-der-stormz

**Word Stress**

- TAF → TAFF (one syllable)
- TEMPO → TEM-po
- Forecast → FOR-kast
- Becoming → bih-KUM-ing (BECMG)

Requesting hold... due forecast TEMPO thunderstorms... advise endurance.

ANAC123, requesting hold due forecast TEMPO thunderstorms at destination.

TAF TEMPO at your ETA: visibility 800 m, OVC005. Minima are higher. Hold fuel is thirty minutes.

**Your first task**

What do you tell ATC if already airborne?

**Your second task**

Explain BECMG versus TEMPO.

First task: ANAC123, destination forecast below minima at ETA, requesting hold with thirty minutes endurance or diversion to alternate.
