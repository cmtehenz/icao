# Dual Engine Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0419  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Dual Engine Failure — simultaneous loss of both engines on a twin-engine helicopter, requiring autorotation or forced landing and immediate mayday communication.

Dual engine failure is a total power emergency. ICAO helicopter English exams and Brazilian multi-engine operations demand crisp distress phraseology even when your daily type is the single-engine H130.

For ICAO Delta pilots operating in Brazil — including those who fly or train toward twin types — you must declare mayday, state dual engine failure, give position, and communicate landing intentions while flying the autorotation.

Understanding how to report dual engine failure in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Dual Engine Failure is the loss of power from both engines on a multi-engine helicopter, leaving no powered thrust and requiring immediate entry into autorotation or a forced landing profile with distress communication to ATC.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha dos dois motores / falha dupla de motor.

Perda de potência de ambos os motores em um helicóptero multimotor, sem empuxo motorizado, exigindo entrada imediata em autorrotação ou perfil de pouso forçado com comunicação de emergência ao ATC.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When dual engine failure occurs, the crew should:

- enter autorotation / forced landing profile immediately per AFM
- declare mayday with dual engine failure and position
- select and fly toward the best landing area
- transmit souls and fuel only when the flight path allows
- report on ground when able

Dual engine failure reporting commonly includes:

- mayday, dual engine failure
- autorotating / forced landing
- position and altitude
- on ground, status

**Important:** The H130 is single-engine; total power loss there is a single engine failure into autorotation. Dual engine failure phraseology remains exam-critical for twin-engine helicopter operations common in Brazilian commercial fleets.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to dual engine failure maydays to:

- clear all conflicting traffic immediately
- alert emergency services to the intended landing area
- provide wind and nearest suitable sites if requested
- keep the frequency clear during autorotation
- locate the aircraft for rescue after landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are training dual-engine emergency English for Brazilian twin operations while building ICAO Delta proficiency. Scenario: both engines fail ten miles offshore from Santos at three thousand feet.

Mayday mayday mayday, Santos Approach, dual engine failure, autorotating, one zero miles east of Santos, three thousand, ditching if necessary.

Approach clears traffic, provides wind, and alerts SAR. You fly the profile and transmit on water or on ground when able.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, mayday acknowledged, all stations, dual engine failure, say position.
2. Approach: ANAC123, wind two two zero at one five, nearest land your nine o'clock eight miles.
3. Approach: ANAC123, SAR alerted, squawk seven seven zero zero if able.
4. Approach: ANAC123, souls on board and fuel remaining when able.
5. Approach: ANAC123, roger ditching, rescue assets inbound.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, mayday, dual engine failure, autorotating, one zero miles east Santos.
2. ANAC123, wind copied, proceeding toward land if able.
3. ANAC123, squawking seven seven zero zero.
4. ANAC123, souls six, fuel not applicable, both engines failed.
5. ANAC123, on water, aircraft floating, request immediate rescue.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Dual engine failure English is autorotation English.

One mayday. Nature. Position. Intention. Then silence while you fly the flare.

In Brazilian offshore and coastal twin operations, water may be the only option. Say ditching if that is the plan so SAR starts correctly.

For H130 pilots in ICAO Delta: treat total power loss the same way — mayday, engine failure, autorotating, position. The dual-engine label is the twin-specific form of the same emergency discipline.

Do not waste altitude explaining why both engines quit. Fly Nr. Fly the spot. Speak facts.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Both engines fail on a twin-engine helicopter. What do you transmit?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare: Mayday mayday mayday, dual engine failure, autorotating, with position and altitude, and intentions such as forced landing or ditching. I would squawk 7700 if able and give souls when workload allows. Flying the autorotation comes before long radio exchanges.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Long explanation of engine indications during the descent.  
  ✔ Mayday, dual engine failure, autorotating, position.

- ❌ Forgetting position in the offshore mayday.  
  ✔ Position is what launches SAR to the right place.

- ❌ Using pan-pan for total power loss.  
  ✔ Dual engine failure is mayday.

- ❌ Blocking the frequency after the initial call.  
  ✔ Fly; answer briefly when ATC asks.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Dual engine failure ten miles east of Santos at three thousand feet.

**Your first task**

Give the mayday call.

**Your second task**

Approach asks for souls while you are in the flare. What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday mayday mayday, Santos Approach, ANAC123, dual engine failure, autorotating, one zero miles east Santos, three thousand. Second task: Prioritize the flare — if needed say ANAC123 stand by, then report souls after touchdown or when the flight path is stable. Aviate first in the final seconds.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Dual engine failure ends powered flight. It must not end clear communication.

Mayday. Dual engine failure. Autorotating. Position.

Whether you fly twins on the Brazilian coast or train the phraseology in ICAO Delta beside the H130, the discipline is the same: fly Nr, pick the spot, speak once, land.

Power is gone. Clarity must remain.
