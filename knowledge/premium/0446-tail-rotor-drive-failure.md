# Tail Rotor Drive Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0446  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Tail Rotor Drive Failure — complete or partial loss of drive to the tail rotor, resulting in loss of anti-torque control and potentially violent uncommanded yaw.

This is not LTE. LTE is insufficient thrust in certain conditions. Tail rotor drive failure is a mechanical loss of the anti-torque system itself.

For H130 helicopter operations, recognition must be immediate: enter the appropriate emergency procedure, establish a controllable flight path or autorotation as required, and declare Mayday without delay.

Understanding how to report tail rotor drive failure in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Tail Rotor Drive Failure is a mechanical failure of the drive system that powers the tail rotor, causing loss or severe reduction of anti-torque thrust and directional control.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha do acionamento do rotor de cauda.

Falha mecânica do sistema que aciona o rotor de cauda, causando perda ou redução severa do empuxo antitorque e do controle direcional.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When tail rotor drive fails, the pilot should:

- recognize uncommanded yaw and loss of pedal effectiveness immediately
- enter the AFM emergency procedure for the flight regime — hover, low speed, or cruise
- reduce torque as required and establish a controllable descent or autorotation
- select a suitable landing area aligned with the aircraft's yaw behavior
- declare Mayday as soon as control actions allow

Tail rotor drive failure reporting commonly includes:

- tail rotor drive failure or loss of anti-torque
- entering emergency landing or autorotation
- position and request clear landing area
- souls on board when able

**Important:** Aviate first. A perfect Mayday call means nothing if the aircraft is not under control. Transmit when you can — but fly the procedure first.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to tail rotor drive failure to:

- clear all traffic from the aircraft's path and landing area
- keep the frequency clear for the distressed aircraft
- coordinate fire and rescue to the expected landing site
- provide wind information useful for a run-on or special landing
- avoid any instruction that conflicts with the emergency maneuver

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are in cruise at one thousand five hundred feet in your H130 approaching the coast near Santos.

A loud noise is followed by sudden right yaw. Pedals are ineffective. You reduce throttle and collective per procedure, establish a controllable descent toward an open field, and transmit Mayday when able.

Approach clears the frequency and traffic. You complete an emergency run-on landing into wind as published for the failure mode, shut down, and report on the ground.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Mayday, tail rotor drive failure, say position and souls on board.
2. Approach: ANAC123, all traffic clear, wind two two zero at one zero, land at your discretion.
3. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services rolling to your reported field.
4. Approach: ANAC123, confirm landing site and cancel Mayday when able.
5. Tower: ANAC123, wind information two three zero at one two, report on ground.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Tail rotor drive failure is one of the most demanding helicopter emergencies.

You lose the ability to cancel main rotor torque with pedals. The aircraft will yaw. Your response depends on airspeed and altitude — and it must match the AFM, not improvisation.

On the H130, know the procedure cold for hover, transition, and cruise. Reduce torque. Use airspeed and collective as the procedure directs. Aim for a suitable surface for a run-on landing when that is the published technique.

Do not confuse this with LTE. LTE may recover with pedal and airspeed. Drive failure will not restore pedal authority.

Communicate Mayday when control actions allow. ATC needs position and the nature of the failure so rescue goes to the right place.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You lose tail rotor drive in cruise and establish a controllable emergency descent. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, tail rotor drive failure, emergency landing three miles east of Santos, souls on board four. I would fly the procedure first and transmit as soon as control allows.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Transmitting a long explanation before flying the emergency procedure.  
  ✔ Aviate first; Mayday when able.

- ❌ Calling the event LTE when pedals are completely ineffective after a mechanical bang.  
  ✔ Report tail rotor drive failure or loss of anti-torque.

- ❌ Trying to hover to a spot landing against the procedure.  
  ✔ Follow the AFM technique for the failure and flight regime.

- ❌ Forgetting to report on the ground so rescue can stand down or proceed.  
  ✔ Report on the ground and cancel or update Mayday.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Tail rotor drive failure is pure priority: fly the aircraft, then talk.

Know the procedure for every regime. Reduce torque. Choose the landing. Speak Mayday.

On the H130, anti-torque is not optional. When drive fails, procedure and a clear Mayday are what bring the crew home.
