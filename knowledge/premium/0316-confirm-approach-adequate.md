# Confirm Approach Adequate

**Catalog ID:** 0316  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Confirm Approach Adequate — the ATC or operational check that the assigned or available approach is suitable for the aircraft, weather, and crew capability, and the phraseology used to confirm or decline it. Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: accept only an approach you can fly safely, and say so clearly.

Confirm approach adequate is Medium priority in the catalog and High importance whenever minima, equipment, or helicopter procedures do not match the offered approach.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for accepting, declining, or requesting an alternate approach under DECEA/ICAO procedures.

Understanding how to confirm approach adequacy in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Confirm Approach Adequate is a request or self-check to verify that a particular instrument or visual approach is suitable for the aircraft type, equipment, weather minima, and operational constraints, resulting in acceptance, a request for a different approach, or a diversion if no adequate approach is available.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Confirmar se a aproximação é adequada / Confirm Approach Adequate.

Solicitação ou verificação para confirmar que determinada aproximação por instrumentos ou visual é adequada ao tipo de aeronave, equipamento, mínimos meteorológicos e restrições operacionais, resultando em aceitação, solicitação de outra aproximação ou desvio se nenhuma aproximação adequada estiver disponível.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When confirming whether an approach is adequate, the crew should:

- check weather against minima for that approach and crew currency
- verify aircraft equipment and database support the procedure
- for H130: confirm the approach and missed approach are compatible with helicopter performance and category
- accept with a clear readback, or request another approach if inadequate
- divert if no suitable approach exists at the destination
- never accept an approach you cannot legally or safely fly to avoid delaying ATC

Approach-adequacy exchanges commonly include:

- confirm approach adequate / approach acceptable
- unable [approach], request [alternative]
- request ILS / RNAV / visual approach
- approach not suitable due weather / equipment
- diverting, no adequate approach

**Important:** "Unable" is professional. Accepting an inadequate approach to be cooperative is not.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers confirm approach adequacy to:

- assign a procedure the aircraft can actually fly
- avoid late go-arounds caused by unsuitable clearances
- offer alternatives when weather or equipment limits options
- coordinate helicopter-specific or visual arrivals when needed
- plan diversions early when no adequate approach remains

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 inbound to an airport where Approach offers an ILS. Your equipment or minima do not support that procedure today.

Approach: ANAC123, confirm ILS runway one four adequate.

You reply:

ANAC123, unable ILS one four, request RNAV runway one four.

Or, if weather is below all available minima:

ANAC123, no adequate approach available, diverting to [alternate], request routing.

ATC adapts. The frequency stays honest. The approach you fly is the one you can complete.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, confirm approach adequate.
2. Approach: ANAC123, confirm ILS runway one four acceptable.
3. Approach: ANAC123, say if able RNAV approach.
4. Control: ANAC123, confirm visual approach adequate.
5. Approach: ANAC123, advise if approach not suitable.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

2. ANAC123, unable ILS one four, request RNAV one four.
3. ANAC123, visual approach acceptable.
4. ANAC123, approach not suitable due weather, request hold.
5. ANAC123, no adequate approach, diverting, request routing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Approach adequacy is a go/no-go judgment spoken in radio words.

If it is adequate, say affirmative and fly it. If not, say unable and request what you need. In the H130, not every fixed-wing-centric procedure is the best or even usable option — know your AFM, minima, and company limitations before you accept.

Part 2 examiners respect a clean unable. They do not respect a polite acceptance followed by an inevitable mess inside the procedure.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC offers an approach that is below your minima or not supported by your equipment. How do you respond?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would state unable for that approach and request an alternative procedure that is adequate for the aircraft and weather, or request holding while assessing options. If no adequate approach is available at the destination, I would divert to a suitable alternate and request routing. I would not accept an approach I cannot legally or safely complete.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Accepting an approach "to take a look" below minima.  
  ✔ Do not begin an approach you are not prepared to fly to minima rules.

- ❌ Saying only "negative" without an alternative request.  
  ✔ State unable and request another approach or hold/divert.

- ❌ Discovering equipment limits after already established.  
  ✔ Confirm adequacy when the approach is offered.

- ❌ Confusing "adequate" with "convenient."  
  ✔ Adequate means safe, legal, and supported — not merely preferred.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**ADEQUATE** — **A**ssess minima and equipment, **D**ecline if unsuitable, **E**xpress unable clearly, **Q**uery alternatives, **U**se hold if needed, **A**lternate divert when none work, **T**ell intentions, **E**xecute only what you accepted.
