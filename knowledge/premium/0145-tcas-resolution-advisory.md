# TCAS Resolution Advisory

**Catalog ID:** 0145  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers TCAS Resolution Advisory — the automated collision avoidance instruction issued by the Traffic Collision Avoidance System, and the pilot procedures and phraseology required to respond correctly.

A TCAS Resolution Advisory (RA) is the highest-priority traffic conflict alert in modern aviation.

When a TCAS RA activates, one rule overrides all others: follow TCAS, not ATC.

In the H130, if equipped with TCAS II, the system may issue an RA while operating in the São Paulo TMA, the Curitiba terminal area, or any congested airspace.

The correct ICAO response — immediate compliance and ATC notification — is the subject of this critical lesson.

## 📖 Meaning

A TCAS Resolution Advisory is an automated guidance command issued by the airborne collision avoidance system directing the pilot to immediately execute a specific vertical maneuver — climb, descend, or maintain — to resolve a mid-air collision threat.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Aviso de resolução TCAS (RA).

Comando de orientação automático emitido pelo sistema aerotransportado de prevenção de colisão, instruindo o piloto a executar imediatamente uma manobra vertical específica — subir, descer ou manter — para resolver uma ameaça de colisão.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a TCAS Resolution Advisory is received, the pilot must:

- immediately follow the TCAS RA command — climb or descend as directed
- notify ATC immediately: ANAC123, TCAS RA
- do not wait for ATC clearance before executing the RA maneuver
- maintain the RA maneuver until the TCAS clear-of-conflict advisory is issued
- advise ATC when the RA is resolved and returning to cleared altitude

The TCAS RA sequence:

1. TCAS issues RA — auditory and visual command on EFIS or VSI
2. Pilot executes immediately — no hesitation
3. Pilot notifies ATC — ANAC123, TCAS RA
4. ATC acknowledges — does not countermand the RA
5. TCAS issues clear-of-conflict — pilot returns to cleared altitude
6. Pilot advises ATC — ANAC123, clear of conflict, returning flight level eight zero

**Important:** If ATC issues an instruction contrary to the TCAS RA, follow the TCAS RA. ATC does not see the RA on their screen in real time. The TCAS geometry is computed faster than ATC radar updates. TCAS has priority. Inform ATC of the conflict and comply with TCAS.

## 🎯 Why This Matters

The TCAS Resolution Advisory exists because:

- mid-air collisions can occur within seconds of detection
- ATC radar update rates are insufficient for last-second avoidance
- TCAS coordinates between both aircraft simultaneously
- both aircraft receive complementary RAs — one climbs, one descends
- deviation from the RA breaks the coordinated geometry and may cause the collision TCAS is trying to prevent

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are at flight level eight zero between São Paulo and Curitiba in your H130.

TCAS activates: auditory alert — "CLIMB, CLIMB, CLIMB."

The vertical speed indicator commands a climb.

You immediately advance the collective and climb.

You transmit:

São Paulo Control, ANAC123, TCAS RA, climbing.

Control responds:

ANAC123, roger, TCAS RA.

TCAS announces: "Clear of conflict."

You level off and transmit:

ANAC123, clear of conflict, returning flight level eight zero.

Control: ANAC123, roger, resume flight level eight zero, advise when established.

From RA to clear-of-conflict: approximately twenty-five seconds.

Your compliance kept both aircraft separated. The other aircraft received a complementary descent RA.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger, TCAS RA. Report clear of conflict.
2. Control: ANAC123, roger, maintain own separation, advise returning cleared altitude.
3. Control: ANAC123, TCAS RA acknowledged, confirm returning flight level eight zero.
4. Control: ANAC123, roger, what altitude are you now?
5. Control: ANAC123, resume flight level eight zero, cleared.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

TCAS Resolution Advisory is the highest-authority command in the cockpit.

Higher than ATC.

Higher than your cleared altitude.

Higher than standard operating procedure.

When the box says CLIMB — you climb.

When the box says DESCEND — you descend.

You transmit simultaneously: ANAC123, TCAS RA, climbing — or descending.

You do not ask ATC for permission.

You do not wait.

You do not second-guess.

The RA was computed using the threat aircraft's position and your position simultaneously. Both aircraft receive coordinated commands. If you deviate from the RA, the geometry breaks. The collision risk increases.

Brazilian pilots sometimes hesitate because they feel they are violating the ATC clearance.

You are. That is correct. ICAO mandates it.

ICAO Doc 4444 states that pilots must follow TCAS RA even if it conflicts with ATC instructions.

Follow TCAS. Notify ATC. Return when clear.

In the H130 at lower altitudes, TCAS TAs are more common than RAs. But an RA can activate even at three thousand feet if the geometry demands it.

Train this response until it is automatic.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What do you do if TCAS issues a Resolution Advisory while ATC is giving you a conflicting instruction?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I follow the TCAS Resolution Advisory immediately and notify ATC: ANAC123, TCAS RA, climbing — or descending. I do not follow ATC instructions that conflict with the RA. ICAO requires pilots to comply with TCAS RA even when ATC instructions differ, because TCAS has computed the maneuver to maintain separation based on real-time geometry. When TCAS issues clear-of-conflict, I report to ATC and return to my cleared altitude.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Hesitating to climb or descend because the RA conflicts with ATC clearance.  
  ✔ Follow TCAS immediately. Notify ATC simultaneously. TCAS has priority.

- ❌ Asking ATC for permission before executing the RA maneuver.  
  ✔ Execute first. Notify simultaneously. Do not wait for ATC approval.

- ❌ Not notifying ATC after following the RA.  
  ✔ Always transmit: ANAC123, TCAS RA, climbing — the moment you begin the maneuver.

- ❌ Returning to cleared altitude before TCAS issues clear-of-conflict.  
  ✔ Maintain the RA until TCAS says clear. Then return and advise ATC.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are at flight level nine zero between Florianópolis and São Paulo in your H130. TCAS activates: "DESCEND, DESCEND, DESCEND." At the same moment, Curitiba Control transmits: "ANAC123, climb flight level one zero zero, traffic in your area."

**Your first task**

What do you do? Do you climb per ATC or descend per TCAS?

**Your second task**

TCAS announces: "Clear of conflict." What do you transmit to ATC and what do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I follow TCAS. I descend and transmit: Curitiba Control, ANAC123, TCAS RA, descending — unable climb. ATC will acknowledge without countermanding the RA. When TCAS clears, I transmit: ANAC123, clear of conflict, returning flight level nine zero, request confirm. ATC then clears me back to the original altitude. I never climb against a TCAS descent RA, even when ATC instructs a climb.
