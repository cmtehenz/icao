# Say Heading

**Catalog ID:** 0320  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Say Heading — the ATC request for the crew to report the current magnetic heading. Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: read the heading indicator correctly and report three-digit headings clearly.

Say heading is Medium priority because heading reports confirm vectors, detect navigation errors, and support radar identification and sequencing.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for heading reports under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including turning reports when still in a turn.

Understanding how to say heading in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Say Heading is an ATC request for the pilot to report the aircraft's current magnetic heading, normally as three digits, so that ATC can verify vector compliance, confirm orientation, and maintain an accurate traffic picture.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Informe a proa / Say Heading.

Solicitação do controle para que o piloto informe a proa magnética atual da aeronave, normalmente em três dígitos, para que o ATC verifique o cumprimento de vetores, confirme a orientação e mantenha um quadro de tráfego preciso.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC says say heading, the crew should:

- read magnetic heading from the HSI / heading indicator
- report as three digits: heading two seven zero
- add turning left/right to [heading] if still in the turn
- for H130: heading can change quickly in the circuit — report present heading, not an old one
- distinguish heading from track or course when speaking
- read back assigned headings as clearances; report present heading when asked to say heading

Heading exchanges commonly include:

- say heading / confirm heading
- heading [three digits]
- turning left/right heading [digits]
- fly heading [digits] / continue present heading
- present heading

**Important:** Report present heading when asked to say heading. Do not report the assigned heading until you are established on it, unless ATC asks you to confirm the assigned heading.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers request heading to:

- verify that radar vectors are being flown
- identify aircraft when radar labels are ambiguous
- correct wrong-way turns early
- plan the next vector from your actual orientation
- support sequencing in busy Brazilian terminal radar environments

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 under São Paulo Approach radar vectors.

Approach: ANAC123, say heading.

You reply:

ANAC123, heading two seven zero.

If you are still turning to zero nine zero:

ANAC123, turning right heading zero nine zero.

Approach uses that to time the next vector. A late or invented heading report breaks the vector plan. In the H130 the nose moves fast — glance at the instrument, then speak.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, say heading.
2. Radar: ANAC123, confirm heading.
3. Approach: ANAC123, say present heading.
4. Tower: ANAC123, confirm heading on downwind.
5. Radar: ANAC123, fly heading three six zero, say heading when established.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, heading two seven zero.
2. ANAC123, turning left heading one eight zero.
3. ANAC123, present heading zero nine zero.
4. ANAC123, heading three six zero, established.
5. ANAC123, flying heading two five zero.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Heading reports are orientation in three digits.

Look at the compass rose. Say heading, then the digits. If you are turning, say turning and the target. Do not give track from the GPS when ATC asked for heading.

In Brazilian radar environments, vectors are constant for the H130. Part 2 wants crisp heading calls and crisp readbacks of assigned headings — and the discipline to know which one ATC asked for.

Present heading when asked to say. Assigned heading when reading back a vector.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC asks you to say heading while you are turning right from two seven zero to zero nine zero. What do you report?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report that I am turning right to heading zero nine zero, or give the present heading if already passing a specific value and still turning. Once established, I would report heading zero nine zero. I would report magnetic heading from the heading indicator, not GPS track, unless ATC specifically asks for track.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting GPS track when asked for heading.  
  ✔ Report magnetic heading from the heading indicator.

- ❌ Using two digits: "heading ninety."  
  ✔ Use three digits: heading zero nine zero.

- ❌ Reporting the assigned heading before the turn is complete when asked present heading.  
  ✔ Say turning to... or give the present value.

- ❌ Confusing say heading with a new vector clearance.  
  ✔ Say heading is a report request; fly heading is an instruction.

Approach asks for heading. You are turning right from two seven zero to zero nine zero.

**Your first task**

What do you say during the turn?

**Your second task**

You roll out on zero nine zero. Approach asks again. What do you say?

First task: ANAC123, turning right heading zero nine zero — or present heading if Approach wants the value now. Second task: ANAC123, heading zero nine zero. Use three-digit magnetic heading, not GPS track.
