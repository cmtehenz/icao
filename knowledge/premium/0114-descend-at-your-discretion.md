# Descend at Your Discretion

**Catalog ID:** 0114  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Descend at Your Discretion — one of the most powerful and misunderstood clearances in aviation.

Descend at Your Discretion authorizes the pilot to begin descent when the pilot judges appropriate, to a cleared lower altitude, without needing further ATC permission to start the descent.

In the H130, this clearance is issued in Brazilian en route and approach phases, particularly in Curitiba, São Paulo, and Congonhas area traffic.

You must understand what freedom it grants, what limits remain, and how to manage the descent professionally within the clearance.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and pilot discretion descent management.

## 📖 Meaning

Descend at Your Discretion is an ATC clearance authorizing the pilot to commence descent at a time of their choosing, to the cleared altitude, without further ATC approval to initiate the descent.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Desça a sua critério.

Autorização do controle para o piloto iniciar a descida quando julgar apropriado, para a altitude autorizada, sem necessitar de nova aprovação do controle para iniciar a descida.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Descend at Your Discretion is received, the pilot should:

- read back the cleared altitude
- begin descent when operationally appropriate
- not exceed the cleared lower altitude
- comply with any published altitude restrictions on the route
- advise ATC when established at the cleared altitude

Descend at Your Discretion commonly includes:

- descent initiation timing chosen by the pilot
- compliance with any intermediate altitude crossing restrictions
- speed management at pilot discretion during the descent
- terrain clearance responsibility during the descent

**Important:** Descend at your discretion does not remove altitude restrictions on published procedures. Crossing restrictions on STARs and approach transitions remain in effect unless explicitly cancelled. Check your charts before descending below any restriction.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC issues Descend at Your Discretion to:

- allow the pilot to optimize descent profile for fuel and comfort
- reduce radio workload on en route sectors
- allow performance-based descents such as continuous descent operations
- give flexibility during low traffic periods
- authorize descent without further coordination by ATC

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at ten thousand feet bound for Curitiba in your H130.

Center transmits:

ANAC123, descend to three thousand, at your discretion.

You plan a continuous descent for fuel efficiency and passenger comfort.

You read back:

Descend to three thousand at discretion, ANAC123.

You begin descent when the top of descent calculation indicates, crossing all published restrictions, and arrive at three thousand as planned.

You report:

ANAC123, three thousand.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Center: ANAC123, descend to three thousand, at your discretion.
2. Approach: ANAC123, descend to two thousand, pilot's discretion.
3. Center: ANAC123, when ready, descend to five thousand.
4. Approach: ANAC123, descend at your discretion, comply with STAR restrictions.
5. Center: ANAC123, cancel at your discretion, descend now to four thousand.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Descend to three thousand at discretion, ANAC123.
2. Descend to two thousand at discretion, ANAC123.
3. When ready, descend to five thousand, ANAC123.
4. Descend at discretion, STAR restrictions, ANAC123.
5. Descend to four thousand, leaving [current altitude], ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Pilot discretion is a freedom that demands judgment.

ATC is handing the timing to you. Use it wisely.

The sequence:

Receive clearance. Read back the altitude. Calculate top of descent. Check STAR restrictions. Descend when optimal.

Brazilian pilots sometimes treat this as permission to delay indefinitely — or to disregard published crossing restrictions.

Both are wrong.

Descend when operationally appropriate — not five minutes after you should have started.

In the H130, plan your descent so you arrive at the cleared altitude with energy managed.

Published STAR restrictions remain in force unless ATC specifically cancels them. Check the plate.

If ATC cancels the discretion with "descend now": comply immediately.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What restrictions remain in place when ATC clears you to descend at your discretion?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

When ATC clears me to descend at my discretion, I choose when to begin the descent but I must not descend below the cleared altitude and I must comply with all published altitude crossing restrictions on the STAR or transition unless ATC specifically cancels them. The clearance gives me timing discretion — it does not cancel published procedure constraints. I remain responsible for terrain clearance and procedure compliance throughout the descent.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating pilot discretion as permission to ignore STAR crossing restrictions.  
  ✔ Published restrictions remain in force unless ATC cancels them explicitly.

- ❌ Delaying the descent until too late for an efficient arrival.  
  ✔ Use the discretion operationally — descend at the optimal top of descent.

- ❌ Not reading back the cleared altitude.  
  ✔ Always confirm: Descend to [altitude] at discretion, ANAC123.

- ❌ Forgetting to report when established at the cleared altitude.  
  ✔ Report: ANAC123, [altitude] when reaching the cleared level.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Cruising at eight thousand feet to Curitiba, Center clears you: ANAC123, descend to two thousand, at your discretion, comply with STAR restrictions.

**Your first task**

What do you read back and what do you check before descending?

**Your second task**

At five thousand you realize you will bust a published crossing restriction of four thousand at ORTUS. What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would read back: Descend to two thousand at discretion, STAR restrictions, ANAC123. Before descending I would review all crossing restrictions on the STAR. If I realized I would bust the ORTUS crossing restriction I would adjust my rate of descent to cross at or above four thousand, or advise ATC: ANAC123, unable to meet ORTUS restriction, request amended clearance.
