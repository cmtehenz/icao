# Engine Overtorque

**Catalog ID:** 0454  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Engine Overtorque — torque exceeding published engine or transmission limits, indicating the powertrain is being loaded beyond design limits for the condition.

Overtorque stresses the engine, main gearbox, and drive train. Transient exceedances may require inspection; sustained or severe overtorque can lead to failure.

For H130 helicopter operations, the response is immediate: reduce collective to bring torque within limits, assess severity, land as required by the AFM, and communicate with ATC using the appropriate urgency.

Understanding how to report engine overtorque in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Engine Overtorque is a condition in which measured torque exceeds the maximum permitted limit for the engine or transmission, risking mechanical damage to the powertrain.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Sobretorque do motor (overtorque).

Condição em que o torque medido excede o limite máximo permitido para o motor ou a transmissão, com risco de dano mecânico ao conjunto de potência.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When engine overtorque occurs, the pilot should:

- reduce collective smoothly to bring torque within limits
- avoid abrupt power changes that could worsen the exceedance
- note peak torque and duration for maintenance
- land as soon as practicable, or as soon as possible for severe exceedance
- declare Pan Pan for precautionary landing after significant overtorque; Mayday if failure symptoms appear

Engine overtorque reporting commonly includes:

- engine overtorque or torque exceedance
- reducing power, request priority landing
- possible transmission stress, request inspection on ground
- souls on board when requested

**Important:** Record the value. Maintenance decision depends on how high and how long. Your radio call should get you on the ground; your tech log gets the engine inspected.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to engine overtorque to:

- provide priority landing without high-power demands
- avoid climbs, holds, or go-arounds that require high torque
- coordinate emergency services if drive train failure is possible
- request souls on board and intentions
- clear a simple approach path

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are departing a confined area near the Serra do Mar in your H130.

Torque spikes above the limit during the departure. You reduce collective as soon as clear of obstacles, and torque returns to the green. You note the peak reading.

You declare Pan Pan, report engine overtorque, and request priority landing at the nearest suitable airport for inspection. Approach provides routing. You land with conservative power and report the exceedance to maintenance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Torque is the load on the entire drive train.

When you overtorque, you may have already marked the gearbox and engine for inspection — even if everything feels normal afterward.

On the H130, reduce collective smoothly. Do not slam the power off and create a second problem with Nr. Once within limits, decide: how severe was the exceedance? The AFM and maintenance criteria drive whether you land ASAP or continue briefly to a suitable site.

Communicate. Pan Pan gets you priority without pretending the event was nothing. If vibration, noise, or pressure abnormalities follow — Mayday.

Never hide an overtorque from maintenance. The next crew inherits what you do not write down.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You overtorque on departure, then torque returns to normal with no vibration. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Pan Pan Pan Pan, Approach, engine overtorque on departure, torque now normal, request priority landing nearest suitable for inspection. I would state souls on board when asked and upgrade to Mayday if abnormal indications appear.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing the full mission after a significant overtorque with no report.  
  ✔ Land for inspection per AFM and company criteria.

- ❌ Reducing collective so abruptly that Nr exceeds limits.  
  ✔ Reduce smoothly while protecting rotor RPM.

- ❌ Saying "power exceedance" without torque.  
  ✔ State engine overtorque clearly.

- ❌ Accepting a high-power departure or go-around after overtorque.  
  ✔ Plan a conservative approach; avoid unnecessary high torque.

Overtorque is a mechanical insult — even when the gauges look calm afterward.

Reduce smoothly. Protect Nr. Land for inspection. Speak Pan Pan; speak Mayday if the airframe complains.
