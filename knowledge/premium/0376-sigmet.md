# SIGMET

**Catalog ID:** 0376  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers SIGMET — Significant Meteorological Information — warnings of weather phenomena that are potentially hazardous to all aircraft, such as severe turbulence, severe icing, volcanic ash, dust storms, and thunderstorms (including convective SIGMET-type products).

A SIGMET raises the weather decision from "monitor" to "act." For H130 operations in Brazil, a SIGMET for severe icing or severe turbulence on the route is a reason to delay, re-route, or divert — not to press and hope.

Clear English for SIGMET-related deviations, holds, and diversions is core Part 2 and Part 3 skill.

## 📖 Meaning

A SIGMET is a concise meteorological warning message describing the occurrence or expected occurrence of specified en-route weather phenomena that may affect the safety of aircraft operations, valid for a defined area and period, and addressed to all aircraft regardless of size or performance.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

SIGMET / Informação Meteorológica Significativa.

Mensagem meteorológica concisa descrevendo a ocorrência ou ocorrência prevista de fenômenos de tempo em rota especificados que podem afetar a segurança das operações aéreas, válida para uma área e período definidos, e dirigida a todas as aeronaves independentemente de porte ou desempenho.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a SIGMET is issued for your route or area, the crew should:

- identify phenomenon, flight levels, polygon/area, and valid time
- avoid the SIGMET area when the hazard is severe icing, severe turbulence, ash, or active convection
- request deviation, altitude change, hold, or diversion as required
- delay departure if the SIGMET covers climb-out or initial route
- report confirming PIREPs to strengthen ATC awareness
- treat volcanic ash SIGMETs as absolute avoidance for turbine engines

SIGMET responses commonly include:

- unable present routing due SIGMET, request deviation
- holding / delaying for SIGMET to expire or move
- diverting due severe icing / turbulence SIGMET
- requesting lower/higher outside the SIGMET band

**Important:** SIGMET is for all aircraft. Helicopter size does not grant exemption. The H130 is more vulnerable to severe icing and hail than a large jet — respect the product.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use SIGMETs to:

- approve large weather deviations and reroutes
- keep traffic out of ash, severe icing, and severe turbulence areas
- manage holding when SIGMETs block destinations
- pass hazard information across sectors
- prioritize aircraft exiting dangerous weather

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

A SIGMET warns of severe icing between five and twelve thousand over southern Brazil. You are IFR at eight thousand toward Porto Alegre in the H130.

You transmit:

Curitiba Center, ANAC123, unable continue present altitude due severe icing SIGMET, request descent to four thousand or deviation east.

Center: ANAC123, descend and maintain four thousand, report conditions.

You leave the SIGMET band and continue only if clear of the hazard — otherwise divert.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Center: ANAC123, SIGMET severe icing along your route, advise intentions.
2. Center: ANAC123, deviation east approved, report clear of SIGMET area.
3. Approach: ANAC123, hold as published, SIGMET over destination.
4. Center: ANAC123, descend and maintain four thousand, outside icing SIGMET.
5. Center: ANAC123, volcanic ash SIGMET, say if able to divert south.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, requesting descent to four thousand due severe icing SIGMET.
2. ANAC123, deviation east approved, will report clear of SIGMET.
3. ANAC123, holding as published, destination under SIGMET.
4. ANAC123, diverting Florianópolis due SIGMET, request vectors.
5. ANAC123, unable present routing due SIGMET, request alternate clearance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

SIGMET means the weather office believes this can hurt any aircraft.

You do not need to verify severe icing with your own rotor. Leave the band. Leave the area.

In Brazil, SIGMETs cover severe turbulence from convection, severe icing in winter layers, and occasionally ash from distant volcanic events affecting routes. Turbine inlets and ash do not mix — divert.

Part 3 speaking loves the definition. Part 2 flying loves the request: unable present routing due SIGMET, request deviation.

Say unable when the clearance would put you inside the hazard. That single word protects the flight.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is a SIGMET and what would you do if one covers your cruise altitude with severe icing?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A SIGMET is a warning of significant en-route weather hazardous to all aircraft, such as severe icing, severe turbulence, thunderstorms, or volcanic ash. If severe icing SIGMET covered my cruise level, I would request an altitude outside the band or a route around the area, and I would exit icing immediately if already in cloud. I would not continue in known severe icing in the H130. If I could not avoid it, I would divert and advise ATC. I would treat the SIGMET as a hard constraint, not optional advice.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Confusing SIGMET with AIRMET severity.  
  ✔ SIGMET = more severe / hazardous to all aircraft; AIRMET = generally moderate en-route hazards.

- ❌ Continuing through a SIGMET "gap" without a real exit plan.  
  ✔ Plan deviation or delay; do not sample severe icing.

- ❌ Vague radio: "weather problem."  
  ✔ Name it: severe icing SIGMET, request descent/deviation.

- ❌ Ignoring ash SIGMETs because the volcano is far away.  
  ✔ Ash can travel; turbines avoid ash always.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

SIGMET for severe turbulence and thunderstorms covers your destination for one hour.

**Your first task**

What do you say to Approach while still en route?

**Your second task**

ATC clears you direct through the SIGMET area. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, requesting hold or diversion, destination under SIGMET, advise alternate. Second task: ANAC123, unable present routing due SIGMET, request deviation / hold. Never accept a clearance that drives you into a known severe hazard. Unable is correct and expected.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

SIGMET is significant — by definition.

Read the hazard. Change the route or the level. Say unable when you must.

In the H130 over Brazil, SIGMET compliance is not bureaucracy. It is how you keep severe ice, hail, and ash off a turbine helicopter that cannot afford them.
