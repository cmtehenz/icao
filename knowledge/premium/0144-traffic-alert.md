# Traffic Alert

**Catalog ID:** 0144  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Traffic Alert — an urgent ATC transmission issued when a potentially conflicting aircraft poses an immediate or imminent collision risk that requires prompt pilot action.

A Traffic Alert is not a routine traffic advisory.

It is an urgent warning.

In the H130, operating in the dense terminal airspace around Congonhas, São Paulo, or Florianópolis, a Traffic Alert can be issued when a radar separation violation is developing or when ATC detects a potential collision scenario.

Your response must be immediate: acknowledge, look, and take action if required.

This lesson covers both ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency response.

## 📖 Meaning

A Traffic Alert is an urgent ATC advisory warning the pilot of potentially conflicting traffic that poses an immediate collision threat, requiring immediate pilot attention and possible evasive action.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Alerta de tráfego.

Aviso urgente do controle alertando o piloto sobre tráfego potencialmente conflitante que representa uma ameaça iminente de colisão, exigindo atenção imediata e possível ação evasiva do piloto.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a Traffic Alert is received, the pilot should:

- acknowledge immediately
- scan for the traffic in the described position
- take evasive action if traffic is acquired and conflict is imminent
- advise ATC of any evasive action taken
- coordinate the return to cleared routing after the conflict passes

Traffic Alerts are issued when:

- radar separation drops below minimum for the class of airspace
- ATC detects a non-transponding intruder near the aircraft
- a TCAS traffic advisory (TA) is followed by an ATC verbal alert
- traffic conflict develops during approach or departure
- a runway incursion risk is detected on the ground radar

**Important:** A Traffic Alert requires immediate action. Unlike a routine traffic advisory, the pilot must not passively scan. They must look urgently, assess the situation, and be prepared to deviate from the cleared routing. After deviating, advise ATC immediately.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC issues Traffic Alerts to:

- urgently warn of imminent collision risk
- prompt immediate pilot action before ATC-directed separation can be established
- cover situations where radar separation has already been compromised
- complement the TCAS traffic advisory system
- comply with Safety Alert requirements per ICAO and DECEA regulations

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are climbing through three thousand feet out of Congonhas in your H130.

São Paulo Approach transmits urgently:

ANAC123, traffic alert, traffic twelve o'clock, one mile, opposite direction, altitude three thousand five hundred, turn right immediately.

You look up. You see a light aircraft descending directly ahead.

You bank right and climb.

You transmit:

ANAC123, traffic in sight, turning right, climbing.

Approach confirms:

ANAC123, roger, clear of traffic, traffic alert cancelled, resume own navigation, report altitude.

You report:

ANAC123, turning to heading zero six zero, altitude three thousand five hundred, resuming.

Total time: fifteen seconds.

The Traffic Alert and your immediate response prevented a collision.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, traffic alert, traffic twelve o'clock, two miles, opposite direction, climb immediately.
2. Approach: ANAC123, traffic alert, traffic, nine o'clock, one mile, same altitude, turn right immediately.
3. Control: ANAC123, traffic alert, unknown traffic, two o'clock, three miles, altitude unconfirmed.
4. Tower: ANAC123, traffic alert, aircraft on runway, go around immediately.
5. Approach: ANAC123, traffic alert cancelled, traffic clear, resume own navigation.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Traffic Alert is urgent language for an urgent situation.

When you hear it, one thing stops: routine procedure.

What starts: immediate action.

Acknowledge. Look. Act if required.

The key error Brazilian pilots make is treating a Traffic Alert like a normal traffic advisory — scanning leisurely, reporting methodically.

The Traffic Alert is the controller saying: there is no time for normal procedure.

Act now.

In the H130, your maneuverability is an asset in a Traffic Alert. You can turn sharply, climb quickly, and reduce risk before a larger aircraft could.

Use that performance.

After the maneuver, report your action to ATC: ANAC123, turned right, now heading zero nine zero.

Do not leave ATC guessing where you went.

If TCAS also activates at the same time, follow TCAS. See lesson 0145.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How do you respond to an ATC Traffic Alert, and what information do you give after taking action?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

When ATC issues a Traffic Alert, I acknowledge immediately, scan urgently for the traffic, and take evasive action if the conflict is imminent. I advise ATC of my action: ANAC123, turning right, traffic in sight — or: ANAC123, climbing, traffic in sight. After the conflict passes, I report my position and altitude and request clearance to resume my routing. I do not wait for ATC to ask — I report spontaneously.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating a Traffic Alert like a routine advisory and responding slowly.  
  ✔ A Traffic Alert is urgent. Respond immediately. Act if required.

- ❌ Not reporting evasive action to ATC after deviating.  
  ✔ Always advise ATC: ANAC123, turning right, now heading zero nine zero.

- ❌ Waiting for ATC instructions before taking evasive action.  
  ✔ If the conflict is imminent and ATC says "turn right immediately," turn right. Do not wait for further explanation.

- ❌ Confusing Traffic Alert with TCAS — following verbal ATC instead of TCAS if both activate.  
  ✔ If TCAS activates with ATC Traffic Alert, follow TCAS. See 0145.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are on approach to Curitiba runway two one at three thousand feet in your H130. Approach transmits urgently: "ANAC123, traffic alert, traffic, ten o'clock, one mile, opposite direction, altitude three thousand, turn left immediately."

**Your first task**

What do you do and what do you transmit immediately?

**Your second task**

After turning left, you see the traffic pass to your right. The conflict is clear. What do you report to ATC?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I immediately turn left and transmit: ANAC123, turning left, traffic alert acknowledged. Once the traffic passes clear, I report: ANAC123, clear of traffic, heading two seven zero, altitude three thousand, request resume approach. ATC will then clear me to return to the approach sequence or provide alternate routing instructions.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

A Traffic Alert means one thing.

Act now.

Not in a moment. Not after another scan. Now.

Your acknowledgment and your action are both required.

Acknowledge with your voice.

Act with your controls.

Report with your radio.

All three together.

ATC will help you after the conflict passes.

Your job in that first five seconds is simply to avoid the aircraft.

Do that. Then talk.
