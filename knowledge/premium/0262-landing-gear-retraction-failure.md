# Landing Gear Retraction Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0262  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Landing Gear Retraction Failure — a malfunction in which the landing gear fails to retract after takeoff, leaving the gear down or partially retracted, with performance penalties and the need to advise ATC and decide whether to return or continue.

Gear retraction failure is usually less dramatic than gear stuck up on landing — but it is still abnormal. Drag and fuel burn increase. Speed and climb performance may be limited. Some systems may inhibit further retraction attempts. The crew must fly the airplane, run the checklist, and tell ATC the limitation.

For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, many H130s use skids and do not retract gear. Teach the ICAO exam English anyway. Relate it: wheeled types climb dirty after retraction failure; skid helicopters always fly "gear down" in a sense — but exam phraseology remains "landing gear retraction failure" for Part 2 and Part 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Landing Gear Retraction Failure is a malfunction in which the landing gear does not retract normally after takeoff, remaining extended or partially retracted, increasing drag and requiring abnormal procedures and ATC notification of the performance limitation.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha na retração do trem de pouso / Landing Gear Retraction Failure.

Malfuncionamento em que o trem de pouso não se recolhe normalmente após a decolagem, permanecendo estendido ou parcialmente recolhido, aumentando o arrasto e exigindo procedimentos anormais e notificação ao ATC da limitação de desempenho.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When landing gear retraction fails, the crew should:

- maintain safe climb and aircraft control
- apply the AFM retraction-failure or gear abnormal checklist
- do not repeatedly recycle against procedure limits
- advise ATC of landing gear retraction failure and performance limitation
- decide return for landing or continue based on fuel, weather, and AFM guidance
- request priority return if continuing is inappropriate

Retraction failure scenarios commonly include:

- gear remains down after gear-up selection
- partial retraction with unsafe or disagreement lights
- climb gradient insufficient for planned departure with gear down
- fuel planning no longer valid for the filed destination
- H130 note: skid aircraft do not retract — still learn wheeled exam English

**Important:** Flying with gear down may be safe but inefficient. Tell ATC early if you need to return. Do not silently continue a long IFR sector with unexpected drag and fuel burn.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to retraction-failure reports to:

- understand reduced climb and cruise performance
- approve returns or amended clearances
- avoid assigning altitudes or speeds the aircraft cannot meet
- sequence a priority return if requested
- coordinate with tower for an overweight or precautionary landing if applicable

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Wheeled-aircraft scenario: after takeoff from Curitiba, you select gear up. The gear remains down. Checklist confirms retraction failure. Fuel will not support Florianópolis with the drag penalty.

You transmit:

ANAC123, landing gear retraction failure, gear remains down, requesting return to land Curitiba.

Departure: ANAC123, roger gear retraction failure, turn left heading three six zero, expect vectors for runway one five.

You return. You land with gear already down and locked. Simple outcome — because you reported the limitation early.

An H130 departing the same airport on skids hears the call and understands why the jet is coming back.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Departure: ANAC123, roger landing gear retraction failure, say intentions.
2. Departure: ANAC123, return approved, expect vectors runway one five.
3. Center: ANAC123, advise if able to maintain flight level, gear down.
4. Approach: ANAC123, priority return approved, squawk ident.
5. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, confirm gear down and locked.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, landing gear retraction failure, requesting return to land.

3. ANAC123, unable maintain filed level with gear down, requesting lower.
4. ANAC123, priority return approved, ident.
5. ANAC123, cleared to land, gear down and locked.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Retraction failure is a performance problem wearing an abnormal checklist.

You can often still fly — just not as far, not as high, and not as efficiently. The professional move is early honesty with ATC: gear remains down, requesting return or amended clearance.

On the ICAO exam, distinguish this from gear stuck up on approach. Retraction failure is after takeoff, gear will not go up. Extension problems are a different lesson.

In the H130 with skids, you will not practice this malfunction in the aircraft. You will still say the English correctly in the exam room — and you will understand why a turboprop returns to Congonhas dirty.

Do not force repeated gear cycles against the AFM. One procedure path. Then a decision: return or continue with a new fuel plan.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

After takeoff the landing gear will not retract. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain a safe climb, apply the retraction-failure checklist, and avoid unauthorized repeated recycling. I would advise ATC of landing gear retraction failure with gear remaining down, state the performance limitation, and request a return to land or an amended clearance if fuel and climb performance no longer support the original plan.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Silently continuing to destination with gear down and inadequate fuel.  
  ✔ Recalculate; request return or diversion early.

- ❌ Confusing retraction failure with gear stuck up on landing.  
  ✔ Retraction failure = after takeoff, gear will not retract.

- ❌ Repeatedly cycling the gear against checklist limits.  
  ✔ Follow the AFM; then decide return or continue.

- ❌ Ignoring the concept because the H130 uses skids.  
  ✔ Learn exam English; keep real configuration reports accurate.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Landing Gear Retraction Failure, Request Return

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, LAN-ding GEER rih-TRAK-shun FAYL-yer, reh-KWEST rih-TURN

**Word Stress**

- Retraction → rih-TRAK-shun (stress on TRAK)
- Failure → FAYL-yer
- Request → reh-KWEST
- Return → rih-TURN

Practice:

Landing gear retraction failure... gear remains down... request return.

Together:

ANAC123, landing gear retraction failure, requesting return to land.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**RETRACT** — **R**eport early, **E**valuate climb/fuel, **T**ake the checklist once, **R**eturn if needed, **A**mend clearance, **C**onfirm gear down for landing, **T**ell ATC the limitation.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

After takeoff, gear will not retract. Drag is high. Destination fuel is no longer realistic. Departure airport weather is good.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

ATC asks if you can continue to destination at a lower altitude. What do you say if fuel is insufficient?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, landing gear retraction failure, gear remains down, requesting return to land. Second task: ANAC123, unable continue to destination with gear down, fuel insufficient, require return. I would not accept a continuation that violates safe fuel planning.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Landing gear retraction failure is drag, fuel, and honesty.

Fly the climb. Run the checklist. Tell ATC. Return when the plan no longer works.

For ICAO exams, keep it distinct from extension failures. For the H130 on skids, keep it as exam-ready English and shared-traffic awareness.

Limitation spoken early is a simple return. Limitation hidden late is a fuel emergency.

Speak early. Land smart.
