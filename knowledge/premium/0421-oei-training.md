# OEI Training

**Catalog ID:** 0421  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers OEI Training — deliberate practice of One Engine Inoperative procedures on a twin-engine helicopter, including how to coordinate with ATC so training emergencies are not mistaken for real ones.

OEI training builds the skills behind single engine operation — but the radio must mark the difference between training and distress.

For ICAO Delta pilots in Brazil preparing for multi-engine helicopter work beyond the H130, you must request training airspace or approaches, state "OEI training," and keep ATC informed of simulated versus real failures.

Understanding how to coordinate OEI training in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 4 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

OEI Training is structured practice of One Engine Inoperative flight profiles — including simulated engine failure, OEI climbs, approaches, and landings — conducted under training rules with clear communication so ATC can protect the exercise without treating it as an actual emergency unless one occurs.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Treinamento OEI (One Engine Inoperative).

Prática estruturada de perfis de voo com um motor inoperante — incluindo falha simulada de motor, subidas, aproximações e pousos OEI — realizada sob regras de treinamento, com comunicação clara para que o ATC proteja o exercício sem tratá-lo como emergência real, a menos que uma ocorra.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When conducting OEI training, the crew should:

- brief ATC that the exercise is OEI training / simulated engine failure
- request suitable altitude, area, or runway for the maneuver
- state "simulated" clearly on frequency
- be ready to declare a real emergency if the training becomes actual
- recover to normal operations and advise ATC when the exercise is complete

OEI training reporting commonly includes:

- request OEI training, simulated engine failure
- commencing OEI approach
- going around, OEI training
- OEI training complete, normal operations

**Important:** Never use a bare "engine failure" call during training without "simulated" or "training." Controllers will treat unexplained engine failure as real.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers support OEI training by:

- allocating practice areas or quiet approach slots
- providing traffic information during low-performance profiles
- keeping emergency services on standby only when requested
- distinguishing simulated calls from real mayday/pan-pan
- sequencing training go-arounds safely

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on a twin-engine training detail out of Campinas for OEI approaches. Before the maneuver you call:

Campinas Approach, request OEI training, simulated engine failure on approach runway one five, will advise commencing.

Approach approves with traffic information. You fly the OEI profile, go around once, then:

OEI training complete, normal operations, request landing.

No ambiguity. No false emergency scramble.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, OEI training approved, report commencing simulated engine failure.
2. Approach: ANAC123, traffic two o'clock three miles, advise in sight.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared low approach runway one five, OEI training, go around at your discretion.
4. Approach: ANAC123, roger go-around, remain in the pattern, report next intention.
5. Tower: ANAC123, roger training complete, cleared to land runway one five.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, OEI training, will report commencing simulated engine failure.
2. ANAC123, traffic in sight, continuing.
3. ANAC123, cleared low approach one five, OEI training.
4. ANAC123, going around, OEI training, remaining in the pattern.
5. ANAC123, OEI training complete, cleared to land one five.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

OEI training English is labeling English.

The word "simulated" protects fire trucks from rolling and protects you from being treated as a mayday when you are not.

In Brazilian training environments — Campinas, São José, coastal twin schools — frequencies are mixed with real traffic. Label every critical call: OEI training, simulated engine failure, training complete.

If the simulation becomes real — actual engine issue, fire light, uncontrollable yaw — drop the training label and declare for real. ATC would rather scramble once than miss a true failure.

Part 4 interaction skill is coordination: request the exercise, accept constraints, report complete. That is professional training radio.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You want to practice an OEI approach. What do you say to ATC before starting?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would request the exercise clearly: Request OEI training, simulated engine failure on approach, and I would report when commencing. I would avoid saying "engine failure" alone. When finished I would say OEI training complete, normal operations.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Calling "engine failure" during a sim without "simulated."  
  ✔ Always say simulated engine failure or OEI training.

- ❌ Not telling ATC when training is complete.  
  ✔ Close the loop: training complete, normal operations.

- ❌ Blocking a busy approach without a prior request.  
  ✔ Request OEI training and accept an alternate time or area.

- ❌ Continuing to say "training" after a real failure occurs.  
  ✔ Upgrade to real pan-pan or mayday immediately.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Request OEI Training, Simulated Engine Failure

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, reh-KWEST OH-EE-EYE TRAY-ning, SIM-yuh-lay-tid EN-jin FAYL-yer

**Word Stress**

- Training → TRAY-ning
- Simulated → SIM-yuh-lay-tid
- Failure → FAYL-yer

Practice:

OEI training... simulated engine failure.

Together:

Request OEI training, simulated engine failure on approach runway one five.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**TRAIN** — **T**ell ATC it is OEI training, **R**eport simulated clearly, **A**dvise commencing, **I**nform on go-around, **N**ormal ops when complete.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Campinas. You will fly two OEI approaches then a full stop.

**Your first task**

Request the training with ATC.

**Your second task**

During the second approach the real engine fails. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, request OEI training, simulated engine failure on approach runway one five, will advise commencing. Second task: Drop training language — Pan-pan or Mayday as required: ANAC123, engine failure, single engine operation, requesting priority landing — this is a real emergency, not simulated.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

OEI training builds the hands. Clear labels protect the system.

Request the exercise. Say simulated. Report complete. If it becomes real, declare for real.

In ICAO Delta and Brazilian twin training, the difference between a useful practice and a false alarm is one word on frequency.

Train hard. Speak precisely. Never leave ATC guessing.
