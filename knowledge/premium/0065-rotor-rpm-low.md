# Rotor RPM Low

**Catalog ID:** 0065  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers low rotor RPM — one of the most dangerous helicopter conditions.

Rotor RPM Low means main rotor RPM has dropped below safe operating limits — lift decreases and autorotation may become necessary.

In the H130, maintaining rotor RPM in the green arc is the pilot's primary responsibility.

Low rotor RPM can lead to rotor stall and catastrophic loss of lift.

You must aviate first, lower collective immediately, then communicate with Mayday if altitude cannot be maintained.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Rotor RPM Low is the condition where main rotor RPM falls below minimum safe limits, threatening loss of lift and requiring immediate corrective action.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

RPM do rotor baixo.

Condição em que o RPM do rotor principal cai abaixo dos limites mínimos seguros, ameaçando perda de sustentação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Rotor RPM Low occurs, the pilot should:

- lower collective immediately to recover rotor RPM
- maintain aircraft control and avoid abrupt aft cyclic
- enter autorotation if RPM cannot be recovered and altitude is being lost
- declare Mayday if autorotation or emergency landing is required
- inform ATC of low rotor RPM, position, and intentions

Rotor RPM Low reporting commonly includes:

- low rotor RPM or rotor RPM decay
- lowering collective, attempting RPM recovery
- entering autorotation if altitude cannot be maintained
- souls on board and position

**Important:** Low rotor RPM with altitude loss requiring autorotation is distress — Mayday. Brief RPM decay corrected promptly may allow Pan Pan. Aviate first — lower collective before you speak.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Rotor RPM Low declarations to:

- clear all traffic from the aircraft's route immediately
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable landing area
- coordinate emergency services on the ground
- request souls on board and exact position
- prepare for possible autorotation or emergency landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are at six hundred feet on approach to Congonhas helipad in your H130.

During a high-power hover, rotor RPM decays into the yellow arc and continues dropping.

You lower collective aggressively and transition to forward flight.

RPM recovers but altitude was lost.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, São Paulo Tower, ANAC123, low rotor RPM, RPM recovered, request immediate landing helipad.

Tower clears all traffic and emergency services respond.

You land safely on the helipad.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, Mayday acknowledged, say souls on board and current rotor RPM.
2. Tower: ANAC123, cleared direct helipad, all traffic cleared, emergency services standing by.
3. Approach: ANAC123, radar contact, say intentions and altitude.
4. Tower: ANAC123, wind calm, cleared to land any area.
5. Tower: ANAC123, emergency vehicles dispatched to helipad.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Low rotor RPM is the helicopter pilot's oldest enemy.

Below minimum RPM, the blades stall aerodynamically — lift disappears.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Collective down. Aviate. Mayday.

Lower collective first — before anything else.

Do not pull aft cyclic while RPM is low.

If RPM does not recover and altitude is lost — autorotate.

Then Mayday.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "RPM baixo" or "perdendo RPM" on frequency.

Use low rotor RPM or rotor RPM decay — ICAO standard terms.

In the H130, high-power hover on hot days is a classic low RPM trap.

Train the collective-down reflex until it is automatic.

The radio comes after collective — always.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the first action when rotor RPM becomes low during hover?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would lower collective immediately to recover rotor RPM. I would avoid abrupt aft cyclic, transition to forward flight if appropriate, and maintain aircraft control. If RPM recovers, I would declare Pan Pan or Mayday depending on altitude lost and declare my intentions. If RPM cannot be recovered and altitude is being lost, I would enter autorotation and declare Mayday Mayday Mayday with position and souls on board.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Pulling collective up when RPM is low.  
  ✔ Lower collective immediately — this is the only recovery action.

- ❌ Calling Mayday before lowering collective.  
  ✔ Collective down first — radio second.

- ❌ Saying "RPM caindo" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use low rotor RPM or rotor RPM decay.

- ❌ Not preparing for autorotation if RPM does not recover.  
  ✔ Enter autorotation if altitude cannot be maintained.

During a high-power hover at four hundred feet near Navegantes, rotor RPM drops into the yellow arc in your H130.

**Your first task**
