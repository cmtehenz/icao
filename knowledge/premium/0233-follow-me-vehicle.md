# Follow Me Vehicle

**Catalog ID:** 0233  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Follow Me Vehicle — a specially marked airport vehicle that leads an aircraft along a designated route on the maneuvering area or apron when the crew needs guidance due to complex layout, low visibility, temporary works, unfamiliar routing, or ATC instruction.

"Follow me" is not a suggestion to chase casually. It is a guided taxi: you follow the vehicle's path, maintain safe spacing, and remain responsible for obstacle clearance and ATC clearances such as hold-short. The vehicle shows the way; you still fly — or taxi — the aircraft safely.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations at Brazilian airports, follow-me guidance appears for unfamiliar aprons, night arrivals, works in progress, and sometimes for air-taxi to remote helipads. Rotor wash, spacing behind the vehicle, and clear communication with Ground matter.

This lesson builds Part 2 proficiency in follow-me phraseology, readbacks, and safe guided taxi technique.

## 📖 Meaning

A Follow Me Vehicle is an aerodrome vehicle, conspicuously marked and lit, assigned to lead an aircraft on a specified route on the apron or maneuvering area to provide positional guidance when routing is complex, visibility is reduced, or ATC requires guided taxi.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Veículo Follow Me / Follow Me Vehicle.

Veículo do aeródromo, conspicuamente sinalizado e iluminado, designado para conduzir uma aeronave em rota especificada no pátio ou na área de manobras, fornecendo orientação de posição quando o roteamento é complexo, a visibilidade está reduzida ou o ATC exige táxi guiado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When instructed to follow a follow-me vehicle, the crew should:

- read back the follow-me instruction and any route or apron destination
- establish visual contact with the vehicle before moving
- maintain safe distance — do not overtake or crowd the vehicle
- continue to comply with hold-short and runway-crossing clearances
- advise ATC or the vehicle (as applicable) if visual contact is lost
- for helicopters, manage downwash so as not to hazard the vehicle or loose objects

Follow-me operations commonly include:

- low visibility taxi to the stand or runway
- routing around works and closed taxiways
- unfamiliar foreign or complex apron layouts
- leading to a remote stand or helipad
- guiding after runway vacation when signs are hard to see

**Important:** Following the vehicle does not cancel ATC instructions. If the vehicle approaches a runway holding point, you still need a clearance to cross or enter. In the H130, keep rotor clearance from the vehicle and from apron equipment — guided taxi is not automatic obstacle clearance.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers and apron management use follow-me vehicles to:

- guide aircraft when standard taxi would be confusing or unsafe
- reduce wrong-stand and wrong-taxiway events
- support low visibility and night operations
- route traffic around temporary works efficiently
- assist crews unfamiliar with the aerodrome layout

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You land at Guarulhos at night in the H130. The apron routing to the training stand is complex, and works have changed the usual path.

Ground: taxi via Alpha, follow the follow-me vehicle to stand two four.

You spot the vehicle with the distinctive markings and lights. You taxi behind at a safe distance. At a runway crossing the vehicle stops. You stop. Tower clears the crossing. The vehicle proceeds. You follow only after you also have the crossing clearance and are ready.

At the stand the vehicle stops and signals the position. You park as marshalled or cleared. Guided, but still PIC-responsible.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Alpha, follow the follow-me vehicle to stand two four.
2. Ground: ANAC123, follow-me vehicle ahead, follow to the apron.
3. Tower: ANAC123, vacate right, follow-me vehicle will lead you to the helipad.
4. Ground: ANAC123, hold position, follow-me vehicle approaching from your left.
5. Ground: ANAC123, follow-me to stand one two, give way to opposite traffic on Bravo.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Follow-me means guided eyes on the ground — not autopilot for taxi.

You still own wingtip and rotor clearance. You still own hold-short. The vehicle leads; you decide whether the path is safe for the H130.

Keep spacing. If you lose the vehicle in rain or darkness, stop and say so. Do not invent the rest of the route.

At Brazilian majors, follow-me often appears with works and night arrivals. Expect it. Brief who is looking outside. In a two-crew situation, one pilot monitors the vehicle while the other monitors ATC and the diagram.

Never cross a runway just because the follow-me did. Your clearance is yours.

At night, confirm the vehicle identity early — markings, lights, and Ground's description. Following the wrong vehicle is rare but expensive. When in doubt, hold and ask Ground to confirm.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC instructs you to follow a follow-me vehicle to your stand. Describe your responsibilities while following.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would read back the instruction, acquire the vehicle visually, and taxi behind at a safe distance along the guided route. I would still comply with any hold-short or crossing clearances and would not enter a runway without ATC authorization even if the vehicle proceeds. If I lost visual contact with the vehicle, I would stop and advise ATC. I remain responsible for obstacle and rotor clearance throughout.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Overtaking the follow-me vehicle to "get to the stand faster."  
  ✔ Stay behind. The vehicle sets the path and pace.

- ❌ Crossing a runway because the follow-me crossed.  
  ✔ You need your own ATC clearance to cross or enter.

- ❌ Losing the vehicle in low visibility and continuing from memory.  
  ✔ Stop and report lost visual contact. Request further guidance.

- ❌ Ignoring rotor wash effects on the vehicle and nearby equipment.  
  ✔ Manage hover height and distance so downwash does not hazard the guide.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Night arrival. Ground tells you to follow the follow-me vehicle to the training stand. Mid-route the vehicle approaches a runway holding point.

**Your first task**

What do you say if you have not received a crossing clearance and the vehicle stops at the hold-short line?

**Your second task**

After crossing with clearance, you lose the vehicle in heavy rain. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, holding short runway one seven Left — or simply hold position and wait for crossing clearance; I would not cross with the vehicle until cleared. Second task: ANAC123, lost visual contact with follow-me vehicle, holding position, request further taxi guidance. I would not continue guessing the route.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Follow-me is guidance, not a transfer of command.

Acquire the vehicle. Follow with spacing. Keep ATC clearances. Own your clearance to cross any runway.

In the H130, protect the vehicle from downwash and protect the aircraft from apron obstacles.

Lose the vehicle? Stop and say so.

Guided taxi done right is calm, spaced, and clearance-aware — all the way to the stand.
