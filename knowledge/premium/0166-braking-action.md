# Braking Action

**Catalog ID:** 0166  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Braking Action — the pilot report and ATC advisory describing how effectively an aircraft's brakes perform on a given runway surface.

Braking action is not a fixed property of the runway. It changes with surface conditions: rain, standing water, rubber deposits, frost, and temperature all affect braking performance.

When braking action deteriorates, stopping distance increases. Pilots who are unaware of poor braking action may exit the runway at excessive speed, or fail to stop within the available distance.

In Brazil, southern airports including Florianópolis, Curitiba, and Navegantes regularly experience surface contamination events that reduce braking action — rain, tropical downpours, and occasional winter frost near Curitiba.

ATC reports braking action received from previous aircraft, and pilots are expected to pass on their own braking action reports to help crews behind them.

Understanding this vocabulary is essential for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 operational communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Braking Action is a qualitative measure of the retardation force available to a landing or taxiing aircraft from the runway surface, reported as Good, Medium, Poor, or Nil.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Ação de frenagem.

Medida qualitativa da força de retardamento disponível para a aeronave em pouso ou taxiamento a partir da superfície da pista, reportada como Boa, Média, Ruim ou Nula.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When braking action is reported or suspected to have deteriorated, the pilot should:

- request the latest braking action report before landing or takeoff
- compare the reported braking action against aircraft performance data
- apply appropriate landing distance corrections for the reported condition
- transmit a braking action report after landing if the condition differs from what was advertised
- consider going around or diverting if braking action is reported Nil or Poor and performance margins are insufficient

Braking Action reports use standardized terms:

- **Good** — braking is normal; no unusual deceleration required
- **Medium** — noticeable reduction in braking effectiveness
- **Poor** — significant degradation; considerably longer stopping distance
- **Nil** — essentially no braking available; runway effectively unusable

**Important:** Braking Action reports reflect the experience of the previous aircraft, which may have a different weight, tire type, and speed. Always apply conservative performance margins.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers pass on Braking Action reports to:

- warn arriving aircraft of degraded runway surface conditions
- support pilot decision-making for landing and takeoff
- comply with mandatory reporting obligations when Poor or Nil is reported
- coordinate go-arounds and diversions when the runway is unsafe
- update ATIS and NOTAM information when conditions change

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on approach to Florianópolis in your H130 after a heavy rain shower.

Tower advises:

ANAC123, braking action report, previous aircraft reported braking action Medium on runway two eight.

You calculate your landing distance with a Medium braking action correction.

Your H130 performance data confirms sufficient margin.

You respond:

ANAC123, copied braking action Medium runway two eight.

You land, apply brakes firmly, and decelerate within the expected distance.

After clearing the runway, you transmit your own report:

ANAC123, braking action report runway two eight, Good, wet surface with normal deceleration.

ATC relays your report to the next arriving aircraft.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, braking action report, previous aircraft reported braking action Poor on runway one five.
2. Tower: ANAC123, be advised, braking action Nil reported on runway one five, runway closed.
3. Tower: ANAC123, latest braking action report runway two eight, Medium, reported by a light aircraft.
4. Tower: ANAC123, pilot report of braking action Good on runway three three, wind calm.
5. Tower: ANAC123, braking action unknown on runway one five, no recent reports available.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Braking action is a message from the pilot ahead of you.

They just landed on the same runway. They know what it felt like.

Good, Medium, Poor, Nil — four words that directly affect your stopping distance calculation.

Brazilian pilots often ignore this report or underestimate its impact.

Do not land with a Poor braking action report and a full passenger load without running the performance numbers first.

The H130 is light and nimble, but physics still applies.

When you land — always transmit your own braking action report.

The crew behind you is counting on it.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How would you report your braking action after landing, and why is it important?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

After landing I would transmit my braking action report on the tower frequency, for example: ANAC123, braking action report runway two eight, Medium. This is important because it gives the next crew real-time information about the runway surface that may not yet be reflected in the ATIS or NOTAM. Braking action can change quickly with rain, and a prompt pilot report supports safe decision-making for other aircraft.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Landing without requesting a braking action report after rain.  
  ✔ Always request the latest report if precipitation has occurred.

- ❌ Using Portuguese terms like "pista molhada ruim" on frequency.  
  ✔ Use the standard terms: Good, Medium, Poor, Nil.

- ❌ Not transmitting a braking action report after landing.  
  ✔ Always report your experience to ATC after landing if conditions differ from the last report.

- ❌ Applying no performance correction for Medium braking action.  
  ✔ Any braking action below Good requires a landing distance correction.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Tower advises: ANAC123, braking action report, previous aircraft reported braking action Poor on runway one five, Florianópolis.

Your H130 performance data shows a 35% landing distance increase is required for Poor braking action, and you are close to your available distance margin.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit to ATC, and what options do you consider?

**Your second task**

If you choose to land and decelerate within the available distance, what do you transmit after clearing the runway?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would acknowledge the report and advise ATC that I am conducting a performance check: ANAC123, copied braking action Poor runway one five, stand by for performance assessment. If the corrected landing distance exceeds my available runway, I would request a go-around and diversion: ANAC123, unable to accept braking action Poor runway one five, request alternate. If I land and performance matches expectations, I would transmit: ANAC123, braking action report runway one five, Poor confirmed, standing water in touchdown zone.
