# Runway Excursion

**Catalog ID:** 0225  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Runway Excursion — an event in which an aircraft veers off or overruns the runway during takeoff or landing, and the critical ATC and pilot phraseology used when an excursion closes the runway or requires emergency response.

Runway excursion is a critical aerodrome emergency. The runway may be blocked by the aircraft, wreckage, or debris. Other traffic go around, hold, or divert. RFF responds. The involved crew must communicate state of aircraft, injuries, evacuation needs, and hazards such as fire or smoke.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, excursions are less common than for fixed-wing aircraft, but helicopters share airports where fixed-wing excursions close runways — Congonhas, Guarulhos, Santos Dumont, Curitiba, Florianópolis. You must handle the radio as traffic affected by the closure, and understand the language if ever involved in an off-surface event. Part 2 and Part 3 both treat this as critical.

Understanding how to go around, divert, and speak clearly when a runway excursion has occurred is essential for ICAO Level 4 performance under pressure.

## 📖 Meaning

Runway Excursion is an occurrence in which an aircraft unintentionally leaves the runway surface during takeoff or landing, either by veering off the side or overrunning the end, often resulting in runway closure, emergency response, and disruption to other aerodrome traffic.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Runway excursion / Saída de pista / overrun / veer-off.

Ocorrência em que uma aeronave deixa involuntariamente a superfície da pista durante decolagem ou pouso, seja saindo pela lateral ou ultrapassando o fim da pista, frequentemente resultando em interdição da pista, resposta de emergência e interrupção do demais tráfego do aeródromo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a runway excursion is reported or observed, the pilot should:

- if on approach, expect and execute go-around when instructed — or when the runway is clearly blocked
- acknowledge runway closed / excursion advisory
- state intentions: hold, divert, or request alternate runway if available
- remain clear of the emergency site and RFF routes
- if you are the involved aircraft: secure as able, report souls on board, fuel, fire/smoke, and evacuation status
- for helicopters offered an alternate pad, confirm the approach does not conflict with the response

Runway excursion commonly involves:

- wet or contaminated runway overrun
- directional control loss and veer-off
- rejected takeoff ending off the paved surface
- landing long with insufficient stopping distance
- subsequent wreckage, FOD, and full runway closure

**Important:** An excursion on the runway complex can close the entire airport for a period. Training traffic diverts early. In the H130, do not request a low pass over the excursion site. Critical events need empty sky for RFF, not spectators.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers communicate runway excursion to:

- stop further landings and takeoffs on the affected runway
- launch RFF and protect the accident site
- vector go-arounds and organize holding or diversion
- obtain essential information from the involved crew
- coordinate airport operations closed or partial reopening

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are number two on approach to Congonhas in the H130. Ahead, a jet lands long and veers off the side of the runway.

Tower: ANAC123, go around, runway excursion, runway closed.

You go around. You climb on the missed approach. Approach asks intentions. Fuel supports Curitiba. You request diversion.

Behind you, firefighters roll to the excursion site. Your clean go-around and early diversion kept the frequency and the sky usable for the emergency.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, go around, runway excursion, runway closed.
2. Approach: ANAC123, runway closed due runway excursion, advise intentions.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cancel takeoff clearance, runway excursion on the field, hold position.
4. Approach: ANAC123, expect holding, Congonhas closed, runway excursion in progress.
5. Tower: ANAC123, firefighters on the way, remain clear of the runway.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, going around, runway closed.
2. ANAC123, runway closed due excursion, request diversion to Curitiba.
3. ANAC123, cancel takeoff, holding position.
4. ANAC123, expecting holding, airport closed copied.
5. ANAC123, remaining clear of the runway, firefighters on the way.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Runway excursion is one of the phrases that deletes your landing clearance instantly.

The runway is no longer a runway. It is an emergency scene.

Your job as other traffic: go around, get clean, then intentions. Short. Fuel-honest. No sightseeing.

If you were ever the aircraft off the pavement: survival first, then radio essentials — position if able, souls, fuel, fire, evacuation. That is Part 3 gold because it is real priority language.

Brazilian wet-runway and short-field environments make excursion a living risk for fixed-wing traffic at places like Santos Dumont and Congonhas. Academy helicopter crews must be ready for sudden closure on every inbound.

Critical English is not dramatic English. It is ordered English.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

On final, ATC reports a runway excursion and instructs a go-around. What do you do next?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would execute the go-around immediately and follow the missed approach or ATC vectors. I would read back going around and that the runway is closed. After the aircraft is stable, I would review fuel and alternates and advise intentions — request holding only if a short reopening is realistic, or request diversion if the runway will remain blocked. I would remain clear of the excursion site and RFF operations and I would not request a visual pass over the accident area.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Delaying the go-around to see the aircraft off the runway.  
  ✔ Go around on instruction; visual curiosity is not a plan.

- ❌ Holding until fuel is critical hoping the runway reopens.  
  ✔ Divert while alternate fuel is intact.

- ❌ Blocking tower with long descriptions during the missed approach.  
  ✔ Short readback first; intentions when stable.

- ❌ Self-clearing to a parallel runway or helipad without approval.  
  ✔ Request and confirm; the whole complex may be restricted.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**EXCURSION** — **E**xit the approach now, **X** out the landing plan, **C**all intentions after stable, **U**se alternate fuel wisely, **R**emain clear of RFF, **S**hort radio only, **I**gnore sightseeing urges, **O**bey closure fully, **N**avigate to the alternate.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are on a three-mile final at Florianópolis in the H130 with fuel for diversion to Curitiba.

**Your first task**

Tower says: "ANAC123, go around, runway excursion, runway closed." What do you say?

**Your second task**

Approach asks intentions and mentions possible reopening in one hour. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, going around, runway closed. Second task: ANAC123, request diversion to Curitiba, fuel insufficient for one hour hold — to refuse an unsafe hold and commit early to the alternate while RFF handles the excursion.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Runway excursion turns the airport into an emergency scene.

Go around. Read it back. Choose hold or divert with fuel truth.

Stay clear. Let firefighters work. Do not watch from low altitude.

Critical events reward the crew that sounds calm because the plan is already rebuilding.

That is ICAO Level 4 when the runway is gone.
