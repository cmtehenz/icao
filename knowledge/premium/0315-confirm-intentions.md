# Confirm Intentions

**Catalog ID:** 0315  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Confirm Intentions — the ATC request for the crew to state what they plan to do next when the clearance, traffic picture, or situation is unclear. Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: answer with a short, concrete next action.

Confirm intentions is High priority because controllers cannot sequence or separate traffic on silence and ambiguity.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for stating intentions under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including approach, holding, diversion, and VFR/IFR transitions.

Understanding how to confirm intentions in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Confirm Intentions is an ATC request for the pilot to state the planned next action — for example continue approach, break off, hold, divert, or request a specific clearance — so that ATC can issue an appropriate instruction and maintain an accurate traffic plan.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Confirmar intenções / Confirm Intentions.

Solicitação do controle para que o piloto declare a próxima ação planejada — por exemplo continuar a aproximação, interromper, entrar em espera, desviar ou solicitar uma autorização específica — para que o ATC emita a instrução adequada e mantenha um plano de tráfego preciso.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC asks you to confirm intentions, the crew should:

- answer with one clear next action, not a long discussion
- include the relevant place or procedure: runway, fix, alternate
- request the clearance you need if intentions depend on ATC approval
- for H130: state hover, landing, or air-taxi intentions when in the circuit or at a heliport
- change the stated intention on frequency if the plan changes
- avoid "we are thinking" — give a decision or a specific request

Intention exchanges commonly include:

- confirm intentions / say intentions
- intending to... / request...
- continue approach / go around
- divert to... / enter hold
- request landing / request air taxi

**Important:** Intentions are what you plan to do; clearances are what ATC authorizes. State intentions, then comply with the clearance issued.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers ask for intentions to:

- resolve ambiguity after a missed approach, weather change, or radio break
- choose between landing, holding, and diversion options
- coordinate the next sector or tower movement
- prevent two aircraft from planning incompatible paths
- issue the correct follow-on clearance quickly

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 on a missed approach at Congonhas. Weather is marginal.

Approach: ANAC123, confirm intentions.

You decide and reply:

ANAC123, request another approach runway one seven Left.

Or, if weather is unworkable:

ANAC123, diverting to Campo de Marte, request routing.

ATC now has a plan to work with. Confirming intentions is how you reopen a clear traffic picture after uncertainty.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, confirm intentions.
2. Tower: ANAC123, say intentions.
3. Approach: ANAC123, confirm intentions after missed approach.
4. Control: ANAC123, advise intentions due weather.
5. Tower: ANAC123, confirm landing or air taxi intentions.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Confirm intentions means: decide, then speak one action.

ATC is not asking for your feelings about the weather. They need the next move. Another approach. Hold. Divert. Land. Air taxi.

In the H130 around São Paulo, missed approaches and pad changes happen. The crew that answers intentions in one line gets the next clearance faster. Part 2 rewards decisiveness on frequency.

If you need ATC permission to do it, phrase it as a request that reveals the intention.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

After a missed approach, ATC asks you to confirm intentions. How should you reply?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would state one clear next action, such as requesting another approach to a specific runway, requesting holding, or stating a diversion to an alternate with a routing request. I would keep the transmission short and wait for the clearance that matches those intentions. If the plan changes, I would update intentions on frequency immediately.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Answering with a long weather analysis and no decision.  
  ✔ Give the next action or a specific request.

- ❌ Saying "continue" without saying continue to what.  
  ✔ Name the approach, runway, hold, or alternate.

- ❌ Stating intentions that contradict an existing clearance without requesting an amendment.  
  ✔ Request what you need; do not freestyle.

- ❌ Remaining silent because the crew is still discussing.  
  ✔ If needed, say standby, then return with a decision quickly.

**Your second task**

First task: ANAC123, request another approach runway one seven Left — or the runway in use. Second task: ANAC123, diverting to Campo de Marte, request routing. One clear intention each time; do not leave ATC guessing.
