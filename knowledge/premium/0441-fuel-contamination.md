# Fuel Contamination

**Catalog ID:** 0441  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Fuel Contamination — the presence of water, sediment, microbial growth, or wrong fuel type in the aircraft fuel system that can interrupt engine feed or damage components.

Fuel contamination is not a minor maintenance note. It is a systems threat that can produce power loss, flameout, or filter bypass in flight.

For H130 helicopter operations, fuel quality discipline starts on the ground: sampling, draining, and verifying uplift. In flight, contamination may appear as fluctuating fuel pressure, filter bypass indications, or unexplained power anomalies.

Understanding how to report fuel contamination in ICAO-standard English — including Pan Pan or Mayday when power is threatened — is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Fuel Contamination is the presence of foreign substances — water, particulates, microbial growth, or incorrect fuel grade — in the aircraft fuel system that can degrade combustion, clog filters, or interrupt continuous fuel supply to the engine.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Contaminação de combustível.

Presença de substâncias estranhas — água, partículas, crescimento microbiano ou tipo incorreto de combustível — no sistema de combustível da aeronave que pode degradar a combustão, obstruir filtros ou interromper o fornecimento contínuo ao motor.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When fuel contamination is suspected or confirmed, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control and monitor engine parameters continuously
- reduce power demand if fuel pressure or power delivery becomes unstable
- land as soon as practicable at a suitable site
- declare Pan Pan if flight remains controllable with degraded fuel feed
- declare Mayday if power loss or flameout is imminent or occurring

Fuel contamination reporting commonly includes:

- suspected fuel contamination or water in fuel
- fluctuating fuel pressure, reducing power
- request vectors to nearest suitable landing site
- souls on board and fuel remaining when requested

**Important:** Contamination discovered on the ground is a no-go. Contamination suspected in flight is a systems emergency — aviate, navigate, then communicate with the correct urgency.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to fuel contamination declarations to:

- clear traffic and provide priority routing to a suitable landing site
- coordinate emergency services for possible power loss on approach
- request souls on board and fuel remaining
- avoid assigning holding or delays that extend exposure
- prepare tower and rescue for a precautionary or forced landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at two thousand feet in your H130 from Florianópolis toward Joinville.

Fuel pressure begins to fluctuate. You reduce collective and monitor Ng and T4. The indication is consistent with possible contamination or filter restriction.

When stable, you transmit a Pan Pan, report suspected fuel contamination, and request vectors to Joinville for a precautionary landing.

Approach clears traffic and provides direct routing. You land, shut down, and request maintenance inspection before any further flight.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say nature of problem and souls on board.
2. Approach: ANAC123, turn left heading three four zero, Joinville one five miles, descend one thousand five hundred.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway one five cleared to land, emergency services standing by.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say fuel remaining and intentions after landing.
5. Tower: ANAC123, wind zero nine zero at one zero, report final.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Fuel contamination is often preventable — and always serious when it appears in flight.

Water settles. Sediment migrates. Wrong fuel destroys engines. Microbial growth clogs filters over time.

On the H130, your defense is ground discipline: sample, drain, verify uplift, and refuse suspect fuel. In flight, treat unstable fuel pressure or unexplained power changes as a systems threat until proven otherwise.

Do not troubleshoot forever at altitude. Reduce demand, protect the engine, and get the aircraft on the ground where maintenance can sample and inspect.

Communicate early. ATC cannot help a silent crew. Pan Pan for controllable degradation. Mayday for power loss.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You notice fluctuating fuel pressure in cruise with no other abnormal indications. Describe what you say to ATC.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain control, reduce power as required, and declare urgency: Pan Pan Pan Pan, Approach, suspected fuel contamination, fluctuating fuel pressure, request vectors to nearest suitable airport. I would state souls on board and fuel remaining when asked, and upgrade to Mayday if power loss develops.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing to destination without declaring because the engine is still running.  
  ✔ Land as soon as practicable and declare Pan Pan when fuel feed is unstable.

- ❌ Saying only "fuel problem" without nature or intentions.  
  ✔ State suspected fuel contamination, parameters affected, and requested action.

- ❌ Delaying the call while troubleshooting indefinitely.  
  ✔ Aviate first, then communicate early so ATC can clear a path.

- ❌ Using Mayday for a stable precautionary landing with no power loss.  
  ✔ Use Pan Pan when controllable; reserve Mayday for distress.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Fuel contamination is a systems emergency that starts with quality control on the ground.

Sample. Drain. Verify. Refuse bad fuel.

In flight, unstable pressure or power is not a curiosity — it is a reason to land.

Declare early. Protect the engine. Get maintenance involved before the next departure.

That is how H130 crews keep fuel problems from becoming forced landings.
