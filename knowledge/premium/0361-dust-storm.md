# Dust Storm

**Catalog ID:** 0361  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Dust Storm — strong winds lifting fine dust into the air over a wide area, reducing visibility significantly and creating operational hazards similar to sandstorms, often with dust carried to higher altitudes.

Dust storms can arrive as a wall of reduced visibility. Crews must report dust storm, state intentions, and avoid low-visibility penetration — especially in helicopters operating near the surface.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, dry-season dust and blowing dust occur over interior plateaus and unpaved areas. ICAO exams also use dust storm as standard meteorological vocabulary.

Understanding dust storm phraseology is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Dust Storm is a meteorological condition in which strong winds raise fine dust particles into the atmosphere over a large area, causing widespread low visibility and hazardous flight conditions that normally require delay, holding, or diversion rather than flight into the dust.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Tempestade de poeira / Dust Storm.

Condição meteorológica em que ventos fortes levantam finas partículas de poeira na atmosfera sobre uma área ampla, causando baixa visibilidade generalizada e condições de voo perigosas que normalmente exigem atraso, espera ou desvio em vez de voo para dentro da poeira.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a dust storm is present or forecast, the crew should:

- review METAR/TAF for DU, DS, and visibility values
- avoid departure into deteriorating dust if departure minima cannot be maintained
- on arrival, request hold or divert when the field is obscured
- report dust storm observations with approximate visibility and location
- minimize hover and ground taxi in heavy blowing dust when able
- after exposure, consider inspection for filter and blade contamination

Dust storm reporting commonly includes:

- dust storm ahead / over the field
- visibility reduced in dust
- request hold / divert / delay
- unable visual approach due to dust storm

**Important:** Dust may loft higher than sand. A route that looks clear at low level can still hide a dust layer. Climb or divert based on reports — do not assume you can underfly the problem.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use dust storm reports to:

- advise arrivals and departures of visibility collapse
- manage holds, ground stops, and diversions
- relay PIREPs of dust layers and tops if reported
- coordinate alternate aerodromes
- protect multiple aircraft from the same widespread event

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Inbound to an interior field in the H130, you see a brown wall ahead. Approach confirms dust storm over the field, visibility eight hundred meters.

You request diversion to an alternate east of the dust. Center clears the divert. You remain in VMC, land clean, and file the weather reason as dust storm at destination.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, dust storm over the field, visibility eight hundred meters, advise intentions.
2. Tower: ANAC123, blowing dust, runway not in sight from tower, expect delay.
3. Approach: ANAC123, divert approved to the east alternate.
4. Center: ANAC123, dust storm reported along airway, say ride and visibility.
5. Approach: ANAC123, dust storm moving south, improvement expected after one eight three zero.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, dust storm over the field, request divert.
2. ANAC123, unable visual approach, dust storm, request hold.
3. ANAC123, dust storm ahead, visibility reduced, request weather deviation.
4. ANAC123, diverting east alternate due to dust storm.
5. ANAC123, clear of dust, field in sight, request continue.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Dust storm is sandstorm's wider, finer cousin.

Same decision: do not penetrate for schedule.

Brazilian interior dry season can produce blowing dust that surprises VFR helicopters. If the horizon turns brown, turn around or climb with ATC — do not press into the wall.

Say dust storm so meteorology and ATC log the correct phenomenon. "Não dá para ver" is not enough for a professional PIREP.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You encounter a dust storm that obscures your destination. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report dust storm over the field or ahead, state that I am unable to continue the visual approach, and request hold if a short delay will help or diversion to a suitable alternate. I would not enter the dust storm to find the runway.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Calling every visibility loss "fog."  
  ✔ Use dust storm when dust is the cause.

- ❌ Trying to underfly the dust at low level.  
  ✔ Divert or hold — low-level dust is especially dangerous for helicopters.

- ❌ No intention with the weather report.  
  ✔ Always add request hold or request divert.

- ❌ Not reporting when clear of dust.  
  ✔ Report clear of dust / field in sight to help ATC reopen flow.
