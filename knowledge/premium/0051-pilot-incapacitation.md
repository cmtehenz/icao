# Pilot Incapacitation

**Catalog ID:** 0051  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the most serious crew emergencies.

Pilot Incapacitation means a flight crew member becomes unable to perform their duties — due to medical emergency, unconsciousness, or sudden illness.

In a single-pilot H130 operation, this is immediately critical.

In two-crew operations, the remaining pilot must assume full control and declare the emergency.

You must fly the aircraft first, then communicate clearly with Mayday.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Pilot Incapacitation is the inability of a flight crew member to continue performing their duties due to medical or other emergency.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Incapacitação do piloto.

Incapacidade de um membro da tripulação de voo de continuar exercendo suas funções.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Pilot Incapacitation occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the absolute first priority
- assume all duties if the other pilot is incapacitated
- declare Mayday immediately
- request vectors to nearest suitable airport
- request medical assistance on landing

Pilot Incapacitation reporting commonly includes:

- pilot incapacitated or captain incapacitated
- first officer assuming control
- declaring emergency, pilot medical emergency
- request medical assistance and priority landing

**Important:** Pilot incapacitation is always a distress situation — Mayday. State who has control and request medical assistance. Aviate first — then communicate clearly with standard phraseology.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Pilot Incapacitation declarations to:

- clear all traffic from the aircraft's route
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable airport
- coordinate priority landing and medical services
- request souls on board and nature of emergency
- alert airport authorities and ambulance services

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are the first officer on a two-crew helicopter training flight, callsign ANAC123, approaching Navegantes.

The captain slumps forward and does not respond.

You take full control of the aircraft.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Navegantes Approach, ANAC123, captain incapacitated, first officer assuming control, request vectors to Navegantes.

Approach clears all traffic and coordinates medical services.

You complete a priority landing and report safe on the ground.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Mayday, say souls on board and nature of incapacitation.
2. Approach: ANAC123, turn right heading zero nine zero, descend at your discretion, Navegantes in sight.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway two nine cleared to land, medical services standing by.
4. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services alerted, wind two four zero at one zero knots.
5. Control: ANAC123, all traffic cleared from your approach, say assistance required on ground.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Pilot incapacitation is rare but tested in every ICAO exam.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Fly. Take control. Communicate.

Do not diagnose on frequency.

Do not delay the Mayday to check vitals.

Take the controls.

Declare Mayday.

Request vectors and medical assistance.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "piloto passou mal" or "comandante desmaiou" on frequency.

Use pilot incapacitated or captain incapacitated — ICAO standard terms.

State who has control: first officer assuming control.

In single-pilot H130 operations, your survival depends on training and autopilot if equipped.

In two-crew operations, the surviving pilot must act decisively.

The radio call brings help — but aircraft control saves lives.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if the captain became incapacitated during flight?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

After taking full control of the aircraft, I would declare Mayday Mayday Mayday, state my callsign, report captain incapacitated, state that the first officer is assuming control, and request vectors to the nearest suitable airport. I would request medical assistance on landing and provide souls on board when ATC asks.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "piloto passou mal" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use pilot incapacitated or captain incapacitated.

- ❌ Forgetting to state who has control of the aircraft.  
  ✔ Report first officer assuming control or pilot flying identity.

- ❌ Delaying Mayday to assess the medical condition.  
  ✔ Take control and declare Mayday immediately.

- ❌ Not requesting medical assistance on landing.  
  ✔ Request medical assistance — ATC coordinates ambulances.
