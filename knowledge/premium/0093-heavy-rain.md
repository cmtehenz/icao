# Heavy Rain

**Catalog ID:** 0093  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers heavy rain — intense precipitation that reduces visibility and can hide worse hazards.

Heavy Rain can obscure the windscreen, reduce visual references, and conceal windshear or embedded thunderstorms.

In the H130, heavy rain showers along the Brazilian coast are common and can make approaches unsafe.

You must report heavy rain, consider go-around or diversion, and remain alert for associated convective hazards.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 operational communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Heavy Rain is intense rainfall that significantly reduces visibility and may be associated with convective weather and windshear.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Chuva forte.

Precipitação intensa que reduz significativamente a visibilidade e pode estar associada a tempo convectivo e windshear.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Heavy Rain is encountered, the pilot should:

- assess visibility and whether the approach remains safe
- go around or divert if visual references are lost
- report heavy rain to ATC
- watch for windshear and thunderstorm activity
- delay takeoff or landing if rain intensity is unsafe

Heavy Rain reporting commonly includes:

- heavy rain on final or on departure
- visibility reduced in heavy rain
- going around due heavy rain
- request delay or diversion

**Important:** Heavy rain may be the visible edge of a thunderstorm. Treat it as a warning, not just water on the windscreen.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Heavy Rain reports to:

- update weather information for other aircraft
- advise of reduced visibility on final
- coordinate go-arounds and delays
- warn of possible windshear associated with rain shafts
- assist with diversions when needed

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on final to runway one four at Florianópolis in your H130.

A heavy rain shower reduces visibility and washes out visual references.

You go around and transmit:

Florianópolis Tower, ANAC123, going around, heavy rain on final, visibility reduced.

Tower advises following traffic and you hold until the shower moves through.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger heavy rain, cleared go-around.
2. Tower: ANAC123, heavy rain shower on final, advise intentions.
3. Approach: ANAC123, previous aircraft reported heavy rain and reduced visibility.
4. Tower: ANAC123, say if able to continue the approach.
5. Tower: ANAC123, delay takeoff, heavy rain over the field.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Florianópolis Tower, ANAC123, going around, heavy rain on final.
2. ANAC123, heavy rain, visibility reduced, request delay.
3. ANAC123, unable to depart due heavy rain, will advise when ready.
4. Approach, ANAC123, heavy rain shower ahead, request weather deviation.
5. ANAC123, clear of heavy rain, request another approach.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Heavy rain is more than wet glass.

It hides things — runway lights, wires, the horizon, and sometimes a microburst.

The sequence:

Assess. Go around if blind. Report. Wait or divert.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "chuva forte" on frequency.

Use heavy rain — ICAO standard term.

In the H130, a coastal shower can erase the approach path in seconds.

If you cannot see what you need to land — go around.

Train that decision until it is automatic.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if heavy rain made your approach unsafe?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would go around and report going around due heavy rain on final, with visibility reduced. I would request delay or another approach when the shower has passed, or divert if conditions do not improve.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing when the windscreen is opaque with rain.  
  ✔ Go around — you need visual references to land safely.

- ❌ Saying only "chuva" without intensity.  
  ✔ Use heavy rain when intensity is significant.

- ❌ Ignoring possible windshear in a rain shaft.  
  ✔ Treat heavy convective rain as a windshear warning.

- ❌ Not reporting after the go-around.  
  ✔ Tell ATC so other aircraft are warned.
