# MGB Chip Detector

**Catalog ID:** 0444  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the MGB Chip Detector — a warning that ferrous metal particles have been detected in the main gearbox oil, indicating possible internal wear or impending gearbox failure.

Metal in the main gearbox oil is never routine. It means gears, bearings, or other internal components may be shedding material into the lubrication system.

For H130 helicopter operations, an MGB chip detector caution requires power reduction, careful monitoring for vibration and temperature changes, and a landing as soon as practicable — with Mayday if secondary symptoms appear.

Understanding how to report an MGB chip detector warning in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

MGB Chip Detector is an indication that ferrous metal particles have been sensed in the main gearbox oil system, suggesting internal mechanical degradation of the main gearbox.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Detector de partículas da caixa de transmissão principal (MGB).

Indicação de que partículas metálicas ferrosas foram detectadas no sistema de óleo da caixa principal, sugerindo degradação mecânica interna da caixa de engrenagens principal.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the MGB chip detector activates, the pilot should:

- maintain control and reduce power to minimize gearbox load
- monitor MGB temperature, oil pressure, and vibration
- land as soon as practicable at a suitable site
- declare Pan Pan if indications are isolated and flight is stable
- declare Mayday if vibration, temperature rise, or pressure loss accompanies the chip

MGB chip detector reporting commonly includes:

- main gearbox chip detector activated
- reducing power, request priority landing
- abnormal vibration or temperature if present
- souls on board and fuel remaining when requested

**Important:** An isolated chip with stable parameters may be Pan Pan. A chip with vibration or rising temperature is distress — Mayday. Aviate first.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to MGB chip declarations to:

- provide priority routing to a suitable landing site
- clear traffic from the aircraft's path
- coordinate emergency services for possible transmission failure
- request souls on board and fuel remaining
- prepare for a possible forced landing if the gearbox fails

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at one thousand five hundred feet in your H130 from Joinville toward Curitiba.

The MGB chip detector caution illuminates. Temperature and pressure remain normal. There is no unusual vibration.

You reduce collective, declare Pan Pan, report main gearbox chip detector, and request vectors to Curitiba for a precautionary landing. Approach provides priority. After landing you shut down and request maintenance chip inspection before any further flight.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Chip means metal. Metal in the MGB means the transmission may be failing from the inside.

On the H130, treat every MGB chip as a reason to land soon. The absence of vibration is good news — not permission to continue the mission.

Correlate indications. Chip alone: Pan Pan, land as soon as practicable. Chip plus vibration, hot oil, or low pressure: Mayday, land as soon as possible.

Do not reset and forget. Do not stretch to a distant maintenance base if a suitable site is closer. Get the aircraft on the ground and let maintenance pull and inspect the chip detector.

Speak clearly: "main gearbox chip" — not a vague "chip light." Location matters.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

MGB chip illuminates with rising gearbox temperature and light vibration. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, main gearbox chip detector with vibration and temperature rising, reducing power, request immediate landing nearest suitable. I would give position and souls on board when able.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing because "it is only a chip light."  
  ✔ Land as soon as practicable; upgrade urgency if secondary symptoms appear.

- ❌ Saying "chip warning" without specifying main gearbox.  
  ✔ State MGB or main gearbox chip detector.

- ❌ Using Mayday for an isolated stable chip with no secondary symptoms.  
  ✔ Pan Pan is appropriate when stable; Mayday when distress signs appear.

- ❌ Hovering for a long time after landing before shutdown.  
  ✔ Land, shut down, and protect the gearbox from further load.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

An MGB chip is metal speaking through the oil.

Listen. Reduce load. Land.

Stable chip: Pan Pan and precautionary landing. Chip with vibration or heat: Mayday.

On the H130, the main gearbox carries everything. Do not ask it to carry a full mission after it has already shed metal.
