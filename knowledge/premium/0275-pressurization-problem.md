# Pressurization Problem

**Catalog ID:** 0275  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Pressurization Problem — abnormal cabin pressure control including failure to pressurize, overpressurization, or gradual or rapid depressurization, requiring oxygen use when applicable, emergency descent when required, and precise urgency or distress communication with ATC.

Pressurization problems are classic ICAO exam topics. Rapid decompression is typically distress — Mayday — with immediate descent. Gradual loss of pressure may be urgency — Pan Pan — still demanding prompt descent and clear reports.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, the H130 is normally unpressurized. You will not manage cabin differential like a jet — but you must speak the concept fluently for Part 2 and Part 3. Relate "get lower now" to emergency descent discipline without inventing a pressurized H130 cabin.

Master the English: pressurization problem, rapid decompression, emergency descent, requesting lower altitude.

## 📖 Meaning

Pressurization Problem is any abnormal condition of the aircraft pressurization system in which cabin altitude or differential pressure is not controlled as required — including failure to pressurize, excessive differential, or gradual or rapid depressurization — requiring abnormal or emergency procedures and ATC notification for descent and priority handling.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Problema de pressurização / Pressurization Problem.

Qualquer condição anormal do sistema de pressurização da aeronave em que a altitude de cabine ou a pressão diferencial não é controlada como necessário — incluindo falha de pressurizar, diferencial excessivo, ou despressurização gradual ou rápida — exigindo procedimentos anormais ou de emergência e notificação ao ATC para descida e prioridade.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a pressurization problem occurs, the crew should:

- don oxygen masks if cabin altitude is high or rising (pressurized aircraft)
- initiate emergency descent if required by AFM / cabin altitude
- declare Mayday for rapid decompression or critical cabin altitude; Pan Pan for manageable gradual issues as appropriate
- advise ATC of the pressurization problem and emergency descent if started
- request lower altitude and priority vectors / diversion as needed
- for H130: know the exam phraseology; do not claim a pressurized cabin the type does not have

Pressurization problem scenarios commonly include:

- rapid decompression — Mayday, oxygen, emergency descent
- gradual depressurization — Pan Pan, descent, diversion
- failure to pressurize after climb — level off / return
- cabin altitude warning in cruise
- exam radio: pressurization problem, descending, request lower

**Important:** Do not negotiate altitude before oxygen and descent when the situation demands them. Descend first when required; communicate as soon as practicable. On frequency use pressurization problem, rapid decompression, or cabin depressurization — not only Portuguese slang.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use pressurization problem reports to:

- clear lower altitudes immediately for emergency descent
- provide priority handling and traffic avoidance below
- coordinate the nearest suitable airport for diversion
- alert emergency services for a possible medical or structural event
- stop assigning high flight levels the crew cannot sustain

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are in a pressurized fixed-wing training scenario at flight level two five zero toward Brasília. Cabin altitude climbs rapidly. You don oxygen and begin emergency descent.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, rapid decompression, emergency descent to ten thousand, request priority.

Center: ANAC123, Mayday acknowledged, descend as required, all traffic advised, say souls and fuel when able.

Passing twelve thousand you update:

ANAC123, leveling ten thousand, pressurization problem, request vectors for diversion Curitiba, souls six, fuel two hours.

You flew the descent. Then you refined the plan. Oxygen first. Radio second — as soon as you can.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Center: ANAC123, Mayday acknowledged, descend at pilot's discretion, report level.
2. Center: ANAC123, roger pressurization problem, say altitude request.
3. Center: ANAC123, cleared present position direct diversion airport, priority handling.
4. Approach: ANAC123, expect straight-in, emergency services standing by.
5. Center: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Pressurization problems are altitude and oxygen problems before they are radio problems.

Rapid decompression: oxygen, descend, Mayday. Gradual problem: still get lower, Pan Pan or as required, do not sit at flight level hoping the cabin settles.

Brazilian pilots often say "perdeu pressurização." Prefer rapid decompression, cabin depressurization, or pressurization problem in ICAO English — then the action: emergency descent, requesting lower.

In the H130 you usually live in an unpressurized cabin. That does not excuse weak exam English. Know the calls. Know Mayday versus Pan Pan. Know that ATC needs your altitude intention immediately.

Never climb further with an unresolved pressurization failure. Lower is life.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Cabin altitude rises rapidly at high level. What do you do and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would don oxygen, begin an emergency descent per the AFM, and declare Mayday with rapid decompression or pressurization problem, stating that I am in emergency descent and requesting priority. When able I would report leveling at a safe altitude, souls and fuel, and request diversion to the nearest suitable airport.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Calling ATC before donning oxygen in a rapid decompression.  
  ✔ Oxygen and descent first; communicate as soon as practicable.

- ❌ Using only "pressurization problem" for a violent rapid decompression without Mayday.  
  ✔ Rapid decompression is distress — Mayday.

- ❌ Staying high to "finish the checklist" while cabin altitude climbs.  
  ✔ Descend to a safe cabin/ambient altitude per procedure.

- ❌ Inventing H130 pressurization drills as if the helicopter were a jet.  
  ✔ Teach exam English; keep type systems factual.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Pressurization problem is an altitude emergency with a systems name.

Oxygen. Descend. Declare. Divert.

The H130 may not pressurize — ICAO English still will. Learn both truths.

Rapid means Mayday. Gradual still means get lower. Tell ATC your altitude plan as soon as you can speak.

That is how pressurization problems end as landings — not as hypoxia stories.
