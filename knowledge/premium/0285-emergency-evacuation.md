# Emergency Evacuation

**Catalog ID:** 0285  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Emergency Evacuation — the rapid egress of all occupants from the aircraft after an emergency landing, rejected takeoff, fire, or other life-threatening condition on the ground. ICAO exams expect clear English for the decision, the command, and the radio call to ATC and fire services.

On airliners this includes slides, exits, and cabin crew commands. For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, evacuation still applies — rotors, doors, fire, and getting clear of the aircraft — without inventing escape slides the type does not have. Teach exam slide/exit language where required, then map to helicopter egress.

Master: emergency evacuation, evacuating, remain seated, fire services, slides armed (exam) / doors open (H130).

## 📖 Meaning

Emergency Evacuation is the immediate, ordered exit of all crew and passengers from the aircraft using available exits when remaining on board is more dangerous than leaving — typically due to fire, smoke, structural damage, or other ground emergencies — coordinated with ATC and airport fire services.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Evacuação de emergência / Emergency Evacuation.

Saída imediata e ordenada de toda a tripulação e passageiros da aeronave pelas saídas disponíveis quando permanecer a bordo é mais perigoso do que sair — tipicamente devido a fogo, fumaça, dano estrutural ou outras emergências no solo — coordenada com o ATC e os serviços de combate a incêndio do aeroporto.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When emergency evacuation is required, the crew should:

- stop the aircraft; set parking brake / secure as per AFM
- shut down engines / isolate as required for safe egress (and rotor considerations on H130)
- command evacuation; cabin crew open exits / deploy slides on airliners
- advise ATC: evacuating / emergency evacuation in progress
- for H130: stop, shut down or secure rotors per procedure, open doors, get clear — no slide language unless answering an airliner exam item
- account for souls when able; do not re-enter for belongings

Emergency evacuation reporting commonly includes:

- emergency evacuation / evacuating the aircraft
- stop on the runway / evacuating now
- fire / smoke / uncontrollable fire (reason)
- request fire services
- souls evacuated / all souls clear (when known)

**Important:** Evacuate for fire, smoke, or life threat. Do not evacuate for a minor abnormal with no hazard — unnecessary evacuation creates its own injuries. Decide, command, tell ATC.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to evacuation reports to:

- keep the runway or movement area clear for fire services
- stop other traffic and ground vehicles from approaching hazards
- confirm fire services are rolling to the aircraft
- obtain souls status when the crew can report
- protect the evacuation zone from jet blast, rotors, and vehicles

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Training scenario: after landing with a cabin fire not fully extinguished, you stop on the runway.

You transmit:

ANAC123, stopping on the runway, emergency evacuation, request fire services.

Tower: ANAC123, roger, evacuate, fire services rolling, advise when all souls are clear.

You command the cabin evacuation. On an airliner exam item you may reference slides and exits. In the H130 you shut down as required, open doors, and move clear of the rotor disc.

When able:

ANAC123, all souls clear of the aircraft, fire in cabin continuing.

The radio call supports the evacuation — it does not replace it.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger, stop on the runway, evacuate, fire services rolling.
2. Tower: ANAC123, advise when all souls are clear.
3. Ground: ANAC123, fire services at your position, say if still evacuating.
4. Tower: ANAC123, confirm emergency evacuation in progress.
5. Tower: ANAC123, remain clear of the runway after evacuation, do not re-enter.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, stopping on the runway, emergency evacuation, request fire services.
2. ANAC123, evacuating now, cabin fire.
3. ANAC123, all souls clear of the aircraft.
4. ANAC123, evacuation complete, fire services on scene.
5. ANAC123, will not re-enter, all souls clear, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Emergency evacuation is a ground decision with airborne preparation.

If you know you will evacuate — brief it before landing. Tell cabin. Tell ATC you will evacuate after landing. Then stop, secure, command, get out.

Brazilian pilots may say "evacuação" only. Prefer emergency evacuation or evacuating the aircraft in ICAO English — plus the reason: fire, smoke, structural damage.

On airliner exams, know escape slide and exit language. In the H130, know rotor hazard, door egress, and getting clear of the disc. Do not claim slides on a helicopter. Do not fail the exam for not knowing slide vocabulary when the question is airliner-based.

Never delay evacuation to finish a long radio monologue while fire grows. Secure. Evacuate. Transmit when you can.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You land with an uncontrollable cabin fire. What do you do after landing and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would stop on the runway, secure the aircraft per procedure, command an emergency evacuation, and advise ATC that we are evacuating and request fire services. When able I would report all souls clear. I would not taxi to the gate with an uncontrollable fire, and I would not re-enter the aircraft for personal items.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Taxiing to the apron with active fire before deciding to evacuate.  
  ✔ Stop, evacuate, tell ATC — fire services to the aircraft.

- ❌ Using only "we leave the airplane" on the ICAO exam.  
  ✔ Say emergency evacuation / evacuating the aircraft.

- ❌ Claiming escape slides on the H130.  
  ✔ Exam slide language for airliners; helicopter doors and rotor clearance for the type.

- ❌ Long radio calls before commanding evacuation.  
  ✔ Evacuate first; update ATC as soon as practicable.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You land with cabin fire not under control. You stop on the runway.

**Your first task**

What do you say to Tower as you begin evacuation?

**Your second task**

Fire services ask if everyone is out. What do you say when all are clear?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, stopping on the runway, emergency evacuation, cabin fire, request fire services. Second task: ANAC123, all souls clear of the aircraft, fire in cabin continuing. I would command the evacuation immediately and not delay egress for a longer transmission.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Emergency evacuation is the last checklist — and the first priority once the aircraft is stopped in a life-threatening condition.

Stop. Secure. Command. Get clear. Tell ATC.

Airliner exams want slides and exits. The H130 wants doors and rotor awareness. Learn both. Use the one that matches the question and the aircraft.

Do not evacuate without a reason. Do not stay on board when fire or smoke makes the cabin unsurvivable.

That is how evacuations save lives — by being decisive, clear on the radio, and faster than the fire.
