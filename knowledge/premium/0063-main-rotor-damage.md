# Main Rotor Damage

**Catalog ID:** 0063  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers main rotor damage — a catastrophic helicopter emergency.

Main Rotor Damage means the main rotor blades or hub have been damaged — vibration, imbalance, or structural compromise threatens flight safety.

In the H130, the main rotor provides all lift and thrust.

Damaged main rotor blades can fail catastrophically without warning.

You must aviate first, reduce power, then communicate with Mayday.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Main Rotor Damage is physical damage to the main rotor blades or hub, causing abnormal vibration and threatening catastrophic failure.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Dano no rotor principal.

Dano físico às pás ou ao hub do rotor principal, causando vibração anormal e ameaçando falha catastrófica.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Main Rotor Damage occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the absolute first priority
- reduce power smoothly to minimize rotor loads
- avoid abrupt control inputs that could worsen damage
- declare Mayday immediately
- request vectors to nearest suitable landing area

Main Rotor Damage reporting commonly includes:

- main rotor damage or abnormal main rotor vibration
- reducing power, request immediate landing
- souls on board and position
- possible autorotation if power loss follows

**Important:** Main rotor damage is always a distress situation — Mayday. Structural rotor failure can be fatal. Aviate first — then communicate clearly with standard phraseology.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Main Rotor Damage declarations to:

- clear all traffic from the aircraft's route immediately
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable landing area
- coordinate emergency services on the ground
- request souls on board and exact position
- prepare for possible autorotation or crash landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at one thousand five hundred feet near Florianópolis in your H130 when you hear a loud bang and severe vibration begins.

You suspect main rotor blade strike or damage — possibly bird strike or foreign object.

You reduce collective smoothly and maintain rotor RPM.

When stable, you transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, main rotor damage, severe vibration, request immediate landing.

Approach clears all traffic and vectors you to the nearest helipad.

You execute a cautious approach and land safely.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, Mayday acknowledged, say souls on board and nature of damage.

4. Tower: ANAC123, wind one eight zero at one five knots, cleared to land any area.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Main rotor damage is a race against structural failure.

Causes include bird strike, foreign object, ground contact, or material fatigue.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Aviate. Gentle. Mayday.

Fly the aircraft first.

Reduce power smoothly — abrupt inputs can worsen the damage.

Maintain rotor RPM within limits.

Do not fight severe vibration with aggressive cyclic.

When stable — Mayday immediately.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "dano no rotor" or "vibração forte" on frequency.

Use main rotor damage or abnormal main rotor vibration — ICAO standard terms.

In the H130, any severe unexplained vibration is Mayday until proven otherwise.

Train the reflex to reduce power gently and land immediately.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if you suspected main rotor damage during flight?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain aircraft control, reduce power smoothly, and declare Mayday Mayday Mayday. I would state my callsign, report main rotor damage or abnormal main rotor vibration, describe severity, state position and souls on board, and request immediate landing at the nearest suitable area. I would be prepared to autorotate if power loss follows.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Making abrupt control inputs with severe vibration.  
  ✔ Reduce power smoothly and avoid aggressive cyclic movements.

- ❌ Saying "rotor principal danificado" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use main rotor damage or abnormal main rotor vibration.

- ❌ Continuing flight to a distant airport.  
  ✔ Land at the nearest suitable area immediately.

- ❌ Treating severe vibration as Pan Pan.  
  ✔ Main rotor damage is always Mayday.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You hear a loud impact and severe vibration begins at eight hundred feet near Joinville in your H130.

**Your first task**

After reducing power smoothly and maintaining control, transmit your distress declaration.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

Approach asks for the nature of the damage. What do you report?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report clearly: main rotor damage, ANAC123. I would describe severe abnormal vibration following a loud impact, state I suspect possible blade damage or foreign object strike, confirm I am reducing power and maintaining rotor RPM, and request immediate landing at Joinville.
