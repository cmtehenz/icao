# Running Landing

**Catalog ID:** 0069  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the running landing — a technique used when a normal hover landing is not possible or advisable.

Running Landing means a landing with forward ground speed, where the helicopter touches down while still moving forward and may slide or roll to a stop.

In the H130, running landings are used in autorotation, high-density altitude conditions, or when power is insufficient for a hover.

You must inform ATC when executing a non-standard landing and report safe on the ground.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Running Landing is a helicopter landing technique with forward speed at touchdown, used when hover capability is limited or unavailable.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a Running Landing is planned or executed, the pilot should:

- select a suitable landing area with sufficient length and surface
- inform ATC of intentions if in controlled airspace
- execute approach with appropriate airspeed and flare
- touch down with forward speed and bring the aircraft to a stop
- report safe on the ground after landing

Running Landing reporting commonly includes:

- executing running landing
- landing area and position
- souls on board when in emergency context
- safe on the ground after touchdown

**Important:** Running landings in emergency or autorotation context require Mayday. Planned running landings due to density altitude may warrant informing Tower in advance.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Running Landing reports to:

- clear traffic from the landing area
- coordinate with ground services if emergency landing
- anticipate non-standard landing geometry at heliports
- request position and souls on board in emergency cases
- monitor until safe on the ground is reported

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are completing an autorotation after engine failure near Joinville in your H130.

You selected a level field with sufficient length for a running landing.

During the approach, you transmitted:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Joinville Tower, ANAC123, engine failure, autorotation, executing running landing field two miles east.

You touch down at thirty knots, slide briefly on the grass, and come to a stop.

You report safe on the ground.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, Mayday acknowledged, say landing area and souls on board.
2. Tower: ANAC123, all traffic cleared, report safe on the ground.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land helipad, wind calm, caution light tailwind.
4. Ground: ANAC123, report when clear of the landing area.
5. Tower: ANAC123, running landing approved at your selected area, emergency services notified.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Not every landing is a hover.

Sometimes power, weight, or altitude means you must land with forward speed.

The sequence:

Pick the surface. Flare. Touch down rolling. Stop.

A running landing is not a crash — it is a controlled technique.

In autorotation, it is often the only option.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "pouso corrido" or "pouso com velocidade" on frequency.

Use executing running landing — ICAO standard phraseology.

In the H130, train running landings in a safe environment before you need one for real.

Inform ATC when they need to clear traffic or send help.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

When would you use a running landing and what would you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would use a running landing when hover capability is insufficient, such as in autorotation, high density altitude, or maximum weight conditions. I would inform ATC of my intentions, state executing running landing, describe the landing area, and report safe on the ground after touchdown. In an emergency, I would declare Mayday first.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Attempting hover landing when power is insufficient.  
  ✔ Recognize when running landing is the safer option.

- ❌ Saying "pouso corrido" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use executing running landing.

- ❌ Not informing ATC in controlled airspace.  
  ✔ Report intentions so Tower can clear traffic.

- ❌ Forgetting to report safe on the ground.  
  ✔ Always confirm landing complete to ATC.
