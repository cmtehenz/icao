# Terrain Awareness Warning

**Catalog ID:** 0402  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Terrain Awareness Warning — predictive alerts from TAWS or similar systems that the projected flight path conflicts with terrain ahead, often before classic GPWS reactive modes activate.

Terrain awareness gives you time — if you use it.

In the H130, predictive terrain alerts matter on night VFR transitions, IFR arrivals into Confins and mountainous Minas Gerais, and low-level coastal routes along cliffs. ICAO Part 2 and Part 3 require clear reporting of terrain warnings and escape actions.

## 📖 Meaning

A Terrain Awareness Warning is a predictive caution or warning from a Terrain Awareness and Warning System indicating that the aircraft's projected flight path conflicts with terrain ahead, requiring an immediate change of trajectory, typically a climb and/or turn away from the hazard.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Aviso de consciência situacional de terreno / Terrain Awareness Warning (TAWS).

Alerta preditivo de cautela ou aviso de um sistema de consciência de terreno indicando que a trajetória projetada da aeronave conflita com o relevo à frente, exigindo mudança imediata de trajetória, tipicamente subida e/ou curva para longe do perigo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a Terrain Awareness Warning activates, the crew should:

- climb and/or turn away from the displayed terrain conflict immediately
- do not wait for a pull-up mode if a predictive warning already demands action
- compare the alert with charts and MSA when workload allows — after the escape starts
- advise ATC: terrain warning, climbing, or turning away from terrain
- request a higher altitude or revised routing

Terrain Awareness Warning reporting commonly includes:

- terrain warning, climbing
- terrain ahead, request turn and climb
- TAWS warning, diverting left of track
- clear of terrain, request further clearance

**Important:** Predictive warnings exist to prevent the pull-up. Treat a terrain caution as a command to change path early.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to Terrain Awareness Warning reports to:

- issue immediate climb or turn clearances
- verify minimum vectoring altitudes for the sector
- stop further descent clearances into high terrain
- coordinate revised STAR or approach routing
- protect other aircraft following the same track

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Confins at night in your H130 on a vector that feels low relative to the ridges ahead.

A terrain awareness warning activates: terrain ahead.

You climb and request a turn.

You transmit:

Confins Approach, ANAC123, terrain warning, climbing, request turn left and four thousand.

Approach issues an immediate left turn and climb. The warning ceases. You continue for a safe approach from a proper altitude.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger terrain warning, climb and maintain four thousand, turn left heading three three zero.
2. Approach: ANAC123, minimum altitude this sector three thousand five hundred, say altitude.
3. Control: ANAC123, radar terrain advisory, confirm you are climbing.
4. Approach: ANAC123, cleared present heading, maintain four thousand until established.
5. Approach: ANAC123, when ready, expect ILS runway one six.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, terrain warning, climbing four thousand, left heading three three zero.

3. ANAC123, affirmative climbing, request further left for terrain.
4. ANAC123, maintaining four thousand until established.
5. ANAC123, ready for ILS runway one six.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Terrain awareness is the system saying: your future path hits rock.

That is better than pull up — if you react now.

Do not silence the alert and continue the same descent. Change something: altitude, heading, or both.

In the H130, moving map terrain displays and radio altimeter trends are part of the same mental model. If the picture shows red ahead, climb or turn before the voice gets angry.

Brazilian night arrivals punish "almost MSA" flying. Brief the MSA. Honor the warning. Tell ATC why you climbed so they do not push you back down into the same ridge.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You receive a terrain awareness warning on vectors. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would immediately climb and, if needed, turn away from the terrain conflict. I would transmit: ANAC123, terrain warning, climbing, request turn left and four thousand. I would not continue the descent on the same track. After the warning clears, I would request revised vectors for the approach at or above a safe altitude.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Waiting for "pull up" before reacting to a predictive caution.  
  ✔ Act on the terrain awareness warning early.

- ❌ Explaining chart interpretation on frequency during the escape.  
  ✔ Climb/turn first. Brief ATC with short standard phrases.

- ❌ Accepting a lower altitude again on the same heading.  
  ✔ Request routing that clears terrain, not a repeat of the conflict.

- ❌ Saying only "we had a warning" without the word terrain.  
  ✔ Say terrain warning so ATC understands the hazard type.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Terrain Warning, Climbing, Request Turn

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, tuh-RAYN WOR-ning, KLIME-ing, reh-KWEST TURN

**Word Stress**

- Terrain → tuh-RAYN (stress on second)
- Warning → WOR-ning
- Request → reh-KWEST

Practice:

Terrain warning... climbing... request turn.

Together:

ANAC123, terrain warning, climbing, request turn left and four thousand.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**TAWS** — **T**urn or climb now, **A**nnounce terrain warning, **W**ait until clear, **S**tay at or above MSA.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Terrain awareness warning on a night vector toward high ground near Belo Horizonte.

**Your first task**

What do you say?

**Your second task**

Approach clears climb but keeps you on the same heading toward the ridge. Warning continues. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, terrain warning, climbing, request turn left and four thousand. Second task: ANAC123, still terrain warning, request immediate turn away from terrain, unable present heading. Be explicit when the heading remains unsafe.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Terrain awareness warnings buy time. Spend that time climbing or turning — not debating.

Name the hazard. Change the path. Protect the MSA.

In the H130 over Brazilian terrain, predictive alerts are part of professional IFR discipline.

React early. Speak clearly. Arrive above the rocks — not through them.
