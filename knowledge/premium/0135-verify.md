# Verify

**Catalog ID:** 0135  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Verify — an ICAO-standard phrase requesting that a specific value, setting, or condition be checked and confirmed.

Verify is used when ATC has reason to question whether a pilot has a correct value set — for example, a wrong altitude read on radar, a squawk code that does not match, or an altimeter setting that appears off.

In the H130, flying in and out of Brazilian airports like Curitiba, Congonhas, or São Paulo, ATC will occasionally use Verify to prompt you to check a specific setting. Responding to Verify correctly — and understanding how it differs from Confirm and Say Again — is a core ICAO Level 4 radio skill.

## 📖 Meaning

Verify is an ICAO-standard word instructing the addressed station to check a specific value, setting, or condition and report back. It is typically used by ATC to prompt a pilot to check whether a value they are transmitting or displaying matches what was assigned or is correct.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Verifique / Confirme verificando.

Palavra ICAO padrão que instrui a estação destinatária a verificar um valor, configuração ou condição específica e relatar de volta. É tipicamente usada pelo ATC para solicitar ao piloto que verifique se um valor que está transmitindo ou exibindo corresponde ao que foi atribuído ou está correto.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC uses Verify, the pilot should:

- immediately check the specific value or setting referenced
- report back what the instrument or system reads
- correct any discrepancy found
- read back the corrected value and await ATC confirmation

When pilots use Verify:

- to ask ATC to check a specific value in their system
- to request verification of a clearance limit or boundary
- to confirm ATC has the correct information about the flight

Verify operations commonly include:

- Verify altitude — you are showing below cleared level
- Verify squawk — transponder is showing a different code
- Verify QNH — your altimetry appears off from expected
- Verify your position — you appear off course on radar
- Verify you are on the correct frequency

**Important:** Verify prompts a check and report. It is different from Confirm, which verifies a specific piece of information already held. Verify says: check your system and tell me what you see. Confirm says: is this specific value correct?

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers use Verify when:

- radar shows a pilot at an altitude different from their clearance
- the transponder code does not match the assigned squawk
- the aircraft track appears inconsistent with the clearance
- a pilot reports a condition that contradicts ATC data
- altimetry discrepancy is detected between pilot report and radar

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at flight level one two zero inbound to Curitiba.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, verify altitude — you are showing one one five hundred.

ATC's radar shows you lower than cleared. You check your altimeter and instruments:

ANAC123, verifying... altitude one one five hundred — we may have a QNH error. QNH one zero one six set.

Approach responds:

ANAC123, QNH one zero zero eight, not one zero one six — correct your altimeter.

You correct:

ANAC123, correcting, QNH one zero zero eight, now passing altitude one two zero.

The Verify exchange caught an altimetry discrepancy before it developed into an altitude deviation. Your altimeter was set to the wrong QNH. ATC's radar data alerted them. Verify opened the check.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, verify altitude — you are indicating one one thousand.
2. Control: ANAC123, verify squawk — you are showing four five zero zero, not four four five two.
3. Approach: ANAC123, verify QNH setting.
4. Tower: ANAC123, verify you are holding short of runway one seven right.
5. Approach: ANAC123, verify you are tracking the correct ILS course.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, verifying altitude — we are at one two thousand, flight level one two zero.
2. ANAC123, verifying squawk — we have four four five two set.
3. ANAC123, verifying QNH — we have one zero one six. Is that correct?
4. ANAC123, affirm, holding short runway one seven right.
5. ANAC123, verifying — we are tracking localizer, course correct.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Verify is ATC telling you: something does not look right on my radar. Check your systems.

When you receive a Verify call, treat it as a priority.

Stop other tasks. Check the referenced system immediately. Report back what you see.

Do not say "we are fine" without actually checking.

Verify is a prompt for action and reporting — not a reassurance request.

In the H130, single-pilot operations mean you are the only person to check the altimeter, transponder, or course. When ATC says Verify, you are the entire quality control system for that check.

Common scenarios in Brazilian operations:

QNH transition errors — especially around the São Paulo TMA where the QNH can change rapidly between ATIS updates.

Transponder code errors — after a frequency change, the squawk may not have been updated.

Altitude deviations — turbulence or autopilot disconnect causing unnoticed altitude change.

All of these are caught early by a Verify call. Respond professionally: check, report, correct.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What should a pilot do when ATC transmits "Verify altitude"?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would immediately check my altimeter and crosscheck with the VSI and any backup instruments. I would report back what I see: ANAC123, verifying — altitude is [current altitude]. If there is a discrepancy from my cleared altitude, I would report it and ask for guidance. If my altimeter setting appears incorrect, I would report the QNH I have set and ask for the correct one. Verify is a prompt to check immediately and report honestly — not to reassure ATC that everything is fine without checking.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Responding to Verify with "affirm, all correct" without actually checking.  
  ✔ Check the referenced system immediately. Report what you find, accurately.

- ❌ Confusing Verify and Confirm — using them interchangeably.  
  ✔ Verify prompts a system check and report. Confirm requests verification of a specific known value.

- ❌ Delaying the response to a Verify call while continuing other tasks.  
  ✔ Verify is a priority call. Check immediately and respond promptly.

- ❌ Saying only "verifying" without reporting the result.  
  ✔ Complete the check and report the value: Verifying — altitude one two thousand, ANAC123.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

São Paulo Control transmits: "ANAC123, verify squawk — you are showing four five zero zero, assigned was four four five two."

**Your first task**

You check your transponder and find you have four five zero zero set — an error from the last frequency change. What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

You correct the squawk to four four five two. What do you transmit to ATC after the correction?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

After checking and finding the error, I would transmit: ANAC123, verifying squawk — we have four five zero zero, correcting now to four four five two. After setting the correct code and confirming it is mode C active: ANAC123, squawk four four five two now set. I would then listen for ATC to confirm the correct code is showing on radar. Transparency about the error and prompt correction is the professional response. Discovering a transponder code error and self-correcting immediately is exactly what Verify is designed to prompt.
