# Fog

**Catalog ID:** 0092  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers fog — a cloud on the surface that can close airports and trap VFR helicopters.

Fog is visible moisture that reduces visibility near the ground, often forming overnight or along the Brazilian coast.

In the H130, fog can make helipads and runways unusable within minutes.

You must recognize fog formation, divert early, and use precise phraseology with ATC.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 operational communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Fog is a surface-based cloud of tiny water droplets that reduces visibility, often to less than one kilometer.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Nevoeiro / névoa.

Nuvem junto à superfície formada por gotículas de água que reduz a visibilidade, frequentemente para menos de um quilômetro.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Fog is present or forming, the pilot should:

- check current visibility and trends before departure or approach
- divert or hold if fog makes the destination unusable
- inform ATC of fog and intentions
- request IFR approach only if equipped and cleared
- avoid scud running under fog layers

Fog reporting commonly includes:

- fog at the airport or fog forming
- visibility in fog
- request diversion due fog
- unable to land due fog, going around

**Important:** Fog is a destination killer. Always have an alternate when fog is forecast or possible.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Fog conditions by:

- issuing visibility and RVR updates
- closing or restricting visual approaches when needed
- providing diversion assistance
- sequencing IFR approaches in low visibility
- advising aircraft of rapid visibility changes

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Navegantes in your H130 at dawn.

Tower reports fog forming and visibility dropping to eight hundred meters.

You assess and transmit:

Navegantes Tower, ANAC123, unable to land due fog, request diversion to Florianópolis.

Tower coordinates the diversion and you proceed to Florianópolis where visibility remains acceptable.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, visibility eight hundred meters in fog, advise intentions.
2. Tower: ANAC123, fog forming rapidly, runway visual range decreasing.
3. Approach: ANAC123, diversion to Florianópolis approved, contact Approach.
4. Tower: ANAC123, fog bank on final, say if continuing.
5. Approach: ANAC123, destination reporting fog, say alternate.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Navegantes Tower, ANAC123, unable to land due fog, request diversion to Florianópolis.
2. ANAC123, fog on final, going around, request vectors to alternate.
3. ANAC123, delaying departure due fog, will advise when ready.
4. Approach, ANAC123, destination in fog, proceeding to alternate.
5. ANAC123, request current visibility in fog.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Fog is patience weather.

It forms. It thickens. It lifts — sometimes slowly.

The sequence:

Check. Decide. Divert. Do not scud run.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "nevoeiro" or "cerração" on frequency.

Use fog — ICAO standard term.

In the H130, a coastal fog bank can block the entire approach path.

Never descend below safe altitude hoping to "find the pad."

Train diversion calls for fog until they feel routine.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if fog made your destination unusable?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report unable to land due fog and request diversion to my alternate. I would state the alternate airport and request vectors or clearance as required. If already on approach, I would go around and then divert.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Scud running under fog to find the helipad.  
  ✔ Divert or hold — do not descend into unknown visibility.

- ❌ Saying "nevoeiro fechado" without clear intention.  
  ✔ Use unable to land due fog, request diversion.

- ❌ Departing without an alternate when fog is possible.  
  ✔ Always plan an alternate for fog-prone destinations.

- ❌ Not going around when fog appears on final.  
  ✔ Go around early and reassess.

- Diversion → dy-VER-zhun

Unable to land due fog, request diversion to Florianópolis, ANAC123.

Speak the alternate clearly.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**FOG** — **F**orecast and check trends, **O**ptions — divert early, **G**o around if fog appears on final.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

On final to Congonhas at dawn, fog reduces visibility below minima.

**Your first task**

Transmit your go-around and intention.

**Your second task**

Tower asks for your alternate. What do you report?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report: going around, unable to land due fog, ANAC123, request diversion to Guarulhos — or another suitable alternate. I would confirm fuel remaining if requested.
