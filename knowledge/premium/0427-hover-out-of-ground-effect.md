# Hover Out of Ground Effect

**Catalog ID:** 0427  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Hover Out of Ground Effect — HOGE — holding a stationary hover above the height where ground cushion assists the rotor system, requiring greater power and precise control.

HOGE is daily H130 reality: elevated helipads, offshore decks, winching, and confined-area approaches where you cannot rely on ground effect.

You must communicate power-limited hover, inability to maintain HOGE, and go-around intentions in ICAO English for Parts 2 and 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Hover Out of Ground Effect is a helicopter hover performed at a height where the rotor system no longer benefits significantly from ground cushion, requiring higher power to maintain a stationary position and leaving less margin for wind, density altitude, and weight.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pairamento fora do efeito solo / Hover Out of Ground Effect (HOGE).

Pairamento de helicóptero realizado a uma altura em que o sistema de rotor não se beneficia significativamente do colchão de ar do solo, exigindo mais potência para manter a posição estacionária e deixando menor margem para vento, altitude-densidade e peso.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When planning or performing a HOGE, the pilot should:

- calculate HOGE power required versus available before the approach
- account for density altitude, weight, and wind
- brief a go-around if power margin is insufficient
- avoid prolonged HOGE when unnecessary
- report unable to maintain hover if power or control is lost

HOGE reporting commonly includes:

- established hover out of ground effect
- unable to maintain HOGE, going around
- power limited, request alternate landing site
- lifting / departing from HOGE

**Important:** HOGE is a performance exam. If charts or feel say you cannot hold it, do not attempt a long hover. Plan an approach that uses ground effect or a running landing when available.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers need HOGE-related calls to:

- understand why a helicopter may delay landing or go around
- clear airspace for a power-limited departure or orbit
- coordinate alternate pads when HOGE is not possible
- support winching or elevated-pad operations
- sequence traffic around a helicopter holding high hover

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are approaching an elevated hospital helipad in your H130 on a hot afternoon. Density altitude is high. Weight is near max.

You attempt a HOGE to confirm pad clear. Collective rises. Power margin is thin. The helicopter settles.

You go around:

Unable to maintain hover out of ground effect, going around, request another approach with reduced fuel burn or alternate pad.

You reduce weight or wait for cooler conditions, then return with adequate margin.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, report established hover, pad traffic advised.
2. Tower: ANAC123, roger going around, report intentions.
3. Tower: ANAC123, alternate pad available north, say if able.
4. Tower: ANAC123, orbit east, advise when ready for another approach.
5. Tower: ANAC123, cleared approach, report on pad.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, wilco, will report established hover.
2. ANAC123, going around, unable to maintain HOGE, will advise intentions.
3. ANAC123, able alternate pad north, request approach.
4. ANAC123, orbiting east, will advise ready.
5. ANAC123, commencing approach, will report on pad.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

HOGE is where honesty beats ego.

Ground effect is a gift. Outside it, the H130 needs real power. Hot, high, heavy — the three H's that shrink HOGE margin.

Part 3 loves: why did you go around from the elevated pad? Answer: unable to maintain hover out of ground effect, power limited, going around for performance.

Never sit in a settling HOGE hoping for a miracle. Leave early. Communicate clearly. Come back with margin — less fuel, cooler air, better wind, or a different site.

Brazilian rooftop pads and offshore decks often demand HOGE. Know the chart before you commit.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You cannot maintain a hover out of ground effect over an elevated pad. What do you say and do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would initiate an immediate go-around on a clear path and transmit: unable to maintain hover out of ground effect, going around, request orbit or alternate landing site. I would not continue a settling hover. After assessing weight, density altitude, and wind, I would request another approach only when performance allows, or divert to a suitable alternate.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Forcing a HOGE when power is clearly insufficient.  
  ✔ Go around early. Performance is not negotiable.

- ❌ Not telling ATC why you went around.  
  ✔ State unable to maintain HOGE so they can help with sequencing or alternates.

- ❌ Ignoring density altitude on hot Brazilian afternoons.  
  ✔ Recalculate HOGE before every elevated approach.

- ❌ Prolonged HOGE for "a better look" with thin margin.  
  ✔ One look, then land or leave.
