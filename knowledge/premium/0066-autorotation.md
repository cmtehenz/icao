# Autorotation

**Catalog ID:** 0066  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers autorotation — the most important emergency procedure every helicopter pilot must master.

Autorotation is a state of flight in which the engine is no longer driving the main rotor, and the rotor is turned by upward airflow through the blades.

In the H130, autorotation is the emergency procedure when engine power is lost and a safe powered landing is not possible.

You must aviate first, enter autorotation immediately, then communicate with Mayday.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Autorotation is an emergency flight condition where the main rotor is driven by upward airflow rather than engine power, allowing a controlled descent and landing.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Autorrotação.

Condição de voo de emergência em que o rotor principal é acionado pelo fluxo de ar ascendente, permitindo descida e pouso controlados sem potência do motor.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When entering Autorotation, the pilot should:

- lower collective immediately to maintain rotor RPM in the green arc
- establish a stable autorotative glide at recommended airspeed
- select a suitable landing area within glide range
- declare Mayday and inform ATC of position and intentions
- flare and cushion landing at the appropriate height above ground

Autorotation reporting commonly includes:

- entering autorotation or engine failure, entering autorotation
- position and approximate landing area
- souls on board
- request traffic cleared from landing area

**Important:** Engine failure requiring autorotation is distress — Mayday. Enter autorotation before you speak. Aviate first — collective down, then communicate.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Autorotation declarations to:

- clear all traffic from the aircraft's route and landing area immediately
- coordinate with other sectors and airport authorities
- request souls on board and position
- prepare emergency services at the anticipated landing site
- monitor the aircraft until landing or power restoration

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are at eight hundred feet over the outskirts of Florianópolis in your H130 when the engine fails without warning.

You lower collective immediately and enter autorotation.

Rotor RPM stabilizes in the green arc as you establish glide at sixty knots.

You select a sports field ahead and transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Florianópolis Tower, ANAC123, engine failure, entering autorotation, landing sports field south of the airport.

Tower clears traffic and dispatches emergency services.

You flare at the appropriate height and land safely in the field.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, Mayday acknowledged, say souls on board and landing area.
2. Tower: ANAC123, all traffic cleared, emergency services notified.
3. Approach: ANAC123, radar contact, say position and intentions.
4. Tower: ANAC123, wind calm at the field, report when safe on the ground.
5. Tower: ANAC123, emergency vehicles en route to your position.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Autorotation is not optional training — it is survival.

The sequence every helicopter pilot learns:

Collective down. Glide. Land.

Lower collective immediately when power is lost.

Maintain rotor RPM — without RPM, you have no lift at the flare.

Pick your landing area early.

Mayday when you can speak — but collective comes first.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "autorotação" or "sem motor" on frequency.

Use entering autorotation or engine failure, entering autorotation — ICAO standard terms.

In the H130, train autorotation until collective-down is reflex.

The radio does not restore engine power — autorotation does.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if your engine failed and you needed to enter autorotation?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would lower collective immediately to enter autorotation and maintain rotor RPM. I would declare Mayday Mayday Mayday, state my callsign, report engine failure and entering autorotation, state my position and intended landing area, and provide souls on board. I would report safe on the ground after landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Calling Mayday before lowering collective.  
  ✔ Collective down first — enter autorotation, then transmit.

- ❌ Saying "sem potência" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use entering autorotation or engine failure, entering autorotation.

- ❌ Raising collective during autorotation descent.  
  ✔ Maintain recommended autorotative glide — collective down until flare.

- ❌ Not selecting a landing area early.  
  ✔ Choose landing area within glide range and inform ATC.
