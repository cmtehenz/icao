# Embedded Thunderstorm

**Catalog ID:** 0084  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers embedded thunderstorms — convective cells hidden inside cloud layers or IMC.

An Embedded Thunderstorm is a thunderstorm that cannot be seen visually because it is obscured by surrounding clouds or precipitation.

In the H130, embedded thunderstorms are especially dangerous on IFR coastal and mountain routes in Brazil when radar or visual cues are limited.

You must use all available weather information, request ATC assistance, and deviate early when turbulence or precipitation suggests a hidden cell.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

An Embedded Thunderstorm is a thunderstorm obscured by cloud or precipitation, making visual detection difficult or impossible.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Tempestade embutida.

Tempestade oculta por nuvens ou precipitação, dificultando ou impossibilitando a detecção visual.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When an Embedded Thunderstorm is suspected, the pilot should:

- report unexpected turbulence, hail, or heavy precipitation to ATC
- request weather deviation or radar vectors immediately
- avoid continuing into increasing convective activity
- divert if a safe path around the weather cannot be established
- expect severe turbulence and wind shear near the cell

Embedded Thunderstorm reporting commonly includes:

- embedded thunderstorm suspected
- request weather deviation or radar vectors
- encountering severe turbulence in IMC
- unable to continue present routing due weather

**Important:** What you cannot see can still destroy the aircraft. Treat unexplained severe turbulence in IMC as a possible embedded thunderstorm.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Embedded Thunderstorm reports to:

- provide radar vectors around known convective cells
- approve weather deviations when traffic permits
- relay pilot reports to other aircraft
- coordinate diversions to suitable airports
- sequence traffic clear of convective weather

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are IFR in IMC between Curitiba and Florianópolis in your H130.

Without visual cues, you encounter rapidly increasing turbulence and heavy rain.

You transmit immediately:

Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, embedded thunderstorm suspected, encountering severe turbulence, request weather deviation and radar vectors.

Approach provides vectors clear of the cell.

You divert slightly and continue when clear of weather.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger embedded thunderstorm, turn right heading one two zero, vectors around weather.
2. Approach: ANAC123, weather radar shows cells ahead, advise intentions.
3. Center: ANAC123, deviation approved, report clear of weather.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say ride conditions and altitude.
5. Approach: ANAC123, previous aircraft reported embedded thunderstorm, request your PIREP.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Embedded thunderstorms hide inside the cloud.

You may not see lightning. You may not see the cell.

You feel it — turbulence, hail, torrential rain.

The sequence:

Report. Deviate. Vector. Divert if needed.

Do not wait for visual confirmation in IMC.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "tempestade escondida" or "cumulonimbus embutido" on frequency.

Use embedded thunderstorm — ICAO and FAA standard term.

In the H130, you have less structural and performance margin than a transport jet.

Treat unexplained severe turbulence in cloud as a threat until proven otherwise.

Train early deviation and clear PIREPs.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is an embedded thunderstorm and what would you tell ATC if you encountered one?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

An embedded thunderstorm is a thunderstorm hidden inside cloud or precipitation. If I encountered unexplained severe turbulence or heavy convective precipitation in IMC, I would report embedded thunderstorm suspected, request weather deviation or radar vectors, and divert if a safe path was not available. I would report when clear of weather.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing in IMC through increasing convective turbulence.  
  ✔ Request radar vectors or weather deviation immediately.

- ❌ Saying "tempestade escondida" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use embedded thunderstorm.

- ❌ Waiting to see the cell visually before acting.  
  ✔ In IMC, feel and instruments may be your only warning.

- ❌ Not filing a PIREP after the encounter.  
  ✔ Report conditions to help ATC and other aircraft.
