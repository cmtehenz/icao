# Firefighters on the Way

**Catalog ID:** 0215  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2, Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Firefighters on the Way — the advisory that airport rescue and firefighting (ARFF) vehicles and personnel are proceeding to an aircraft, runway, taxiway, or stand in response to an emergency or precautionary event.

This is high-priority ground language. When firefighters are rolling, other traffic holds, runways may close, and the affected crew must communicate clearly: nature of the problem, souls on board, fuel on board, and hazardous materials if any.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, firefighters on the way may follow a precautionary landing, smoke smell, hard landing, fuel leak, or declared emergency. Rotorcraft incidents on Brazilian aerodromes still bring the RFF category response. Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 speaking both test this scenario.

Understanding how to acknowledge, coordinate, and speak calmly while firefighters are on the way is essential for ICAO proficiency and real emergency discipline.

## 📖 Meaning

Firefighters on the way means airport rescue and firefighting services have been dispatched and are proceeding to a specified location to assist an aircraft or handle a fire, smoke, leak, crash, or other emergency on the aerodrome.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Bombeiros a caminho / firefighters on the way / RFF responding.

Significa que o serviço de salvamento e combate a incendio do aeródromo (SCI/ARFF) foi acionado e está se deslocando até um local especificado para assistir uma aeronave ou lidar com fogo, fumaça, vazamento, acidente ou outra emergência no aeródromo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When firefighters are on the way, the crew should:

- follow emergency memory items and secure the aircraft as required
- stay on the assigned frequency unless instructed to change
- provide souls on board, fuel on board, and problem description when asked
- evacuate only when and how the situation and SOP require
- hold other intentions — do not taxi away into the response path
- acknowledge RFF instructions once firefighters are on scene

Firefighters on the way commonly follows:

- declared MAYDAY or PAN PAN on the aerodrome
- smoke, fire, or fuel leak indications
- crash or hard landing alerts
- precautionary RFF standby for abnormal landings
- tower-observed smoke or sparks from an aircraft

**Important:** "Firefighters on the way" is not the end of your radio work. Continue concise updates. In the H130, if you have a hot engine compartment smell after landing, shut down per checklist, report status, and keep passengers clear of exits that firefighters will use. Do not hover-taxi away from the pad if that spreads fuel or blocks the response.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers announce firefighters on the way to:

- alert the affected crew that help is rolling
- warn other aircraft to hold clear of response routes
- coordinate runway and taxiway closures during the emergency
- prioritize RFF vehicles over normal ground traffic
- manage the aerodrome emergency plan with clear status calls

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You land the H130 on the academy pad after smelling smoke in the cabin during final. You declare intentions, shut down, and request assistance.

Tower advises that firefighters are on the way and asks for souls on board and fuel on board.

You answer clearly. You keep the crew and any passengers briefed. You do not reopen doors into smoke without checklist guidance.

Ground holds a departing trainer short because RFF vehicles are crossing Alpha to your pad.

Firefighters arrive, assess, and either clear the aircraft or manage evacuation. You stay on frequency until Tower or the fire chief releases you.

The phrase told everyone the priority had changed from traffic flow to rescue response.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, firefighters on the way, report souls on board and fuel on board.
2. Ground: ANAC123, hold position, RFF vehicles crossing Alpha, firefighters on the way to stand two.
3. Tower: ANAC123, firefighters on the way, evacuate if required, advise intentions.
4. Ground: All stations, taxiway Bravo closed, firefighters on the way to disabled aircraft.
5. Tower: ANAC123, firefighters on scene, contact them on the interphone if able, remain this frequency.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, firefighters on the way, souls on board three, fuel on board two hours.
2. ANAC123, holding position, RFF crossing Alpha.
3. ANAC123, firefighters on the way, evacuating, will advise when clear.
4. ANAC123, roger, Bravo closed, standing by.
5. ANAC123, firefighters on scene, remaining this frequency.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

When firefighters are rolling, your voice must get smaller and clearer — not louder and longer.

Say what is burning or smelling. Say how many people. Say how much fuel. Say if you are evacuating. Then work the checklist.

Part 3 questions love this scenario. Structure answers: fly or secure the aircraft, communicate, evacuate if needed, cooperate with RFF.

In the H130 academy, even a precautionary standby after a hard landing may bring firefighters on the way. Treat standby with respect. Do not wave them off casually if the checklist is incomplete.

Other traffic must give absolute priority to RFF. If you are not the emergency aircraft, hold clear and keep the frequency clean.

Never block the response road with a hover taxi "to get out of the way" without clearance. Ask Ground where to go.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

After landing you have smoke in the cabin. Tower says firefighters are on the way and asks for souls and fuel on board. Explain your actions and radio response.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would secure the aircraft according to the smoke or fire checklist, including engine shutdown if required, and keep the crew and passengers briefed. On the radio I would report the number of souls on board and the fuel on board in a clear, short transmission. I would state whether we are remaining on board or evacuating. I would stay on the assigned frequency, follow further Tower instructions, and cooperate with the firefighters when they arrive. I would not taxi away unless specifically instructed and safe to do so.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Giving a long story instead of souls on board and fuel on board.  
  ✔ Answer the items asked first: souls, fuel, then brief problem status.

- ❌ Leaving the frequency to call company while RFF is responding.  
  ✔ Stay on the ATC frequency until released.

- ❌ Taxiing or hover-taxiing across the path of approaching fire trucks.  
  ✔ Hold position; give RFF absolute priority.

- ❌ Saying "bombeiros" only and freezing in English.  
  ✔ Use: firefighters on the way / RFF on the way / souls on board / fuel on board.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Souls on Board Three, Fuel on Board Two Hours

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, SOHLZ on BOARD THREE, FYOOL on BOARD TOO OW-erz

**Word Stress**

- Souls → SOHLZ
- Board → BOARD
- Fuel → FYOOL
- Hours → OW-erz

Practice:

Souls on board three... fuel on board two hours.

Together:

ANAC123, firefighters on the way, souls on board three, fuel on board two hours, smoke in cabin, shutting down.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**FIRE** — **F**requency stay assigned, **I**tems: souls and fuel, **R**FF gets priority on the surface, **E**vacuate only per checklist and need.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You land the H130 with a fuel smell after shutdown on stand two. Tower rolls the fire service.

**Your first task**

Tower says: "ANAC123, firefighters on the way, report souls on board and fuel on board." What do you reply? (Assume two crew, one passenger, one hour thirty minutes fuel.)

**Your second task**

In Part 3 style, explain what you do while waiting for the firefighters to arrive.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, souls on board three, fuel on board one hour three zero minutes, fuel smell after shutdown, standing by. Second task: I would complete the appropriate checklist, keep everyone briefed, not restart or move the aircraft, stay on frequency, and prepare to evacuate if the smell increases or firefighters instruct evacuation. I would keep exits clear and wait for RFF assessment.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Firefighters on the way means the aerodrome has switched to rescue mode.

Secure the aircraft. Answer souls and fuel. Keep transmissions short. Give RFF the road.

In H130 operations, smoke, fuel leaks, and hard landings can all trigger this call. Train the English before you need it. Part 2 and Part 3 both expect calm structure under pressure.

You do not manage the fire trucks. You manage your aircraft and your communication until they arrive.

That is professional emergency English for Brazilian aerodromes — and it saves time when time is life.
