# Backtrack

**Catalog ID:** 0027  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers a surface maneuver that surprises many pilots the first time they hear it.

Backtrack means taxiing on the runway itself — opposite to the landing direction — to reach the departure end for takeoff.

This is common at smaller airports and heliports where there is no parallel taxiway to the runway threshold.

You must understand when backtracking is authorized, how to report it, and why controllers need to protect the runway while you are on it.

## 📖 Meaning

Backtrack instructs or authorizes the pilot to taxi along the runway surface, typically toward the departure end opposite to the landing direction.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Retornar pela pista.

Táxi ao longo da pista em uso, geralmente em sentido contrário ao pouso, para posicionar-se para decolagem.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC authorizes Backtrack, the pilot should:

- confirm which runway to backtrack
- report when backtracking begins
- remain alert for other traffic on or approaching the runway
- complete the backtrack expeditiously unless otherwise instructed
- report vacating the runway or reaching the departure end as required

Backtrack is commonly used when:

- no taxiway connects to the departure threshold
- the aircraft landed and must depart from the same runway
- helicopter operations require positioning at the runway end

**Important:** Backtrack is not a takeoff clearance. You need separate authorization to line up and take off.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Tower uses Backtrack to:

- position aircraft at the departure end when no parallel taxiway exists
- coordinate runway use at airports with limited infrastructure
- maintain awareness of aircraft on the runway surface
- sequence departures after arrivals on the same runway
- protect landing traffic while aircraft backtrack

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You have just landed at Balneário Camboriú in your H130 on runway two seven.

There is no taxiway to the departure end.

Tower instructs you to backtrack for departure.

Tower: ANAC123, backtrack runway two seven for departure.

You report:

Tower, ANAC123 backtracking runway two seven for departure.

You taxi carefully along the runway centerline, scanning for traffic, until you reach the departure end and await further clearance.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, backtrack runway two seven for departure.
2. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to backtrack runway one eight.
3. Tower: ANAC123, backtrack runway three five, report reaching the threshold.
4. Tower: ANAC123, expedite backtrack, traffic on a three-mile final.
5. Tower: ANAC123, cancel backtrack, taxi via Alpha to holding point Bravo.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Backtracking runway two seven for departure, ANAC123.
2. Cleared to backtrack runway one eight, ANAC123.
3. Backtracking runway three five, will report reaching the threshold, ANAC123.
4. Expediting backtrack, ANAC123.
5. Cancel backtrack, taxi via Alpha to holding point Bravo, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine you landed on a one-way street and need to drive to the other end to depart.

You cannot use the sidewalk — you must use the street itself.

That is backtrack.

The runway is active airspace even when you are taxiing on it.

Tower must know you are there.

Other aircraft may be on final approach.

At Brazilian coastal heliports like Balneário Camboriú, backtrack is routine.

Treat it with the same alertness as a takeoff or landing roll.

Never backtrack without explicit tower authorization.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How would you report that you are backtracking the runway for departure?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report to Tower: ANAC123 backtracking runway two seven for departure. I include my callsign, the word backtracking, the runway designator, and the purpose.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Backtracking without tower clearance.  
  ✔ Wait for explicit authorization before entering the runway to backtrack.

- ❌ Saying "returning on the runway" instead of standard phraseology.  
  ✔ Use backtracking runway XX for departure.

- ❌ Assuming backtrack includes takeoff clearance.  
  ✔ Backtrack only positions you; you still need line up and takeoff clearance.

- ❌ Moving slowly without reporting when traffic is on final.  
  ✔ Expedite when instructed and keep tower informed of your position.

Speak runway numbers digit by digit: two seven, not twenty-seven.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**BACK** on the runway — **B**efore takeoff, **A**uthorized by tower, **C**enterline, **K**eep scanning for traffic.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

Tower: ANAC123, backtrack runway two seven for departure, report reaching the threshold.

**Your first task**

Read back the instruction and report when you begin backtracking.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

You have completed the backtrack and are at the departure threshold. May you take off?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

No. Backtrack only authorized movement on the runway to reach the departure end. I must receive line up and wait or cleared for takeoff from Tower before beginning the takeoff roll.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Backtrack puts you on the most critical surface at the airport — the runway itself.

Get authorization first.

Report your movement clearly.

Scan for traffic continuously.

Expedite when traffic is on final.

The runway is not a taxiway you can use casually.

Respect it, and Tower will keep everyone safe.
