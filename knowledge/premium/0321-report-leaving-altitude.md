# Report Leaving Altitude

**Catalog ID:** 0321  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Report Leaving Altitude — the standard pilot report that advises ATC when the aircraft has begun leaving a previously assigned altitude or flight level during climb or descent.

Report Leaving Altitude is not optional chatter. It closes the loop between clearance and execution. Controllers issue climb or descent clearances based on traffic and airspace structure; the leaving report confirms the aircraft has vacated the previous level and frees that altitude for other traffic.

For H130 helicopter operations in Brazilian IFR and controlled VFR environments, this report is especially useful when radar coverage is intermittent, when ATC has asked you to advise leaving a level, or when you are climbing or descending through busy terminal layers around Congonhas, Guarulhos, or coastal approach sectors.

Understanding how to report leaving altitude in ICAO-standard English — including when ATC requests the report and when professional practice requires it — is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Report Leaving Altitude instructs the pilot to advise ATC when the aircraft has commenced leaving a previously assigned altitude or flight level, confirming that the previous level is being vacated for traffic and airspace management.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Reporte deixando a altitude / Report Leaving Altitude.

Instrução para o piloto informar ao controle quando a aeronave iniciou a saída de uma altitude ou nível de voo previamente atribuído, confirmando que o nível anterior está sendo liberado para gerenciamento de tráfego e espaço aéreo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Report Leaving Altitude, or when good practice requires the report, the crew should:

- acknowledge the climb or descent clearance first
- begin the climb or descent only when cleared
- report promptly when leaving the previous altitude or flight level
- include the altitude being left and the cleared altitude when useful
- continue monitoring the assigned level and any intermediate restrictions

Report Leaving Altitude commonly includes:

- leaving flight level nine zero, climbing flight level one one zero
- leaving three thousand, descending two thousand
- advise leaving cruise when cleared for descent
- report leaving altitude when requested by ATC for traffic below or above

**Important:** Do not report leaving an altitude before you have actually started the climb or descent. A false leaving report can cause ATC to clear another aircraft into a level you still occupy. In the H130, vertical rates can change quickly — report when the altimeter shows you are leaving, not when you merely intend to leave.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers work with Report Leaving Altitude to:

- confirm the previous level is being vacated for crossing or opposite traffic
- sequence climb and descent clearances safely
- free altitudes for other aircraft without waiting for a level-off report
- maintain an accurate altitude picture when radar altitude readout is delayed or unavailable
- coordinate between sectors during level changes

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at flight level nine zero toward Florianópolis in the H130.

Radar clears you:

Radar: ANAC123, descend and maintain flight level six zero, report leaving altitude.

You set the descent and begin leaving nine zero.

You report:

ANAC123, leaving flight level nine zero, descending flight level six zero.

Radar now knows flight level nine zero is free and can clear traffic above or through that level. Your descent continues on profile toward the coast.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Radar: ANAC123, climb and maintain flight level one one zero, report leaving altitude.
2. Control: ANAC123, descend and maintain four thousand, advise leaving cruise.
3. Approach: ANAC123, descend two thousand, report leaving three thousand.
4. Radar: ANAC123, climb flight level nine zero, report leaving five thousand.
5. Control: ANAC123, descend pilot's discretion flight level six zero, report leaving altitude.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, climbing flight level one one zero, will report leaving altitude.
2. ANAC123, leaving flight level nine zero, descending flight level six zero.
3. ANAC123, leaving three thousand, descending two thousand.
4. ANAC123, leaving five thousand, climbing flight level nine zero.
5. ANAC123, descending flight level six zero at pilot's discretion, will report leaving altitude.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Leaving altitude is the moment the previous level becomes available again.

ATC does not guess that you have started down. They wait for confirmation — especially when traffic is stacked above or below you.

In Brazilian terminal airspace, that confirmation can be the difference between a smooth sequence and a delayed clearance for the next aircraft.

Keep the report short. State what you are leaving and what you are going to. Do not narrate the whole descent profile.

In the H130, you can leave a level quickly. That is an advantage only if your radio matches your altimeter. If you say you are leaving and you are still level, you have created a false traffic picture.

Clearance first. Leave second. Report third. That order never changes.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC clears you to descend and asks you to report leaving altitude. What do you say when you begin the descent, and why is the report important?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

When I begin the descent I report that I am leaving the previous altitude for the cleared altitude. The report is important because it confirms to ATC that the previous level is being vacated, which allows the controller to manage traffic through or into that altitude safely. I would not report leaving until the aircraft has actually started the descent.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting leaving altitude before starting the climb or descent.  
  ✔ Report only when the altimeter shows you are leaving the previous level.

- ❌ Saying only "leaving" without altitude information.  
  ✔ Say: leaving flight level nine zero, descending flight level six zero.

- ❌ Confusing leaving altitude with reaching altitude.  
  ✔ Leaving = vacating the old level. Reaching = arriving at the new level.

- ❌ Beginning descent without clearance because you planned to leave soon.  
  ✔ Never leave an assigned altitude without ATC clearance.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Leaving Flight Level Nine Zero, Descending Flight Level Six Zero

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, LEE-ving FLITE LEV-ul NINE ZEER-oh, dih-SEND-ing FLITE LEV-ul SIX ZEER-oh

**Word Stress**

- Leaving → LEE-ving (stress on first)
- Flight → FLITE
- Level → LEV-ul
- Descending → dih-SEND-ing (stress on second)

Practice:

Leaving flight level nine zero... descending flight level six zero.

Together:

ANAC123, leaving flight level nine zero, descending flight level six zero.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**LEAVE** — **L**evel vacated only after climb or descent starts, **E**nunciate old and new altitudes, **A**dvise ATC promptly, **V**erify clearance before leaving, **E**xpect ATC to free the old level for traffic.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Report Leaving Altitude is a traffic-management tool, not a courtesy call.

It tells ATC the old level is opening. It protects the aircraft still at that level — including you, until you actually leave.

In Brazilian IFR work with the H130, make the report crisp and timely.

Clearance. Leave. Report.

That sequence keeps the altitude structure honest for everyone on the frequency.
