# Fuel Starvation

**Catalog ID:** 0044  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers a technical fuel emergency that many pilots confuse with low fuel.

Fuel Starvation means the engines are starved of fuel despite fuel possibly remaining in the tanks.

The fuel is on board — but it is not reaching the engine due to selector error, crossfeed issue, or blockage.

This is a delivery failure, not a quantity problem. Mayday may be required if engines are affected.

In your H130, a fuel selector misconfiguration can cause starvation with full tanks showing on the gauge.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Fuel Starvation is a condition where engines are starved of fuel despite fuel possibly remaining in the tanks — a fuel delivery failure.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Fuel Starvation occurs, the pilot should:

- attempt fuel system checklist actions immediately
- check fuel selector, crossfeed, and pump status
- declare Mayday if engines are affected and safe flight is in doubt
- inform ATC of fuel starvation, not low fuel
- state which engines are affected and restart status

Fuel Starvation reporting commonly includes:

- Mayday, fuel starvation imminent
- we suspect fuel starvation on engine two
- fuel starvation, both engines fluctuating
- engine relit after fuel selector change

**Important:** Fuel starvation is not low fuel. Gauges may show fuel on board while engines starve. Use the correct technical term.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Fuel Starvation declarations to:

- provide immediate vectors to the nearest suitable airport
- clear all traffic from the approach path
- assign any runway for landing
- request souls on board and fuel status
- alert fire services for possible forced landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at three thousand five hundred feet in your H130 near Porto Alegre.

Both engine parameters fluctuate. Fuel gauges show adequate quantity — but power is dropping.

You run the fuel system checklist and find the selector in the wrong position.

You correct it, but engine one does not recover immediately.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Porto Alegre Approach, ANAC123, fuel starvation, both engines fluctuating, request immediate vectors to the field.

Approach clears traffic and vectors you toward the airport.

After selector correction, engine one relights. You report:

ANAC123, engine one relit after fuel selector change, single engine, continuing to Porto Alegre.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Fuel starvation versus low fuel — know the difference cold.

Low fuel: not enough quantity, fuel is reaching the engine. Pan Pan.

Fuel starvation: fuel on board, not reaching the engine. Mayday if engines affected.

Brazilian pilots say no fuel when they mean starvation — that confuses ATC.

Say fuel starvation — it tells ATC the gauges may show fuel but engines are dying.

Attempt the fuel system checklist before or during your first transmission if possible.

Report selector changes and engine restart status.

In the H130, fuel selector misconfiguration after maintenance or tank switching is a documented risk.

Technical precision on the radio helps ATC understand why your gauges do not match your emergency.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How is fuel starvation different from low fuel?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Low fuel means fuel quantity is below reserves but fuel reaches the engine. Fuel starvation means fuel is in the tanks but not reaching the engine due to a delivery problem such as selector error or blockage. I use Pan Pan for low fuel and Mayday for fuel starvation when engines are affected.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Confusing fuel starvation with low fuel.  
  ✔ Use fuel starvation when delivery fails despite fuel on board.

- ❌ Saying no fuel when starvation is the technical issue.  
  ✔ Report fuel starvation — gauges may still show quantity.

- ❌ Not attempting fuel system checklist before declaring.  
  ✔ Run checklist and report actions taken to ATC.

- ❌ Not reporting engine restart status after correction.  
  ✔ Report engine relit or negative restart after selector change.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Your H130 engine fluctuates at three thousand feet near Porto Alegre. Fuel gauges show one hour remaining, but power is dropping.

**Your first task**

After identifying a possible fuel selector issue, transmit your emergency declaration to Approach.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

You correct the selector and engine relights. What do you report?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report engine one relit after fuel selector change, state that we had fuel starvation, confirm current engine status, and continue to Porto Alegre with caution. I would update souls on board and fuel remaining when requested, ANAC123.
