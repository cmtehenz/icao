# Runway Closure

**Catalog ID:** 0227  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Runway Closure — the temporary or planned unavailability of a runway for takeoff and landing, communicated by ATC, ATIS, NOTAM, or airport authority, requiring crews to use an alternate runway or divert.

A closed runway is not available. Crossing it may still be authorized under specific clearance, but landing and takeoff on it are prohibited until it is reopened. Closures happen for maintenance, inspection after an incident, snow or debris removal, lighting failure, or VIP and security operations.

For H130 helicopter training academy flights at Congonhas, Guarulhos, Santos Dumont, Curitiba, and Florianópolis, runway closure often means a change of active runway, a longer taxi, or use of a helipad or alternate landing area. Crews must hear the closure, read it back when instructed, and replan approach and departure without assuming the familiar runway is open.

Understanding runway closure phraseology and speaking about alternate plans in ICAO English supports Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

A Runway Closure is the formal status in which a runway is withdrawn from use for aircraft landing and takeoff operations, temporarily or for a published period, due to maintenance, inspection, contamination, equipment failure, incident, or other aerodrome restriction, and is notified through NOTAM, ATIS, and ATC.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Status formal em que uma pista é retirada de uso para operações de pouso e decolagem, temporariamente ou por período publicado, devido a manutenção, inspeção, contaminação, falha de equipamento, incidente ou outra restrição do aeródromo, e é notificado por NOTAM, ATIS e ATC.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a runway is closed, the crew should:

- check NOTAMs and ATIS before departure and before arrival for runway status
- listen for ATC advisories of unexpected closures after an incident or inspection
- accept the assigned alternate runway or request an alternative if performance or weather requires it
- never land or take off on a closed runway
- request clearance before crossing a closed runway if the taxi route requires it
- update fuel and alternate planning if the closure forces a longer approach or diversion

Runway closure commonly involves:

- scheduled maintenance and rubber removal
- inspection after bird strike, FOD, or overrun
- lighting or ILS unserviceability that effectively closes the runway for certain operations
- temporary closure for VIP or security movements
- weather-related surface recovery

**Important:** A closed runway may still appear visually usable. Paint and pavement do not equal availability. ATC and NOTAM status govern. In the H130, if both main runways are closed, confirm whether a helipad, taxiway landing, or diversion is authorized — do not invent a landing surface without clearance.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers announce runway closure to:

- keep traffic off a surface that is unsafe or under work
- reassign arrivals and departures to the active runway
- protect workers, vehicles, and inspection teams on the closed runway
- coordinate crossings of closed runways with ground control
- update ATIS so inbound crews plan the correct approach

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Curitiba in the H130. ATIS states runway one five is closed due to maintenance. Expect runway three three.

Approach clears you for the approach to runway three three. You brief the new runway, winds, and missed approach. You do not continue planning runway one five because "it looked open on the last flight."

On the ground after landing, Ground says taxi via Alpha, hold short of runway one five — runway closed, works in progress. You hold short until cleared to cross. Workers and vehicles are on the closed runway. Your hold-short protects them.

That is runway closure in Brazilian ops: NOTAM and ATIS first, ATC assignment second, never assume availability from appearance alone.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, runway one seven Left closed due to maintenance, expect runway one seven Right.
2. Tower: ANAC123, runway two eight closed, inspection in progress, enter left downwind runway one zero.
3. ATIS / Tower: ANAC123, information Charlie, runway one five closed, works in progress.
4. Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Bravo, hold short of runway one seven Left, runway closed.
5. Tower: ANAC123, runway one seven Left reopened, cleared to land runway one seven Left.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, runway one seven Left closed, expect runway one seven Right.
2. ANAC123, runway two eight closed, left downwind runway one zero.
3. ANAC123, information Charlie, runway one five closed.
4. ANAC123, taxi via Bravo, holding short runway one seven Left, closed.
5. ANAC123, cleared to land runway one seven Left.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Closed means closed. The runway is not a backup option because you prefer the wind or the shorter taxi.

Build the habit: ATIS and NOTAM before you brief the approach. If the familiar runway is closed, rebuild the brief — minima, missed approach, taxi plan, performance.

Crossing a closed runway still needs a clearance. Workers may be on the pavement. Hold short until Ground or Tower explicitly clears the crossing.

In the H130 academy environment, closures often appear with little notice after FOD or bird inspection. Stay flexible. Ask for the alternate runway early if fuel or weather makes the long way around a problem.

Never line up or land on a runway ATC has closed. Visual emptiness is not authorization.

When a closure forces a longer approach or a different missed approach, update fuel and alternate thinking before you accept the clearance. Flexibility without fuel awareness is incomplete professionalism.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATIS reports your preferred runway is closed for maintenance. Describe how you prepare for arrival and what you confirm with ATC.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would note the closed runway from the ATIS and NOTAM, identify the expected active runway, and rebrief the approach, minima, missed approach, and landing performance for that runway. On first contact I would confirm the active runway with ATC and request any preferred alternative if wind or performance makes the assigned runway unsuitable. I would not plan to use the closed runway under any circumstance until ATC advises it is reopened.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Briefing the usual runway without checking ATIS or NOTAM for closure.  
  ✔ Check runway status every flight before the approach brief.

- ❌ Crossing a closed runway without a specific clearance because "it is closed anyway."  
  ✔ Closed runways often have people and vehicles. Hold short until cleared.

- ❌ Asking to land on a closed runway because wind favors it.  
  ✔ Request another open runway or divert. Closed is not negotiable for landing.

- ❌ Missing the ATIS update when a runway reopens or closes mid-flight.  
  ✔ Monitor ATIS changes and ATC advisories on arrival.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are ten miles from Congonhas. ATIS said runway one seven Left was active. Tower now advises runway one seven Left is closed for inspection.

**Your first task**

What do you say when tower assigns runway one seven Right instead?

**Your second task**

After landing on one seven Right, Ground clears you to taxi via Alpha and cross runway one seven Left. What do you say, and what do you verify visually?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, expect runway one seven Right, runway one seven Left closed — or the full clearance readback if an approach clearance is issued. Second task: ANAC123, taxi via Alpha, crossing runway one seven Left — and I would verify the runway is clear of conflicting traffic as I cross, knowing workers or vehicles may still be associated with the closure. I would not cross until the clearance is explicit.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

A closed runway is unavailable for takeoff and landing. Appearance does not override status.

Check ATIS. Check NOTAM. Brief the open runway. Hold short of closed pavement until cleared to cross.

In academy ops, flexibility is professional. The familiar runway will close. Your plan must move with it.

Closed means closed. Replan early. Cross only when cleared.
