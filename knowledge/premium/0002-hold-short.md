# Hold Short

**Catalog ID:** 0002  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## Mission Brief

Today you'll learn one of the most important safety instructions in aviation.

Every year, runway incursions occur because a pilot misunderstood—or ignored—a simple instruction:

Hold Short.

Professional pilots treat these two words with absolute respect because they protect aircraft during takeoff and landing.

## Meaning

Stop before a runway, taxiway, or designated holding position.

Do not proceed until ATC explicitly authorizes you.

## Portuguese Meaning

Pare antes da pista ou do ponto de espera indicado.

Não avance sem autorização.

## Operational Meaning

A Hold Short instruction requires the aircraft to stop before reaching a runway, taxiway, or any specified holding point.

Even if the runway appears empty, the pilot must not cross until receiving an explicit crossing or takeoff clearance.

This instruction is one of the most critical barriers against runway incursions.

## Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Hold Short to:

- protect landing traffic
- protect departing traffic
- prevent runway incursions
- separate aircraft safely
- coordinate ground movement
- maintain airport safety
- sequence arrivals and departures

## Real Operational Context

You're taxiing your H130 for departure.

Ground instructs:

"ANAC123, taxi via Alpha, hold short runway one eight."

As you approach the holding position, you notice the runway looks completely empty.

You do not cross.

A few seconds later, an Embraer 195 lands at high speed.

If you had crossed without clearance, the consequences could have been catastrophic.

Professional pilots never assume.

They wait for the clearance.

## Real ATC Phraseology

1. ANAC123, hold short runway one eight.
2. ANAC123, taxi via Alpha, hold short runway two seven.
3. ANAC123, hold short the active runway.
4. ANAC123, cross runway zero niner, hold short runway one eight.
5. ANAC123, hold short taxiway Bravo.

## Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Holding short runway one eight, ANAC123.
2. Taxi via Alpha, holding short runway two seven, ANAC123.
3. Holding short the active runway, ANAC123.
4. Crossing runway zero niner, holding short runway one eight, ANAC123.
5. Holding short taxiway Bravo, ANAC123.

## Captain Delta Teaching

Gustavo, imagine you're approaching a railroad crossing. The gates are down, but you can't see the train yet. You don't cross just because it looks safe—you wait until it's truly safe.

A Hold Short instruction works the same way. ATC has a complete picture of the airport that you may not have from the cockpit.

Never guess.

Never assume.

Never cross until the controller clearly authorizes you.

Remember: A taxi clearance is not a runway crossing clearance.

## Common ICAO Speaking Question

Why is it important to read back Hold Short instructions?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Hold Short instructions protect active runways. Reading them back correctly confirms that the pilot understood the restriction and helps prevent runway incursions.

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Holding short runway one eight, ANAC123.

- ❌ Saying: Holding position.  
  ✔ Better: Holding short runway one eight.

- ❌ Assuming a taxi clearance allows runway crossing.  
  It doesn't. Runway crossings require an explicit clearance.

- ❌ Forgetting the runway number in the readback.  
  Always include the runway or holding point whenever possible.

## Memory Trick

Imagine a red stop sign painted across the taxiway.

You stop.

You wait.

You do not cross until someone removes the stop sign.

That's exactly what Hold Short means.
