# Cyclic Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0416  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Cyclic Failure — loss of normal cyclic control, compromising the pilot's ability to tilt the main rotor disc for directional and attitude control and requiring immediate emergency handling and distress communication.

Cyclic failure is among the most critical helicopter control emergencies. Attitude and flight-path control are threatened. Phraseology must be standard and brief.

For H130 operations in Brazil — dense urban routes, coastal legs, and mountain valleys — you must declare mayday, state cyclic failure, and communicate landing intentions while using remaining control authority.

Understanding how to report cyclic failure in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Cyclic Failure is a malfunction of the cyclic control system that prevents normal tilting of the main rotor disc, severely degrading attitude and directional flight-path control and requiring immediate emergency procedures and landing.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha do cíclico.

Malfuncionamento do sistema de comando cíclico que impede a inclinação normal do disco do rotor principal, degradando severamente o controle de atitude e da trajetória e exigindo procedimentos de emergência imediatos e pouso.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When cyclic failure occurs, the crew should:

- use remaining control authority and AFM emergency procedures immediately
- establish the most controllable flight path toward a suitable landing area
- declare mayday with cyclic failure and intentions
- request clear area / emergency services as needed
- minimize radio talk until the aircraft is under the best achievable control

Cyclic failure reporting commonly includes:

- mayday, cyclic failure
- limited control, emergency landing
- position and altitude
- souls and fuel when able

**Important:** Partial cyclic authority is still an emergency. Do not delay the mayday because "some control remains."

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to cyclic failure maydays to:

- clear airspace and surface movement near the intended landing
- provide nearest suitable landing options
- alert fire and rescue without delay
- avoid frequency congestion during the critical phase
- track the aircraft to the landing site for rescue coordination

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

East of Rio, outbound from Santos Dumont in your H130, cyclic response becomes severely restricted. You fight for a controllable attitude and transmit:

Mayday mayday mayday, Rio Approach, cyclic failure, limited control, making emergency landing, east of Santos Dumont, one thousand five hundred feet.

Approach clears traffic, alerts emergency services, and stands by for souls and fuel. You put the aircraft down on the most suitable open area you can reach.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, mayday acknowledged, traffic cleared, say intentions and position.
2. Approach: ANAC123, open area your two o'clock one mile, emergency services rolling.
3. Approach: ANAC123, souls on board and fuel remaining when able.
4. Approach: ANAC123, squawk seven seven zero zero if able.
5. Approach: ANAC123, roger on ground, assistance inbound.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, mayday, cyclic failure, emergency landing east of Santos Dumont.
2. ANAC123, proceeding open area two o'clock one mile.
3. ANAC123, souls three, fuel four five minutes.
4. ANAC123, squawking seven seven zero zero.
5. ANAC123, on ground, request immediate assistance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Cyclic failure English is survival English.

Attitude control is the helicopter. When cyclic is compromised, every transmission must earn its airtime.

In the H130, follow the emergency memory items. Then one mayday with nature and place. ATC will clear the sky if you give them something to work with.

Brazilian coastal and city environments offer few long runways for helicopters in trouble — open areas, beaches, and pads matter. Ask for the nearest suitable site if you cannot see one.

Do not debate controllability on frequency. "Limited control, emergency landing" is enough. Save the technical debrief for the ground.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Cyclic control becomes severely limited in flight. What do you say to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare: Mayday mayday mayday, cyclic failure, limited control, making emergency landing, with position and altitude. I would request the nearest suitable landing area if needed, then give souls and fuel when workload allows.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Using pan-pan for a cyclic control emergency.  
  ✔ Cyclic failure with limited control is mayday.

- ❌ Long technical description of what the cyclic is doing.  
  ✔ Cyclic failure, limited control, intentions — then fly.

- ❌ Not giving position because you are "too busy."  
  ✔ One short position phrase enables rescue.

- ❌ Refusing to squawk 7700 when able.  
  ✔ Squawk distress if capacity remains; it helps radar identify you.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Mayday, Cyclic Failure, Limited Control

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, MAY-day, SY-klik FAYL-yer, LIM-i-tid kun-TROHL

**Word Stress**

- Cyclic → SY-klik
- Failure → FAYL-yer
- Limited → LIM-i-tid
- Control → kun-TROHL (stress on second)

Practice:

Cyclic failure... limited control.

Together:

Mayday mayday mayday, cyclic failure, limited control, making emergency landing.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**DISC** — **D**eclare mayday, **I**dentify cyclic failure, **S**tate limited control / landing, **C**all position then souls/fuel.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Cyclic failure east of Santos Dumont. Limited lateral control. One thousand five hundred feet.

**Your first task**

Transmit the mayday.

**Your second task**

Approach offers a vector away from your chosen open area. Workload is extreme. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday mayday mayday, Rio Approach, ANAC123, cyclic failure, limited control, making emergency landing east of Santos Dumont, one thousand five hundred. Second task: ANAC123, unable vector, proceeding to open area in sight, emergency landing — do not accept a vector that increases workload when you already have a landing site.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Cyclic failure attacks the disc. Your English must not attack your attention.

Mayday. Cyclic failure. Limited control. Landing. Position.

In the H130 over Brazil, choose the open area you can still reach. Tell ATC once. Fly the landing.

Control what remains. Speak what matters. Get on the ground.
