# First Officer

**Catalog ID:** 0345  
**Category:** Crew  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers First Officer — the co-pilot on a multi-crew flight deck, second-in-command to the captain, sharing handling, monitoring, and radio duties under the operator's SOP and CRM principles.

ICAO Part 1 and Part 2 expect accurate crew titles: captain, first officer, pilot flying, pilot monitoring. Controllers may hear either pilot on frequency; the callsign remains the aircraft callsign.

For H130 ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, many operations are single-pilot. Two-crew training, check flights, and exam scenarios still require correct first officer language — including assuming control after captain incapacitation.

Master: first officer, co-pilot, assuming control, pilot monitoring.

## 📖 Meaning

First Officer is the licensed co-pilot who serves as second-in-command on a multi-crew aircraft, performing pilot flying or pilot monitoring duties as assigned and assuming command if the captain becomes incapacitated.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Primeiro oficial / First Officer / Copiloto.

Copiloto licenciado que atua como segundo em comando em aeronave multi-crew, desempenhando funções de piloto voando ou piloto monitorando conforme designado e assumindo o comando se o capitão ficar incapacitado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When operating as or referring to a first officer, the crew should:

- use first officer (not only "co-pilot") in formal ICAO English answers
- identify pilot flying / pilot monitoring roles clearly in briefings
- follow sterile cockpit and standard callouts
- assume control immediately if the captain is incapacitated
- make radio calls using the aircraft callsign, not a personal name

First officer communications commonly include:

- first officer assuming control
- request vectors / priority as directed by SOP after takeover
- standard readbacks while pilot monitoring
- incapacitation Mayday phraseology when required

**Important:** On frequency, you are the aircraft callsign. Role titles matter in crew coordination and exam answers more than in every ATC transmission.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers care about who is flying when:

- pilot incapacitation is declared and control is transferred
- a single pilot reports assuming control
- examiners evaluate correct role vocabulary in English
- crew reports clarify who can continue the flight
- priority handling depends on remaining crew capability

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Two-crew H130 training flight toward Navegantes. The first officer is pilot monitoring, handling radios.

The captain briefs the approach. The first officer reads back clearances and monitors altitudes.

If the captain becomes unresponsive, the first officer takes control and declares Mayday: captain incapacitated, first officer assuming control.

Daily Brazilian single-pilot H130 flights may have no first officer — the exam still tests the role.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Mayday, confirm first officer assuming control.
2. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and assistance required.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway cleared to land, medical services standing by.
4. Control: ANAC123, report if able to continue approach single pilot.
5. Tower: ANAC123, confirm pilot flying identity if requested by emergency services.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, ANAC123, captain incapacitated, first officer assuming control, request vectors.
2. ANAC123, first officer flying, request priority landing and medical assistance.
3. ANAC123, unable to continue with captain incapacitated, diverting nearest suitable airport.
4. ANAC123, readback confirmed, descending three thousand.
5. ANAC123, first officer assuming control, souls on board four, request medical assistance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

First officer means second-in-command — not "assistant who only talks on the radio."

In modern CRM, the first officer challenges, monitors, and flies when assigned. Authority gradient must not silence a safety call.

Brazilian Portuguese: primeiro oficial or copiloto. Prefer first officer in ICAO English.

If you take over after incapacitation, say first officer assuming control. Controllers and examiners need that clarity.

In single-pilot H130 ops, you are not a first officer — do not invent multi-crew structure. When the scenario is two-crew, use the titles correctly.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is a first officer, and what should you say if the captain becomes incapacitated?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

The first officer is the co-pilot and second-in-command. If the captain becomes incapacitated, I would take control of the aircraft and declare Mayday, reporting captain incapacitated, first officer assuming control, and request vectors to the nearest suitable airport and medical assistance.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "copiloto" in a formal English answer.  
  ✔ Prefer first officer.

- ❌ Taking control after incapacitation without stating who is flying.  
  ✔ Report first officer assuming control.

- ❌ Using a personal name instead of the aircraft callsign on frequency.  
  ✔ Keep ANAC-style callsign discipline on the radio.

- ❌ Treating the first officer as unable to challenge the captain.  
  ✔ CRM requires assertive monitoring from the first officer.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are first officer on approach when the captain becomes unconscious.

**Your first task**

Transmit the distress call after taking control.

**Your second task**

Approach asks who is flying. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, ANAC123, captain incapacitated, first officer assuming control, request vectors and medical assistance. Second task: ANAC123, first officer flying, captain incapacitated. I would keep flying the approach or divert as required and answer souls on board when asked.

First officer is a command-capable co-pilot, not a radio clerk.
