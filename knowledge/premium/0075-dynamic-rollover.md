# Dynamic Rollover

**Catalog ID:** 0075  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers dynamic rollover — one of the most insidious helicopter accidents.

Dynamic Rollover occurs when the helicopter pivots about one skid or wheel while the rotor still produces thrust, rolling the aircraft onto its side.

In the H130, dynamic rollover risk exists on slopes, uneven ground, and during hover with one skid on a fixed object.

You must recognize the conditions and inform ATC immediately if a rollover occurs or is imminent.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Dynamic Rollover is an accident sequence where the helicopter rolls about a fixed point on the landing gear while the rotor produces thrust, leading to catastrophic rollover.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Dynamic Rollover risk exists, the pilot should:

- avoid slopes beyond flight manual limits
- avoid hover with one skid on a fixed object or soft surface
- lower collective immediately if rollover tendency is felt
- avoid sideward flight low to the ground on uneven terrain
- declare Mayday immediately if dynamic rollover occurs or aircraft is on its side

Dynamic Rollover reporting commonly includes:

- dynamic rollover or aircraft on its side
- position and souls on board
- request emergency services
- report injuries and fuel leak if applicable

**Important:** Prevention is the only cure — once dynamic rollover begins, recovery is often impossible. Lower collective immediately if rollover tendency is detected.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Dynamic Rollover reports to:

- dispatch emergency services immediately
- clear the area of other traffic
- request souls on board and injuries
- coordinate with airport fire and rescue
- notify authorities of accident location

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are hover taxiing to a helipad at a construction site near São Paulo in your H130.

One skid contacts a wooden plank that shifts under weight.

You feel the aircraft begin to roll right and lower collective immediately.

The roll stops but the aircraft is unstable.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, São Paulo Tower, ANAC123, dynamic rollover tendency on helipad, aircraft stable, request emergency services standby.

Tower dispatches fire and rescue.

You shut down safely and report no injuries.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What causes dynamic rollover and how do you prevent it?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Dynamic rollover occurs when the helicopter pivots about a fixed point on the landing gear while the rotor produces thrust. Causes include excessive slope angle, one skid on a fixed object, or sideward drift during hover. Prevention includes staying within slope limits, avoiding hover on unsecured surfaces, and lowering collective immediately if roll tendency is felt. If rollover occurs, declare Mayday and request emergency services.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Hovering on unsecured or sloped surfaces without assessment.  
  ✔ Assess surface and slope before touchdown — stay within limits.

- ❌ Raising collective when roll tendency is felt.  
  ✔ Lower collective immediately — raising collective worsens rollover.

- ❌ Saying "quase capotou" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use dynamic rollover tendency or aircraft unstable.

- ❌ Not declaring Mayday after a rollover event.  
  ✔ Mayday immediately — request emergency services and report injuries.
