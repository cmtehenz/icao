# Say Altitude

**Catalog ID:** 0319  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Say Altitude — the ATC request for the crew to report current altitude or flight level. Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: read the altimeter correctly and report altitude in standard phraseology.

Say altitude is Medium priority because altitude reports confirm level-off, climb/descent progress, and separation assumptions.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for altitude and flight level reports under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including transition altitude discipline.

Understanding how to say altitude in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Say Altitude is an ATC request for the pilot to report the aircraft's current altitude or flight level, using feet below transition or flight levels above, so that ATC can verify vertical position, monitor climb or descent, and maintain separation.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Informe a altitude / Say Altitude.

Solicitação do controle para que o piloto informe a altitude ou o nível de voo atual da aeronave, usando pés abaixo da transição ou níveis de voo acima, para que o ATC verifique a posição vertical, monitore subida ou descida e mantenha a separação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC says say altitude, the crew should:

- read the current altitude or flight level from the altimeter / PFD
- report altitude in feet or flight level as appropriate for the airspace
- include climbing or descending if still in vertical motion when useful
- for H130: expect frequent low-altitude reports in terminal and city operations
- cross-check altimeter setting when altitude reports seem inconsistent
- never report a cleared altitude as current if you have not reached it

Altitude exchanges commonly include:

- say altitude / confirm altitude
- altitude [number] feet / flight level [number]
- leaving / reaching / maintaining [altitude]
- climbing / descending to [altitude]
- level at [altitude]

**Important:** Report present altitude, not the cleared altitude, unless ATC asks you to confirm the assigned level.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers request altitude to:

- verify mode C / surveillance data against pilot reports
- confirm level-off after climb or descent clearances
- restore vertical awareness after radio congestion or blocked calls
- separate traffic when radar data is limited or conflicting
- check that helicopter low-level operations remain as cleared

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 descending into São Paulo terminal airspace.

Approach: ANAC123, say altitude.

You reply:

ANAC123, altitude three thousand five hundred, descending two thousand.

Or if level:

ANAC123, maintaining two thousand.

If Approach's radar label disagrees with your report, expect an altimeter check. Your spoken altitude is still a primary safety cross-check, especially for the H130 operating among taller traffic.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, say altitude.
2. Radar: ANAC123, confirm altitude.
3. Tower: ANAC123, say altitude on downwind.
4. Control: ANAC123, verify flight level nine zero.
5. Approach: ANAC123, say altitude leaving three thousand.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Altitude reports are vertical truth on demand.

Read the instrument. Say the number. Add climbing or descending when it helps the picture. Do not report the clearance you wish you had already reached.

In Brazilian terminal work with the H130, you change altitude often. Part 2 listens for clean level reports and correct flight-level versus altitude language around transition.

If the altimeter setting is wrong, every altitude call is wrong. Check the setting when something feels off.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC asks you to say altitude while you are descending to two thousand feet and currently passing three thousand five hundred. What do you report?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report the present altitude and the fact that I am descending, for example altitude three thousand five hundred, descending two thousand. I would not report two thousand until I am level there. If ATC asks for flight level, I would report the current flight level using standard flight-level phraseology.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting the cleared altitude instead of present altitude.  
  ✔ Say where you are now; add cleared level only if relevant.

- ❌ Using flight level phraseology below transition altitude.  
  ✔ Use altitude in feet below transition; flight level above as required.

- ❌ Omitting "climbing" or "descending" when still moving vertically.  
  ✔ Add vertical trend when it clarifies the report.

- ❌ Ignoring a mismatch between radar altitude and your report.  
  ✔ Recheck altimeter setting and advise ATC if needed.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**LEVEL** — **L**ook at the altimeter, **E**xpress present value, **V**oice climb/descent if moving, **E**mploy feet or flight level correctly, **L**eave cleared altitude out of the "say altitude" answer until reached.
