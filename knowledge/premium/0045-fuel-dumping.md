# Fuel Dumping

**Catalog ID:** 0045  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers intentional fuel release — a procedure common on fixed-wing aircraft but rare in helicopters.

Fuel Dumping means deliberately releasing fuel to reduce landing weight below maximum landing weight.

Large jets use jettison systems after emergency takeoff or early return with full tanks.

The H130 does not have a fuel jettison system — helicopters rarely dump fuel.

But ICAO phraseology for fuel dumping appears on exams, and you must understand the fixed-wing context.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 vocabulary and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Fuel Dumping is the intentional release of fuel from an aircraft to reduce weight for a safe landing.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Descarte de combustível.

Liberação intencional de combustível para reduzir o peso da aeronave para pouso seguro.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Fuel Dumping is required, the crew should:

- request a fuel dumping area and altitude from ATC before starting
- declare Mayday or Pan Pan for the underlying emergency
- dump fuel only in assigned airspace after ATC clears traffic
- monitor weight reduction and report when dumping is complete
- proceed to land after reaching safe landing weight

Fuel Dumping reporting commonly includes:

- Mayday, requesting fuel dump
- requesting fuel dumping area
- we need to dump fuel before returning
- fuel dumping complete, request descent

**Important:** The H130 cannot dump fuel. Learn this phraseology for ICAO exams and fixed-wing operations. Always request ATC clearance before dumping.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Fuel Dumping requests to:

- assign a designated fuel dumping area and altitude block
- clear traffic from the dumping area
- notify other aircraft in the vicinity
- monitor progress and request dump complete report
- clear the aircraft to descend and land after dump is complete

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are training on ICAO phraseology in a fixed-wing simulator scenario based on a departure from Guarulhos.

Shortly after takeoff, the aircraft must return due to a cabin pressurization issue with full fuel tanks — overweight for landing.

The captain transmits:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, São Paulo Departure, ANAC123, requesting fuel dump before return to Guarulhos.

Departure assigns a fuel dumping area between flight level one zero zero and flight level one five zero.

After fifteen minutes, the captain reports:

ANAC123, fuel dumping complete, request descent to Guarulhos.

In your H130 operations, you would never perform this procedure — but you may hear it on frequency near major airports.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Departure: ANAC123, cleared to dump fuel between flight level one zero zero and flight level one five zero.
2. Departure: ANAC123, report fuel dumping complete.
3. Departure: ANAC123, traffic cleared from your area.
4. Approach: ANAC123, descend when able after dump complete.
5. Tower: ANAC123, runway cleared to land when ready.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Fuel dumping is fixed-wing territory.

The H130 has no jettison valves. You cannot dump fuel in a helicopter.

But the ICAO Delta exam tests this phraseology — know it even if you never use it in the H130.

Large jets departing Guarulhos or Galeão with full tanks may need to dump after early return.

The sequence: declare emergency, request fuel dumping area, wait for ATC clearance, dump in assigned block, report complete, then descend.

Never dump without ATC clearance — other aircraft may be below you.

The related concept for helicopters is fuel burn in holding — circling to reduce weight, not jettisoning.

The Bogota cabin pressure scenario uses hold to burn fuel — different procedure, same goal: reduce weight for landing.

For your exam, know requesting fuel dump and fuel dumping complete.

For your H130 career, know that you will land overweight if needed — helicopters handle it differently.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you request from ATC before starting fuel dump?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare Mayday or Pan Pan for the underlying emergency, request a fuel dumping area and altitude, and wait for ATC to clear traffic before starting. I would report fuel dumping complete before requesting descent to land.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Not informing ATC before dumping — ATC must clear traffic.  
  ✔ Request fuel dumping area and wait for clearance.

- ❌ Saying throw fuel instead of dump fuel.  
  ✔ Use dump fuel or fuel dumping — standard ICAO phraseology.

- ❌ Forgetting to report dump complete before descent.  
  ✔ Report fuel dumping complete, then request descent.

- ❌ Assuming helicopters dump fuel like jets.  
  ✔ Know the H130 has no jettison system — this is fixed-wing phraseology.
