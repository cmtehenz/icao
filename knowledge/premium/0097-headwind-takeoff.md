# Headwind Takeoff

**Catalog ID:** 0097  
**Category:** Performance  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers headwind takeoff — departing into a wind component from ahead of the aircraft, reducing ground roll, improving climb gradient, and increasing safety margins.

Headwind Takeoff is the preferred condition for all aircraft, especially in hot and humid conditions in Brazil where performance margins can be limited.

In the H130, a headwind on departure provides better initial climb, shorter ground roll, and more obstacle clearance — critical at airports like Congonhas and Florianópolis.

You must request a headwind runway when available, and communicate your performance requirements to ATC when necessary.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 performance communication.

## 📖 Meaning

A Headwind Takeoff is a departure made with wind blowing from ahead of the aircraft, reducing the takeoff ground roll and improving initial climb performance.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a Headwind Takeoff is planned, the pilot should:

- identify the runway that gives the greatest headwind component
- request the preferred runway when a tailwind is assigned
- calculate takeoff performance with the actual wind
- advise ATC if a runway change is needed for performance reasons
- depart with full awareness of wind gradient at altitude

Headwind Takeoff reporting commonly includes:

- request runway into wind for performance
- advise unable to depart with tailwind component
- performance limited, request preferred runway
- backtrack for full runway if required

**Important:** A ten-knot headwind can reduce ground roll by twenty percent or more. At high-altitude or hot airports, this difference can be the margin between a safe departure and an overrun.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC supports Headwind Takeoffs by:

- providing current wind direction and speed before departure
- assigning the most into-wind runway when traffic permits
- approving runway changes for performance or safety reasons
- advising of wind gradient or windshear reports on departure
- accommodating specific performance requests

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are ready for departure at Florianópolis in your H130 on a hot afternoon.

Tower assigns runway one four — giving a direct tailwind with wind from three three zero at ten knots.

Performance calculations show the tailwind is marginal.

You transmit:

Florianópolis Tower, ANAC123, request runway three two, into wind, performance limited with tailwind on one four.

Tower approves runway three two.

You depart with a comfortable headwind and full performance margins.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, wind three three zero at one zero knots, runway one four, cleared for takeoff.
2. Tower: ANAC123, runway three two available, say ready.
3. Tower: ANAC123, confirm runway change request, say reason.
4. Tower: ANAC123, roger performance limitation, runway three two, line up and wait.
5. Tower: ANAC123, wind three four zero at one two knots, expect headwind component on runway three two.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if you needed a headwind runway for performance reasons?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would inform ATC that I have a performance limitation and request a runway that gives a headwind component. I would state my reason clearly — for example, performance limited with tailwind on assigned runway. If no other runway was available, I would calculate whether the takeoff could be completed safely within the available distance.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Accepting a tailwind runway without checking performance.  
  ✔ Always check headwind or tailwind component against your departure calculations.

- ❌ Saying "preciso de outra pista" instead of a professional request.  
  ✔ Use request runway into wind, performance limited.

- ❌ Not mentioning the reason for the runway change request.  
  ✔ State performance limited so ATC understands the urgency.

- ❌ Forgetting to verify the full runway length is available.  
  ✔ Confirm backtrack or full runway if performance is marginal.

Tower clears you for takeoff on runway one four at Florianópolis. Wind is three three zero at twelve knots. Your H130 performance calculation shows the tailwind is marginal for the available runway.

**Your first task**

What do you request and how do you phrase it?
