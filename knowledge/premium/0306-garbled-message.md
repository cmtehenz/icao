# Garbled Message

**Catalog ID:** 0306  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Garbled Message — a radiotelephony transmission that is distorted, scrambled, or only partly intelligible so that the receiving station cannot safely copy the full meaning. Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: recognize garble, request a repeat, and never act on a half-heard clearance.

Garbled message is High priority because a distorted altitude, heading, or frequency can look almost correct and still be wrong.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for garbled transmissions, say-again requests, and clean retransmits under DECEA/ICAO procedures.

Understanding garbled messages in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Garbled Message is a radiotelephony transmission that is distorted, overlapping, or otherwise unintelligible in whole or in part, so that the receiving station cannot reliably understand the intended instruction or report and must request a repeat or clarification before acting.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Mensagem distorcida / Garbled Message.

Transmissão radiotelefônica distorcida, sobreposta ou de outro modo ininteligível no todo ou em parte, de forma que a estação receptora não consegue compreender com segurança a instrução ou o relatório pretendido e deve solicitar repetição ou esclarecimento antes de agir.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a message is garbled, the crew should:

- admit immediately that the transmission was not copied clearly
- request say again, or say again all after / all before the last clear word
- never fill gaps with assumptions about altitude, heading, or runway
- slow speech, shorten the next call, and check headset and volume
- for H130: expect garble near terrain, cities, and during high cabin noise or hover
- if ATC reports your call garbled, retransmit once, clearly and slowly

Garbled-message exchanges commonly include:

- garbled / you are garbled
- say again / say again all after...
- unreadable / broken
- retransmit your message
- how do you read?

**Important:** A garbled clearance is not a clearance. If any critical element is missing, request a full repeat before changing altitude, heading, or frequency.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers manage garbled messages to:

- recover instructions that were distorted on the frequency
- force a clean retransmit before the aircraft acts
- identify weak transmitters, stuck overlap, or frequency problems
- protect separation when only part of a clearance was heard
- keep Brazilian terminal and en-route frequencies usable under congestion

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 inbound to Congonhas on São Paulo Approach. Approach issues a heading and altitude, but the call is distorted by overlap.

You hear only fragments — something about two thousand and a turn, but not the full instruction.

You do not guess. You call:

ANAC123, say again, you were garbled.

Approach repeats cleanly. You read back the full clearance. Only then do you turn and descend.

Later Approach reports your own call garbled. You slow down, shorten the message, and retransmit once. Garble is common; acting on garble is not.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, you are garbled, say again.
2. Approach: ANAC123, garbled, say again all after altitude.
3. Tower: ANAC123, transmission garbled, retransmit intentions.
4. Control: ANAC123, unreadable, say again slowly.
5. Ground: ANAC123, garbled on first call, go ahead again.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, say again, you were garbled.
2. ANAC123, say again all after heading.
3. ANAC123, request repeat clearance, previous call garbled.
4. ANAC123, retransmitting — request descent two thousand.
5. ANAC123, clearance copied, readback follows.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Garble is a honesty test on the radio.

If you only heard half the clearance, you heard nothing you can use. Say again. Do not invent the missing digits.

Brazilian terminal frequencies get busy. Overlap, terrain, and cabin noise in the H130 all create distortion. That is normal. Pretending you copied is the failure.

When ATC says you are garbled, do not defend the call — fix it. Slow down. Shorten. Retransmit once. Part 2 examiners listen for that recovery, not for perfect first transmissions.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC issues a clearance but the transmission is garbled and you only copy part of the altitude. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would not act on a partial clearance. I would advise that the transmission was garbled and request say again, or say again all after the last word I copied clearly. After receiving a clean repeat, I would read back the full clearance and only then comply. If my own transmission is reported garbled, I would slow down, shorten the message, and retransmit once.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Guessing the missing altitude or heading from context.  
  ✔ Request say again until every critical element is clear.

- ❌ Saying "roger" after a garbled call.  
  ✔ Roger means you understood — do not use it if you did not.

- ❌ Retransmitting a long speech when ATC said you were garbled.  
  ✔ One short, slow, clear retransmit.

- ❌ Changing frequency while still unsure of the instruction.  
  ✔ Confirm the clearance first, then switch if required.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Say Again, You Were Garbled

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, say uh-GEN, yoo wer GAR-buld

**Word Stress**

- Again → uh-GEN
- Garbled → GAR-buld
- Retransmit → ree-tranz-MIT
- Clearance → KLEER-ans

Practice:

Say again, you were garbled... say again all after altitude.

Together:

ANAC123, say again, you were garbled. ANAC123, say again all after altitude.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Approach issues a heading and altitude; the call is garbled. Later Approach says your readback was garbled.

**Your first task**

What do you say when Approach's clearance is garbled?

**Your second task**

What do you say when Approach reports your transmission garbled?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, say again, you were garbled — or ANAC123, say again all after heading. Second task: Slow down and retransmit once, for example ANAC123, heading two seven zero, descending two thousand. Do not assume either call was understood until a clean exchange and readback are complete.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Garbled message is High-priority Part 2 discipline for Brazilian H130 operations.

Distortion is common. Guessing is not. If the message is garbled, say again. If you are garbled, retransmit cleanly.

Never change the aircraft on a half-heard clearance. Copy. Confirm. Then fly.

That is the garbled message lesson — protect the clearance by protecting the words.
