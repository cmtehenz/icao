# Go-Around Decision

**Catalog ID:** 0485  
**Category:** CRM  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Go-Around Decision — the crew process of recognizing that an approach or landing should be abandoned and executing a missed approach or rejected landing without delay.

The go-around decision is CRM under pressure. It combines unstable-approach criteria, plan-continuation resistance, assertiveness, and clear ATC communication. A late go-around is better than a forced landing; an early go-around is better than a late one.

In H130 operations — confined areas, obstacle-rich finals, and changing winds — the go-around is a normal maneuver, not a failure. Treating it as failure is how crews talk themselves into continuing.

Understanding how to make and communicate the go-around decision in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

A Go-Around Decision is the determination by the flight crew to discontinue an approach or landing and execute a missed approach or rejected landing because continuation would be unsafe, unstable, or outside operational limits.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Decisão de arremetida / Go-Around Decision.

Determinação da tripulação de interromper uma aproximação ou pouso e executar uma arremetida ou pouso rejeitado porque continuar seria inseguro, instável ou fora dos limites operacionais.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a go-around decision is required, the crew should:

- state "going around" clearly — flying pilot or monitoring pilot per SOP
- set go-around attitude and power immediately
- follow the published missed approach or ATC go-around instructions
- communicate to ATC as soon as workload allows: going around
- decide next intention: second approach, hold, or divert

Go-around triggers include:

- unstable approach at the gate
- runway occupied or insufficient landing assurance
- GPWS or terrain warning
- windshear, sudden weather below minima, or loss of visual references
- any crew member calling go-around for safety

**Important:** Either pilot can call the go-around. The response is execution, not debate. In CRM terms, the go-around call is a non-negotiable safety command.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers facilitate go-around decisions by:

- issuing immediate missed-approach instructions
- keeping the go-around path clear of traffic
- re-sequencing for a second approach without pressure
- providing wind, runway, and weather updates that support the decision
- coordinating diversion when a second approach is not appropriate

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Final to Florianópolis. At four hundred feet a vehicle enters the runway. Your first officer calls go-around.

You execute and transmit:

ANAC123, going around, runway occupied.

Tower: ANAC123, go around, fly runway heading, climb three thousand, contact approach.

You respond:

ANAC123, going around, runway heading, climbing three thousand.

No hesitation. No attempt to "see if they clear." The decision was made at the call.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, go around, fly runway heading, climb three thousand.
2. Tower: ANAC123, go around, traffic on the runway.
3. Approach: ANAC123, radar contact, climb four thousand, say intentions.
4. Tower: ANAC123, confirm going around.
5. Approach: ANAC123, cleared for second approach when ready, advise.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

The go-around decision is CRM's sharpest tool.

It ends arguments with physics before physics wins.

In the H130, brief go-around as the default escape, not the embarrassing exit. Praise the call. Execute the call. Never punish the call.

If you are deciding whether to go around, you have already waited too long — go around.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Your first officer calls go-around on short final for an unstable condition. What do you do and say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would execute immediately and report: ANAC123, going around, unstable approach. I would fly the missed-approach instructions, then state intentions for a second approach or diversion. I would not debate the call on short final. CRM means the go-around call is actioned, then reviewed on the ground if needed.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Questioning the go-around call while still on final.  
  ✔ Execute first; debrief later.

- ❌ Delaying the ATC call until established in the climb.  
  ✔ Transmit "going around" as soon as workload allows — often immediately.

- ❌ Treating go-around as personal failure in front of the crew.  
  ✔ Treat it as a normal professional outcome.

- ❌ Returning for the same approach without addressing the trigger.  
  ✔ Fix the cause — energy, weather, runway — before the next attempt.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

On short final a vehicle enters the runway. Your first officer calls go-around. You are tempted to wait two seconds.

**Your first task**

What do you say to ATC as you execute?

**Your second task**

Approach asks intentions after the missed approach. Weather is good. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, going around, runway occupied. Second task: ANAC123, request vectors for second approach. The go-around decision is executed on the call, not after a hope that the runway clears. CRM means action first, sequencing second.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

The go-around decision is where CRM becomes kinetic.

Call it. Fly it. Tell ATC. Then choose the next safe plan.

In the H130, a go-around is a success condition whenever continuation is not. Make the decision early, make the radio clear, and make the maneuver routine.
