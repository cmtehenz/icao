# Unaccompanied Minor

**Catalog ID:** 0333  
**Category:** Passenger  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Unaccompanied Minor — a child traveling without a parent or adult guardian, requiring special handling, custody transfer, and clear coordination with ground staff and, when needed, ATC or apron services.

Unaccompanied Minor is Medium priority and mainly Part 2 operational English: identifying the passenger category, coordinating meet-and-assist, and ensuring the child is never left unsupervised during boarding, flight, or disembarkation.

For H130 Brazilian operations — training flights, air taxi, and inter-city transfers — unaccompanied minors may travel with operator procedures rather than airline UMNR desks. The English still matters: unaccompanied minor on board, request assistance on arrival, guardian meeting the aircraft.

Understanding how to communicate unaccompanied minor handling in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Unaccompanied Minor is a child passenger traveling without a parent or legal adult guardian, who must be supervised under operator procedures from acceptance through delivery to the authorized receiving adult.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Menor desacompanhado / Unaccompanied Minor.

Passageiro criança viajando sem pai, mãe ou responsável adulto legal, que deve ser supervisionado conforme procedimentos do operador desde a aceitação até a entrega ao adulto autorizado no destino.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When an unaccompanied minor is on board, the crew should:

- confirm operator acceptance paperwork and receiving-adult details before departure
- brief the child simply on seat belt, doors, and when to speak to the pilot
- keep continuous supervision — critical in H130 ops with no cabin crew
- coordinate with Ground or handling for meet-and-assist on arrival
- do not release the child except to the authorized adult or handling agent

Unaccompanied Minor communication commonly includes:

- unaccompanied minor on board, request assistance on arrival
- request meet-and-assist for unaccompanied minor
- child passenger, guardian meeting at parking
- advise delay if receiving adult is not present

**Important:** On the H130, the pilot is often the only crew member. That means the minor is your responsibility for the entire flight. Never leave a child alone on the apron under rotor wash. Shut down or keep rotors safe before handover. If the receiving adult is missing, keep the child and advise operations or Ground.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers and ground agencies coordinate unaccompanied minor handling to:

- arrange meet-and-assist at parking or the helipad
- hold the aircraft or delay disembarkation if the guardian is not present
- connect the crew with handling or operations frequency when needed
- avoid releasing a child into an unsafe apron environment
- support operator custody procedures with clear radio coordination

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You arrive at Navegantes in the H130 with one unaccompanied minor and no cabin crew.

After landing and shutdown, you call Ground:

Ground, ANAC123, unaccompanied minor on board, request meet-and-assist, guardian should be at parking.

Ground confirms handling is on the way. You keep the child seated until the authorized adult is identified and paperwork checked. Only then do you release custody. Rotor area remains clear throughout.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, roger, handling will meet you for the unaccompanied minor.
2. Tower: ANAC123, after landing contact Ground for passenger assistance.
3. Ground: ANAC123, confirm unaccompanied minor on board.
4. Ground: ANAC123, hold passengers on board, receiving adult not yet present.
5. Apron: ANAC123, meet-and-assist at stand, guardian identified.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, unaccompanied minor on board, request meet-and-assist on arrival.
2. ANAC123, requesting handling for unaccompanied minor at parking.
3. ANAC123, wilco, will hold the minor on board until guardian arrives.
4. ANAC123, child passenger for custody transfer, request assistance.
5. ANAC123, unaccompanied minor delivered to handling, ready to continue.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Unaccompanied minor is a custody word, not a medical word.

Your job is continuous responsibility until the authorized adult or handling agent takes over.

Airlines have UMNR procedures and cabin crew. The H130 often has you alone. That raises the standard of your briefing and your apron discipline.

Use clear English with Ground: unaccompanied minor, meet-and-assist, holding on board until guardian present.

Do not improvise custody. If the adult at the fence is not the authorized person, keep the child and call operations.

Rotor safety and custody safety are the same landing: nobody runs, nobody is released early.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What must a pilot ensure when carrying an unaccompanied minor, especially in a helicopter without cabin crew?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I must supervise the child for the entire flight and only release the child to the authorized receiving adult or handling agent. I should coordinate meet-and-assist on arrival and never leave the minor unsupervised on the apron, especially with rotors turning.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Releasing the child to any adult who waves on the apron.  
  ✔ Verify the authorized receiving adult or handling agent.

- ❌ Hot-disembarking a child under turning rotors without strict control.  
  ✔ Prefer shutdown or a fully briefed safe arc with marshaller control.

- ❌ Saying only "child on board" when you need handling help.  
  ✔ Say unaccompanied minor and request meet-and-assist.

- ❌ Leaving the minor alone in the aircraft while you walk to operations.  
  ✔ Maintain continuous supervision.

**Pronunciation:** un-uh-KUM-puh-need MY-ner on BOARD, reh-KWEST MEET and uh-SIST

**Word Stress**

- Unaccompanied → un-uh-KUM-puh-need (stress on third)
- Minor → MY-ner
- Meet-and-assist → MEET and uh-SIST

Together:

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**MINOR** — **M**eet-and-assist requested, **I**D the receiving adult, **N**ever leave the child alone, **O**nly authorized handover, **R**otors safe before release.
