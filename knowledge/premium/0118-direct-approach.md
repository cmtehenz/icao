# Direct Approach

**Catalog ID:** 0118  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the Direct Approach — a routing instruction that shortens the inbound path and saves time, fuel, and workload.

A Direct Approach means ATC clears the aircraft to proceed directly toward the runway or final approach fix without flying the full published approach procedure.

In the H130, Direct Approaches are common at Navegantes, Joinville, and Curitiba when traffic is light and conditions allow.

Understanding this instruction allows you to accept it confidently, read it back correctly, and manage your approach profile.

## 📖 Meaning

A Direct Approach is an ATC instruction clearing an aircraft to proceed directly toward the runway, threshold, or final approach fix, bypassing portions of the standard approach procedure.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Aproximação direta.

Instrução do controle de tráfego aéreo que autoriza a aeronave a prosseguir diretamente para a pista, cabeceira ou ponto de aproximação final, dispensando partes do procedimento padrão de aproximação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When cleared for a Direct Approach, the pilot should:

- confirm the runway or fix toward which direct routing is authorized
- assess whether the aircraft can achieve the required descent profile
- ensure adequate distance remains to configure and stabilize
- accept only when a stable approach is achievable

Direct Approach operations commonly include:

- visual conditions with ceiling and visibility above minima
- radar environment where ATC can provide vectors
- pilot confirming runway or fix in sight
- adjustment of descent rate to account for shortened distance

**Important:** A Direct Approach is only safe if you have enough distance to configure, descend, and stabilize. If the routing is too tight, request additional track miles.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Direct Approach to:

- reduce delays in light traffic or VFR conditions
- increase airport capacity by shortening approach time
- accommodate a pilot's request for an expedited arrival
- avoid unnecessary track miles in uncongested airspace
- improve fuel efficiency during arrival

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Navegantes from the north at 3,000 feet in your H130.

Approach advises:

ANAC123, traffic is light, direct approach runway two nine approved, report final.

You assess your position and altitude.

You have sufficient distance to configure and stabilize.

You respond:

Direct approach runway two nine, ANAC123.

You adjust your descent, configure the aircraft, and join final stabilized by 500 feet.

You report:

ANAC123, final, runway two nine.

Tower acknowledges and clears you to land.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, direct approach runway two nine approved, report final.
2. Approach: ANAC123, cleared direct approach, advise runway in sight.
3. Approach: ANAC123, direct approach available, confirm able.
4. Tower: ANAC123, direct approach runway one five, cleared to land.
5. Approach: ANAC123, direct approach approved, no delay, traffic behind.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Direct approach runway two nine, ANAC123.
2. ANAC123, direct approach, wilco.
3. ANAC123, unable direct approach, request additional track miles to configure.
4. ANAC123, direct approach accepted, will report final.
5. Direct approach runway one five, ANAC123, wilco.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

A Direct Approach feels like a gift.

Less flying. Less time. Less fuel.

But every gift comes with responsibility.

When ATC says direct approach, the question is not "should I accept?"

The question is "can I stabilize?"

In the H130, direct approaches at Navegantes are common. The runway appears ahead of you, the controller offers the shortcut, and it feels easy to say yes.

Say yes only when you have the distance to configure and stabilize.

Never say yes because you feel you should.

Brazilian pilots sometimes accept direct approaches and then descend steeply or configure late.

The correct answer when the geometry does not work is: unable direct approach, request additional track miles.

One sentence. No embarrassment. Full safety.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How would you respond if ATC offered a direct approach but you needed more distance to configure?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would advise ATC that I was unable to accept the direct approach and request additional track miles to configure and stabilize. I would say: ANAC123, unable direct approach, request additional track miles. Safety of the approach takes priority over expedition.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Accepting a direct approach when there is insufficient distance to stabilize.  
  ✔ Only accept if a stable approach is achievable.

- ❌ Not reading back the runway number in the readback.  
  ✔ Include the runway: Direct approach runway two nine, ANAC123.

- ❌ Saying "direto para a pista" on the English frequency.  
  ✔ Use direct approach, runway [number].

- ❌ Forgetting to report final after accepting a direct approach.  
  ✔ Report as instructed: ANAC123, final, runway two nine.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Direct Approach

**Pronunciation:** dih-REKT uh-PROHCH

**Word Stress**

- Direct → dih-REKT (stress on second syllable)
- Approach → uh-PROHCH (stress on second syllable)

Practice:

Direct... Approach... Direct Approach.

Together:

Direct approach runway two nine, ANAC123.

Avoid: *direto proch* — this is a common Portuguese interference. Say both words fully and clearly.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Approach at Joinville transmits: "ANAC123, direct approach runway one five approved, report final."

**Your first task**

You have sufficient distance to configure and stabilize. What do you say?

**Your second task**

You assess the geometry and realize you are too close for a stable approach. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

If able, I would respond: Direct approach runway one five, ANAC123, will report final. If unable, I would say: ANAC123, unable direct approach, request additional track miles to configure. The safety of the approach always takes priority over the expedited routing.
