# Cargo Door Separated

**Catalog ID:** 0280  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Cargo Door Separated — complete separation of a cargo door from the aircraft in flight, a structural and aerodynamic emergency requiring immediate control assessment, possible emergency descent, distress communication, and priority landing. This is classic airliner ICAO exam English.

For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, you may not have a transport-category cargo door, but you must speak the concept fluently. Relate it to door or panel separation, baggage door loss, or external load hardware failure using the same radio structure: what happened, aircraft controllability, intentions.

Master: cargo door separated, request immediate landing, aircraft controllable / not fully controllable.

## 📖 Meaning

Cargo Door Separated is the complete detachment of a cargo door from the aircraft during flight, creating structural damage risk, possible cabin or hold pressure effects on pressurized types, aerodynamic disturbance, and requiring immediate assessment of controllability, ATC notification, and landing at the nearest suitable airport.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Porta de carga separada / Cargo Door Separated.

Desprendimento completo de uma porta de carga da aeronave em voo, criando risco de dano estrutural, possíveis efeitos de pressão em tipos pressurizados, distúrbio aerodinâmico, e exigindo avaliação imediata da controlabilidade, notificação ao ATC e pouso no aeroporto adequado mais próximo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a cargo door separates, the crew should:

- maintain aircraft control; reduce speed if required by AFM / structural considerations
- assess controllability, vibration, and any pressurization or systems effects
- declare Mayday for door separation with structural risk; Pan Pan only if clearly minor and fully controllable per judgment — prefer Mayday when separation is confirmed
- request immediate landing / diversion
- report nature (cargo door separated), controllability, intentions; souls and fuel when asked
- for H130: use door separated / panel separated for actual events; keep cargo door separated for exam English

Cargo door separated reporting commonly includes:

- cargo door separated / cargo door lost
- aircraft controllable (or describe control difficulty)
- request immediate landing
- possible debris on departure path (if just after takeoff)
- souls and fuel

**Important:** Advise ATC if debris may have fallen on the airport or departure path so they can inspect runways and warn other traffic.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to cargo door separation reports to:

- provide priority landing and traffic avoidance
- inspect or close areas where debris may have fallen
- alert emergency services for structural damage on arrival
- request controllability and souls/fuel information
- coordinate the shortest safe approach

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Training scenario: after takeoff, a loud bang and the cargo door is gone. Aircraft remains controllable with vibration.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Departure, ANAC123, cargo door separated, aircraft controllable, request immediate return to land.

Departure: ANAC123, roger Mayday, turn left heading two seven zero, descend, runway available, say souls and fuel when able. Possible debris on departure path — we will inspect.

You continue:

ANAC123, turning left two seven zero, cargo door separated, mild vibration, souls eight, fuel two hours, request shortest approach.

For H130, same structure if a door or panel separates — state what separated and that you are returning.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Departure: ANAC123, roger Mayday cargo door separated, turn left heading two seven zero, cleared to return.
2. Approach: ANAC123, say if the aircraft is fully controllable.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway cleared, emergency services standing by.
4. Departure: ANAC123, confirm possible debris location if known.
5. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Cargo door separated is structural until proven otherwise.

Fly the aircraft. Slow if required. Mayday. Return. Tell ATC about debris.

Brazilian pilots may say "porta de carga saiu" or "perdeu a porta." Prefer cargo door separated in ICAO English — then controllability and intentions.

In the H130, doors and panels can still depart. Use accurate location words. Do not invent jet cargo-door systems you do not have — but do not fail the exam for not knowing the phrase.

Never continue the flight to "see how it feels" at cruise with a missing door and unknown structural damage.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

A cargo door separates after takeoff. The aircraft is still controllable. What do you do and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain control, reduce speed if required, declare Mayday stating cargo door separated and that the aircraft is controllable, and request an immediate return or diversion. I would advise ATC of possible debris on the departure path, report souls and fuel when able, and land with emergency services standing by. I would not continue the flight with unknown structural damage.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "door problem" without "separated."  
  ✔ State cargo door separated clearly.

- ❌ Not reporting controllability.  
  ✔ Say aircraft controllable or describe the difficulty.

- ❌ Forgetting possible debris for other traffic.  
  ✔ Advise ATC if debris may be on the airport or departure path.

- ❌ Treating H130 as if it has a jet cargo hold door system.  
  ✔ Exam English yes; type systems factual.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Cargo door separated is a structural emergency with a clear name.

Control. Mayday. Return. Warn about debris.

The H130 may not have a jet cargo door — ICAO English still will. Learn the phrase. Fly your type's truth.

Tell ATC you are controllable — or that you are not. Either way, get on the ground and let them protect other traffic from debris.

That is how door separations end as inspections on the ramp — not as continued flights with unknown damage.
