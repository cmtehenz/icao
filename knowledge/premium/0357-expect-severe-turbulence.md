# Expect Severe Turbulence

**Catalog ID:** 0357  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Expect Severe Turbulence — an ATC or meteorological advisory that severe turbulence is forecast or reported ahead, requiring immediate preparation, possible route or altitude change before entry, and precise distress or urgency language if control becomes difficult.

Severe turbulence is large abrupt changes in attitude and altitude, with the aircraft momentarily difficult to control. An "expect" advisory is your chance to avoid or minimize exposure — not a reason to fly blindly into the area.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, severe turbulence near thunderstorms, mountain waves, and strong frontal gradients is a real threat to rotorcraft. Part 2 requires clean recognition of the advisory and correct response phraseology.

Understanding expect severe turbulence in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Expect Severe Turbulence is an advisory indicating that severe turbulence — large abrupt changes in attitude and altitude with momentary difficulty of control — is forecast or reported ahead, requiring crews to prepare, consider avoidance, and report actual conditions if encountered.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Expectativa de turbulência severa / Expect Severe Turbulence.

Aviso indicando que turbulência severa — grandes mudanças abruptas de atitude e altitude com dificuldade momentânea de controle — é prevista ou reportada à frente, exigindo que as tripulações se preparem, considerem desvio e reportem as condições reais se encontradas.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC says expect severe turbulence, the crew should:

- acknowledge and treat the advisory as high priority
- secure cabin immediately; consider delaying entry into the area
- request deviation, climb, or descent before entering if possible
- reduce to turbulence penetration speed if published and appropriate
- if encountered, maintain attitude control and report severe turbulence
- declare urgency if control difficulty threatens the flight path

Expect-severe handling commonly includes:

- roger, request weather deviation / alternate altitude
- unable to accept route due to severe turbulence
- severe turbulence encountered at altitude
- request immediate climb / descent / diversion

**Important:** Do not enter known severe turbulence for schedule. Avoidance is the primary strategy for helicopters. Report honestly if you encounter it.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers issue expect severe turbulence to:

- warn crews before they enter dangerous air
- enable early deviations and altitude changes
- collect PIREPs that protect following traffic
- coordinate sector weather avoidance plans
- prevent multiple aircraft from stacking into the same hazard

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

West of Curitiba at six thousand feet in the H130, Center advises expect severe turbulence associated with convective activity ahead.

You request weather deviation left of course and a climb. Center approves. You remain clear of the cell. A following aircraft that stayed on course reports severe turbulence. Your early request kept the H130 out of the worst air.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Center: ANAC123, expect severe turbulence ahead, advise intentions.
2. Center: ANAC123, deviation left of course approved, report clear of weather.
3. Approach: ANAC123, previous aircraft reported severe turbulence at six thousand.
4. Center: ANAC123, say if able to accept present routing.
5. Center: ANAC123, climb and maintain eight thousand, severe turbulence reported below.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, roger, expect severe turbulence, request weather deviation left.
2. ANAC123, unable present routing, request deviation for severe turbulence.
3. ANAC123, severe turbulence at six thousand, request immediate climb.
4. ANAC123, clear of weather, light turbulence now.
5. ANAC123, diverting for weather, severe turbulence reported on course.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Expect severe turbulence is a red flag, not a suggestion.

Prepare. Avoid. Report if you must enter.

Brazilian frequency sometimes gets "turbulência forte" — use severe turbulence, the ICAO intensity term.

In the H130, rotor and mast loads make severe air a no-go when you have an exit. Ask for deviation early — before you are inside the cell and the radio is saturated.

Part 2 checks whether you escalate correctly from expect moderate to expect severe in decision and language.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC advises expect severe turbulence on your route. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would acknowledge and request weather deviation or an alternate altitude before entering the area. If I had already encountered severe turbulence, I would report severe turbulence with altitude and request immediate climb, descent, or diversion. Avoidance is the priority, especially in a helicopter.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing into the area without requesting deviation.  
  ✔ Request weather deviation or altitude change immediately.

- ❌ Saying "turbulência forte" instead of severe.  
  ✔ Use severe turbulence.

- ❌ Downgrading severe to moderate to avoid paperwork or delay.  
  ✔ Report true intensity — other aircraft depend on it.

- ❌ Waiting until inside the cell to ask for help.  
  ✔ Ask early while options still exist.
