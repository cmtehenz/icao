# Windshear Escape Maneuver

**Catalog ID:** 0399  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the Windshear Escape Maneuver — the maximum-performance procedure used to escape severe windshear or microburst conditions on takeoff or approach.

Windshear escape is not a gentle go-around. It is a committed, maximum-power climb to survive a sudden loss of performance.

In the H130, windshear near the ground can exceed available climb performance. Brazilian coastal and plateau airports — Congonhas, Florianópolis, Confins — see convective shear that demands early escape and clear ATC communication.

## 📖 Meaning

The Windshear Escape Maneuver is a maximum-performance climb procedure flown when severe windshear or microburst is encountered, using maximum power, an appropriate climb attitude, and configuration per the flight manual until the aircraft is clear of the shear.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Manobra de escape de windshear / cisalhamento.

Procedimento de subida de máximo desempenho executado ao encontrar windshear severo ou microburst, utilizando potência máxima, atitude de subida apropriada e configuração conforme o manual de voo até a aeronave sair da zona de cisalhamento.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When executing a Windshear Escape Maneuver, the pilot should:

- apply maximum power immediately
- pitch to the escape attitude — do not chase airspeed into the ground
- maintain configuration until clear of shear per AFM guidance
- go around if on approach — do not attempt to salvage the landing
- report windshear escape to ATC as soon as workload permits

Windshear Escape reporting commonly includes:

- windshear escape, going around
- severe windshear on final or departure
- request climb and vectors
- microburst, escaping

**Important:** Escape first. Talk second. A delayed call is better than a delayed escape — but once climbing, warn ATC so following traffic is protected.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to Windshear Escape reports to:

- suspend arrivals and departures if needed
- warn all aircraft on frequency
- clear altitude and heading for the escaping aircraft
- relay PIREPs to subsequent traffic
- coordinate holding until conditions improve

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on short final to Congonhas in your H130. Airspeed bleeds off and a violent downdraft begins.

You apply maximum power, set escape attitude, and abandon the approach.

You transmit:

Congonhas Tower, ANAC123, windshear escape, going around, severe downdraft on final, request climb two thousand.

Tower suspends approaches and alerts all aircraft. You climb clear and hold until the cell moves.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger windshear escape, climb and maintain two thousand, all aircraft advised.
2. Tower: ANAC123, approaches suspended, say intentions when able.
3. Approach: ANAC123, previous aircraft reported windshear on final, advise if continuing.
4. Tower: ANAC123, cleared go-around, windshear alert in effect.
5. Approach: ANAC123, hold over XINGU, expect further clearance when shear abates.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Windshear escape is survival flying, not a normal missed approach.

Maximum power. Escape attitude. Fly through the shear. Do not dump nose for airspeed if that puts you into the ground. Do not chase a landing that is already lost.

In the H130, you have less inertia than a jet and less excess power than many twins. Escape early. If the rain shaft is on final, do not enter it to "have a look."

The radio call must include the word windshear or microburst. "Going around" alone does not warn the next aircraft.

Brazilian convective afternoons punish late decisions. Escape, climb, report, then decide whether to return or divert.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You encounter severe windshear on short final. Describe the maneuver and your ATC call.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would execute a windshear escape maneuver: maximum power, escape pitch attitude, maintain configuration as required, and climb until clear of the shear. I would not continue the landing. When able, I would transmit: ANAC123, windshear escape, going around, severe windshear on final, request climb two thousand. Then I would advise intentions — hold, divert, or return when conditions improve.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Trying to continue the landing after the first shear encounter.  
  ✔ Escape immediately. One encounter is enough.

- ❌ Calling only "going around" without saying windshear.  
  ✔ State windshear escape so ATC can warn everyone.

- ❌ Reducing power to "stabilize" during the escape.  
  ✔ Keep maximum power until clear of the shear.

- ❌ Re-entering the same rain shaft for a second attempt.  
  ✔ Hold or divert until the cell has moved.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Windshear escape is a committed maneuver.

Maximum performance. Clear of shear. Then communicate.

Say the word windshear. Protect the next crew. Decide return versus divert with fuel and weather — not with hope.

In the H130, early escape is the only reliable strategy.

Fly it. Call it. Survive it.
