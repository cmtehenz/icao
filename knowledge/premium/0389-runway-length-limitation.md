# Runway Length Limitation

**Catalog ID:** 0389  
**Category:** Performance  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Runway Length Limitation — the operational condition in which available runway length is insufficient for the calculated takeoff or landing distance under current weight and environmental conditions.

Runway Length Limitation is a hard stop on the plan. If the runway is too short for the numbers, you change weight, runway, or destination — you do not hope.

For H130 Brazilian operations — Santos Dumont, short municipal strips, and intersection departures — runway length often limits the mission. Communicating that limitation to ATC is essential Part 2 and Part 3 English.

## 📖 Meaning

Runway Length Limitation means that the available takeoff or landing distance is less than the distance required by the aircraft performance calculation for the actual weight, wind, and surface conditions, making the operation unsafe or non-compliant unless the plan is changed.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Limitação por comprimento de pista / Runway Length Limitation.

Condição em que a distância disponível para decolagem ou pouso é menor que a distância exigida pelo cálculo de desempenho da aeronave para o peso, vento e condições de superfície reais, tornando a operação insegura ou não conforme a menos que o plano seja alterado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When runway length is limiting, the crew should:

- compare required takeoff/landing distance to declared distances
- reject intersection departures that shorten available length
- request full length or a longer runway
- reduce weight or divert if no adequate runway exists
- advise ATC clearly: runway length insufficient / performance limited

Runway Length Limitation planning includes:

- TORA, TODA, ASDA, LDA as applicable to the operation
- wet and contaminated factors increasing required distance
- density altitude increasing takeoff distance
- helicopter pad size versus required landing area
- NOTAMs reducing available length (works, displaced threshold)

**Important:** A displaced threshold or temporary works can make a familiar runway suddenly limiting. Check NOTAMs and ATIS. Do not use "usual" length from memory.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers manage runway length limitations by:

- providing full runway when requested
- denying or offering intersection departures appropriately
- assigning longer runways when available
- publishing reduced lengths via ATIS and NOTAM coordination
- sequencing aircraft that cannot accept short intersections

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Santos Dumont. Your H130 landing calculation at current weight on a wet runway requires more distance than available with the reported tailwind.

You call:

Santos Dumont Tower, ANAC123, runway length insufficient for current conditions, request opposite runway or diversion.

Tower: ANAC123, opposite runway available in five minutes, orbit east.

You respond:

ANAC123, orbiting east, expecting opposite runway.

Length limitation stated — plan changed before short final.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, intersection departure available, advise able.
2. Tower: ANAC123, full length available, line up and wait.
3. Tower: ANAC123, displaced threshold, available landing distance reduced.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say runway requirement.
5. Tower: ANAC123, runway works, length reduced, advise intentions.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Runway length is a number match: required versus available.

If required is greater — stop the plan. Full length, other runway, less weight, or divert.

Intersection departures steal length. If you are length-limited, say unable intersection every time. Habit saves you on the day you are tired.

In Brazil, Santos Dumont and short strips punish complacency. The H130 can use small areas — but wet surfaces, weight, and obstacles still demand real distance calculations.

NOTAMs that shorten the runway are easy to miss. Read them. Recalculate. Speak up.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Tower offers an intersection departure. Your takeoff requires full runway length. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would say: ANAC123, unable intersection departure, runway length limited, request full length. I would not accept a shorter takeoff run than calculated. If full length is not available, I would delay, reduce weight, or cancel. Clear limitation language helps ATC re-sequence without argument.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Accepting intersection departure when full length is required.  
  ✔ Unable intersection, request full length.

- ❌ Ignoring displaced threshold reductions in available distance.  
  ✔ Recalculate with the reduced LDA/TORA from ATIS or NOTAM.

- ❌ Assuming helicopter operations never have runway length issues.  
  ✔ Weight, wet surface, and pad size still limit the H130.

- ❌ Waiting until lined up to reject a short runway plan.  
  ✔ State the limitation before accepting the clearance.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Tower offers intersection Alpha departure. Your charts require full TORA. ATIS also mentions a displaced threshold on the reciprocal for landing later.

**Your first task**

What do you say about the departure?

**Your second task**

On return, landing distance with displaced threshold is insufficient wet. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, unable intersection Alpha, request full length runway. Second task: ANAC123, runway length insufficient with displaced threshold wet, request full landing distance or diversion. I would treat reduced declared distances as real limits, not suggestions.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Runway length limitation is simple arithmetic with serious consequences.

Required versus available. If short — change the plan.

Full length. Other runway. Less weight. Divert.

In the H130 on Brazilian short fields, say unable intersection without hesitation.

Match the numbers. Speak the limit. Then go — or do not.

That is runway length discipline.
