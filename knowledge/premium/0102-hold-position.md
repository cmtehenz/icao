# Hold Position

**Catalog ID:** 0102  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers hold position — an ATC ground instruction with the same critical effect as maintain position: stop and do not move.

Hold Position is used interchangeably with maintain position at many airports. Both mean stop completely and wait for clearance.

In the H130, hold position is a standard instruction at Curitiba, São Paulo, and Florianópolis during busy traffic sequences or runway protection events.

You must treat hold position with the same urgency as maintain position — stop, readback with callsign, and wait for the explicit clearance to proceed.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 critical phraseology.

## 📖 Meaning

Hold Position is an ATC ground instruction directing the aircraft to stop immediately at its current position on the movement area and await further clearance before continuing. It is equivalent in meaning to maintain position.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Mantenha posição / Aguarde (hold position).

Instrução do controle de tráfego aéreo para que a aeronave pare imediatamente em sua posição atual na área de movimentação e aguarde autorização antes de prosseguir.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Hold Position is issued, the pilot should:

- stop the aircraft completely at the current location
- readback hold position with callsign
- remain stationary until ATC issues clearance to continue
- not interpret the instruction as reduce speed
- request clarification if the position is ambiguous

Hold Position reporting commonly includes:

- readback hold position with callsign
- query ATC if hold is unexpectedly long
- confirm holding short of specific runway or taxiway
- advise ready when awaiting departure clearance

**Important:** Hold Position and Maintain Position are both absolute stop instructions. There is no middle ground — the aircraft must be completely stationary until cleared.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC issues Hold Position to:

- protect an active runway from traffic
- sequence vehicles or aircraft on the movement area
- allow a landing or departing aircraft to clear
- coordinate runway crossings at towered airports
- manage high-traffic ground movement

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are taxiing toward the runway at Florianópolis in your H130.

An aircraft is backtracking on the runway.

Tower transmits:

ANAC123, hold position, traffic backtracking on runway.

You stop immediately and readback:

Hold position, ANAC123.

The backtracking aircraft clears the runway and Tower clears you to line up and wait.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, hold position, aircraft backtracking on runway.
2. Tower: ANAC123, hold position, runway not clear.
3. Tower: ANAC123, hold position, expect two minutes.
4. Tower: ANAC123, hold position short of runway two seven.
5. Tower: ANAC123, hold position, vehicle on the runway.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between hold position and maintain position?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Both instructions mean the aircraft must stop completely and not move. Hold position and maintain position are equivalent ground instructions. When I receive either, I stop immediately, readback the instruction with my callsign, and remain stationary until ATC provides the next clearance. The practical procedures are identical.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Confusing hold position with continue slowly.  
  ✔ Hold position means full stop — not reduced speed.

- ❌ Reading back only "tá bom" or callsign without repeating the instruction.  
  ✔ Always readback: hold position, ANAC123.

- ❌ Moving again without receiving an explicit clearance to proceed.  
  ✔ Wait for ATC to issue line up and wait, cleared for takeoff, or continue taxi.

- ❌ Not clarifying the exact hold point when the instruction is ambiguous.  
  ✔ If unclear, confirm: ANAC123, holding position short of runway two seven, confirm.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are holding short of the active runway at Florianópolis in your H130. Tower says: ANAC123, hold position, vehicle on the runway.

**Your first task**

What is your exact response?

**Your second task**

After ninety seconds, Tower has not called you. What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

My response would be: Hold position, ANAC123. I would stop completely and remain stationary. After ninety seconds without a follow-up clearance, I would transmit: Florianópolis Tower, ANAC123, holding position, vehicle clear, request clearance to proceed. I would not move until receiving an explicit clearance.
