# Maximum Landing Weight

**Catalog ID:** 0388  
**Category:** Performance  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Maximum Landing Weight (MLW) — the highest weight at which the aircraft is certified or performance-allowed to land, limited by structure, landing distance, or both.

MLW protects the airframe and the landing roll. Landing above it risks structural exceedance and insufficient stopping or landing performance.

For H130 Brazilian operations — short returns, fuel still high after early diversion, and short runways — you may need to burn or dump plan fuel, hold to reduce weight, or divert to a longer field. Discussing that with ATC requires precise ICAO English for Part 2 and Part 4.

## 📖 Meaning

Maximum Landing Weight is the maximum weight approved for landing, which may be the structural certified maximum or a lower weight limited by landing distance and conditions at the destination or alternate.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Peso máximo de pouso / Maximum Landing Weight (MLW).

Peso máximo aprovado para pouso, que pode ser o máximo estrutural certificado ou um peso menor limitado pela distância de pouso e pelas condições no destino ou alternado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When managing MLW, the crew should:

- predict landing weight from takeoff weight minus expected burn
- compare predicted landing weight to structural and performance MLW
- plan hold, delay, or diversion if landing would exceed MLW
- advise ATC if unable to land due to overweight
- recalculate after early return when little fuel has been burned

MLW management includes:

- structural MLW vs landing-distance-limited weight
- wet runway reducing allowable landing weight
- early return with high fuel remaining
- alternate selection with longer landing distance
- company procedures for overweight landing if ever authorized

**Important:** An immediate return after takeoff often means landing weight near takeoff weight. Check MLW before accepting a quick return. Request hold to burn fuel if required and safe.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers handle MLW situations when:

- aircraft request hold to reduce weight before landing
- crews report unable to land overweight
- diversion is requested for a longer runway
- early returns need sequencing for fuel burn
- overweight landing declarations require emergency services

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Minutes after departure from Congonhas, you have a non-critical system issue and elect to return. Fuel burn has been minimal. Predicted landing weight exceeds MLW.

You call:

São Paulo Approach, ANAC123, request hold to reduce landing weight, currently above maximum landing weight.

Approach: ANAC123, roger, hold as published, advise when ready for approach.

Later:

ANAC123, ready for approach, landing weight within limits.

You solved weight with a hold — not an overweight landing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Maximum landing weight is checked before you commit to land — especially on early returns.

Takeoff weight minus burn equals landing weight. If that number is above MLW, you do not land yet. Hold, burn, or divert to a suitable field.

In the H130, structural MLW and performance-limited landing weight can both matter on short wet Brazilian runways. Use the more restrictive.

If you must declare an overweight landing in a true emergency — say so clearly so ATC alerts services. That is not a normal technique for a non-critical return.

Plan the return. Speak the weight problem early. Land within limits.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You depart and must return immediately. Landing weight would exceed MLW. What do you request from ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would request hold to reduce landing weight: ANAC123, request hold, currently above maximum landing weight, will advise when ready for approach. If holding is not possible or the emergency requires immediate landing, I would declare the situation and overweight landing as required. For a non-critical return, I burn to MLW first. Clear communication lets ATC plan the hold or the emergency response.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Returning immediately without checking landing weight vs MLW.  
  ✔ Calculate landing weight; hold or divert if above MLW.

- ❌ Not telling ATC why you need to hold after a return.  
  ✔ State: hold to reduce landing weight / above MLW.

- ❌ Using only structural MLW on a short wet runway.  
  ✔ Apply performance-limited landing weight when more restrictive.

- ❌ Treating overweight landing as routine.  
  ✔ Overweight landing is exceptional; plan to avoid it.
