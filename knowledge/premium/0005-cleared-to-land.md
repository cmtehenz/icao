# Cleared to Land

**Catalog ID:** 0005  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## Mission Brief

Landing is one of the busiest phases of flight, and one of the easiest moments to make assumptions.

Today you'll learn an essential rule:

Seeing the runway does NOT mean you're authorized to land.

At a controlled airport, the landing is only authorized when Tower says:

"Cleared to Land."

These three words transfer the runway to you.

## Meaning

ATC authorizes the aircraft to land on the specified runway.

## Portuguese Meaning

Autorizado para pouso.

## Operational Meaning

A Cleared to Land clearance authorizes the pilot to complete the landing on the assigned runway.

Until this clearance is received, the pilot must continue the approach and remain prepared to:

- continue the approach,
- extend downwind,
- go around,
- or execute a missed approach if necessary.

Even if the runway appears empty, the aircraft is not authorized to land without the appropriate clearance at a controlled airport.

## Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Cleared to Land to:

- authorize runway occupancy
- ensure runway separation
- protect departing aircraft
- coordinate runway crossings
- maintain airport safety
- sequence arriving traffic

## Real Operational Context

You're approaching Navegantes Airport in your H130.

Tower instructs:

"PT-ABC, continue approach."

You continue descending.

The runway is clearly visible.

Everything looks perfect.

However, an aircraft is still backtracking on the runway.

Only after the runway becomes available does Tower transmit:

"PT-ABC, runway zero seven, cleared to land."

Now the runway is officially yours.

You continue the landing.

## Real ATC Phraseology

1. PT-ABC, runway zero seven, cleared to land.
2. PT-ABC, wind zero eight zero at seven knots, runway one eight, cleared to land.
3. PT-ABC, number one, runway two seven, cleared to land.
4. PT-ABC, runway zero seven, cleared to land, caution wake turbulence.
5. PT-ABC, cleared to land, traffic vacating the runway.

## Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Runway zero seven, cleared to land, PT-ABC.
2. Cleared to land runway one eight, PT-ABC.
3. Number one, cleared to land runway two seven, PT-ABC.
4. Cleared to land, caution wake turbulence acknowledged, PT-ABC.
5. Cleared to land, PT-ABC.

## Captain Delta Teaching

Gustavo, imagine you're arriving at a hotel.

You can see your room.

Your luggage is ready.

The door is right in front of you.

But you still don't enter until the receptionist hands you the key.

Cleared to Land is the key.

The runway may look empty from your cockpit, but ATC has a complete picture of the airport.

There may be a vehicle crossing.

Another aircraft may still be vacating.

Or a departure may be rolling.

Professional pilots never land because they think it's safe.

They land because the controller has transferred the runway to them.

## Common ICAO Speaking Question

What should a pilot do if the runway is in sight but landing clearance has not been received?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

The pilot should continue the approach while monitoring the radio and be prepared to follow ATC instructions. If landing clearance is not received in time, or if the approach becomes unsafe, the pilot should perform a go-around in accordance with ATC instructions or standard procedures.

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Confusing Continue Approach with Cleared to Land.  
  They are completely different clearances.

- ❌ Assuming an empty runway means landing is authorized.  
  Only ATC can issue landing clearance at a controlled airport.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Cleared to land runway zero seven, PT-ABC.

- ❌ Forgetting the runway number.  
  Whenever practical, include the runway in the readback.

## Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Cleared to Land

**Pronunciation:** kleerd tə LAND

**Word Stress**

- Cleared → clear and positive
- Land → strongest word in the phrase

Practice:

Cleared... To land... Cleared to land.

Now inside a complete transmission:

PT-ABC, runway zero seven, cleared to land.

Speak smoothly, avoiding long pauses between the words.

Cleared to Land.
