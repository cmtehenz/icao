# Fire Suppression

**Catalog ID:** 0351  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Fire Suppression — the actions and systems used to extinguish or control fire on board, including fire agent discharge, isolation of fuel and electrical sources, and coordination with airport fire services after landing.

Fire suppression is not only a checklist step. It is a communication event: ATC and rescue need to know whether the fire is out, continuing, or under control with agent discharged.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, fire suppression may involve engine fire bottles, cabin extinguishers, or ground fire services meeting the aircraft. Cabin volume is small; smoke fills fast. English must state fire status and suppression actions clearly.

Understanding fire suppression language in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Fire Suppression is the set of crew and system actions used to extinguish or control an aircraft fire — typically shutting off fuel and electrical sources to the affected area and discharging fire extinguishing agent — followed by reports to ATC on fire status and requests for fire services as required.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Supressão de fogo / Fire Suppression.

Conjunto de ações da tripulação e dos sistemas usados para extinguir ou controlar um fogo na aeronave — tipicamente cortando combustível e fontes elétricas da área afetada e descarregando o agente extintor — seguido de informes ao ATC sobre o status do fogo e solicitações de serviços de combate a incêndio conforme necessário.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When fire suppression is required, the crew should:

- fly the aircraft and complete fire memory items and checklist
- isolate fuel and electrical sources to the affected zone as published
- discharge fire agent as required by the AFM or QRH
- declare Mayday for fire and report suppression status to ATC
- request fire services for landing if fire continues or smoke remains
- advise if fire is extinguished, under control, or still burning

Fire suppression reporting commonly includes:

- fire agent discharged / fire extinguished
- fire continuing / smoke in cabin
- request fire services on landing
- evacuating / remaining on board after landing

**Important:** Say what the fire is doing after suppression actions — extinguished, under control, or continuing. Rescue response depends on that update, not only on the first Mayday.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use fire suppression reports to:

- alert and update airport fire and rescue services
- decide runway or helipad clearance and traffic hold
- prepare for possible evacuation after landing
- keep other aircraft clear of smoke and emergency vehicles
- coordinate the shortest safe path to land with equipment standing by

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Climbing out of Congonhas in the H130, an engine fire warning appears. Memory items complete — engine secured, fire agent discharged. Smoke indications decrease.

You declare Mayday for engine fire, then update that fire agent is discharged and fire appears extinguished. Approach clears priority landing and rolls fire services anyway.

On short final you confirm: fire extinguished, request fire services standing by for inspection. You land. Rescue meets the aircraft. No evacuation required.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Departure: ANAC123, roger Mayday engine fire, say if fire agent discharged.
2. Approach: ANAC123, priority landing runway one seven, fire services rolling.
3. Approach: ANAC123, confirm fire extinguished or continuing.
4. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land runway one seven, equipment standing by.
5. Tower: ANAC123, after landing advise if evacuating or remaining on board.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Mayday Mayday Mayday, Departure, ANAC123, engine fire, fire agent discharged, returning immediately.
2. ANAC123, fire agent discharged, fire appears extinguished, request priority landing and fire services.
3. ANAC123, fire continuing, smoke in cabin, request immediate landing, fire services.
4. ANAC123, cleared to land runway one seven, fire extinguished, will advise if evacuating.
5. ANAC123, on ground, fire suppressed, remaining on board, request fire services inspection.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Fire suppression has two languages: checklist language and radio language.

Checklist: isolate, discharge, confirm indications.

Radio: fire, agent discharged, extinguished or continuing, fire services.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say only "apagamos o fogo" without standard English. Prefer fire extinguished or fire agent discharged, fire continuing.

In the H130, one bottle and limited cabin volume mean updates matter. If smoke clears, say so. If it returns, say so immediately.

Do not wait for a perfect diagnosis before the first Mayday. Suppress, then update status in short transmissions.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You discharge the fire agent and the fire warning goes out. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare Mayday for the fire if not already done, then report that fire agent is discharged and the fire appears extinguished. I would still request priority landing and fire services for inspection, and I would update immediately if smoke or fire indications return.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "apagamos o fogo" without standard terms.  
  ✔ Use fire extinguished or fire agent discharged.

- ❌ Not updating ATC after the first Mayday.  
  ✔ Report suppression status: extinguished, under control, or continuing.

- ❌ Declining fire services because the warning light went out.  
  ✔ Request fire services for inspection after any onboard fire.

- ❌ Long explanations of checklist steps on frequency.  
  ✔ Short status: agent discharged, fire extinguished, request landing.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**SUPPRESS** — **S**ecure the aircraft, **U**se checklist and agent, **P**ass Mayday early, **P**hrase status clearly, **R**equest fire services, **E**valuate evacuate or remain, **S**ay if continuing, **S**tay short on the radio.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

After takeoff from Congonhas, engine fire warning. You discharge the agent. Warning goes out. Smoke smell remains.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit about suppression status?

**Your second task**

On landing, Tower asks if you are evacuating. Fire is out. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, engine fire, fire agent discharged, fire appears extinguished, smoke smell remaining, request priority landing and fire services. Second task: ANAC123, fire extinguished, remaining on board, request fire services inspection. I would evacuate only if smoke or fire returns.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Fire suppression is action plus status.

Isolate. Discharge. Confirm. Report.

Say extinguished or continuing — never leave ATC guessing.

In the H130, small cabin and single-engine urgency make short English critical.

Train the words before you need them under smoke and stress.

Suppress the fire. Speak the status. Land with rescue ready.
