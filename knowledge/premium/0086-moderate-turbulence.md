# Moderate Turbulence

**Catalog ID:** 0086  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers moderate turbulence — definite strain on the aircraft and occupants, but still controllable.

Moderate Turbulence causes changes in altitude and attitude, with loose objects moving about and occupants feeling definite strain against seat belts.

In the H130, moderate turbulence is common near coastal wind gradients, convective clouds, and mountain ridges in Brazil.

You must report intensity correctly, request altitude or route change if needed, and maintain smooth control inputs.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 operational communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Moderate Turbulence is turbulence that causes definite strain on occupants and changes in altitude or attitude, while the aircraft remains under control.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Turbulência moderada.

Turbulência que causa esforço definido nos ocupantes e mudanças de altitude ou atitude, mantendo a aeronave sob controle.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Moderate Turbulence occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control with smooth inputs
- reduce to recommended turbulence speed if published
- report moderate turbulence to ATC with altitude and location
- request climb, descent, or weather deviation if conditions worsen
- secure loose objects and brief passengers if applicable

Moderate Turbulence reporting commonly includes:

- moderate turbulence at altitude
- request climb or descent
- request weather deviation
- ride improving or ride deteriorating

**Important:** Use the correct intensity word — moderate is not severe. Accurate PIREPs protect other aircraft.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Moderate Turbulence reports to:

- warn other aircraft with PIREPs
- offer altitude or route changes
- coordinate weather deviations
- update expected ride conditions on the route
- prepare for possible escalation to severe turbulence reports

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at three thousand feet toward Florianópolis in your H130.

The aircraft encounters definite bumps and altitude changes near coastal convective clouds.

You transmit:

Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, moderate turbulence at three thousand feet, request climb to five thousand.

Approach clears the climb.

Ride improves and you continue to destination.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger moderate turbulence, climb and maintain five thousand.
2. Approach: ANAC123, previous aircraft reported moderate turbulence at three thousand, advise.
3. Center: ANAC123, deviation approved, report ride conditions.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say if turbulence is continuous or occasional.
5. Approach: ANAC123, thank you for the PIREP.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, moderate turbulence at three thousand, request climb.
2. ANAC123, moderate turbulence, request weather deviation left of course.
3. ANAC123, moderate turbulence occasional, ride improving at five thousand.
4. Approach, ANAC123, moderate turbulence continuous, request descent.
5. ANAC123, clear of moderate turbulence, light turbulence now.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Turbulence intensity has a language.

Light. Moderate. Severe. Extreme.

Moderate means definite strain — not violent loss of control.

The sequence:

Fly attitude. Report intensity. Request change if needed.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "turbulência média" or "sacolejo" on frequency.

Use moderate turbulence — ICAO standard intensity term.

In the H130, report altitude and whether turbulence is occasional or continuous.

Train accurate PIREPs — other pilots depend on your words.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is moderate turbulence and how would you report it to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Moderate turbulence causes definite strain on occupants and changes in altitude or attitude, while the aircraft remains controllable. I would report moderate turbulence with my altitude and position, state whether it is occasional or continuous, and request climb, descent, or weather deviation if needed.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "turbulência média" instead of the standard intensity.  
  ✔ Use moderate turbulence.

- ❌ Calling every bump "severe."  
  ✔ Reserve severe for large abrupt changes and difficult control.

- ❌ Not stating altitude with the PIREP.  
  ✔ Always include altitude and approximate location.

- ❌ Not updating ATC when the ride improves.  
  ✔ Report ride improving or clear of moderate turbulence.
