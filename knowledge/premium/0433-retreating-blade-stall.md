# Retreating Blade Stall

**Catalog ID:** 0433  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Retreating Blade Stall — a high-speed aerodynamic limit where the retreating blade exceeds its critical angle of attack, causing roll, pitch, and vibration that demand immediate recovery.

Retreating blade stall awareness is critical for H130 crews at high forward speeds, high density altitude, and high gross weight in Brazilian operations.

You must describe recognition, recovery, and related ATC communication in ICAO English for Parts 2 and 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Retreating Blade Stall is a helicopter flight condition in which the retreating main rotor blade reaches a critical angle of attack due to high forward speed, high blade loading, or adverse conditions, producing stall on that side of the disc, typically with roll toward the retreating side, nose pitch-up, and vibration.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Estol da pá que recua / retreating blade stall.

Condição de voo de helicóptero em que a pá do rotor principal que recua atinge o ângulo de ataque crítico devido a alta velocidade de avanço, alta carga na pá ou condições adversas, produzindo estol desse lado do disco, tipicamente com rolagem para o lado que recua, arfagem de nariz para cima e vibração.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When recognizing or recovering from retreating blade stall, the pilot should:

- reduce collective to decrease blade angle of attack
- reduce airspeed — do not pull aft cyclic aggressively into the stall
- maintain controlled attitude and allow recovery
- avoid the combination of high speed, high weight, high density altitude, and turbulence
- divert or land if vibration or damage is suspected after the event

Retreating blade stall reporting commonly includes:

- reducing speed, encountering vibration, request altitude change
- diverting, possible rotor issue, request priority
- declaring emergency if control or structural concern continues
- requesting smooth altitude / heading for recovery

**Important:** Recovery is collective down and speed down — not a sharp aft cyclic pull that can worsen the stall. Know Vne and environmental factors. Respect the envelope.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers assist after retreating blade stall events by:

- approving speed or altitude changes without delay
- providing priority handling and diversion routing
- coordinating emergency services on landing
- keeping traffic clear during an unstable recovery
- obtaining souls and fuel when an emergency is declared

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are in cruise in your H130 at high density altitude, near the upper speed range, with a gusty headwind.

Vibration increases. The nose wants up. A roll develops toward the retreating side.

You reduce collective and airspeed, recover, and transmit:

Reducing speed, encountering vibration, request lower altitude / deviation, will advise.

You do not chase the nose with aggressive aft cyclic. You stay within the recovered envelope for the rest of the flight or divert for inspection if required.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger reducing speed, descend and maintain four thousand, report conditions.
2. Control: ANAC123, deviation approved, say intentions.
3. Control: ANAC123, roger priority, cleared direct alternate, report souls and fuel when able.
4. Control: ANAC123, squawk seven seven zero zero if declaring.
5. Approach: ANAC123, emergency services standing by, cleared straight-in.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Retreating blade stall is the high-speed wall.

Vibration. Pitch up. Roll to the retreating side. Collective down. Speed down. Recover.

In the H130, respect Vne, weight, and density altitude — especially on hot Brazilian days and in turbulence. Part 3 loves the recovery: reduce collective, reduce airspeed, maintain control, then communicate.

Do not pull into a secondary problem. Do not ignore post-event vibration — land and inspect if anything feels wrong.

The envelope is written in blood. Fly inside it.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You recognize retreating blade stall symptoms in cruise. What do you do and say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would reduce collective and reduce airspeed to recover from the stall, avoiding aggressive aft cyclic. After recovery, I would transmit: reducing speed, encountering vibration, request lower altitude or deviation. If vibration continues or control is in doubt, I would declare an emergency and request priority landing for inspection.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Pulling aft cyclic hard when the nose pitches up.  
  ✔ Reduce collective and speed first.

- ❌ Ignoring high density altitude when pushing cruise speed.  
  ✔ Lower Vne margins when hot, high, and heavy.

- ❌ Saying nothing after a significant stall event.  
  ✔ Advise ATC; divert if structural concern exists.

- ❌ Re-accelerating immediately to the same speed.  
  ✔ Stay well below the onset speed; evaluate the aircraft.
