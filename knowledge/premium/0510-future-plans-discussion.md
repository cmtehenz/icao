# Future Plans Discussion

**Catalog ID:** 0510  
**Category:** ICAO Speaking  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Future Plans Discussion — the ICAO oral exam task in which you talk about upcoming flying plans, career goals, or intended actions using future forms at Level 4 or above.

Future plans discussion is not vague wishing. It is clear intention language with reasons and conditions.

In Part 1, the examiner may ask about your next rating, a planned route, training goals, or what you will do after the test. You must use will / going to / plan to appropriately, give reasons, and handle follow-ups about timing and obstacles.

For H130 ICAO Delta candidates, this Medium-priority task still matters: it rounds out discourse range and often appears as a closing Part 1 exchange when the examiner shifts from technical topics to personal professional plans.

Understanding how to discuss future plans in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 1 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Future Plans Discussion is the ICAO speaking task type in which the candidate describes intended future actions related to aviation — training, operations, career, or flight planning — using appropriate future forms and supporting reasons, so that temporal language, coherence, and interactive skill can be assessed at Operational Level 4 or higher.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When discussing future plans, the candidate should:

- state the plan clearly: I am going to... / I plan to... / I will...
- add when if known: next month, this year, after the checkride
- give a reason for the plan
- mention conditions or obstacles honestly
- answer follow-ups about alternatives if the plan changes
- for H130: connect plans to helicopter operations, ratings, or mission types when relevant
- keep the tone professional — career and training, not private life detail

A Level 4 future-plans answer typically includes:

- intention
- timeframe
- reason
- preparation already started or next step
- brief contingency if asked

**Important:** "I don't know" alone is weak. If unsure, give a provisional plan: "I am considering an instrument rating next year because..." Uncertainty with structure still scores.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Future-plans discussion helps examiners to:

- elicit future forms in natural interaction
- close Part 1 with lower-pressure professional topics
- test ability to explain intentions and conditions
- extend conversation with why / when / what if follow-ups
- assess fluency when the topic is personal-professional rather than emergency

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Examiner: What are your plans after this language test?

You answer: I plan to complete my ICAO Level 4 documentation and continue H130 IFR line flying. Next month I am going to attend recurrent training. I want to improve my radiotelephony confidence in busy terminal airspace because that is where I still feel the most pressure. If I have time this year, I will also study for an additional rating.

Clear intention. Timeframe. Reason. Optional extra. The examiner asks one more why — you are ready.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Examiner: ANAC123, what are your plans for the next training season?
2. Approach: ANAC123, advise your planned route after departure.
3. Radar: ANAC123, confirm you will continue as filed or request change.
4. Examiner: ANAC123, what will you do if the weather delays your flight tomorrow?
5. Tower: ANAC123, report your intentions after landing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Future plans need intention plus reason.

"I will do training" is thin. "I am going to do recurrent training next month because my checkride window opens" is Level 4.

Choose forms with purpose: going to for planned decisions, will for decisions made in the moment or offers, plan to for formal intentions. Perfect accuracy is less important than clear meaning.

When the examiner asks "what if that fails?", shift briefly into hypothetical language, then return to the main plan. Interaction and future discussion overlap.

Keep it aviation-professional. Long personal stories waste time and dilute vocabulary demonstration.

In H130 careers, plans often include type ratings, EMS missions, offshore work, or instructor paths. Pick real goals and practice saying them with timeframe and reason.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What are your professional plans for the next two years as a helicopter pilot?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Over the next two years I plan to continue flying the H130 on IFR and EMS-type missions. I am going to complete recurrent training on schedule and improve my ICAO English to stay comfortably above Level 4. I also want to gain more experience in confined-area and hospital helipad operations because those missions require strong decision-making. If opportunities appear, I will consider an instructor path later, but my first priority is line proficiency and safe operations.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Using only present tense for future meaning without markers.  
  ✔ Use will / going to / plan to with a timeframe.

- ❌ Saying "I don't know" with no provisional plan.  
  ✔ Offer a considered option and a reason.

- ❌ Giving personal life details instead of professional plans.  
  ✔ Keep the focus on training, flying, and ratings.

- ❌ Stating a plan with no reason when asked why.  
  ✔ Always support intentions with because + operational reason.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** I Plan to Continue IFR Line Flying

**Pronunciation:** eye PLAN too kun-TIN-yoo EYE-eff-AR LYN FLY-ing

**Word Stress**

- Plan → PLAN
- Continue → kun-TIN-yoo
- Line → LYN
- Flying → FLY-ing

Practice:

I plan to continue... IFR line flying.

Together:

I plan to continue IFR line flying and complete recurrent training next month.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**FUTURE** — **F**orm the intention clearly, **U**tter a timeframe, **T**ie a reason with because, **U**pdate with next steps, **R**espond to what-if briefly, **E**nd on a professional aviation goal.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

The examiner asks about your plans to improve radiotelephony in Brazilian terminal airspace.

**Your first task**

Give a Level 4 future-plans answer with intention, timeframe, and reason.

**Your second task**

The examiner asks what you will do if progress is slow. Answer, then give one example radio intention you want to sound automatic.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: I am going to practice Part 2 role play every week this month because busy terminal frequencies are where I lose fluency. I plan to record myself reading back clearances and correct incomplete readbacks. Second task: If progress is slow, I will add shorter daily drills and focus on standard phraseology first. Example automatic call: ANAC123, requesting vectors for ILS runway one four. Plans with practice beat plans with hope.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Future plans discussion is intention with structure.

Say what you will do. Say when. Say why. Add the next step.

Handle what-if without abandoning the main plan.

You already schedule training and missions in the H130 world. Put those plans into clear exam English.

That is how a Medium-priority closing task still demonstrates solid Level 4 discourse.
