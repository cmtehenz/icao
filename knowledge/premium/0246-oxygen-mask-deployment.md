# Oxygen Mask Deployment

**Catalog ID:** 0246  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Oxygen Mask Deployment — the automatic or manual release of passenger and crew oxygen masks when cabin altitude exceeds a safe threshold, typically after pressurization failure or rapid decompression on pressurized aircraft. The H130 is not a high-altitude pressurized airliner; this lesson still teaches ICAO exam English and maps the same urgency logic to cabin altitude awareness, hypoxia risk, and clear ATC communication during any oxygen-related emergency.

Oxygen mask deployment is a classic ICAO speaking and radiotelephony topic. Examiners expect you to name the event, state crew actions, and declare the appropriate urgency level with intentions for descent and landing.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master the standard phraseology, then transfer the structure to any situation where oxygen, cabin altitude, or hypoxia becomes a factor — including engine anomalies that affect environmental systems.

Understanding how to discuss oxygen mask deployment in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Oxygen Mask Deployment is the automatic or crew-initiated release of oxygen masks in the cabin and/or flight deck when cabin altitude rises above a safe limit, requiring immediate use of oxygen, emergency descent if applicable, checklist completion, and clear notification to ATC of the nature of the emergency and intentions.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Acionamento / liberação das máscaras de oxigênio / Oxygen Mask Deployment.

Liberação automática ou iniciada pela tripulação das máscaras de oxigênio na cabine e/ou no cockpit quando a altitude de cabine sobe acima de um limite seguro, exigindo uso imediato de oxigênio, descida de emergência se aplicável, cumprimento de checklist e notificação clara ao ATC sobre a natureza da emergência e as intenções.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When oxygen masks deploy, the crew should:

- don crew oxygen masks immediately and establish crew communication
- confirm cabin altitude and pressurization status
- initiate emergency descent if cabin altitude is uncontrolled (pressurized aircraft)
- declare Mayday or Pan Pan based on severity and control of the situation
- report nature (oxygen masks deployed / cabin altitude high), intentions, and request priority
- for H130 training: practice the radio structure for hypoxia or cabin environmental anomalies even without airline-style mask drop

Oxygen mask deployment reporting commonly includes:

- oxygen masks deployed / passenger oxygen deployed
- cabin altitude high / pressurization problem
- commencing emergency descent
- request priority vectors / diversion
- souls on board and fuel remaining when asked

**Important:** On the ICAO speaking test, use "oxygen masks deployed" correctly. In H130 line flying, you may not see automatic passenger mask drop — retain exam vocabulary and transfer the urgency logic to any oxygen or cabin-altitude related event.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to oxygen mask deployment reports to:

- recognize a pressurization or cabin-altitude emergency
- clear airspace below for emergency descent
- provide priority handling and shortest routing to a suitable airport
- obtain souls and fuel for emergency services coordination
- alert fire and rescue and medical support for possible hypoxia-related issues on arrival

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Exam-style scenario: you are in cruise when the cabin altitude warning activates and passenger oxygen masks deploy.

You don crew masks, establish communication, and begin emergency descent procedures while transmitting:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Control, ANAC123, oxygen masks deployed, cabin altitude high, commencing emergency descent, request priority vectors to nearest suitable airport.

ATC clears the altitude block below and vectors you for landing. You update when cabin altitude is under control and when you are established on approach.

For H130 reality, replace automatic mask deployment with cabin altitude or hypoxia awareness — keep the urgency and radio structure identical.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger Mayday oxygen masks deployed, descend immediately flight level one zero zero, say intentions.
2. Control: ANAC123, emergency descent approved, traffic cleared below, report leaving each flight level.
3. Approach: ANAC123, priority handling approved, expect straight-in runway one five.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.
5. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services standing by, advise if medical assistance required.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

3. ANAC123, priority vectors accepted, runway one five.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Oxygen mask deployment is exam vocabulary with real airline blood behind it — cabin altitude kills quietly if you delay.

Your first action is oxygen for the crew. Then communicate. Then descend if the cabin is not controlled. The radio must name the event: oxygen masks deployed. Not "we have a problem with the air."

ATC needs three things: what happened, what you are doing, what you need. Masks deployed. Emergency descent. Priority to nearest suitable airport.

Do not use vague language. Do not delay the Mayday while you finish a long checklist discussion. Don masks, fly the aircraft, declare, descend.

Brazilian examiners may ask oxygen mask deployment even in a helicopter oral. Answer with standard ICAO phrasing, then show you understand applicability: H130 crews still manage hypoxia risk, cabin environment, and clear emergency English.

Name it. Oxygen on. Descend if required. Land with priority.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Passenger oxygen masks have deployed in cruise. What are your immediate actions, and what do you say to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would don the crew oxygen mask immediately, establish crew communication, and check cabin altitude and pressurization. If cabin altitude were high and uncontrolled, I would commence an emergency descent and declare Mayday, stating that oxygen masks have deployed and requesting priority vectors to the nearest suitable airport. I would provide souls and fuel when asked and update ATC when cabin altitude is under control. On a helicopter without automatic passenger mask deployment, I would use the same structure for any hypoxia or cabin-altitude related emergency.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "the masks fell" or "máscaras caíram" instead of "oxygen masks deployed."  
  ✔ Use ICAO-standard "oxygen masks deployed" in exams and international phraseology.

- ❌ Declaring the emergency only after completing the full checklist.  
  ✔ Don oxygen, fly the aircraft, declare early, then continue procedures.

- ❌ Not stating that emergency descent has commenced.  
  ✔ Report commencing emergency descent so ATC can clear traffic below.

- ❌ Memorizing mask deployment without linking to hypoxia and cabin altitude.  
  ✔ Keep exam term + operational transfer ready for H130 and pressurized types.
