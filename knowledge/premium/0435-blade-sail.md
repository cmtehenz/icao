# Blade Sail

**Catalog ID:** 0435  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Blade Sail — excessive flapping or sailing of rotor blades at low RPM, typically during start or shutdown in high wind, which can lead to blade strikes or control difficulty.

Blade sail awareness matters for H130 crews on windy Brazilian coastal pads, offshore decks, and open heliports during rotor engagement and shutdown.

You must describe wind limits, start/shutdown discipline, and related calls in ICAO English for Parts 2 and 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Blade Sail is a condition in which rotor blades flap or sail excessively at low rotor RPM, usually during start or shutdown in strong or gusty wind, potentially causing blade contact with the fuselage or ground or placing abnormal loads on the rotor system.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Vela de pá / blade sail.

Condição em que as pás do rotor batem ou "velejam" excessivamente em baixo RPM do rotor, geralmente durante a partida ou o corte em vento forte ou rajado, podendo causar contato da pá com a fuselagem ou o solo ou impor cargas anormais ao sistema de rotor.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When blade sail risk exists, the pilot should:

- respect AFM and company wind limits for start and shutdown
- position the helicopter into wind when practical
- use correct start and shutdown RPM schedules without rushing
- stop the start or secure the aircraft if blades sail excessively
- delay rotor engagement or shutdown when gusts exceed limits

Blade sail related reporting commonly includes:

- delaying start, wind above limits
- shutting down into wind, request time on pad
- aborting start, blade sail, remaining on pad
- request tow or hangar due to wind

**Important:** Low RPM plus high wind is the danger zone. Do not start or shut down casually in a gale. Into-wind orientation and patience prevent most blade sail events.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers help with blade sail risk by:

- allowing helicopters to hold on the pad until wind eases
- sequencing starts when gusts are reported
- coordinating hangar or sheltered parking
- understanding delayed departure or extended pad occupancy
- assisting after a blade strike emergency

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on a coastal helipad in your H130. Wind is gusting above start limits.

You delay rotor engagement and advise:

Delaying start, wind above limits, remaining on pad, will advise when ready.

When wind decreases and you are into wind, you start normally. During shutdown later, you again orient into wind and follow the RPM schedule carefully.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger delaying start, advise when ready for departure.
2. Tower: ANAC123, wind three six zero at two five gusting three two, say intentions.
3. Tower: ANAC123, pad time approved, report shutting down.
4. Tower: ANAC123, roger aborting start, remain on frequency.
5. Ground: ANAC123, tow available to hangar if required.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Blade sail is a low-RPM wind problem.

The blades are soft at low speed. Gusts push them. Strikes happen in the start and shutdown window — not at flight RPM.

In the H130, know the wind limit. Face into wind. Do not rush the clutch or shutdown. Part 3 loves: wind above limits on the pad — what do you do? Answer: delay start or shut down into wind within limits; advise ATC delaying start, wind above limits.

Coastal Brazil and offshore decks will test this weekly. Patience is cheaper than a blade.

If gusts are rising during shutdown, pause and reassess before the blades enter the soft RPM range.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Wind is above start limits and gusty. What do you say and do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would not engage the rotors until wind is within AFM limits and the aircraft is oriented into wind as practical. I would transmit: delaying start, wind above limits, remaining on pad, will advise when ready. If a hangar or sheltered area is available, I might request tow. I would not force a start in out-of-limit gusts.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Starting in known out-of-limit wind to "save time."  
  ✔ Delay. Blade damage costs more than schedule.

- ❌ Shutting down crosswind without need.  
  ✔ Orient into wind when possible.

- ❌ Not telling ATC why you are occupying the pad.  
  ✔ Advise delaying start, wind above limits.

- ❌ Rushing RPM through the sail-prone range.  
  ✔ Follow the AFM schedule.

Blade sail rewards patience.
