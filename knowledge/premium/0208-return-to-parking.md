# Return to Parking

**Catalog ID:** 0208  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Return to Parking — the instruction or pilot request to leave the active movement area and go back to the assigned stand, ramp, or helipad parking position.

Return to parking happens when a departure is cancelled, a technical issue appears after startup, weather closes the departure, or ATC needs the aircraft off the taxiway. It is a controlled reverse of the outbound taxi plan.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, return to parking is highly relevant. Training sorties are cancelled for weather, student limits, or maintenance write-ups discovered after start. The H130 may hover taxi or air taxi back to the academy stand. Clear radio English keeps Ground informed and protects other traffic.

Understanding how to request, acknowledge, and execute return to parking in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency and safe Brazilian apron operations.

## 📖 Meaning

Return to parking is an ATC instruction or pilot request for an aircraft to proceed from the taxiway, holding point, or departure area back to its assigned parking stand, ramp, or helipad.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Retornar ao estacionamento / return to parking.

Instrução do controle ou solicitação do piloto para a aeronave deixar a área de manobras ou ponto de espera e voltar à posição de estacionamento, rampa ou heliponto designado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When returning to parking, the crew should:

- acknowledge the instruction or clearly request return to parking with a reason if required
- read back the route, stand number, and any hold-short restrictions
- shut down departure intentions mentally — you are no longer in the departure sequence
- taxi or hover taxi only on the cleared route back to the stand
- report reaching parking or blocks on as required
- advise Ground if unable to return via the assigned route

Return to parking commonly occurs when:

- departure is cancelled by ATC or the operator
- a technical abnormality appears after startup
- weather or visibility drops below departure minima
- the crew aborts the lesson for training or fatigue reasons
- ATC needs the holding point cleared for other traffic

**Important:** Returning to parking is still movement on the aerodrome. You need a clearance, a route, and correct readbacks. Do not turn around on a taxiway without authorization. In the H130, plan rotor wash and downwash near parked aircraft when re-entering the ramp.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use return to parking to:

- remove cancelled departures from the active sequence
- free holding points and taxiways for other aircraft
- reorganize flow when weather or runway changes occur
- keep the movement area predictable when plans change
- coordinate apron capacity when multiple aircraft come back

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You have started the H130 and hover taxied to the holding point at Florianópolis for a training departure on runway three one.

The student reports an unusual vibration indication during hover checks. You decide to cancel the sortie and return.

You call Ground: request return to parking, training stand two, technical check required.

Ground clears you to hover taxi via Alpha back to stand two and to report arrival.

You read back the route, leave the holding point, and return carefully along Alpha. On the ramp you set down, complete shutdown, and report on blocks if required.

The departure slot is gone. The apron is safe. Maintenance can inspect. That is a professional return to parking — not an improvised U-turn.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, return to parking via Alpha, stand two.
2. Ground: ANAC123, cancel departure, taxi back to the ramp, parking as required.
3. Tower: ANAC123, vacate holding point, contact Ground for return to parking.
4. Ground: ANAC123, return to parking stand five via Bravo, give way to inbound helicopter.
5. Ground: ANAC123, after landing taxi to parking, stand three, report on blocks.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, return to parking via Alpha, stand two.
2. ANAC123, cancel departure, taxi back to the ramp.
3. ANAC123, vacating holding point, contacting Ground for return to parking.
4. ANAC123, return to parking stand five via Bravo, give way to inbound helicopter.
5. ANAC123, taxi to parking stand three, wilco, report on blocks.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Plans change. Parking is always available — if you ask correctly.

When you abort a departure, say so early. Ground needs time to re-sequence traffic behind you. Silence at the holding point blocks everyone.

In the H130 academy, returning to parking is normal training reality: weather, student overload, caution lights, fuel planning. Treat it as a standard procedure, not a failure.

Request return to parking with your stand if known. Read back the full route. Watch for opposite traffic that was expecting you to depart. Report when you are in and shut down.

Never assume you can reverse along the same path you used outbound without a new clearance. The surface picture changes every minute.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You are at the holding point after startup. You must cancel the flight for a technical reason. Explain what you say and how you return to the stand.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would advise Ground that I need to cancel departure for a technical reason and request return to parking, stating my stand if known. I would wait for a taxi or hover taxi clearance with a route, read it back completely, then proceed only on that clearance. On arrival at the stand I would shut down as required and report on blocks if asked. I would not turn around without clearance or re-enter the departure sequence.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Turning back on the taxiway without a new clearance.  
  ✔ Request return to parking and wait for route clearance.

- ❌ Saying only "going back" without stand or reason when useful.  
  ✔ Request return to parking, stand number, and brief reason if needed.

- ❌ Forgetting to read back hold-short instructions on the return route.  
  ✔ Read back every restriction — return routes still cross active areas.

- ❌ Remaining on Tower frequency after leaving the holding point.  
  ✔ Contact Ground when instructed for return to parking coordination.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Request Return to Parking, Stand Two

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, reh-KWEST ree-TURN too PAR-king, STAND TOO

**Word Stress**

- Request → reh-KWEST
- Return → ree-TURN (stress on second)
- Parking → PAR-king
- Stand → STAND

Practice:

Request return to parking... stand two.

Together:

ANAC123, request return to parking stand two, technical reason.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**PARK** — **P**ilot cancels or ATC instructs, **A**sk for return clearance, **R**ead back route and stand, **K**eep holding until cleared to move back.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are hover taxiing the H130 toward runway three one at Florianópolis. Weather deteriorates below your training minima. You decide to cancel.

**Your first task**

What do you say to Ground?

**Your second task**

Ground replies: "ANAC123, return to parking via Alpha, stand two, give way to helicopter on Bravo." What is your full readback?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, request return to parking stand two, weather below minima. Second task: ANAC123, return to parking via Alpha, stand two, give way to helicopter on Bravo. I would hold or sequence as required until the conflicting helicopter is clear, then continue to the stand.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Return to parking is professionalism when the plan dies.

Cancel early. Request clearly. Read back the route. Get home to the stand without creating a second problem on the taxiway.

In H130 training, this phrase will be part of your week — weather, maintenance, and instructional decisions all lead back to the ramp.

Leaving the holding point without clearance is not "being helpful." It is a surface conflict.

Ask. Clearance. Return. Shutdown.

That is how you close a cancelled sortie cleanly under DECEA and ICAO English standards.
