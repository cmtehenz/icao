# Settling With Power

**Catalog ID:** 0078  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers settling with power — closely related to vortex ring state.

Settling With Power means the helicopter is descending under power into conditions where the rotor cannot produce enough lift to arrest the descent.

In the H130, settling with power often appears during steep approaches, downwind approaches, or high-density altitude operations with low airspeed.

You must recognize the sink, recover with forward cyclic and airspeed, then communicate clearly.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Settling With Power is a condition in which a powered helicopter descends at a rate that the rotor system cannot arrest, often associated with vortex ring state.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Descida com potência (settling with power).

Condição em que o helicóptero com potência desce a uma taxa que o sistema de rotor não consegue interromper.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Settling With Power occurs, the pilot should:

- recognize increasing rate of descent despite available power
- apply forward cyclic to gain airspeed
- reduce collective if altitude permits during recovery
- establish a climb or go-around once clear of the condition
- declare Pan Pan or Mayday based on altitude remaining

Settling With Power reporting commonly includes:

- settling with power or vortex ring state
- recovering or going around
- request another approach
- souls on board when requested

**Important:** Settling with power and vortex ring state are often used interchangeably in operational speech. Recovery is the same — forward cyclic and airspeed. Aviate first.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Settling With Power declarations to:

- clear traffic from the go-around path
- provide vectors for another approach
- coordinate priority handling if altitude is critical
- request souls on board and intentions
- prepare emergency services if a hard landing is possible

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are approaching a mountain helipad near Curitiba in your H130 on a hot afternoon.

Density altitude is high and you attempt a steep approach with low airspeed.

The aircraft settles despite full available power.

You push cyclic forward, gain airspeed, and climb away.

When stable, you transmit:

Pan Pan Pan Pan, Curitiba Approach, ANAC123, settling with power, recovering, going around, request another approach.

Approach clears traffic and you reattempt with a shallower profile.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say altitude and intentions.
2. Approach: ANAC123, climb and maintain three thousand, report when ready for another approach.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared go-around, wind two seven zero at one two knots.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board.
5. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land when ready, emergency services notified.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Settling with power sounds like a contradiction.

You have power — but you are still sinking.

That is the trap.

The sequence:

Recognize. Cyclic forward. Airspeed. Go around.

Do not pull more collective alone.

More power into the vortex makes it worse.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "não segura" or "está caindo com potência" on frequency.

Use settling with power — ICAO and FAA standard term.

In the H130, watch density altitude, steep approaches, and downwind approaches.

If the aircraft settles — exit forward, then climb.

Train the recovery until cyclic forward is automatic.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is settling with power and how would you report it to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Settling with power is when a powered helicopter descends at a rate the rotor cannot arrest, often linked to vortex ring state. I would apply forward cyclic to gain airspeed, reduce collective if altitude permits, and go around. I would declare Pan Pan or Mayday depending on altitude, report settling with power, and request another approach or immediate landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Pulling more collective when the aircraft settles.  
  ✔ Forward cyclic and airspeed — more collective alone worsens the condition.

- ❌ Saying "não segura a descida" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use settling with power.

- ❌ Continuing a steep approach at high density altitude.  
  ✔ Use a shallower profile and maintain translational lift airspeed.

- ❌ Not going around after recovery.  
  ✔ Go around and reattempt with a safer approach path.
