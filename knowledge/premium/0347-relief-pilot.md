# Relief Pilot

**Catalog ID:** 0347  
**Category:** Crew  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Relief Pilot — a qualified pilot carried to relieve the captain or first officer from flight deck duty during long-range or extended operations, supporting fatigue management and controlled transfer of control.

ICAO Part 1 vocabulary distinguishes relief pilot from captain, first officer, and check pilot. Controllers may never hear the words "relief pilot," but examiners will. Operational English must describe controlled seat changes and who is flying.

For H130 ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, true cruise-relief operations are uncommon on short helicopter sectors — yet the term appears in multi-crew airline English and must be answered correctly.

Master: relief pilot, controlled rest, transfer of control, fatigue management.

## 📖 Meaning

Relief Pilot is a crew member qualified to take a flight deck duty position temporarily, relieving the captain or first officer so that fatigue is managed and continuous safe operation is maintained during long flights.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Piloto de alívio / Relief Pilot.

Membro da tripulação qualificado para assumir temporariamente uma posição no flightdeck, aliviando o capitão ou o primeiro oficial para gerenciar a fadiga e manter a operação segura contínua em voos longos.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a relief pilot is used, the crew should:

- brief who is being relieved and who becomes pilot flying / monitoring
- transfer control with clear verbal confirmation
- keep sterile cockpit discipline during the seat change
- ensure the PIC authority status remains clear under operator SOP
- avoid radio ambiguity about who is handling the aircraft

Relief pilot coordination commonly includes:

- relief pilot taking the seat
- captain / first officer returning to duty
- controlled rest procedures when authorized
- confirmation of altitude, heading, and clearance state at handover

**Important:** A relief pilot does not automatically become captain. Command authority follows operator rules and formal PIC designation — not merely sitting in a seat.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

ATC usually does not manage relief-pilot titles, but clarity matters when:

- a frequency handoff coincides with a crew seat change
- examiners test correct role vocabulary
- incapacitation or fatigue issues require stating who remains on duty
- readbacks must continue without interruption during crew rotation
- operators describe augmented crew in English accurately

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Long-haul airliner exam scenario: mid-Atlantic cruise, the relief pilot enters the flight deck to relieve the first officer.

Control is transferred with standard callouts. Radios remain monitored. ATC hears only normal position reports from the callsign.

In H130 Brazilian short-sector flying, you may never roster a relief pilot. If asked what a relief pilot is, answer with fatigue-relief and temporary duty assumption — not "extra passenger."

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Relief pilot is about fatigue and continuity — not a third captain by default.

Use the term correctly on exams. Describe a controlled transfer of control. Emphasize briefings and callouts.

Brazilian Portuguese: piloto de alívio. Prefer relief pilot in ICAO English.

Do not confuse relief pilot with check pilot. A check pilot evaluates. A relief pilot replaces a duty period.

In H130 ops, manage fatigue with duty limits and realistic scheduling instead of inventing cruise-relief procedures the operation does not use. Still know the airline term.

If seat changes happen, freeze non-essential tasks. Fly. Confirm. Then talk.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is a relief pilot?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A relief pilot is a qualified pilot who temporarily takes a flight deck duty position to relieve the captain or first officer during long operations, supporting fatigue management. Control is transferred with clear briefings and callouts, and command authority follows the operator's procedures.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Calling any extra pilot on board a relief pilot without duty qualification.  
  ✔ Relief pilot means qualified to take the duty seat under procedure.

- ❌ Confusing relief pilot with check pilot.  
  ✔ Relief covers fatigue/duty; check covers evaluation.

- ❌ Transferring control silently during a critical phase.  
  ✔ Use clear verbal transfer and avoid sterile-cockpit conflicts.

- ❌ Assuming the relief pilot automatically becomes PIC.  
  ✔ PIC authority follows formal designation and SOP.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

During cruise the relief pilot is about to replace the first officer. ATC issues a frequency change at the same moment.

**Your first task**

What should the crew prioritize before accepting complex new tasks?

**Your second task**

After a stable handover, read back the frequency change.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Complete or pause the seat change so one pilot is clearly flying and monitoring before non-essential tasks; do not transfer control and change frequency in confusion. Second task: ANAC123, contacting one two four decimal five. I would ensure the active pilot has the clearance state briefed before the swap completes.
