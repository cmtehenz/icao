# Ident

**Catalog ID:** 0141  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Ident — the pilot's response and the transponder function itself when the IDENT button is pressed following an ATC instruction.

This lesson is distinct from Squawk Ident (0106), which teaches the full ATC instruction "Squawk Ident." This lesson focuses on the word Ident as the pilot's action, confirmation, and the meaning of the IDENT function in secondary radar operations.

In the H130, operating into Navegantes, Florianópolis, or Curitiba, "Ident" is the one-word pilot response that confirms you pressed the IDENT button — crisp, immediate, and unambiguous.

Knowing when to say it, why it matters, and how to use it professionally completes your transponder communication picture.

## 📖 Meaning

Ident is the pilot's verbal confirmation that the IDENT button on the transponder has been pressed, producing a Special Position Identification (SPI) pulse visible to ATC on radar. It is both an action and a one-word transmission.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Ident.

Confirmação verbal do piloto de que o botão IDENT do transponder foi acionado, produzindo um pulso especial de identificação de posição visível ao controlador no radar. É tanto uma ação física quanto uma transmissão de uma palavra.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

Ident as a pilot response involves:

- pressing the IDENT button physically on the transponder
- transmitting the word "Ident" followed by the callsign
- waiting for ATC confirmation of radar identification
- not pressing IDENT again unless instructed

The IDENT function works because:

- the transponder emits a special SPI pulse on the next reply
- this pulse causes the radar blip to blossom or highlight on the controller's display
- the controller can unambiguously identify which return is the aircraft
- the identification is confirmed verbally by ATC

**Important:** Ident is only ever pressed when instructed by ATC. The word Ident is only ever transmitted after pressing the button — not as a standalone acknowledgment. Saying "Ident" without pressing the button is incorrect procedure.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC relies on Ident to:

- positively identify an aircraft among multiple radar returns
- confirm the aircraft is transmitting on the correct squawk code
- re-establish identification after a transponder anomaly
- verify position in a complex traffic environment
- complete the radar identification process at initial contact

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You have just checked in with Florianópolis Approach inbound from Navegantes in your H130.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, squawk ident.

You press the IDENT button on the transponder.

You transmit:

Ident, ANAC123.

Approach responds:

ANAC123, identified, thirty miles north of Florianópolis, descend to altitude five thousand, QNH one zero one seven.

The entire exchange is three seconds.

That one button press and one word gave the controller your exact position among a dozen radar returns.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Ident is a one-word transmission with one physical action.

The two must happen together.

Press. Then speak.

Not speak then press. Not press without speaking.

The discipline is: when you hear "squawk ident," your hand moves to the IDENT button before your mouth opens.

Action first. Confirmation second.

In the H130, the IDENT button is on the transponder control head. It is a dedicated button — not a menu selection, not a soft key. One press. One pulse.

Many Brazilian pilots say "Roger" when instructed to ident. That is incorrect.

"Roger" tells ATC you heard the instruction.

"Ident, ANAC123" tells ATC you executed it.

ATC is looking for your blip to highlight on radar. "Roger" does not highlight anything.

Press. Report. Done.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Explain the Ident function and how a pilot confirms it to ATC.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Ident refers to pressing the IDENT button on the transponder, which causes the aircraft's radar return to highlight on the controller's display with a Special Position Identification pulse. When instructed by ATC to ident, I press the button and confirm with the transmission: Ident, ANAC123. I do not press IDENT unless instructed. The confirmation tells ATC the action was taken, and they then acknowledge by confirming radar identification.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "Roger" instead of "Ident" when instructed to ident.  
  ✔ Ident, ANAC123 — confirms the action, not just acknowledgment.

- ❌ Pressing IDENT without transmitting a confirmation.  
  ✔ Always say: Ident, ANAC123 immediately after pressing.

- ❌ Pressing IDENT spontaneously without ATC instruction.  
  ✔ Only press IDENT when instructed. Unauthorized ident can confuse radar displays.

- ❌ Confusing Ident with Squawk — thinking "ident" means set a code.  
  ✔ Ident = IDENT button. Squawk = transponder code setting. Different actions.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Ident, ANAC123

**Pronunciation:** EYE-dent, AY-EN-AY-SEE WUN TOO THREE

**Word Stress**

- Ident → EYE-dent (stress on first syllable)
- Callsign → each element spoken clearly

Practice:

Ident...

Together:

Ident, ANAC123.

Note: short, confident, immediate. No filler words. No "I am ident-ing" or "pressing ident now." Just: Ident, ANAC123.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Navegantes Approach calls you shortly after you check in: "ANAC123, squawk two four zero zero and ident."

**Your first task**

What do you do and what do you transmit?

**Your second task**

Approach comes back: "ANAC123, ident not received, please re-ident." The transponder is showing ALT mode and code 2400. What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I enter code 2400 on the transponder, verify ALT mode, press IDENT, and transmit: Squawking two four zero zero and ident, ANAC123. If Approach advises ident not received, I verify the code and mode again, press IDENT a second time, and transmit: Re-ident, ANAC123. If the issue persists, I advise: ANAC123, ident not functional, transponder showing ALT, code two four zero zero — and follow ATC guidance for radar identification by position report.
