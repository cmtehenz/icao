# Forward Cargo Door Open

**Catalog ID:** 0282  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Forward Cargo Door Open — an unsafe or open indication for the forward cargo door in flight or after takeoff, requiring checklist action, possible return, and clear ATC communication. On airliners this is a frequent ICAO exam item distinct from aft cargo door open and from door separated.

For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, teach the exam English — forward cargo door open, door unsafe, request return — and relate it to any forward baggage or cabin door unsafe indication on the helicopter. Location words matter: forward versus aft changes inspection and fire-service expectations on the ground.

Master precise location: forward cargo door open.

## 📖 Meaning

Forward Cargo Door Open is an indication or confirmation that the forward cargo door is not closed and locked as required, occurring on the ground, during takeoff, or in flight, and requiring abnormal or emergency procedures, ATC notification, and often rejected takeoff or immediate return / diversion.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Porta de carga dianteira aberta / Forward Cargo Door Open.

Indicação ou confirmação de que a porta de carga dianteira não está fechada e travada como necessário, ocorrendo no solo, durante a decolagem ou em voo, e exigindo procedimentos anormais ou de emergência, notificação ao ATC e frequentemente rejeição de decolagem ou retorno / desvio imediato.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When forward cargo door open is indicated, the crew should:

- apply the AFM door open / unsafe checklist immediately
- reject takeoff if still on the runway and below decision speed per procedure
- if airborne: do not cycle the door; return or divert as required
- declare Pan Pan for door open with aircraft controllable; Mayday if separation, structural concern, or severe handling issues develop
- report nature (forward cargo door open), phase of flight, intentions
- for H130: use forward door open / baggage door open for the actual type; keep forward cargo door open for exam English

Forward cargo door open reporting commonly includes:

- forward cargo door open / forward cargo door unsafe
- request immediate return
- aircraft controllable
- after landing: do not open until inspected if required
- souls and fuel when asked

**Important:** Say forward — not only "cargo door." ATC and maintenance need the location. Distinguish open/unsafe from separated.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to forward cargo door open reports to:

- clear priority return after takeoff
- alert emergency services for possible door or structural issues
- keep other traffic clear of possible debris if the door fails
- request door status and controllability
- coordinate runway inspection if debris is suspected

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Training scenario: shortly after takeoff, forward cargo door open warning. Aircraft controllable. You elect to return.

You transmit:

Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan, Departure, ANAC123, forward cargo door open, aircraft controllable, request immediate return to land.

Departure: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, turn left heading three six zero, descend, runway three two left available, say souls and fuel when able.

You continue:

ANAC123, turning left three six zero, forward cargo door open, souls twelve, fuel three hours, request shortest approach.

If the door separates, upgrade to Mayday and report cargo door separated.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Departure: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan forward cargo door open, cleared to return, turn left heading three six zero.
2. Approach: ANAC123, say if the aircraft is fully controllable.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway cleared, emergency services standing by.
4. Approach: ANAC123, report door status on final if able.
5. Tower: ANAC123, after landing remain on the runway for inspection if required.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Forward cargo door open is a location-specific door emergency.

Checklist. Return. Say forward. Say open — not separated — unless it has left the aircraft.

Brazilian pilots may say "porta dianteira aberta." Prefer forward cargo door open in ICAO English. If ATC hears only "door," they may assume passenger door or aft cargo — correct them.

In the H130, map the concept to your actual forward baggage or cabin door. Do not invent a jet forward hold — but do not miss the exam phrase either.

If handling changes or the door departs, upgrade urgency immediately.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

After takeoff you get a forward cargo door open warning. The aircraft is controllable. What do you do and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would apply the checklist, declare Pan Pan stating forward cargo door open and that the aircraft is controllable, and request an immediate return. I would report souls and fuel when able and land with emergency services standing by. If the door separated or control became difficult, I would upgrade to Mayday and report cargo door separated or the control problem.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "cargo door open" without forward/aft.  
  ✔ Specify forward cargo door open.

- ❌ Confusing open/unsafe with separated.  
  ✔ Open means indication/unsafe; separated means the door is gone.

- ❌ Continuing the flight with an unresolved door open warning.  
  ✔ Return or divert; do not press on to destination.

- ❌ Describing H130 jet-style forward cargo holds inaccurately.  
  ✔ Exam English yes; type systems factual.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Forward cargo door open is precise English for a precise problem.

Say forward. Say open. Return. Upgrade if it separates.

The H130 may not have that door — ICAO exams still do. Learn the words. Fly your type.

Location tells maintenance and fire crews where to look. Controllability tells ATC how urgent the vectors must be.

That is how door-open warnings end as inspected landings — not as continued climbs with an unsafe door ignored.
