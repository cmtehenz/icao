# RNAV Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0191  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers RNAV Failure — loss or degradation of area navigation capability that prevents the aircraft from accurately flying an RNAV route, SID, STAR, or approach as cleared, requiring reversion to conventional navigation or ATC radar vectors.

RNAV failure is not only a cockpit message. It is a clearance problem. If you can no longer navigate the RNAV procedure you were cleared for, ATC must know immediately so they can provide an alternative — vectors, conventional aids, or a different procedure.

For H130 helicopter training academy IFR operations in Brazil, RNAV procedures are increasingly common into and out of Congonhas, Guarulhos, Curitiba, Florianópolis, and Santos Dumont. Crews must be ready to declare RNAV inability, accept vectors, and continue safely on raw data or radar.

Understanding the phraseology for RNAV failure and the speaking skills to explain the situation supports Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

RNAV Failure is the loss, degradation, or unavailability of area navigation performance required for the cleared RNAV route or procedure, caused by GNSS problems, FMS or navigation computer fault, database issues, or integrity alerts, such that the aircraft can no longer meet the navigation accuracy required and must revert to alternative navigation or ATC assistance.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha de RNAV / RNAV Failure.

Perda, degradação ou indisponibilidade do desempenho de navegação de área exigido para a rota ou procedimento RNAV autorizado, causada por problemas de GNSS, falha do FMS ou computador de navegação, problemas de banco de dados ou alertas de integridade, de modo que a aeronave não pode mais atender à precisão de navegação exigida e deve reverter para navegação alternativa ou assistência do ATC.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When RNAV capability is lost or degraded, the crew should:

- recognize integrity alerts, map shifts, or inability to maintain the RNAV track
- stop relying on the failed RNAV guidance for lateral navigation
- advise ATC immediately: unable RNAV, request vectors or conventional procedure
- select an alternate navigation method (VOR/DME, visual, radar vectors) as available
- request an amended clearance if the SID, STAR, or approach requires RNAV
- comply with AFM and operator procedures for GNSS/RNAV abnormal conditions

RNAV failure scenarios commonly include:

- GNSS integrity alert during an RNAV arrival
- FMS failure removing RNAV steering
- inability to load or activate an RNAV procedure
- ATC clearance for RNAV SID when the aircraft is no longer RNAV capable
- need to break off an RNAV approach and request ILS or vectors

**Important:** Do not continue an RNAV procedure after you know you cannot meet its navigation requirements. In the H130, declare the limitation early. Brazilian terminal airspace is busy — ATC can almost always give vectors, but only if they know you need them. "Unable RNAV" is a professional call, not an admission of poor flying.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers work with RNAV failure reports to:

- remove the aircraft from RNAV-dependent routes and procedures
- provide radar vectors or conventional clearances
- resequence traffic when an arrival can no longer fly the STAR as published
- protect separation when an aircraft may leave the expected RNAV track
- coordinate an alternate approach (ILS, VOR, visual) into the destination

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cleared for an RNAV arrival into Florianópolis. Approaching the initial segments, a GNSS integrity alert appears and RNAV accuracy is no longer assured.

You disconnect from RNAV lateral guidance for the procedure, maintain present heading, and call Approach: unable RNAV, request radar vectors for the approach.

Approach acknowledges, issues vectors for sequencing, and later clears you for an ILS approach.

You never attempt to "guess" the RNAV track. You told ATC. You received vectors. You continued safely.

That is the correct RNAV failure response — immediate declaration, alternative clearance, stable continuation.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger unable RNAV, fly heading one eight zero, vectors for ILS runway one four.
2. Radar: ANAC123, unable RNAV SID, cleared heading two seven zero, radar vectors departure.
3. Control: ANAC123, RNAV not available, expect conventional route via the VOR.
4. Approach: ANAC123, cancel RNAV arrival, radar vectors for sequencing.
5. Radar: ANAC123, confirm RNAV capability restored or still unable.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, unable RNAV, heading one eight zero, vectors for ILS one four.
2. ANAC123, unable RNAV SID, heading two seven zero, radar vectors departure.
3. ANAC123, RNAV not available, expecting conventional route via the VOR.
4. ANAC123, RNAV arrival cancelled, standing by for radar vectors.
5. ANAC123, still unable RNAV, request continued vectors.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

RNAV is a capability clearance. When the capability fails, the clearance must change.

The moment you know you cannot fly the RNAV procedure, say unable RNAV. Do not wait until you are off track and ATC asks why.

In Brazilian ops, vectors are the normal backup. Conventional aids may also be available. Ask for what you need: vectors for ILS, clearance via the VOR, or a visual approach if conditions permit.

Do not try to hand-fly an approximate RNAV path from memory. That creates a navigation bust and a separation risk.

Part 3 answers should explain recognition, immediate ATC notification, reversion method, and continuation plan. Keep the language operational and calm.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You are cleared for an RNAV STAR when a GNSS integrity alert makes RNAV navigation unreliable. Describe what you do.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would stop using RNAV guidance for the arrival, maintain a safe heading or last assigned track as appropriate, and advise ATC immediately that I am unable RNAV and request radar vectors or a conventional procedure. I would not continue the RNAV STAR without the required navigation performance. Once ATC issues vectors or an alternate clearance such as an ILS, I would comply, brief the new procedure, and continue the approach with the available navigation sources.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing the RNAV procedure "approximately" after an integrity alert.  
  ✔ Declare unable RNAV and request vectors or a conventional clearance.

- ❌ Waiting for ATC to notice you are off the RNAV track.  
  ✔ Report the failure as soon as it affects your ability to comply.

- ❌ Saying only "navigation problem" without specifying RNAV.  
  ✔ Be specific: unable RNAV, request radar vectors.

- ❌ Rejecting vectors because you hoped GNSS would recover in one minute.  
  ✔ Accept the safe alternative; you can report capability restored later if it returns.

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, un-AY-bul AR-nav, reh-KWEST RAY-dar VEK-terz

**Word Stress**

- Unable → un-AY-bul (stress on second)
- RNAV → AR-nav
- Radar → RAY-dar
- Vectors → VEK-terz

Practice:

Unable RNAV... request radar vectors... for the ILS.

Together:

ANAC123, unable RNAV, request radar vectors for ILS runway one four.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

RNAV failure is a clearance change waiting for your call.

Recognize it. Say unable RNAV. Take vectors or conventional navigation.

Do not invent the procedure from memory. Do not hope the signal returns while you drift off track.

In Brazilian terminal airspace, ATC is ready to help — if you speak up early.

Declare the limitation. Accept the alternative. Brief the new plan. Continue safely.

That is how RNAV contingency looks in a professional H130 cockpit.
