# Channelized Attention

**Catalog ID:** 0474  
**Category:** Human Factors  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Channelized Attention — a human-factors state in which the pilot focuses intensely on one cue, task, or problem and fails to monitor other critical information.

Channelized attention is not concentration. Concentration is deliberate. Channelization is narrowing: the world shrinks to one instrument, one radio call, or one malfunction while altitude, traffic, or fuel quietly leave the picture.

In H130 operations — confined areas, instrument approaches, and emergency checklists — channelized attention is a common precursor to altitude busts, airspace infringements, and CFIT risk. The task that feels most urgent is not always the task that keeps you alive.

Understanding how to recognize and break channelized attention in ICAO-standard English — including traffic calls, altitude reports, and intention updates — supports Part 1, Part 2, and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Channelized Attention is a cognitive state in which attention becomes locked on a single stimulus or task to the exclusion of other critical flight cues, reducing situational awareness and increasing the likelihood of altitude, navigation, or traffic conflicts.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Atenção canalizada / Channelized Attention.

Estado cognitivo em que a atenção fica presa a um único estímulo ou tarefa, excluindo outros indícios críticos de voo, reduzindo a consciência situacional e aumentando a probabilidade de conflitos de altitude, navegação ou tráfego.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When channelized attention is suspected, the crew should:

- expand the scan deliberately: attitude, altitude, airspeed, traffic, fuel
- use the other pilot as an attention breaker — call out neglected parameters
- time-box troubleshooting: fix in short cycles, then return to aviate
- shed non-essential tasks when workload peaks
- advise ATC if temporary inability to comply is caused by high workload

Attention management includes:

- prioritizing aviate, navigate, communicate
- assigning one pilot to fly while the other troubleshoots
- using altitude alerters and bugs as external reminders
- refusing to stare at a single caution light through a level bust

**Important:** If you have been looking at one thing for more than a few seconds in a dynamic phase of flight, look away. Channelization feels productive. It is often dangerous.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers help when attention narrows by:

- repeating critical altitude or heading instructions
- issuing traffic alerts that force a wider scan
- asking the crew to confirm altitude when the track looks wrong
- reducing frequency congestion during high-workload recoveries
- providing simple, single-task clearances when a crew is overloaded

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are troubleshooting a caution light on departure from Congonhas. Both pilots are heads-down on the QRH. Altitude is climbing through the cleared three thousand toward four thousand.

Approach calls:

ANAC123, confirm altitude, maintain three thousand.

You break the channel:

ANAC123, correcting, leveling three thousand, working a caution.

Approach: ANAC123, maintain three thousand, report when ready for further climb.

You respond:

ANAC123, maintaining three thousand, will report ready.

One pilot flies. One troubleshoots. The scan is restored.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, confirm altitude, maintain three thousand.
2. Radar: ANAC123, traffic twelve o'clock, two miles, opposite direction.
3. Tower: ANAC123, say altitude, you appear high on final.
4. Approach: ANAC123, stop climb, maintain four thousand.
5. Control: ANAC123, confirm able to copy clearance, or request delay.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

5. ANAC123, request short delay to copy, working system caution.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Channelized attention is a silent hijack.

The caution light wins. The radio wins. The passenger question wins. Altitude loses.

In the H130, protect the flying pilot. If both heads go down, both of you are passengers.

Use verbal scan calls: "Altitude." "Speed." "Traffic." Short words break tunnels.

The aircraft does not care how important your checklist feels. Gravity does not wait.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Both pilots are focused on a caution light and miss an altitude restriction. ATC queries your altitude. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would correct immediately and report: ANAC123, correcting, leveling three thousand, working a caution. Then I would split duties — one pilot flies and monitors, one continues the checklist. I would not keep both crew members channelized on the malfunction while the flight path drifts.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Both pilots troubleshooting while nobody flies.  
  ✔ Assign a flying pilot before opening the QRH.

- ❌ Ignoring ATC altitude queries while finishing a checklist.  
  ✔ Answer and correct the path first, then resume the checklist.

- ❌ Staring at one instrument through a traffic conflict.  
  ✔ Expand the scan when traffic or terrain is a factor.

- ❌ Accepting complex clearances while overloaded.  
  ✔ Request delay to copy when attention is saturated.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

On climb-out you and your first officer are both heads-down on a caution. ATC says you are above the cleared altitude.

**Your first task**

What do you say while correcting?

**Your second task**

ATC asks if you can copy a complex reroute. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, correcting, leveling three thousand, working a caution. Second task: ANAC123, request short delay to copy, one pilot flying. I would break the attention tunnel, restore the flight path, then accept new tasks only when the scan is healthy.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Channelized attention makes one problem feel like the only problem.

It is not.

In the H130, fly first. Scan wide. Split the work. Tell ATC when the path needs correcting.

If your eyes have not moved, your awareness has not either.
