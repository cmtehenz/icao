# Cleared ILS Approach

**Catalog ID:** 0147  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Cleared ILS Approach — the formal ATC authorization to commence and fly the Instrument Landing System approach to the assigned runway.

Cleared ILS Approach is not an advisory. It is not preparation. It is authorization.

The moment you receive this clearance, you are authorized to intercept the localizer, follow the glideslope, and proceed to the runway threshold.

This is the most critical clearance in instrument approach operations. Florianópolis, Curitiba, Congonhas, and São Paulo all have ILS procedures active in IMC conditions.

Knowing the difference between Expect ILS Approach and Cleared ILS Approach is non-negotiable ICAO knowledge.

This lesson connects Part 2 phraseology with Part 4 operational precision required at ICAO Level 4.

## 📖 Meaning

Cleared ILS Approach is an ATC authorization for the pilot to commence the Instrument Landing System approach to the specified runway, intercepting the localizer and descending on the glideslope to the decision height, where the crew must execute either a landing or a missed approach.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Autorização do controle de tráfego aéreo para o piloto iniciar a aproximação ILS para a pista especificada, interceptando o localizador e descendo no glide slope até a altura de decisão, onde a tripulação deve executar o pouso ou a arremetida.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the crew receives Cleared ILS Approach, they should:

- confirm the localizer frequency, ident, and course are set and verified
- confirm the glideslope intercept altitude as the current target altitude
- intercept the localizer from below and in level flight — never from above
- arm the approach mode or select full ILS on the navigation instruments
- call stabilized on localizer and glideslope before intercepting the final fix
- at the decision height, execute landing or missed approach — no middle position

Cleared ILS Approach operations require:

- current QNH set and confirmed
- approach briefing fully completed before the clearance
- final approach checklist complete
- speed and configuration appropriate for the approach
- crew callouts for localizer alive, glideslope alive, and established

**Important:** Cleared ILS Approach is the authorization. Expect ILS Approach was the advisory. Never begin descending on the glideslope without the cleared instruction. Never confuse advisory with authorization.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Cleared ILS Approach to:

- formally authorize descent on the precision approach path
- sequence the aircraft for landing after vectoring or hold
- provide clearance once the aircraft is established in the approach sector
- confirm the runway assignment and approach type simultaneously
- allow the crew to commence and complete the precision approach

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Florianópolis in your H130 after holding at IDAMO.

You briefed the ILS runway one four twenty minutes ago. You are ready.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, turn right heading one seven zero, intercept localizer runway one four, cleared ILS approach runway one four.

You are now authorized to fly the approach.

You respond:

Heading one seven zero, intercept localizer, cleared ILS approach runway one four, ANAC123.

You select approach mode. The localizer comes alive at ten miles. You intercept from level flight.

Glideslope alive at three thousand feet. Gear down. Flaps set. Checks complete.

Full intercept. Stable descent. Decision height — runway in sight.

Cleared. Briefed. Professional.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

There are two phrases you must never confuse in instrument flying.

Expect ILS Approach — advisory. Brief the procedure. Wait.

Cleared ILS Approach — authorization. Fly the procedure. Now.

The difference is everything.

When you hear "cleared ILS approach," the glideslope is yours to descend. The localizer is yours to track. The runway is at the end of those two beams.

In the H130, the ILS is raw data. No AFCS coupling. The scan is yours.

Cross-check the CDI and the glideslope indicator on every cycle.

Localizer: keep the needle centered. One dot displacement means a correction is needed.

Glideslope: always intercept from below. Never intercept from above — you risk a false glideslope.

At decision height: three words guide you — land or go around.

There is no third option.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Explain the difference between Expect ILS Approach and Cleared ILS Approach.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Expect ILS Approach is an advisory from ATC that the ILS has been assigned for the destination runway. It allows the crew to brief the approach and configure the aircraft before the formal clearance. Cleared ILS Approach is the authorization to commence the approach, meaning the crew may intercept the localizer and descend on the glideslope. Without the cleared instruction, the crew must not begin the approach even if the localizer is tuned and the aircraft is aligned. The cleared instruction is the legal and operational authorization — the expect advisory is preparation only.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Beginning the approach descent after hearing Expect ILS Approach.  
  ✔ Expect is advisory. Wait for the Cleared instruction before intercepting.

- ❌ Intercepting the glideslope from above.  
  ✔ Always intercept from below. Fly to intercept altitude, then capture the glideslope.

- ❌ Not reading back the runway designation in the clearance.  
  ✔ Always include the runway: Cleared ILS approach runway one four, ANAC123.

- ❌ Omitting the restriction if ATC assigns a crossing altitude.  
  ✔ Read back all restrictions: cross IDAMO at or above three thousand.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Cleared ILS Approach Runway One Four

**Pronunciation:** kleerd eye-ELL-ESS uh-PROHCH RUN-way WUN FOR

**Word Stress**

- Cleared → kleerd (one syllable, strong)
- ILS → eye-ELL-ESS (each letter individually)
- Approach → uh-PROHCH
- Runway → RUN-way
- One Four → WUN FOR

Practice:

Cleared... ILS... Approach... Runway... One Four.

Together:

Cleared ILS approach runway one four, ANAC123.

Say each ILS letter separately and clearly — it is never a single word.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**CLEAR** — **C**learance received, **L**ocalizer ident confirmed, **E**stablish from level flight, **A**lways intercept glideslope from below, **R**unway or go-around at DH.
