# Ceiling Below Minimums

**Catalog ID:** 0379  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Ceiling Below Minimums — when the reported or observed cloud ceiling is lower than the published minimum ceiling (or equivalent height) required for a specific approach or landing procedure.

Ceiling below minimums is a hard stop for a legal landing, not a suggestion. Continuing "to take a look" without the required ceiling or without being established on a suitable approach invites CFIT and regulatory failure. In the H130, low ceilings over Brazilian terrain and obstacles demand strict minima discipline and clear ATC communication for hold, delay, or diversion.

## 📖 Meaning

Ceiling Below Minimums describes the situation in which the height of the lowest broken or overcast cloud layer (or vertical visibility) at the aerodrome is lower than the minimum ceiling published for the intended approach or landing procedure, making a landing under those rules unauthorized unless conditions improve or an alternate procedure with lower minima is available and appropriate.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Teto abaixo dos mínimos / Ceiling Below Minimums.

Situação em que a altura da camada de nuvens quebrada ou encoberta mais baixa (ou a visibilidade vertical) no aeródromo é inferior ao teto mínimo publicado para o procedimento de aproximação ou pouso pretendido, tornando o pouso sob essas regras não autorizado a menos que as condições melhorem ou exista um procedimento alternativo com mínimos mais baixos disponível e adequado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ceiling is below minimums, the crew should:

- compare METAR/ATIS ceiling with charted minima for the planned approach
- do not descend below minimums without the required visual references
- request hold and latest weather, or divert to an alternate with suitable ceiling
- avoid "look-see" approaches that violate minima policy
- advise ATC: weather below minima, requesting hold or diversion
- consider a different approach only if its published minima are met

Ceiling-below-minima actions commonly include:

- holding for improvement
- diverting to alternate
- delaying departure when destination ceiling is forecast/reported below
- missed approach if ceiling closes in before visual references

**Important:** Ceiling is typically the lowest BKN or OVC layer. Scattered cloud is not ceiling — but a broken layer at 300 feet with minima of 500 feet is still below. Brazilian plateau airports in winter often sit under low overcast; respect the number on the chart.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use ceiling-below-minima information to:

- advise aircraft when official weather is below landing minima
- expect holding or diversion requests
- manage airport acceptance when only some approaches remain viable
- coordinate missed approaches when ceiling drops suddenly
- pass SPECI updates that restore or remove landing capability

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cleared for the ILS into Curitiba. ATIS: overcast 300 feet. Your minima require 500 feet ceiling (example training case). Weather is below minima.

You transmit:

Curitiba Approach, ANAC123, weather below minima, requesting hold and latest ceiling, or diversion if no improvement.

Approach: ANAC123, hold as published, ceiling remains three hundred, say intentions.

You divert rather than attempt an illegal look-see.

ANAC123, diverting Florianópolis due ceiling below minima, request vectors.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Minimums exist because terrain and obstacles do not move when the cloud does.

Ceiling below minimums means you do not land — period — unless you have another legal procedure that is above minima.

"Just one look" is how helicopters hit hills in IMC. The H130 will not save you from a low broken layer over the Serra.

Say the phrase ATC expects: weather below minima, requesting hold or diversion. Clear. Professional. Final.

When ceiling improves to at or above minima and visibility is adequate, then request the approach. Until then, hold fuel or leave.

Brazilian winter OVC003 at Curitiba will test your ego. Let the chart win.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATIS reports a ceiling lower than your approach minima. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would not commence or continue an approach to landing under those minima. I would advise ATC that weather is below minima and request holding for improvement or diversion to an alternate where ceiling is suitable. I would monitor SPECI and ATIS updates. If already on approach and ceiling falls below minima before I have the required visual references, I would execute a missed approach. In the H130 I will not conduct a look-see below published minima.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing for a visual "check" when ceiling is below minima.  
  ✔ Hold or divert; do not descend below minima without legal visual references.

- ❌ Confusing SCT layers with ceiling.  
  ✔ Use BKN/OVC (or VV) for ceiling vs minima.

- ❌ Saying only "unable" without the weather reason.  
  ✔ State: ceiling below minima / weather below minima.

- ❌ No alternate when destination is chronically low ceiling.  
  ✔ Plan fuel and alternate before you need them on frequency.
