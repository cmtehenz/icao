# Radar Contact Lost

**Catalog ID:** 0302  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Radar Contact Lost — the situation in which ATC no longer has radar identification of the aircraft, requiring position reports, possible squawk changes, and disciplined radiotelephony until radar contact is re-established. Core Part 2 skill for Brazilian IFR and VFR controlled operations; for H130 crews, radar contact lost is common at low altitude, in mountainous areas, and near the edge of radar coverage.

Radar contact lost is High priority because separation and traffic information may change the moment the controller loses your blip.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO/DECEA phraseology for radar contact lost, position reporting, and re-identification.

Understanding radar contact lost in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Radar Contact Lost is the loss of radar identification of an aircraft by ATC, requiring the crew to continue on the last clearance, make position reports as instructed, squawk as directed, and maintain two-way communication until radar contact is re-established or alternative separation is applied.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Contato radar perdido / Radar Contact Lost.

Perda da identificação radar de uma aeronave pelo ATC, exigindo que a tripulação continue na última autorização, faça reports de posição conforme instruído, squawk conforme determinado e mantenha comunicação bidirecional até o restabelecimento do contato radar ou a aplicação de separação alternativa.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When radar contact is lost, the crew should:

- acknowledge the advisory and continue on the last ATC clearance unless otherwise instructed
- squawk the assigned code or Mode A/C/S as directed
- provide position, altitude, and estimate reports when requested
- monitor frequency and read back new clearances carefully
- advise ATC if you believe you are outside radar coverage or have navigation uncertainty
- for H130: expect radar contact lost more often at low level — keep position reports crisp

Radar contact lost exchanges commonly include:

- radar contact lost / ident, please
- report position / altitude / estimate
- squawk seven seven zero zero only if emergency — otherwise assigned code
- radar contact re-established
- resume own navigation / radar vectors as applicable

**Important:** Radar contact lost is not automatically lost communication. Keep talking. Fly the clearance. Report when asked. Do not invent a new route without ATC.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers advise radar contact lost to:

- warn the crew that radar separation may no longer apply
- request position reports for procedural separation
- re-identify the aircraft with ident or code changes
- manage traffic information without a reliable radar track
- re-establish radar contact as soon as coverage allows

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: IFR cruise toward Florianópolis in the H130. Radar says contact lost — possible low altitude or coverage gap.

Radar transmits:

ANAC123, radar contact lost, report position and altitude.

You respond with a clear position report and continue the clearance. Later:

ANAC123, radar contact re-established, identified, continue present heading.

You acknowledge and resume normal radar environment discipline — shorter calls, vectors as assigned.

For Brazilian mountain and coastal routes, treat radar contact lost as routine professionalism, not panic.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Radar: ANAC123, radar contact lost, report position, altitude and estimate Florianópolis.
2. Radar: ANAC123, squawk two one zero four, ident.
3. Radar: ANAC123, radar contact re-established, identified.
4. Control: ANAC123, radar contact lost, continue present heading, report reaching flight level six zero.
5. Approach: ANAC123, radar contact lost due to low altitude, report field in sight.

3. ANAC123, radar contact re-established, identified, continuing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Radar contact lost means the controller's picture changed — not that your aircraft disappeared from the sky.

Continue the last clearance. Answer position and altitude cleanly. Ident when asked. Do not freestyle a shortcut.

In the H130, you will hear this more than jet crews at high flight levels. Low-level routes and terrain eat radar coverage. Expect it. Brief it. Sound bored and competent when it happens.

Part 2 examiners love this topic because it tests whether you can report position without radar babysitting. Practice the report structure until it is automatic.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC advises radar contact lost and asks for your position and altitude. What do you do, and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would continue on the last clearance, then report my position, altitude, and estimate as requested in a clear, structured call. I would squawk or ident if instructed and maintain listening watch until radar contact is re-established. I would not change route or altitude without a new clearance. On a helicopter at low level, I would expect this more often and keep position reports short and accurate.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating radar contact lost as lost communication and going silent.  
  ✔ Keep two-way radio; only the radar track is missing.

- ❌ Changing heading or altitude without clearance because "radar is gone."  
  ✔ Fly the last clearance until ATC amends it.

- ❌ Giving a long story instead of position, altitude, estimate.  
  ✔ Structured report: place, level, time estimate.

- ❌ Panicking on the oral when the phrase appears.  
  ✔ It is routine IFR English — acknowledge and report.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Radar Contact Lost, Report Position and Altitude

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, RAY-dar KON-takt LOST, rih-PORT puh-ZISH-un and AL-ti-tood

**Word Stress**

- Radar → RAY-dar
- Contact → KON-takt

- Altitude → AL-ti-tood

Practice:

Together:

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**LOST** — **L**ast clearance continues, **O**ffer position and altitude, **S**quawk/ident as told, **T**rack re-established — then resume normal radar talk.
