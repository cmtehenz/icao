# Approved Taxi

**Catalog ID:** 0203  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Approved Taxi — confirmation that a requested or proposed taxi route or maneuver is approved by ATC, or phraseology indicating the taxi movement is authorized as stated.

Pilots sometimes request a specific taxi route, backtrack, intersection departure taxi, or apron exit. ATC responds with approval, amendment, or denial. "Approved" means the requested movement is authorized — still subject to any conditions ATC adds, such as hold short or give way.

For H130 operations at Congonhas, Santos Dumont, Guarulhos, Curitiba, and Florianópolis, approved taxi may include ground taxi, hover taxi, or a specific path around congested aprons. The key skill is requesting clearly and reading back the approval with all conditions.

This lesson builds Part 2 proficiency for request-and-approval taxi exchanges in DECEA/ICAO phraseology.

## 📖 Meaning

Approved Taxi is ATC authorization confirming that a requested taxi route, taxi maneuver, or taxi intention is approved, allowing the pilot to proceed as requested or as restated by ATC, including any attached restrictions.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Approved taxi / Táxi aprovado.

Autorização do controle confirmando que a rota de táxi, a manobra ou a intenção solicitada está aprovada, permitindo ao piloto prosseguir conforme solicitado ou conforme reafirmado pelo controle, incluindo quaisquer restrições associadas.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When taxi is approved, the pilot should:

- read back the approval and every condition (route, hold short, caution, give way)
- not begin the movement until the approval is understood
- follow the approved path exactly — approval of one route is not approval of a shortcut
- advise ATC if unable to accept the approved taxi as stated
- for helicopters, confirm whether ground taxi or hover taxi was approved if that distinction matters

Approved taxi situations include:

- pilot requests taxi via an alternate taxiway due to works
- pilot requests backtrack or specific runway entry point
- pilot requests taxi to a different ramp or stand
- ATC approves present position taxi or progressive taxi continuation

**Important:** Silence is not approval. If you requested a taxi route and ATC did not clearly approve it, ask again. Do not move on assumption.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use taxi approvals to:

- authorize non-standard or pilot-requested ground movements
- keep surface flow flexible when the default route is blocked
- attach safety conditions to an otherwise acceptable request
- prevent unauthorized shortcuts across the maneuvering area
- coordinate conflicting taxi requests between multiple aircraft

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are at the Congonhas apron in the H130 ready to depart.

You: ANAC123, request taxi via Alpha due to works on Bravo.

Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Alpha approved, hold short of runway one seven Right.

You read back the approved route and the hold short. You taxi only via Alpha. You do not decide that Charlie looks empty and switch routes.

Later, if you need hover taxi instead of wheel taxi on a soft edge, you request it. You move that way only when approved.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Alpha approved, hold short runway one seven Right.
2. Ground: ANAC123, request approved, taxi to holding point runway two eight via Charlie.
3. Tower: ANAC123, backtrack approved, report ready for departure.
4. Ground: ANAC123, negative Alpha, taxi via Bravo approved.
5. Ground: ANAC123, hover taxi to the helipad approved, taxi with caution.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Approved taxi is a contract.

You asked for something. ATC agreed — sometimes with changes. Your job is to fly the contract on the ground: exact route, exact restrictions.

If ATC says negative on your request but approves an alternate, the alternate is the clearance. Do not silently return to your original idea.

In helicopter operations, be explicit when the mode matters. "Request hover taxi to the ramp" is clearer than "request taxi" if you cannot ground-taxi safely.

Never treat a previous approval as permanent. Each movement needs a current clearance. Yesterday's approved shortcut is today's runway incursion risk.

At dense Brazilian airports, good requests help ATC. Bad assumptions create go-arounds on the ground.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You request taxi via Alpha because Bravo has works. ATC says taxi via Alpha approved, hold short of the runway. What does this mean?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

It means my requested route via Alpha is authorized, but I must hold short of the runway and must not cross without further clearance. I would read back both the approved route and the hold-short restriction, then taxi only via Alpha. If I became unable to continue on Alpha, I would stop and request a new clearance.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Moving after a request without hearing a clear approval.  
  ✔ Wait for explicit approval; ask again if unsure.

- ❌ Reading back only "approved" without route and hold-short conditions.  
  ✔ Read back the full approved clearance with restrictions.

- ❌ Switching to a different taxiway after approval of another route.  
  ✔ Follow the approved path or request a change.

- ❌ Assuming hover taxi is included in a generic taxi approval.  
  ✔ Request hover taxi specifically when required.

- Approved → uh-PROOVD
- Hold → HOLD
- Short → short
- Runway → RUN-way

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**APPROVED** — **A**sk clearly, **P**ause for ATC answer, **P**ick up all conditions, **R**ead back fully, **O**bey exact route, **V**erify hold-shorts, **E**scalate if unable, **D**on't assume silence means yes.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Approved taxi means ATC accepted your plan — or gave you a better one.

Hear the approval. Capture every condition. Move only on that contract.

In H130 operations, request the taxi mode you need and fly the route you were given.

No assumptions. No shortcuts. No silent changes.

That is professional ground movement.
