# Stick Shaker

**Catalog ID:** 0397  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Stick Shaker — the tactile stall warning that vibrates the control column when the aircraft approaches a critical angle of attack.

Stick Shaker language appears frequently in ICAO English exams and airline phraseology, even for helicopter pilots who must demonstrate fixed-wing system vocabulary.

The H130 does not use a stick shaker. Captain Delta still requires you to speak this concept fluently for Part 2 and Part 3 exams, and to map the same urgency onto helicopter low-energy and low-rotor-RPM emergencies in Brazilian operations.

Understanding how to report stick shaker activation, recovery, and intentions is essential professional English.

## 📖 Meaning

Stick Shaker is a stall warning device that vibrates the control column when the aircraft approaches a critical angle of attack, providing a tactile cue that aerodynamic stall is imminent and immediate recovery is required.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Stick shaker / agitador do manche.

Dispositivo de aviso de estol que vibra a coluna de comando quando a aeronave se aproxima de um ângulo de ataque crítico, fornecendo um alerta tátil de que o estol aerodinâmico é iminente e a recuperação imediata é necessária.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the Stick Shaker activates, the crew should:

- reduce angle of attack immediately — lower the nose
- apply maximum appropriate thrust or power
- roll wings level, then recover to a safe climb or flight path
- disconnect autopilot if it is fighting the recovery
- advise ATC once control is restored: stick shaker, recovering, going around

Stick Shaker reporting commonly includes:

- stick shaker on approach or climb
- recovering, going around
- request altitude block or vectors
- pan-pan or mayday if stall develops

**Important:** Stick shaker means you are at the edge. Do not pull against it. Reduce angle of attack first. In the H130, treat equivalent low-energy cues with the same priority even without a shaker.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to Stick Shaker reports to:

- clear conflicting traffic from the recovery path
- issue immediate go-around or climb instructions
- protect the missed approach corridor
- coordinate emergency return or diversion
- alert following aircraft to possible missed approach

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are briefing a Part 2 oral scenario at Congonhas while operating H130 IFR training flights in the São Paulo terminal area.

The examiner asks you to demonstrate stick shaker phraseology as if on a jet approach.

You transmit the standard call:

Congonhas Tower, ANAC123, going around, stick shaker, recovering, request climb two thousand.

Tower acknowledges and clears the missed approach. You explain to the examiner that the H130 has no stick shaker, but the communication standard is identical for any critical low-energy recovery.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger stick shaker, cleared go-around, climb and maintain two thousand.
2. Approach: ANAC123, report when recovered, say intentions.
3. Tower: ANAC123, traffic on final advised, expect delay for your return.
4. Approach: ANAC123, turn right heading zero niner zero, climb three thousand.
5. Control: ANAC123, roger pan-pan, radar vectors available, advise when ready.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Stick shaker is exam English you must own — even in a helicopter.

ICAO raters expect the vocabulary: stick shaker, stall warning, recovering, going around. If you freeze on the term because "we fly H130," you lose points and, worse, you lose a communication tool.

Map it: stick shaker equals critical angle of attack or critical energy state. In the H130, low rotor RPM or settling with power demands the same sequence — fly, recover, then call.

Do not invent long stories on frequency. Three elements: what happened, what you are doing, what you need. Stick shaker. Recovering. Request climb.

Brazilian ops around Congonhas and Santos Dumont leave little margin on short final. If energy collapses, go around and say why.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Explain stick shaker and what you would say to ATC if it activates on approach.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Stick shaker is a tactile stall warning that vibrates the control column near critical angle of attack. I would reduce angle of attack, add power, and go around. Then I would say: ANAC123, going around, stick shaker, recovering, request climb two thousand. On the H130 there is no stick shaker, but I would use the same urgency and clear English for any critical low-energy recovery.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "the stick is shaking" instead of standard phraseology.  
  ✔ Use: stick shaker, recovering, going around.

- ❌ Claiming the topic is irrelevant because the H130 has no shaker.  
  ✔ Learn the exam English, then map it to helicopter energy emergencies.

- ❌ Pulling back against the shaker to hold the glidepath.  
  ✔ Reduce angle of attack first. Glidepath is secondary to flying speed.

- ❌ Omitting the reason on the go-around call.  
  ✔ State stick shaker so ATC understands the urgency.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Going Around, Stick Shaker, Recovering

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, GO-ing uh-ROUND, STIK SHAY-ker, ree-KUH-vuh-ring

**Word Stress**

- Stick → STIK
- Shaker → SHAY-ker (stress on first)
- Recovering → ree-KUH-vuh-ring

Practice:

Stick shaker... recovering... going around.

Together:

ANAC123, going around, stick shaker, recovering, request climb two thousand.
