# Hover In Ground Effect

**Catalog ID:** 0428  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Hover In Ground Effect — HIGE — a stationary hover within the cushion of air between the rotor and the surface, requiring less power than a hover out of ground effect.

HIGE is the standard H130 hover for surface helipads, training areas, and many confined-site landings across Brazil when surface and obstacles allow.

You must describe HIGE intentions, surface hazards, and transitions to landing or takeoff in ICAO English for Parts 2 and 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Hover In Ground Effect is a helicopter hover performed at a height where the rotor system benefits from ground cushion, reducing the power required to remain stationary relative to the surface compared with a hover out of ground effect.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pairamento no efeito solo / Hover In Ground Effect (HIGE).

Pairamento de helicóptero realizado a uma altura em que o sistema de rotor se beneficia do colchão de ar do solo, reduzindo a potência necessária para permanecer estacionário em relação à superfície em comparação com o pairamento fora do efeito solo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When establishing a HIGE, the pilot should:

- confirm surface suitable for ground-effect cushion (not tall grass, water spray, or soft dust that destroys the cushion)
- set hover height within ground-effect range per AFM and training standards
- use the power saving to improve control margin
- watch for recirculation, brownout, or whiteout that can erase visual cues
- transition deliberately to landing or to a climb out of ground effect

HIGE reporting commonly includes:

- established hover in ground effect
- landing from hover
- lifting from hover, remaining in ground effect / climbing out
- unable to maintain hover, going around

**Important:** Ground effect is not magic. Soft surfaces, slopes, and recirculation can cancel the cushion. If visual references disappear, depart before you lose control.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use HIGE-related information to:

- know a helicopter is holding near the surface versus translating
- protect the hover area from ground vehicles and other aircraft
- sequence departures after a helicopter lifts from hover
- support training patterns that include prolonged HIGE
- coordinate emergency or HEMS surface positioning

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You arrive at a surface helipad in your H130. Wind is steady. Surface is firm.

You establish a stable hover in ground effect, check pad clear, then land.

You transmit when required:

Established hover in ground effect, landing.

On departure you lift to a HIGE, confirm instruments and controls, then climb and depart.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, report established in hover, pad clear.
2. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land from hover.
3. Tower: ANAC123, lift when ready, report airborne.
4. Tower: ANAC123, hold hover, vehicle approaching, expect landing shortly.
5. Tower: ANAC123, cleared departure, climb on course.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

HIGE is your friend — until the surface lies to you.

Firm, flat, clear: ground effect gives power margin. Tall grass, loose dust, sloping ground: the cushion weakens or your eyes lose the world.

In the H130, use HIGE for the final check before skids down. Do not linger in recirculating dust. Part 3 loves brownout: if you lose visual references in the hover, go around on instruments and briefed escape heading.

Brazilian dry-season pads and beach operations can destroy HIGE visual cues in seconds. Brief the surface. Brief the out.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You are in a hover in ground effect and dust obscures the surface. What do you say and do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would initiate a go-around on the briefed escape path before losing control references and transmit: unable to maintain hover, dust obscuring surface, going around. I would not sit in a brownout hoping for a glimpse of the ground. After conditions improve or I select a better surface, I would request another approach.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Assuming HIGE always needs less power regardless of surface.  
  ✔ Soft or irregular surfaces can cancel ground-effect benefit.

- ❌ Staying in dusty HIGE too long.  
  ✔ Depart early when visual cues degrade.

- ❌ Not reporting holding hover when blocking the pad approach.  
  ✔ Advise ATC: holding hover, standing by.

- ❌ Climbing abruptly from HIGE without checking power for HOGE.  
  ✔ Brief the transition; power required rises out of ground effect.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

HIGE saves power. It does not save poor judgment.

Use the cushion. Protect the visual picture. Land when stable. Leave when the surface disappears.

In the H130, hover in ground effect is the standard gate before touchdown — short, precise, and honest.

Clear English. Clear escape. Clean landing.
