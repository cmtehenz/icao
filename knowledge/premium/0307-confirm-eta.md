# Confirm ETA

**Catalog ID:** 0307  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Confirm ETA — the ATC request for the crew to state or verify the estimated time of arrival at a fix, destination, or reporting point. Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: give a clear, realistic ETA and update it when the estimate changes.

Confirm ETA is High priority because controllers use arrival estimates to sequence traffic, coordinate sectors, and plan approach spacing.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for ETA reports under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including time format and fix identification.

Understanding how to confirm ETA in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Confirm ETA is an ATC request for the pilot to state or verify the estimated time of arrival at a specified point — destination, fix, or reporting point — usually in hours and minutes UTC, so that ATC can plan sequencing, coordination, and traffic flow.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Confirmar ETA / Confirm ETA (Estimated Time of Arrival).

Solicitação do controle para que o piloto informe ou verifique o horário estimado de chegada a um ponto especificado — destino, fix ou ponto de reporte — geralmente em horas e minutos UTC, para planejamento de sequenciamento, coordenação e fluxo de tráfego.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC asks you to confirm ETA, the crew should:

- identify the point ATC means: destination, IAF, or named fix
- compute ETA from current ground speed and remaining distance
- report time in UTC hours and minutes, clearly and slowly
- update ATC if the ETA changes by a significant amount
- for H130: account for wind, holding, and slower cruise than fixed-wing traffic
- if unsure of the point, ask: confirm ETA to which point?

ETA exchanges commonly include:

- confirm ETA / say ETA
- ETA [time] / estimating [fix] at [time]
- revise ETA / new ETA
- ETA destination / ETA [fix]
- time check if clocks disagree

**Important:** An ETA is an estimate, not a clearance. Still, a careless ETA can disrupt sequencing. Recalculate when speed or routing changes.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers request ETA confirmation to:

- sequence arrivals into terminal airspace
- coordinate handoffs between sectors and approach units
- plan holding and delay absorption
- compare your estimate with radar-derived timing
- manage mixed fixed-wing and helicopter traffic flows in Brazil

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 en route from Campinas to Congonhas. Approach needs timing for sequencing.

Approach: ANAC123, confirm ETA Congonhas.

You check ground speed and distance, then reply:

ANAC123, ETA Congonhas one five three zero.

If wind shifts and you will be five minutes late, you update:

ANAC123, revise ETA Congonhas one five three five.

ATC uses that time to fit you into the arrival stream. A silent wrong ETA helps nobody.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, confirm ETA destination.
2. Control: ANAC123, say ETA Campo de Marte.
3. Approach: ANAC123, confirm ETA initial approach fix.
4. Radar: ANAC123, revise ETA when able.
5. Tower: ANAC123, confirm ETA runway threshold.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

ETA is a planning number you owe ATC — not a guess you invent under pressure.

Look at distance and ground speed. Round honestly. Speak the time digit by digit. If the estimate moves, say so.

In the H130, you often fly slower than the jets around you. Do not copy a jet-like ETA that you cannot meet. Brazilian approach units sequence mixed traffic; your realistic time protects spacing.

Part 2 wants clarity: point plus time. Not a long story about wind.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC asks you to confirm ETA to destination while you are still en route. How do you respond, and when would you revise it?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would calculate ETA from remaining distance and current ground speed, then report the destination and the time in UTC hours and minutes. If wind, routing, or holding changes the estimate significantly, I would call again with a revised ETA. If I am unsure which point ATC means, I would ask for confirmation of the fix or destination before giving a time.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Giving local time instead of UTC.  
  ✔ Use UTC hours and minutes unless ATC specifies otherwise.

- ❌ Saying "about fifteen thirty" without the point.  
  ✔ State the fix or destination with the ETA.

- ❌ Never updating after a big speed or route change.  
  ✔ Revise ETA when the estimate is no longer valid.

- ❌ Inventing a precise time without checking the instruments.  
  ✔ Compute from ground speed and distance first.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are fifty miles from Congonhas at a steady ground speed. Approach asks for ETA, then wind slows you.

**Your first task**

Approach says: confirm ETA Congonhas. What do you reply?

**Your second task**

Your new estimate is five minutes later. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, ETA Congonhas one five three zero — using the computed UTC time. Second task: ANAC123, revise ETA Congonhas one five three five. Keep the point clear and the time digit by digit; do not leave ATC with the old estimate.
