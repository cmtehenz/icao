# Hot Refueling

**Catalog ID:** 0439  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Hot Refueling — refueling with engines and/or rotors running, used to minimize turnaround time in operational missions while accepting elevated fire and procedural risk.

Hot refueling is common H130 mission support for HEMS, offshore shuttle, and utility work in Brazil when cold shutdown turnaround is not practical.

You must communicate pad status, hot refuel intentions, and emergencies in ICAO English for Parts 2 and 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Hot Refueling is the process of adding fuel to a helicopter while one or more engines are running and, in many procedures, with rotors turning, conducted under strict safety protocols to reduce ground time while managing fire, FOD, and personnel hazards.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Reabastecimento a quente / hot refueling.

Processo de abastecer um helicóptero com um ou mais motores em funcionamento e, em muitos procedimentos, com os rotores girando, conduzido sob protocolos rigorosos de segurança para reduzir o tempo no solo enquanto se gerenciam riscos de fogo, FOD e pessoal.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When conducting hot refueling, the pilot should:

- confirm company and AFM authorization for hot refuel
- brief crew and refuel team on signals, abort, and fire actions
- keep unnecessary personnel clear of the rotor disc
- monitor for fuel spills, fire, and improper bonding/grounding
- be ready to lift, shut down, or evacuate per emergency procedure

Hot refuel reporting commonly includes:

- on pad, rotors running, request hot refuel
- hot refuel in progress, remaining on frequency
- aborting hot refuel, fuel spill / fire, request fire services
- refuel complete, ready for departure

**Important:** Hot refuel is a controlled exception, not a casual habit. If wind, thunderstorms, or equipment make it unsafe, shut down and cold refuel. Fire services awareness matters.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers support hot refueling by:

- knowing the helicopter will occupy the pad with rotors turning
- keeping other traffic and vehicles clear
- responding immediately to fire or spill emergencies
- sequencing departure after refuel complete
- coordinating alternate pads if hot refuel is aborted

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You land at a staging helipad in your H130 for a quick turn. Company procedure allows hot refuel.

You advise:

On pad, rotors running, hot refuel in progress, will advise ready for departure.

A fuel spill occurs. You abort per procedure and transmit:

Aborting hot refuel, fuel spill, request fire services, shutting down as required.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger on pad, hot refuel approved as far as ATC is concerned, advise complete.
2. Tower: ANAC123, keep this frequency, report ready for departure.
3. Tower: ANAC123, roger spill, fire services rolling, say souls on board.
4. Tower: ANAC123, area cleared, advise shutdown status.
5. Tower: ANAC123, roger refuel complete, cleared departure when ready.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Hot refuel buys time. It spends safety margin.

Rotors turning. Fuel flowing. People near the disc. That is a high-stakes ground operation.

In the H130, follow company hot-refuel SOP exactly — signals, bonding, abort criteria. Part 3 loves: fuel spill during hot refuel — what do you do? Answer: abort refuel, execute emergency actions, request fire services, communicate clearly.

If the weather or site is wrong, say no. Cold refuel is always available. Ego about schedule is not.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

During hot refuel you see a fuel spill near the aircraft. What do you say and do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would abort the hot refuel immediately per company procedure, stop fuel flow, and carry out fire-prevention and shutdown or evacuation actions as required. I would transmit: aborting hot refuel, fuel spill, request fire services. I would keep non-essential people clear and follow the emergency checklist before considering further flight.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Hot refueling without a briefed abort and fire plan.  
  ✔ Brief signals and emergencies before fuel flows.

- ❌ Not telling ATC the aircraft will remain rotors-running on the pad.  
  ✔ Advise hot refuel in progress.

- ❌ Continuing fuel flow after a spill "to finish quickly."  
  ✔ Abort immediately. Request fire services.

- ❌ Allowing passengers to walk near the disc during hot refuel.  
  ✔ Control personnel strictly.
