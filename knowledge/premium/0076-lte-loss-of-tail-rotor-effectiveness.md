# LTE (Loss of Tail Rotor Effectiveness)

**Catalog ID:** 0076  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers LTE — Loss of Tail Rotor Effectiveness.

LTE is an uncommanded yaw that occurs when the tail rotor cannot produce enough thrust to counteract main rotor torque.

In the H130, LTE is most likely in high-power, low-airspeed flight with a left crosswind or during a right turn at the hover.

You must recognize the early yaw, apply recovery technique immediately, then communicate with Mayday if control is lost.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

LTE (Loss of Tail Rotor Effectiveness) is an uncommanded yaw caused by insufficient tail rotor thrust to counteract main rotor torque in certain wind and power conditions.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Perda de efetividade do rotor de cauda (LTE).

Guinada não comandada causada por empuxo insuficiente do rotor de cauda para contrapor o torque do rotor principal.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When LTE occurs, the pilot should:

- recognize uncommanded yaw early — usually to the right in American helicopters
- apply full left pedal immediately
- reduce power if altitude permits
- accelerate into forward flight to restore relative airflow over the tail rotor
- declare Mayday if yaw cannot be controlled

LTE reporting commonly includes:

- loss of tail rotor effectiveness or uncommanded yaw
- recovering or unable to control yaw
- request immediate landing
- souls on board and position

**Important:** LTE is not the same as complete tail rotor drive failure. Recovery is often possible if recognized early. Aviate first — then communicate.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to LTE declarations to:

- clear traffic from the aircraft's route immediately
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable landing area
- coordinate emergency services on the ground
- request souls on board and exact position
- prepare for possible uncontrolled yaw or emergency landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are hovering at fifty feet near a construction site outside Joinville in your H130.

A left crosswind develops and the helicopter begins an uncommanded right yaw.

You apply full left pedal, reduce collective slightly, and accelerate into forward flight.

Yaw control returns as airspeed increases.

You transmit:

Pan Pan Pan Pan, Joinville Tower, ANAC123, loss of tail rotor effectiveness, recovering, request landing helipad.

Tower clears traffic and you land safely for inspection.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say nature of problem and souls on board.
2. Tower: ANAC123, cleared direct helipad, all traffic cleared.
3. Approach: ANAC123, radar contact, say intentions.
4. Tower: ANAC123, wind two seven zero at one five knots, cleared to land any area.
5. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services standing by.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

LTE is a trap at the hover.

High power. Low airspeed. Wrong wind.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Pedal. Power. Airspeed.

Full left pedal immediately.

Reduce power if altitude allows.

Accelerate into forward flight.

Relative airflow restores tail rotor effectiveness.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "perdeu o pedal" or "girando" on frequency.

Use loss of tail rotor effectiveness or uncommanded yaw — ICAO standard terms.

Know the difference:

LTE — aerodynamic loss of effectiveness — often recoverable.

Tail rotor failure — mechanical drive loss — Mayday and procedure.

In the H130, avoid prolonged high-power hover with left crosswind when possible.

Train recognition until the first yaw twitch triggers pedal — not panic.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is LTE and what would you tell ATC if it occurred?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

LTE is loss of tail rotor effectiveness — an uncommanded yaw when the tail rotor cannot counteract main rotor torque. I would apply full left pedal, reduce power if altitude permits, and accelerate into forward flight. I would declare Pan Pan or Mayday depending on severity, report loss of tail rotor effectiveness, state my position and souls on board, and request immediate landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Confusing LTE with complete tail rotor drive failure.  
  ✔ LTE is often recoverable with pedal, power reduction, and forward flight.

- ❌ Saying "perdeu o rotor de cauda" for LTE.  
  ✔ Use loss of tail rotor effectiveness — different from tail rotor failure.

- ❌ Raising collective when yaw begins.  
  ✔ Reduce power if altitude permits — more power worsens the yaw.

- ❌ Delaying the radio call until after recovery.  
  ✔ Declare Pan Pan or Mayday as soon as you can speak from a stable aircraft.
