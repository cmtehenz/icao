# Taxiway Closure

**Catalog ID:** 0228  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Taxiway Closure — the temporary or planned unavailability of a taxiway or portion of a taxiway for aircraft movement, requiring alternate taxi routing issued by ATC and published by NOTAM or ATIS when applicable.

A closed taxiway changes the ground picture. The yellow line you always use may be blocked by cones, barriers, or works. ATC will issue a revised route. Your job is to read it back correctly, follow the cleared route, and not enter the closed segment.

For H130 helicopter operations at Brazilian airports, taxiway closures affect ground taxi and air-taxi routes, hover-taxi corridors, and access to ramps and helipads. Rotor clearance near works and barriers matters. Academy crews must treat closures as hard boundaries unless ATC issues a specific routing around or across an authorized segment.

This lesson builds Part 2 proficiency in taxiway closure phraseology, readbacks, and safe alternate routing.

## 📖 Meaning

A Taxiway Closure is the formal withdrawal of a taxiway, or a defined portion of a taxiway, from use for aircraft taxi operations due to maintenance, construction, surface damage, equipment, or other restriction, notified by NOTAM, ATIS, and ATC, with alternate taxi routes provided as required.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a taxiway is closed, the crew should:

- review airport NOTAMs for taxiway closures before pushback or after landing
- copy and read back the full alternate taxi route
- identify closed segments on the airport diagram before moving
- not enter a closed taxiway even if it appears empty
- request progressive taxi if the alternate route is complex or unfamiliar
- for helicopters, assess hover-taxi clearance relative to barriers, cranes, and works

Taxiway closure commonly involves:

- resurfacing and construction zones
- lighting or guidance sign unserviceability
- FOD or pavement failure
- temporary barriers for apron expansion
- partial closure (for example, Alpha between Bravo and Charlie closed)

**Important:** Partial closures are easy to miss. "Taxiway Alpha closed between Bravo and Charlie" still allows Alpha elsewhere. Read the whole restriction. In the H130, do not air-taxi over a closed taxiway as a shortcut unless ATC explicitly clears that path — closure protects people and equipment on the surface.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers manage taxiway closures to:

- keep aircraft clear of works, holes, and unserviceable pavement
- issue unambiguous alternate routes on the maneuvering area
- protect construction personnel and vehicles
- prevent ground conflicts when standard routings are blocked
- update ATIS and coordinate with apron management

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You have landed at Guarulhos and expect the usual route: vacate via Bravo, taxi Alpha to the apron.

Ground: taxi to the apron via Charlie and Delta, taxiway Alpha closed between Bravo and the apron, works in progress.

You read back the full route. You open the airport diagram, find the closed segment, and taxi only where cleared. You see cones on Alpha. You do not cut through.

At the apron you shut down. The closure added two minutes and removed ambiguity. That is how taxiway closures work when the crew treats the diagram and the clearance as one plan.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, taxi to the apron via Charlie and Delta, taxiway Alpha closed, works in progress.
2. Ground: ANAC123, taxi holding point runway one seven Right via Bravo, taxiway Charlie closed between Alpha and Bravo.
3. Tower: ANAC123, vacate via Delta, Alpha unavailable, taxiway closed.
4. Ground: ANAC123, give way to opposite traffic on Echo, taxiway Foxtrot closed.
5. Ground: ANAC123, air-taxi to the helipad via the eastern taxi lane, western taxiway closed.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, taxi apron via Charlie and Delta, Alpha closed.
2. ANAC123, taxi holding point runway one seven Right via Bravo, Charlie closed between Alpha and Bravo.
3. ANAC123, vacating via Delta, Alpha closed.
4. ANAC123, giving way on Echo, Foxtrot closed.
5. ANAC123, air-taxi helipad via eastern taxi lane, western taxiway closed.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Taxiway closure is a routing problem, not a debate.

Copy the whole clearance. Partial closures have boundaries — between which intersections matters. If you are unsure, ask for progressive taxi. Confusion on the ground creates runway incursions and conflict with works.

In the H130, barriers and cranes near a closed taxiway are rotor hazards. Slow the taxi. Widen the scan. Do not hover-taxi across a closed segment to save time.

Build the diagram into the brief after landing or before taxi out. If ATIS or NOTAM listed a closure, expect Ground to route you around it. If Ground's route surprises you, stop and clarify before you move.

Closed pavement is not a shortcut. It is a boundary.

After landing, expect Ground to change the route you briefed in cruise. Listen fully before you release the brakes. A partial hear-back of "taxi to the apron" without the via-points is how crews wander into closed Alpha.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Ground advises that your usual taxiway is closed and issues a longer alternate route. Describe what you do before you move the aircraft.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would read back the full alternate taxi route, identify the closed taxiway segment on the airport diagram, and confirm I understand where I must not enter. If the route is complex, I would request progressive taxi. Only after I am oriented would I begin taxi, remaining clear of the closed portion and complying with any hold-short or give-way instructions along the new route.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Entering a closed taxiway because the cones are only on one side.  
  ✔ Treat any marked or stated closure as a hard no-entry unless cleared otherwise.

- ❌ Reading back the destination but omitting the alternate route.  
  ✔ Read back the full routing, including the closed-taxiway element when given.

- ❌ Missing partial closure limits ("between Bravo and Charlie").  
  ✔ Note the exact segment. Other parts of the same taxiway may remain open.

- ❌ Air-taxiing over closed works as a shortcut in the helicopter.  
  ✔ Follow the cleared route. Closure protects people and equipment below.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

After landing at Santos Dumont, you expect taxiway Alpha to the apron. Ground advises Alpha is closed between the runway exit and the apron.

**Your first task**

Ground says: taxi to the apron via Bravo and Charlie, taxiway Alpha closed. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, taxi apron via Bravo and Charlie, Alpha closed. Second task: ANAC123, request progressive taxi — or ANAC123, confirm route to the apron via Charlie. I would stop or slow if needed rather than enter the wrong pavement or the closed Alpha segment.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Taxiway closures rewrite the ground map for that day.

Read the NOTAM. Copy the route. Find the closed segment on the diagram. Taxi only where cleared.

In the H130, treat barriers and works as rotor hazards, not scenery.

If the route is unclear, ask. Progressive taxi is professional. Guessing is how aircraft meet cones — or worse.

Closed taxiway. Alternate route. Full readback. Clear diagram. That is the standard.
