# Cabin Crew

**Catalog ID:** 0344  
**Category:** Crew  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Cabin Crew — the collective term for crew members who work in the passenger cabin, including flight attendants and the flight purser, responsible for cabin safety, passenger care, and emergency procedures.

ICAO English uses cabin crew as the group name. Pilots must know when to say cabin crew versus flight attendant versus flight purser.

For H130 ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, many flights operate without dedicated cabin crew. The vocabulary remains essential for Part 1 comprehension, airline scenarios, and clear answers about who manages passengers in an emergency.

Master: cabin crew, cabin secure, cabin report, passenger safety.

## 📖 Meaning

Cabin Crew are the crew members assigned to the passenger cabin whose primary duties are passenger safety, cabin emergency procedures, and cabin service, operating under cabin leadership and in coordination with the flight crew.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Tripulação de cabine / Cabin Crew.

Membros da tripulação designados à cabine de passageiros cujas funções principais são a segurança dos passageiros, os procedimentos de emergência na cabine e o serviço de bordo, atuando sob liderança de cabine e em coordenação com a tripulação de voo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When referring to cabin crew, the flight crew should:

- use cabin crew for the group; flight attendant / purser for individuals/roles
- brief cabin crew before abnormal landings, diversions, and evacuations
- require cabin secure reports before takeoff and landing when cabin crew are carried
- convert cabin crew information into accurate ATC reports when needed
- avoid sterile cockpit violations except for safety-critical cabin calls

Cabin crew coordination commonly includes:

- cabin secure / not secure
- passenger counts and special assistance
- medical events and smoke/fire cabin reports
- evacuation commands and exit management on airliners

**Important:** "Cabin crew" is not interchangeable with "flight crew." Flight crew means pilots; cabin crew means cabin safety team.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers interact with the flight crew, but cabin crew information drives many calls when:

- medical or smoke events originate in the cabin
- souls on board are confirmed through cabin counts
- evacuation status depends on cabin crew actions
- examiners test group-role vocabulary in English
- pilots must summarize cabin reports without confusion

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

On a multi-crew airliner scenario into Guarulhos, cabin crew complete the secure check and report ready for landing.

Later, cabin crew report a passenger allergic reaction. Pilots declare Pan Pan and request medical assistance.

On an H130 charter with no cabin crew, the pilot briefs passengers directly. For the exam question "Who is responsible for passenger cabin safety on an airliner?" the answer remains cabin crew.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger, say cabin status.
2. Tower: ANAC123, confirm souls on board.
3. Approach: ANAC123, advise if cabin crew request medical assistance on landing.
4. Control: ANAC123, say if evacuation is in progress after landing.
5. Tower: ANAC123, fire services available, advise cabin conditions.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, cabin crew report cabin secure, ready for landing.
2. ANAC123, cabin crew report medical emergency, request priority landing and medical assistance.
3. ANAC123, cabin not secure, request vectors, will call when ready.
4. ANAC123, souls on board confirmed with cabin crew.
5. ANAC123, cabin crew preparing for possible evacuation, request fire services standing by.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Cabin crew is the umbrella term.

Under it: flight attendants and, when assigned, a flight purser.

Brazilian Portuguese: tripulação de cabine. Use cabin crew in ICAO English.

Do not say "the stewards" in a formal exam answer. Do not call pilots cabin crew.

In H130 Brazilian operations, passenger safety may sit entirely with the pilot. Still answer role questions with standard airline vocabulary when the scenario is multi-crew.

When cabin crew speak, listen for safety content: secure, smoke, medical, exits. That content becomes your ATC message if the ground needs to act.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is cabin crew, and how do they support the flight crew in an emergency?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Cabin crew are the crew members responsible for passenger safety and cabin emergency procedures. In an emergency they secure the cabin, manage passengers, report conditions to the flight deck, and carry out commands such as brace or evacuate. The pilots then communicate with ATC using that cabin information.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Calling pilots "cabin crew."  
  ✔ Pilots are flight crew; cabin crew work in the cabin.

- ❌ Using only "comissários" without the group English term.  
  ✔ Prefer cabin crew in ICAO answers.

- ❌ Skipping a cabin secure call when cabin crew report not secure.  
  ✔ Delay landing phase actions until cabin is secure if required.

- ❌ Relaying confused cabin details to ATC.  
  ✔ Summarize: medical emergency / smoke in cabin / cabin secure.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Cabin crew report smoke in the rear cabin on final. You continue for immediate landing.

**Your first task**

What do you tell Tower?

**Your second task**

After landing, cabin crew begin evacuation. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, smoke in cabin reported by cabin crew, request immediate landing and fire services standing by. Second task: ANAC123, stopping on the runway, emergency evacuation, request fire services. I would secure the aircraft and support cabin crew egress commands as applicable.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Cabin crew is the correct group name for the cabin safety team.

Use it when you mean the team. Use flight attendant or purser when you mean the person or the leader.

Know the difference between cabin crew and flight crew.

Whether your H130 carries cabin crew or not, ICAO English still expects the standard terms.

Clear roles. Clear reports. Clear radio.
