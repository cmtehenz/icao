# Maintain Runway Heading

**Catalog ID:** 0012  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the first instructions you'll hear immediately after departure.

Many pilots instinctively want to turn toward their destination after takeoff.

However, if ATC instructs:

"Maintain runway heading."

Your responsibility is simple:

Continue flying the runway heading until another clearance is issued.

This instruction is extremely common during IFR departures and at busy controlled airports where aircraft must be separated immediately after takeoff.

## 📖 Meaning

Maintain Runway Heading means continue flying the magnetic heading of the departure runway after takeoff until ATC gives another instruction.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Mantenha o rumo da pista.

Após a decolagem, continue voando no rumo magnético da pista até receber nova autorização.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC instructs Maintain Runway Heading, the pilot must continue climbing while maintaining the magnetic heading of the departure runway.

This instruction does not mean:

- follow the runway centerline over the ground
- turn toward the next waypoint
- intercept the flight plan

It simply means:

Keep the assigned heading until ATC tells you otherwise.

Remember:

Heading is the direction the aircraft's nose is pointing.

Strong crosswinds may cause the aircraft's ground track to differ from the runway centerline.

This is expected.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Maintain Runway Heading to:

- establish radar separation
- sequence departures safely
- avoid conflicts with arriving traffic
- position aircraft for radar vectors
- simplify departure procedures
- reduce controller workload

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're departing Navegantes Airport on runway 07 in your H130.

Tower clears you for takeoff:

Tower: ANAC123, runway zero seven, cleared for takeoff. Maintain runway heading.

A crosswind from the south begins pushing the helicopter sideways.

Instead of correcting toward your planned GPS route, you continue flying the assigned magnetic heading.

About one minute later, Departure transmits:

Departure: ANAC123, turn right heading one two zero.

Only now do you begin the turn.

Until that instruction, your only responsibility was to maintain the runway heading.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, runway zero seven, cleared for takeoff. Maintain runway heading.
2. Departure: ANAC123, maintain runway heading, climb and maintain three thousand.
3. Departure: ANAC123, maintain runway heading, expect radar vectors.
4. Departure: ANAC123, maintain runway heading until advised.
5. Departure: ANAC123, maintain runway heading, contact Departure on one two four decimal seven.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Cleared for takeoff, maintaining runway heading, ANAC123.
2. Maintaining runway heading, climbing three thousand, ANAC123.
3. Maintaining runway heading, expecting radar vectors, ANAC123.
4. Maintaining runway heading until advised, ANAC123.
5. Maintaining runway heading, switching to Departure one two four decimal seven, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine you're leaving a hospital parking lot.

The exit lane is narrow and carefully designed to keep every vehicle separated from incoming traffic.

If you decide to turn early because you know where you're going, you could create a dangerous conflict.

That's exactly why ATC sometimes says:

Maintain runway heading.

The controller already has the complete traffic picture.

There may be another aircraft climbing nearby.

Another aircraft may be approaching to land.

Or another departure may be joining a different route.

Your destination isn't the priority yet.

Safe separation is.

Professional pilots don't anticipate turns.

They wait for the clearance.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Why would ATC instruct an aircraft to maintain runway heading after departure?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

ATC may instruct an aircraft to maintain runway heading to ensure safe separation from other traffic, simplify departure sequencing, and position the aircraft for radar vectors or another clearance. The pilot should maintain the assigned heading until receiving further instructions.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Turning toward the first waypoint immediately after takeoff.  
  ✔ Wait for another ATC instruction.

- ❌ Thinking runway heading means following the runway centerline exactly.  
  ✔ Runway heading refers to magnetic heading. Wind may cause drift.

- ❌ Returning to the GPS route automatically.  
  ✔ Continue flying the assigned heading until ATC changes it.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Maintaining runway heading, ANAC123.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Maintain Runway Heading

**Pronunciation:** main-TAIN RUN-way HED-ing

**Word Stress**

- Maintain → stress the second syllable
- Runway → stress the first syllable
- Heading → stress the first syllable

Practice:

Maintain... Runway... Heading...

Now naturally:

Maintaining runway heading.

Then practice the full readback:

Cleared for takeoff, maintaining runway heading, ANAC123.

Keep a steady rhythm and pronounce heading clearly, without dropping the final "-ing."

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Imagine you're leaving an airport parking garage.

There are arrows painted on the exit lane.

You don't choose your own direction until you're safely outside.

ATC is doing the same thing.

The runway heading is your exit lane.

Follow it until the controller tells you it's time to turn.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

Tower: ANAC123, runway zero seven, cleared for takeoff. Maintain runway heading.

**Your first task**

Read back the complete clearance exactly as a professional pilot.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

Why shouldn't a pilot turn toward the destination immediately after takeoff when instructed to maintain runway heading?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Because ATC is using runway heading to maintain safe separation from other aircraft. I should continue flying the assigned magnetic heading until ATC issues another instruction, such as a turn, radar vectors, or resume own navigation.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Today's lesson reinforces one of the most important habits after departure.

After takeoff, don't think about where you want to go.

Think about what you've been cleared to do.

A professional pilot follows the clearance first and the flight plan second.

Remember this sequence:

Takeoff → Maintain Runway Heading → New ATC Instruction → Turn → Continue the Flight

Safe departures begin with disciplined flying, and disciplined flying begins by following every clearance exactly as issued.
