# Food Poisoning

**Catalog ID:** 0331  
**Category:** Passenger  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Food Poisoning — an in-flight illness, usually from contaminated food or drink, causing nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or weakness in one or more passengers.

Food Poisoning is Medium priority because many cases are uncomfortable rather than immediately life-threatening. Still, ICAO exams expect clear English for medical problem reporting, possible diversion, and medical assistance when symptoms are severe or multiple passengers are affected.

For H130 operations, passengers may have eaten before boarding rather than from an airline galley. The radio English is the same: medical problem, suspected food poisoning, assistance required. Multiple sick passengers can degrade the cabin environment quickly in a small helicopter.

Understanding how to communicate food poisoning in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Food Poisoning is illness believed to be caused by contaminated food or drink, typically producing gastrointestinal symptoms that may require in-flight care, medical assistance on arrival, or diversion if symptoms become severe.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Intoxicação alimentar / Food Poisoning.

Doença acreditada ser causada por alimento ou bebida contaminados, geralmente com sintomas gastrointestinais que podem exigir cuidados a bordo, assistência médica na chegada ou desvio se os sintomas se agravarem.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When food poisoning is suspected, the crew should:

- maintain normal safe flight unless symptoms become critical
- assist affected passengers and manage hygiene as far as practical
- assess whether one passenger or several are affected
- advise ATC if medical assistance is needed on arrival
- request priority landing or diversion only if the condition becomes serious — dehydration, fainting, or severe distress

Food Poisoning communication commonly includes:

- medical problem on board, suspected food poisoning
- passenger with severe vomiting, request medical assistance
- several passengers ill, suspected food poisoning
- request priority if condition worsens

**Important:** Medium priority does not mean ignore it. On the H130, vomiting in a confined cabin is an operational distraction. Secure the passenger, ventilate if procedures allow, and fly the aircraft. If the pilot ate the same food and feels ill, treat that as a developing incapacitation risk and land sooner.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to food poisoning reports to:

- arrange medical assistance when requested
- coordinate diversion if the pilot elects to divert
- understand that the issue is medical, not technical
- request number of persons affected and souls on board
- alert airport services for passenger care after landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are en route from Curitiba toward Navegantes in the H130.

Two passengers become severely nauseated and start vomiting. They say they ate the same meal before departure.

You maintain cruise and transmit:

Approach, ANAC123, medical problem on board, two passengers suspected food poisoning, request medical assistance on arrival.

Approach notes the request. You do not need Pan Pan yet — symptoms are unpleasant but stable. On landing, medical staff evaluate the passengers. If either had fainted or worsened, you would have escalated.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger, say number of passengers affected and assistance required.
2. Approach: ANAC123, medical services will meet you on arrival.
3. Tower: ANAC123, after landing expect medical team at parking.
4. Approach: ANAC123, confirm if priority landing is required.
5. Control: ANAC123, diversion approved if required, say intentions.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, medical problem on board, suspected food poisoning, request medical assistance on arrival.
2. ANAC123, two passengers ill, suspected food poisoning, souls on board five.
3. Pan Pan Pan, ANAC123, passenger severe vomiting and weakness, request priority landing.
4. ANAC123, diverting to Navegantes, medical problem, suspected food poisoning.
5. ANAC123, no priority required at this time, medical meet on arrival please.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Food poisoning teaches proportional communication.

Not every illness is Pan Pan. Not every illness is silence.

Match the radio to the severity. Mild cases: medical assistance on arrival. Severe cases or pilot illness: urgency and priority.

Examiners listen for the phrase suspected food poisoning and a clear request.

In the H130, ask the critical CRM question: did the flying pilot eat the same food? If yes, shorten the flight before you become the emergency.

Keep descriptions short. ATC does not need the menu. They need the medical label and your intention.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

When would suspected food poisoning require Pan Pan and priority landing?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

If a passenger becomes severely weak, faints, shows signs of serious dehydration, or if several passengers are badly affected and I need expedited landing. Also if I, as the pilot, start to feel ill and may become incapacitated. Otherwise I may only request medical assistance on arrival.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Declaring Mayday for mild nausea.  
  ✔ Match urgency level to severity.

- ❌ Saying "passengers have stomachache" with no clear request.  
  ✔ Say suspected food poisoning and request medical assistance if needed.

- ❌ Ignoring pilot exposure to the same food.  
  ✔ Treat pilot illness risk as a flight safety issue.

- ❌ Diverting without telling ATC the reason.  
  ✔ State medical problem and intentions clearly.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**FOOD** — **F**it the urgency to severity, **O**bserve how many are affected, **O**ffer clear request to ATC, **D**ivert or prioritize only if safety or health demands it.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Two passengers have vomiting and abdominal pain. You feel fine. Destination is thirty minutes away.

**Your first task**

What do you say if you only need medical meet-and-assist?

**Your second task**

What changes if you start feeling the same symptoms?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, medical problem on board, two passengers suspected food poisoning, request medical assistance on arrival. Second task: I would declare urgency, request priority landing or diversion, and land as soon as practical because pilot illness can become incapacitation.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Food Poisoning is proportional medicine on the radio.

Label it. Count the affected. Request what you truly need.

On the H130, watch the pilot as carefully as the passengers.

Medium priority — until it is not.
