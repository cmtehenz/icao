# Allergic Reaction

**Catalog ID:** 0338  
**Category:** Passenger  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Allergic Reaction — a passenger medical event ranging from mild symptoms to anaphylaxis, where swelling, breathing difficulty, or shock may develop rapidly and require priority landing and medical assistance.

ICAO speaking tests expect clear English: allergic reaction, difficulty breathing, request medical assistance — not long clinical explanations.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, cabin first-aid options are limited. If breathing is compromised, time to a suitable airport matters. The crew must decide urgency level, divert if needed, and communicate precisely with ATC.

Master the operational language so controllers can prioritize you and alert ambulances without confusion.

## 📖 Meaning

Allergic Reaction is an adverse immune response to food, medication, insect sting, or other allergen that may cause rash, swelling, breathing difficulty, or anaphylactic shock, treated operationally as a passenger medical emergency when severe.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Reação alérgica / Allergic Reaction.

Resposta adversa do sistema imunológico a alimento, medicamento, picada de inseto ou outro alérgeno que pode causar erupção, inchaço, dificuldade respiratória ou choque anafilático, tratada operacionalmente como emergência médica de passageiro quando grave.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a severe allergic reaction occurs, the crew should:

- obtain cabin status: conscious, breathing, swelling, medication administered if any
- declare Pan Pan for urgency; escalate if the passenger stops breathing or becomes critical
- divert to the nearest suitable airport when breathing difficulty is significant
- request medical assistance and priority landing
- report souls on board and condition updates when asked

Allergic reaction reporting commonly includes:

- passenger allergic reaction
- difficulty breathing / facial swelling
- request priority landing and medical assistance
- passenger improving / condition worsening after treatment

**Important:** Do not debate allergens on frequency. Report the operational problem: allergic reaction with difficulty breathing. Request help. Fly the aircraft.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to allergic reaction reports to:

- provide priority handling when breathing is compromised
- coordinate medical services for possible anaphylaxis
- obtain condition updates that affect diversion urgency
- clear conflicting traffic from the approach
- ensure ambulances meet the aircraft on landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising toward Curitiba when cabin reports a passenger with facial swelling and difficulty breathing after a snack.

You declare Pan Pan, request priority diversion to the nearest suitable airport, and ask for medical assistance.

Approach coordinates ambulances. You land. Medical services meet the aircraft. The passenger is transferred for treatment.

Short updates only: condition stable or worsening. No allergen lecture on frequency.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say if passenger is breathing normally.
2. Approach: ANAC123, divert approved, expect priority vectors, medical services alerted.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, ambulances standing by.
4. Control: ANAC123, say souls on board and assistance required.
5. Tower: ANAC123, vacate when able, medical team will meet you.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, passenger allergic reaction, difficulty breathing, request priority landing and medical assistance.
2. ANAC123, diverting, severe allergic reaction, request ambulances standing by.
3. ANAC123, passenger conscious, breathing difficult, facial swelling.
4. ANAC123, allergic reaction improving after medication, still request medical assistance.
5. ANAC123, on final, medical emergency continuing, request medical team meet aircraft.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Allergic reaction can escalate in minutes. Your English must stay ahead of the escalation.

Mild rash may allow continue. Difficulty breathing is a diversion decision.

Brazilian pilots often say "alergia" or "passou mal da comida." Prefer passenger allergic reaction, difficulty breathing.

If an epinephrine auto-injector or cabin medication is used, you may report passenger treated, condition improving or not improving — still request medical assistance on landing.

In the H130, airflow and cabin volume are limited. A passenger in respiratory distress needs ground care quickly.

Do not ask ATC which hospital is best for allergies. Ask for the nearest suitable airport with medical services and priority landing.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

A passenger develops facial swelling and difficulty breathing. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare Pan Pan, report passenger allergic reaction with difficulty breathing, request priority landing at the nearest suitable airport, and request medical assistance. I would update ATC if the passenger becomes unconscious or stops breathing, and provide souls on board when asked.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "alergia" without describing breathing status.  
  ✔ Report allergic reaction and whether there is difficulty breathing.

- ❌ Continuing to destination with progressive respiratory distress.  
  ✔ Divert when breathing is compromised.

- ❌ Listing every possible food allergen on frequency.  
  ✔ Keep it operational: allergic reaction, assistance required.

- ❌ Cancelling medical assistance because symptoms improve slightly.  
  ✔ Still request medical assessment on landing after a severe reaction.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

A passenger has a severe allergic reaction with difficulty breathing. You are thirty minutes from destination; a suitable airport is ten minutes away.

**Your first task**

Transmit your urgency call and diversion request.

**Your second task**

Approach asks if the passenger is conscious. What do you say if the passenger is conscious but breathing is worsening?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, passenger allergic reaction, difficulty breathing, diverting, request priority landing and medical assistance. Second task: ANAC123, passenger conscious, breathing worsening, request ambulances standing by. I would keep updates short and land as soon as cleared.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Allergic reaction is a race between airway compromise and time to medical care.

Assess breathing. Declare Pan Pan. Divert when needed. Request medical assistance.

Use clear ICAO English — not informal Portuguese alone.

In H130 Brazilian operations, early diversion is often the safest cabin medical decision you can make.

Speak clearly. Land promptly. Hand over to professionals.
