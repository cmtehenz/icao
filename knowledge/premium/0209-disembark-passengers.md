# Disembark Passengers

**Catalog ID:** 0209  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Disembark Passengers — the process and radio English for allowing passengers to leave the aircraft after landing or when a flight is interrupted on the ground.

Disembarkation is not only cabin service language. Pilots coordinate with ATC and apron when passengers must leave at a non-standard time, during a diversion, after an aborted departure, or when rotors are still turning and safety conditions must be confirmed.

For H130 helicopter training academy and passenger transfer operations, disembark passengers is highly relevant. The H130 often lands at helipads, hospital pads, and busy Brazilian aprons where passengers step out under rotor wash risk. Clear English protects people on the ground.

Understanding how to request time to disembark, report passengers clear, and coordinate with Ground in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency and real academy operations.

## 📖 Meaning

Disembark passengers means to allow passengers to leave the aircraft after arrival or during a ground stop, normally after the aircraft is parked, secured, and the area is safe for people to walk clear of rotors, propellers, and jet blast.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Desembarque de passageiros / disembark passengers.

Permitir que os passageiros deixem a aeronave após a chegada ou durante uma parada no solo, normalmente depois que a aeronave está estacionada, assegurada e a área está segura para circulação fora do alcance de rotores, hélices e jato dos motores.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When passengers must disembark, the crew should:

- park or set down at the assigned stand or safe disembarkation point
- complete shutdown or rotor brake procedures as required by the operation
- confirm the area is clear of hazards before opening doors
- advise Ground if extra time on stand is needed for disembarkation
- report passengers disembarked or doors closed when ready to continue
- never allow passengers to walk behind or toward turning rotors

Disembark passengers coordination commonly includes:

- request time to disembark passengers on the stand
- advise unable to taxi — passengers disembarking
- report passengers clear, ready for shutdown or ready to depart again
- request assistance for passenger transfer on the apron
- hold position with rotors running only if procedures and briefing allow hot disembarkation

**Important:** In helicopter operations, "hot" disembarkation with rotors turning requires strict briefing, marshaller control, and a clear safe arc. If in doubt, shut down. Never rush passengers under an H130 main rotor or near the tail rotor. Brazilian hospital and offshore transfers make this vocabulary life-critical.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers need disembarkation status to:

- know how long an aircraft will occupy a stand or pad
- avoid clearing another aircraft into a passenger walk path
- coordinate apron vehicles and ambulances for transfers
- plan taxi sequencing when an arrival will not vacate quickly
- manage diversions where passengers must leave unexpectedly

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You land the H130 at the academy helipad in Florianópolis after a passenger familiarization flight. Ground clears you to the pad and asks you to report when passengers are clear.

You set down, reduce to idle as per SOP, and with a marshaller in place you disembark the passengers along the briefed safe path away from the tail rotor.

When everyone is clear of the rotor disc and walking to the terminal, you report passengers disembarked, ready for shutdown — or ready for the next training lift if that is the plan.

If you had diverted to Congonhas with passengers still on board and needed time on a remote stand, you would request time to disembark and hold taxi clearances until people were safely off and clear of the apron path.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, park on stand two, report when passengers disembarked.
2. Ground: ANAC123, time approved to disembark passengers, advise ready to taxi.
3. Apron: ANAC123, hold position, passengers disembarking on stand four.
4. Ground: ANAC123, confirm passengers clear before startup.
5. Tower: ANAC123, after landing proceed to the pad, disembark as required, contact Ground.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, parking stand two, wilco, report passengers disembarked.
2. ANAC123, time approved to disembark, will advise ready to taxi.
3. ANAC123, holding position, passengers disembarking in sight.
4. ANAC123, passengers clear, ready for startup.
5. ANAC123, proceeding to the pad, disembark as required, contacting Ground.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Passengers are not clear until they are outside the danger area — not merely outside the cabin.

In airplanes, jet blast and propellers matter. In the H130, the main rotor disc and tail rotor define a lethal circle. Disembarkation English must match that reality.

Tell Ground when you need time. Tell Ground when people are clear. Do not accept a taxi or startup clearance into a passenger stream.

If ATC asks whether passengers have disembarked, answer precisely: passengers disembarking, or passengers clear. Vague answers create apron conflicts.

Hot disembarkation is a trained procedure, not a shortcut. If your academy SOP requires shutdown, shut down. Schedule pressure never beats a tail-rotor strike on a passenger.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You have landed and parked. Passengers need to leave before you can accept the next taxi clearance. Explain what you communicate to Ground.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would advise Ground that I need time on the stand to disembark passengers and that I am not ready to taxi. I would keep the aircraft secured according to procedure, ensure a safe path clear of the rotors, and only report ready when all passengers are clear of the aircraft and the apron path. If Ground asks for status, I would say passengers disembarking or passengers clear, ready to continue. I would not move the aircraft while people are still near the rotor disc.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "passengers going down" instead of disembark.  
  ✔ Use standard English: disembark passengers / passengers disembarked / passengers clear.

- ❌ Reporting ready to taxi while passengers are still walking near the aircraft.  
  ✔ Report ready only when passengers are clear of the danger area.

- ❌ Allowing unbriefed hot disembarkation under turning rotors.  
  ✔ Follow SOP — shut down if hot disembarkation is not approved and briefed.

- ❌ Blocking the taxiway to disembark instead of using the stand or pad.  
  ✔ Request parking or pad position; do not disembark on an active taxiway.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Passengers Disembarked, Ready for Shutdown

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, PAS-en-jerz dis-em-BARKT, REH-dee for SHUT-down

**Word Stress**

- Passengers → PAS-en-jerz (stress on first)
- Disembarked → dis-em-BARKT (stress on third)
- Ready → REH-dee
- Shutdown → SHUT-down

Practice:

Passengers disembarked... ready for shutdown.

Together:

ANAC123, passengers disembarked, ready for shutdown on stand two.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**CLEAR** — **C**abin open only when parked, **L**eave rotors safe (stop or briefed hot), **E**xit along the safe arc, **A**dvise Ground of status, **R**eport passengers clear before any movement.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You land the H130 on stand two at Congonhas with three passengers. Ground wants the stand free soon for another helicopter.

**Your first task**

Ground says: "ANAC123, report when passengers disembarked." What do you reply while people are still leaving?

**Your second task**

All passengers are clear of the rotor disc and walking to the terminal. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, passengers disembarking, stand by. Second task: ANAC123, passengers disembarked, ready for shutdown — or ready to vacate if that is the next clearance. I would not report clear until every passenger is outside the danger area.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Disembark passengers is people safety spoken in ICAO English.

Park. Secure. Brief the path. Move people clear. Then report.

In the H130, the rotor disc is the boundary between a normal arrival and a tragedy. Your radio calls must match what your eyes see on the apron.

Need time? Say so. Still disembarking? Say so. Clear? Say so — and mean it.

That is how Brazilian helicopter operations keep passengers safe after the flight ends.
