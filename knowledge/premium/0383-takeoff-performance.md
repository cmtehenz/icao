# Takeoff Performance

**Catalog ID:** 0383  
**Category:** Performance  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Takeoff Performance — the calculated capability of the aircraft to accelerate, lift off, and climb safely from a given runway or helipad under current weight, weather, and obstacle conditions.

Takeoff Performance is not a guess from experience. It is a table-driven decision: go, reduce weight, change runway, or delay.

For H130 operations in Brazil — hot Congonhas afternoons, short pads, and obstacle-rich city environments — takeoff performance limits payload and departure path. You must calculate it and communicate limitations to ATC in ICAO English.

This lesson prepares you for Part 2, Part 3, and Part 4 communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Takeoff Performance is the assessed ability of the aircraft to complete a safe takeoff and initial climb from the departure surface, considering weight, density altitude, wind, surface condition, and obstacle clearance requirements.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Desempenho de decolagem / Takeoff Performance.

Capacidade avaliada da aeronave de completar uma decolagem e subida inicial seguras a partir da superfície de partida, considerando peso, altitude-densidade, vento, condição da superfície e requisitos de afastamento de obstáculos.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When assessing takeoff performance, the crew should:

- calculate weight, density altitude, and wind effect before departure
- verify runway or pad length and obstacle environment
- reduce payload or delay if performance is insufficient
- request full runway or preferred runway when performance-limited
- advise ATC clearly when unable to accept intersection departure

Takeoff Performance planning includes:

- density altitude and OAT
- takeoff weight versus maximum allowable for conditions
- headwind or tailwind component
- wet or contaminated surface effects
- climb gradient and departure procedure obstacles

**Important:** If performance is marginal, do not accept an intersection departure or a downwind runway for convenience. State the limitation and request what you need.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers support takeoff performance by:

- assigning full runway length when requested
- approving runway changes for wind or performance
- avoiding intersection departures for performance-limited aircraft
- sequencing departures that need longer takeoff rolls or specific headings
- relaying temperature and wind accurately for crew calculations

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Hot afternoon at Congonhas. Density altitude is high. Your H130 is near maximum takeoff weight for the conditions.

Ground offers an intersection departure to save time.

You transmit:

São Paulo Congonhas Tower, ANAC123, performance limited, request full length runway one seven, unable intersection departure.

Tower: ANAC123, roger, taxi full length runway one seven, hold short.

You respond:

ANAC123, taxi full length one seven, holding short.

Performance protected by a clear request.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Takeoff performance is decided before you call ready.

If the tables say you need full length, headwind, or less weight — that is the plan. ATC can help with runway and sequencing. They cannot invent performance you do not have.

In Brazilian summer heat, Congonhas and inland fields punish heavy takeoffs. The H130 will hover and climb — until density altitude and weight say otherwise.

Never accept an intersection "to help the flow" when you are performance-limited. Say: unable intersection, request full length. Professional, not difficult.

Brief the departure path for obstacles. Performance is not only ground roll — it is the climb that follows.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Ground offers an intersection departure. Your takeoff performance calculation requires full runway length. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would decline clearly: ANAC123, unable intersection departure, performance limited, request full length runway. I would explain briefly if asked — high density altitude or weight. I would not accept a departure that the performance tables do not support. Clear limitation language helps ATC re-sequence without confusion.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Accepting intersection departure when performance requires full length.  
  ✔ State unable intersection, request full runway available.

- ❌ Calling ready without completing performance calculations.  
  ✔ Calculate weight, density altitude, and obstacles before ready for departure.

- ❌ Not requesting the into-wind runway when performance is marginal.  
  ✔ Request runway change for performance when needed.

- ❌ Confusing helicopter hover capability with published takeoff performance.  
  ✔ Use AFM charts for the actual weight and conditions.
