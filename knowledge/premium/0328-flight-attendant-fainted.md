# Flight Attendant Fainted

**Catalog ID:** 0328  
**Category:** Crew  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Flight Attendant Fainted — a cabin crew medical event in which a flight attendant loses consciousness and can no longer perform cabin duties.

This concept appears in ICAO exam English because many Part 2 and Part 3 scenarios assume airliner cabin crew. You must know the standard phraseology even when your daily aircraft is an H130.

For H130 Brazilian operations, there is often no flight attendant at all. Treat this lesson as exam language plus transferable CRM: any crew member or passenger helper who becomes unconscious reduces your support capacity. The radio message still centers on medical problem, crew status, and assistance required.

Understanding how to communicate a flight attendant fainted event in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Flight Attendant Fainted describes a cabin crew member who has lost consciousness and is unable to continue cabin duties, requiring first aid, possible crew reassignment, and ATC notification if medical assistance or priority handling is needed.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Comissário(a) desmaiado(a) / Flight Attendant Fainted.

Situação em que um membro da tripulação de cabine perde a consciência e não pode continuar as funções de cabine, exigindo primeiros socorros, possível redistribuição de tarefas e notificação ao controle se for necessária assistência médica ou prioridade.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a flight attendant faints, the crew should:

- ensure flight deck control and safety are maintained
- have remaining cabin crew or capable persons provide first aid
- determine whether cabin safety duties can still be covered
- advise ATC of a medical problem involving a crew member if assistance or priority is required
- request medical assistance on arrival and consider priority landing if the condition is serious

Flight Attendant Fainted communication commonly includes:

- medical problem on board, flight attendant unconscious
- cabin crew member fainted, request medical assistance
- request priority landing if required
- confirm cabin still secure and passengers supervised

**Important:** On the H130, you may never have a flight attendant. Still learn the exam phrase "flight attendant fainted." In helicopter terms, translate the skill: if your only passenger helper or second crew member becomes unconscious, say crew member unconscious or passenger assisting became unconscious, and request medical help. Do not abandon aircraft control.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to cabin crew medical events to:

- arrange medical assistance on arrival
- provide priority handling when the pilot requests it
- understand reduced cabin capability for evacuation or passenger management
- request souls on board and nature of the problem
- coordinate emergency services at the gate, apron, or helipad

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

ICAO exam scenario: you are the captain of a multi-crew aircraft when cabin reports a flight attendant has fainted in the aisle.

You confirm another attendant is providing care and the cabin remains secure.

You transmit:

Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, medical problem on board, flight attendant unconscious, request medical assistance on landing.

H130 note: if your second pilot or a passenger helper collapses instead, the structure is the same — medical problem, who is affected, what you need from ATC.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say souls on board and assistance required.
2. Approach: ANAC123, medical services will meet you on arrival.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, ambulance standing by.
4. Approach: ANAC123, confirm cabin crew still able to perform duties.
5. Control: ANAC123, priority approach available if required.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, flight attendant fainted, request medical assistance on landing.
2. ANAC123, medical problem on board, cabin crew member unconscious.
3. ANAC123, flight attendant regained consciousness, still request medical meet on arrival.
4. ANAC123, requesting priority landing, crew medical problem.
5. ANAC123, souls on board one two eight, one cabin crew unconscious.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Examiners want two things: correct medical English and a clear operational request.

"Flight attendant fainted" is specific. It tells ATC the problem is crew medical, not a random passenger story.

Then say what you need: medical assistance, priority landing, or both.

In airline CRM, remaining attendants cover doors and cabin. In the H130 world, there may be nobody left to cover anything. That raises the urgency of a clean landing and medical meet.

Do not confuse this with pilot incapacitation. A fainted attendant is serious; an incapacitated flying pilot is immediately critical. Use the right label.

Learn the airliner phrase. Apply the helicopter judgment.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Why must pilots be able to say "flight attendant fainted" clearly even if they fly helicopters without cabin crew?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Because ICAO speaking tests use standard multi-crew cabin scenarios. The phrase shows I can report a crew medical problem and request assistance. In helicopter operations without cabin crew, I apply the same structure to any crew member or helper who becomes unconscious.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "the stewardess fell" or informal slang on frequency.  
  ✔ Say flight attendant fainted or cabin crew member unconscious.

- ❌ Reporting the event without requesting assistance.  
  ✔ Add request medical assistance on landing.

- ❌ Treating it automatically as Mayday.  
  ✔ Use Pan Pan for urgency unless life is immediately threatened and distress is required.

- ❌ Ignoring cabin safety after the faint.  
  ✔ Confirm remaining crew can still manage passengers and exits.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Flight Attendant Fainted, Request Medical Assistance

**Pronunciation:** FLITE uh-TEN-dant FAYNT-ed, reh-KWEST MED-ih-kul uh-SIS-tans

**Word Stress**

- Attendant → uh-TEN-dant (stress on second)
- Fainted → FAYNT-ed
- Medical → MED-ih-kul
- Assistance → uh-SIS-tans

Practice:

Flight attendant fainted... request medical assistance.

Together:

Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, flight attendant fainted, request medical assistance on landing.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**CREW** — **C**all it cabin crew clearly, **R**equest medical help, **E**valuate remaining cabin capability, **W**atch that this is not pilot incapacitation.
