# Visibility Below Minimums

**Catalog ID:** 0380  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Visibility Below Minimums — when reported ground visibility or RVR is lower than the minimum visibility required for the intended takeoff, approach, or landing procedure.

Visibility below minimums stops the landing even when the ceiling looks workable. Fog, heavy rain, smoke, and mist can erase the runway and helipad references the H130 needs for the visual segment. Brazilian coastal fog, Congonhas rain showers, and Amazonian morning mist make this a routine Part 2 decision: hold, delay, or divert — communicated in clear ICAO English.

## 📖 Meaning

Visibility Below Minimums describes the situation in which the prevailing visibility or runway visual range at the aerodrome is lower than the minimum visibility published for the intended takeoff or approach procedure, so that commencing or continuing to landing under those rules is not authorized until visibility improves or an alternate procedure/airport is used.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Visibilidade abaixo dos mínimos / Visibility Below Minimums.

Situação em que a visibilidade predominante ou o alcance visual de pista no aeródromo é inferior à visibilidade mínima publicada para o procedimento de decolagem ou aproximação pretendido, de modo que iniciar ou continuar para o pouso sob essas regras não é autorizado até que a visibilidade melhore ou se use um procedimento/aeroporto alternativo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When visibility is below minimums, the crew should:

- compare METAR/ATIS visibility and RVR with charted minima
- do not continue below DA/MDA without the required visual references
- request hold and weather updates, or divert to an alternate above minima
- delay takeoff when departure visibility is below takeoff minima
- advise ATC: visibility below minima, requesting hold or diversion
- go around if visibility is lost inside the approach before required references

Visibility-below-minima actions commonly include:

- holding for fog burn-off or shower passage
- diverting to alternate
- requesting RVR values when available
- rejecting takeoff clearance when below takeoff minima

**Important:** Helicopters sometimes have different visibility minima than airplanes for the same airport — use the minima that apply to your operation and chart. Still, below means below. Brazilian fog at Santos Dumont or morning mist inland is not a reason to invent a landing. If you cannot see what the procedure requires, you do not land.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use visibility-below-minima reports to:

- state when the airport is below landing or takeoff minima
- expect holding, diversion, or departure delays
- pass RVR and SPECI updates
- manage low-visibility operations when applicable
- coordinate missed approaches when fog or rain closes the visual segment

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on the ILS toward Santos Dumont. ATIS: visibility 800 meters in fog, RVR not meeting your approach minima.

You transmit:

Santos Approach, ANAC123, visibility below minima, requesting hold and latest RVR, will divert if no improvement.

Approach: ANAC123, hold as published, visibility eight hundred meters, say intentions.

After ten minutes with no improvement:

ANAC123, diverting Jacarepaguá due visibility below minima, request vectors.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Visibility is the difference between a minima crossing and a landing.

Ceiling can be legal while fog still hides the runway. Check both. Always.

In the H130 the visual segment and hover references are everything. If visibility is below minima, you are not "almost there" — you are not authorized.

Say visibility below minima. Request RVR if it helps. Hold only with fuel and a clock. Divert when the fog will not burn off in time.

Brazilian coastal mornings will offer you beautiful approaches into gray nothing. Take the diversion. The passengers prefer a late landing to a newspaper story.

Takeoff minima matter too. Do not accept departure into visibility you cannot legally use for return or escape.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Visibility is reported below your approach minima. What do you do and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would not continue for landing under those minima. I would advise ATC that visibility is below minima and request holding for improvement or diversion to an alternate with adequate visibility. I would ask for RVR if available. If visibility is lost after passing minima without the required visual references, I would go around. In the H130 I also delay takeoff when departure visibility is below takeoff minima. I treat below-minima visibility as a firm operational limit.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Checking ceiling only and ignoring visibility/RVR.  
  ✔ Both must meet minima; fog can kill visibility under a usable ceiling.

- ❌ "Taking a look" in fog below minima.  
  ✔ Hold or divert; do not descend without required references.

- ❌ Accepting takeoff with visibility below takeoff minima.  
  ✔ Delay departure; say unable due visibility.

- ❌ Vague: "foggy, maybe try."  
  ✔ Precise: visibility below minima, requesting hold or diversion.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Visibility Below Minima, Request Hold

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, viz-ih-BIL-ih-tee bih-LOH MIN-ih-muh, reh-KWEST HOLD

**Word Stress**

- Visibility → viz-ih-BIL-ih-tee (stress on third)
- Below → bih-LOH
- Minima → MIN-ih-muh
- RVR → AR-VEE-AR (letters) or "runway visual range"

Practice:

Visibility below minima... request hold... or diversion.

Together:

ANAC123, visibility below minima, requesting hold and latest RVR.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Visibility 600 m. Your minima require 1 200 m. Ceiling is fine.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

On short final, fog thickens and you lose the runway lights after descending to minima. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, visibility below minima, requesting hold or diversion. Second task: ANAC123, going around, lost visual references. Ceiling alone never authorizes the landing — visibility must meet minima, and required references must be in sight. When they disappear, go around.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Visibility below minimums closes the airport for your procedure — even if the ceiling looks kind.

Compare RVR and visibility to the chart. Hold with a plan. Divert without ego. Go around if the lights disappear.

In the H130 on Brazilian fog and rain days, clear radio and strict minima keep you out of the murk that does not forgive.
