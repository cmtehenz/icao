# Air Conditioning Smoke

**Catalog ID:** 0242  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Air Conditioning Smoke — smoke or fumes associated with the air conditioning or environmental control system, which may fill the cabin or cockpit with contaminated air, requiring immediate crew protection, checklist action, and a clear emergency radio report with nature, location, intentions, and souls/fuel when asked.

Air conditioning smoke is not "just a smell." Contaminated bleed or pack air can incapacitate a crew quickly. The priority is oxygen or smoke protection as equipped, isolate the source per AFM, land as soon as practicable, and communicate with ATC in structured ICAO English.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, environmental systems differ from airliners, but cabin/cockpit smoke and fume events remain highly relevant. Brazilian IFR and VFR training flights near Congonhas, Santos Dumont, and coastal routes demand fast diversion language for Part 2 and Part 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Air Conditioning Smoke is smoke, haze, or fumes originating from or distributed by the air conditioning or environmental control system into the cockpit or cabin, indicating possible overheating, oil contamination, electrical fault, or bleed-source problems, and requiring immediate protective action, abnormal or emergency procedures, and priority landing.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Fumaça, névoa ou vapores originados ou distribuídos pelo sistema de ar-condicionado ou controle ambiental para o cockpit ou cabine, indicando possível superaquecimento, contaminação por óleo, falha elétrica ou problemas na fonte de sangria, e exigindo ação protetora imediata, procedimentos anormais ou de emergência e pouso prioritário.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When air conditioning smoke occurs, the crew should:

- don oxygen or smoke protection immediately as equipped
- apply the AFM smoke or air conditioning smoke checklist
- isolate packs, bleeds, or affected environmental sources per procedure
- declare Pan Pan or Mayday based on severity and crew impairment risk
- request immediate vectors to the nearest suitable airport and land
- report nature, location (cockpit/cabin via air conditioning), and intentions; give souls and fuel when asked

Air conditioning smoke reporting commonly includes:

- smoke or fumes from air conditioning
- cockpit or cabin filling with smoke
- request immediate descent and vectors to land
- smoke dissipating or continuing after checklist
- souls on board and fuel remaining

**Important:** Structure the radio call: urgency signal, who you are, what (air conditioning smoke), where (cockpit/cabin), what you need (immediate vectors to land), and intentions. Upgrade to Mayday if vision, consciousness, or safe flight is in doubt. In the H130, you are often near suitable landing sites — use that advantage early.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to air conditioning smoke reports to:

- provide immediate priority handling and shortest vectors to land
- clear traffic from the approach path
- alert fire and rescue services
- request souls on board and fuel remaining
- obtain smoke status updates (continuing or dissipating)

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at six thousand feet toward Santos Dumont. A hot electrical smell becomes visible haze from the air conditioning vents. Your eyes sting.

You don oxygen, begin the checklist, and transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, ANAC123, air conditioning smoke in the cockpit, request immediate vectors to land nearest suitable airport.

Approach: ANAC123, turn left heading one five zero, descend three thousand, nearest airport Santos Dumont, twelve o'clock, one zero miles, say souls and fuel.

You reply with souls and fuel, continue the checklist, and update if smoke decreases after isolation.

You land with fire services standing by. Nature, location, intentions — all clear on the first call.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Smoke from the air conditioning system is an atmosphere attack on the crew.

Protect yourself first. Checklist second. Radio third — but do not delay the radio if you need priority now.

The best emergency call answers four questions: how bad (Pan Pan or Mayday), what it is, where it is, and what you want. "Air conditioning smoke in the cockpit, request immediate vectors to land" is complete. "We have smoke" is not.

In helicopters, cabin and cockpit are often the same small volume. Contamination spreads fast. Do not wait for flames. Land.

Brazilian controllers will ask souls and fuel. Know the numbers before you need them. Update smoke status when it changes — dissipating or continuing guides rescue response.

If the checklist isolates the source and smoke clears, say so. If it does not, say so and keep priority. Either way, land as soon as practicable.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You detect smoke coming from the air conditioning vents in flight. What do you do and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would immediately protect myself with oxygen or smoke equipment as available, apply the smoke checklist, and declare an emergency. I would report air conditioning smoke, specify cockpit or cabin, and request immediate vectors to the nearest suitable airport. When asked, I would give souls on board and fuel remaining, and I would update ATC whether smoke is continuing or dissipating after checklist actions.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "smoke" without location or source.  
  ✔ Say air conditioning smoke in the cockpit or cabin.

- ❌ Delaying the urgency call to finish the entire checklist first.  
  ✔ Protect, start checklist, declare early for priority vectors.

- ❌ Forgetting souls and fuel when ATC asks.  
  ✔ Prepare souls and fuel as part of the emergency brief.

- ❌ Cancelling urgency too early because the smell improved slightly.  
  ✔ Continue to land; update status but keep priority until safe on ground.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Haze and a burning smell come from the air conditioning vents. Vision is becoming difficult.

**Your first task**

Compose your initial emergency call.

**Your second task**

ATC asks souls and fuel. Smoke is still present after isolation. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, ANAC123, air conditioning smoke in the cockpit, request immediate vectors to nearest suitable airport. Second task: ANAC123, souls four, fuel one hour, smoke continuing, request shortest vectors to land. I would not delay landing because the checklist was incomplete, and I would keep Mayday active while smoke threatened safe flight.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Air conditioning smoke attacks the air you breathe and the vision you need.

Oxygen. Checklist. Mayday. Vectors. Land.

Say nature, location, and intentions on the first transmission. Give souls and fuel when asked. Update whether smoke continues.

In the H130, near Brazilian airports, time is often available if you use it immediately — and gone if you wait.

Protect the crew. Tell ATC clearly. Get on the ground.

That is how you survive an environmental smoke emergency in ICAO English and in the aircraft.
