# Pushback

**Catalog ID:** 0204  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Pushback — the ground maneuver in which an aircraft is pushed backward from a parking stand by a tug or pushback tractor so it can start taxi from a clear position.

Pushback is classic fixed-wing airline phraseology. For H130 helicopter operations it is uncommon: helicopters usually ground-taxi, hover-taxi, or depart from the spot. You still must know pushback phraseology for ICAO exams, mixed-traffic airports, and situations where a helicopter or other aircraft on frequency uses pushback clearances.

At Guarulhos, Congonhas, and Santos Dumont, you will hear pushback requests and approvals constantly on Ground. Understanding them prevents confusion and prepares you for Part 2 radiotelephony standards.

This lesson teaches the request, approval, readback, and operational meaning of pushback — with helicopter context noted where relevant.

## 📖 Meaning

Pushback is the authorized rearward movement of an aircraft from a parking stand using a ground tug or tractor, performed only with ATC (and where required apron) approval, to position the aircraft for engine start completion and taxi.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pushback / Empurrão (saída de stand com tractor).

Movimento autorizado da aeronave para trás a partir da posição de estacionamento, realizado com tractor ou reboque, somente com aprovação do controle (e do pátio, quando aplicável), para posicionar a aeronave para partida e táxi.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When pushback is required, the crew should:

- complete before-push checks and confirm tug connected and communications established with ground crew
- request pushback from ATC/apron as locally required
- read back the pushback approval and any direction or restriction
- push only when cleared and ground crew confirms ready
- advise ATC when pushback is complete and ready to taxi
- for helicopters: normally request taxi or hover taxi instead; know pushback phraseology for exam and multi-traffic awareness

Pushback operations commonly include:

- pushback approved, face east / face north (or similar direction)
- pushback approved, expect taxi via Alpha
- hold position, expect pushback delay
- pushback complete, request taxi

**Important:** Pushback approval is not taxi clearance. After pushback, you still need an explicit taxi clearance before moving forward under your own power on the maneuvering area.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers manage pushback to:

- sequence departures from congested stands and gates
- prevent two aircraft from pushing into the same taxi lane
- protect taxi routes behind parked aircraft
- coordinate tug movements with active taxi traffic
- time engine start and push with overall departure flow

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are monitoring Ground at Guarulhos while another aircraft prepares to depart.

Pilot: ANAC456, stand twenty-one, request pushback.

Ground: ANAC456, pushback approved, face west.

After the push: ANAC456, pushback complete, request taxi.

Ground: ANAC456, taxi to holding point runway two seven Left via Alpha.

In your H130 on the same airport, you would typically say request taxi or request hover taxi from the stand — not pushback — unless a specific local procedure required tug assistance. You still understand every pushback call on frequency so you know when the taxi lane behind a gate is about to become occupied.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, pushback approved.
2. Ground: ANAC123, pushback approved, face east.
3. Ground: ANAC123, expect pushback delay due to traffic, hold position.
4. Ground: ANAC123, pushback approved, after pushback expect taxi via Bravo.
5. Ground: ANAC123, negative pushback, traffic behind, hold position.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Pushback is a controlled reverse move into a live taxi environment.

Even if you fly an H130 that rarely needs a tug, ICAO expects you to understand the phraseology: request, approval, direction, and the critical separation between pushback complete and taxi clearance.

Two clearances. Two moments. Do not blend them.

For helicopters, the operational equivalent is often: start, request taxi or hover taxi, leave the spot forward or laterally as cleared. If a lesson or exam scenario gives you pushback language, answer with standard pushback phraseology — then, in real H130 line flying, use the helicopter-appropriate request.

When you hear pushback on frequency at Congonhas or Guarulhos, build a mental picture: that stand is about to occupy the alley. It makes you a better traffic partner on the ground.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Explain the difference between pushback approval and taxi clearance. How does this apply to helicopter operations?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Pushback approval authorizes rearward movement from the stand with tug assistance. It does not authorize taxi. After pushback is complete, the crew must request and receive a taxi clearance before moving under the aircraft's own power on the maneuvering area. In helicopter operations, pushback is uncommon because helicopters usually taxi or hover-taxi from the parking position. Helicopter crews should still understand pushback phraseology for exams and for situational awareness in mixed traffic, while normally requesting taxi or hover taxi instead of pushback.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating pushback approval as permission to taxi away.  
  ✔ After pushback complete, request taxi and wait for clearance.

- ❌ Starting pushback before ATC approval.  
  ✔ Request and receive approval first; coordinate with ground crew.

- ❌ Omitting facing direction when ATC includes it.  
  ✔ Read back face east/west/north/south as issued.

- ❌ Helicopter pilots ignoring pushback calls on frequency.  
  ✔ Use them for traffic awareness even when you do not push back.

**PUSH** — **P**ermit from ATC first, **U**nder tug control only as cleared, **S**tate facing direction in readback, **H**old for separate taxi clearance after complete.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Pushback is airline ground choreography — and ICAO vocabulary you must own.

Approval to push is not clearance to taxi. Complete the push. Then request taxi.

In the H130, you may seldom push back. You will still hear it, answer exam items about it, and share aprons with aircraft that depend on it.

Know the phraseology. Use helicopter taxi when that is the real procedure. Stay precise either way.
