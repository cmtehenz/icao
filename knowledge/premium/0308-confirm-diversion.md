# Confirm Diversion

**Catalog ID:** 0308  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Confirm Diversion — the ATC request for the crew to confirm that they are diverting, or to state diversion intentions and the alternate aerodrome. Core Part 2 and Part 3 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: declare the diversion clearly, name the alternate, and keep ATC in the loop.

Confirm diversion is High priority because a diversion changes routing, fuel planning, and emergency coordination across sectors.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for diversion confirmation under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including weather, fuel, and technical diversion cases.

Understanding how to confirm diversion in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Confirm Diversion is an ATC request for the pilot to verify or state diversion intentions — typically confirming that the aircraft will not continue to the planned destination and identifying the alternate aerodrome, reason if required, and any assistance needed.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Confirmar desvio / Confirm Diversion.

Solicitação do controle para que o piloto verifique ou declare a intenção de desviar — tipicamente confirmando que a aeronave não seguirá para o destino planejado e identificando o aeródromo alternativo, o motivo se necessário e qualquer assistência requerida.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC asks you to confirm diversion, the crew should:

- state clearly that you are diverting and name the alternate
- give the reason briefly if ATC asks or if it affects priority: weather, fuel, technical
- request routing, altitude, and any approach information for the alternate
- advise fuel remaining and souls on board if the situation is abnormal
- for H130: choose an alternate within performance and fuel range, including heliports when appropriate
- update ATC if the diversion destination changes again

Diversion exchanges commonly include:

- confirm diversion / diverting to...
- request diversion to [aerodrome]
- reason for diversion
- request vectors / routing to alternate
- request assistance upon arrival

**Important:** Diversion is a decision the crew owns; ATC coordinates the airspace. Say the alternate early so sectors can plan.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers confirm diversion to:

- stop sequencing you toward the original destination
- coordinate the new route with adjacent sectors
- prepare the alternate aerodrome for your arrival
- assess whether priority or emergency handling is required
- protect other traffic while your routing changes

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 inbound to Florianópolis. Destination weather drops below minima. You elect to divert to Navegantes.

Approach: ANAC123, confirm diversion.

You reply:

ANAC123, affirmative, diverting to Navegantes due weather, request routing.

Approach issues a heading and altitude toward the alternate. You read back and continue. If fuel is becoming a factor, you add that information so ATC can prioritize.

Confirming diversion is not failure — it is professional decision-making spoken clearly on the frequency.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, confirm diversion.
2. Control: ANAC123, confirm diverting to Navegantes.
3. Approach: ANAC123, say reason for diversion.
4. Radar: ANAC123, diversion approved, fly heading zero nine zero.
5. Control: ANAC123, confirm alternate aerodrome.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

2. ANAC123, diverting to Navegantes due weather.
3. ANAC123, request diversion Campo de Marte, fuel.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Diversion language must be short and decisive.

Affirmative, diverting to [place]. Reason if needed. Request routing. That is the core.

Do not narrate the entire weather discussion on a busy frequency. Decide in the cockpit, then speak the decision. In Brazilian IFR operations with the H130, weather and fuel diversions are real — Part 3 expects you to communicate them without panic and without delay.

If ATC asks you to confirm diversion, they already suspect the plan changed. Give them the alternate immediately.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Destination weather is below minima and you decide to divert. ATC asks you to confirm diversion. What do you say and what else should you request?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would confirm the diversion, name the alternate aerodrome, and briefly state the reason if relevant, such as weather or fuel. I would request routing or vectors to the alternate and any approach information needed. If the situation is abnormal, I would also be ready to report fuel remaining and souls on board and to request assistance upon arrival.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying you "might divert" after the decision is already made.  
  ✔ State affirmative diversion and the alternate clearly.

- ❌ Forgetting to name the alternate aerodrome.  
  ✔ Always include the diversion destination.

- ❌ Giving a long weather briefing on frequency.  
  ✔ Short reason: due weather / due fuel / technical.

- ❌ Not requesting routing after declaring diversion.  
  ✔ Ask for heading, altitude, or route to the alternate.
