# Expect Vectors to Intercept

**Catalog ID:** 0110  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Expect Vectors to Intercept — an ATC advisory informing the pilot that radar vectors will be used to position the aircraft onto the final approach course.

Expect Vectors to Intercept means the pilot will not fly the full published approach procedure — ATC will issue headings to intercept the localizer, RNAV final, or VOR course.

In the H130, radar vectoring is the standard sequencing method at Florianópolis, Curitiba, Congonhas, and São Paulo area airports.

You must acknowledge the advisory, remain on assigned headings until vectored onto final, and confirm intercept when established.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and radar vector approach operations.

## 📖 Meaning

Expect Vectors to Intercept is an ATC advisory indicating that the pilot should anticipate receiving radar headings to position the aircraft for intercept of the final approach course, rather than flying the full published procedure.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Espere vetores para interceptar.

Aviso do controle de que serão fornecidos rumos de radar para posicionar a aeronave na interceptação do eixo final da aproximação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Expect Vectors to Intercept is received, the pilot should:

- acknowledge the advisory
- maintain current heading and altitude until new instructions
- review the final approach course, initial altitude, and speed
- be prepared for heading, altitude, and speed instructions
- confirm intercept and report established on the final approach course

Expect Vectors to Intercept operations commonly include:

- radar headings to intercept localizer or RNAV final
- speed reductions for sequencing
- descents to intercept glide slope or approach altitude
- confirmation of approach type before vectoring

**Important:** When being vectored, do not turn toward the final approach course unless instructed. Follow ATC headings exactly and report established only when stabilized on the course.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC issues Expect Vectors to Intercept to:

- sequence multiple aircraft into the final approach
- bypass the full published approach procedure for efficiency
- optimize runway throughput at busy airports
- avoid congested holding or transition areas
- position aircraft at the correct intercept angle

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Florianópolis at three thousand feet.

Approach advises:

ANAC123, expect vectors ILS runway one four, report ready for vectors.

You complete the approach briefing and transmit:

Ready for vectors, ANAC123.

Approach issues a series of headings:

ANAC123, turn right heading two eight zero, descend and maintain two thousand.

Then:

ANAC123, turn left heading two zero zero, intercept localizer runway one four.

You intercept and report:

Established ILS runway one four, ANAC123.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, expect vectors ILS runway one four.
2. Approach: ANAC123, vectors for ILS runway one four, report approach brief complete.
3. Approach: ANAC123, turn left heading one eight zero, intercept localizer.
4. Approach: ANAC123, you are twelve miles from touchdown, report established.
5. Approach: ANAC123, cleared ILS runway one four, report established.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Vectors to intercept means you are in ATC hands until you are on the final course.

Your job on vectors: follow headings, descend as cleared, manage speed, and confirm intercept.

The sequence:

Receive expect vectors. Brief approach. Report ready. Follow headings. Intercept. Report established. Continue approach.

Brazilian pilots sometimes turn toward the final course before receiving the intercept instruction.

Wait for the instruction. ATC sees the picture — you see only your cockpit.

In the H130, manage speed on vectors so you arrive at the intercept point configured.

If ATC turns you inside the final approach course, confirm you can establish before the final approach fix.

Report established only when the needle is centered and tracking stable.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How do you confirm you are established on the final approach course after being vectored?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

After receiving the intercept heading I confirm I am on the correct frequency, monitor the localizer or RNAV course needle, and report established only when the course is centered and tracking stable. I transmit: Established ILS runway one four, ANAC123. If I am turned inside the final approach fix without being established, I advise ATC so they can vector me for another intercept.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Turning toward the final course before receiving the intercept instruction.  
  ✔ Hold the assigned vector heading until ATC clears you to intercept.

- ❌ Reporting established before the needle is centered.  
  ✔ Report established only when the course is stable and tracking.

- ❌ Forgetting to report approach brief complete before vectors begin.  
  ✔ When asked, report: Approach brief complete, ready vectors, ANAC123.

- ❌ Allowing airspeed to be too high on final intercept.  
  ✔ Manage speed on vectors to arrive configured at the intercept point.

Approaching Florianópolis, Approach advises: ANAC123, expect vectors ILS runway one four, report approach brief complete.

**Your second task**
