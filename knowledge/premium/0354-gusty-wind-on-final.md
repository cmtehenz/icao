# Gusty Wind on Final

**Catalog ID:** 0354  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Gusty Wind on Final — rapidly changing wind speed on the approach path that increases workload, alters airspeed and groundspeed, and may require a go-around if the approach becomes unstable.

Gusty wind is not a reason to invent non-standard radio chatter. It is a reason to fly a stable profile, use correct technique, and communicate go-around or wind conditions clearly when needed.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, gusty coastal and plateau winds are common into Congonhas, Santos Dumont, and Santa Catarina fields. Power margins and translational lift make gust management a core skill.

Understanding gusty wind phraseology in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Gusty Wind on Final is a condition of rapidly varying wind speed along the final approach path that causes fluctuating airspeed and aircraft performance, requiring active control, possible approach speed adjustment within limits, and a go-around if a stable approach cannot be maintained.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Vento rajado na final / Gusty Wind on Final.

Condição de velocidade do vento variando rapidamente ao longo da trajetória de aproximação final, causando flutuações de velocidade aerodinâmica e desempenho, exigindo controle ativo, possível ajuste de velocidade de aproximação dentro dos limites e arremetida se uma aproximação estável não puder ser mantida.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When gusty wind is present on final, the crew should:

- obtain latest wind including gusts from ATIS or Tower
- fly a stable approach; add gust factor only as published or trained
- be prepared to go around if airspeed or flight path becomes unstable
- report go-around with reason if executed
- request another runway or delay if gusts exceed personal or aircraft limits
- brief passengers for possible firm landing or go-around

Gusty wind reporting commonly includes:

- going around, unstable approach / gusty wind
- request wind check
- request runway change if available
- windshear alert if sudden loss/gain meets criteria

**Important:** Gusty is not the same as windshear. Use windshear language only for sudden significant airspeed changes associated with shear. Otherwise say gusty wind or report the go-around reason clearly.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers manage gusty wind on final to:

- pass current wind and gust values to arriving aircraft
- sequence go-arounds and re-entries to the circuit or vectors
- offer alternate runways when wind favors another direction
- warn following traffic of reported conditions
- keep the approach path clear during missed approaches

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

On final to runway one seven at Congonhas in the H130, Tower reports wind one eight zero at one five gusting two eight. Airspeed fluctuates. The approach becomes unstable below five hundred feet.

You go around, state going around due to unstable approach in gusty wind, and request vectors for another approach. Tower passes updated wind. On the second approach you stabilize and land.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, wind one eight zero degrees one five knots gusting two eight.
2. Tower: ANAC123, caution gusty wind on final.
3. Tower: ANAC123, roger go-around, fly runway heading, climb two thousand.
4. Approach: ANAC123, radar vectors for another approach, wind gusting.
5. Tower: ANAC123, wind now one seven zero at one two gusting two zero, cleared to land.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, going around, unstable approach, gusty wind.
2. ANAC123, request wind check on final.
3. ANAC123, runway heading, climbing two thousand, going around.
4. ANAC123, request runway two five Left if wind favors.
5. ANAC123, cleared to land runway one seven, wind copied.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Gusty wind on final is a stability problem.

Fly the profile. Watch airspeed. Go around early if it falls apart.

Brazilian pilots often say "vento rajado" on frequency without a clear intention. Prefer going around, unstable approach, or request wind check.

In the H130, gusts near the surface change power required quickly. Do not chase every knot with large collective inputs — smooth corrections, decisive go-around.

High priority because missed go-around decisions create hard landings and loss of control near the ground.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Wind is gusting on final and your approach becomes unstable. What do you do and say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would go around, maintain aircraft control, and report going around due to an unstable approach in gusty wind. I would follow missed approach or Tower instructions, request updated wind, and either make another approach or request an alternate runway if gusts remain outside safe limits.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Forcing a landing when the approach is unstable in gusts.  
  ✔ Go around — unstable approach is a valid, professional reason.

- ❌ Saying only "vento ruim" without intention.  
  ✔ State going around or request wind check / runway change.

- ❌ Calling every gust "windshear."  
  ✔ Use windshear only when criteria are met; otherwise gusty wind.

- ❌ Not reading back go-around altitude and heading.  
  ✔ Read back missed approach instructions fully.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

On final at Congonhas, wind gusting through twenty-eight knots. Airspeed unstable at three hundred feet.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

Tower gives runway heading and two thousand feet. What is your readback?

First task: ANAC123, going around, unstable approach, gusty wind. Second task: ANAC123, runway heading, climbing two thousand. I would then request wind check or another approach when ready.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Gusty wind on final demands honesty with yourself.

Stable — continue. Unstable — go around.

Say the reason in standard English. Copy the wind. Try again or change runway.

In the H130, surface gusts are part of Brazilian coastal and city flying. Train the go-around call until it is automatic.

Fly stable. Speak clear. Land only when it is under control.
