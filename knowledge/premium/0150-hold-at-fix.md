# Hold at Fix

**Catalog ID:** 0150  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Hold at Fix — the ATC instruction to enter and maintain a holding pattern at a specific named navigational fix.

Hold at Fix is an explicit instruction. ATC specifies the exact fix, the inbound course or direction, the turn direction, and the leg length or time. Nothing is assumed from published charts.

At Brazilian airports and en-route fixes around Florianópolis, Curitiba, and Navegantes, holding patterns are used daily for traffic sequencing, weather delays, and fuel planning.

Knowing the difference between Hold at Fix and Hold as Published is a core ICAO knowledge requirement — one is fully specified by ATC, the other references published charts.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and the holding pattern skills required in professional operations.

## 📖 Meaning

Hold at Fix is an ATC instruction directing the pilot to enter and maintain a holding pattern at a designated navigational fix, where ATC specifies the inbound course, direction of turns, and leg length or timing as part of the clearance.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Espere no fix.

Instrução do controle de tráfego aéreo determinando que a aeronave entre e mantenha uma espera em um fix de navegação designado, com o controle especificando o curso de entrada, a direção das curvas e o comprimento do trecho ou o tempo como parte da autorização.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the crew receives Hold at Fix, they should:

- identify the fix on the navigation display and confirm it is loaded
- note the specified inbound course, turn direction, and leg length
- select the appropriate holding entry (direct, teardrop, or parallel) based on the entry sector
- set the holding timer for leg timing if distance-based DME is not available
- maintain the assigned altitude and speed for the holding pattern
- report entering the hold if requested and estimate fuel endurance if relevant

Hold at Fix clearances commonly include:

- the fix name (e.g., IDAMO, GUARA, ARMIN)
- the inbound course or cardinal direction
- left or right turns (right turns are standard; left turns are specified)
- the leg length in miles or minutes
- an expect further clearance time

**Important:** Hold at Fix provides all parameters in the clearance. Do not reference the chart for the holding pattern parameters — fly exactly what ATC specified. This is the key difference from Hold as Published.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Hold at Fix to:

- assign a holding pattern at a fix that does not have a published hold
- modify an existing published hold with different parameters
- sequence arriving aircraft at a specific fix before approach
- manage traffic flow during weather or runway restrictions
- provide an explicit clearance when the standard published procedure is not appropriate

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Florianópolis in your H130 and traffic ahead has caused a sequencing delay.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, hold at IDAMO, inbound course zero three six, right turns, one minute legs, expect further clearance time zero five three zero.

You identify IDAMO on the GPS, determine the entry type, and respond:

Hold at IDAMO, inbound course zero three six, right turns, one minute legs, expect further clearance zero five three zero, ANAC123.

You enter the hold with a direct entry, turn right after crossing IDAMO outbound, and maintain the assigned altitude.

After two orbits, Approach transmits the onward clearance.

You held. You were precise. You were professional.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

A Hold at Fix clearance is a complete set of instructions.

The fix. The inbound course. The turn direction. The leg length.

ATC gives you all four. You fly all four exactly as specified.

The only decision left to you is the entry type — direct, teardrop, or parallel — based on your heading when you reach the fix.

In the H130, holding in Brazilian airspace means managing fuel carefully.

When you receive a Hold at Fix, the first thing after the readback is mental fuel arithmetic.

How long can we hold?

At what endurance limit do we need to declare minimum fuel?

At what endurance limit do we declare emergency?

Know those numbers before entering the hold.

Brief your co-pilot. Monitor the fuel. State your endurance to ATC proactively if the delay is extended.

Hold at Fix is also your opportunity to think ahead.

Use the hold to brief the approach.

A holding pattern is not a delay — it is preparation time.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What are the parameters ATC must specify in a Hold at Fix clearance, and what is the difference from Hold as Published?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A Hold at Fix clearance must include the fix name, the inbound course or direction, the direction of turns — right or left — and the leg length in minutes or nautical miles. Optionally ATC may include an expect further clearance time. The crew flies exactly the parameters given in the clearance. In a Hold as Published instruction, ATC does not re-specify the parameters because the crew is expected to use the holding pattern as printed on the applicable approach or navigation chart. Hold at Fix is a complete explicit instruction; Hold as Published is a reference to the published procedure.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Not reading back the inbound course in the Hold at Fix clearance.  
  ✔ Always include the inbound course: hold at IDAMO, inbound course zero three six.

- ❌ Using the published hold parameters when ATC specified different ones.  
  ✔ Fly exactly what ATC specified in the clearance — not what the chart shows.

- ❌ Not considering fuel endurance when entering a hold.  
  ✔ Calculate endurance immediately on receiving the hold clearance. Advise ATC proactively.

- ❌ Confusing the leg length with the total hold circuit time.  
  ✔ One minute legs means one minute outbound. The full circuit is approximately four minutes.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Hold at IDAMO, Inbound Course Zero Three Six

**Pronunciation:** HOHLD at eye-DAH-moh, IN-bound CORS ZE-ro THREE SIX

**Word Stress**

- Hold → HOHLD (strong)
- Inbound → IN-bound
- Course → CORS
- Zero Three Six → ZE-ro THREE SIX (each digit spoken individually)

Practice:

Hold at IDAMO... inbound course... zero three six...

Together:

Hold at IDAMO, inbound course zero three six, right turns, one minute legs, ANAC123.

Speak fix names clearly and at a measured pace — ATC must copy the exact designation.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Florianópolis Approach transmits: "ANAC123, hold at IDAMO, inbound course zero three six, right turns, one minute legs, maintain four thousand, expect further clearance time zero six zero zero."

**Your first task**

Read back the complete holding clearance.

**Your second task**

Your fuel endurance at current burn is forty minutes. Minimum fuel declaration is required at thirty minutes endurance. What do you advise ATC?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would respond: Hold at IDAMO, inbound course zero three six, right turns, one minute legs, maintain four thousand, expect further clearance zero six zero zero, ANAC123. With forty minutes endurance I would advise ATC proactively: ANAC123, advise fuel endurance forty minutes. If clearance is not received before endurance reaches thirty minutes I would declare: ANAC123, minimum fuel, request immediate approach or diversion.
