# Uncontrollable Fire

**Catalog ID:** 0279  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Uncontrollable Fire — fire that cannot be extinguished with available means, requiring immediate landing or forced landing, clear distress communication, and preparation for emergency evacuation. This is among the most critical ICAO Part 2 and Part 3 fire scenarios.

Whether the fire is in cabin, hold, engine, or electrical systems, "uncontrollable" means time has run out for troubleshooting in the air. Land now. Evacuate as soon as the aircraft is stopped and safe to leave.

For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, the same principle applies: if the fire cannot be put out, get on the ground and get out. Master the English: uncontrollable fire, fire not under control, request immediate landing, evacuate.

## 📖 Meaning

Uncontrollable Fire is an active fire that cannot be extinguished or contained with the aircraft's available firefighting equipment and procedures, requiring immediate landing or forced landing, continuous distress communication, and preparation for emergency evacuation upon stopping.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Fogo incontrolável / Uncontrollable Fire.

Fogo ativo que não pode ser extinto ou contido com o equipamento e os procedimentos de combate a incêndio disponíveis na aeronave, exigindo pouso imediato ou pouso forçado, comunicação contínua de emergência e preparação para evacuação de emergência ao parar.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the fire is uncontrollable, the crew should:

- continue firefighting attempts only as the checklist allows while prioritizing landing
- declare or update Mayday: uncontrollable fire / fire not under control
- request immediate landing — any suitable surface if an airport cannot be reached in time
- prepare cabin for emergency evacuation; brief passengers as able
- report nature, location, intentions, souls and fuel; advise evacuation after landing
- for H130: same urgency — land and evacuate; do not delay for a distant airport if fire is spreading

Uncontrollable fire reporting commonly includes:

- uncontrollable fire / fire not under control
- location (cabin, hold, engine, electrical)
- request immediate landing / forced landing
- will evacuate after landing
- souls and fuel

**Important:** Do not soften the message. If the fire is not under control, say so. ATC and fire services need the truth to position trucks and clear the runway.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to uncontrollable fire reports to:

- clear every obstacle between the aircraft and the ground
- alert maximum fire and rescue response
- offer nearest landing options including alternate fields
- prepare for on-runway stop and mass evacuation
- keep frequency clear for essential transmissions only

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Training scenario: cabin fire fought with all available extinguishers. Flames continue. Smoke increases.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Control, ANAC123, uncontrollable fire in the cabin, request immediate landing, will evacuate after landing.

Control: ANAC123, nearest airport twelve miles, turn left heading two four zero, descend at pilot's discretion, fire services full response, say souls and fuel when able.

You reply:

ANAC123, turning left two four zero, uncontrollable fire, souls six, fuel one hour, evacuating after landing.

In the H130, if fire spreads in the cabin, the same words apply — land immediately, even off-airport if required by time.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger Mayday uncontrollable fire, nearest airport twelve miles, turn left heading two four zero.
2. Approach: ANAC123, fire services full response, say souls and fuel.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway cleared, stop on the runway, evacuate when able.
4. Approach: ANAC123, report if you require off-airport landing.
5. Tower: ANAC123, wind calm, cleared to land, evacuate after stopping.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

2. ANAC123, turning left two four zero, descending, uncontrollable fire.
3. ANAC123, souls six, fuel one hour, will evacuate after landing.
4. ANAC123, stopping on the runway, evacuating, ANAC123.
5. ANAC123, requesting off-airport landing if unable to reach the field.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Uncontrollable fire means the aircraft is on a clock measured in minutes, not checklists.

You still fly the aircraft. You still communicate. But the destination is the nearest place you can stop and get people out — not the filed destination, not a convenient alternate an hour away.

Say uncontrollable fire or fire not under control. Do not hide severity behind vague "smoke" language when flames are winning.

In the H130, off-airport landing may be the correct answer sooner than on a jet. Terrain, wires, and rotor clearance matter — but so does fire. Choose the survivable option fastest.

Evacuate when stopped and safe. Do not taxi to the gate with an uncontrollable fire.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

The cabin fire cannot be extinguished. What do you do and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare or update Mayday stating uncontrollable fire and the location, request immediate landing at the nearest suitable place, and prepare for emergency evacuation after stopping. I would report souls and fuel when able and advise that we will evacuate after landing. If the airport cannot be reached in time, I would request or execute an off-airport landing as required for survival.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "fire on board" without "uncontrollable" when extinguishers failed.  
  ✔ State uncontrollable fire or fire not under control.

- ❌ Continuing toward a distant preferred airport.  
  ✔ Nearest suitable — or off-airport if time demands it.

- ❌ Planning to taxi clear with active uncontrollable fire.  
  ✔ Stop and evacuate; tell ATC you will evacuate.

- ❌ Softening the call to avoid "alarming" ATC.  
  ✔ ATC needs the truth for full fire response.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Cabin fire is not under control. Nearest airport is twelve miles. Smoke is increasing.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

Tower clears you to land and asks your intentions after landing. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, uncontrollable fire in the cabin, request immediate landing, will evacuate after landing. Second task: ANAC123, cleared to land, stopping on the runway, evacuating, request fire services. I would not plan to taxi to the apron with an uncontrollable fire still active.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Uncontrollable fire ends only on the ground — with people outside the aircraft.

Fight what you can. Say the truth. Land now. Evacuate.

The H130 may reach a field or a clearing faster than a jet reaches a runway. Use that. Do not waste it flying past safe options.

Tell ATC you will evacuate. Let the trucks meet you on the runway.

That is how uncontrollable fires are survived — by refusing to stay in the air one minute longer than necessary.
