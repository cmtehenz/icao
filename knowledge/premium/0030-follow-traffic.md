# Follow Traffic

**Catalog ID:** 0030  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson introduces visual sequencing — one of the most efficient tools ATC uses in good weather.

When Tower or Approach says Follow Traffic, they are instructing you to maintain visual contact with another aircraft and proceed behind it in the landing or departure sequence.

You become responsible for seeing and following that traffic while still complying with all other ATC instructions.

This is not a clearance to land.

It is a sequencing instruction that requires positive visual contact.

## 📖 Meaning

Follow Traffic instructs the pilot to visually acquire specified traffic and proceed behind it while maintaining safe separation under ATC control.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Siga o tráfego.

Mantenha contato visual com a aeronave indicada e prossiga atrás dela na sequência.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When instructed to Follow Traffic, the pilot should:

- report when the traffic is in sight
- maintain visual contact with the specified aircraft
- follow at a safe distance unless otherwise instructed
- continue complying with altitude, heading, and speed assignments
- advise ATC immediately if visual contact is lost

Follow Traffic is commonly used during:

- visual approaches
- VFR sequencing in controlled airspace
- helicopter traffic management
- pattern operations at busy airports

**Important:** You must have the traffic in sight before accepting the instruction. If you lose sight, inform ATC immediately.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Follow Traffic to:

- sequence aircraft efficiently in visual conditions
- reduce radar vectoring and controller workload
- increase airport capacity in VMC
- simplify visual approaches and pattern operations
- manage helicopter traffic with visual separation

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on approach to Florianópolis in your H130 on a clear afternoon.

Another helicopter is ahead of you on the same approach path.

Approach: ANAC123, follow the traffic, report traffic in sight.

You scan ahead, acquire the traffic, and reply:

Traffic in sight, ANAC123.

You maintain a safe distance behind the leading helicopter while Approach vectors you for the visual approach.

Tower later clears you to land after the traffic ahead has landed and vacated.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, follow the traffic ahead, report traffic in sight.
2. Tower: ANAC123, follow the helicopter on a two-mile final.
3. Approach: ANAC123, follow traffic, maintain own separation.
4. Tower: ANAC123, number two, follow the traffic on final runway one four.
5. Approach: ANAC123, follow the traffic, cleared visual approach runway two seven.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Traffic in sight, following the traffic, ANAC123.
2. Following the helicopter on final, ANAC123.
3. Traffic in sight, maintaining own separation, ANAC123.
4. Number two, following the traffic on final, ANAC123.
5. Following the traffic, cleared visual approach runway two seven, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine driving on a highway behind another car.

You can see the car ahead.

You keep a safe distance.

You do not need a traffic light telling you exactly how far back to stay — but you must pay attention.

Follow Traffic works the same way in the air.

ATC identifies the aircraft.

You confirm visual contact.

You maintain safe spacing.

At Congonhas and Florianópolis, helicopter traffic is dense on good weather days.

Never accept follow traffic if you cannot see the aircraft.

And never assume landing clearance — follow traffic is sequencing, not authorization to land.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What should you do if ATC instructs you to follow traffic but you cannot see the aircraft?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I should immediately advise ATC that I do not have the traffic in sight. I cannot accept a follow traffic instruction until I have positive visual contact with the specified aircraft.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Accepting follow traffic without visual contact.  
  ✔ Report traffic in sight only when you have positive identification.

- ❌ Assuming follow traffic includes landing clearance.  
  ✔ Wait for cleared to land from Tower.

- ❌ Losing sight of traffic without advising ATC.  
  ✔ Report immediately if visual contact is lost.

- ❌ Reading back only "Roger" without confirming traffic in sight.  
  ✔ Traffic in sight, following the traffic, ANAC123.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

Tower: ANAC123, follow the traffic on a two-mile final runway one eight.

**Your first task**

Read back the instruction as a professional pilot.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

You have the traffic in sight and are following. May you land?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

No. Follow traffic is a sequencing instruction only. I must wait for Tower to issue cleared to land before beginning my landing. I continue following the traffic at a safe distance until landing clearance is received.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Follow Traffic is visual sequencing — efficient, but responsibility shifts to your eyes.

Confirm traffic in sight before accepting.

Maintain safe spacing.

Report immediately if you lose visual contact.

Do not confuse sequencing with landing clearance.

The best pilots see the traffic, follow the traffic, and wait for clearance like professionals.
