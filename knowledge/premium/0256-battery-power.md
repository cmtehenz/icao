# Battery Power

**Catalog ID:** 0256  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Battery Power — operating the aircraft on battery electrical supply after generator, alternator, or bus failures have left the battery as the remaining source of electrical energy for essential systems and communication.

Battery Power is not the same as battery failure. Battery failure means the battery itself cannot supply power. Battery power means you are still flying with usable battery energy — but that energy is finite, and every radio call, every instrument, and every unnecessary load shortens the time you have left.

For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, electrical abnormal procedures often leave the crew managing limited battery endurance while navigating to a suitable landing site. You must aviate, shed non-essential loads per the AFM, communicate early and clearly, then land before the battery is exhausted.

Understanding how to report operating on battery power in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Battery Power is the condition in which the aircraft is operating on battery electrical supply alone — typically after loss of generator or alternator output — with limited remaining endurance for radios, instruments, and essential avionics until a landing can be completed.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Energia da bateria / Operando em bateria / Battery Power.

Condição em que a aeronave opera apenas com o suprimento elétrico da bateria — tipicamente após perda do gerador ou alternador — com autonomia limitada para rádios, instrumentos e aviônicos essenciais até que um pouso possa ser concluído.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When operating on battery power, the crew should:

- maintain aircraft control as the first priority
- apply the AFM electrical abnormal checklist and shed non-essential loads
- estimate remaining battery endurance and plan an immediate suitable landing
- advise ATC that you are operating on battery power and may lose radio contact
- request priority handling and vectors to the nearest suitable airport or helipad
- transmit essential information early: position, intentions, souls, fuel if requested

Battery power scenarios commonly include:

- generator or alternator failure with battery still online
- dual electrical source loss leaving only battery bus power
- progressive voltage drop with instruments and radios still usable
- need to land before communication or essential avionics fail
- H130 note: manage electrical load carefully; battery endurance is limited

**Important:** Communicate while you still can. Operating on battery power is an urgency or distress situation depending on remaining endurance and weather. Do not wait until the radios die to tell ATC your plan.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to battery-power reports to:

- provide priority routing and landing clearance
- understand that radio contact may be lost soon
- avoid unnecessary frequency changes or long clearances
- coordinate the nearest suitable aerodrome or helipad
- gather souls on board and fuel remaining when required

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are IFR toward Florianópolis in the H130. The generator fails. Checklist complete. You are now on battery power. Voltage is stable for now, but endurance is limited.

You transmit:

ANAC123, operating on battery power, generator failed, request priority landing Florianópolis, may lose radio contact.

Radar: ANAC123, roger battery power, vectors for runway one four, squawk ident if able.

You keep transmissions short. You land with radios still alive. That is the goal — land on battery power, not after it.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Center: ANAC123, roger operating on battery power, say intentions and endurance if known.
2. Approach: ANAC123, priority approved, expect straight-in runway one eight.
3. Approach: ANAC123, if radio contact lost, continue present heading, expect light signals on final.
4. Center: ANAC123, nearest suitable is Navegantes, advise if acceptable.
5. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Battery power is a countdown, not a status light.

You still have electricity — but you are spending a finite reserve. Every unnecessary load, every long radio conversation, every delay to decide where to land burns minutes you may need for the approach.

In the H130 academy, treat generator loss with battery remaining as a time-critical abnormal. Checklist. Shed loads. Tell ATC early. Land at the nearest suitable site — not the most convenient one an hour away.

Brazilian IFR weather can force you toward coastal airports with good approaches. Choose early while the radios still work. If contact may be lost, say so before it happens so ATC can plan light signals or lost-comm procedures.

Battery failure is when the reserve is gone. Battery power is when you still have a chance — if you use it wisely.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Your generator fails and you are operating on battery power with limited endurance. What do you do and what do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain control, apply the electrical abnormal checklist, shed non-essential loads, and plan an immediate landing at the nearest suitable airport. I would advise ATC that I am operating on battery power after generator failure, request priority handling, and warn that radio contact may be lost. I would keep transmissions short and land before the battery is exhausted.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing a long IFR sector on battery power to reach the planned destination.  
  ✔ Divert to the nearest suitable aerodrome while radios and instruments still work.

- ❌ Making long radio explanations that drain battery and block the frequency.  
  ✔ Short, essential transmissions: failure, limitation, intention, request.

- ❌ Confusing "battery power" with "battery failure" in the report.  
  ✔ Battery power means you still have supply; battery failure means the reserve is gone.

- ❌ Not warning ATC that radio contact may be lost.  
  ✔ State early: may lose radio contact, so ATC can prepare lost-comm handling.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

IFR at eight thousand feet toward Curitiba. Generator fails. You are on battery power. Nearest suitable is Joinville, twenty minutes away. Destination is forty-five minutes.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

ATC asks you to continue to Curitiba for traffic sequencing. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, operating on battery power, generator failed, request priority diversion Joinville, may lose radio contact. Second task: ANAC123, unable continue to Curitiba on battery power, require diversion Joinville. I would not accept a longer routing that risks losing radios and instruments before landing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Battery power means you still have a window — and the window is closing.

Checklist. Shed loads. Speak early. Land soon.

Do not confuse this lesson with battery failure. Failure is silence. Battery power is the last useful minutes of electricity.

In the H130, those minutes buy you a safe landing if you spend them on essentials only.

Name the condition. Warn about possible radio loss. Request priority. Get on the ground.

That is how you finish the flight still talking — and still flying.
