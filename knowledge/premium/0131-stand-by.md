# Stand By

**Catalog ID:** 0131  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Stand By — an ICAO-standard phrase used to request a brief pause in communication.

Stand By means: wait. I will call you.

It is issued by ATC when the controller needs time to handle another priority, look up information, or coordinate before responding. Pilots can also use Stand By when they need a moment before responding to a non-urgent ATC inquiry.

In the H130, flying into congested Brazilian terminal areas like Congonhas or São Paulo, Stand By is a common phrase — and understanding it correctly prevents unnecessary re-calls and frequency congestion.

## 📖 Meaning

Stand By is an ICAO-standard phrase meaning do not call me — I will call you. It indicates that the addressed station should wait for a transmission on the same frequency without reinitiating contact. It does not grant any clearance or permission. It requests a brief pause in communication only.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Aguarde / Espere.

Frase ICAO padrão que significa "não me chame — eu chamarei você." Indica que a estação destinatária deve aguardar uma transmissão na mesma frequência sem reiniciar o contato. Não concede nenhuma autorização. Solicita apenas uma breve pausa na comunicação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC transmits Stand By, the pilot should:

- cease transmissions on that frequency immediately
- wait on the frequency for ATC to respond
- not re-call ATC unless a genuine safety need arises
- not interpret Stand By as a clearance or permission to act
- wait a reasonable time, then re-call if no response is received

Stand By operations commonly include:

- ATC needs to coordinate your request with another sector
- controller is handling a priority transmission on another frequency
- your departure clearance is being calculated or coordinated
- ATC needs a moment to verify traffic information

**Important:** Stand By is not a clearance. Stand By is not approval. If ATC says Stand By after you request takeoff clearance, you do not have clearance to depart. You wait. Act only when the clearance is explicitly issued.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers use Stand By to:

- manage high-workload moments on frequency
- hold a pilot's request while coordinating with adjacent sectors
- acknowledge receipt of a request without yet answering it
- prevent unnecessary re-calls that clutter the frequency
- signal that a response is coming

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on the ground at Congonhas, holding short of runway one seven right, ready for departure.

You transmit:

Congonhas Tower, ANAC123, holding short runway one seven right, ready for departure.

Tower is handling an arriving aircraft on short final. Tower responds:

ANAC123, stand by.

This means: I heard you, I am busy, do not call again, I will come back to you.

You continue to hold position and monitor the frequency.

Thirty seconds later:

ANAC123, runway one seven right, cleared for takeoff, wind one eight zero at ten.

Now you have a clearance. You read back:

Cleared for takeoff runway one seven right, ANAC123.

If you had interpreted Stand By as permission to line up or begin your takeoff roll, that would have been a serious runway incursion error. Stand By is a pause — nothing more.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, stand by — I have traffic on short final.
2. Ground: ANAC123, stand by for start clearance.
3. Approach: ANAC123, stand by — coordinating your request.
4. Delivery: ANAC123, stand by for your clearance.
5. Control: ANAC123, stand by — frequency congestion.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, standing by.
2. Standing by, ANAC123.
3. ANAC123, wilco, standing by.
4. ANAC123, roger, standing by.
5. Standing by on this frequency, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Stand By is a professional pause.

When you receive Stand By, the frequency discipline required is to go quiet.

Many pilots — especially newer ones — re-call after thirty or sixty seconds because they are anxious or unsure if ATC heard them.

That re-call clogs the frequency.

If ATC said Stand By, ATC heard you. ATC is working on your request. Wait.

The exception: if a genuine safety need arises while you are standing by — another aircraft on final, an incursion developing, a mayday — you call immediately.

But for routine requests, Stand By means: be patient, stay on frequency, wait for the call.

In the H130, single-pilot operations in busy Brazilian terminal areas mean you will receive Stand By regularly. Use the time productively:

Check performance data. Review the approach plate. Confirm the ATIS. Be ready.

When ATC returns, have your read back prepared.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC says "Stand By" after you request a departure clearance. What does this mean, and what do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Stand By means ATC has received my request and I should wait on the frequency for a response without re-calling. It does not grant a clearance. I would stop transmitting and wait on the frequency for ATC to come back. I would not begin my takeoff roll or move the aircraft. Only an explicit clearance authorizes action. I would re-call only if a safety issue developed or after an unreasonably long time with no response.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Interpreting Stand By as approval to proceed with the requested action.  
  ✔ Stand By is a pause only. Wait for an explicit clearance before acting.

- ❌ Re-calling ATC after thirty seconds because you are impatient.  
  ✔ Wait on frequency. ATC heard you. They will return when able.

- ❌ Responding "Stand By, wilco" — using Wilco with a pause instruction.  
  ✔ Respond with Standing by, ANAC123 — not Wilco, which implies compliance with an action.

- ❌ Switching frequency while standing by, missing the ATC response.  
  ✔ Stay on the frequency ATC told you to stand by on until they return.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are at Curitiba requesting your IFR clearance from Delivery. Delivery responds: "ANAC123, stand by."

**Your first task**

What do you do? How long do you wait before calling again?

**Your second task**

After two minutes, Delivery still has not called back and you are approaching your departure slot. What is the appropriate action?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would respond "Standing by, ANAC123" and remain on frequency without re-calling. For a routine IFR clearance, I would allow at least three to five minutes before a follow-up call, as coordination with adjacent centers can take time. If my departure slot was approaching and I had not received my clearance, I would re-call professionally: Curitiba Delivery, ANAC123, standing by for clearance, departure slot in five minutes. This is a legitimate operational need — not impatience — and the timing information helps ATC prioritize.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Stand By is a test of frequency discipline.

The professional pilot waits quietly, monitors the frequency, and remains ready.

The anxious pilot re-calls every thirty seconds and clutters the frequency.

Train the discipline to wait.

ATC is working.

When they are ready, they will call you.

When they do — be ready with your readback.

Stand By. Wait professionally. Act only on clearance.
