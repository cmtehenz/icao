# Check Pilot

**Catalog ID:** 0348  
**Category:** Crew  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Check Pilot — a qualified pilot authorized to conduct line checks, proficiency checks, or training assessments from the flight deck, evaluating standards while the operation remains safe under clear role definition.

ICAO Part 1 English distinguishes check pilot from captain, first officer, and relief pilot. The check pilot may occupy a seat and intervene for safety, but the training or checking purpose is evaluation — not routine cruise relief.

For H130 ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, check flights are common: instructor or check pilot on board, candidate under assessment, ATC still hearing only the aircraft callsign. Role clarity keeps CRM clean.

Master: check pilot, line check, proficiency check, safety pilot intervention.

## 📖 Meaning

Check Pilot is a pilot authorized to examine or assess another pilot's performance during training, line operation, or proficiency checks, ensuring standards are met while retaining the authority to intervene for safety.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Piloto examinador / Check Pilot.

Piloto autorizado a examinar ou avaliar o desempenho de outro piloto durante treinamento, operação de linha ou cheques de proficiência, garantindo o cumprimento dos padrões e mantendo autoridade para intervir por segurança.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a check pilot is on board, the crew should:

- brief who is candidate, who is check pilot, and who is PIC under the check rules
- define intervention policy before engine start
- keep ATC communications standard under the aircraft callsign
- allow the check pilot to take control if safety requires
- avoid letting evaluation pressure silence safety callouts

Check pilot scenarios commonly include:

- line check / proficiency check in progress
- check pilot intervention, assuming control
- continue / discontinue check after an abnormal event
- normal ATC phraseology regardless of checking status

**Important:** Do not tell ATC "we are on a check ride" unless operationally relevant. Fly and talk as a normal flight. Role titles matter inside the crew briefing and exam answers.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers generally treat the flight as any other flight, but the concept matters when:

- a safety intervention changes who is flying
- an examiner question tests role vocabulary in English
- training flights request specific patterns or repeats
- incapacitation or abnormal events require clear control identity
- operators describe checking roles accurately in English

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

H130 training out of Jundiaí: a check pilot occupies the seat beside the candidate captain.

ATC issues clearances to the callsign. The candidate flies. The check pilot monitors and is ready to intervene.

If the candidate mishandles a critical moment, the check pilot takes control and may say, internally, "I have control." ATC may simply hear a go-around or a revised intention — not a lecture about the check.

Professional. Quiet. Safe.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, cleared for takeoff, wind calm.
2. Approach: ANAC123, make another approach, report runway in sight.
3. Tower: ANAC123, go around, I say again go around, fly runway heading.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say intentions after the go-around.
5. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, full stop approved.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, cleared for takeoff.
2. ANAC123, wilco, will report runway in sight.
3. ANAC123, going around, runway heading.
4. ANAC123, request vectors for another approach.
5. ANAC123, cleared to land, full stop.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Check pilot means standards and safety net.

It does not mean the candidate stops thinking. CRM still requires challenge and response.

Brazilian Portuguese: piloto examinador or instrutor/check. Prefer check pilot for ICAO English when the role is assessment.

Do not confuse check pilot with relief pilot. One evaluates. One relieves fatigue duty.

In ICAO Delta H130 training, treat every check as a real flight on frequency. Perfect English readbacks. No "exam excuses" to ATC.

If the check pilot takes control, the priority is aircraft path. Explain roles later on the ground.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is a check pilot, and how is that different from a relief pilot?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A check pilot is authorized to assess another pilot's performance during training or proficiency checks and may intervene for safety. A relief pilot temporarily takes a duty seat to manage fatigue on long operations. Checking is evaluation; relief is duty substitution.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Calling every instructor a relief pilot.  
  ✔ Use check pilot / instructor for assessment roles.

- ❌ Announcing check-ride details unnecessarily on frequency.  
  ✔ Use standard operational ATC English unless relevant.

- ❌ Freezing CRM because "the examiner is watching."  
  ✔ Make safety callouts anyway — that is the standard.

- ❌ Unclear "I have control" discipline during intervention.  
  ✔ Use clear transfer-of-control words immediately.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

On an H130 proficiency check the candidate destabilizes on short final. The check pilot takes control and goes around.

**Your first task**

What should be transmitted to Tower?

**Your second task**

Tower asks intentions. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, going around. Second task: ANAC123, request vectors for another approach. I would not explain the check details on frequency unless asked something operationally relevant; safety and intentions come first.
