# Steep Descent

**Catalog ID:** 0180  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Steep Descent — the high-rate-of-descent maneuver executed either as an emergency technique, a performance requirement, or an ATC-expedited descent instruction.

A steep descent is not a standard descent. It is a deliberate choice to lose altitude rapidly — whether to clear traffic, meet a constraint, respond to a depressurization, or take advantage of helicopter performance.

For the H130, steep descent capability is one of the aircraft's operational advantages. Helicopters can descend at angles and rates that fixed-wing aircraft cannot match. That capability is valuable — and it requires precise communication with ATC to avoid creating hazards.

Understanding when ATC requires an expedite descent, how to acknowledge it, and how to communicate actual descent capability in ICAO English is essential for professional operations.

## 📖 Meaning

Steep Descent is a high-rate-of-descent maneuver in which an aircraft descends at an angle or rate significantly greater than standard cruise descent, executed at pilot discretion in performance operations, at ATC request to expedite descent, or as an emergency technique to vacate altitude rapidly in response to an abnormal condition.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Descida acentuada / Descida íngreme.

Manobra de descida com alta taxa na qual uma aeronave desce em ângulo ou taxa significativamente maior do que a descida de cruzeiro padrão, executada à discrição do piloto em operações de desempenho, a pedido do controle para agilizar a descida, ou como técnica de emergência para abandonar a altitude rapidamente em resposta a uma condição anormal.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When executing a steep descent, the crew should:

- confirm the rate of descent with ATC if expedite is requested
- advise ATC of actual descent performance in feet per minute if relevant
- comply with speed restrictions during the descent
- confirm level-off altitude before initiating the maneuver
- advise ATC when descent rate returns to normal
- monitor terrain clearance and traffic carefully during high-rate descent

Steep descent operations commonly include:

- ATC instruction to expedite descent through a flight level
- emergency descent after rapid depressurization
- performance descent to meet a crossing restriction
- helicopter operational descent exploiting higher descent rates
- traffic avoidance maneuver at ATC request

**Important:** For the H130, the operational descent rate capability is considerably higher than most fixed-wing traffic in the same airspace. If ATC requests expedite descent, confirm you can comply and advise when reaching the assigned altitude. Never exceed aircraft limits in steep descent — Vne in descent configuration and rotor RPM limits are always active.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers request expedite descent to:

- separate conflicting traffic at adjacent levels
- clear an aircraft from an airspace block required by another aircraft
- meet a time-critical crossing restriction in terminal airspace
- sequence arrivals more efficiently during high-traffic periods
- respond to traffic conflict with descending aircraft

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are in your H130 at flight level nine zero, inbound to Congonhas from Navegantes.

Radar calls:

ANAC123, traffic alert, expedite descent to flight level six zero, traffic at flight level eight zero climbing through your level, report leaving flight level nine zero.

You configure for steep descent — nose down, collective reduced, speed controlled.

You respond:

ANAC123, expediting descent to flight level six zero, leaving flight level nine zero now.

Radar acknowledges.

You descend at one thousand five hundred feet per minute, significantly faster than the traffic climbing below you.

Reaching flight level six zero, you stabilize.

ANAC123, level flight level six zero.

Radar:

ANAC123, roger, maintain flight level six zero, traffic separated.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Radar: ANAC123, expedite descent to flight level six zero, traffic below climbing.
2. Radar: ANAC123, descend immediately to flight level five five, report leaving current level.
3. Control: ANAC123, what is your maximum descent rate available?
4. Radar: ANAC123, maintain two thousand feet per minute or greater to flight level five zero.
5. Radar: ANAC123, normal rate of descent approved, continue descent flight level four zero.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

When ATC says "expedite" — they mean now.

It is not "descend when convenient." It is not "please descend a little faster."

Expedite means there is a traffic conflict and your rapid descent is the solution.

In the H130, you have an advantage here. Your descent rate in a steep maneuver is far greater than most commercial fixed-wing traffic sharing the same airspace. Use it.

But control it. Speed limits in descent exist. Vne exists. Rotor RPM limits exist.

A steep descent is not an uncontrolled drop. It is maximum performance — within the aircraft's envelope.

Also: report. Tell ATC when you are leaving a level. Tell ATC when you reach the assigned level. These are not optional calls during traffic conflict situations — they are how ATC confirms separation is achieved.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC requests you expedite descent from flight level nine zero to flight level six zero due to traffic. Describe what you do and the communications required.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would immediately configure for the highest safe descent rate — in the H130, reducing collective and controlling speed to maintain descent within aircraft limits. My first transmission: ANAC123, expediting descent to flight level six zero, leaving flight level nine zero. I would monitor descent rate and control speed throughout. On reaching flight level six zero I would call: ANAC123, level flight level six zero. If ATC requested my descent rate, I would advise: ANAC123, approximately one thousand five hundred feet per minute.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Responding "wilco" to an expedite descent request and then descending at normal rate.  
  ✔ Expedite means maximum practicable descent rate within aircraft limits.

- ❌ Not reporting when leaving the current altitude after expedite instruction.  
  ✔ Report immediately: ANAC123, leaving flight level nine zero.

- ❌ Not reporting level after reaching the expedite target altitude.  
  ✔ Always report level: ANAC123, level flight level six zero.

- ❌ Exceeding aircraft limits during steep descent.  
  ✔ High-rate descent is within limits. Vne and rotor RPM must always be respected.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Expediting Descent to Flight Level Six Zero, Leaving Flight Level Nine Zero

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, EK-speh-dy-ting deh-SENT to FLYT LEV-ul SIX ZE-ro, LEE-ving FLYT LEV-ul NYN ZE-ro

**Word Stress**

- Expediting → EK-speh-dy-ting (four syllables, stress on first)
- Descent → deh-SENT (stress on second syllable)
- Leaving → LEE-ving

Together:

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**STEEP** — **S**tart immediately when expedite is ordered, **T**ransmit when leaving current level, **E**nforce aircraft speed and RPM limits throughout, **E**nunciate rate if ATC asks, **P**rovide level report when target altitude is reached.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are at flight level nine zero inbound to Congonhas. Radar calls: "ANAC123, expedite descent to flight level six zero, traffic at flight level eight zero climbing, report leaving."

**Your first task**

Respond to ATC and initiate the maneuver.

**Your second task**

Reaching flight level seven five, you notice a vibration that limits your safe descent rate to five hundred feet per minute. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First response: ANAC123, expediting descent to flight level six zero, leaving flight level nine zero. For the second task, I would advise ATC immediately: ANAC123, reducing descent rate, airframe vibration, maximum descent five hundred feet per minute, continuing to flight level six zero. This keeps ATC informed of the changed situation so they can adjust separation if needed.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Steep descent is maximum performance — not maximum risk.

When ATC says expedite, they have a reason. A conflict. A traffic situation. Time is short.

Configure the aircraft. Descend at your best safe rate. Report departure from the level. Report arrival at the target.

In the H130, your performance is an asset. You can descend fast, slow, vertically if needed.

Use that performance. Control it. Report it.

Steep descent done well is invisible to the passengers. It is precise, controlled, and professional.

Fast down. Level. Reported. Done.
