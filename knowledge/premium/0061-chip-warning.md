# Chip Warning

**Catalog ID:** 0061  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the chip detector warning — a sign of metal in your oil system.

Chip Warning means the chip detector has found ferrous metal particles in the engine or gearbox oil — indicating internal component wear or damage.

In the H130, chip detectors monitor the main gearbox and engine oil systems.

Metal in the oil means something inside is breaking apart.

You must aviate first, reduce power, then communicate with the correct urgency call.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Chip Warning is an indication that ferrous metal particles have been detected in the engine or gearbox oil, suggesting internal mechanical damage.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Chip Warning occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the first priority
- reduce power and monitor engine and gearbox parameters
- perform appropriate emergency procedures from the flight manual
- declare Mayday if catastrophic failure is suspected
- declare Pan Pan if warning is active but flight remains stable

Chip Warning reporting commonly includes:

- chip warning or chip detector activated
- metal particles in oil, reducing power
- request vectors to nearest suitable airport
- souls on board and fuel remaining when requested

**Important:** A chip warning with abnormal vibrations or rising temperature is distress — Mayday. An isolated chip warning with stable flight may warrant Pan Pan. Aviate first — then communicate clearly with standard phraseology.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Chip Warning declarations to:

- clear traffic from the aircraft's route
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable airport
- coordinate priority landing and emergency services
- request souls on board and fuel remaining
- prepare for possible mechanical failure on approach

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at one thousand eight hundred feet in your H130 from Curitiba toward Joinville.

The chip detector warning illuminates on the caution panel.

You reduce collective and monitor for unusual vibrations.

When stable, you transmit:

Pan Pan Pan Pan, Curitiba Approach, ANAC123, chip warning, reducing power, request vectors to Joinville.

Approach clears traffic and provides direct routing.

You complete a precautionary landing and report safe on the ground for maintenance inspection.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say nature of chip warning and souls on board.
2. Approach: ANAC123, turn right heading zero four five, descend one thousand five hundred, Joinville one two miles.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway one five cleared to land, emergency services standing by.
4. Tower: ANAC123, wind zero nine zero at zero eight knots, report final.
5. Departure: ANAC123, say engine parameters and intentions.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Chip warning means metal is moving through your oil.

Something inside is wearing or breaking.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Aviate. Listen. Communicate.

Fly the aircraft first.

Reduce power to reduce load on damaged components.

Listen and feel for vibrations, noise, or parameter changes.

Then declare your urgency.

Chip warning alone with stable flight — Pan Pan.

Chip warning with vibration or parameter loss — Mayday.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "chip acionou" or "detector de partículas" on frequency.

Use chip warning or chip detector activated — ICAO standard terms.

In the H130, never reset a chip warning without maintenance inspection.

Metal does not disappear — it accumulates.

Train the reflex to reduce power and land before catastrophic failure.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What does a chip warning indicate and what would you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A chip warning indicates ferrous metal particles detected in the engine or gearbox oil, suggesting internal mechanical damage. I would maintain aircraft control, reduce power, declare Pan Pan Pan Pan or Mayday depending on severity, report chip warning or chip detector activated, and request vectors to the nearest suitable airport. I would upgrade to Mayday if vibrations or abnormal engine parameters develop.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Resetting the chip warning and continuing the flight.  
  ✔ Land and inspect — metal in oil means internal damage.

- ❌ Saying "detector de chip" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use chip warning or chip detector activated.

- ❌ Ignoring chip warning because engine sounds normal.  
  ✔ Damage may be developing — reduce power and land.

- ❌ Not reporting abnormal vibrations with chip warning.  
  ✔ Vibration plus chip warning is Mayday territory.
