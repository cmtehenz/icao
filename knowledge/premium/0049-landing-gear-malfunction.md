# Landing Gear Malfunction

**Catalog ID:** 0049  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers a landing gear malfunction — adapted for helicopter operations.

Landing Gear Malfunction means the landing gear or skid system does not indicate safe configuration for landing.

Most H130 helicopters use fixed skids — but retractable-gear helicopters and ICAO exams still require this phraseology.

You may also report skid damage, gear unsafe indications, or landing gear stuck.

You must assess the situation, prepare for landing, then communicate with the correct urgency call.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Landing Gear Malfunction is any abnormal condition of the landing gear or skid system that affects safe landing configuration.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha no trem de pouso.

Condição anormal do trem de pouso ou sistema de patins que afeta a configuração segura para pouso.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Landing Gear Malfunction occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the first priority
- verify gear or skid position using all available indications
- perform appropriate emergency procedures from the flight manual
- declare Pan Pan or Mayday based on severity
- inform ATC of landing gear unsafe, intentions, and souls on board

Landing Gear Malfunction reporting commonly includes:

- landing gear unsafe or landing gear stuck
- main gear is stuck
- request low pass for gear inspection
- preparing for precautionary landing

**Important:** Unsafe gear indication before landing is urgency — Pan Pan. Collapsed gear or confirmed unsafe landing is distress — Mayday. In skid-equipped H130, report skid damage or abnormal landing configuration using the same standard structure.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Landing Gear Malfunction declarations to:

- clear the runway for inspection or emergency landing
- coordinate low pass for visual gear check
- alert emergency services and airport fire rescue
- request souls on board and fuel remaining
- clear traffic from the approach path

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on final approach to Guarulhos in a retractable-gear helicopter on a training flight, callsign ANAC123.

The gear unsafe warning illuminates and the main gear does not indicate down and locked.

You go around and enter the hold while running the emergency checklist.

You transmit:

Pan Pan Pan Pan, Guarulhos Tower, ANAC123, landing gear unsafe, request low pass for gear inspection.

Tower coordinates a low pass and emergency services.

After confirming gear down, you land safely.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, cleared low pass runway zero nine left for gear inspection.
2. Tower: ANAC123, gear appears down, cleared to land runway zero nine left.
3. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.
4. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services standing by, wind two seven zero at one four knots.
5. Tower: ANAC123, all traffic cleared from your approach, say intentions.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if you received a landing gear unsafe indication on approach?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

After maintaining aircraft control and running the emergency checklist, I would declare Pan Pan Pan Pan, state my callsign, report landing gear unsafe or main gear is stuck, and request a low pass for gear inspection or vectors to hold. I would provide souls on board and fuel remaining when ATC requests. I would upgrade to Mayday if a safe landing becomes seriously doubtful.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "trem travado" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use landing gear unsafe or main gear is stuck.

- ❌ Attempting to land without informing ATC.  
  ✔ Declare Pan Pan and request low pass or emergency services.

- ❌ Declaring Mayday for a simple unsafe indication before assessment.  
  ✔ Use Pan Pan for urgency; Mayday when safe landing is in serious doubt.

- ❌ Forgetting to request low pass for visual gear inspection.  
  ✔ Request low pass — ATC can confirm gear position from the ground.

You are five miles final to Congonhas when the gear unsafe warning activates in your retractable-gear helicopter, callsign ANAC123.
