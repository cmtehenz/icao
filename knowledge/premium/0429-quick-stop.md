# Quick Stop

**Catalog ID:** 0429  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Quick Stop — a rapid deceleration from forward flight to a hover or near-hover, used to stop short of a landing area or to abort a translating approach.

Quick stop is core H130 handling for confined areas, unexpected obstacles, and training profiles across Brazilian operations.

You must explain the maneuver, go-around alternatives, and radio calls when aborting an approach in ICAO English for Parts 2 and 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Quick Stop is a helicopter maneuver that rapidly converts forward kinetic energy into a controlled deceleration to a hover or slow flight, typically by coordinated flare, collective management, and anti-torque control, without exceeding aircraft limitations.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Parada rápida / quick stop.

Manobra de helicóptero que converte rapidamente a energia cinética de avanço em desaceleração controlada até o pairamento ou voo lento, tipicamente por flare coordenado, gestão do coletivo e controle antitorque, sem exceder as limitações da aeronave.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When performing a quick stop, the pilot should:

- initiate early enough to stop before the obstacle or landing point
- flare smoothly while managing rotor RPM and collective
- maintain coordinated anti-torque as power changes
- avoid excessive nose-up attitudes and low-G or settling conditions
- transition to a stable hover or go around if the stop is unstable

Quick stop reporting commonly includes:

- aborting approach, quick stop to hover
- stopping short, remaining clear of obstacle
- unable to complete landing, going around
- established hover, request landing or departure

**Important:** A quick stop is a precision energy-management maneuver — not an abrupt yank. Poor technique can lead to low rotor RPM, settling, or loss of tail rotor effectiveness. Train it. Brief it. Do not invent it in a panic.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers need to understand quick-stop related calls to:

- know an approach has been aborted short of the landing area
- protect the hover or go-around path from other traffic
- re-sequence the helicopter for another approach
- coordinate ground obstacles or vehicles that forced the stop
- maintain awareness of aircraft that suddenly stop translating

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on approach to a confined helipad in your H130. On short final a crane swings into the approach path.

You execute a quick stop to a hover clear of the crane and transmit:

Aborting approach, stopping short, crane obstacle, remaining clear, request orbit.

You hold or depart, then return when the obstacle is clear.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger aborting approach, report established hover or going around.
2. Tower: ANAC123, remain clear of crane, orbit east, advise ready.
3. Tower: ANAC123, obstacle reported clear, cleared for another approach.
4. Tower: ANAC123, say intentions after quick stop.
5. Tower: ANAC123, cleared departure from present position.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, aborting approach, will report established hover.
2. ANAC123, remaining clear, orbiting east, will advise ready.
3. ANAC123, commencing another approach, obstacle clear.
4. ANAC123, established hover, request landing — or going around.
5. ANAC123, departing present position as cleared.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Quick stop buys you a second chance — if you fly it correctly.

Flare, collective, pedals, eyes on the landing area and the escape. In the H130, energy bleeds fast; do not balloon into obstacles or settle with power.

Part 3 loves: obstacle on short final — what do you do? Answer: abort approach, quick stop or go around clear of the obstacle, report stopping short or going around, request orbit.

If the stop is messy, convert to a go-around. A bad hover after a rushed stop is worse than leaving the area.

Brazilian confined pads and construction sites make this a real call, not a textbook trick.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

An obstacle appears on short final. You perform a quick stop. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would stop clear of the obstacle and transmit: aborting approach, stopping short, obstacle ahead, remaining clear, request orbit. If the quick stop is unstable, I would go around instead and report going around, obstacle on approach. I would not continue to land through an obstructed path.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing the landing after seeing a late obstacle.  
  ✔ Abort early. Quick stop or go around.

- ❌ Not telling ATC why you stopped.  
  ✔ State obstacle and remaining clear so traffic can be managed.

- ❌ Aggressive flare that risks low-G or rotor droop.  
  ✔ Smooth energy management within AFM limits.

- ❌ Hovering indefinitely in the approach path after the stop.  
  ✔ Orbit or depart; free the approach for others.
