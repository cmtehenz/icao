# Trim Runaway

**Catalog ID:** 0273  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Trim Runaway — uncommanded continuous trim movement that can rapidly create heavy control forces or dangerous pitch or roll attitudes, requiring immediate memory actions, system isolation, and urgent ATC communication.

Trim runaway is time-critical. A runaway stabilizer or trim tab can outrun the pilot's ability to hold the aircraft if not stopped quickly. The first priority is stop the trim — cutout switches, breakers, or AFM memory items — then fly, then talk.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, trim systems differ from transport-jet stabilizer trim; do not invent a runaway H130 stabilizer. Many helicopters have force trim or beep trim concepts — use analogies carefully. ICAO exams heavily test jet-style trim runaway English.

Part 2 and Part 3: trim runaway, controls heavy, request priority landing after the aircraft is secured.

## 📖 Meaning

Trim Runaway is an uncommanded continuous operation of a trim system that moves control surfaces or tabs without pilot input, potentially producing severe out-of-trim forces or unsafe attitudes, and requiring immediate memory actions to stop the trim, abnormal procedures, and ATC notification when the aircraft is under control but limited.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Trim runaway / Corrida de trim (trim em runaway).

Operação contínua não comandada de um sistema de trim que move superfícies ou tabs sem comando do piloto, podendo produzir forças severas fora de trim ou atitudes inseguras, e exigindo ações de memória imediatas para parar o trim, procedimentos anormais e notificação ao ATC quando a aeronave estiver sob controle mas limitada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When trim runs away, the crew should:

- execute memory items immediately: hold the controls, stop the trim with cutouts per AFM
- do not delay the cutout to make a radio call
- re-trim manually if available and assess residual control forces
- advise ATC of trim runaway, aircraft under control or not, and intentions
- request priority landing if control forces remain high or systems are isolated
- keep H130 discussion factual — no invented stabilizer runaway on the type

Trim runaway scenarios commonly include:

- pitch trim runaway nose-up or nose-down
- roll or yaw trim runaway on some types
- high control forces after cutout
- need for immediate level-off and priority return
- exam: memory action first, then Mayday/Pan Pan as required

**Important:** Fly the airplane. Stop the trim. Then communicate. A perfect Mayday while the stabilizer runs to the stop is the wrong order. Once secured, tell ATC you had a trim runaway and need priority if forces remain abnormal.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use trim runaway reports to:

- provide immediate priority and simple vectors
- avoid assigning climbs, descents, or turns that increase workload
- clear the longest suitable runway
- alert emergency services for a possible control-force emergency
- give the crew time and altitude to complete checklists

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are climbing out of Guarulhos in a jet training scenario. Pitch trim runs nose-down uncommanded. Control forces build fast.

You hold the column, hit the trim cutouts, and stop the runaway. The aircraft is heavy but controllable.

Then you call:

ANAC123, trim runaway, trim isolated, controls heavy, request priority return Guarulhos, longest runway.

Departure: ANAC123, roger trim runaway, turn left heading one eight zero, descend three thousand, priority approved, expect runway two seven Right.

You fly with two hands if needed. You do not re-engage electric trim out of curiosity.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Departure: ANAC123, roger trim runaway, say aircraft control status.
2. Departure: ANAC123, priority return approved, expect longest runway.
3. Approach: ANAC123, shallow turns, maintain present speed if able.
4. Tower: ANAC123, runway two seven Right, cleared to land, services standing by.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Trim runaway is a hands-on emergency first. Radio is second.

Hold. Cut out. Breathe. Then speak: trim runaway, trim isolated, controls heavy — that sequence tells ATC you are flying a wounded but controlled airplane.

If you cannot control pitch, it is Mayday. If you have stopped the runaway but forces are high, Pan Pan or priority urgency still fits — do not under-call.

In the H130, respect whatever force-trim or beep-trim system the AFM describes. Do not import jet stabilizer runaway memory items onto the helicopter without type basis. Do import the communication discipline: secure, then report, then land.

Never "test" the electric trim again after a runaway until maintenance says so.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Pitch trim runs away. You stop it with the cutout. Controls are heavy but the aircraft is flyable. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

After stopping the trim with the AFM memory actions, I would advise ATC of a trim runaway, that trim is isolated, and that controls are heavy but the aircraft is under control. I would request priority return or diversion to the nearest suitable airport and the longest runway, and ask for simple vectors and shallow maneuvers.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Making the radio call before stopping the runaway trim.  
  ✔ Memory items first; communicate when the aircraft is secured.

- ❌ Saying only "trim problem" without "runaway" or control status.  
  ✔ Use trim runaway, trim isolated, controls heavy / under control.

- ❌ Re-engaging electric trim to "see if it works now."  
  ✔ Leave it isolated; land; write it up.

- ❌ Accepting complex SID turns with extreme control forces.  
  ✔ Request simple vectors and priority return.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Trim runaway is stopped with hands and cutouts — not with a speech.

Secure the airplane. Then say trim runaway, trim isolated, controls heavy. Ask for priority and a simple path home.

Stop the trim. Fly the force. Talk when you can. Land with the system left alone.
