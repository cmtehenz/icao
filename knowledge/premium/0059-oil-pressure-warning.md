# Oil Pressure Warning

**Catalog ID:** 0059  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the most serious engine warnings in flight.

Oil Pressure Warning means oil pressure has dropped below safe limits — the engine may seize if lubrication is lost.

In the H130, the turboshaft engine depends on continuous oil pressure for cooling and lubrication.

A low oil pressure warning demands immediate action and clear communication.

You must aviate first, reduce power if required, then communicate with the correct urgency call.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Oil Pressure Warning is an indication that engine oil pressure has fallen below safe operating limits, threatening engine damage or failure.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Oil Pressure Warning occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the first priority
- reduce power and monitor oil pressure and temperature
- perform appropriate emergency procedures from the flight manual
- declare Mayday if engine failure is imminent
- declare Pan Pan if pressure is low but stable

Oil Pressure Warning reporting commonly includes:

- low oil pressure or oil pressure warning
- reducing power, monitoring engine parameters
- request vectors to nearest suitable airport
- souls on board and fuel remaining when requested

**Important:** Rapidly falling oil pressure with rising temperature is distress — Mayday. A marginal warning with stable parameters may warrant Pan Pan. Aviate first — then communicate clearly with standard phraseology.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Oil Pressure Warning declarations to:

- clear traffic from the aircraft's route
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable airport
- coordinate priority landing and emergency services
- request souls on board and fuel remaining
- prepare for possible engine failure on approach

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at two thousand five hundred feet in your H130 over the coast near Florianópolis.

The oil pressure warning illuminates and the gauge shows pressure dropping toward the red line.

You reduce collective and monitor engine parameters.

When stable, you transmit:

Pan Pan Pan Pan, Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, low oil pressure, reducing power, request vectors to Florianópolis.

Approach clears traffic and provides direct routing.

Oil pressure stabilizes at a low but manageable level.

You complete a precautionary landing and report safe on the ground.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say oil pressure reading and souls on board.
2. Approach: ANAC123, turn right heading one eight zero, descend two thousand, Florianópolis one zero miles.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway one four cleared to land, emergency services standing by.
4. Tower: ANAC123, wind one six zero at one two knots, report final.
5. Departure: ANAC123, say nature of engine problem and intentions.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Oil pressure is the lifeblood of your engine.

Without it, metal eats metal.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Aviate. Reduce. Communicate.

Fly the aircraft first.

Reduce power to reduce engine load.

Watch pressure and temperature together.

Then declare your urgency.

Rapid drop with high temperature — Mayday.

Low but stable — Pan Pan.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "pressão de óleo baixa" or "óleo caindo" on frequency.

Use low oil pressure or oil pressure warning — ICAO standard terms.

In the H130, do not ignore a flickering oil pressure light.

A seized engine in a helicopter is a very bad day.

Train the reflex to reduce power and call ATC from a stable aircraft.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if you received an oil pressure warning during cruise?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

After securing the aircraft, I would declare Pan Pan Pan Pan or Mayday depending on severity, state my callsign, report low oil pressure or oil pressure warning, describe that I am reducing power and monitoring engine parameters, and request vectors to the nearest suitable airport. I would upgrade to Mayday if engine failure appears imminent. I would provide souls on board and fuel remaining when ATC requests.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Ignoring a brief oil pressure flicker.  
  ✔ Any oil pressure warning demands immediate attention.

- ❌ Saying "pressão do óleo" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use low oil pressure or oil pressure warning.

- ❌ Maintaining full power with low oil pressure.  
  ✔ Reduce power per flight manual procedures.

- ❌ Not monitoring oil temperature alongside pressure.  
  ✔ Rising temperature with falling pressure indicates imminent failure.

You are twelve miles west of Navegantes at two thousand feet when the oil pressure warning activates in your H130.
