# Density Altitude

**Catalog ID:** 0098  
**Category:** Performance  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers density altitude — the pressure altitude corrected for non-standard temperature, representing the actual performance altitude of the aircraft.

Density Altitude is invisible but critical. High temperature and high humidity reduce air density, which directly reduces engine power, rotor efficiency, and aircraft performance.

In the H130, hot and humid Brazilian summer days can produce density altitudes well above the field elevation at low-altitude airports like Congonhas and Navegantes.

You must calculate density altitude before every departure and landing, understand its effect on performance, and communicate limitations to ATC when necessary.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2, Part 3, and Part 4 communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Density Altitude is the pressure altitude corrected for non-standard temperature. It represents the altitude in the International Standard Atmosphere at which the air density equals the actual air density at the point of operation.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Altitude-densidade.

Altitude de pressão corrigida para temperatura não padrão. Representa a altitude na atmosfera padrão internacional na qual a densidade do ar é igual à densidade real no local de operação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Density Altitude is high, the pilot should:

- calculate density altitude using pressure altitude and OAT
- assess takeoff and landing performance using actual density altitude
- consider payload reduction if performance is insufficient
- request longer runway or prefer runway with headwind
- delay departure to cooler part of day if possible

Density Altitude reporting commonly includes:

- performance limited due high density altitude
- request delay for cooler conditions
- unable to depart with current weight, density altitude limiting
- advise performance-limited departure

**Important:** Density altitude is not directly shown on any instrument. High temperature plus high humidity is a silent performance trap. Calculate before every hot weather operation.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC supports density altitude operations by:

- providing accurate altimeter setting and temperature
- approving runway changes for performance reasons
- accommodating delayed departures for performance
- relaying performance-limited PIREPs to other traffic
- assigning longer runways when possible for departures

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

It is a hot January afternoon in Congonhas — thirty-five degrees Celsius, humidity high.

You calculate density altitude for your H130 departure: nearly three thousand feet, despite the field being at two thousand seven hundred feet.

Your performance tables show you are at maximum allowable takeoff weight.

You transmit:

São Paulo Congonhas Tower, ANAC123, ready for departure, performance limited due high density altitude, request full runway available.

Tower clears you with full runway length and no intersection departure.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How would you explain to ATC that you need the full runway for departure due to high density altitude?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report to ATC that I am performance limited due to high density altitude and request the full runway available with no intersection departure. I would state the reason clearly so ATC understands and can accommodate the request. If the full runway is not sufficient, I would delay departure to cooler conditions or reduce weight.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Assuming performance tables are only for high-altitude airports.  
  ✔ Density altitude affects performance at any field elevation in hot conditions.

- ❌ Not calculating density altitude on hot summer days.  
  ✔ Always calculate OAT, pressure altitude, and resulting density altitude before departure.

- ❌ Saying "calor demais" on frequency instead of a professional report.  
  ✔ Use performance limited due high density altitude.

- ❌ Accepting an intersection departure without verifying the remaining runway is adequate.  
  ✔ Request full runway when performance is marginal.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are departing Congonhas in January at 14:00 local time. Temperature is 35°C. QNH is 1010 hPa. Your H130 is at maximum takeoff weight. The assigned runway offers only an intersection departure.

**Your first task**

What do you request and what is your technical reasoning?

**Your second task**

Tower says the full runway is occupied for twenty minutes. What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would request the full runway with no intersection departure, citing performance limited due high density altitude. The thin hot air reduces power and increases required takeoff distance. If the full runway is occupied for twenty minutes, I would accept the delay — waiting for the full runway or considering a weight reduction to operate safely.
