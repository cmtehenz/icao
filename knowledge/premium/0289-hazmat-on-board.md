# Hazmat on Board

**Catalog ID:** 0289  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Hazmat on Board — reporting hazardous materials carried on the aircraft when an emergency, leak, fire, or diversion requires ATC and emergency services to treat the arrival as a hazmat event. Related to dangerous goods, but taught as a distinct exam phrase: say "hazmat on board" when that is the wording expected in the scenario or by the examiner.

Hazmat on board is a High Part 2 and Part 3 topic. RFF response, isolation distance, and passenger handling change when hazardous materials are involved.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training with H130 Brazilian operations, master both DG and hazmat phrasing as separate tools, then use the one the situation and exam require.

Understanding hazmat on board in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Hazmat on Board means hazardous materials are present on the aircraft, and this fact must be reported to ATC and emergency services during an abnormal or emergency situation so that ground response applies hazmat procedures, protective equipment, and isolation as required by the material and the event.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Material perigoso (hazmat) a bordo / Hazmat on Board.

Significa que materiais perigosos estão presentes na aeronave, e esse fato deve ser reportado ao ATC e aos serviços de emergência durante uma situação anormal ou de emergência para que a resposta em solo aplique procedimentos de hazmat, equipamento de proteção e isolamento conforme o material e o evento.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When hazmat is relevant to an emergency, the crew should:

- declare Mayday or Pan Pan according to the primary threat
- state clearly: hazmat on board
- describe leak, fumes, fire, or contamination if present
- provide material identity, class, or UN number if known
- request firefighters with hazmat capability / emergency services
- for H130: report hazmat the same way for company material, cargo, or spill scenarios
- keep "hazmat" distinct from "dangerous goods" as exam phrases

Hazmat reporting commonly includes:

- hazmat on board
- leak / fumes / fire / no leak
- material identity if known
- request remain clear / hazmat response
- souls and fuel when asked

**Important:** Dangerous goods and hazmat overlap in meaning in the real world. In this academy they are taught as distinct exam phrases. Do not substitute one for the other in a graded answer unless the question allows it. Use the exact wording required.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use hazmat reports to:

- alert RFF for hazmat-level response, not only standard aircraft fire
- keep vehicles and persons outside isolation distances
- coordinate with airport operations and environmental response
- choose parking or runway hold points away from terminals
- pass accurate hazard information to the incident commander

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You smell strong chemical fumes after a container shift in the cabin area. You declare and divert.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Control, ANAC123, chemical fumes, hazmat on board, diverting, request priority landing and hazmat response.

Tower clears you and holds traffic. After landing you stop on the runway:

ANAC123, landed, hazmat on board, fumes continuing, request remain clear, passengers on board pending instructions.

For H130 utility work with chemicals or batteries as regulated cargo, the same call structure protects the responders who approach the aircraft.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger Mayday, confirm hazmat on board, say nature of material if known.
2. Approach: ANAC123, priority approved, hazmat response alerted, expect runway one zero.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, remain on runway, emergency services standing by.
4. Tower: ANAC123, say if leak or fire, souls on board and fuel remaining.
5. Ground: ANAC123, remain in position, hazmat team approaching, keep doors closed until advised.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, affirmative, hazmat on board, corrosive liquid, details from documentation.
2. ANAC123, priority runway one zero, hazmat response alerted.
3. ANAC123, cleared to land, will remain on runway.
4. ANAC123, fumes, no fire visible, souls six, fuel one hour thirty.
5. ANAC123, remaining in position, doors closed until advised.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Hazmat on board tells RFF to change their approach.

It is not a casual synonym for every cargo flight. Use it when hazardous materials are part of the emergency picture.

Lead with the threat: fumes, leak, fire. Then: hazmat on board. Then: what you know about the material.

In ICAO Delta, keep this lesson separate from Dangerous Goods on Board. Same family of risk. Different exam phrase. Answer with the words you are asked for.

On the H130, a battery fire or chemical spill still needs this clarity. Responders cannot guess what is in the cabin.

Say hazmat when that is the correct phrase. Protect the people on the ground.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You have chemical fumes and hazardous materials in the aircraft. What do you report to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare the emergency, report chemical fumes, state that hazmat is on board, and request priority landing with hazmat response or firefighters. I would give the material identity if known, provide souls and fuel when asked, and after landing advise whether there is a leak or fire and follow instructions to remain clear or keep doors closed.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "I have a chemical problem" without "hazmat on board."  
  ✔ Use the standard phrase when hazmat is present.

- ❌ Swapping "dangerous goods" into every hazmat question automatically.  
  ✔ Use "hazmat on board" when that is the taught/exam phrase required.

- ❌ Opening doors into a fume cloud without RFF guidance.  
  ✔ Report status; follow checklist and remain-clear instructions.

- ❌ Omitting leak versus fire in the update.  
  ✔ State fumes, leak, fire, or no fire clearly.
