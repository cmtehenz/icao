# Forced Landing

**Catalog ID:** 0292  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Forced Landing — an immediate landing off-airport or at the nearest suitable site when continued flight is impossible or unsafe, typically after engine failure, fire, or critical systems loss. For H130 helicopter operations over Brazilian terrain — cities, mountains, jungle, and offshore approaches — forced landing is a Critical Part 2 and Part 3 skill tightly linked to autorotation and site selection.

Forced landing is highly relevant for helicopters. Examiners expect Mayday, nature of failure, intentions, and position — then a clear post-landing status.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training, master the radio structure that runs in parallel with flying the forced landing profile.

Understanding forced landing in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Forced Landing is an emergency landing performed when the aircraft cannot continue safe flight to a planned aerodrome, requiring immediate selection of a suitable landing site, Mayday declaration with nature of emergency and intentions, and coordination with ATC for emergency services and possible SAR if the site is remote.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pouso forçado / Forced Landing.

Pouso de emergência realizado quando a aeronave não pode continuar o voo seguro até um aeródromo planejado, exigindo seleção imediata de um local adequado, declaração de Mayday com a natureza da emergência e as intenções, e coordenação com o ATC para serviços de emergência e possível SAR se o local for remoto.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a forced landing is required, the crew should:

- fly the aircraft first — autorotation or forced-landing profile per AFM
- declare Mayday: nature of failure, forced landing, position
- select the best available site: open area, into wind, clear of obstacles
- brief passengers for brace and evacuation
- after landing: report status, request emergency services or SAR as needed
- for H130: treat forced landing as core emergency English for engine failure over terrain

Forced landing reporting commonly includes:

- Mayday, engine failure / fire / other
- forced landing / landing off-airport
- position and site description if able
- souls on board
- landed safely / injuries / request assistance

**Important:** Aviate, navigate, communicate — in that order. A short Mayday during the descent is enough. Do not talk yourself into the trees. Position and intentions matter more than long explanations.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to forced landing calls to:

- alert emergency services or SAR to the landing site
- keep the last known position and track
- clear traffic and coordinate with approach/tower if near an airport
- pass souls and nature of emergency to responders
- maintain contact through the descent and after landing if possible

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are an H130 en route over rural São Paulo state when the engine fails. You enter autorotation and pick a field.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Control, ANAC123, engine failure, forced landing, position fifteen miles west of Sorocaba, souls four.

You complete the autorotation into the field. After touchdown:

ANAC123, forced landing complete, no fire, all souls okay, request assistance.

ATC vectors local responders. Your call turned a silent crash into a located emergency.

Over water the parallel skill is ditching; over terrain it is forced landing. Both are Critical for the H130.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger Mayday forced landing, confirm position and souls on board.
2. Control: ANAC123, SAR and emergency services coordinating, squawk seven seven zero zero.
3. Control: ANAC123, say nature of failure and landing site if known.
4. Approach: ANAC123, radar contact last position, rescue advised.
5. Control: ANAC123, report when landed if able.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, position one five miles west Sorocaba, souls four, forced landing.
2. ANAC123, squawking seven seven zero zero, engine failure.
3. ANAC123, engine failure, landing in open field, forced landing.
4. ANAC123, roger rescue advised, continuing forced landing.
5. ANAC123, landed, no fire, all souls okay, request assistance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Forced landing is the helicopter captain's core emergency: site, profile, radio.

Enter the procedure. Pick the field. Then speak.

Mayday. Failure. Forced landing. Position. Souls.

After landing: status. Fire or no fire. Injuries or okay. Assistance required.

Do not confuse forced landing with ditching. Forced landing is the land/off-airport case. Ditching is water. Know both phrases. Use the correct one.

In Brazilian H130 ops — terrain, wires, confined areas — forced landing English must be as sharp as your autorotation.

Fly it. Say it. Survive it.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You have an engine failure over terrain and will make a forced landing. Describe your actions and radio calls.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would fly the forced landing or autorotation profile, select the best available landing site, and declare Mayday with the nature of the failure, that I am making a forced landing, and my position and souls on board. After landing I would secure the aircraft, check for fire and injuries, and report status to ATC requesting assistance if needed.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Making a long radio speech during autorotation instead of flying.  
  ✔ Short Mayday; aviate first.

- ❌ Saying "ditching" when landing in a field.  
  ✔ Use forced landing for land; ditching for water.

- ❌ Not giving position, hoping radar will "see everything."  
  ✔ Give the best position you can — distance and bearing from a known point.

- ❌ Going silent after a successful touchdown.  
  ✔ Report landed, status, and assistance required.

**Word Stress**

- Engine → EN-jin
- Failure → FAYL-yer
- Forced → FORST
- Landing → LAN-ding

Practice:

Together:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, engine failure, forced landing.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**FORCED** — **F**ly the profile first, **O**pen the Mayday, **R**eport failure type, **C**all forced landing and position, **E**xit after landing with status, **D**emand assistance if needed.
