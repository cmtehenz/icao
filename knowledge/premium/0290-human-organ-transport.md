# Human Organ Transport

**Catalog ID:** 0290  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Human Organ Transport — carriage of human organs for transplant, often under time-critical priority handling, with clear ATC coordination for expeditious routing, approach, and ground transfer. For H130 helicopter operations in Brazil, organ transfer flights between hospitals and airports are highly realistic: short sectors, urban helipads, and priority requests are part of the mission profile.

Human organ transport is a Medium Part 2 and Part 3 topic. Examiners expect polite, precise priority language — usually Pan Pan or a priority request, not dramatic Mayday unless the flight itself is in distress.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training, master the phraseology that gets you sequenced without blocking true emergencies, then apply it to H130 hospital and inter-airport organ runs.

Understanding human organ transport in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Human Organ Transport is the carriage of human organs for transplant, requiring time-critical handling, clear notification to ATC of the medical priority cargo, and coordination for expeditious routing, approach, landing, and ground transfer while maintaining normal flight safety standards.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Transporte de órgão humano / Human Organ Transport.

Transporte de órgãos humanos para transplante, exigindo tratamento com criticidade de tempo, notificação clara ao ATC da carga médica prioritária e coordenação para roteamento, aproximação, pouso e transferência em solo de forma expedita, mantendo os padrões normais de segurança de voo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When transporting a human organ, the crew should:

- notify ATC early: human organ on board / organ transplant flight, request priority if needed
- use Pan Pan for urgency when time critically affects the organ, unless the aircraft itself is in distress
- request direct routing, expedite climb/descent, or priority approach as required
- coordinate expected landing time for ground ambulance/hospital transfer
- for H130: include helipad destination and estimated time on deck
- never sacrifice aircraft safety for schedule — priority is for ATC sequencing, not for breaking minima

Human organ transport reporting commonly includes:

- human organ on board / organ transport flight
- request priority / expedite
- destination hospital helipad or airport
- estimated time of arrival
- souls and fuel when asked

**Important:** Organ priority is not a blank cheque. You still fly stable approaches and respect weather minima. Ask for priority clearly; do not invent a Mayday for a healthy aircraft.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers handle organ flights to:

- provide expeditious routing without compromising separation
- prioritize approach sequencing when medically justified
- coordinate with tower and ground for rapid ambulance access
- balance organ priority against higher-priority aircraft emergencies
- pass ETA information to the destination aerodrome

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You depart a hospital helipad in São Paulo with a transplant organ for Campinas. Time is critical.

You call:

Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, human organ on board, request priority routing to Campinas, organ transport.

Approach gives direct tracking and expedite. Nearing destination:

ANAC123, human organ on board, request priority approach, ambulance standing by on the ground.

After landing at the helipad you report on deck so the transfer can start immediately.

This is core H130 Brazilian mission English — calm, precise, time-aware.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan organ transport, priority approved, proceed direct.
2. Approach: ANAC123, expedite descent, traffic permitting, report field in sight.
3. Tower: ANAC123, priority landing approved, ambulance advised, wind calm, cleared to land.
4. Tower: ANAC123, after landing remain clear of the ambulance access point.
5. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and estimated time of arrival.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Human organ transport is urgency with discipline.

Tell ATC early. Use Pan Pan when the organ timeline justifies urgency. Request priority routing and approach. Give a useful ETA.

Do not shout Mayday because the cooler is on board and the aircraft is fine. Save Mayday for aircraft distress.

In the H130, this mission is real: rooftops, helipads, short hops, ambulance waiting. Your English must be as clean as your landing.

Priority from ATC. Safety from you. Transfer on time because both worked.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You are carrying a human organ for transplant and need priority handling. What do you say to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would advise ATC that I have a human organ on board for transplant and request priority routing or priority approach as needed. If time is critical I would use Pan Pan, state organ transport, and give destination and estimate. I would still fly a safe approach and coordinate for ambulance transfer after landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Declaring Mayday only because an organ is on board and the aircraft is normal.  
  ✔ Use Pan Pan or a clear priority request unless the aircraft is in distress.

- ❌ Waiting until short final to mention the organ.  
  ✔ Notify early so ATC can sequence and alert ground.

- ❌ Accepting an unstable approach "because the organ cannot wait."  
  ✔ Organ priority never overrides safe flight path and minima.

- ❌ Not giving ETA for the ambulance/hospital transfer.  
  ✔ Pass a realistic estimate when useful.
