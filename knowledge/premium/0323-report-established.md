# Report Established

**Catalog ID:** 0323  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Report Established — the pilot report that advises ATC when the aircraft is established on a specified course, radial, localizer, approach path, or holding pattern.

Report Established is a precision checkpoint. Controllers use it to confirm you are on the intended track before issuing the next clearance — approach continuation, descent on the glide path, or further vectors.

For H130 IFR and instrument training in Brazil, established reports appear on localizer intercepts, VOR radials, RNAV final approach courses, and holds. Even when flying visually, ATC may ask you to report established on final or on a published track.

Understanding how to report established in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Report Established instructs the pilot to advise ATC when the aircraft is established on a specified navigational course, approach aid, radial, track, or holding pattern as cleared or requested.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Reporte estabelecido / Report Established.

Instrução para o piloto informar ao controle quando a aeronave estiver estabelecida no curso, auxílio de navegação, radial, rota ou padrão de espera especificado, conforme autorizado ou solicitado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Report Established, the crew should:

- intercept and stabilize on the assigned course, localizer, radial, or hold
- confirm navigation indications are correct and centered
- report established only when truly on the course — not while still intercepting
- include the aid or course when useful: established localizer, established inbound
- continue the procedure and await further clearance as required

Report Established commonly includes:

- established localizer runway one four
- established on the radial
- established inbound on the approach
- established in the hold
- report established on final

**Important:** Established means the aircraft is on course with a stable intercept complete. In the H130, a quick turn can put you near the localizer without being established. Wait for a centered indication and stable track before reporting.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers work with Report Established to:

- confirm successful intercept of the approach or airway course
- authorize further descent on the procedure when required
- sequence traffic once the aircraft is on a known track
- detect failed intercepts early and issue corrective vectors
- coordinate approach clearances with tower and radar

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are being vectored for the ILS runway one four at Florianópolis in the H130.

Approach says:

Approach: ANAC123, turn left heading one one zero, cleared ILS runway one four, report established.

You intercept the localizer, center the course, and stabilize.

You report:

ANAC123, established localizer runway one four.

Approach may then clear you for the approach continuation or hand you to Tower. The established report was the gate for the next instruction.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, cleared ILS runway one four, report established.
2. Approach: ANAC123, intercept the localizer, report established.
3. Control: ANAC123, proceed direct REKPA, report established on course.
4. Approach: ANAC123, enter the hold as published, report established.
5. Approach: ANAC123, cleared RNAV approach, report established final approach course.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

2. ANAC123, established localizer runway one four.
3. ANAC123, established on course.
4. ANAC123, established in the hold.
5. ANAC123, established final approach course.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Established is a status, not a hope.

You are either on the course or you are still joining it. ATC needs the truth.

Brazilian instrument exams and real approach frequencies both punish early established reports. If you call established while still thirty degrees off, the controller may clear descent you are not ready to fly.

In the H130, intercept angles can be steep on vectors. Fly through the cut, center up, then speak.

Localizer. Radial. Final approach course. Hold inbound. Same rule: stable first, report second.

When you say established, you are telling ATC the navigation problem is solved and the procedure can continue.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC clears you for an ILS and asks you to report established. When exactly should you make that report?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I should report established when the aircraft is on the localizer course with a stable intercept complete — course centered and tracking inbound. I should not report established while still turning to intercept or while the course is still moving through a large intercept angle.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting established during the intercept turn.  
  ✔ Report only when on course and stable.

- ❌ Saying only "established" with no reference.  
  ✔ Say: established localizer runway one four.

- ❌ Assuming established means cleared to land.  
  ✔ Established confirms the course. Landing clearance is separate.

- ❌ Continuing descent on the glide path before being established when the procedure requires it.  
  ✔ Follow the published profile and ATC clearance.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Approach says:

Approach: ANAC123, cleared ILS runway one four, report established.

You are still twenty degrees from the localizer during the intercept.

**Your first task**

Do you report established now? What do you say when you are truly established?

**Your second task**

Why does ATC need the established report before further approach instructions?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: No. When established: ANAC123, established localizer runway one four. Second task: The report confirms I am on the published approach course, so ATC can authorize further descent, sequence traffic, or transfer me to Tower with a known track.

Report Established is the handshake between navigation and clearance.

You tell ATC the intercept worked. They tell you what comes next.

In Brazilian H130 IFR flying, protect that word. Use it only when the course is yours.

Stable. Centered. Established. Then speak.
