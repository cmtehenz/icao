# Fire in the Wheel Well

**Catalog ID:** 0277  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Fire in the Wheel Well — fire or overheat indication associated with the landing gear bay, often after takeoff or during climb, requiring checklist action, possible gear extension, distress or urgency communication, and priority return or diversion.

On airliners this is a classic exam topic: wheel-well fire after gear retraction, hot brakes, or hydraulic/electrical fire in the bay. For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations, the type has no retractable wheel well like a jet — but you must speak the concept fluently for Part 2 and Part 3, and relate overheat or brake fire language to skid/wheel gear realities when useful.

Master the English: fire in the wheel well, gear down, request immediate return, fire services standing by.

## 📖 Meaning

Fire in the Wheel Well is a fire or overheat condition in the landing gear bay or wheel-well area, typically indicated by a warning after gear retraction or associated with hot brakes or bay systems, requiring abnormal or emergency procedures, ATC notification, and often an immediate return or diversion with fire services alerted.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Fogo no compartimento do trem / Fire in the Wheel Well.

Condição de fogo ou superaquecimento no compartimento do trem de pouso, tipicamente indicada por um alerta após a retração do trem ou associada a freios quentes ou sistemas do compartimento, exigindo procedimentos anormais ou de emergência, notificação ao ATC e frequentemente retorno ou desvio imediato com serviços de combate a incêndio alertados.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When fire in the wheel well is indicated, the crew should:

- apply the AFM wheel-well fire / overheat checklist immediately
- extend the landing gear if the procedure requires it (cooling / access)
- declare Mayday for confirmed fire; Pan Pan may apply for overheat if procedure and severity allow — prefer Mayday if fire is indicated
- request immediate return or diversion and fire services
- report nature, gear position, intentions; souls and fuel when asked
- for H130: know exam phraseology; relate to brake/gear fire without inventing a jet wheel well

Wheel-well fire reporting commonly includes:

- fire in the wheel well / wheel-well overheat
- gear down (if extended per checklist)
- request immediate return / landing
- fire services standing by
- souls and fuel

**Important:** Do not leave gear up if the checklist requires extension. Communicate gear position so ATC and fire crews know what to expect on landing.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to wheel-well fire reports to:

- clear priority return or diversion path
- alert airport fire and rescue for gear-bay or brake fire risk
- request gear status and landing intentions
- keep the runway clear for possible stop-and-evacuate
- obtain souls, fuel, and fire status updates

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Training scenario: shortly after takeoff from Congonhas, a wheel-well fire warning activates. You run the checklist and extend the gear as required.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Tower, ANAC123, fire in the wheel well, gear down, request immediate return to land.

Tower: ANAC123, roger Mayday, left turn approved, runway three five left available, fire services alerted, say souls and fuel when able.

You continue:

ANAC123, turning left, gear down and locked, fire in the wheel well, souls four, fuel two hours, request shortest approach.

For H130 training, keep the urgency structure; use brake fire or gear fire wording when describing the actual helicopter.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger Mayday fire in the wheel well, cleared to return, runway three five left.
2. Approach: ANAC123, say gear position and fire status.
3. Tower: ANAC123, fire services standing by for possible gear fire.
4. Approach: ANAC123, report if smoke or fire indications continue.
5. Tower: ANAC123, after landing stop on the runway, evacuate if required.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Wheel-well fire is often a takeoff-and-return emergency.

Checklist first — especially gear extension if required. Then Mayday. Then land with fire services ready.

Brazilian pilots may say "fogo no trem" or "freio quente." Prefer fire in the wheel well or wheel-well overheat in ICAO English when that is the exam concept — then state gear position and intentions.

In the H130 you do not retract into a classic jet bay. Still learn the calls. ATC needs to know if you have a gear or brake fire risk on landing.

Do not delay the return to "see if it goes out" at altitude with a fire warning active.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

After takeoff a wheel-well fire warning activates. What do you do and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would apply the checklist immediately, extend the gear if required, declare Mayday stating fire in the wheel well, and request an immediate return or diversion with fire services standing by. I would report gear position, souls and fuel when able, and whether fire or smoke indications continue. After landing I would stop as required and evacuate if necessary.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Leaving gear up when the checklist requires extension.  
  ✔ Follow the AFM — extend if required, then report gear down.

- ❌ Calling only "problem with the gear" without fire language.  
  ✔ Say fire in the wheel well or wheel-well overheat clearly.

- ❌ Continuing the departure without priority return.  
  ✔ Request immediate return; treat as fire until resolved.

- ❌ Describing H130 as having a jet-style wheel well.  
  ✔ Exam English yes; type systems factual.

**Pronunciation:** MAY-day MAY-day MAY-day, AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, FY-er in thuh WEEL WEL, GEER down, reh-KWEST ih-MEE-dee-it ree-TURN

**Word Stress**

- Wheel → WEEL
- Well → WEL
- Gear → GEER
- Immediate → ih-MEE-dee-it
- Return → ree-TURN

Practice:

Fire in the wheel well... gear down... request immediate return.

Together:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, fire in the wheel well, gear down, request immediate return.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**WELL** — **W**arning means checklist now, **E**xtend gear if required, **L**and immediately with Mayday, **L**et fire services know.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Fire in the wheel well is a return-now emergency.

Checklist. Gear as required. Mayday. Land. Fire crews ready.

The H130 may not have a jet wheel well — ICAO English still will. Learn both truths.

Tell ATC the fire location, gear position, and that you need the runway and the trucks.

That is how wheel-well fires end as controlled returns — not as continued climbs with a warning ignored.
