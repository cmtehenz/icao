# Landing Gear Stuck

**Catalog ID:** 0259  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Landing Gear Stuck — a condition in which the landing gear cannot be extended or retracted normally, requiring abnormal or emergency procedures, possible alternate extension, and clear ATC communication for priority handling and emergency services.

Landing gear stuck is critical ICAO exam English. The crew must state whether the gear is stuck up, stuck down, or in an intermediate position, what they are attempting, and what kind of landing they expect. Controllers need that detail to alert RFF and clear the runway.

For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, many H130s use skids rather than retractable wheeled gear. Still teach gear phraseology for the ICAO exam and for multi-type careers. When flying a skid-equipped H130, relate the concept honestly: you do not "stick the gear," but you must still master the English and understand airport emergency response language when other traffic has a gear problem.

Understanding landing gear stuck phraseology is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 critical performance.

## 📖 Meaning

Landing Gear Stuck is a malfunction in which one or more landing gear legs cannot be moved to the commanded position — up, down, or locked — preventing normal extension or retraction and requiring alternate procedures and ATC coordination for a safe landing.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Trem de pouso travado / Landing Gear Stuck.

Malfuncionamento em que uma ou mais pernas do trem de pouso não podem ser movidas para a posição comandada — recolhido, baixado ou travado — impedindo a extensão ou retração normal e exigindo procedimentos alternativos e coordenação com o ATC para um pouso seguro.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When landing gear is stuck, the crew should:

- maintain control and apply the AFM abnormal or emergency gear checklist
- attempt alternate extension if the gear is stuck up
- determine gear position indications: down and locked, unsafe, or unknown
- advise ATC of landing gear stuck and the expected landing type
- request priority landing and RFF standby
- prepare passengers and cabin for a possible abnormal landing

Landing gear stuck scenarios commonly include:

- gear stuck in the up position after failed extension
- gear stuck partially extended with unsafe indication
- one main gear or nose gear stuck, others down
- gear stuck down after takeoff (retraction failure — related concept)
- H130 note: many aircraft use skids; still learn wheeled-gear exam English

**Important:** Be specific. "Landing gear stuck up, attempting alternate extension, request long runway and firefighters" tells ATC what to prepare. "Gear problem" does not.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to stuck-gear reports to:

- provide priority approach and the most suitable runway
- alert RFF for a possible gear-up or abnormal landing
- keep the runway clear and hold other traffic
- obtain souls, fuel, and landing intentions
- coordinate airport emergency plans

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Exam / wheeled-gear scenario: on approach to Congonhas, the gear will not extend normally. Alternate extension is in progress. Indication remains unsafe.

You transmit:

ANAC123, landing gear stuck, unsafe indication, requesting priority landing runway one seven L, firefighters standby.

Tower: ANAC123, roger gear stuck, runway one seven L, wind calm, firefighters rolling, cleared to land at your discretion.

You fly a stable approach. You land only when the checklist and indications allow — or you execute the planned abnormal landing with RFF ready.

In the skid-equipped H130, you may never extend wheels — but you will hear this language when sharing Brazilian airports with jets and turboprops.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger landing gear stuck, say gear position and intentions.
2. Tower: ANAC123, firefighters alerted, expect runway one seven L, advise when ready.
3. Approach: ANAC123, confirm attempting alternate gear extension.
4. Tower: ANAC123, runway cleared, all traffic held, cleared to land.
5. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, landing gear stuck up, attempting alternate extension, requesting priority.
2. ANAC123, firefighters copied, expecting one seven L.
3. ANAC123, affirmative, alternate extension in progress.
4. ANAC123, runway cleared copied, cleared to land.
5. ANAC123, souls five, fuel fifty minutes.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Stuck gear is a configuration emergency with a communication requirement.

Fly the airplane. Run the checklist. Then tell ATC three facts: stuck where, what you are doing about it, and what landing you expect.

On the ICAO exam, examiners want "landing gear stuck," alternate extension language, priority, and firefighters. Give them that structure.

In the H130 with skids, do not invent a retractable gear emergency you do not have. Learn the English for the test. In real academy flying, use accurate skid and landing-gear-of-other-traffic language when Congonhas or Guarulhos holds everyone for a jet with gear stuck.

Critical English is specific English. Position. Procedure. Intention. Request.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Your landing gear will not extend and remains stuck up. What do you do and what do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain control, apply the gear abnormal checklist, and attempt alternate extension as published. I would advise ATC that the landing gear is stuck up, state whether indications remain unsafe, and request priority landing with firefighters on standby. I would land only according to the procedure and prepare for an abnormal or wheels-up landing if the gear cannot be extended.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "gear problem" without position or intention.  
  ✔ State: landing gear stuck up / stuck down / unsafe, requesting priority and RFF.

- ❌ Forcing a normal landing without alternate extension attempts.  
  ✔ Complete the AFM procedure before committing to the landing type.

- ❌ Not requesting firefighters for a possible abnormal landing.  
  ✔ Request RFF standby early so emergency services are in place.

- ❌ Ignoring H130 skid context and inventing false gear calls in real ops.  
  ✔ Use accurate aircraft configuration in real flying; use gear English for exams.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

On final, the gear will not extend. Alternate extension fails. You will land wheels-up. RFF must be ready.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

Tower asks if you can make a low pass for visual gear check. What do you say if fuel and control allow?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, landing gear stuck up, alternate extension failed, requesting wheels-up landing, firefighters. Second task: ANAC123, able low pass for gear check, then full stop. I would only accept the low pass if safe and useful; otherwise I would proceed to the planned abnormal landing.
