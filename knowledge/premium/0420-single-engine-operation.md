# Single Engine Operation

**Catalog ID:** 0420  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Single Engine Operation — continued flight on one engine after failure of the other on a twin-engine helicopter, requiring performance management, OEI procedures, and precise urgency communication with ATC.

Single engine operation is not "almost normal." Performance margins shrink. Climb, hover, and rejected landing options change. ATC must hear the limitation early.

For ICAO Delta pilots in Brazil — including those transitioning to or operating twins alongside H130 experience — you must report engine failure, state single engine, and request priority routing and landing.

Understanding how to communicate single engine operation in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Single Engine Operation is flight continued after one engine has failed on a multi-engine helicopter, using One Engine Inoperative (OEI) procedures to maintain control and performance within remaining limits while diverting or landing as soon as practicable.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Operação com um motor / monomotor (após falha).

Voo continuado após a falha de um motor em helicóptero multimotor, usando procedimentos OEI (One Engine Inoperative) para manter controle e desempenho dentro dos limites restantes, desviando ou pousando assim que possível.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When operating single engine after an engine failure, the crew should:

- complete engine-failure memory items and secure the failed engine per AFM
- assess OEI climb and landing performance for the intended site
- declare pan-pan or mayday based on performance and terrain
- advise ATC: engine failure, single engine operation, intentions
- request priority landing and avoid hover profiles that exceed OEI capability

Single engine reporting commonly includes:

- pan-pan / mayday, engine failure, single engine
- requesting priority landing
- unable hover / requesting runway
- souls and fuel

**Important:** The H130 is single-engine by design; this lesson trains twin-engine OEI English required in ICAO exams and Brazilian multi-engine operations. On the H130, total engine failure is autorotation — different emergency, same need for clear distress English.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers support single engine operation by:

- granting priority landing and simple routing
- offering runways instead of confined pads when requested
- providing wind and weather for performance decisions
- alerting emergency services
- avoiding holds and complex SID/STAR clearances

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Scenario for twin-engine Brazilian ops: outbound from Congonhas, one engine fails. You secure it, confirm OEI controllable flight, and transmit:

Pan-pan pan-pan pan-pan, São Paulo Approach, engine failure, single engine operation, requesting priority return Congonhas for landing.

Approach gives a straight-in, wind, and emergency services. You avoid a hover landing to a tight pad and take the runway.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, pan-pan acknowledged, priority return approved, cleared direct Congonhas.
2. Approach: ANAC123, expect runway one seven left, wind one seven zero at one zero.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land one seven left, emergency services standing by.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say if unable hover or pad landing.
5. Approach: ANAC123, souls on board and fuel remaining.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, pan-pan, engine failure, single engine, priority return Congonhas.
2. ANAC123, runway one seven left, wind copied.
3. ANAC123, cleared to land one seven left.
4. ANAC123, unable hover, require runway landing.
5. ANAC123, souls five, fuel one hour one zero.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Single engine English must carry the performance truth.

"Engine failure, single engine" tells ATC the aircraft still flies — but not like before. Add "unable hover" or "require runway" when OEI charts say so.

In Brazilian city ops, the temptation is to return to the same rooftop you left. OEI hover may be impossible. Say what the aircraft can do, not what the schedule wants.

Upgrade to mayday if you cannot maintain altitude, if fire is present, or if the landing site is marginal. Pan-pan is for controllable OEI with priority need — not for denial.

Train the words until they are automatic. In the real failure, your hands are busy with the AFM.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

One engine fails on a twin-engine helicopter but you can maintain level flight. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare: Pan-pan pan-pan pan-pan, engine failure, single engine operation, requesting priority landing at the nearest suitable runway. I would state unable hover if performance is limited, and give souls and fuel when asked. If I cannot maintain altitude I would upgrade to mayday.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Returning to a confined pad without checking OEI hover capability.  
  ✔ Request a runway; state unable hover if applicable.

- ❌ Saying only "engine problem" without "single engine."  
  ✔ Engine failure, single engine operation — clear and standard.

- ❌ Accepting a long vector or hold.  
  ✔ Request priority and simple routing; decline holds if performance is tight.

- ❌ Never upgrading when climb capability is lost.  
  ✔ Cannot maintain altitude = mayday.

**Word Stress**

- Single → SING-gul
- Operation → op-uh-RAY-shun (stress on "ray")
- Priority → pry-OR-i-tee

Practice:

Engine failure... single engine operation... priority landing.

Together:

Pan-pan, engine failure, single engine operation, requesting priority landing Congonhas.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**OEI** — **O**wn the failure call, **E**xpress single engine + limitation, **I**nsist on priority suitable landing.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Engine failure after takeoff from Congonhas on a twin. OEI level flight possible. Hover to hospital pad not possible.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

Approach clears you to the hospital pad. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Pan-pan pan-pan pan-pan, ANAC123, engine failure, single engine operation, requesting priority landing Congonhas runway. Second task: ANAC123, unable hospital pad, unable hover single engine, require runway landing Congonhas. Never accept a pad that exceeds OEI capability.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Single engine operation is continued flight with a smaller envelope.

Say engine failure. Say single engine. Say the landing you can actually make.

In Brazilian twin ops — and in ICAO Delta English training beside the H130 — performance honesty on the radio is part of the emergency procedure.

Priority. Suitable site. No hover if you cannot hover. Land while the remaining engine still gives you choices.
