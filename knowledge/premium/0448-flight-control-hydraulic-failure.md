# Flight Control Hydraulic Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0448  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Flight Control Hydraulic Failure — loss of hydraulic power to the flight control system that may leave residual manual control, jammed controls, or severely degraded authority depending on system design and failure mode.

This is more severe than a simple boost caution when control authority itself is compromised. The crew must immediately assess what still works, fly the AFM procedure, and land as soon as possible.

For H130 helicopter operations, flight control hydraulic failure is a critical emergency requiring Mayday when control is in doubt, and clear, concise communication so ATC can clear a path to land.

Understanding how to report flight control hydraulic failure in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Flight Control Hydraulic Failure is a loss of hydraulic power affecting the flight control system, resulting in heavy, restricted, or unreliable control response that threatens continued safe flight and landing.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha hidráulica dos comandos de voo.

Perda de potência hidráulica que afeta o sistema de comandos de voo, resultando em resposta pesada, restrita ou não confiável que ameaça a continuidade do voo e do pouso seguros.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When flight control hydraulics fail, the pilot should:

- maintain whatever control remains with smooth, minimal inputs
- follow the AFM emergency procedure for hydraulic failure
- land as soon as possible at the nearest suitable site
- declare Mayday if control is degraded or uncertain
- request an uncomplicated approach and emergency services

Flight control hydraulic failure reporting commonly includes:

- flight control hydraulic failure
- limited or heavy controls, request immediate landing
- position and souls on board
- request clear landing area and fire services

**Important:** If you cannot predict the next control response, you are in distress. Mayday. Do not delay for a preferred airport.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to flight control hydraulic failure to:

- clear traffic immediately from the aircraft's route
- provide the shortest practical path to a suitable landing site
- coordinate maximum emergency response
- keep the frequency clear for the distressed crew
- avoid complex clearances, holds, or runway changes

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are at one thousand five hundred feet in your H130 near Congonhas.

Hydraulic failure indications appear and cyclic response becomes irregular and very heavy. You declare Mayday, report flight control hydraulic failure with limited control, and request immediate landing at the nearest suitable site.

Approach clears traffic and offers the closest available landing option. You fly the minimum necessary inputs, land, shut down, and request fire services until the aircraft is secured.

2. Approach: ANAC123, nearest suitable Congonhas, turn left heading three six zero, cleared as requested.
3. Tower: ANAC123, any runway cleared to land, emergency services rolling.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Flight control hydraulics are not optional comfort — they are how you move the rotor disk with precision.

When they fail, every unnecessary input is risk. Fly the procedure. Use the smallest corrections that keep the aircraft upright and on path.

On the H130, distinguish boost-heavy but predictable controls from unpredictable or jammed response. Predictable heavy controls may allow Pan Pan and a prompt landing. Unreliable controls are Mayday and land now.

Ask ATC for simplicity: nearest site, straight-in, no last-minute changes. Your bandwidth belongs to the controls, not to a complex clearance.

After landing, shut down and keep rescue nearby until you confirm nothing is on fire and the aircraft is safe.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Flight control hydraulics fail and cyclic response becomes unpredictable. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, flight control hydraulic failure, limited and unreliable control, request immediate landing nearest suitable. I would give position and souls on board as soon as able.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating any hydraulic caption as routine boost failure without assessing control quality.  
  ✔ Assess authority — unreliable controls mean Mayday and immediate landing.

- ❌ Accepting a complex STAR or circling approach.  
  ✔ Request simplest path to the nearest suitable site.

- ❌ Overcontrolling with large cyclic inputs.  
  ✔ Use minimal, smooth inputs only.

- ❌ Delaying the Mayday to finish a checklist discussion on frequency.  
  ✔ Declare distress early; fly the procedure offline as needed.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Flight control hydraulic failure is a control emergency first and a radio event second.

Assess what still works. Speak Mayday when authority is in doubt. Demand the simplest landing path.

On the H130, get the aircraft on the ground before fatigue and unpredictability combine. Minimal inputs. Nearest site. Clear Mayday.
