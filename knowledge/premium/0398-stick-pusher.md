# Stick Pusher

**Catalog ID:** 0398  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Stick Pusher — the automatic system that pushes the control column forward when the aircraft reaches a critical angle of attack, forcing a reduction in pitch to prevent or recover from stall.

Stick Pusher is advanced stall-protection vocabulary in ICAO English exams and transport-category operations.

The H130 does not have a stick pusher. Captain Delta still requires fluent exam English for this system, plus the operational discipline to accept automatic or procedural nose-down recovery cues and to communicate clearly during Brazilian IFR recoveries.

## 📖 Meaning

Stick Pusher is an automatic stall-protection system that applies a forward force on the control column when a critical angle of attack is reached, reducing pitch attitude to prevent aerodynamic stall or to assist recovery.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Stick pusher / empurrador do manche.

Sistema automático de proteção contra estol que aplica força à frente na coluna de comando quando um ângulo de ataque crítico é atingido, reduzindo a atitude de arfagem para prevenir o estol aerodinâmico ou auxiliar a recuperação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the Stick Pusher activates, the crew should:

- allow the pusher to reduce angle of attack — do not override unless the QRH directs otherwise
- apply power as required and roll wings level
- follow the stall recovery procedure to a safe flight path
- advise ATC: stick pusher, recovering, going around or climbing
- declare mayday if the aircraft remains near stall or control is in doubt

Stick Pusher reporting commonly includes:

- stick pusher activation on approach or climb
- recovering, do not override
- going around, request altitude
- mayday, stall recovery in progress

**Important:** Fighting the stick pusher can take you deeper into stall. Respect the system. In the H130, respect equivalent recovery cues — lower attitude, restore energy, then communicate.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to Stick Pusher reports to:

- provide immediate altitude and heading flexibility for recovery
- clear the missed approach and surrounding traffic
- treat the event as a serious flight-control emergency
- coordinate priority return or diversion
- relay the situation to approach and tower sectors

During an ICAO English check in São Paulo, you are asked to describe stick pusher activation on a jet approach into Guarulhos, then relate it to H130 recovery priorities.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Stick pusher is the airplane taking the nose away from you to save the wing.

Exam English demands the exact term. "The stick pushed alone" is not enough. Say stick pusher.

Do not narrate a fight with the system on frequency. Say: stick pusher, recovering, request climb. If you must declare: mayday, stall recovery.

For H130 pilots, the lesson is acceptance of recovery geometry. When the aircraft — or the procedure — demands nose down and energy restoration, comply first. Ego kills more approaches than stick pushers do.

Around Brazilian high-density airports, a stick-pusher-style recovery means an immediate missed approach and a clean call so Tower can move traffic.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is a stick pusher, and what would you report to ATC if it activates?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A stick pusher automatically pushes the control column forward at critical angle of attack to prevent or recover from stall. I would allow the recovery, add power, and go around. I would report: ANAC123, going around, stick pusher, recovering, request climb three thousand. The H130 has no stick pusher, but I would use the same clear structure for any critical stall-protection or low-energy recovery.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Overriding the pusher to hold the glidepath.  
  ✔ Respect the pusher. Recover first, then re-brief the approach.

- ❌ Using vague language: "controls moved by themselves."  
  ✔ Say stick pusher — the standard ICAO exam term.

- ❌ Declaring nothing because the recovery was quick.  
  ✔ Still report the event so ATC can protect traffic.

- ❌ Ignoring the topic as "jet only."  
  ✔ Master the English; apply the recovery discipline to the H130.
