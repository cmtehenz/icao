# Convective SIGMET

**Catalog ID:** 0374  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Convective SIGMET — a weather advisory for thunderstorms and related convective hazards that are significant to aircraft safety, including severe turbulence, hail, and wind shear associated with embedded or widespread convection.

A Convective SIGMET is not background noise on the ATIS. It means organized thunderstorm activity that can destroy an approach plan, block an airway, and threaten the H130 with hail, microbursts, and severe turbulence.

For Brazilian operations — summer convection over São Paulo, Amazônia afternoon cells, squall lines in the Center-West — crews must brief the SIGMET area, deviate early, and communicate routing requests in ICAO English.

## 📖 Meaning

A Convective SIGMET is a significant meteorological information message warning of thunderstorms and associated convective hazards — such as severe turbulence, hail, icing, and wind shear — that affect a defined area and are considered hazardous to all aircraft, requiring avoidance planning and possible ATC deviations.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

SIGMET convectivo / Convective SIGMET.

Mensagem de informação meteorológica significativa alertando sobre trovoadas e riscos convectivos associados — como turbulência severa, granizo, gelo e cisalhamento do vento — que afetam uma área definida e são considerados perigosos para todas as aeronaves, exigindo planejamento de desvio e possíveis desvios com o ATC.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a Convective SIGMET is in effect, the crew should:

- brief the geographic area, valid time, and hazard type before departure and en route
- plan routing that avoids the convective area by a safe margin
- request weather deviation early — do not wait until embedded in cells
- delay departure or hold if the SIGMET covers the departure or destination
- report encounters that confirm or refine the SIGMET for ATC
- never penetrate severe thunderstorm cores in the H130

Convective SIGMET actions commonly include:

- request deviation left/right of track due weather
- request delay / hold for convective activity
- divert due Convective SIGMET over destination
- confirm ATIS / ATC weather updates

**Important:** In Brazil, convective SIGMET-type information may be issued through SIGMET / Aviso de Tempo Severo channels consistent with DECEA/ICAO practice. Treat any thunderstorm SIGMET covering your route as a hard planning constraint — especially for helicopters with limited speed and icing/hail tolerance.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use Convective SIGMET information to:

- approve weather deviations around storm cells
- flow traffic away from active convective areas
- delay departures into SIGMET-covered airspace
- relay updated storm positions and PIREPs
- coordinate holding and diversions when destinations are impacted

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

A Convective SIGMET covers the São Paulo terminal area for embedded thunderstorms. You are fifty miles out IFR in the H130. Cells paint ahead on the weather display / you see build-ups on the nose.

You transmit:

São Paulo Approach, ANAC123, request deviation twenty miles left of course due convective weather, Convective SIGMET area.

Approach: ANAC123, deviation left approved, report clear of weather.

You arc around the line, stay visual with the cells, and rejoin when clear.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, deviation left of course approved, report clear of weather.
2. Center: ANAC123, Convective SIGMET in effect along your route, advise intentions.
3. Approach: ANAC123, hold as published, destination under thunderstorm SIGMET.
4. Tower: ANAC123, departure delay, Convective SIGMET over the field.
5. Approach: ANAC123, when able say tops and deviation complete.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

A Convective SIGMET is the atmosphere filing a NOTAM on your route.

Read the area. Read the time. Then decide if the flight still makes sense.

In the H130 you cannot outrun a squall line. You deviate early, hold, or stay on the ground.

Brazilian summer afternoons write Convective SIGMETs in capital letters over the Southeast. Crews who press "because the gap looks wide enough" meet hail and shear.

Ask for the deviation before the frequency gets saturated with everyone else's panic. Calm, early, specific: miles left or right, reason convective weather.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

A Convective SIGMET covers your destination for the next two hours. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would reassess whether to depart or continue. If already airborne, I would plan holding or diversion to an alternate outside the convective area and coordinate with ATC. I would request weather deviation as needed en route and I would not attempt an approach through active thunderstorms. I would brief fuel and alternate options. In the H130 I treat a Convective SIGMET over the field as a reason to delay or divert rather than to force an arrival through cells.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Ignoring SIGMET text because "we have radar."  
  ✔ Use both: SIGMET for planning area, radar for tactical gaps — still avoid cores.

- ❌ Requesting deviation too late when already in heavy precipitation.  
  ✔ Request early; state miles left/right due weather.

- ❌ Taking off into a SIGMET-covered departure path to "beat the storm."  
  ✔ Delay; thunderstorms win that race.

- ❌ Vague: "request weather."  
  ✔ Specific: deviation [direction/distance] due convective weather.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**SIGMET-C** — **S**torms significant, **I**n area and time, **G**o around early, **M**iles left/right on the request, **E**xit or hold, **T**ower delay if over the field; **C**onvective means thunderstorms.
