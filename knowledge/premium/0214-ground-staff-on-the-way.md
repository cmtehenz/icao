# Ground Staff on the Way

**Catalog ID:** 0214  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Ground Staff on the Way — the advisory that apron personnel, marshallers, handlers, or other ground staff are proceeding to an aircraft or location on the ramp.

Unlike maintenance technicians, ground staff may be connecting GPUs, placing chocks, guiding parking, handling bags, or marshalling the H130 onto a pad. The safety principle is identical: people are approaching, so aircraft movement and rotor energy must be controlled.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, ground staff on the way is highly relevant on every arrival and departure. Marshallers walk into the hover taxi path. Handlers approach after set-down. Clear English and disciplined holds prevent apron injuries.

Understanding this phraseology in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency and daily Brazilian heliport and airport ramp coordination.

## 📖 Meaning

Ground staff on the way means apron or ground personnel are proceeding to a specified aircraft or stand to perform handling, marshalling, chocking, or related ground services, and pilots must protect them from blast, rotor wash, and unexpected movement.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Equipe de solo a caminho / ground staff on the way.

Significa que pessoal de pátio ou de solo está se deslocando até uma aeronave ou posição para handling, sinalização, calços ou serviços relacionados, e os pilotos devem protegê-los de jato, downwash de rotor e movimentos inesperados.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ground staff are on the way or around the aircraft, the crew should:

- hold position or remain parked as cleared
- avoid increasing power, starting, or lifting to hover until staff are briefed and clear or in a safe marshalling position
- follow marshaller signals once visual contact is established
- report ready only when doors, chocks, and staff status match the SOP
- warn Ground if staff are not visible when expected

Ground staff on the way commonly includes:

- marshaller walking to the arrival pad
- handlers bringing chocks and grounding equipment
- staff preparing passenger steps or transfer equipment
- apron crew crossing a taxi lane to reach your stand
- dispatch or operations staff delivering papers or briefing items

**Important:** In helicopter operations, ground staff must approach only through briefed safe corridors. Tail rotor awareness is mandatory. If Ground says staff are on the way and you still have high RPM, confirm SOP before anyone walks in. Never wave people under the disc.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers and apron management use this advisory to:

- alert pilots that personnel are crossing active apron routes
- hold taxi and hover taxi clearances that would endanger staff
- sequence vehicles and pedestrians with aircraft movements
- protect stands during simultaneous arrivals
- resume movement when staff report in position or clear

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You inbound to the academy pad at Florianópolis. Tower clears you to the pad and Apron advises that ground staff are on the way for marshalling.

You approach slowly, establish hover, and wait for the marshaller to take position at the briefed spot. You do not set down on top of empty pavement while people are still jogging across Alpha.

Once the marshaller is in place and signals, you land, reduce power as required, and wait for chocks if that is the procedure.

Outbound, before startup, Ground may say ground staff still on the way to remove equipment from your stand. You hold the start until they are clear.

People first. Clearance second. Lift third.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, hold position, ground staff on the way to your stand.
2. Apron: ANAC123, ground staff crossing Alpha, delay hover taxi.
3. Ground: ANAC123, marshaller on the way, report staff in sight.
4. Apron: ANAC123, ground staff in position, cleared to land on the pad.
5. Ground: ANAC123, ground staff clear of stand two, cleared for startup.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Ground staff on the way is the human version of traffic on the apron.

You would not hover taxi through an airplane. Do not hover taxi through people.

In the H130 academy, marshallers are part of every well-run arrival. Wait for them. See them. Follow them. If they are late, hold a stable position and tell Apron — do not invent a parking spot that traps staff behind the tail boom.

Outbound, chocks and tools left near skids are startup killers. Confirm staff clear before you request start.

ICAO English here is simple, but the discipline behind it separates professional crews from dangerous ones.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Apron advises that ground staff are on the way to marshall you onto the helipad. Explain how you continue the arrival.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would reduce speed or hold as instructed and look for the marshaller. I would not complete the landing onto the pad until ground staff are in a safe position or I am cleared to proceed. Once I have the marshaller in sight and receive landing or set-down clearance, I would follow the signals to the landing spot. After landing I would keep rotors and controls stable until staff are clear or chocks are in place according to procedure.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Landing or lifting while staff are still crossing the approach path.  
  ✔ Hold until staff are in position or clear as required.

- ❌ Using only gestures without radio confirmation when ATC asked for a report.  
  ✔ Report staff in sight or staff clear when requested.

- ❌ Starting engines with handlers still removing equipment near the skids.  
  ✔ Confirm ground staff clear before startup clearance is used.

- ❌ Assuming "on the way" means they are already safely in the marshalling spot.  
  ✔ On the way means coming — wait for in position or in sight.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Ground Staff in Sight, Ready to Set Down

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, GROUND STAFF in SITE, REH-dee too SET DOWN

**Word Stress**

- Ground → GROUND
- Staff → STAFF
- Sight → SITE
- Ready → REH-dee

Practice:

Ground staff in sight... ready to set down.

Together:

ANAC123, ground staff in sight, ready to set down on the pad.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**STAFF** — **S**top or hold movement, **T**rack people visually, **A**wait marshaller position, **F**ollow signals, **F**ree to start only when staff clear.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are cleared to the academy pad. Apron says ground staff are on the way and asks you to report them in sight.

**Your first task**

What do you say while you are still looking for the marshaller?

**Your second task**

You see the marshaller in the briefed position. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, looking for ground staff, standing by. Second task: ANAC123, ground staff in sight, ready to set down — then follow the marshaller and any landing clearance already issued or newly given.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Ground staff on the way means the apron is alive with people.

Hold for them. See them. Protect them from the rotor disc. Then move.

In H130 training on Brazilian ramps, this is everyday English with everyday consequences. A late marshaller is inconvenient. A struck marshaller is catastrophic.

Acknowledge the call. Report sight. Confirm clear before startup.

That is how professional helicopter crews share the apron with the humans who keep the operation running.
