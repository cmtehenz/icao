# Report Airborne

**Catalog ID:** 0324  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Report Airborne — the pilot report that advises ATC when the aircraft has become airborne after takeoff or lift-off.

Report Airborne confirms departure is complete. Controllers use it to release the runway or helipad, start departure sequencing, and hand the aircraft to the next frequency with a known airborne time and status.

For H130 helicopter operations in Brazil, airborne reports matter at controlled airports, busy helipads, and hospital pads where Tower or Ground needs confirmation that you are clear of the surface and climbing away. Helicopters may lift off vertically; the report still means you are airborne and no longer occupying the departure point.

Understanding how to report airborne in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Report Airborne instructs the pilot to advise ATC when the aircraft has left the ground or departure surface and is airborne following takeoff clearance or lift-off authorization.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Reporte no ar / Report Airborne.

Instrução para o piloto informar ao controle quando a aeronave deixou o solo ou a superfície de decolagem e está no ar após a autorização de decolagem ou decolagem vertical.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Report Airborne, or when departure procedure requires it, the crew should:

- complete the takeoff or lift-off as cleared
- confirm positive climb and clear of the runway, helipad, or FATO
- report airborne promptly after becoming airborne
- include runway or departure point when useful
- continue the SID or departure instructions without delay

Report Airborne commonly includes:

- airborne
- airborne runway one seven right
- airborne, climbing runway heading
- report airborne for departure timing and runway release

**Important:** Airborne means the aircraft is off the surface with positive climb — not rolling, not light on the skids, not still in ground effect with intent only. In the H130, call airborne when you are clearly flying and clear of the departure point so ATC can free the pad or runway.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers work with Report Airborne to:

- confirm the runway or helipad is vacated after departure
- start departure and arrival sequencing for the next aircraft
- record airborne time for traffic and coordination
- transfer the aircraft to departure or approach with known status
- manage mixed fixed-wing and helicopter operations on the same airport

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cleared for takeoff from runway one eight at Navegantes in the H130.

Tower says:

Tower: ANAC123, runway one eight cleared for takeoff, report airborne.

You lift off, establish positive climb, and clear the runway environment.

You report:

ANAC123, airborne.

Tower may then issue the frequency change to Approach. The next departure can be sequenced because your airborne report confirmed the runway is free.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, runway one eight cleared for takeoff, report airborne.
2. Tower: ANAC123, cleared for takeoff, wind two zero zero at eight, report airborne.
3. Tower: ANAC123, helipad cleared for takeoff, report airborne.
4. Ground: ANAC123, after airborne contact Approach one two four decimal five.
5. Tower: ANAC123, cleared for immediate takeoff, report airborne.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Airborne is the first truth of the departure.

Until you report it — or until Tower sees it — the surface is still considered occupied for sequencing purposes in many operations.

Fixed-wing pilots think of rotation and climb. Helicopter pilots think of lift-off and clear of the pad. The English word is the same: airborne.

In Brazilian tower environments with mixed traffic, a late airborne report delays the next clearance. A premature report risks someone else being cleared onto a surface you still occupy.

Fly first. Positive climb. Then report.

Do not bury the airborne call inside a long story about heading and altitude unless ATC asked for more. Often one word plus callsign is enough: airborne.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Why does ATC ask helicopters and airplanes to report airborne after takeoff?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

The airborne report confirms the aircraft has left the runway or helipad and is climbing away. This allows ATC to release the departure surface for other traffic, start sequencing, and transfer the flight to the next frequency with a clear airborne status.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting airborne while still on the runway or pad.  
  ✔ Report only when clearly off the surface with positive climb.

- ❌ Forgetting the report when Tower specifically requested it.  
  ✔ Treat "report airborne" as part of the takeoff clearance.

- ❌ Changing frequency before reporting airborne when instructed to report first.  
  ✔ Report airborne, then switch if cleared to contact the next unit.

- ❌ Overloading the first airborne call with unnecessary details.  
  ✔ Keep it concise unless ATC requests more information.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Airborne

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, AIR-born

**Word Stress**

- Airborne → AIR-born (stress on first)
- Clear and clipped — do not say "air-BORNE" with rising uncertainty

Practice:

Airborne...

Together:

ANAC123, airborne.

Then with runway:

ANAC123, airborne runway one eight.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**AIR** — **A**ctually off the surface, **I**nitiate positive climb, **R**eport promptly so ATC can free the pad or runway.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Tower says:

Tower: ANAC123, runway one eight cleared for takeoff, report airborne.

You lift off and establish climb.

**Your first task**

What do you say?

**Your second task**

May you change to Approach frequency before the airborne report if Tower asked you to report airborne?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, airborne. Second task: No. I report airborne first as instructed, then change frequency when Tower clears me to contact Approach or when the clearance already includes the frequency change after airborne.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Report Airborne closes the departure on the surface and opens the flight in the air.

One clean call protects the runway, the helipad, and the next aircraft in sequence.

In the H130, lift off with discipline. Climb with purpose. Report airborne without delay.

That is how Brazilian tower frequencies stay efficient.
