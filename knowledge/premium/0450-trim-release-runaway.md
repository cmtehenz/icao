# Trim Release Runaway

**Catalog ID:** 0450  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Trim Release Runaway — an uncommanded or continuous trim release condition that causes the flight controls to move or lose trim force without pilot command, creating a sudden change in control feel and aircraft attitude.

A runaway trim release can produce rapid attitude changes and very high workload. The pilot must override, hold the controls firmly, execute the AFM memory items, and land as soon as possible.

For H130 helicopter operations, trim release runaway is a critical flight control emergency. Communicate Mayday when the aircraft is difficult to control, and request an immediate simple landing path.

Understanding how to report trim release runaway in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Trim Release Runaway is an abnormal condition in which the trim release function activates continuously or without command, removing or altering trim force so that control positions and aircraft attitude change unexpectedly.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Runaway do trim release (liberação de trim descontrolada).

Condição anormal em que a função de liberação de trim se ativa de forma contínua ou sem comando, removendo ou alterando a força de trim de modo que as posições de comando e a atitude da aeronave mudam inesperadamente.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When trim release runaway occurs, the pilot should:

- grip the controls firmly and override uncommanded movement
- execute AFM memory items to isolate or stop the runaway
- stabilize attitude and airspeed as soon as possible
- land as soon as possible at a suitable site
- declare Mayday if control is difficult or attitude excursions continue

Trim release runaway reporting commonly includes:

- trim release runaway or uncommanded trim release
- difficulty controlling attitude, request immediate landing
- position and souls on board
- request simple approach and emergency services

**Important:** Fight the runaway with physical control first. Radio second. A stable aircraft can speak; an unstabilized one cannot.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to trim release runaway to:

- clear traffic for an immediate landing
- provide the simplest routing available
- coordinate emergency services
- avoid any clearance requiring precise maneuvering
- keep the frequency clear for the distressed crew

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are in cruise at two thousand feet in your H130 near São José dos Campos.

The cyclic suddenly goes light and the nose pitches as trim release runs without command. You hold the cyclic firmly, run memory items, and regain a rough trim state with high control forces.

You declare Mayday, report trim release runaway, and request immediate landing. Approach clears traffic. You fly a stable, simple approach with firm control inputs and land.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Mayday, trim release runaway, say souls on board.
2. Approach: ANAC123, nearest suitable São José, turn right heading two seven zero, six miles.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land any runway, emergency services rolling.
4. Approach: ANAC123, fly present heading, land at your discretion.
5. Tower: ANAC123, wind two five zero at zero nine, report short final.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Trim release runaway is a hands-on emergency.

The system is trying to remove the force you rely on for a stable cyclic or collective feel. Your hands become the trim.

On the H130, override immediately. Do not let the nose wander while you diagnose on frequency. Memory items first. Then Mayday. Then the nearest suitable landing.

Expect heavy or unusual forces after you stop the runaway. Plan a long, straight approach. No steep turns for convenience.

If you regain solid control after isolation, you may still land ASAP — the system has already proven it can misbehave.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Trim release runs away in cruise and you regain partial control after memory items. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, trim release runaway, control regained but forces abnormal, request immediate landing nearest suitable. I would state position and souls on board when able.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Diagnosing on the radio before restraining the controls.  
  ✔ Hold and override first; transmit when stable enough.

- ❌ Continuing to destination after a runaway that was "fixed."  
  ✔ Land as soon as possible — the system failed once.

- ❌ Accepting a circling approach with high maneuvering demand.  
  ✔ Request straight-in to the nearest suitable site.

- ❌ Using vague "trim problem" without saying runaway.  
  ✔ Say trim release runaway so urgency is clear.
