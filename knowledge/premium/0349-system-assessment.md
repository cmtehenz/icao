# System Assessment

**Catalog ID:** 0349  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers System Assessment — the structured evaluation of aircraft system status after a caution, warning, or abnormal indication, used to decide continue, divert, or declare urgency with accurate English to ATC.

ICAO exams reward pilots who can describe what they are assessing without dumping raw checklist noise on frequency. Controllers need operational effect: what you can and cannot do, and what you request.

For H130 ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, system assessment includes caution panel lights, engine parameters, hydraulics, electrics, and flight controls as applicable. The discipline is the same: identify, assess, decide, communicate.

Master: system assessment, abnormal indication, remaining capability, intentions.

## 📖 Meaning

System Assessment is the crew process of identifying an abnormal system indication, determining the operational effect on the aircraft, applying the checklist, and deciding the safest plan — including whether ATC must be advised or an urgency call is required.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Avaliação de sistema / System Assessment.

Processo da tripulação de identificar uma indicação anormal de sistema, determinar o efeito operacional na aeronave, aplicar o checklist e decidir o plano mais seguro — incluindo se o ATC deve ser avisado ou se uma chamada de urgência é necessária.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When performing a system assessment, the crew should:

- fly the aircraft first; do not troubleshoot at the cost of control
- identify the system and the indication (caution / warning / parameter)
- run the applicable checklist memory items then QRH/AFM steps
- assess remaining capability: continue, divert, land ASAP
- advise ATC with operational effect and intentions, not a parts lecture

System assessment reporting commonly includes:

- system malfunction / abnormal indication
- request level / vectors / priority as needed
- diverting due to system problem
- remaining capability: able / unable specific clearances

**Important:** ATC does not need the internal part number. ATC needs: nature in plain English, effect on flight, and what you want next.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use system-assessment outcomes to:

- provide priority or uncomplicated routing when capability is reduced
- avoid assigning clearances the aircraft can no longer fly
- coordinate emergency services if landing is time-critical
- obtain fuel, souls, and assistance requirements
- sequence traffic around a diverting or restricted aircraft

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Cruising in the H130 toward Florianópolis, a caution illuminates and parameters confirm a developing system abnormality.

You fly, identify, run the checklist, and assess: diversion is prudent; no immediate Mayday.

You advise Approach you have a system malfunction, request vectors to the nearest suitable airport, and ask for fire services standing by if the checklist recommends it.

Assessment first. Then a clean radio call.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger system malfunction, say intentions and assistance required.
2. Approach: ANAC123, divert approved, expect priority vectors.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, say if emergency services required.
4. Control: ANAC123, unable that altitude? say maximum able.
5. Tower: ANAC123, vacate when able, follow progressive taxi if required.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, system malfunction, request vectors to nearest suitable airport.

4. ANAC123, system assessment complete, continuing with reduced capability, request uncomplicated approach.
5. ANAC123, request fire services standing by, system problem, landing shortly.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

System assessment is thinking before transmitting.

Brazilian pilots sometimes read the caution light name in Portuguese on frequency. Prefer plain operational English: hydraulic problem, electrical failure, engine chip caution — then the plan.

Do not declare Mayday for every caution. Do not stay silent when capability is reduced and ATC is about to clear you for something you cannot do.

In the H130, keep one pilot-brain on flying even in single-pilot ops: trade workload — aviate, navigate, then assess, then talk.

Part 3 answers should show structure: indication, checklist, effect, decision, ATC request.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You get an abnormal system indication. How do you assess it and what might you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain aircraft control, identify the system indication, complete the checklist, and assess whether I can continue or must divert. I would tell ATC the operational effect and intentions — for example, system malfunction, diverting to the nearest suitable airport, request priority landing — and request emergency services if the checklist or condition requires it.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reading long checklist steps on frequency.  
  ✔ Summarize system problem, effect, and intentions.

- ❌ Saying only "problema no sistema" with no plan.  
  ✔ Add diverting / requesting lower / unable as applicable.

- ❌ Continuing into complex clearances after capability is reduced.  
  ✔ Advise unable early and request an alternative.

- ❌ Upgrading every caution to Mayday automatically.  
  ✔ Match Pan Pan / Mayday to actual urgency and control state.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

A caution and abnormal parameters appear fifty miles from destination. Checklist says land as soon as practicable. A suitable airport is twenty miles left of track.

**Your first task**

What do you say to Approach?

**Your second task**

Approach asks if you are declaring an emergency. The aircraft is controllable. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, system malfunction, diverting nearest suitable airport, request vectors and priority landing. Second task: ANAC123, Pan Pan, system abnormality, aircraft controllable, request priority handling and emergency services standing by. I would escalate to Mayday only if control or time becomes critical.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

System assessment turns lights and parameters into a plan.

Fly. Identify. Checklist. Decide. Tell ATC what matters.

In Brazilian H130 operations, keep the radio operational — not technical theatre.

Good assessment is quiet inside the cockpit and clear on frequency.
