# Overrun Evacuation

**Catalog ID:** 0294  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Overrun Evacuation — emergency evacuation after the aircraft overruns the runway end during take-off or landing, often with possible fire, obstacles, or soft ground. For H130 operations, classic high-speed runway overruns are less common than on jets, but the ICAO English for overrun, stop, and evacuate remains High-priority exam material — and helicopter rejected take-off or landing excursions still need the same clear status calls.

Overrun evacuation is a High Part 2 and Part 3 topic. ATC must stop other traffic, alert RFF, and know whether people are leaving the aircraft into the overrun area.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master the phraseology, then map it to any excursion and evacuation scenario on airport surfaces.

Understanding overrun evacuation in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Overrun Evacuation is the emergency evacuation of an aircraft after it has overrun the runway end or stopway during take-off or landing, requiring immediate notification to ATC, fire fighting response, passenger evacuation away from fire and hazards, and accountability of souls on board.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Evacuação após overrun / Overrun Evacuation.

Evacuação de emergência de uma aeronave após ultrapassar o fim da pista ou stopway durante decolagem ou pouso, exigindo notificação imediata ao ATC, resposta de combate a incêndio, evacuação dos passageiros para longe de fogo e riscos, e prestação de contas das almas a bordo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When an overrun leads to evacuation, the crew should:

- stop the aircraft as able and assess fire, smoke, and structural condition
- declare Mayday if not already declared: overrun, evacuating
- command evacuate using available exits away from fire and hazards
- request firefighters immediately
- report souls and whether evacuation is complete
- for H130: transfer the same "excursion / evacuating / firefighters" structure to airport surface excursions

Overrun evacuation reporting commonly includes:

- overrun / runway overrun
- evacuating / evacuation in progress
- fire / no fire / smoke
- request firefighters
- evacuation complete, souls accounted for

**Important:** After an overrun, the aircraft may be off hard surface, in RESA, or near lights and antennas. Say overrun and evacuating so RFF knows where to look and that people are outside the aircraft.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to overrun evacuation calls to:

- stop all runway and approach traffic immediately
- alert RFF to the overrun area, not only the runway threshold
- protect persons evacuating across the field
- coordinate airport closure and rescue access routes
- collect souls and fire status for the incident commander

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Exam-style scenario: landing long and fast, you overrun the end of the runway into the RESA. Smoke appears from the brakes or gear.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Tower, ANAC123, runway overrun, evacuating, request firefighters.

Tower stops arrivals. RFF rolls to the overrun. You evacuate away from smoke.

When clear:

ANAC123, evacuation complete, no fire visible, all souls accounted for.

For H130 training, a rejected take-off excursion or landing roll problem uses the same radio skeleton: where you are, evacuating or not, fire or not, firefighters.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger Mayday overrun, firefighters rolling to the overrun area.
2. Tower: ANAC123, confirm evacuating, say if fire.
3. Tower: ANAC123, all traffic stopped, remain on frequency if able.
4. Tower: ANAC123, say souls on board and evacuation status.
5. Ground: ANAC123, RFF on scene, report when all persons clear of the aircraft.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, roger, firefighters to the overrun, evacuating.
2. ANAC123, evacuating, smoke, no open fire visible.
3. ANAC123, wilco, remaining on frequency.
4. ANAC123, souls one two zero, evacuation in progress.
5. ANAC123, evacuation complete, all persons clear of the aircraft.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Overrun evacuation is two facts in one call: you left the runway, and people are leaving the aircraft.

Say overrun. Say evacuating. Say fire or no fire. Request firefighters.

Do not only say "we had a problem at the end." RFF needs the overrun location and the evacuation state.

After people are out, report evacuation complete and souls if you can account for them.

For the H130, you may never do a jet-style high-speed overrun — but excursion and evacuate English still belongs in your toolkit for airport operations and exams.

Location. Evacuation. Fire status. That is the order.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You overrun the runway on landing and must evacuate because of smoke. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare Mayday, report runway overrun, state that we are evacuating, mention smoke or fire status, and request firefighters. When evacuation is complete I would report that all persons are clear of the aircraft and whether there is fire.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "we went off the runway" without "evacuating."  
  ✔ State overrun and evacuating so RFF knows people are outside.

- ❌ Evacuating toward visible fire or into active traffic without direction.  
  ✔ Command exits away from fire and hazards; follow CRM.

- ❌ Not updating when evacuation is complete.  
  ✔ Report evacuation complete and souls if accounted for.

- ❌ Ignoring the topic because H130 overruns are rare.  
  ✔ Learn exam English; transfer to any surface excursion.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Runway Overrun, Evacuating

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, RUN-way OH-ver-run, ih-VAK-yoo-ay-ting

**Word Stress**

- Runway → RUN-way
- Overrun → OH-ver-run
- Evacuating → ih-VAK-yoo-ay-ting

Practice:

Runway overrun... evacuating... request firefighters.

Together:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, runway overrun, evacuating.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**OVERRUN** — **O**ut past the end, **V**oice Mayday, **E**vacuating stated clearly, **R**equest firefighters, **R**eport fire/smoke, **U**pdate when complete, **N**umber the souls if known.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Landing overrun into the RESA. Smoke from the gear. You evacuate.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

Everyone is clear. No open fire. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday Mayday Mayday, Tower, ANAC123, runway overrun, evacuating, smoke, request firefighters. Second task: ANAC123, evacuation complete, no fire visible, all souls accounted for. I would remain in contact until RFF and ATC release the scene.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Overrun evacuation is location plus people.

Overrun. Evacuating. Fire status. Firefighters.

Then: evacuation complete.

Learn it for the ICAO exam. Transfer it to any H130 airport excursion that puts people outside the aircraft.

Say where you are. Say what people are doing. That is how RFF finds you in the grass past the lights.
