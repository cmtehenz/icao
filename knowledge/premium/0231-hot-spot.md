# Hot Spot

**Catalog ID:** 0231  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Hot Spot — a location on an aerodrome movement area with a history of potential risk of collision or runway incursion, and where heightened attention by pilots and drivers is necessary.

Hot spots are charted. They are not surprises invented by ATC on the day. Airport diagrams mark them because taxiway geometry, runway crossings, or confusing intersections have produced incidents before. Your job is to know where they are before you taxi, brief them, and move through them with deliberate attention.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations at Congonhas, Guarulhos, Santos Dumont, Curitiba, and Florianópolis, hot spots often sit at complex runway/taxiway intersections and short taxi segments near active runways. Ground taxi and air-taxi both require the same discipline: slow down, verify clearance, confirm you are where you think you are.

This lesson builds Part 2 proficiency in hot spot awareness, cautionary taxi phraseology, and runway incursion prevention.

## 📖 Meaning

A Hot Spot is a location on an aerodrome movement area identified in aerodrome charts as presenting a history or risk of collision or runway incursion, requiring increased attention by pilots and vehicle drivers when taxiing through or operating near that location.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Hot Spot / Ponto crítico do aeródromo.

Localização na área de movimento do aeródromo identificada nas cartas como apresentando histórico ou risco de colisão ou incursão em pista, exigindo atenção redobrada de pilotos e motoristas ao taxiar ou operar próximo a esse local.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When operating near a hot spot, the crew should:

- identify hot spots on the airport diagram during pre-taxi and after-landing brief
- brief the specific risk (wrong turn, runway entry, confusing intersection)
- reduce taxi speed and increase position awareness approaching the hot spot
- verify hold-short and runway-crossing clearances before moving onto the runway
- request progressive taxi if unsure of position near a hot spot
- for helicopters, maintain hover-taxi control and avoid rushed turns toward the runway

Hot spot risk typically involves:

- complex taxiway intersections near runway thresholds
- easy wrong-path entry onto an active runway
- closely spaced parallel taxiways with similar designations
- short taxi segments between runway exits and crossings
- history of runway incursions at that exact point

**Important:** A hot spot is a permanent chart warning, not a temporary NOTAM. Knowing it before engine start is part of runway safety. In the H130, do not let air-taxi speed or training tempo erase the pause at a hot spot — the geometry that traps airplanes traps helicopters too.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers and aerodrome authorities highlight hot spots to:

- reduce runway incursions at known problem locations
- alert crews via charts and, when needed, cautionary taxi instructions
- support progressive taxi and hold-short discipline
- focus airport safety programs on high-risk intersections
- protect simultaneous runway and taxi operations

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are taxiing for departure at Congonhas in the H130. The airport diagram shows a hot spot where taxiway Alpha meets the runway holding point — a place where crews have mistakenly lined up or crossed without clearance.

Ground: taxi to holding point runway one seven Left via Alpha, caution hot spot at the holding point.

You read back the route. Approaching the hot spot you slow down, confirm the hold-short marking, and stop at the holding point. You do not edge onto the runway. Tower later clears you to line up. Only then do you enter.

The hot spot did its job: it put the risk in your brief before the wheels moved.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, taxi holding point runway one seven Left via Alpha, caution hot spot.
2. Ground: ANAC123, hold short of runway two eight, hot spot ahead, give way to landing traffic.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cross runway one seven Right at Charlie, caution hot spot, report vacated.
4. Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Bravo, caution hot spot at Bravo and Delta intersection.
5. Ground: ANAC123, request confirm position, hot spot vicinity, progressive taxi available.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Hot spots are where runway incursions are born.

They exist because other crews already made the expensive mistake at that intersection. The chart is trying to save you from repeating it.

Brief the hot spot before taxi. Say the risk out loud: wrong turn onto the runway, confusing hold-short, short distance from exit to crossing. Then fly the brief — slow down when you get there.

Never let familiarity erase the pause. Congonhas and Santos Dumont hot spots do not care that you have taxied there a hundred times.

In the H130, position awareness includes rotor clearance and hover direction. A rushed pedal turn near a hot spot can point you at the runway before you meant to enter. Stop. Verify. Then move on clearance.

If you are lost near a hot spot, say so. Progressive taxi is cheaper than an incursion.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Explain what an aerodrome hot spot is and how you prepare for taxi through one.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A hot spot is a charted location on the movement area with a history or risk of collision or runway incursion. Before taxi I would identify hot spots on the airport diagram, brief the specific risk at that location, and plan to reduce speed and confirm hold-short or crossing clearances before entering any runway. If I became unsure of my position near a hot spot, I would stop and request progressive taxi rather than continue guessing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Ignoring hot spot symbols on the airport diagram because the airport is familiar.  
  ✔ Brief hot spots every departure and arrival at that aerodrome.

- ❌ Taxiing at normal speed through a hot spot while copying a new clearance.  
  ✔ Slow down. Finish the clearance. Confirm position. Then move.

- ❌ Crossing the hold-short line slightly while "almost cleared."  
  ✔ Hold short means stop before the marking. Clearance must be explicit.

- ❌ Feeling embarrassed to request progressive taxi near a hot spot.  
  ✔ Progressive taxi prevents incursions. Ask early.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are taxiing to the holding point. Ground cautions a hot spot ahead. You become unsure whether the next intersection leads to the runway or to taxiway Bravo.

**Your first task**

What do you say before you continue?

**Your second task**

Ground confirms you are short of the runway and clears you to the holding point via Bravo. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, request progressive taxi, unsure of position near the hot spot — and I would stop if needed. Second task: ANAC123, taxi holding point via Bravo. I would not enter the runway until I receive an explicit lineup or crossing clearance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Hot spots are charted warnings written in other crews' incidents.

Find them before taxi. Brief the risk. Slow down when you arrive. Hold short until cleared.

In the H130, the same geometry applies — plus rotor awareness. Do not rush the turn toward the runway.

Lost near a hot spot? Stop and ask. Progressive taxi is professionalism, not weakness.

That is how you keep a hot spot from becoming an incursion.
