# Ditching

**Catalog ID:** 0291  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Ditching — a controlled emergency landing on water when landing on land is impossible or more hazardous. For H130 helicopter operations over water — offshore, coastal, and river crossings in Brazil — ditching is a Critical Part 2 and Part 3 skill: Mayday, position, intentions, and survival actions must be spoken clearly under extreme pressure.

Ditching is highly relevant for helicopters. Examiners and real operations both expect immediate distress communication, accurate position, and confirmation of emergency services or SAR alert.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training, master Mayday ditching phraseology, brace and evacuation language, and post-ditching status reports.

Understanding ditching in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Ditching is a controlled emergency landing on water, performed when continued flight to a land landing site is impossible or more dangerous, requiring immediate Mayday declaration, position report, preparation for water impact and evacuation, and coordination with ATC and search and rescue services.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Amerissagem / Ditching.

Pouso de emergência controlado na água, realizado quando o voo continuado até um local de pouso em terra é impossível ou mais perigoso, exigindo declaração imediata de Mayday, reporte de posição, preparação para o impacto na água e evacuação, e coordenação com o ATC e os serviços de busca e salvamento.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ditching is required, the crew should:

- declare Mayday immediately: ditching, position, souls on board
- configure per AFM/QRH for ditching — speed, attitude, flotation if installed
- brief passengers for brace and life jackets / rafts as applicable
- squawk 7700 if able
- after water landing: report status if able, activate ELT/PLB, evacuate to rafts
- for H130 over water: treat ditching as a primary emergency skill, not a rare fixed-wing only topic

Ditching reporting commonly includes:

- Mayday, ditching
- position / last known position
- souls on board and fuel
- ditching imminent / ditched / evacuating to rafts
- request SAR / emergency services

**Important:** Transmit before impact if possible. A short Mayday with position is more valuable than a perfect sentence after the radio is underwater. Fly the aircraft through the flare; keep the call short.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to ditching calls to:

- alert search and rescue immediately
- fix the last known position and track
- clear other traffic and coordinate maritime/aeronautical SAR
- pass souls and fuel to rescue assets
- maintain communication as long as the aircraft can transmit

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are an H130 offshore from Santos when engine indications force a water landing. Land is beyond glide.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Control, ANAC123, engine failure, ditching, position twenty miles offshore Santos, souls six, request SAR.

You brief brace, prepare flotation, and ditch into wind and swell as trained.

If able after impact:

ANAC123, ditched, evacuating to rafts, position same.

ATC confirms SAR airborne. Your English bought the rescue clock minutes.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger Mayday ditching, SAR alerted, confirm position and souls on board.
2. Control: ANAC123, squawk seven seven zero zero, radar contact, rescue coordinating.
3. Control: ANAC123, say fuel remaining and life rafts available.
4. Rescue coord: ANAC123, ditching acknowledged, vessels and helicopter SAR en route.
5. Control: ANAC123, if able report after ditching, remain on frequency.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, position two zero miles offshore Santos, souls six, ditching.
2. ANAC123, squawking seven seven zero zero, ditching imminent.
3. ANAC123, fuel three zero minutes, life rafts available.
4. ANAC123, roger SAR en route, preparing to ditch.
5. ANAC123, ditched, evacuating to rafts, will remain on frequency if able.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Ditching is a water landing you plan in seconds and fly with discipline.

Mayday. Position. Souls. Ditching. That is the core call.

Then fly the profile. Into wind when you can. Flotation armed if installed. Brace. Evacuate.

For H130 crews over Brazilian coastal and offshore routes, this is not theoretical. Over-water flight means ditching English must be automatic.

Do not wait for a perfect sentence. Position saves lives. Transmit early.

After impact, if the radio still works, say ditched and evacuating. Then survive.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You must ditch following engine failure over water. What do you say to ATC and what do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare Mayday, state engine failure and that I am ditching, give my position, and report souls on board. I would request SAR, prepare the aircraft and passengers for water landing, ditch according to procedures, then evacuate to life rafts and activate emergency beacons. If able I would report after ditching that we are in the rafts.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "going to the water" without Mayday and position.  
  ✔ Mayday, ditching, position, souls.

- ❌ Delaying the call until after impact.  
  ✔ Transmit before ditching whenever possible.

- ❌ Treating ditching as fixed-wing only and under-preparing helicopter English.  
  ✔ For H130 over water, ditching phraseology is Critical.

- ❌ Forgetting life rafts / flotation status when asked.  
  ✔ Answer clearly: available, inflated, or not installed.

- Ditching → DICH-ing
- Request → reh-KWEST
- SAR → ESS-AY-AR (each letter clear)
