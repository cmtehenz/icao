# Read Back

**Catalog ID:** 0126  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Read Back — one of the most fundamental safety procedures in aviation radio communication.

Read Back is not optional. It is required.

ICAO mandates that pilots read back specific ATC clearances and instructions to confirm correct receipt. When ATC hears a correct readback, it closes the communication loop and confirms the pilot has understood.

In the H130, flying between Congonhas, Navegantes, and Curitiba, you will issue and receive dozens of readbacks per flight. Errors in readback — or failure to read back — are among the most cited ICAO Level 4 deficiencies in Brazilian pilot assessments.

This lesson builds the habit of precise, complete, professional readback.

## 📖 Meaning

Read Back is the procedure by which a receiving station repeats received information back to the transmitting station to confirm correct receipt. In aviation, it refers specifically to the pilot repeating ATC clearances, instructions, and safety-related information verbatim or in ICAO-standard abbreviated form.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Leitura de volta / Readback.

Procedimento pelo qual a estação receptora repete as informações recebidas para confirmar o recebimento correto. Na aviação, refere-se ao piloto repetindo instruções e autorizações do controle de tráfego aéreo de forma fiel ou em forma abreviada ICAO-padrão.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues an instruction requiring readback, the pilot should:

- repeat the key elements of the clearance in the correct order
- include the callsign at the beginning or end of the readback
- use ICAO standard phraseology — no paraphrasing
- never substitute roger or wilco alone when readback is required
- wait for ATC to acknowledge or correct the readback before acting

Read Back operations commonly include:

- altitude and level clearances
- runway assignments and landing clearances
- taxi routing instructions
- heading and speed assignments
- frequency changes
- transponder codes

**Important:** Roger does not constitute a readback. Saying only "Roger, ANAC123" after receiving a heading assignment is non-compliant. You must repeat the specific instruction.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers rely on readback to:

- confirm the pilot received the correct clearance
- detect mis-hearings before they become incidents
- verify the readback contains the right altitude, runway, or routing
- satisfy ICAO safety standards for safety-critical information
- maintain separation assurance through communication confirmation

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Congonhas in your H130.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, descend to altitude three thousand, QNH one zero one three.

You must read back:

Descend altitude three thousand, QNH one zero one three, ANAC123.

Approach then clears you to land:

ANAC123, cleared to land runway one seven right.

You read back:

Cleared to land runway one seven right, ANAC123.

If you had said only "Roger" after either transmission, Approach could not confirm you received the correct altitude or runway. A confusion between runway one seven left and one seven right could result in a conflict with departing traffic.

Read Back closes the loop. It saves lives.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, descend to altitude three thousand, QNH one zero one three.
2. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land runway two eight left, wind two seven zero at one two knots.

4. Approach: ANAC123, fly heading two four zero, intercept localizer.
5. Control: ANAC123, squawk four four five two.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Read Back is not a formality — it is a safety system.

ICAO designed it as a cross-check between two human beings communicating in a noisy, high-workload environment.

The controller issues. You read back. They verify. If something is wrong, they correct before you act.

Brazilian pilots often shorten readbacks under pressure. The frequency is busy. There is workload. It feels faster to say "Roger, ANAC123" and get back to flying.

That shortcut is the readback failure mode.

Train the full readback. Runway number. Altitude. QNH. Taxi route. All of it.

It takes three extra seconds.

Those three seconds may one day prevent a runway incursion, an altitude bust, or a traffic conflict.

Read Back is not bureaucracy — it is your last safety net before the aircraft moves.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Why is it insufficient to reply only "Roger" when ATC issues a runway clearance?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Roger confirms receipt of a message but does not demonstrate that the specific content was correctly understood. ICAO requires a readback of runway assignments, altitudes, and other safety-critical instructions so that ATC can verify the correct information was received. If I read back the wrong runway, ATC can correct me before I take action. Roger alone gives ATC no way to detect a miscommunication.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Replacing the readback with only "Roger, ANAC123."  
  ✔ Repeat the specific instruction: Cleared to land runway two eight left, ANAC123.

- ❌ Paraphrasing or summarizing instead of repeating key elements.  
  ✔ Use ICAO-standard terminology and repeat the critical values verbatim.

- ❌ Omitting the callsign from the readback.  
  ✔ Include your callsign at the start or end of every readback.

- ❌ Reading back altitude in meters when ATC issued feet, or vice versa.  
  ✔ Use the same units ATC used, or confirm the units explicitly.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Cleared to land runway two eight left, ANAC123.

**Pronunciation:** KLEERD too land RUN-way too AYT left, ay-NAK wun too three.

**Word Stress**

- Cleared → KLEERD (strong)
- Runway → RUN-way
- Numbers → individual digits — two eight (not twenty-eight)

Practice:

Cleared... to land... runway... two eight left...

Together:

Cleared to land runway two eight left, ANAC123.

Speak runway digits individually — two eight, not twenty-eight.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**LOOP** — **L**isten completely, **O**utput the key values, **O**mit nothing critical, **P**ause for ATC acknowledgement.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Curitiba Approach transmits: "ANAC123, descend to altitude four thousand five hundred, QNH one zero one five, expect ILS runway one five."

**Your first task**

Provide a complete professional readback of this transmission.

**Your second task**

ATC corrects: "ANAC123, negative, QNH is one zero one seven, I say again, one zero one seven." How do you respond?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

My readback: Descend altitude four thousand five hundred, QNH one zero one five, expect ILS runway one five, ANAC123. When corrected, I would acknowledge the correction and read back the new QNH: QNH one zero one seven, ANAC123. A correction from ATC is information that something was mis-heard or mis-transmitted — I must confirm the correct value before setting it.
