# Belly Landing

**Catalog ID:** 0265  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Belly Landing — a landing on the underside of the fuselage, usually with landing gear retracted or unavailable, closely related to wheels-up landing and requiring the same emergency coordination, RFF readiness, and clear ICAO English.

Belly landing is widely used emergency vocabulary on ICAO exams and in media, often overlapping with wheels-up landing. Professionally, it describes fuselage contact with the surface when gear cannot support the aircraft. The radio priorities are identical: declare, request firefighters, land controlled, secure, evacuate if needed.

For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, many H130s land on skids — that is not a belly landing. Teach belly-landing English for exams and wheeled types. Note the distinction so academy pilots do not misuse "belly landing" for a normal skid arrival or for an autorotative landing on skids.

Understanding belly landing phraseology supports Part 2 and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Belly Landing is an emergency or abnormal landing in which the aircraft contacts the runway or surface primarily on the fuselage underside because the landing gear is retracted, missing, or unable to support the aircraft, typically requiring RFF response and possible evacuation.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pouso de barriga / Belly landing / Pouso sobre a fuselagem.

Pouso de emergência ou anormal em que a aeronave contata a pista ou a superfície principalmente pela parte inferior da fuselagem porque o trem de pouso está recolhido, ausente ou incapaz de sustentar a aeronave, tipicamente exigindo resposta de RFF e possível evacuação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a belly landing is anticipated, the crew should:

- complete all gear extension attempts per AFM before committing
- advise ATC of belly landing or wheels-up landing intention
- request priority and firefighters
- configure fuel, electrics, and cabin for emergency landing and evacuation
- aim for a controlled, wings-level touchdown on a suitable long runway
- after stop: secure engines and systems, assess fire, evacuate if required

Belly landing scenarios commonly include:

- intentional fuselage landing after gear will not extend
- overlap with wheels-up landing terminology in radio and exams
- structural and fire risk from friction and fuel lines
- runway closure after the event
- H130 note: normal skid landing ≠ belly landing; keep terms accurate

**Important:** On frequency, "belly landing" and "wheels-up landing" are often understood the same way by ATC — fuselage on the runway, RFF needed. Use one clearly, state gear status, and request firefighters. Do not debate synonyms while configuring for the landing.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to belly-landing declarations to:

- clear and protect the landing runway
- alert and position RFF
- stop conflicting traffic with go-arounds and holds
- collect souls, fuel, and evacuation status
- manage post-landing runway closure and recovery

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Wheeled-aircraft emergency into Guarulhos. Gear will not extend. You will land on the belly.

You transmit:

ANAC123, Mayday, unable to extend landing gear, requesting belly landing runway two seven L, firefighters.

Tower: ANAC123, roger belly landing, runway two seven L, firefighters in position, cleared to land.

You land controlled. You stop. No fire. You advise. Evacuation as required by checklist.

Nearby, an H130 holds or diverts. Shared Brazilian airports treat belly landings as full runway emergencies — academy crews must react as other traffic, not as spectators.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger belly landing, firefighters rolling, cleared to land.
2. Approach: ANAC123, confirm gear unavailable, say souls and fuel remaining.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway cleared for your belly landing, wind calm.
4. Tower: ANAC123, after landing advise fire status and evacuation.
5. Approach: ANAC456, go around, runway closed for emergency landing.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, Mayday, requesting belly landing, firefighters.
2. ANAC123, affirmative gear unavailable, souls six, fuel twenty-five minutes.
3. ANAC123, runway cleared copied, cleared to land belly landing.
4. ANAC123, no fire, evacuating — or remaining secured as applicable.
5. ANAC456, going around, runway closed.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Belly landing is fuselage-to-pavement reality when the gear cannot do its job.

Use the word if it is natural for you; wheels-up is equally professional. What matters is gear status, intention, firefighters, and a stable approach.

On ICAO exams, either term may appear. Answer with clear structure: unable to extend gear, belly or wheels-up landing, request RFF, then post-landing actions.

In the H130 with skids, a normal landing is on skids. An emergency autorotation may end on rough terrain — still not automatically a "belly landing" in wheeled-aircraft sense. Keep exam vocabulary and real rotorcraft language from contaminating each other.

High priority emergency. Calm English. Empty runway. RFF ready.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You cannot extend the landing gear and must land on the fuselage. What do you tell ATC and what do you do after landing?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare the emergency, advise that I am unable to extend the landing gear, and request a belly landing or wheels-up landing with firefighters. After landing I would secure the aircraft, check for fire, and evacuate if there is fire or if the procedure requires evacuation, then report status to ATC when able.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Using "belly landing" for a normal H130 skid landing.  
  ✔ Reserve belly/wheels-up for retracted or unavailable wheeled gear emergencies.

- ❌ Not requesting firefighters for a planned fuselage landing.  
  ✔ Always request RFF before touchdown.

- ❌ Arguing terminology (belly vs wheels-up) on the frequency.  
  ✔ Pick one clear phrase, state gear status, and fly the procedure.

- ❌ Other traffic staying on approach to watch.  
  ✔ Go around and divert; the runway is an emergency scene.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Unable to extend gear. You will land on the fuselage. Firefighters must be ready. After stop, no fire.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit before landing?

**Your second task**

What do you transmit after landing with no fire?

First task: ANAC123, Mayday, unable to extend landing gear, requesting belly landing, firefighters. Second task: ANAC123, belly landing complete, no fire, remaining on runway — evacuating or standing by as checklist requires. I would evacuate if any fire risk or procedure demanded it.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Belly landing is fuselage contact when the gear cannot save you a normal touchdown.

Declare early. Request firefighters. Land controlled. Secure. Evacuate if needed.

For exams, treat belly and wheels-up as sibling phrases with the same operational spine. For the H130 on skids, do not misuse belly landing for normal skid operations.

High-stakes English. Short sentences. Clear intentions.

That is how belly landings are briefed, flown, and survived on the radio.
