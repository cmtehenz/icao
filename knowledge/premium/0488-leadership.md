# Leadership

**Catalog ID:** 0488  
**Category:** CRM  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Leadership — the CRM responsibility to set direction, invite input, decide clearly, and keep the crew aligned under normal and abnormal conditions.

Leadership in the cockpit is not rank alone. It is the behavior that creates shared understanding, manages workload, and owns the final decision.

For H130 two-crew or pilot-plus-crewman operations, leadership shows in briefings, task allocation, and radio priorities. Part 1 and Part 2 English must support that authority without arrogance.

Understanding leadership in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 1 and Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Leadership is the CRM function of directing the flight through clear goals, open communication, appropriate task sharing, and timely decisions, while remaining receptive to crew input and accountable for the safety outcome of the operation.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Liderança / Leadership.

Função de CRM de conduzir o voo por meio de objetivos claros, comunicação aberta, divisão adequada de tarefas e decisões oportunas, mantendo-se receptivo às contribuições da tripulação e responsável pelo resultado de segurança da operação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When exercising leadership, the captain or PF should:

- set the plan in a clear briefing before the critical phase
- invite challenges and questions early
- allocate PF / PM and radio duties explicitly
- decide when time is short and announce the decision
- for H130: lead confined-area, deck, and sling plans with one clear owner
- communicate intentions to ATC when the plan changes

Leadership behaviors commonly include:

- this is the plan / my decision is...
- challenge me if you see a problem
- you fly, I talk / I fly, you talk
- standby, decision in progress
- we are discontinuing / going around

**Important:** Leadership listens before it decides when time allows — and decides without delay when time does not.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers depend on clear aircraft leadership to:

- receive one coherent set of intentions from the flight
- avoid conflicting calls from uncoordinated crew members
- get timely decisions on diversion, delay, or go-around
- coordinate helicopter special operations with a single point of contact
- maintain orderly flow when the crew changes plan

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You command an H130 into a mountain landing site near Campos do Jordão. Weather is marginal.

Before the approach you brief: you will fly, the other pilot monitors instruments and calls minima, and if visual references are not acquired you will climb on the escape heading.

Approach asks your intentions. You answer with one clear plan: request hold, assess weather, then advise. Leadership on the radio sounds like a single, decided aircraft — not a debate.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, advise intentions.
2. Control: ANAC123, say again diversion airport.
3. Tower: ANAC123, report decision on landing or go-around.
4. Approach: ANAC123, hold as published, advise when ready for approach.
5. Control: ANAC123, request fuel endurance and alternate.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, requesting hold, will advise intentions in two minutes.
2. ANAC123, diverting Florianópolis, request routing.
3. ANAC123, going around, will advise further intentions.
4. ANAC123, ready for approach, request vectors.
5. ANAC123, endurance one hour two zero, alternate Congonhas.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Leadership is clarity under pressure.

A weak leader hides behind “what do you think?” forever. A strong leader asks for input, then owns the call.

In Part 1 answers, examiners want structure: situation, options, decision, communication. That is leadership language.

In the H130, leadership often means stopping a rushed landing. Decks, pads, and wires punish indecision and punish ego equally. Brief the escape. Invite the challenge. Decide early.

On frequency, one voice should carry the plan. Internally you may discuss; externally ATC needs a decision.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Describe how a captain demonstrates leadership when weather at destination becomes marginal.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

The captain should brief a clear plan, invite crew input, then decide early whether to hold, divert, or continue. Leadership means allocating tasks, stating the decision, and communicating intentions to ATC without delay. The captain remains open to challenges but does not leave the aircraft without a plan.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Confusing leadership with never accepting crew input.  
  ✔ Strong leaders invite challenges, then decide.

- ❌ Debating options on frequency instead of stating intentions.  
  ✔ Decide in the cockpit; transmit one clear plan.

- ❌ Delaying the diversion decision until fuel is critical.  
  ✔ Lead early — options shrink with fuel and weather.

- ❌ Giving vague briefings (“be careful”).  
  ✔ Brief roles, minima, escape, and go-around triggers.

Requesting hold, will advise intentions, possible diversion.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Destination weather at Santos drops below your company minima while you are twenty miles out in the H130. Fuel allows one hold then diversion to Congonhas.

**Your first task**

Approach asks you to advise intentions. What do you say?

**Your second task**

After one hold, weather remains below minima. What do you say to ATC?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, requesting hold, will advise intentions after weather assessment. Second task: ANAC123, diverting Congonhas, request routing and altitude. Leadership is a clear plan transmitted once — hold to assess, then divert without delay when minima are not available.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Leadership is the plan people can follow.

Brief it. Invite the challenge. Decide. Communicate.

On the radio, one aircraft — one intention. In the cockpit, shared understanding before shared risk.

In the H130, leadership often means choosing the escape before the pad. That decision, spoken clearly, is CRM at its best.
