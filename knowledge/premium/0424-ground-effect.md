# Ground Effect

**Catalog ID:** 0424  
**Category:** Performance  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Ground Effect — the increase in rotor efficiency when hovering or flying near the surface, caused by reduced induced flow as the ground interrupts downwash, allowing the helicopter to require less power than in an out-of-ground-effect hover.

Ground effect is a performance gift near the pad — and a trap if you count on it when you must climb away.

For H130 operations in Brazil — rooftops, hospital pads, Amazon clearings, and sloping terrain — you must understand IGE versus OGE and explain power-limited intentions to ATC when ground effect will not save the departure.

Understanding how to discuss ground effect in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 4 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Ground Effect is the aerodynamic condition near the surface in which a helicopter's rotor system becomes more efficient because the ground reduces induced downwash, decreasing power required for hover or slow flight compared with the same weight out of ground effect.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Efeito solo / Ground Effect.

Condição aerodinâmica perto da superfície em que o sistema de rotor do helicóptero se torna mais eficiente porque o solo reduz o fluxo descendente induzido, diminuindo a potência necessária para pairar ou voar lento em comparação com o mesmo peso fora do efeito solo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When considering ground effect, the pilot should:

- distinguish in-ground-effect (IGE) hover from out-of-ground-effect (OGE) hover performance
- not plan a departure that requires OGE hover capability if only IGE power exists
- use ground effect for landing cushion and confined-area precision when appropriate
- request runway or into-wind departure when OGE climb is not available
- explain unable high hover or vertical departure when power limited

Ground effect reporting commonly includes:

- unable high hover
- request low hover / air taxi
- unable vertical departure, request runway
- power limited for OGE

**Important:** A comfortable IGE hover does not prove you can climb vertically out of ground effect. Brief the difference before you lift.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers support ground-effect-limited operations by:

- allowing air taxi instead of high hover crossings
- approving runway use for helicopters
- providing wind for better hover margins
- understanding why a helicopter cannot "pop up" vertically on request
- keeping traffic clear during slow IGE air taxi

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You lift your H130 into an IGE hover on a Congonhas pad. Power margin looks acceptable. Tower asks you to climb vertically to two hundred feet and hold for traffic.

You check OGE charts mentally — not available at this weight and temperature.

Tower, unable high hover, power limited, request air taxi or delay for departure into wind.

Tower delays the jet and clears your into-wind departure. You never leave ground effect without a plan to replace it with translational lift.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, climb to two hundred and hold, traffic on final.
2. Tower: ANAC123, roger unable high hover, hold position, expect short delay.
3. Tower: ANAC123, air taxi via alpha approved, remain low.
4. Tower: ANAC123, cleared for takeoff, departure into wind.
5. Tower: ANAC123, wind calm, say if you require runway for departure.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, unable climb to two hundred, power limited for high hover.
2. ANAC123, holding position, standing by.
3. ANAC123, air taxi via alpha, remaining low.
4. ANAC123, cleared for takeoff, into wind.
5. ANAC123, affirmative, request runway departure.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Ground effect English is the difference between IGE and OGE said in operational words.

ATC hears "unable high hover" and "request air taxi." They do not need the induced-flow lecture.

In the H130 on Brazilian pads, a skimming IGE hover can feel safe while an OGE hover at the same weight is impossible. That gap kills departures that "looked fine" in the low hover.

Use ground effect to land and to position. Do not use it as proof you can climb vertically over wires and rooftops.

When wind is calm and density altitude is high, say the limitation early. Part 4 negotiation beats a settling-with-power surprise.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Tower asks you to climb vertically and hover high for traffic. You are power limited for OGE. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would refuse the high hover clearly: Unable high hover, power limited, request air taxi or delay for into-wind departure. I would not climb out of ground effect without performance to sustain the hover or transition.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Accepting a high hover because the low hover felt easy.  
  ✔ IGE comfort ≠ OGE capability; say unable high hover.

- ❌ Explaining "efeito solo" in long Portuguese on frequency.  
  ✔ Use operational English: unable high hover, request air taxi.

- ❌ Planning a vertical rooftop departure on IGE-only power.  
  ✔ Plan translational lift departure or reduce weight.

- ❌ Ignoring calm wind that removes helpful inflow.  
  ✔ Request delay or runway when margins are thin.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

IGE hover at Congonhas. Tower wants a two-hundred-foot hover for a landing jet.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

After the jet lands, you still cannot do OGE. How do you request departure?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, unable high hover, power limited, request hold on pad or air taxi clear. Second task: ANAC123, request departure into wind, will accelerate then climb — or request runway if needed. Do not accept an OGE hover you cannot sustain.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Ground effect helps near the surface. It does not follow you up.

Know IGE from OGE. Say unable high hover when charts say so. Replace ground effect with translational lift on departure.

In the H130 across Brazil, the low hover can lie. Believe the performance data — and make ATC requests that match it.
