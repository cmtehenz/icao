# Give Way to Aircraft

**Catalog ID:** 0210  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Give Way to Aircraft — the ATC instruction to yield priority on the apron, taxiway, or intersection so another aircraft can proceed first.

Give way is surface traffic management in plain English. It prevents two aircraft from occupying the same piece of pavement or hover path at the same time. The pilot who receives the instruction holds, slows, or waits until the other aircraft is clear.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, give way is highly relevant. Hover taxi and air taxi paths cross fixed-wing taxi routes on Brazilian airports. Students must hear the instruction, identify the traffic, hold correctly, and continue only when clear.

Understanding give-way phraseology and readbacks in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency and daily mixed-traffic apron safety.

## 📖 Meaning

Give way to aircraft instructs a pilot to yield priority to another specified aircraft and not proceed until that aircraft has passed or is clear of the conflicting route.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Dê passagem à aeronave / give way to aircraft.

Instrução para o piloto ceder prioridade a outra aeronave especificada e não prosseguir até que essa aeronave tenha passado ou esteja livre da rota em conflito.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC says give way to an aircraft, the crew should:

- identify the conflicting aircraft visually or by position report
- stop, hold hover, or slow as required to remain clear
- read back the give-way instruction including the traffic identity if given
- proceed only when the other aircraft is clear and it is safe
- advise ATC if the traffic is not in sight or if unable to give way safely

Give way instructions commonly include:

- give way to the Airbus on your right
- give way to helicopter inbound on Alpha
- give way to aircraft vacating the runway
- give way to company traffic pushing back
- hold and give way, then continue taxi

**Important:** Give way does not mean abandon your clearance forever. It means sequence behind the specified traffic, then continue on your cleared route. In the H130, holding in a hover to give way burns fuel and increases workload — plan a stable hover or set down if a long delay is expected and procedures allow.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use give way to:

- resolve taxiway and apron intersection conflicts
- establish priority between arrivals, departures, and towed aircraft
- protect runway vacation paths
- sequence helicopters and airplanes on shared routes
- keep surface flow moving without stop-all instructions when one yield is enough

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are hover taxiing the H130 via Alpha toward the holding point at Florianópolis.

Ground calls and tells you to give way to a Boeing vacating runway three one onto Alpha.

You hold your hover short of the intersection, confirm the Boeing in sight, and wait until it passes and turns toward the terminal.

When Alpha is clear, you continue hover taxi and report if required.

If you had not given way, rotor wash and wingtip geometry on a narrow Brazilian taxiway could become an incident in seconds. Yielding is not hesitation — it is controlled sequencing.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, give way to the Boeing vacating runway three one.
2. Ground: ANAC123, give way to helicopter on Bravo, then continue via Alpha.
3. Ground: ANAC123, hold short of Charlie, give way to aircraft taxiing from the ramp.
4. Apron: ANAC123, give way to company traffic pushing back from stand five.
5. Ground: ANAC123, give way to ANAC456 on your left, then taxi to holding point Delta.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Give way is the surface version of see and avoid — with ATC naming the traffic for you.

Your job is identification, yield, and continuation. Not negotiation. Not racing to the intersection.

In the H130, you may be more maneuverable than the airplane you are yielding to. That does not give you priority. Priority is what ATC assigns.

If traffic is not in sight, say so immediately. Holding blind at an intersection is dangerous. Ask for the traffic's position or request progressive taxi.

When the other aircraft is clear, continue without waiting for a second invitation unless Ground told you to hold for further clearance. Read the instruction carefully: give way then continue is different from hold short and wait.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

While taxiing, Ground tells you to give way to another aircraft on your right. Explain what you do.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would read back the give-way instruction, identify the aircraft on my right, and stop or hold position so that aircraft can pass clear of my route. I would not proceed into the intersection until the traffic is clear and it is safe. If I cannot see the traffic, I would advise Ground that traffic is not in sight and request further assistance. After the aircraft has passed, I would continue on my cleared taxi or hover taxi route.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing slowly into the intersection while "giving way."  
  ✔ Stop or hold clear — giving way means the other aircraft goes first.

- ❌ Saying "OK" instead of a proper give-way readback.  
  ✔ Read back: giving way to [traffic], then continue as cleared.

- ❌ Assuming helicopter maneuverability grants automatic priority.  
  ✔ Follow ATC priority. Give way when instructed.

- ❌ Forgetting to continue after the traffic is clear.  
  ✔ When clear, proceed on the remaining clearance unless told to hold.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are hover taxiing the H130 on Alpha at Congonhas. An Embraer is vacating the runway onto Alpha ahead of your intersection.

**Your first task**

Ground says: "ANAC123, give way to the Embraer vacating runway one seven left." What do you reply?

**Your second task**

The Embraer passes and Alpha is clear. Ground has already said give way then continue. What do you do and say if anything?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, giving way to the Embraer vacating runway one seven left. Second task: I continue hover taxi via Alpha once the Embraer is clear. If useful or requested, I may report continuing; otherwise I proceed on the remaining clearance without blocking the intersection.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Give way keeps shared pavement honest.

Hear the traffic. See the traffic. Hold clear. Then continue.

In H130 academy flying on Brazilian airports, you will yield to airliners, trainers, tugs, and other helicopters. Maneuverability is not a license to squeeze through.

Clear readbacks. Stable holds. Clean continuations.

That is how mixed traffic moves without metal meeting metal.
