# Confined Area Landing

**Catalog ID:** 0070  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers confined area landing — one of the most demanding helicopter skills.

Confined Area Landing means landing in a restricted space surrounded by obstacles such as trees, buildings, wires, or terrain.

In the H130, confined area operations are common in Brazilian offshore support, construction sites, and hospital helipads in urban areas.

You must assess wind, obstacles, and escape routes before committing, then inform ATC of your intentions.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 operational communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Confined Area Landing is a helicopter landing in a limited space with surrounding obstacles requiring precise approach and departure techniques.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pouso em área confinada.

Pouso de helicóptero em espaço limitado com obstáculos ao redor, exigindo técnicas precisas de aproximação e decolagem.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When conducting a Confined Area Landing, the pilot should:

- reconnaissance the area for obstacles, wires, and wind effects
- select an approach path that allows a safe go-around
- inform ATC or company frequency of landing intentions
- execute a steep approach if required with clearance from obstacles
- report safe on the ground or go-around if conditions are unsafe

Confined Area Landing reporting commonly includes:

- landing confined area or landing at construction site
- position and description of landing site
- request traffic cleared from approach path
- report go-around if unable to land safely

**Important:** Confined area operations require go-around readiness. Inform ATC when operating in controlled airspace near the confined site.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Confined Area Landing reports to:

- clear traffic from the approach and departure paths
- coordinate with other aircraft in the vicinity
- maintain awareness of non-standard landing sites
- request position reports during approach
- prepare for possible go-around calls

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are ferrying personnel to a construction site in a valley near Joinville in your H130.

The site is surrounded by cranes and trees with a twenty-meter landing pad.

You orbit once to assess wind and obstacles, then call:

Joinville Approach, ANAC123, landing confined area construction site five miles north of the airport, request traffic advisory.

Approach acknowledges and clears traffic from your area.

You execute a steep approach and land safely on the pad.

You report safe on the ground.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger confined area landing, report final and safe on the ground.
2. Tower: ANAC123, no reported traffic in your area, cleared to land at your discretion.
3. Approach: ANAC123, say position and description of landing site.
4. Tower: ANAC123, wind two seven zero at one two knots, report go-around if unable.
5. Approach: ANAC123, traffic two miles south, remain clear of your landing area.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Landing confined area construction site five miles north, ANAC123.
2. ANAC123, landing confined area hospital helipad, will report final.
3. ANAC123, unable confined area landing, going around, will reattempt.
4. Tower, ANAC123, safe on the ground confined area landing complete.
5. ANAC123, confined area landing site clear, request departure in five minutes.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Confined area landing is where experience matters most.

You cannot land what you have not assessed.

The sequence:

Reconnaissance. Wind. Escape route. Commit or go around.

One orbit minimum — often two.

Identify wires last — they kill more helicopter pilots than engine failures.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "pouso em local restrito" on frequency.

Use landing confined area — ICAO standard phraseology.

In the H130, always have a go-around plan before you descend below your escape altitude.

Inform ATC — even VFR — so someone knows where you are.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How do you prepare for a confined area landing and what would you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would conduct a reconnaissance orbit to assess obstacles, wind, and escape routes. I would inform ATC of my intention to land at a confined area, state my position and description of the site, and request traffic advisory. I would execute a steep approach if required, go around if unsafe, and report safe on the ground after landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Landing without reconnaissance orbit.  
  ✔ Always assess obstacles and wind before committing.

- ❌ Saying "pouso difícil" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use landing confined area.

- ❌ Not having a planned go-around escape route.  
  ✔ Identify escape path before descending below point of no return.

- ❌ Forgetting to report safe on the ground.  
  ✔ Confirm landing complete to ATC or company frequency.
