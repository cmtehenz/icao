# Air Taxi

**Catalog ID:** 0068  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers air taxi — helicopter movement at higher speed and altitude than hover taxi.

Air Taxi means movement of a helicopter above the surface at more than twenty knots and above one hundred feet AGL, typically between points on or in the vicinity of an airport.

In the H130, air taxi is used to reposition quickly between helipads or to depart the airport traffic area efficiently.

You must obtain clearance, state your route and altitude, and read back correctly.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 radiotelephony.

## 📖 Meaning

Air Taxi is helicopter movement above the surface at speeds above translational lift and at altitudes typically above one hundred feet, in the airport vicinity.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Táxi aéreo.

Movimento do helicóptero acima da superfície em velocidade e altitude maiores que o táxi em sustentação, na vizinhança do aeródromo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Air Taxi is used, the pilot should:

- obtain clearance from Tower before air taxiing
- read back route, altitude, and any restrictions
- maintain assigned altitude and avoid other traffic
- remain within the airport traffic area unless cleared otherwise
- report reaching the assigned point or ready for departure

Air Taxi clearances commonly include:

- air taxi to the helipad at five hundred feet
- air taxi via north route, remain south of the runway
- report ready for departure at the helipad
- remain clear of fixed-wing traffic

**Important:** Air taxi is faster and higher than hover taxi — but still requires clearance. Do not confuse air taxi with departure; you still need takeoff clearance.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC uses Air Taxi to:

- reposition helicopters efficiently on the airport
- separate helicopter traffic from fixed-wing patterns
- maintain traffic flow at busy heliports
- clear helicopters from active runway areas quickly
- coordinate simultaneous fixed-wing and helicopter operations

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are at the maintenance helipad at Florianópolis Airport in your H130.

Tower clears you:

Tower: ANAC123, air taxi to departure helipad via the south route, remain below five hundred feet, report ready.

You read back, depart the maintenance pad at sixty knots, and follow the south route at three hundred feet.

You report ready at the departure helipad and receive takeoff clearance.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, air taxi to helipad two, remain below four hundred feet.
2. Tower: ANAC123, air taxi via north, remain clear of runway one four traffic.
3. Ground: ANAC123, air taxi to the fuel pad, report when ready.
4. Tower: ANAC123, air taxi to departure point, contact tower on this frequency.
5. Tower: ANAC123, air taxi behind the landing helicopter, remain one thousand feet south of the runway.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Air taxi to helipad two, remain below four hundred feet, ANAC123.
2. Air taxi via north, remain clear of runway one four traffic, ANAC123.
3. Air taxi to fuel pad, will report ready, ANAC123.
4. Air taxi to departure point, ANAC123.
5. Air taxi behind landing traffic, remain one thousand feet south of runway, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Air taxi is the helicopter's fast reposition on the airport.

Faster than hover taxi.

Higher than hover taxi.

Same clearance discipline.

You are not departing until Tower says cleared for takeoff.

You are not landing until cleared to land.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "taxi aéreo rápido" or just "air taxi" without the full clearance readback.

Read back altitude restrictions and remain clear instructions — they matter for separation.

In the H130, know the difference:

Hover taxi — low, slow, close to the ground.

Air taxi — faster, higher, still on the airport.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between hover taxi and air taxi?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Hover taxi is helicopter movement at low altitude and slow speed, generally below translational lift airspeed. Air taxi is movement at higher speed, typically above twenty knots and above one hundred feet AGL, in the airport vicinity. Both require ATC clearance. I would read back the assigned route, altitude restrictions, and hold instructions for either procedure.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating air taxi as a departure clearance.  
  ✔ Air taxi is repositioning — you still need takeoff clearance to depart.

- ❌ Ignoring altitude restrictions in the clearance.  
  ✔ Read back and comply with remain below or remain at altitude instructions.

- ❌ Saying "voo baixo" instead of air taxi.  
  ✔ Use air taxi to helipad with route and altitude as cleared.

- ❌ Not reporting ready at the departure point.  
  ✔ Report ready when positioned for departure.

You need to reposition from the fuel pad to the departure helipad at Navegantes.
