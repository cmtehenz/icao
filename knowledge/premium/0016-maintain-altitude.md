# Maintain Altitude

**Catalog ID:** 0016  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the simplest ATC instructions—and one of the easiest to misunderstand.

When a controller says:

"Maintain altitude."

The instruction is not telling you to climb.

It is not telling you to descend.

It simply means:

Stay exactly where you are vertically until further instructed.

Professional pilots know that altitude discipline is one of the foundations of safe flight.

## 📖 Meaning

Maintain Altitude instructs the pilot to continue flying at the current assigned altitude until receiving another ATC clearance.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Mantenha a altitude.

Continue voando exatamente na altitude autorizada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Maintain Altitude, the pilot must:

- keep the current altitude
- avoid climbing or descending
- continue following assigned heading or route
- monitor altitude continuously

This instruction protects vertical separation between aircraft.

Even a deviation of a few hundred feet may create a traffic conflict.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Maintain Altitude to:

- preserve vertical separation
- organize arriving and departing traffic
- prevent altitude conflicts
- delay climbs or descents
- coordinate crossing traffic
- simplify traffic management

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're flying your H130 at 4,000 feet.

Another aircraft is climbing through 3,000 feet beneath you.

Departure says:

Departure: ANAC123, maintain four thousand feet.

You keep your current altitude.

A few moments later, after the conflicting aircraft has passed, ATC transmits:

Departure: ANAC123, climb and maintain six thousand feet.

Only then do you begin climbing.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Departure: ANAC123, maintain four thousand feet.
2. Approach: ANAC123, maintain present altitude.
3. Departure: ANAC123, maintain five thousand until advised.
4. Approach: ANAC123, maintain altitude due crossing traffic.
5. Center: ANAC123, maintain flight level one zero zero.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Maintaining four thousand, ANAC123.
2. Maintaining present altitude, ANAC123.
3. Maintaining five thousand until advised, ANAC123.
4. Maintaining altitude, ANAC123.
5. Maintaining flight level one zero zero, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine you're standing on the fifth floor of a building waiting for an elevator.

Someone tells you:

"Stay right there."

They don't want you to go up.

They don't want you to go down.

They simply want you to remain exactly where you are.

Altitude instructions work the same way.

When ATC says:

Maintain altitude

there is only one correct response:

Stay exactly at the assigned altitude until another clearance is received.

Professional pilots constantly monitor the altimeter because holding altitude accurately is just as important as flying the correct heading.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Why is maintaining the assigned altitude important?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Maintaining the assigned altitude helps ATC keep safe vertical separation between aircraft. Deviating from the assigned altitude may create a conflict with nearby traffic and reduce overall flight safety.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Beginning a climb because the next cruise altitude is known.  
  ✔ Wait for an altitude clearance.

- ❌ Descending early before receiving clearance.  
  ✔ Maintain the assigned altitude.

- ❌ Focusing only on navigation.  
  ✔ Monitor altitude continuously.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Maintaining four thousand, ANAC123.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Imagine cruise control in your car.

Once set, the speed remains constant until you deliberately change it.

Maintain Altitude is cruise control for your altitude.

Don't climb.

Don't descend.

Stay exactly where ATC placed you.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

Departure: ANAC123, maintain four thousand feet.

**Your first task**

Read back the instruction exactly as a professional pilot.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

If you know your cruise altitude will be 8,000 feet, may you begin climbing before ATC clears you? Explain why.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

No. I must remain at the assigned altitude until ATC issues a new altitude clearance. Climbing without authorization could reduce separation from other aircraft and create a hazardous situation.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Today's lesson reinforces a simple but essential principle:

Altitude is assigned—not guessed.

Good pilots don't anticipate climb or descent clearances.

They fly the altitude they have, not the altitude they expect.

Remember:

Maintain means exactly that—maintain.
