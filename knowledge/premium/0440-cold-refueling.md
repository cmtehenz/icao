# Cold Refueling

**Catalog ID:** 0440  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Cold Refueling — refueling with engines shut down and rotors stopped, the standard and safest method for adding fuel on the ground.

Cold refueling is normal H130 turnaround practice at Brazilian airports, helipads, and bases when time allows and hot refuel is not authorized or not required.

You must communicate shutdown, refuel status, and readiness for start in ICAO English for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Cold Refueling is the process of adding fuel to a helicopter with engines shut down and rotors fully stopped, minimizing fire risk associated with running engines and allowing safer personnel movement around the aircraft during servicing.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Reabastecimento a frio / cold refueling.

Processo de abastecer um helicóptero com motores desligados e rotores completamente parados, minimizando o risco de fogo associado a motores em funcionamento e permitindo movimentação mais segura de pessoal ao redor da aeronave durante o serviço.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When conducting cold refueling, the pilot should:

- shut down and confirm rotors stopped before fuel begins to flow
- ensure bonding/grounding and correct fuel grade per procedure
- keep ignition sources and unauthorized personnel clear
- monitor for spills and stop fuel flow if any anomaly occurs
- advise ATC of extended pad time and when ready for start

Cold refuel reporting commonly includes:

- on pad, shutting down for cold refuel
- cold refuel in progress, request time on pad
- refuel complete, request start and departure
- aborting refuel, fuel spill, request assistance

**Important:** Cold refuel is the default. Use it unless operational need and authorization justify hot refuel. Do not rush the rotor stop — blade sail and wind limits still apply during shutdown.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use cold-refuel information to:

- plan pad occupancy time with rotors stopped
- sequence other helicopters around a shut-down aircraft
- respond to spill or fire calls during refueling
- know when to expect start request and departure
- coordinate tow or parking if refuel is delayed

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You land at a base helipad in your H130 after a mission. Turnaround allows cold refuel.

You transmit:

On pad, shutting down for cold refuel, request time on pad, will advise ready for start.

After shutdown and refuel, you call:

Refuel complete, ready for start, request departure clearance.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger shutting down, time on pad approved, advise ready.
2. Tower: ANAC123, report refuel complete and ready for start.
3. Tower: ANAC123, roger spill, assistance rolling, say status.
4. Tower: ANAC123, cleared start, report ready for departure.
5. Tower: ANAC123, cleared departure, climb on course.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, shutting down for cold refuel, will advise ready.
2. ANAC123, wilco, will report refuel complete.
3. ANAC123, fuel spill, aborting refuel, request assistance.
4. ANAC123, ready for start, request departure.
5. ANAC123, cleared departure, climbing on course.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Cold refuel is the professional default.

Engines off. Blades stopped. Fuel flowing under control. People can work around the aircraft with far less rotor risk.

In the H130, cold refuel is how most Brazilian base turns should look. Part 2 loves simple clarity: shutting down for cold refuel; refuel complete, ready for start.

Do not call ready for start while fuel is still connected. Finish the servicing. Then start. Then go.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You need to refuel on the pad with time available. What do you say to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would shut down normally and transmit: on pad, shutting down for cold refuel, request time on pad, will advise when ready for start. After refuel is complete and the aircraft is prepared, I would say: refuel complete, ready for start, request departure clearance. I would not request start while refueling is still in progress.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Requesting start while the fuel hose is still connected.  
  ✔ Complete refuel and secure the aircraft first.

- ❌ Not advising ATC of extended pad occupancy.  
  ✔ Request time on pad for cold refuel.

- ❌ Treating cold refuel as "no radio needed."  
  ✔ Keep ATC informed of status and readiness.

- ❌ Shutting down in out-of-limit wind without care.  
  ✔ Respect blade sail limits during shutdown.
