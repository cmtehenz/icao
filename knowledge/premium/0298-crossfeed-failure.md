# Crossfeed Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0298  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Crossfeed Failure — inability to feed an engine from the opposite tank or to balance fuel through the crossfeed system, which can leave fuel unusable on one side and complicate single-engine or imbalance scenarios. Crossfeed language is classic ICAO systems English; for H130 crews, APU and multi-engine jet crossfeed may not match your airframe — still learn the exam vocabulary and map it to any inability to feed usable fuel where needed.

Crossfeed failure is Medium priority because the aircraft may continue normally until an engine or imbalance event makes crossfeed essential.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for crossfeed failure, then transfer the reporting structure to helicopter fuel-feed limitations under DECEA operations.

Understanding crossfeed failure in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Crossfeed Failure is the inability of the fuel crossfeed system to supply an engine from an alternate tank or to balance fuel between sides as designed, which may leave fuel unusable, worsen imbalance, or reduce options after an engine failure, requiring checklist actions and clear notification to ATC of the malfunction, usable fuel, and intentions.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When crossfeed fails, the crew should:

- confirm crossfeed valve and pump status per AFM/QRH
- assess which fuel remains usable for which engine or tank feed
- recalculate endurance and destination suitability
- declare Pan Pan if the failure forces return, diversion, or limits single-engine options
- report crossfeed failure, effect on usable fuel, and intentions
- for H130: use the same structure for fuel-feed anomalies that prevent using available fuel as planned

Crossfeed failure reporting commonly includes:

- crossfeed failure / unable to crossfeed
- fuel unusable on one side / imbalance developing
- request return / diversion / priority if needed
- souls and fuel remaining when asked
- advise if engine feed is limited

**Important:** On the ICAO speaking test, pronounce and use "crossfeed" correctly. In H130 line flying, you may not have a classic jet crossfeed — retain exam English and transfer the logic to any feed-path failure that traps fuel.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to crossfeed failure reports to:

- understand reduced fuel options without assuming immediate engine failure
- offer return or diversion while endurance is still comfortable
- prioritize if usable fuel becomes limited
- prepare for possible later engine or imbalance complications
- alert emergency services when requested for a systems-related landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Exam-style scenario: after selecting crossfeed to balance tanks, the valve fails to open. Fuel remains trapped on one side. You decide not to continue to destination.

You transmit:

Pan Pan Pan, Control, ANAC123, crossfeed failure, fuel unusable on one side, request return to land.

If the situation combines with low usable fuel:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, crossfeed failure, low usable fuel, request immediate priority landing.

For H130 reality, keep the same urgency structure for any feed failure that changes your fuel plan.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Crossfeed is a backup path. When it fails, you lose options you hoped you would never need.

Do not wait for an engine failure to discover you cannot crossfeed. If the valve fails in normal balancing, treat it as a plan-changer now.

Say "crossfeed failure" clearly. Examiners listen for that exact term. Then add the operational effect: unusable fuel, imbalance, or limited endurance.

In the H130, classic crossfeed may be limited or absent — that does not excuse weak exam English. Learn the word. Transfer the decision: if you cannot feed usable fuel where you need it, change the destination early.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Crossfeed fails and fuel on one side becomes unusable for the planned flight. What do you do, and what do you say to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would apply the crossfeed failure checklist, determine which fuel remains usable, and recalculate range. I would declare Pan Pan, report crossfeed failure with unusable fuel on one side, and request return or diversion as required. If usable fuel becomes critically low, I would declare Mayday and request immediate priority landing. I would provide souls and fuel when asked. On a helicopter, I would use the same structure for any fuel-feed failure that traps usable fuel.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Pronouncing "crossfeed" unclearly or avoiding the term.  
  ✔ Practice "CROSS-feed failure" as a standard systems phrase.

- ❌ Continuing as planned because total fuel quantity still looks adequate.  
  ✔ Plan on usable, feedable fuel only.

- ❌ Mixing crossfeed failure with transfer failure without clarifying.  
  ✔ Use the correct system name; add effect if both apply.

- ❌ Ignoring the topic because H130 may lack jet-style crossfeed.  
  ✔ Learn exam English; transfer feed-path logic to the helicopter.
