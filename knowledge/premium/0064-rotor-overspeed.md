# Rotor Overspeed

**Catalog ID:** 0064  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers rotor overspeed — when rotor RPM exceeds safe limits.

Rotor Overspeed means main rotor RPM has risen above the maximum allowable limit — centrifugal forces on the blades increase and structural failure becomes possible.

In the H130, rotor RPM must stay within the green arc for safe operation.

Overspeed can result from governor failure, collective mismanagement, or emergency descent.

You must aviate first, correct RPM, then communicate with the correct urgency call.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Rotor Overspeed is the condition where main rotor RPM exceeds maximum allowable limits, threatening structural damage or blade separation.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Rotor Overspeed occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the first priority
- reduce collective to decrease rotor RPM
- verify governor operation and engine parameters
- declare Mayday if RPM cannot be controlled
- declare Pan Pan if overspeed is corrected and stable

Rotor Overspeed reporting commonly includes:

- rotor overspeed or rotor RPM high
- correcting RPM, monitoring rotor parameters
- request vectors to nearest suitable landing area
- souls on board when requested

**Important:** Uncontrolled rotor overspeed is distress — Mayday. Brief overspeed corrected promptly may warrant Pan Pan for precautionary landing. Aviate first — then communicate clearly with standard phraseology.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Rotor Overspeed declarations to:

- clear traffic from the aircraft's route
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable landing area
- coordinate emergency services if distress is declared
- request souls on board and position
- prepare for possible autorotation if overspeed leads to failure

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are descending toward Navegantes at one thousand two hundred feet in your H130.

During a steep descent, rotor RPM rises into the red arc and the overspeed warning activates.

You raise collective slightly to reduce RPM and level the descent.

When RPM returns to the green arc, you transmit:

Pan Pan Pan Pan, Navegantes Approach, ANAC123, rotor overspeed, RPM now corrected, request vectors to Navegantes for precautionary landing.

Approach clears traffic and provides direct routing.

You land safely for inspection.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say current rotor RPM and souls on board.
2. Approach: ANAC123, turn right heading one eight zero, descend eight hundred, Navegantes five miles.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway two nine cleared to land, emergency services notified.
4. Tower: ANAC123, wind two seven zero at one two knots, report final.
5. Departure: ANAC123, say nature of rotor problem and intentions.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Rotor overspeed is a numbers game.

RPM above the red line means blades are stressed beyond design limits.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Aviate. RPM. Communicate.

Fly the aircraft first.

Adjust collective to bring RPM back to the green arc.

Check governor and engine parameters.

Then declare your urgency.

Brief overspeed corrected — Pan Pan for precautionary landing.

Uncontrolled overspeed — Mayday.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "RPM alto" or "rotor acelerado" on frequency.

Use rotor overspeed or rotor RPM high — ICAO standard terms.

In the H130, steep descents with low collective are a common overspeed trigger.

Train the collective reflex before you need the radio.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if you experienced rotor overspeed during descent?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain aircraft control, adjust collective to correct rotor RPM, and declare Pan Pan Pan Pan or Mayday depending on whether RPM is controlled. I would report rotor overspeed or rotor RPM high, state whether RPM is now corrected, and request vectors to the nearest suitable landing area for precautionary landing and inspection.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Ignoring a brief overspeed without landing for inspection.  
  ✔ Overspeed stresses blades — precautionary landing and inspection required.

- ❌ Saying "RPM do rotor alto" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use rotor overspeed or rotor RPM high.

- ❌ Continuing flight after uncontrolled overspeed.  
  ✔ Land immediately if RPM cannot be controlled.

- ❌ Not reporting whether RPM is currently corrected.  
  ✔ Tell ATC current RPM status — it determines urgency level.

**Pronunciation:** pan pan pan pan, ROH-ter OH-ver-speed, R-P-M kor-REK-ted

**Word Stress**

- Overspeed → OH-ver-speed

Practice:

Together:

Speak slowly and clearly — controllers need every word.
