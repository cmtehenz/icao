# Flight Purser

**Catalog ID:** 0343  
**Category:** Crew  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Flight Purser — the senior cabin crew member who leads the cabin team, coordinates with the captain on cabin status, and is the primary cabin contact for safety briefings and abnormal procedures.

ICAO and airline English distinguish flight attendant (cabin crew member) from flight purser (cabin leader). Examiners may ask for the difference. Controllers will not usually say "purser," but pilots must use the term correctly in crew coordination answers.

For H130 ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, daily operations may have no cabin crew hierarchy — yet Part 1 vocabulary still requires the concept for multi-crew airline scenarios and professional English precision.

Master: flight purser, senior cabin crew, cabin report to captain.

## 📖 Meaning

Flight Purser is the senior cabin crew member in charge of the cabin crew team, responsible for cabin safety leadership, passenger service coordination, and direct communication with the flight crew regarding cabin readiness and emergencies.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Chefe de cabine / Flight Purser.

Membro sênior da tripulação de cabine responsável pela equipe de cabine, pela liderança de segurança na cabine, pela coordenação do serviço aos passageiros e pela comunicação direta com a tripulação de voo sobre prontidão da cabine e emergências.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When coordinating with a flight purser, the flight crew should:

- treat the purser as the primary cabin point of contact
- brief expected turbulence, diversion, ditching, or evacuation plans through the purser
- receive cabin secure and passenger status from the purser
- confirm emergency commands alignment: brace, evacuate, remain seated
- keep sterile cockpit exceptions limited to safety calls from cabin leadership

Flight purser coordination commonly includes:

- cabin secure for takeoff / landing
- passenger medical summary to the flight deck
- evacuation readiness and exit responsibilities
- all clear / cabin status after abnormal events

**Important:** Purser is a leadership role within cabin crew — not a flight deck rank. Do not confuse purser with first officer or captain.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

ATC does not normally address the purser, but the concept matters when:

- cabin-derived emergency information is relayed accurately by the flight crew
- examiners test correct crew-role vocabulary in English
- souls on board and assistance needs come from cabin leadership counts
- evacuation and medical events require clear flight-deck-to-ATC wording
- professional standard titles reduce ambiguity in multi-crew answers

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Before an approach to Brasília, the captain briefs the flight purser: expect possible go-around, cabin must remain secure, medical passenger in row twelve.

The purser confirms cabin secure and later reports the medical passenger stable.

If the situation worsens, the purser calls the flight deck; the pilots declare urgency to ATC.

In H130 operations without cabin crew, there is no purser — but the exam answer still names the role correctly when asked.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger, say cabin status for landing.
2. Tower: ANAC123, confirm medical assistance still required as reported by cabin.
3. Approach: ANAC123, advise souls on board.
4. Control: ANAC123, say if evacuation is expected after landing.
5. Tower: ANAC123, medical team will meet crew on the apron.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, cabin secure as reported by flight purser, ready for approach.
2. ANAC123, flight purser reports medical emergency, request priority landing and medical assistance.
3. ANAC123, cabin not secure, request delay, will advise when ready.
4. ANAC123, souls on board confirmed with cabin crew, assistance required on landing.
5. ANAC123, no evacuation planned, cabin secure for landing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Flight purser equals cabin lead.

If the question asks who is in charge of cabin crew, the answer is flight purser (or senior cabin crew member), not "the chief steward" as slang.

Brazilian operations may say chefe de cabine. On the ICAO exam, flight purser is the clean English term.

Your radio calls still come from the flight deck. The purser feeds you facts. You translate facts into ATC English.

In H130 Brazilian training flights, you may brief a single cabin-responsible person informally — do not invent a purser where none exists, and do not fail the exam for not knowing the airline term.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between a flight attendant and a flight purser?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A flight attendant is a cabin crew member responsible for passenger safety and cabin duties. A flight purser is the senior cabin crew member in charge of the cabin crew team and the main cabin contact with the captain for cabin secure status and emergency coordination.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Calling every cabin crew member "purser."  
  ✔ Purser is the senior cabin leader; others are flight attendants.

- ❌ Using only "chefe de cabine" in an English exam answer.  
  ✔ Prefer flight purser.

- ❌ Confusing purser with first officer.  
  ✔ Purser is cabin; first officer is flight deck.

- ❌ Briefing random attendants and bypassing the purser on a multi-attendant crew.  
  ✔ Brief the purser; let cabin leadership cascade the plan.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

The flight purser reports cabin smoke during descent. You will land ASAP.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit to Approach?

**Your second task**

Tower asks if you will evacuate. Purser says smoke is decreasing after checklist actions. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, smoke in cabin reported by flight purser, request priority landing and fire services standing by. Second task: ANAC123, smoke decreasing, evacuation not planned at this time, will advise if required. I would still keep fire services available until the situation is confirmed safe.
