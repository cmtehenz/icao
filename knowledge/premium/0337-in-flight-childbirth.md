# In-Flight Childbirth

**Catalog ID:** 0337  
**Category:** Passenger  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers In-Flight Childbirth — a rare but high-visibility passenger medical event in which a passenger goes into labor or delivers a baby during flight, requiring medical assistance, calm cabin management, and clear ATC coordination for priority landing.

ICAO exams may test the English for reporting a birth in progress or a newborn requiring medical care. Controllers need operational facts: urgency level, diversion intention, and medical services required — not clinical detail.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, cabin space is limited and medical equipment is basic. The priority is a safe, early landing at an airport with ambulance support, not attempting a full delivery management plan on frequency.

Master the phraseology: passenger in labor, childbirth in progress, newborn on board, request medical assistance.

## 📖 Meaning

In-Flight Childbirth is the onset of labor or the delivery of a baby during flight, treated as a passenger medical emergency that may require diversion, priority landing, and coordination with ground medical services for mother and newborn.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Parto a bordo / In-Flight Childbirth.

Início do trabalho de parto ou nascimento de um bebê durante o voo, tratado como emergência médica de passageiro que pode exigir desvio, pouso prioritário e coordenação com serviços médicos em solo para a mãe e o recém-nascido.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When in-flight childbirth is occurring or imminent, the crew should:

- obtain cabin status: labor in progress, delivery complete, mother and baby condition
- declare Pan Pan for urgency unless the situation becomes life-threatening
- divert to the nearest suitable airport with medical services when practicable
- request medical assistance for mother and newborn
- report souls on board including the newborn after birth when asked

In-flight childbirth reporting commonly includes:

- passenger in labor / childbirth in progress
- baby born on board / newborn on board
- request priority landing and medical assistance
- mother and baby condition if known (stable / assistance required)

**Important:** Keep radio language factual and calm. Do not narrate the delivery. Report status, intentions, and what you need on the ground.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to in-flight childbirth reports to:

- provide priority routing to a suitable airport with medical coverage
- alert ambulances and airport medical teams for mother and newborn
- obtain souls on board updates after birth
- clear traffic that would delay landing
- coordinate apron meeting point for medical services

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are flying a charter along the São Paulo coast when cabin reports a passenger in advanced labor.

You divert toward Santos Dumont area alternatives and select the nearest suitable airport with ambulance support.

You declare urgency, request priority, and ask for medical assistance for mother and baby.

After landing, medical services board. Mother and newborn are transferred. You update souls on board to include the newborn when ATC asks.

Calm radio. Early diversion. Professional handover.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say if childbirth in progress or completed.
2. Approach: ANAC123, divert approved, expect priority approach, medical services alerted.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, ambulances standing by for mother and newborn.
4. Control: ANAC123, say souls on board including newborn when able.
5. Tower: ANAC123, vacate when able, medical team will meet the aircraft.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

5. ANAC123, on final, mother and baby stable, still request medical assistance on landing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

In-flight childbirth is rare. Panic on the radio is common in poor training.

Your priorities: fly, divert early, speak calmly, get medical help on the ground.

Brazilian pilots may say "passageira vai ter neném" or "parto a bordo." Prefer passenger in labor, childbirth in progress, or baby born on board.

After birth, update souls on board. Controllers and rescue services need the correct count.

In the H130, you cannot create a delivery suite. You can create time — by diverting sooner rather than pressing to destination.

Do not ask ATC for medical advice. Ask for priority and ambulances. Cabin and any medical volunteer manage the passenger. You manage the aircraft and the frequency.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Cabin reports a passenger in labor. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare Pan Pan, report passenger in labor or childbirth in progress, request priority landing at the nearest suitable airport, and request medical assistance for mother and baby. After birth I would report baby born on board and update souls on board when ATC asks.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Using only informal Portuguese such as "vai nascer" on frequency.  
  ✔ Use passenger in labor / childbirth in progress / baby born on board.

- ❌ Continuing to destination when a closer airport has medical services.  
  ✔ Divert early when labor is advanced.

- ❌ Forgetting to update souls on board after the birth.  
  ✔ Include the newborn in the souls count when reporting.

- ❌ Giving clinical delivery details on frequency.  
  ✔ Report status and assistance required — keep it operational.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Cabin reports advanced labor twenty-five minutes from destination. A suitable airport with ambulances is twelve minutes away.

**Your first task**

What do you say to Approach as you divert?

**Your second task**

After landing, Tower asks for souls on board. A baby has just been born. What do you report?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, passenger in labor, childbirth in progress, diverting, request priority landing and medical assistance. Second task: ANAC123, souls on board seven including newborn, mother and baby require medical assistance. I would keep the call short and let medical services take over on the apron.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

In-flight childbirth is a medical event with an aviation solution: early diversion and clear English.

Fly the aircraft. Divert when time matters. Declare Pan Pan. Request medical assistance for mother and newborn. Update souls on board after birth.

Do not dramatize. Do not diagnose. Do not delay for destination convenience.

In Brazilian H130 operations, limited cabin space makes a prompt landing even more important.

Calm voice. Correct words. Medical team on the ground.
