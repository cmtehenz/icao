# Stabilizer Trim Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0274  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Stabilizer Trim Failure — loss of normal horizontal-stabilizer trim capability, leaving the aircraft difficult or impossible to trim for hands-off flight, often with high control forces, requiring abnormal procedures and priority ATC handling.

Stabilizer trim sets the pitch trim reference on many transport aircraft. When it fails — jammed, inoperative, or only manual — the crew may face continuous column force, limited speed flexibility, and a carefully planned approach and landing.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, there is no transport-style movable horizontal stabilizer trim as on large jets. Do not invent one. Teach the ICAO English for exams and multi-type careers; analogize only to "unable to relieve control forces" if discussing helicopter force trim carefully and factually.

Part 2 and Part 3: stabilizer trim failure, controls out of trim / heavy, request priority and a stable long final.

## 📖 Meaning

Stabilizer Trim Failure is a malfunction in which the horizontal stabilizer trim system cannot be operated normally — including jam, motor failure, or loss of electric trim — preventing normal pitch trim, often resulting in persistent control forces, and requiring abnormal procedures and ATC notification for priority landing assistance.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha do trim do estabilizador / Stabilizer Trim Failure.

Malfuncionamento em que o sistema de trim do estabilizador horizontal não pode ser operado normalmente — incluindo travamento, falha do motor ou perda do trim elétrico — impedindo o trim normal de arfagem, frequentemente resultando em forças persistentes nos comandos, e exigindo procedimentos anormais e notificação ao ATC para assistência prioritária ao pouso.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When stabilizer trim fails, the crew should:

- apply the AFM abnormal checklist for stabilizer trim inoperative or jammed
- use alternate trim methods if available (manual crank, backup electric, etc.)
- select a speed and configuration that minimize control forces per procedure
- advise ATC of stabilizer trim failure and high or abnormal control forces
- request priority vectors, a long final, and the longest suitable runway
- avoid last-minute speed or configuration changes that spike pitch force

Stabilizer trim failure scenarios commonly include:

- electric stabilizer trim inoperative — manual trim only
- stabilizer jammed at a takeoff or climb setting — high forces in cruise/approach
- inability to trim for landing configuration
- related to, but distinct from, active trim runaway (already stopped)
- exam radio: stabilizer trim failure, request priority landing

**Important:** Stabilizer trim failure is not the same call as trim runaway. Runaway is uncommanded motion now; failure is loss of normal trim function. Use the precise term. If forces are extreme, escalate urgency.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use stabilizer trim failure reports to:

- provide priority and uninterrupted long finals
- avoid speed changes and complex step-down approaches
- assign the longest runway for an out-of-trim landing
- alert emergency services when control forces are critical
- give crews time to configure early and stabilize

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Florianópolis in a jet training scenario. Stabilizer electric trim fails. Manual trim is slow; approach forces will be high.

You call:

ANAC123, stabilizer trim failure, controls will be heavy on approach, request priority long final runway one four, longest available.

Approach: ANAC123, roger stabilizer trim failure, priority approved, expect runway one four, extend downwind for long final.

You configure early, trim as best you can manually, and add:

ANAC123, configured, ready for long final, stabilizer trim failure.

You do not accept a slam-dunk with changing speeds every mile.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Stabilizer trim failure means you may fly the rest of the flight with your arms in the fight.

Configure early. Choose one speed. Ask for a long final. Do not let ATC step you down through five speed changes on a short approach.

Use the exact words: stabilizer trim failure. If it was a runaway that you already cut out, say trim runaway, trim isolated — then you may also have stabilizer trim inoperative. Be precise.

In the H130 academy, do not brief a phantom jet stabilizer. Brief the exam concept. If discussing helicopter trim, stay inside the AFM.

Heavy controls are manageable with planning. Surprise configuration changes are not.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Stabilizer electric trim fails. You can trim only slowly by hand. What do you tell ATC before approach?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would advise ATC of stabilizer trim failure and that control forces may be heavy on approach. I would request priority handling, an extended or long final, and the longest suitable runway. I would configure early, avoid unnecessary speed changes, and provide souls and fuel if requested.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Calling every trim issue "trim runaway" when the system simply failed.  
  ✔ Use stabilizer trim failure when trim will not operate normally.

- ❌ Accepting a short, stepped approach with multiple speed changes.  
  ✔ Request long final and stable speed.

- ❌ Changing flaps late and fighting a sudden pitch moment.  
  ✔ Configure early per checklist while forces are manageable.

- ❌ Vague "trim problem" without operational effect.  
  ✔ State stabilizer trim failure and controls heavy / out of trim.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Stabilizer trim failure is a long-final problem dressed as a systems caption.

Name it precisely. Configure early. Ask for time and distance. Refuse the slam-dunk.

No fake H130 stabilizer — real ICAO English for the exam and for the jet career ahead.

Trim what you can. Stabilize early. Land with forces you planned for — not forces that surprised you on short final.
