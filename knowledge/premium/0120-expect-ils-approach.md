# Expect ILS Approach

**Catalog ID:** 0120  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Expect ILS Approach — ATC's advisory that an Instrument Landing System approach has been assigned for your arrival.

The ILS is the most precise and most common instrument approach in controlled airspace.

Florianópolis, Curitiba, Congonhas, and São Paulo all have ILS approaches.

Knowing how to receive, read back, brief, and fly an ILS approach in ICAO English is a core professional skill.

This lesson connects Part 2 phraseology with Part 4 operational language — you must speak clearly about navigation, procedures, and system understanding.

## 📖 Meaning

Expect ILS Approach is an ATC advisory indicating that the Instrument Landing System approach has been assigned for the destination runway, allowing the crew to brief and configure for the precision approach before the formal clearance is issued.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Espera aproximação ILS.

Aviso do controle de tráfego aéreo indicando que a aproximação por sistema de pouso por instrumentos foi atribuída para a pista de destino, permitindo que a tripulação realize o briefing e configure para a aproximação de precisão antes da autorização formal.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the crew receives Expect ILS Approach, they should:

- identify and load the ILS procedure for the assigned runway
- set and confirm the localizer frequency and course
- review the glide slope intercept altitude and decision height
- brief the missed approach procedure fully
- confirm visibility and ceiling requirements against current conditions

Expect ILS Approach briefings commonly include:

- final approach course
- glideslope intercept and final approach fix altitude
- decision height or decision altitude
- missed approach instructions
- required visual reference at minimums

**Important:** An ILS approach requires precise navigation management. Intercept the localizer at the correct altitude. Never descend below glideslope. At minimums, land or execute the missed approach — there is no middle position.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Expect ILS Approach to:

- inform the crew early enough to brief a precision approach
- coordinate sequencing onto the localizer
- assign a precision approach in IMC or low visibility conditions
- establish the type of approach before holding or vectoring begins
- ensure crew readiness when workload is high

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Florianópolis in your H130 with the destination reporting three hundred feet overcast.

You are fifty miles out.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, expect ILS approach runway one four, QNH one zero one three.

You respond:

Expect ILS approach runway one four, QNH one zero one three, ANAC123.

Your co-pilot loads the ILS 14 approach.

You brief together: localizer course, glide slope intercept, decision altitude, missed approach.

Fifteen minutes later, Approach clears you for the ILS runway one four.

You are briefed. You are configured. You are ready.

The approach is stable from the first intercept to touchdown.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

The ILS is a beam of precision.

Two signals: one lateral, one vertical.

Keep the localizer centered. Keep the glideslope centered. Land.

When ATC says "expect ILS approach," the geometry of your arrival is now defined.

Your job is to brief it completely before you reach the intercept.

Localizer frequency. Course. Glideslope altitude. Decision height. Missed approach.

In the H130, the ILS is flown by reference to the CDI and glide slope indicator. There is no FMS autoflight — the discipline is your scan and your hand.

Brazilian pilots sometimes skip the full missed approach brief.

That is the part you hope to never use — and the part you must know perfectly.

Brief the missed approach every time, without exception.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Describe how you would prepare after receiving Expect ILS Approach from ATC.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

After reading back the advisory, I would brief the ILS approach for the assigned runway with my crew. We would confirm the localizer frequency, the final approach course, the glide slope intercept altitude, the decision height, and the full missed approach procedure including climb-out heading, altitude, and holding fix. We would cross-check the navaid identification and ensure the aircraft was configured and ready when the clearance was issued.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating Expect ILS Approach as the approach clearance.  
  ✔ It is advisory. Wait for the formal ILS clearance before intercepting.

- ❌ Setting the localizer frequency without confirming the course and ident.  
  ✔ Always confirm localizer identification before commencing the approach.

- ❌ Not briefing the missed approach.  
  ✔ Brief the missed approach every time, even if conditions look good.

- ❌ Descending below the glideslope to intercept from above.  
  ✔ Always intercept the glideslope from below. Descend to intercept altitude first.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Expect ILS Approach

**Pronunciation:** eks-PEKT eye-ELL-ESS uh-PROHCH

**Word Stress**

- Expect → eks-PEKT
- ILS → each letter: I (eye), L (ell), S (ess)
- Approach → uh-PROHCH

Practice:

Expect... I... L... S... Approach...

Together:

Expect ILS approach runway one four, ANAC123.

Say each letter of ILS clearly and separately — it is a three-letter abbreviation, not a word.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**GLIDE** — **G**lide slope from below, **L**ocalizer ident confirmed, **I**ntercept at correct altitude, **D**ecision height reviewed, **E**xecute missed approach if no visual.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Florianópolis Approach transmits: "ANAC123, expect ILS approach runway one four, QNH one zero one three, expect approach in twelve minutes."

**Your first task**

Read back the advisory correctly.

**Your second task**

List the five elements you must brief before receiving the ILS clearance.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would respond: Expect ILS approach runway one four, QNH one zero one three, ANAC123.
