# Flaps-Up Landing

**Catalog ID:** 0266  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Flaps-Up Landing — a landing performed with flaps retracted or unavailable, requiring higher approach speed, longer landing distance, and clear ATC communication of the abnormal configuration and any assistance needed.

Flaps-up landing is a classic fixed-wing ICAO exam topic. Without flaps, the aircraft approaches faster, floats longer, and needs more runway. The radio call must state the configuration limitation and request priority or the longest suitable runway when required.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, the H130 does not use wing flaps like a transport jet. Treat this lesson as exam and multi-type English: you must explain the concept, report the abnormal landing, and request what you need. Where helpful, compare the idea of a higher-energy approach to managing a fast, shallow helicopter approach — without inventing false H130 flap systems.

Understanding flaps-up landing phraseology builds Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency for configuration emergencies.

## 📖 Meaning

Flaps-Up Landing is an approach and landing conducted with flaps retracted or inoperative, resulting in higher approach and touchdown speeds and increased landing distance, and requiring abnormal procedures plus ATC notification when runway length, priority, or emergency services may be needed.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pouso sem flaps / Flaps-Up Landing.

Aproximação e pouso realizados com flaps recolhidos ou inoperantes, resultando em velocidades de aproximação e toque mais altas e maior distância de pouso, e exigindo procedimentos anormais e notificação ao ATC quando o comprimento de pista, prioridade ou serviços de emergência possam ser necessários.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When planning a flaps-up landing, the crew should:

- apply the AFM abnormal checklist for flap failure or flaps-up landing
- calculate landing distance with the higher approach speed and no flap benefit
- brief a stable, faster approach and a firm touchdown technique per procedure
- advise ATC of flaps-up landing and request the longest suitable runway if needed
- request priority or emergency services when landing distance or go-around margins are tight
- for H130 training context: know the English — do not invent flap settings the helicopter does not have

Flaps-up landing scenarios commonly include:

- total flap failure discovered on approach
- asymmetric flap locked up, requiring flaps-up procedure
- overweight or contaminated runway compounding distance required
- ATC offering a short runway that is no longer suitable
- exam scenario: report configuration and request assistance clearly

**Important:** Do not accept a short runway after declaring flaps-up landing without recalculating. Tell ATC you require the longest available runway and state if you need priority. Fly the published abnormal speeds — do not invent a "normal" approach profile.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use flaps-up landing reports to:

- assign the longest suitable runway when available
- provide priority sequencing for a higher-energy approach
- alert airport rescue and fire fighting if requested
- avoid late runway changes that invalidate landing-distance planning
- coordinate go-around airspace if the approach becomes unstable

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on a fixed-wing simulator approach to Guarulhos for ICAO training. Flaps will not extend. You commit to a flaps-up landing.

You transmit:

ANAC123, flap failure, flaps-up landing, request longest runway and priority.

Approach: ANAC123, roger flaps-up landing, expect runway two seven Right, priority approved, say souls and fuel when able.

You continue:

ANAC123, runway two seven Right, priority, souls four, fuel one hour twenty minutes.

You fly the higher speed. You land long enough. You do not surprise ATC with a silent configuration emergency.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger flaps-up landing, expect longest runway available.
2. Approach: ANAC123, priority handling approved, runway two seven Right.
3. Tower: ANAC123, wind two seven zero at one two, runway two seven Right, cleared to land.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.
5. Tower: ANAC123, rescue services standing by, report runway vacated.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Flaps-up landing is about energy and distance — not about pride in a "normal" looking approach.

Without flaps you are faster and longer. Brief it. Calculate it. Tell ATC early enough that they can give you the right runway.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "vou pousar sem flap" on frequency. Prefer flaps-up landing or flap failure, flaps-up landing — clear ICAO English.

In the H130 you will not select flap degrees. You will still meet this topic in exams and in airline-type careers. Learn the words. Learn the request: longest runway, priority if needed, souls and fuel when asked.

Do not change runways late after you have planned the landing distance. Configuration emergencies punish last-minute improvisation.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Flaps fail to extend on final. You will land flaps up. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would advise ATC of the flap failure and that I am performing a flaps-up landing. I would request the longest suitable runway and priority if landing distance or traffic requires it. I would fly the AFM abnormal approach speed, avoid late runway changes, and provide souls on board and fuel remaining if requested.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "problem with flaps" without stating flaps-up landing.  
  ✔ State flap failure, flaps-up landing, and the runway or priority request.

- ❌ Accepting a short runway after declaring the abnormal landing.  
  ✔ Request the longest available runway and recalculate if the offer changes.

- ❌ Delaying the call until short final.  
  ✔ Report early so ATC can sequence and prepare services.

- ❌ Using Portuguese slang on an ICAO English frequency.  
  ✔ Use flaps-up landing / flap failure in standard English.

**Your first task**

What do you say to declare the configuration and request?

**Your second task**

Tower offers the short runway "as number one." What do you say?

First task: ANAC123, flap failure, flaps-up landing, request longest runway and priority. Second task: ANAC123, unable short runway, flaps-up landing, require longest runway available. I would not accept a runway that invalidates the landing-distance plan after a flaps-up commitment.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Flaps-up landing is a configuration emergency told in plain English.

Faster. Longer. Briefed. Reported.

Ask for the runway that matches the performance. Ask for priority when traffic or distance is tight.

In the H130 academy you may never move a flap lever — but you will speak this concept on the ICAO exam.

Name the failure. Name the landing. Request what you need. Land with a plan — not with hope.
