# Weather Deterioration

**Catalog ID:** 0082  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers weather deterioration — when conditions worsen in flight.

Weather Deterioration means visibility, ceiling, wind, or precipitation changes beyond what was forecast or briefed.

In the H130, weather deterioration along the Brazilian coast can develop quickly — fog, rain showers, and low ceilings are common.

You must recognize the change early, decide divert or return, and communicate clearly with ATC.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 operational communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Weather Deterioration is a progressive worsening of meteorological conditions that may require diversion, return, or a change of flight plan.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Deterioração do tempo.

Piora progressiva das condições meteorológicas que pode exigir desvio, retorno ou mudança de plano de voo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Weather Deterioration occurs, the pilot should:

- assess whether VFR or IFR minima can still be maintained
- decide early to divert, return, or hold
- inform ATC of weather deterioration and intentions
- request vectors, diversion, or altitude change as needed
- avoid pressing into worsening conditions

Weather Deterioration reporting commonly includes:

- weather deteriorating or conditions deteriorating
- request diversion to alternate
- unable to continue VFR, request IFR clearance
- request vectors around weather

**Important:** Early diversion is professional. Late diversion is dangerous. Communicate intentions before options disappear.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Weather Deterioration reports to:

- provide diversion routing and alternate airports
- issue weather updates and pilot reports
- coordinate IFR clearances if VFR becomes impossible
- sequence traffic around weather cells
- assist with priority handling when conditions become critical

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising VFR along the coast from Navegantes to Florianópolis in your H130.

Visibility drops rapidly in rain showers and the ceiling lowers.

You decide early and transmit:

Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, weather deteriorating, unable to continue VFR to destination, request diversion to Navegantes.

Approach provides vectors and you land safely at Navegantes before conditions worsen further.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger weather deteriorating, say intentions.
2. Approach: ANAC123, cleared diversion Navegantes, turn left heading zero nine zero.
3. Approach: ANAC123, weather update, visibility reducing to two kilometers south.
4. Tower: ANAC123, runway two nine cleared to land, wind two seven zero at one five knots.
5. Approach: ANAC123, advise if able to continue VFR.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, weather deteriorating, request diversion to Navegantes.
2. ANAC123, weather deteriorating, unable to continue VFR, request IFR clearance.
3. Pan Pan Pan Pan, ANAC123, weather deteriorating, request vectors to nearest suitable airport.
4. ANAC123, conditions deteriorating, returning to departure aerodrome.
5. Approach, ANAC123, weather deteriorating ahead, request vectors around the shower.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Weather deterioration is a decision problem — not a bravery test.

The sequence:

Recognize. Decide. Communicate. Divert.

Do not wait until you are inside the weather to call ATC.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "o tempo piorou" on frequency.

Use weather deteriorating or conditions deteriorating — ICAO standard terms.

In the H130 along the coast, showers and fog can close options in minutes.

Have an alternate before you need it.

Train yourself to divert early and speak clearly.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if weather deteriorated below VFR minima during cruise?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would assess whether I could maintain VFR or needed an IFR clearance or diversion. I would inform ATC that weather is deteriorating, state that I am unable to continue VFR if applicable, and request diversion to the nearest suitable airport or an IFR clearance. I would state my intentions clearly and early.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing into worsening weather hoping it improves.  
  ✔ Divert early — communicate intentions before options disappear.

- ❌ Saying "o tempo fechou" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use weather deteriorating or conditions deteriorating.

- ❌ Not stating intentions after reporting weather.  
  ✔ Always state divert, return, or request IFR.

- ❌ Waiting until inside IMC without a clearance.  
  ✔ Request IFR or divert before entering uncontrolled IMC.

- Weather → WETH-er

Practice:

Weather... deteriorating...

Together:

Weather deteriorating, request diversion to Navegantes, ANAC123.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**EARLY** — **E**valuate minima, **A**nnounce weather deteriorating, **R**equest diversion or IFR, **L**eave options open, **Y**ou divert before trapped.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Visibility drops rapidly on a VFR coastal flight toward Florianópolis in your H130.

**Your first task**

Transmit your weather and intention call.

**Your second task**
