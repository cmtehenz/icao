# Wheels-Up Landing

**Catalog ID:** 0264  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Wheels-Up Landing — an intentional or unintentional landing with the landing gear retracted, typically after gear extension failure, requiring emergency procedures, RFF standby, and precise distress or urgency communication with ATC.

Wheels-up landing is critical ICAO emergency English. The crew must declare the intention early, request firefighters, choose a suitable runway, and brief evacuation. Controllers clear traffic, alert RFF, and protect the landing.

For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, many H130s use skids — a skid landing is normal, not a wheels-up emergency. Still teach wheels-up phraseology for the ICAO exam and for wheeled types. Relate carefully: do not call a normal skid landing "wheels-up." Use the term for retracted wheeled gear landing on the fuselage or gear doors.

Understanding wheels-up landing communication is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 critical performance.

## 📖 Meaning

Wheels-Up Landing is a landing performed with the landing gear retracted — either intentionally after gear extension failure or unintentionally due to configuration error — resulting in the aircraft contacting the runway on the fuselage, nacelles, or gear doors, with elevated fire and structural risk.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pouso com trem recolhido / Wheels-up landing / Pouso sobre a barriga (relacionado).

Pouso realizado com o trem de pouso recolhido — intencionalmente após falha de extensão ou involuntariamente por erro de configuração — resultando no contato da aeronave com a pista sobre a fuselagem, naceles ou portas do trem, com risco elevado de fogo e dano estrutural.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a wheels-up landing is required or anticipated, the crew should:

- complete gear extension and alternate extension attempts per AFM
- declare the intention to land wheels-up when gear cannot be extended
- request priority landing and RFF on the runway
- brief cabin for brace and evacuation
- land with checklist configuration: fuel, electrics, and shutdown as published
- after stop: secure, assess fire, evacuate if required

Wheels-up scenarios commonly include:

- gear stuck up after failed alternate extension
- intentional wheels-up after unsafe gear that will not lock
- unintentional wheels-up from gear-up landing configuration error
- subsequent runway closure and emergency response
- H130 note: normal skid landing is not wheels-up — keep terminology accurate

**Important:** Intentional wheels-up after procedure is an emergency landing plan. Unintentional wheels-up from forgotten gear is a preventable accident. Both need the same RFF response — but only one is professional decision-making.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to wheels-up landing declarations to:

- clear the runway and hold all other traffic
- position RFF for immediate response
- provide the most suitable long runway if available
- obtain souls, fuel, and evacuation plans
- close the runway after the landing for recovery

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Wheeled-aircraft emergency: alternate extension failed. Gear remains up. You will land wheels-up at Florianópolis.

You transmit:

ANAC123, Mayday, landing gear stuck up, requesting wheels-up landing runway one four, firefighters.

Tower: ANAC123, roger wheels-up, runway one four, wind calm, firefighters in position, cleared to land.

You fly a stable approach. You touch, slide, stop. You secure and evacuate as required. The runway closes. An H130 inbound goes around — correct.

Do not call a normal H130 skid touchdown a wheels-up landing. Different machine. Different meaning.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger wheels-up landing, firefighters rolling, cleared to land.
2. Approach: ANAC123, confirm gear up landing intentional, say souls and fuel.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway cleared, all traffic held, RFF in position.
4. Tower: ANAC123, after landing vacate if able, or remain and evacuate.
5. Approach: ANAC456, go around, runway closed, wheels-up landing in progress.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, Mayday, requesting wheels-up landing, firefighters.
2. ANAC123, affirmative intentional wheels-up, souls four, fuel thirty minutes.
3. ANAC123, runway cleared copied, cleared to land wheels-up.
4. ANAC123, will evacuate after stop if required.
5. ANAC456, going around, runway closed.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Wheels-up is a planned abnormal landing when the gear will not come down — or a catastrophe when someone forgot the gear.

For the exam and for real wheeled flying: attempt extension, then decide. Tell ATC early. Firefighters before you flare, not after you slide.

In the H130 with skids, your normal landing gear is the skids. That is not wheels-up. Keep the English for retracted wheeled gear. Mixing the terms confuses examiners and controllers.

Critical radio: Mayday or urgency as required, wheels-up intention, runway, firefighters, souls, fuel. Then fly the most stable approach of your day.

Brace. Touch. Secure. Evacuate if fire or AFM requires it.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Alternate gear extension has failed and you must land with the gear up. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare the emergency, advise ATC that I will make a wheels-up landing, and request firefighters and a suitable runway. I would brief for brace and possible evacuation, configure per the AFM, land as smoothly as possible, then secure the aircraft and evacuate if there is fire or as required by procedure.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Landing wheels-up without declaring and without RFF.  
  ✔ Declare early; request firefighters before the landing.

- ❌ Calling a normal H130 skid landing "wheels-up."  
  ✔ Wheels-up means retracted wheeled gear; skids are normal for many H130s.

- ❌ Forgetting cabin brace and evacuation briefing.  
  ✔ Brief before landing; evacuate after stop if required.

- ❌ Continuing a unstable approach because "it is already an emergency."  
  ✔ Emergencies need the most stable approach you can fly.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Gear stuck up. Alternate extension failed. You will land wheels-up. RFF must be in position.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

After landing you stop on the runway. No fire. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, Mayday, landing gear stuck up, requesting wheels-up landing, firefighters. Second task: ANAC123, wheels-up landing complete, no fire, remaining on runway — evacuating or standing by as required by checklist. I would evacuate if procedure or hazard required it.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Wheels-up landing is an emergency plan when the gear will not extend — not a synonym for every non-wheel landing.

Declare. Request firefighters. Fly stable. Secure. Evacuate if needed.

For ICAO exams, speak it cleanly. For the H130 on skids, never misuse the term for a normal skid touchdown.

Critical English. Critical timing. Critical honesty with ATC.

That is how wheels-up landings are survived and communicated.
