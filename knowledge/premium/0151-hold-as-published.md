# Hold as Published

**Catalog ID:** 0151  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Hold as Published — the ATC instruction to enter and maintain a holding pattern using the parameters that are already printed on the published chart for that fix or approach procedure.

Hold as Published is the shorthand clearance. ATC does not re-specify the fix, the inbound course, the turn direction, or the leg length. They expect you to know the published procedure and fly it correctly.

At Brazilian instrument airports like Florianópolis, Curitiba, and Navegantes, holds are often published on approach plates and terminal area charts. When ATC says "hold as published," you must have the correct chart, know the published parameters, and fly them precisely.

This lesson clarifies the critical distinction between Hold at Fix and Hold as Published — and prepares you to respond professionally at ICAO Level 4.

## 📖 Meaning

Hold as Published is an ATC instruction directing the pilot to enter and maintain the holding pattern as depicted on the published instrument chart for the specified fix, without ATC re-stating the individual parameters of the holding pattern.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Espere conforme publicado.

Instrução do controle de tráfego aéreo determinando que a aeronave entre e mantenha a espera conforme representada na carta de instrumentos publicada para o fix especificado, sem que o controle repita os parâmetros individuais da espera.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the crew receives Hold as Published, they should:

- immediately reference the correct published chart for the fix or approach
- confirm the inbound course, turn direction, and leg length shown on the chart
- identify the holding fix on the navigation display and verify it is loaded
- select the holding entry type based on the aircraft heading relative to the inbound course
- maintain the assigned or published altitude and the applicable holding speed
- advise ATC of fuel endurance if an extended hold is expected

Hold as Published operations require:

- the relevant chart available and reviewed before the hold clearance
- knowledge of which chart depicts the published hold for that fix
- familiarity with the published parameters before entering the hold
- ability to recognize the hold entry sector from the current heading

**Important:** Hold as Published is a concise instruction that depends on crew preparation. If you do not have the published chart or do not know the published parameters, you must request clarification from ATC before entering the hold. Never guess the published parameters.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Hold as Published to:

- reduce radio transmission length when a standard published hold exists
- maintain efficient communication in busy terminal sectors
- sequence arrivals using the established published holding stack
- assign a hold at an approach fix that has a defined published procedure
- instruct a hold when all pilots on frequency are expected to have the same chart

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Curitiba in your H130. Weather at the destination has deteriorated and arrivals are being held.

You have the Curitiba ILS runway one five approach plate open. The published hold at the IAF shows: inbound course one three six, right turns, one minute legs, at five thousand feet.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, hold as published at ARUCU, expect approach in twenty minutes.

You have the chart. You know the parameters.

You respond:

Hold as published at ARUCU, expect approach twenty minutes, ANAC123.

You cross ARUCU, turn right, fly the outbound leg for one minute, turn right again, track inbound on course one three six, and cross ARUCU.

Two orbits. Precise. Professional.

Twenty minutes later — approach clearance issued.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Hold as Published is the confidence test.

When ATC says these three words, they assume you know the chart.

The published inbound course. The turn direction. The leg length. The altitude.

If you do not know the chart, you are not ready.

This is why chart preparation before every flight matters.

Before you fly into Curitiba or Florianópolis, review the approach plate. Note the published holds. Know where they are and what their parameters are.

When Hold as Published comes over the frequency — you should not need to ask.

In the H130, flying without an FMS means you must fly the hold manually: heading reference for the outbound, timer for the leg, VOR tracking for the inbound.

Know the entry type: direct, teardrop, or parallel.

If your heading is within thirty degrees of the inbound course when you cross the fix — direct entry. If it is between thirty and a hundred and ten degrees on the non-holding side — teardrop. Otherwise — parallel.

Hold as Published requires that knowledge to already be in your head.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between Hold at Fix and Hold as Published, and when would you request clarification on a Hold as Published clearance?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Hold at Fix is a complete explicit clearance in which ATC specifies every parameter: the fix, the inbound course, the direction of turns, and the leg length. The crew flies exactly what ATC stated. Hold as Published is an abbreviated clearance in which ATC expects the crew to use the parameters as depicted on the published instrument chart for the designated fix. The crew must have the correct chart and know the published parameters before entering the hold. If I did not have the relevant chart or was uncertain of the published parameters, I would request clarification: ANAC123, request published hold parameters for ARUCU. Guessing is never acceptable.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Flying the wrong hold because the crew did not have the correct chart.  
  ✔ Review approach plates before arrival. Know the published hold parameters.

- ❌ Requesting all hold parameters from ATC when Hold as Published is issued.  
  ✔ Use the published chart. Request clarification only if the chart is not available.

- ❌ Not monitoring fuel endurance during an extended hold.  
  ✔ Calculate endurance immediately and advise ATC: ANAC123, fuel endurance thirty minutes.

- ❌ Confusing Hold as Published with Hold at Fix by checking the chart but flying ATC-specified parameters.  
  ✔ Hold as Published means fly the chart. Hold at Fix means fly what ATC said.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Hold as Published at ARUCU

**Pronunciation:** HOHLD az PUB-lisht at ah-ROO-koo

**Word Stress**

- Hold → HOHLD
- As → az (short, unstressed)
- Published → PUB-lisht (stress on first syllable)
- ARUCU → ah-ROO-koo (Brazilian fix name, stress on second syllable)

Practice:

Hold... as... published... at ARUCU.

Together:

Hold as published at ARUCU, expect approach twenty minutes, ANAC123.

Speak the fix name clearly — fix designations must be unambiguous on the frequency.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**CHART** — **C**heck the published chart immediately, **H**old parameters are on the plate, **A**lways brief before reaching the fix, **R**ight turns unless left is specified, **T**imer set for the outbound leg.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are holding at IDAMO near Florianópolis. Approach transmits: "ANAC123, hold as published at IDAMO, maintain five thousand, expect further clearance time zero seven zero zero."

**Your first task**

Read back the clearance correctly.

**Your second task**

Your co-pilot does not have the Florianópolis approach plate open and asks: "What are the published hold parameters?" What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would respond: Hold as published at IDAMO, maintain five thousand, expect further clearance zero seven zero zero, ANAC123. If my co-pilot did not have the plate, I would immediately pull it up on the available chart system and brief the published parameters: inbound course, turn direction, and leg length. If neither pilot had access to the chart I would request clarification from ATC: ANAC123, request confirmation of published hold parameters at IDAMO. We would not enter the hold without confirmed parameters.
