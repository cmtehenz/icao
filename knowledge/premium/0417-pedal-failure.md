# Pedal Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0417  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Pedal Failure — loss of normal anti-torque pedal control, compromising yaw control and requiring emergency procedures, careful airspeed management, and clear distress communication with ATC.

Pedal failure threatens heading control, especially in the hover and at low speed. The emergency is obvious in the cockpit; it must be equally clear on frequency.

For H130 operations in Brazil — confined pads, rooftop helipads, and low-speed city approaches — you must declare the failure, avoid unnecessary hover, and state landing intentions in ICAO English.

Understanding how to report pedal failure in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Pedal Failure is a malfunction of the anti-torque pedal system that prevents normal yaw control through the tail rotor or equivalent anti-torque system, requiring immediate emergency procedures and an expedited landing, preferably with forward speed rather than a prolonged hover.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha dos pedais (antitorque).

Malfuncionamento do sistema de pedais antitorque que impede o controle normal de guinada pelo rotor de cauda ou sistema antitorque equivalente, exigindo procedimentos de emergência imediatos e pouso expedito, de preferência com velocidade à frente em vez de pairado prolongado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When pedal failure occurs, the crew should:

- follow AFM emergency procedures for stuck or failed pedals
- maintain safe airspeed where directional control is best
- avoid unnecessary hovering and steep approaches to confined areas
- declare mayday with pedal failure and intentions
- request a suitable landing area with space for a run-on landing if needed

Pedal failure reporting commonly includes:

- mayday, pedal failure / loss of yaw control
- requesting run-on landing
- unable hover
- souls and fuel when able

**Important:** Tell ATC you cannot hover if that is true. They may otherwise clear you to a tight pad that requires a hover landing.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to pedal failure reports to:

- offer runways or large open areas instead of confined pads
- clear the landing path for a possible run-on
- alert emergency services
- avoid instructing a hover that the aircraft cannot safely perform
- provide wind information critical for yaw control

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

On approach to a São Paulo hospital pad in your H130, left pedal authority is lost. You discontinue the pad approach and transmit:

Mayday mayday mayday, São Paulo Approach, pedal failure, loss of yaw control, unable hover, requesting run-on landing Congonhas, souls four, fuel one hour.

Approach coordinates a runway arrival with wind and emergency services. You fly a running landing and report on ground.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, mayday acknowledged, cleared to Congonhas, expect runway one seven left, wind one eight zero at one two.
2. Approach: ANAC123, unable hospital pad approved, proceed Congonhas for run-on.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land runway one seven left, emergency services standing by.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.
5. Tower: ANAC123, roger on ground, remain on runway if required, assistance inbound.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, mayday, pedal failure, unable hover, requesting Congonhas run-on.
2. ANAC123, Congonhas runway one seven left, wind copied.
3. ANAC123, cleared to land one seven left, run-on landing.
4. ANAC123, souls four, fuel one hour.
5. ANAC123, on ground, remaining on runway, request assistance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Pedal failure English must include the operational limitation: unable hover.

If you only say "pedal problem," ATC may still offer a rooftop. Say what you need: run-on landing, large area, wind preferred.

In the H130, yaw margin shrinks in the hover and in crosswinds. Brazilian hospital pads are often confined — reject them when anti-torque is gone.

Wind is not small talk in this emergency. Ask for it. Use it. Tell Tower you need a run-on if that is the plan.

Keep the mayday clean: failure, limitation, destination. Then fly the approach you can still control.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You lose pedal control on approach to a confined helipad. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare: Mayday mayday mayday, pedal failure, loss of yaw control, unable hover, requesting run-on landing at the nearest suitable runway, with position. I would discontinue the confined pad approach and give souls and fuel when able.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing to a rooftop pad after losing yaw control.  
  ✔ Divert to a runway or large area; state unable hover.

- ❌ Saying only "problema no pedal" without the limitation.  
  ✔ Pedal failure, unable hover, request run-on landing.

- ❌ Ignoring wind in the request.  
  ✔ Ask for wind; it directly affects remaining yaw control.

- ❌ Using pan-pan for complete loss of yaw control near the ground.  
  ✔ Mayday when control for landing is threatened.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Pedal failure on short final to a hospital pad. Unable to hover safely.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit as you go around from the pad?

**Your second task**

Approach offers another rooftop. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday mayday mayday, ANAC123, pedal failure, unable hover, going around, requesting run-on landing Congonhas. Second task: ANAC123, negative rooftop, unable hover, require runway or large open area for run-on landing. Do not accept a confined pad after yaw control is lost.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Pedal failure is a yaw emergency. Your words must carry the limitation.

Mayday. Pedal failure. Unable hover. Run-on landing.

In the H130 over Brazilian cities, rooftops are optional only when anti-torque works. When it does not, take the runway.

Say what you cannot do. Ask for what you need. Land with speed you can control.
