# Aileron Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0270  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Aileron Failure — loss or restriction of aileron control that reduces or eliminates normal roll authority, requiring abnormal procedures, possible use of spoilers or differential power where available, and urgent ATC communication for priority landing.

Ailerons control roll. When they fail or jam, the aircraft may roll slowly, roll only one way, or require large control inputs and alternate roll methods. This is a handling emergency as much as a systems caption.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, roll is primarily cyclic lateral input, not ailerons. Use that analogy to understand "reduced roll control" — do not invent ailerons on the H130. ICAO exams still test aileron failure English for fixed-wing scenarios.

Part 2 and Part 3 require calm, specific language: aileron failure, limited roll control, request priority landing.

## 📖 Meaning

Aileron Failure is a malfunction in which one or both ailerons jam, disconnect, or otherwise fail to provide normal roll control, reducing the pilot's ability to bank the aircraft normally, and requiring abnormal procedures and ATC notification for priority handling and landing assistance as needed.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha de aileron / Aileron Failure.

Malfuncionamento em que um ou ambos os ailerons travam, desconectam ou de outra forma deixam de fornecer controle normal de rolagem, reduzindo a capacidade do piloto de inclinar a aeronave normalmente, e exigindo procedimentos anormais e notificação ao ATC para prioridade e assistência ao pouso conforme necessário.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ailerons fail, the crew should:

- maintain wings level with remaining roll authority and AFM techniques
- apply the aileron / roll control abnormal checklist
- avoid steep turns, aggressive maneuvering, and unnecessary configuration changes
- advise ATC of aileron failure and limited roll control
- request priority vectors for the nearest suitable airport and a long, straight-in approach
- for H130 analogy: think "degraded lateral cyclic authority" conceptually — not false H130 aileron hardware

Aileron failure scenarios commonly include:

- jammed aileron with restricted bank capability
- asymmetric aileron causing a continuous roll tendency
- need for spoiler-assisted roll on some types
- inability to accept tight circling or sidestep maneuvers
- exam call: priority landing, limited roll control

**Important:** Do not accept a circling approach or tight visual pattern if roll control is marginal. Request a long straight-in. Declare urgency or distress if control is seriously degraded.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use aileron failure reports to:

- provide priority handling and simplified vectors
- avoid assigning steep turns, holds with tight radius, or complex sidesteps
- clear a long final and the most suitable runway
- alert emergency services when requested
- increase separation for an aircraft with limited maneuverability

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are in a fixed-wing simulator east of São Paulo. Roll response becomes sluggish; one aileron indication disagrees. You keep wings mostly level with careful inputs.

You transmit:

ANAC123, aileron failure, limited roll control, request priority vectors for straight-in landing Guarulhos.

Approach: ANAC123, roger aileron failure, turn left heading two five zero, shallow turn, expect runway two seven Left straight-in, priority approved.

You read back and add:

ANAC123, left heading two five zero, shallow turns only, runway two seven Left straight-in, priority.

You refuse a tight visual circle. You fly the gentle path.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Aileron failure is roll authority. Without it, every turn is a risk calculation.

Wings level first. Checklist second. Radio third — but do not delay the urgency call if control is bad.

Ask for shallow turns and a straight-in. Circling with failed ailerons is how a systems problem becomes a loss-of-control story.

In the H130, lateral cyclic is your roll. If you ever face degraded cyclic authority, the same radio logic applies: limited roll control, priority, straight-in. Do not invent aileron captions on the helicopter — invent clear English for degraded control.

Say aileron failure, not "steering problem." Examiners and controllers need the axis.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Ailerons jam and you have only limited roll control. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain the best wings-level control available, apply the AFM procedure, and advise ATC of aileron failure with limited roll control. I would request priority vectors for a long straight-in approach to the nearest suitable airport, ask for shallow turns only, and provide souls and fuel if requested. I would declare urgency or distress if control became seriously degraded.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "problem with controls" without naming ailerons or roll.  
  ✔ Use aileron failure, limited roll control.

- ❌ Accepting a circling approach with marginal roll authority.  
  ✔ Request straight-in only; refuse tight patterns.

- ❌ Making steep banks to "test" remaining aileron.  
  ✔ Fly minimum bank; protect control margins.

- ❌ Delaying the priority call until the aircraft is hard to control.  
  ✔ Report early and request simplified vectors.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Roll control is limited after an aileron jam. Approach offers a visual circle to the reciprocal runway.

**Your first task**

What do you say about the failure and the approach you need?

**Your second task**

What do you say about the circling offer?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, aileron failure, limited roll control, request priority straight-in landing. Second task: ANAC123, unable to circle, require straight-in only, shallow turns. I would not accept a circling maneuver that demands roll authority I no longer have.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Aileron failure is a roll emergency spoken in plain words.

Limited roll control. Priority. Straight-in. Shallow turns.

In helicopter terms, protect lateral control the way you protect cyclic authority — gently, early, and with a simple path to the ground.

Name the axis. Ask for the simple approach. Land while the wings still obey.
