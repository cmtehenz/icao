# Confirm Souls on Board

**Catalog ID:** 0312  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Confirm Souls on Board — the ATC request for the total number of people on the aircraft, used especially in emergencies, diversions, and abnormal situations. Core Part 2 and Part 3 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: give the total immediately and accurately.

Confirm souls on board is High priority because rescue and airport emergency services size their response to that number.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for souls-on-board reports under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including combined fuel and persons reports.

Understanding how to confirm souls on board in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Confirm Souls on Board is an ATC request for the pilot to state the total number of persons on the aircraft — crew and passengers combined — so that ATC and emergency services can plan assistance, rescue, and aerodrome emergency response.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Confirmar almas a bordo / Confirm Souls on Board (pessoas a bordo).

Solicitação do controle para que o piloto informe o número total de pessoas na aeronave — tripulação e passageiros juntos — para que o ATC e os serviços de emergência planejem assistência, resgate e resposta de emergência no aeródromo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC asks you to confirm souls on board, the crew should:

- give the total number of persons: crew plus passengers
- speak the number clearly; use "souls on board" or "persons on board" as trained
- be ready to add fuel remaining when ATC asks both together
- update the number if it changes before departure or after a passenger offload
- for H130: totals are often small — still report exactly, including infants if carried
- do not separate crew and passengers unless ATC specifically asks

Souls-on-board exchanges commonly include:

- confirm souls on board / say souls on board
- souls on board [number] / persons on board [number]
- fuel remaining and souls on board
- confirm number of persons
- request assistance, souls on board...

**Important:** Souls on board means everyone alive on the aircraft. Count before you fly; do not estimate under stress.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers request souls on board to:

- brief airport fire and rescue on the correct headcount
- support search-and-rescue planning after an emergency declaration
- coordinate medical and ground assistance on arrival
- complete abnormal and emergency coordination checklists
- pass accurate information to the destination or diversion aerodrome

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 with a technical problem diverting to Congonhas. Approach prepares emergency services.

Approach: ANAC123, confirm souls on board and fuel remaining.

You reply:

ANAC123, souls on board four, fuel remaining zero plus three five.

Tower and rescue now know how many people to expect and how urgent the fuel picture is. That single line shapes the ground response.

In Part 3 scenarios, this report is as important as the nature of the emergency itself.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, confirm souls on board.
2. Tower: ANAC123, say souls on board.
3. Approach: ANAC123, confirm persons on board and fuel remaining.
4. Control: ANAC123, say number of persons on board.
5. Tower: ANAC123, confirm souls on board for rescue services.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Souls on board is a rescue number, not a passenger-marketing number.

Total people. One figure. Clear delivery. In the H130 the cabin is small — you should know the count before the first call.

When ATC asks during an abnormal or emergency, answer in the same breath as fuel if both are requested. Part 3 examiners listen for that automatic completeness.

Do not list names. Do not explain seats. Give the total that emergency services need.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

During a diversion with a technical problem, ATC asks for souls on board and fuel remaining. What do you report?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report the total number of persons on board — crew and passengers combined — and the usable fuel remaining in hours and minutes. I would keep the transmission short and clear so ATC can pass the information to emergency services. If only souls on board is requested, I would give that total alone and be ready to add fuel remaining immediately if asked.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting passengers only and forgetting the crew.  
  ✔ Souls on board is everyone on the aircraft.

- ❌ Giving a long cabin explanation instead of a number.  
  ✔ State the total clearly in one phrase.

- ❌ Guessing the count under stress.  
  ✔ Know the number before departure; confirm after boarding.

- ❌ Omitting fuel when ATC asked for both.  
  ✔ Answer both items in the same reply.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You divert with a technical issue. Approach asks for souls on board, then for fuel as well.

**Your first task**

You have one pilot and three passengers. What do you say?

**Your second task**

Approach then asks for fuel remaining at thirty-five minutes. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, souls on board four. Second task: ANAC123, fuel remaining zero plus three five — or combine if asked together: ANAC123, souls on board four, fuel remaining zero plus three five. The total must include crew.
