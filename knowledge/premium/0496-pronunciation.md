# Pronunciation

**Catalog ID:** 0496  
**Category:** ICAO Speaking  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Pronunciation — the ICAO language skill concerned with producing sounds, stress, and rhythm clearly enough that a speaker from a different language background can understand you in radiotelephony and operational speaking.

Pronunciation at Level 4 does not require a native accent. It requires intelligibility. If ATC or the examiner must guess your digits, runway, or condition, pronunciation has failed.

For H130 ICAO Delta candidates, pronunciation errors on numbers, callsigns, and key technical words are high-risk. Part 1 scoring listens for clarity first.

Understanding pronunciation as an ICAO skill is essential for Part 1 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Pronunciation is the ICAO language proficiency skill that evaluates whether a speaker’s sounds, word stress, sentence rhythm, and intelligibility are clear enough for effective aeronautical communication, even when a first-language accent remains noticeable.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pronúncia / Pronunciation.

Habilidade de proficiência linguística da OACI que avalia se os sons, a ênfase das palavras, o ritmo da frase e a inteligibilidade do falante são claros o bastante para comunicação aeronáutica eficaz, mesmo quando o sotaque da língua materna permanece perceptível.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When working on pronunciation, the pilot should:

- prioritize intelligibility over sounding “native”
- stress the correct syllable in key aviation words
- separate digits clearly: flight levels, headings, runways
- for H130: practice words like hover, winch, hydraulic, confined
- slow slightly under stress — speed destroys clarity
- listen to standard samples and shadow them aloud

Pronunciation focus areas commonly include:

- digits and callsign groups
- runway and heading numbers
- word stress (descent, alternate, priority)
- consonant endings Brazilians often drop
- rhythm of standard phraseology

**Important:** Accent is allowed. Unintelligible speech is not. Level 4 requires pronunciation that rarely interferes with understanding.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers need intelligible pronunciation to:

- copy altitudes, headings, and runways without doubt
- separate similar-sounding digits under frequency noise
- understand plain-language abnormal reports the first time
- avoid repeated say-again cycles that congest the frequency
- maintain safety with international and Brazilian crews alike

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

In a Part 1 mock test you describe a go-around due to windshear. You know the content, but you swallow the endings of “windshear,” rush the digits in “runway one seven Left,” and flatten stress on “priority.”

The examiner asks you to repeat. That is a pronunciation issue, not a knowledge issue.

You reset: slower rate, clear digits, stressed syllables — wind-SHEAR, runway WUN SEV-en LEFT, pri-OR-ity. Clarity returns. ATC would need the same reset on a live frequency.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, say again altitude, transmission unclear.
2. Tower: ANAC123, confirm runway one seven Left.
3. Approach: ANAC123, climb flight level six zero, say again if required.
4. Control: ANAC123, speak slower, you are barely readable.
5. Tower: ANAC123, confirm going around for windshear.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

2. ANAC123, runway one seven Left confirmed.
3. ANAC123, climbing flight level six zero.
4. ANAC123, wilco, speaking slower.
5. ANAC123, affirmative, going around, windshear.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Pronunciation is kindness to the listener.

You may understand yourself. ATC might not. Digits are where Brazilian speakers most often lose marks and lose clearances — five/nine, tree/three habits, swallowed endings.

Practice the ICAO digit set until it is automatic. Then practice operational words with correct stress: al-TIR-nate, de-SCENT, in-TEN-tions.

In the H130, abnormal words appear suddenly — autorotation, hydraulic, vibes. Pre-load their pronunciation in training so stress does not invent a new accent on frequency.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What does ICAO expect for pronunciation at Level 4?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

At Level 4, pronunciation must be intelligible to the aeronautical community even if a first-language accent is present. Stress, rhythm, and sounds may be influenced by the mother tongue, but they should rarely interfere with understanding. Clear digits and key operational words are especially important for safety.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Speaking fast to hide accent, which reduces intelligibility.  
  ✔ Slow slightly and articulate endings and digits.

- ❌ Dropping final consonants (flight leve / request).  
  ✔ Finish the word — level, request, windshear.

- ❌ Incorrect stress: DE-scent, AL-ternate.  
  ✔ Use de-SCENT, al-TIR-nate / AL-ter-nit as trained.

- ❌ Unclear number groups in runways and flight levels.  
  ✔ Separate digits: one seven / flight level six zero.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Going Around, Windshear, Runway One Seven Left

**Pronunciation:** GO-ing uh-ROUND, WIND-sheer, RUN-way WUN SEV-en LEFT

**Word Stress**

- Around → uh-ROUND
- Windshear → WIND-sheer
- Runway → RUN-way
- Seventeen → sev-en-TEEN (in runway grouping: one seven)

Practice:

Going around... windshear... runway one seven Left.

Together:

Going around due to windshear, runway one seven Left.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**CLEAR** — **C**onsonant endings finished, **L**ower the speed slightly, **E**mphasize correct stress, **A**rticulate every digit, **R**epeat when ATC says unclear.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Approach says your altitude readback was unclear and asks you to say again. You are at flight level six zero on vectors for runway one seven Left.

**Your first task**

What do you say, with deliberate clear pronunciation?

**Your second task**

Name two pronunciation habits you will use under stress.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, maintaining flight level six zero, vectors for runway one seven Left. Second task: I will slow my rate slightly and separate digits clearly while finishing consonant endings — intelligibility first, accent second.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Pronunciation is intelligibility, not imitation of a native speaker.

Finish the word. Separate the digits. Stress the right syllable.

On frequency, unclear speech becomes a safety event. In Part 1, it becomes a score event.

In the H130, pre-practice the hard words. When the abnormal starts, your mouth should already know the path.
