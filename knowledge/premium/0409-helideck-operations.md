# Helideck Operations

**Catalog ID:** 0409  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Helideck Operations — helicopter arrivals, departures, and ground handling on offshore platform helidecks, including status lights, deck crew coordination, and weather or motion limits.

Helideck ops are highly relevant H130 missions supporting Brazilian oil and gas: Campos Basin, Santos Basin approaches, and coastal staging out of Macaé, Rio, and Vitória corridors.

You must use precise English for deck status, landing clearance, go-around, and emergency handling across Parts 2, 3, and 4.

## 📖 Meaning

Helideck Operations are helicopter flight and deck procedures conducted on offshore installation helidecks, involving assessment of deck status, relative wind, obstacles, fire-fighting readiness, and continuous communication with the helideck crew and ATC for safe landing, on-deck time, and departure.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Operações em helideck (plataforma offshore).

Procedimentos de voo e de convés de helicóptero em helidecks de instalações offshore, envolvendo avaliação do status do convés, vento relativo, obstáculos, prontidão de combate a incêndio e comunicação contínua com a equipe do helideck e o ATC para pouso, tempo a bordo e decolagem seguros.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When conducting Helideck Operations, the pilot should:

- obtain helideck status (green/amber/red or equivalent) before final
- confirm relative wind and any crane or hotspot restrictions
- report short final, landing, and lifting as required by the operator and ATC
- go around if deck status changes to unsafe or limits are exceeded
- declare emergency immediately for fire, deck obstacle, or aircraft malfunction on deck

Helideck reporting commonly includes:

- request helideck landing, say status
- short final helideck, deck green
- on deck / rotors running
- lifting, request departure routing

**Important:** Red deck means wave-off. Do not negotiate a landing on a red status. Amber requires the briefed restrictions — if unclear, go around and clarify.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers support Helideck Operations by:

- providing offshore traffic information and position monitoring
- coordinating multiple helicopters into the same field
- assisting diversions when helidecks close for weather or motion
- handling emergencies between platform and shore
- maintaining SAR awareness for overwater legs

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to an offshore platform helideck in your H130, forty miles from the coast.

You transmit:

Macaé Control, ANAC123, two zero miles from platform, request helideck status and weather.

Control relays: deck green, wind three six zero at one eight, no reported traffic.

On short final the crane swings into the obstacle-free sector. Deck goes red.

You go around:

ANAC123, going around, helideck red, crane obstacle, remaining clear, request orbit.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, helideck green, wind three six zero at one eight, report on deck.
2. Control: ANAC123, helideck now amber, hot exhaust advisory, say intentions.
3. Control: ANAC123, roger going around, orbit east of platform, advise when ready.
4. Control: ANAC123, platform reports deck green again, cleared for approach.
5. Control: ANAC123, roger lifting, cleared coastal routing, report field in sight.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, helideck green, will report on deck.
2. ANAC123, amber acknowledged, continuing with hot exhaust caution — or going around if unstable.
3. ANAC123, going around, helideck red, orbiting east.
4. ANAC123, commencing approach, deck green.
5. ANAC123, lifting, coastal routing, will report field in sight.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Helideck operations are a contract between pilot and deck crew.

Green means proceed within limits. Amber means restricted. Red means go around. Learn the operator's light and radio protocol cold.

In the H130, power margin on a high deck with a gusty wind over deck is the daily exam. If the approach is unstable, wave off before the fence.

Brazilian basin radio can be congested with multiple helicopters. Keep calls short: status, position, on deck, lifting.

Part 4 loves: what do you do if deck goes red on short final? Answer: go around, state helideck red, orbit, wait for green.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Helideck status changes to red on short final. What do you say and do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would initiate an immediate go-around on the briefed path clear of the platform structure. I would transmit: ANAC123, going around, helideck red, remaining clear, request orbit. I would not attempt to land on a red deck. When the platform reports green and I am stable, I would request another approach.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing to land after a red deck call "because we were committed."  
  ✔ Go around. Red is mandatory wave-off.

- ❌ Failing to report lifting to coastal control.  
  ✔ Lifting calls maintain traffic and SAR picture.

- ❌ Accepting amber without understanding the restriction.  
  ✔ Clarify or go around. Ambiguity is not clearance.

- ❌ Long company chat on the ATC frequency.  
  ✔ Keep ATC concise; use company frequency for deck detail when available.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Going Around, Helideck Red

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, GO-ing uh-ROUND, HEL-ih-dek RED

**Word Stress**

- Helideck → HEL-ih-dek (stress on first)
- Red → RED
- Orbit → OR-bit

Practice:

Helideck red... going around... request orbit.

Together:

ANAC123, going around, helideck red, crane obstacle, request orbit east.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**DECK** — **D**emand status before final, **E**xecute wave-off on red, **C**all orbit and wait, **K**eep lifting reports to ATC.

Inbound H130 to platform helideck. Status green, then red for crane movement on short final.

Go-around call.

Platform reports green. Fuel adequate. What do you say?

First task: ANAC123, going around, helideck red, crane obstacle, request orbit east. Second task: ANAC123, request another helideck approach, deck green reported, will report on deck.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Helideck operations reward discipline and short radio calls.

Status before final. Wave-off on red. Orbit with purpose. Report on deck and lifting.

In the H130 across Brazilian basins, the deck lights and your limits are the law.

Green to land. Red to leave. Clear English either way.
