# Rotor Brake Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0414  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Rotor Brake Failure — inability of the rotor brake to stop or hold the main rotor after landing or shutdown, creating a prolonged turning-rotor hazard for passengers and ground crew.

Rotor brake failure is a ground emergency with airside consequences: the pad stays hot, people cannot approach, and the next aircraft cannot use the site until the rotor is secured by other means.

For H130 operations in Brazil — hospital pads, Congonhas stands, and offshore decks — you must report the failure, keep personnel clear, and request assistance without understating the hazard.

Understanding how to communicate rotor brake failure in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Rotor Brake Failure is a malfunction in which the rotor brake does not decelerate or hold the main rotor as designed after landing or shutdown, leaving the rotor turning longer than expected and requiring procedures to keep the area clear until the rotor can be stopped safely.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha do freio de rotor.

Malfuncionamento em que o freio de rotor não desacelera ou retém o rotor principal conforme projetado após o pouso ou o desligamento, deixando o rotor girando por mais tempo que o esperado e exigindo procedimentos para manter a área livre até que o rotor possa ser parado com segurança.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When rotor brake failure occurs, the crew should:

- keep all personnel clear of the disc — no passenger egress toward turning blades
- attempt approved alternate stop procedures per AFM / company SOP
- advise ATC or ground that rotors will not stop / rotor brake failed
- request fire or ground assistance if required by procedure
- hold the next aircraft clear of the pad until the rotor is secured

Rotor brake failure reporting commonly includes:

- rotor brake failure, rotors still turning
- keep all personnel clear
- requesting ground assistance
- pad not available, rotor not secured

**Important:** Do not clear ambulances, marshallers, or passengers while the rotor turns. The failure is the brake — the hazard is the disc.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers and apron/helipad agencies use rotor brake failure reports to:

- keep the next arrival holding clear of an unsafe pad
- alert ground emergency services if requested
- protect vehicles and pedestrians near the turning rotor
- coordinate alternate parking or shutdown locations
- update estimated pad availability realistically

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You land your H130 at Santos Dumont helipad after a charter. You shut down and apply the rotor brake. The rotor does not decelerate normally.

You hold the cabin: nobody exits toward the blades. You call:

Tower, rotor brake failure, rotors still turning, keep all personnel clear of the aircraft, requesting ground assistance, pad not available.

Tower holds the next helicopter and sends ground crew with the approved securing procedure. Only after the rotor stops do you allow egress.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger rotor brake failure, keep personnel clear, assistance coordinated.
2. Tower: ANAC123, say if you require fire services.
3. Tower: ANAC123, next helicopter holding, advise when rotor secured.
4. Ground: ANAC123, confirm safe distance for approach to assist.
5. Tower: ANAC123, roger rotors now stopped, report when pad clear.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, rotor brake failure, personnel remaining clear, stand by.
2. ANAC123, negative fire services at this time, request maintenance assistance.
3. ANAC123, wilco, will advise when rotor secured.
4. ANAC123, remain clear until signaled, rotor still turning.
5. ANAC123, rotors stopped, passengers disembarking, pad clear shortly.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Rotor brake failure English is perimeter English.

Your first product on frequency is a clear keep-clear message. Your second is an honest pad-not-available.

In the H130, follow the AFM alternate procedure. Do not invent a "quick stop" that damages the transmission or throws a blade hazard at the crowd.

Brazilian hospital and city pads are crowded. People will walk in if you sound uncertain. Sound certain: rotor brake failure, rotors still turning, keep clear.

When the rotor finally stops, close the loop with ATC so the next flight can move. Silence after the emergency call creates a second problem — a stuck pad with no status.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

After shutdown the rotor brake fails and blades keep turning. What do you tell Tower?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report the failure and the hazard clearly: rotor brake failure, rotors still turning, keep all personnel clear, pad not available, requesting ground assistance. I would not allow passenger egress toward the disc. When the rotor is secured I would update: rotors stopped, pad clearing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Allowing passengers to exit "pelo lado de trás" while blades turn.  
  ✔ Nobody approaches a turning rotor — report keep clear until stopped.

- ❌ Saying only "freio com problema" without pad status.  
  ✔ State rotor brake failure, rotors still turning, pad not available.

- ❌ Forgetting to update when the rotor is finally secured.  
  ✔ Close the loop so ATC can release the next arrival.

- ❌ Requesting ambulance approach for patient transfer too early.  
  ✔ Secure the rotor first; then clear medical approach.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Rotor Brake Failure, Keep Personnel Clear

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, ROH-ter BRAYK FAYL-yer, keep per-suh-NEL KLEER

**Word Stress**

- Rotor → ROH-ter
- Brake → BRAYK
- Failure → FAYL-yer
- Personnel → per-suh-NEL (stress on last)

Practice:

Rotor brake failure... keep personnel clear.

Together:

rotor brake failure, rotors still turning, keep all personnel clear, pad not available.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**STOP** — **S**ecure cabin (no egress), **T**ell ATC brake failed, **O**btain assistance, **P**ad unavailable until rotor stopped.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Hospital pad in Rio. Rotor brake fails after HEMS landing. Patient needs transfer. Blades still turning.

**Your first task**

What is your first transmission to Tower?

**Your second task**

Ground asks if the ambulance can approach from the rear. Rotor still turning. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, rotor brake failure, rotors still turning, keep all personnel clear, pad not available, requesting assistance. Second task: ANAC123, negative, remain clear of the aircraft until rotors stopped — no approach from any direction while the disc is turning. Patient transfer waits for a stopped rotor.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Rotor brake failure turns a quiet shutdown into a ground hazard.

Keep people clear. Tell ATC the pad is blocked. Get help. Update when secured.

In the H130 on Brazilian pads, speed of transfer never beats a turning blade.

Say the failure. Hold the perimeter. Stop the rotor. Then move the mission.
