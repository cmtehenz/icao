# MGB Oil Pressure Low

**Catalog ID:** 0445  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers MGB Oil Pressure Low — a low main gearbox oil pressure indication that threatens lubrication of gears and bearings in the primary transmission.

Without adequate oil pressure, the main gearbox can overheat, seize, or fail catastrophically with little further warning.

For H130 helicopter operations, low MGB oil pressure is a critical emergency: reduce power, land as soon as possible, and communicate with Mayday when pressure is lost or falling with secondary symptoms.

Understanding how to report MGB oil pressure low in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

MGB Oil Pressure Low is an abnormal indication that main gearbox oil pressure has fallen below the minimum required for adequate lubrication of the main transmission.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pressão de óleo baixa da caixa de transmissão principal (MGB).

Indicação anormal de que a pressão do óleo da caixa principal caiu abaixo do mínimo necessário para lubrificação adequada da transmissão principal.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When MGB oil pressure is low, the pilot should:

- reduce collective and gearbox load immediately within safe limits
- monitor temperature, pressure trend, and vibration
- land as soon as possible at a suitable site
- declare Mayday for significant pressure loss or accompanying abnormal indications
- prepare for possible gearbox failure and autorotation if seizure occurs

MGB oil pressure low reporting commonly includes:

- main gearbox oil pressure low
- reducing power, landing immediately
- request clear landing area and emergency services
- souls on board and position

**Important:** Low MGB oil pressure is time-critical. Do not delay the landing to troubleshoot beyond memory items. Aviate, land, communicate.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to low MGB oil pressure to:

- clear an immediate path to the nearest suitable landing site
- coordinate fire and rescue for a possible hard landing or fire
- request souls on board and fuel remaining
- keep other traffic clear of the approach and landing area
- avoid any instruction that increases power or extends flight time

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are at one thousand eight hundred feet in your H130 near Florianópolis.

MGB oil pressure drops into the low caution range and continues falling. Temperature begins to rise. You reduce collective and select the nearest suitable landing site.

You declare Mayday, report main gearbox oil pressure low, and request immediate landing. Approach clears traffic. You land with minimum power, shut down, and request fire services until the area is secured.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Oil pressure is life for the main gearbox.

When pressure falls, lubrication falls. Heat rises. Metal-to-metal contact can follow quickly.

On the H130, treat low MGB oil pressure as an immediate landing item. Reduce load. Do not climb. Do not accept delay.

If pressure collapses with vibration or rapid temperature rise, expect possible seizure. Keep an autorotation plan in mind while you fly to the nearest suitable site.

Say the words clearly: main gearbox oil pressure low. Controllers must understand this is transmission critical — not a routine engine oil call.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

MGB oil pressure falls rapidly in cruise. What do you say to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, main gearbox oil pressure low and falling, reducing power, request immediate landing nearest suitable. I would state position and souls on board as soon as practicable.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Trying to reach home base with critically low MGB pressure.  
  ✔ Land as soon as possible at the nearest suitable site.

- ❌ Declaring only Pan Pan when pressure is collapsing.  
  ✔ Use Mayday for significant MGB oil pressure loss.

- ❌ Omitting "main gearbox" and saying only "low oil pressure."  
  ✔ Specify MGB so ATC understands transmission urgency.

- ❌ Increasing collective to "get there faster."  
  ✔ Reduce load — high power accelerates gearbox damage.

- Gearbox → GEAR-boks

Low MGB oil pressure is a short fuse.
