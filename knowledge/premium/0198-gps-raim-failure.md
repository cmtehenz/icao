# GPS RAIM Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0198  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2, Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers GPS RAIM Failure — the loss of Receiver Autonomous Integrity Monitoring integrity or availability required for GPS-based navigation, especially for RNAV approaches.

This is distinct from GPS Inoperative. GPS may still show a position, but RAIM may be unavailable or indicate that integrity is insufficient for the phase of flight. For RNAV (GNSS) approaches, that distinction matters: without required RAIM/integrity, you must not continue the approach to the published minima.

In H130 IFR training and Brazilian operations into Florianópolis, Curitiba, Congonhas, and similar airports, RNAV approaches are common. RAIM prediction before departure and RAIM status in flight are part of professional GNSS operations.

This lesson focuses on integrity and availability for RNAV — how to recognize the problem, what to tell ATC, and how to describe the decision in ICAO speaking terms.

## 📖 Meaning

GPS RAIM Failure is the condition in which Receiver Autonomous Integrity Monitoring cannot provide the integrity assurance required for the intended GPS/RNAV operation, meaning the GPS position may be available but is not integrity-monitored to the standard needed for that phase of flight, particularly for RNAV approach.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha de RAIM do GPS / GPS RAIM Failure.

Condição em que o monitoramento autônomo de integridade do receptor (RAIM) não consegue fornecer a garantia de integridade exigida para a operação GPS/RNAV pretendida — a posição GPS pode estar disponível, mas sem integridade monitorada no padrão necessário, especialmente para aproximação RNAV.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When GPS RAIM failure or RAIM unavailable occurs, the crew should:

- check whether GPS position remains displayed versus integrity status lost
- discontinue RNAV approach if RAIM/integrity required for the approach is not available
- inform ATC that RAIM is unavailable or integrity is lost for RNAV
- request radar vectors or a conventional approach (ILS, VOR, visual if appropriate)
- consider missed approach if already established and integrity is lost below a safe point to continue

RAIM-related management includes:

- preflight RAIM prediction for the destination and alternate when required
- distinguishing GPS inoperative (no usable GPS) from RAIM failure (integrity/availability problem)
- not descending on an RNAV approach without the required integrity
- having a conventional or visual backup plan before commencing RNAV

**Important:** RAIM failure is about integrity, not necessarily a blank GPS screen. If the approach requires RAIM and RAIM is lost, the approach is no longer valid for you — even if a magenta line still looks convincing.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to RAIM/integrity reports to:

- stop sequencing an aircraft onto an RNAV procedure it cannot legally or safely fly
- provide vectors for ILS, VOR, or visual approaches
- coordinate approach changes with tower and adjacent sectors
- manage missed approaches when integrity is lost late in the procedure
- maintain traffic flow when GNSS approaches become unavailable for one or more aircraft

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cleared RNAV approach runway one four at Florianópolis in the H130.

On intermediate approach, the avionics annunciate RAIM unavailable. GPS position remains, but integrity for approach is lost.

You do not continue to minima on RNAV. You level as appropriate, prepare for missed approach or vectors, and transmit:

ANAC123, RAIM unavailable, unable RNAV approach, request vectors for ILS runway one four.

Approach: ANAC123, roger, climb and maintain three thousand, turn left heading three six zero, vectors for ILS runway one four.

You execute the revised clearance. The GPS still shows a picture. The integrity required for the RNAV approach does not. That is the decision point.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

RAIM is the integrity watchdog for GPS.

GPS can lie quietly. RAIM is how the receiver tells you it can no longer guarantee the position for the procedure you are flying.

For RNAV approaches, integrity is not optional language in the manual — it is the gate. No required RAIM, no RNAV approach to minima.

Do not confuse this with GPS Inoperative. GPS Inoperative means you lost GPS navigation. RAIM failure means integrity monitoring or availability failed for the required operation. The screen may still look useful. That is exactly when pilots make the wrong decision.

In Brazilian ops, say the operational fact: "RAIM unavailable, unable RNAV, request ILS" or "request vectors." That sentence moves ATC immediately.

In the H130, brief the backup approach before you start the RNAV. When RAIM fails, you execute a plan you already own — not a plan you invent at the final approach fix.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You are on an RNAV approach when RAIM becomes unavailable. GPS position is still displayed. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would not continue the RNAV approach to the published minima without the required integrity. I would maintain safe flight path control, discontinue the RNAV procedure as required, and inform ATC that RAIM is unavailable and that I am unable to continue the RNAV approach. I would request radar vectors or a conventional approach such as ILS or VOR. Even if GPS position remains displayed, without required RAIM integrity the RNAV approach is not acceptable. I would execute the missed approach if already at a point where discontinuing requires it, then accept the revised clearance.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing RNAV to minima because "GPS still works."  
  ✔ Without required RAIM/integrity, discontinue RNAV approach procedures.

- ❌ Reporting only "GPS problem" when the issue is RAIM integrity.  
  ✔ Say RAIM unavailable or integrity lost, unable RNAV.

- ❌ Confusing RAIM failure with total GPS inoperative in every case.  
  ✔ Distinguish integrity/availability failure from complete GPS loss.

- ❌ Having no backup approach briefed before commencing RNAV.  
  ✔ Always brief ILS/VOR/visual options before RNAV in IMC.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are on RNAV approach to Curitiba. At intermediate fix, RAIM unavailable annunciates. GPS map still shows the approach path. Weather is IMC.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

Approach says: "ANAC123, climb and maintain four thousand, turn left heading three three zero, vectors for ILS." Give the readback and state the key cockpit decision.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, RAIM unavailable, unable RNAV approach, request vectors for ILS. Second task: ANAC123, climbing four thousand, left heading three three zero, vectors for ILS. Key decision: discontinue the RNAV approach despite the GPS map still displaying — integrity required for the approach is lost, so I set up the ILS and fly the assigned vectors.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

RAIM failure is an integrity decision, not a screen decision.

If the RNAV approach requires RAIM and RAIM is gone, the approach is gone with it.

Say it clearly. Request the backup. Fly the new clearance.

GPS Inoperative is loss of GPS. RAIM failure is loss of trust in GPS for that procedure.

In the H130, that distinction keeps you legal, safe, and professional on Brazilian RNAV approaches.

Integrity first. Magenta second.
