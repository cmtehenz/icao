# Missed Approach

**Catalog ID:** 0008  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson explains one of the most misunderstood concepts in instrument flying.

Many pilots use Go Around and Missed Approach as if they meant exactly the same thing.

They don't.

A professional pilot understands the difference immediately.

Today you'll learn when a Go Around becomes a Missed Approach and why every instrument approach already includes an escape plan before the flight even begins.

## 📖 Meaning

A Missed Approach is the published or assigned procedure followed when an aircraft cannot complete an instrument approach and landing.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Procedimento de aproximação perdida.

É a rota publicada que o piloto deve seguir quando o pouso não pode ser completado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

Every IFR approach includes a Missed Approach Procedure (MAP).

This procedure guarantees:

- obstacle clearance
- terrain separation
- traffic separation
- safe repositioning for another approach

A Missed Approach may occur because:

- the runway environment is not in sight at the decision altitude
- the runway becomes unavailable
- ATC instructs the aircraft to discontinue the approach
- the pilot determines the landing cannot be completed safely

Unlike a simple Go Around, a Missed Approach follows a published procedure unless ATC assigns different instructions.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Missed Approach instructions to:

- maintain IFR separation
- protect obstacle clearance
- sequence arriving traffic
- manage weather-related operations
- organize another approach safely

Sometimes ATC simply instructs:

"Execute published missed approach."

The pilot immediately follows the published procedure.

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're flying your H130 on an IFR approach into Curitiba.

Weather is poor.

Visibility is close to minimums.

As you reach Decision Altitude, you still cannot see the runway.

Instead of descending further, you immediately initiate the published Missed Approach Procedure.

You begin climbing.

Follow the published heading.

Navigate toward the holding fix.

Advise ATC.

The approach was unsuccessful, but the procedure worked exactly as designed.

This is a completely normal IFR operation.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. ANAC123, execute published missed approach.
2. ANAC123, fly runway heading, climb three thousand.
3. ANAC123, execute missed approach, contact Departure.
4. ANAC123, climb and maintain four thousand after the missed approach.
5. ANAC123, report established in the published hold.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between a Go Around and a Missed Approach?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A Go Around is the decision to discontinue a landing. A Missed Approach is the published or assigned IFR procedure followed after discontinuing an instrument approach. Every Missed Approach is a Go Around, but not every Go Around becomes a published Missed Approach.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Using Go Around and Missed Approach as identical terms.  
  ✔ They are closely related but not identical.

- ❌ Forgetting to brief the Missed Approach before starting the approach.  
  Professional crews always review it during the approach briefing.

- ❌ Trying to decide what to do after reaching minimums.  
  The decision should already be planned.

- ❌ Believing a Missed Approach means something went wrong.  
  Many Missed Approaches occur simply because weather is below landing minimums. They are normal IFR procedures.
