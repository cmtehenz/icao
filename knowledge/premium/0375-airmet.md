# AIRMET

**Catalog ID:** 0375  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers AIRMET — Airmen's Meteorological Information — advisories for weather phenomena that are potentially hazardous to aircraft, especially lighter or lower-performance types, but that do not meet SIGMET criteria for severity or extent.

AIRMETs brief the "everyday hazards": moderate icing, moderate turbulence, IFR conditions, and mountain obscuration. For H130 operations in Brazil, an AIRMET for icing or IFR may be the difference between a legal VFR hop and an IFR or no-go decision.

Understanding AIRMET content and how to discuss it in ICAO English supports Part 2 coordination and Part 4 weather interpretation.

## 📖 Meaning

An AIRMET is a meteorological advisory issued for specified en-route weather hazards — such as moderate icing, moderate turbulence, IFR ceilings/visibility, or mountain obscuration — that are significant to aircraft operations, particularly for lighter aircraft, and that are less severe or extensive than those requiring a SIGMET.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

AIRMET / Informação Meteorológica para Aeronavegantes.

Aviso meteorológico emitido para riscos de tempo em rota especificados — como gelo moderado, turbulência moderada, condições IFR de teto/visibilidade ou montanhas obscurecidas — que são significativos para as operações aéreas, especialmente de aeronaves mais leves, e que são menos severos ou extensos do que os que exigem um SIGMET.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When an AIRMET is current, the crew should:

- read hazard type, area, altitude band, and valid time in preflight
- compare AIRMET icing or IFR with H130 limitations and flight rules
- amend route or altitude to avoid moderate icing layers when not equipped
- plan IFR or delay if AIRMET Sierra-type IFR covers a VFR-only flight
- use AIRMET information when explaining weather-related requests to ATC
- update with METARs, TAFs, and PIREPs — AIRMET is not a substitute for destination weather

AIRMET use commonly includes:

- briefing moderate icing along the route — plan exit altitudes
- IFR AIRMET — file IFR or cancel VFR plan
- turbulence AIRMET — brief ride expectations and fuel
- mountain obscuration — avoid VFR ridge flight

**Important:** Product names and formats in Brazil follow DECEA/ICAO meteorological practice; the operational idea remains: AIRMET-class hazards are real for helicopters. Moderate icing AIRMET over your cruise band is a planning stop sign for an H130 not certified for known icing.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use AIRMET information to:

- anticipate altitude change requests out of icing or turbulence
- understand why VFR aircraft request IFR pickup or diversion
- relay hazard awareness along busy routes
- support flow decisions when IFR conditions are widespread
- correlate PIREPs with advisory areas

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Preflight shows an AIRMET for moderate icing between four and eight thousand over the São Paulo plateau. Your H130 cruise was planned at six thousand VFR on top of haze — but into the icing band in cloud.

You replan at three thousand where temperatures are warmer, or you delay. En route, if still exposed:

São Paulo Approach, ANAC123, requesting descent to three thousand due icing, AIRMET icing layer.

Approach: ANAC123, descend and maintain three thousand.

You leave the advisory band and continue.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, descend and maintain three thousand, advise clear of icing.
2. Center: ANAC123, AIRMET icing along your route, say intentions.
3. Approach: ANAC123, IFR conditions reported in AIRMET area, confirm flight rules.
4. Approach: ANAC123, turbulence AIRMET in effect, advise ride quality.
5. Center: ANAC123, request approved, report when clear of advisory area.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, descending three thousand due icing, will advise when clear.
2. ANAC123, requesting IFR pickup, AIRMET IFR conditions along route.
3. ANAC123, moderate turbulence, continuing, will advise if unable.
4. ANAC123, diverting due mountain obscuration, request vectors.
5. ANAC123, clear of icing at three thousand, resuming cruise.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Explain what an AIRMET is and how it affects an H130 flight.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

An AIRMET advises en-route hazards such as moderate icing, moderate turbulence, IFR weather, or mountain obscuration that are significant but below SIGMET severity. For the H130 I would check whether my altitude and route sit inside an icing or IFR AIRMET. If moderate icing is forecast in cloud at my level, I would change altitude, delay, or file a different plan because the helicopter is not certified for known icing. I would use the AIRMET together with METAR and TAF, and I would request ATC altitude changes if I encounter the hazard in flight.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating AIRMET as optional because it is "only moderate."  
  ✔ Moderate icing can still be limiting for light helicopters.

- ❌ Reading AIRMET but not checking altitude bands.  
  ✔ The hazard is often height-specific — plan above or below.

- ❌ Using AIRMET alone without METAR/TAF for destination.  
  ✔ AIRMET is en-route; destination needs aerodrome forecasts/reports.

- ❌ Unable to explain AIRMET vs SIGMET in Part 4 speaking.  
  ✔ AIRMET = significant but lesser; SIGMET = more severe / wider impact.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

AIRMET forecasts moderate icing four to nine thousand on your route. You are at six thousand in cloud.

**Your first task**

What do you request?

**Your second task**

Explain to an examiner the difference between AIRMET and SIGMET.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, requesting descent to three thousand due icing, AIRMET icing layer. Second task: AIRMET covers significant but generally moderate hazards for en-route flight; SIGMET covers more severe or hazardous phenomena such as severe turbulence, severe icing, or volcanic ash / convective extremes. For the H130, both demand action — AIRMET moderate icing still means exit known icing.
