# Wreckage on Runway

**Catalog ID:** 0221  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Wreckage on Runway — the critical advisory that aircraft wreckage, major debris from an accident, or related obstruction is on the runway, making the runway unusable until inspected and cleared.

This is critical language. Wreckage on the runway stops landings and takeoffs. Go-arounds, holds, diversions, and runway closures follow immediately. There is no "continue and have a look" for training traffic.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations into Congonhas, Guarulhos, Santos Dumont, Curitiba, and Florianópolis, a wreckage event may close the runway while helipads or alternate aerodromes remain options — only with ATC clearance. Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 speaking both demand calm, precise communication under pressure.

Understanding how to acknowledge, go around, divert, and describe intentions when wreckage is on the runway is essential for ICAO Level 4 performance and real emergency discipline.

## 📖 Meaning

Wreckage on Runway means aircraft wreckage or major accident-related debris is present on the runway surface, rendering the runway unsafe or closed for landing and takeoff until rescue, investigation, and removal allow reopening.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Wreckage on runway / Destroços / destroços na pista.

Significa que destroços de aeronave ou grandes detritos relacionados a um acidente estão na superfície da pista, tornando-a insegura ou fechada para pouso e decolagem até que o resgate, a investigação e a remoção permitam a reabertura.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When wreckage is reported on the runway, the pilot should:

- abandon any landing or takeoff clearance involving that runway immediately
- execute go-around if on final and follow missed approach or ATC vectors
- acknowledge the runway closed / wreckage advisory
- state intentions: hold, divert, or request alternate runway/helipad if available
- avoid overflying the wreckage site at low altitude unless instructed for operational need
- expect RFF activity, frequency congestion, and possible airport operations closed

Wreckage on runway commonly follows:

- runway excursion or collision on the runway
- rejected takeoff with aircraft still on the strip
- landing accident with wreckage on or near the centerline
- debris field after an earlier incident not yet cleared

**Important:** Curiosity is not a clearance. Do not request a low pass over wreckage for training. In the H130, if an alternate helipad on the same aerodrome is offered, confirm it is clear of the emergency response path before accepting.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers advise wreckage on runway to:

- stop all landings and takeoffs on the affected runway instantly
- trigger go-arounds and protect RFF and investigation teams
- coordinate runway closure and alternate runway or aerodrome use
- give crews the critical reason for sudden plan changes
- manage holding and diversion with shared situational awareness

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on final for runway one seven Left at Congonhas in the H130.

Tower: ANAC123, go around, wreckage on the runway, runway closed.

You go around immediately. You do not continue to "confirm visually."

Tower asks intentions. You have fuel for Curitiba. You request diversion. Approach vectors you away from the emergency site. RFF is on the runway. Your job is to leave them the sky they need.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, go around, wreckage on the runway, runway closed.
2. Approach: ANAC123, Congonhas runway closed due wreckage, advise intentions.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cancel takeoff clearance, wreckage on runway, hold position.
4. Approach: ANAC123, expect vectors for holding, runway closed, wreckage on the strip.
5. Tower: ANAC123, runway one seven Left closed, wreckage, request alternate intentions.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, going around, runway closed.
2. ANAC123, runway closed due wreckage, request diversion to Curitiba.
3. ANAC123, cancel takeoff, holding position.
4. ANAC123, vectors for holding, runway closed copied.
5. ANAC123, one seven Left closed, request alternate runway or diversion.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Wreckage on the runway is a red-line event.

The landing is over. The takeoff is over. The plan rebuilds from fuel, weather, and ATC capacity.

Say the readback short: going around, runway closed. Then think. Then state intentions.

Part 3 wants structure under stress: what happened, what you did, what you will do next. Keep emotion out of the microphone. Keep passengers informed later on the cabin side — not by blocking tower with long stories.

Brazilian major airports can close one runway and keep another — or close everything. Listen for which. Do not assume the parallel is open.

In the H130 you may be offered a helipad. Ask whether the approach path conflicts with RFF. Accept only a clear, authorized option.

Critical English is short English.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

On final, tower tells you there is wreckage on the runway and to go around. What do you do and how do you continue the communication?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would execute an immediate go-around and follow the missed approach procedure or ATC vectors. I would read back going around and that the runway is closed. After cleaning up the aircraft and establishing a safe climb, I would advise intentions based on fuel and alternates — for example request holding if a short delay is realistic, or request diversion if the runway will remain closed. I would not continue toward the wreckage for a closer look and I would stay clear of the emergency response area.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing final to "have a look" after a wreckage go-around order.  
  ✔ Go around immediately; visual curiosity is not authorized.

- ❌ Giving a long story on tower frequency during the go-around.  
  ✔ Short readback first; intentions when level and stable.

- ❌ Assuming a parallel runway or helipad is automatically available.  
  ✔ Request and confirm; do not self-clear to another surface.

- ❌ Overflying the wreckage site low for better viewing.  
  ✔ Remain clear unless ATC specifically requires a path.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are on a three-mile final at Florianópolis in the H130.

**Your first task**

Tower says: "ANAC123, go around, wreckage on the runway, runway closed." What do you say?

**Your second task**

Once established in the climb, Approach asks intentions. You have fuel for Curitiba only as alternate. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, going around, runway closed. Second task: ANAC123, request diversion to Curitiba — to state a clear, fuel-realistic intention without blocking the frequency with unnecessary detail while RFF works the runway.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Wreckage on the runway ends the approach.

Go around. Read it back. Build a new plan from fuel and ATC.

Stay clear of the site. Do not sightsee. Do not invent another runway.

Critical events reward short radio and long discipline.

That is how you sound Level 4 when the airport is having its worst day.
