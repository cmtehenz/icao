# TCAS Traffic Advisory

**Catalog ID:** 0146  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the TCAS Traffic Advisory — the first level of automated conflict alert generated by the Traffic Collision Avoidance System when conflicting traffic is detected.

A Traffic Advisory, or TA, is a warning. It does not command a maneuver. It tells you: traffic is near and closing. Watch. Be ready.

In Brazilian airspace around busy terminals like Congonhas, Florianópolis, and Curitiba, TA events can occur during vectoring, approach transitions, and departure climbs.

This lesson prepares you to respond correctly to a TA, communicate with ATC, and manage the transition to a potential Resolution Advisory — in clear, professional ICAO English.

## 📖 Meaning

TCAS Traffic Advisory is an automated audio and visual alert generated by the Traffic Collision Avoidance System indicating that conflicting traffic has been detected within a defined range and altitude band, requiring the crew to acquire the traffic visually and monitor for a possible Resolution Advisory.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Alerta de tráfego TCAS.

Alerta automatizado gerado pelo Sistema de Prevenção de Colisão de Tráfego Aéreo indicando que um tráfego conflitante foi detectado dentro de uma faixa definida de alcance e altitude, exigindo que a tripulação localize o tráfego visualmente e monitore a possibilidade de uma Instrução de Resolução.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a TCAS Traffic Advisory activates, the crew should:

- respond immediately with eyes outside the cockpit to acquire the traffic visually
- call "Traffic, Traffic" out loud as crew coordination
- identify the traffic on the TCAS display: range, bearing, relative altitude, trend
- maintain current clearance unless a Resolution Advisory follows
- do not maneuver based solely on a TA — wait for an RA if avoidance is required
- advise ATC of the TA and any traffic acquired

Traffic Advisories commonly precede:

- Resolution Advisories requiring a climb or descend command
- visual traffic acquisition that allows the crew to maintain separation
- coordinating calls to ATC when traffic is positively identified
- a return to cleared routing after the event

**Important:** A TA alone does not authorize deviation from ATC clearance. Monitor, search, and advise ATC. If an RA follows, comply with the RA immediately — ATC clearance is secondary to RA commands.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

TCAS Traffic Advisories are aircraft-generated — ATC does not issue them. However, pilots must:

- advise ATC when a TA occurs and is not resolved visually
- inform ATC when maneuvering in response to an RA
- report back on frequency after any TCAS event
- give ATC the information needed to restore separation

Controllers expect:

- a concise advisory call when TCAS activates
- clear communication if an RA maneuver follows
- a return to clearance as soon as the event is resolved

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are climbing out of Florianópolis in your H130, passing through four thousand feet.

TCAS announces: Traffic. Traffic.

The TCAS display shows an intruder aircraft at your level, two miles, closing from the right.

Your co-pilot calls: TCAS Traffic Advisory.

You scan right and visually acquire a turboprop on a converging heading.

You transmit to Approach:

ANAC123, TCAS traffic advisory, traffic in sight, two o'clock, same level.

Approach responds:

ANAC123, roger, identified traffic is BRAVO XY, maintaining radar separation, advise if RA.

You continue the climb on clearance. The traffic diverges. The TA clears.

You transmit:

ANAC123, TA resolved, continuing climb, no RA.

Professional communication throughout. No surprise. No confusion.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

TCAS is your second pair of eyes in the sky.

But TCAS has two voices. Learn the difference.

The Traffic Advisory says: look. Find the traffic. Prepare to act.

The Resolution Advisory says: act now. Climb or descend as directed.

Too many pilots panic on a TA and begin maneuvering without an RA.

That is wrong.

A TA alone: eyes outside, traffic display, advise ATC. Do not maneuver.

When the RA comes: follow it immediately. Forget the ATC clearance until the RA clears.

In the H130, the TCAS display is your best friend in busy Brazilian terminal areas.

Know the symbology: filled diamond is proximate. Open diamond is other traffic. Amber circle with arrow is TA. Red square with arrow is RA.

Know which is which before the event happens.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between a TCAS Traffic Advisory and a Resolution Advisory, and how do you communicate each to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A Traffic Advisory is a warning that conflicting traffic has been detected, requiring visual acquisition and monitoring. A Resolution Advisory is a command to maneuver vertically — climb or descend — to resolve the conflict. When a TA activates, I advise ATC: ANAC123, TCAS traffic advisory, acquiring traffic. If an RA follows, I transmit: ANAC123, TCAS RA, climbing, leaving four thousand. I comply with the RA immediately and return to my ATC clearance when TCAS indicates clear of conflict.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Maneuvering based on a TA without waiting for an RA.  
  ✔ A TA is a watch-and-prepare alert only. Do not maneuver without an RA.

- ❌ Not advising ATC when TCAS activates.  
  ✔ Inform ATC promptly: ANAC123, TCAS traffic advisory.

- ❌ Ignoring an RA to comply with an ATC instruction.  
  ✔ An RA takes priority over ATC clearance. Comply with the RA and inform ATC.

- ❌ Not reporting clear of conflict after the event.  
  ✔ Always close the loop: ANAC123, TA resolved, no RA, resuming clearance.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** TCAS Traffic Advisory

**Pronunciation:** TEE-kas TRA-fik ad-VY-zer-ee

**Word Stress**

- TCAS → TEE-kas (two syllables, stress on first)
- Traffic → TRA-fik
- Advisory → ad-VY-zer-ee (stress on second syllable)

Practice:

TCAS... Traffic... Advisory...

Together:

ANAC123, TCAS traffic advisory, traffic in sight.

Say TCAS as TEE-kas — not as individual letters T-C-A-S in most operational contexts.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are on final approach into Curitiba in your H130. At three thousand feet, TCAS announces: Traffic. Traffic. The display shows traffic at one o'clock, one mile, climbing through your level.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit to Approach?

**Your second task**

Ten seconds later TCAS announces: Climb. Climb Now. What do you say and what do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would transmit: ANAC123, TCAS traffic advisory, one o'clock, one mile, traffic climbing through our level. When the RA commands climb, I would immediately begin climbing and transmit: ANAC123, TCAS RA, climbing, leaving three thousand. I would comply with the RA command regardless of the approach clearance, and when TCAS indicated clear of conflict I would advise: ANAC123, RA resolved, request return to clearance.
