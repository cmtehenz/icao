# Go Around

**Catalog ID:** 0007  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Every professional pilot performs Go Arounds.

A Go Around is not a mistake.

It is not a failed landing.

It is one of the safest decisions a pilot can make.

Good pilots know how to land.

Excellent pilots know when not to land.

Today you'll learn one of the most important safety maneuvers in aviation.

## 📖 Meaning

A Go Around is the decision or instruction to discontinue the landing and climb away for another approach.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Arremeter.

Interromper o pouso e iniciar uma nova aproximação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

A Go Around may be:

- initiated by the pilot
- instructed by ATC

The objective is simple:

Do not continue an unsafe landing.

Common reasons include:

- unstable approach
- runway occupied
- aircraft not configured
- windshear
- bird strike risk
- another aircraft entering the runway
- loss of visual references
- unexpected obstacle

A Go Around is considered a normal maneuver, not an emergency.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers instruct Go Around to:

- maintain runway separation
- prevent collisions
- protect runway occupancy
- resolve traffic conflicts
- avoid unstable approaches
- respond to unexpected runway events

Pilots may also initiate one whenever safety margins are reduced.

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're flying your H130 into Florianópolis Airport.

Everything looks perfect.

The runway is in sight.

Landing checklist complete.

At approximately 200 feet AGL, Tower suddenly transmits:

"PT-ABC, Go Around, traffic still on the runway."

Without hesitation you:

- apply power
- establish a positive climb
- follow published or ATC instructions
- prepare for another approach

The passengers may wonder why.

A professional pilot knows:

The safest landing is sometimes the one you don't complete.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. PT-ABC, Go Around.
2. PT-ABC, Go Around, traffic on the runway.
3. PT-ABC, Go Around, unstable spacing.
4. PT-ABC, Go Around, runway occupied.
5. PT-ABC, Go Around, windshear reported on final.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Going around, PT-ABC.
2. PT-ABC going around.
3. Going around due traffic, PT-ABC.
4. Going around, runway occupied, PT-ABC.
5. Going around, following published missed approach, PT-ABC.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Gustavo, imagine you're driving toward a parking space.

Just as you're about to park, another car suddenly reverses into the space.

You don't force your way in.

You simply drive around the block and try again.

Aviation works the same way.

A Go Around isn't about proving you can land.

It's about proving you know when not to.

Some pilots think a Go Around is embarrassing.

Experienced captains think exactly the opposite.

Every Go Around means someone made a professional decision before a situation became dangerous.

If you ever feel uncomfortable during the approach, remember this:

There is no shame in going around.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

When would you decide to perform a Go Around?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would perform a Go Around whenever the approach became unstable or the landing could no longer be completed safely. For example, if another aircraft was still on the runway, if I experienced windshear, if I lost visual references, or if ATC instructed me to Go Around.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Thinking Go Around is an emergency.  
  ✔ It is a normal safety maneuver.

- ❌ Trying to "save" a bad landing.  
  Professional pilots don't save unstable approaches. They discontinue them.

- ❌ Saying: Abort landing.  
  Although understandable, standard phraseology is: Going Around.

- ❌ Delaying the decision.  
  Every second spent trying to "fix" an unstable approach increases the risk.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Go Around

**Pronunciation:** GO uh-ROUND

**Word Stress**

- GO
- Around (second syllable stronger)

Practice:

Go... Around... Go Around.

Now naturally:

Going around, PT-ABC.

Avoid saying: Goooo Around

Keep the rhythm natural and confident.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Imagine you're walking toward an elevator.

Just before the doors close, someone steps inside carrying a large box.

There isn't enough space.

Do you squeeze in?

No.

You simply wait for the next elevator.

That's exactly what a Go Around is.

You're choosing the next safe opportunity instead of forcing the current one.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

"PT-ABC, Go Around, traffic still on the runway."

**Your first task**

Read back the instruction exactly as a professional pilot.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

Name three situations in which you would initiate a Go Around, even if ATC did not instruct you to do so.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would initiate a Go Around if the approach became unstable, if I experienced windshear, if I lost visual contact with the runway, if another aircraft or vehicle entered the runway, or whenever I believed the landing could not be completed safely.
