# Speed Restriction

**Catalog ID:** 0157  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Speed Restriction — the ATC instruction that assigns a specific airspeed the crew must maintain, not exceed, or comply with at a defined point.

Speed control is one of the most frequently issued ATC tools in terminal airspace.

At Brazilian airports including Congonhas, Navegantes, Florianópolis, and Curitiba, speed restrictions are issued routinely during approach sequencing, departure flow management, and en-route spacing.

ATC issues speed restrictions to maintain separation between aircraft, manage arrival flows, optimize spacing on final approach, and reduce noise impact near airports.

Knowing exactly how to respond to, read back, and comply with a speed restriction — and when to request an amendment — is essential ICAO Level 4 knowledge.

## 📖 Meaning

A Speed Restriction is an ATC instruction assigning a specific airspeed or airspeed limit that the crew must comply with, expressed as an indicated airspeed in knots. ATC may instruct the crew to maintain a speed, not exceed a speed, reduce speed, or increase speed to a specified value.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Restrição de velocidade.

Instrução do controle de tráfego aéreo que atribui uma velocidade aérea específica ou um limite de velocidade que a tripulação deve cumprir, expressa em velocidade indicada em nós. O controle pode instruir a tripulação a manter uma velocidade, não exceder uma velocidade, reduzir a velocidade ou aumentar a velocidade para um valor especificado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the crew receives a speed restriction, they should:

- read back the speed instruction clearly
- comply promptly — speed changes should be initiated within a reasonable time
- maintain the assigned speed until ATC cancels the restriction or assigns a new speed
- do not exceed or fall below the assigned speed unless performance limits require it
- advise ATC if unable to comply with the assigned speed

Speed restriction operations commonly include:

- reduce speed to one eight zero knots for terminal area sequencing
- maintain two five zero knots for en-route spacing
- do not exceed two one zero knots on the STAR
- increase speed to two two zero knots for separation
- resume normal speed when restriction is cancelled

**Important:** Speed restrictions are indicated airspeed instructions. In high-altitude operations, ATC may reference Mach number instead. Unless cancelled, a speed restriction remains in effect throughout the phase of flight for which it was issued. Do not revert to normal speed without ATC clearance.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue speed restrictions to:

- maintain separation between successive arrivals on approach
- equalize spacing in terminal area sequencing
- reduce noise impact near residential areas around airports
- coordinate handoffs between sectors at specific speeds
- manage departure flow and separation during SID operations

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on the STAR inbound to Congonhas at two hundred and forty knots in your H130.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, reduce speed to one eight zero knots, maintain until further advised.

You adjust thrust and configure for one eight zero knots.

You respond:

Reduce speed one eight zero knots, ANAC123.

As you continue the arrival, approach transmits:

ANAC123, reduce speed to one six zero knots, you are number two, following traffic on a six mile final.

One six zero knots, ANAC123.

At the final approach fix, approach transmits:

ANAC123, resume normal speed.

Normal speed, ANAC123.

The arrival was sequenced smoothly. Speed control was the tool that made it happen.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Speed control is ATC's most precise sequencing tool.

One knot of speed difference over twenty miles of approach translates into significant spacing changes.

When ATC assigns you a speed — that speed is your separation contribution.

It is not a suggestion. It is not approximate. Comply precisely.

In the H130, speed restrictions on approach require anticipation. The aircraft has good energy management characteristics, but you must plan ahead — especially in the terminal area.

If a speed restriction prevents you from maintaining the approach profile, say so: ANAC123, unable one six zero knots on approach, minimum approach speed is one seven five.

ATC will adjust the sequence. That is always better than a missed approach or a separation event.

One important nuance: reduce speed to one eight zero is not the same as maintain one eight zero. "Reduce to" establishes a target. "Maintain" means hold it precisely. Know the difference.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC instructs you to maintain one eight zero knots until LUMEN then resume normal speed. What does this mean operationally and how do you read it back?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

This instruction means I must hold an indicated airspeed of one eight zero knots from the point of the instruction until I reach LUMEN waypoint on the arrival. At LUMEN, I am cleared to return to a speed appropriate for the phase of flight — typically the published approach speed or my normal terminal area speed. I would read back: Maintain one eight zero knots until LUMEN, then resume normal speed, ANAC123. I would monitor my speed closely on the segment, and if at any point I could not maintain one eight zero knots — due to weather, turbulence, or aircraft configuration — I would advise ATC immediately and request an amended speed.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Not reading back the specific speed value assigned.  
  ✔ Always read back the exact speed: reduce speed one eight zero knots, ANAC123.

- ❌ Reverting to normal speed before ATC cancels the restriction.  
  ✔ A speed restriction remains in effect until ATC explicitly cancels it or assigns a new speed.

- ❌ Confusing indicated airspeed with ground speed in the readback.  
  ✔ Speed restrictions are always indicated airspeed in knots unless otherwise specified.

- ❌ Not advising ATC when unable to comply with an assigned speed.  
  ✔ If the speed is operationally unachievable, advise ATC: ANAC123, unable one six zero knots, minimum one seven five.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Reduce Speed to One Eight Zero Knots

**Pronunciation:** reh-DOOS speed too WUN AYT ZE-ro NOTS

**Word Stress**

- Reduce → reh-DOOS (stress on second syllable)
- Speed → speed
- One Eight Zero → WUN AYT ZE-ro
- Knots → NOTS

Practice:

Reduce speed... one eight zero... knots.

Together:

Reduce speed one eight zero knots, ANAC123.

Speak the speed as three separate digits — one eight zero — not as a compound number.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**SPEED** — **S**tay at the assigned speed until released, **P**recision matters — one knot off compounds over distance, **E**xact readback of the value assigned, **E**arly configuration for speed changes, **D**eclare immediately if unable to comply.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Congonhas Approach transmits: "ANAC123, reduce speed to one seven zero knots, maintain until the outer marker."

**Your first task**

Read back the instruction.

**Your second task**

Due to gusty crosswind conditions, you determine that one seven zero knots is too close to your approach speed margin. Minimum safe speed with current configuration is one eight five knots. What do you transmit?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would read back: Reduce speed one seven zero knots, maintain until outer marker, ANAC123. If I determined that one seven zero knots was unsafe given current conditions, I would immediately advise: ANAC123, unable one seven zero knots, minimum speed one eight five due wind conditions, request amended speed. I must not comply with an instruction that compromises safety. ATC will accommodate the request and adjust the sequence accordingly. Safety takes priority over sequencing compliance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Speed restrictions are ATC's way of fitting your aircraft into the flow.

Precise compliance protects the spacing that protects other aircraft.

Read back the exact speed. Comply immediately. Hold it precisely.

And if you cannot comply — say so. Immediately and clearly.

The controller needs that information to keep the sequence safe.

Your honesty is as important as your precision.
