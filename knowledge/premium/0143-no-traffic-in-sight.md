# No Traffic in Sight

**Catalog ID:** 0143  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers No Traffic in Sight — the pilot's response when ATC issues a traffic advisory and the pilot is unable to visually acquire the reported traffic.

No Traffic in Sight is an honest report that keeps ATC in the separation game.

When you cannot see the traffic, you say so. ATC remains responsible. ATC continues the advisory.

In the H130, operating into Florianópolis, Curitiba, or São Paulo, not every traffic call results in a visual. Low visibility, cloud, terrain, or workload can prevent acquisition.

Knowing how to report — and what happens next — is as important as the Traffic in Sight lesson.

## 📖 Meaning

No Traffic in Sight is the pilot's verbal report that the traffic described in an ATC advisory has not been visually acquired, directing ATC to maintain the advisory and continue providing separation service.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Tráfego não localizado visualmente.

Informe verbal do piloto de que o tráfego descrito em um aviso ATC não foi adquirido visualmente, indicando ao controlador que mantenha o aviso e continue fornecendo serviço de separação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When No Traffic in Sight is reported, the pilot should:

- continue scanning for the traffic
- maintain safe altitude and routing
- expect ATC to continue or update the advisory
- report Traffic in Sight if subsequently acquired
- advise ATC of any evasive action taken if required

No Traffic in Sight is appropriate when:

- traffic is obscured by cloud or haze
- traffic is hidden by terrain or structure
- workload prevents a thorough visual scan
- the traffic description does not match any visible target
- visibility is reduced to the point where acquisition is unlikely

**Important:** No Traffic in Sight is not a problem. It is an honest report. ATC is equipped to provide separation without pilot visual confirmation. Your honest response is what allows ATC to continue protecting you. A false Traffic in Sight report is far more dangerous than an honest No Traffic in Sight.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

When a pilot reports No Traffic in Sight, ATC:

- maintains radar separation and continues the advisory
- may issue a revised traffic description or updated position
- does not delegate visual separation to the pilot
- may issue instructions to avoid the traffic area
- continues monitoring the situation until separation is assured

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on a visual approach to Florianópolis runway three two in your H130, broken cloud layer below you.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, traffic, nine o'clock, two miles, northbound, altitude two thousand five hundred, unknown type.

You scan nine o'clock. Cloud layer obscures the lower airspace. You see nothing.

You transmit:

No traffic in sight, ANAC123.

Approach responds:

ANAC123, roger, that traffic now turning away, no factor, continue approach.

Your honest report kept ATC responsible.

They tracked it. They assessed it. They resolved it.

All you had to do was look and report honestly.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

No Traffic in Sight is the brave transmission.

Many pilots hesitate to say it because they feel it sounds incompetent.

They scan for two seconds. See nothing. Say Traffic in Sight anyway.

That is the wrong choice.

When you say No Traffic in Sight, you are telling ATC: I looked. I did not find it. You are still responsible.

That is professional aviation.

When you say Traffic in Sight falsely, you are telling ATC: I've got it. Stand down.

And ATC does.

And you do not have it.

And the separation gap closes.

In the H130 around Florianópolis or Congonhas, cloud layers at two thousand to four thousand feet are common. Traffic below cloud is frequently invisible from above.

Scan thoroughly. Report honestly. Trust ATC to handle what you cannot see.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What should you say if ATC issues a traffic advisory and you cannot see the traffic?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report honestly: No traffic in sight, ANAC123. This tells ATC I have scanned for the traffic but have not acquired it visually. ATC then retains separation responsibility and continues the advisory. I continue scanning. If I acquire the traffic later, I report: Traffic in sight, ANAC123. I would never report traffic in sight if I have not actually seen it, as a false report would remove ATC separation protection.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "Traffic in sight" when the traffic has not been visually acquired.  
  ✔ Report honestly. No traffic in sight, ANAC123 keeps ATC responsible.

- ❌ Not continuing to scan after the initial report.  
  ✔ Continue scanning. If traffic is subsequently acquired, report: Traffic in sight, ANAC123.

- ❌ Remaining silent when the traffic advisory is not responded to.  
  ✔ Always respond to a traffic advisory. If you cannot see it, say so.

- ❌ Saying "Negative" alone without the full phrase.  
  ✔ Use: No traffic in sight, ANAC123 — or: Negative contact, ANAC123. Both are acceptable but include your callsign.

**Word Stress**

- No → NOH (firm and clear)
- Traffic → TRAF-ik
- Sight → SYTE
- Callsign → each element clear

Practice:

Together:

No traffic in sight, ANAC123.

Note: Speak with confidence. This is not an apology. It is an accurate operational report.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are descending through four thousand feet toward Curitiba in your H130. There is broken cloud at three thousand five hundred feet. Approach calls: "ANAC123, traffic, two o'clock, four miles, northbound, altitude three thousand, unknown type."

**Your first task**

You scan but the cloud layer obscures the lower airspace. What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

Approach then advises: "ANAC123, that traffic is now turning east, no factor." You break out of cloud at three thousand and see a light aircraft to your east. What do you report?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

With no visual acquisition I transmit: No traffic in sight, ANAC123. After breaking cloud and visually acquiring the traffic, even though Approach said no factor, I report: Traffic in sight, ANAC123 — to close the advisory and confirm my awareness. This keeps the situational picture accurate for both myself and ATC.
