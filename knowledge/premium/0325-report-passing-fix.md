# Report Passing Fix

**Catalog ID:** 0325  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Report Passing Fix — the pilot report that advises ATC when the aircraft is passing a named waypoint, navigation aid, or reporting point.

Report Passing Fix is classic position awareness. Even with radar and ADS-B, controllers still request fix reports to confirm route progress, estimate timing, and manage traffic where surveillance is limited or procedures require compulsory reports.

For H130 coastal and IFR routes in Brazil — Florianópolis, Navegantes, mountain transitions, and offshore corridors — passing-fix reports keep ATC synchronized with your actual progress along the cleared route.

Understanding how to report passing a fix in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Report Passing Fix instructs the pilot to advise ATC when the aircraft is passing a specified waypoint, VOR, NDB, RNAV fix, or published reporting point along the route or procedure.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Reporte passando o fixo / Report Passing Fix.

Instrução para o piloto informar ao controle quando a aeronave estiver passando por um waypoint, VOR, NDB, fixo RNAV ou ponto de reporte publicado ao longo da rota ou do procedimento.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Report Passing Fix, the crew should:

- identify the named fix on the route or procedure
- monitor navigation to the fix
- report promptly when passing abeam or overhead the fix as applicable
- include the fix name, altitude, and estimate to the next fix when required
- continue the cleared route without waiting for acknowledgment unless instructed

Report Passing Fix commonly includes:

- passing REKPA, flight level nine zero
- overhead AFA VOR, maintaining four thousand
- passing the fix, estimating next waypoint
- compulsory reporting points on IFR routes

**Important:** Passing means you are at the fix now — not ten miles before. In the H130 with GPS, the fix can pass quickly at cruise speed. Arm the report early in your scan so you transmit on time, not after ATC asks again.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers work with Report Passing Fix to:

- confirm progress along the cleared route
- update estimates for sequencing and separation
- maintain situational awareness where radar is limited
- trigger next clearances linked to route position
- coordinate sector handoffs at known points

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are IFR along the coast toward Florianópolis in the H130 at flight level nine zero.

Radar says:

Radar: ANAC123, report passing REKPA.

You monitor the FMS. Approaching REKPA, you prepare the call.

At the fix:

ANAC123, passing REKPA, flight level nine zero.

Radar updates your progress and may clear the next step or transfer you to the next sector. The fix report kept the traffic picture current.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

A fix report is a timestamp on your route.

ATC is not asking for a story. They want the point, the level, and sometimes the next estimate.

Brazilian IFR routes still use named fixes that matter for separation and sector boundaries. Missing a compulsory report makes you invisible in the procedural sense — even if the radar paint looks fine.

In the H130, workload can spike near the coast with weather and traffic. Build the habit: when ATC says report passing, put that fix at the top of your next-call list.

Say the fix name clearly. Brazilian and international fix names are easy to garble. Slow the name; keep the rest crisp.

Passing the fix is navigation discipline made audible.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC asks you to report passing a waypoint. What information should you include in a good passing-fix report?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I should include the aircraft identification, the fix name, and usually the altitude or flight level. If ATC requests an estimate, I should also give the estimated time to the next significant point. The report should be made when actually passing the fix, not long before.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting the fix ten miles early.  
  ✔ Report when passing the fix.

- ❌ Omitting altitude when it helps ATC.  
  ✔ Include flight level or altitude with the fix name.

- ❌ Mispronouncing or inventing the fix name.  
  ✔ Use the published name clearly and slowly if needed.

- ❌ Waiting for ATC to ask a second time.  
  ✔ If instructed to report passing, transmit promptly at the fix.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Passing REKPA, Flight Level Nine Zero

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, PASS-ing REK-pah, FLITE LEV-ul NINE ZEER-oh

**Word Stress**

- Passing → PASS-ing
- Fix name → clear syllables, no rush
- Flight Level → FLITE LEV-ul

Practice:

Passing REKPA... flight level nine zero.

Together:

ANAC123, passing REKPA, flight level nine zero.

## 🧠 Memory Trick
