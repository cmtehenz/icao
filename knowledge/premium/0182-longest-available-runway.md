# Longest Available Runway

**Catalog ID:** 0182  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Longest Available Runway — the pilot request or ATC statement identifying the longest runway in service at an aerodrome, typically requested when maximum runway length is needed for safety, performance, or abnormal operations.

Requesting the longest available runway is not a sign of poor planning. It is professional risk management.

A pilot may request the longest runway for landing overweight, with reduced braking effectiveness, in degraded braking conditions, with a system abnormality, or when performance calculations require the additional margin.

For the H130 helicopter, runway length is rarely a limitation in normal operations. But in abnormal conditions — hydraulic issues reducing braking, or a precautionary landing at an unfamiliar aerodrome — knowing how to request and receive the longest available runway is part of the professional toolkit.

## 📖 Meaning

Longest Available Runway is the pilot's request or ATC's advisory identifying the longest runway currently available and in service at an aerodrome, provided to maximize the ground roll distance available for landing or takeoff when performance, weight, system status, or safety considerations require maximum runway length.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pista mais longa disponível.

Pedido do piloto ou informação do controle identificando a pista mais longa atualmente disponível e em operação em um aeródromo, fornecida para maximizar a distância de rolagem disponível para pouso ou decolagem quando considerações de desempenho, peso, status de sistemas ou segurança exigem o comprimento máximo de pista.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When requesting the longest available runway, the crew should:

- state the reason if it involves a declared emergency or abnormal condition
- confirm the runway available length meets minimum performance requirements
- advise ATC of any implications for the approach: ILS available, visual only, RNAV
- cross-check the longest available runway against applicable approach procedures
- brief the approach and landing performance using the specific runway length

Longest available runway is requested in contexts including:

- overweight landing requiring maximum stopping distance
- braking system degradation or antiskid failure
- contaminated runway (wet, standing water) requiring additional margin
- turbine engine failure requiring maximum ground roll for taxi-in
- precautionary landing with unknown performance factor
- practice maximum performance landing during training

**Important:** ATC will assign the longest available runway if operationally feasible, but traffic flow, active runway configuration, and other aircraft may not permit it. Be prepared to state the urgency if a specific runway is critical for safety.

## 🎯 Why ATC Assists

Controllers accommodate longest available runway requests to:

- support aircraft performance limitations or system abnormalities
- facilitate emergency or precautionary landing requests
- reduce the risk of runway excursion in degraded conditions
- coordinate traffic to allow use of the longer runway
- provide a safety buffer beyond the minimum required landing distance

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Curitiba in your H130 following a hydraulic caution annunciation. Your braking effectiveness is uncertain. Landing performance requires maximum stopping distance.

You call Approach:

ANAC123, inbound to Curitiba, hydraulic caution, request longest available runway for precautionary landing.

Approach responds:

ANAC123, longest available runway is one five, three thousand meters, ILS available, expect vectors ILS runway one five, advise souls on board and fuel.

You respond:

ANAC123, ILS runway one five, three thousand meters, two souls on board, fuel forty minutes, ANAC123.

Approach sequences you for runway one five. You configure full flaps, plan a firm stable approach, use all available braking, and stop well within the runway length.

Hydraulic check after landing reveals a minor leak — manageable, but the conservative decision was correct.

1. Approach: ANAC123, longest available runway is three two, two thousand eight hundred meters, ILS available.
2. Tower: ANAC123, runway one five approved, three thousand meters, wind one eight zero at ten.

4. Tower: ANAC123, runway one five cleared to land, advise full stop or touch-and-go.
5. Ground: ANAC123, runway one five, three thousand meters, vacate at Delta if able.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Asking for the longest runway is professional judgment, not weakness.

When your aircraft has a system abnormality — hydraulic, braking, weight — the stopping distance calculation changes. More runway means more margin. More margin means safer outcome.

Controllers cannot give you what you do not ask for.

Brazilian airports have runways of varying lengths. Congonhas is shorter than Curitiba. Florianópolis is longer than Navegantes. You must know the lengths at your planned destination and any likely alternate.

In the H130, hydraulic issues can affect tail rotor authority and rotor braking systems. A precautionary landing with reduced hydraulic pressure is an abnormal condition. Request the longest available runway, brief the worst-case stopping performance, and be ready to declare an emergency if conditions worsen on approach.

The question is not whether to ask. The question is whether you asked early enough.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Describe when you would request the longest available runway and how you would phrase the request to ATC.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would request the longest available runway whenever performance, system status, or safety considerations require additional stopping distance beyond the minimum. Common situations include landing overweight, braking system degradation, wet or contaminated runway with performance concern, or hydraulic issue affecting braking. My phrasing: ANAC123, request longest available runway — and if an abnormal condition is involved, I would state the reason: ANAC123, hydraulic caution, request longest available runway, precautionary landing. I would advise ATC of souls on board and fuel when requested. If the longest available runway is critical for safety and an emergency exists, I would declare: ANAC123, Mayday, braking failure, request longest available runway.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Not requesting the longest runway when system abnormalities affect stopping performance.  
  ✔ Any abnormal condition affecting braking is reason to request the longest runway.

- ❌ Requesting a longer runway without stating the reason.  
  ✔ State the reason: hydraulic caution, overweight, braking issue — ATC needs context to prioritize.

- ❌ Accepting a shorter runway without confirming landing performance.  
  ✔ If the assigned runway does not meet performance requirements, advise ATC and request the alternative.

- ❌ Not briefing the landing with the specific available runway length.  
  ✔ Use the actual available runway length in your landing performance calculation.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are inbound to Florianópolis in your H130 when an antiskid INOP annunciation appears. Braking is available but degraded — manual braking only, no antiskid assist.

**Your first task**

What do you request from ATC and how do you phrase it?

**Your second task**

ATC advises the longest available runway is runway one four, two thousand two hundred meters, but current traffic requires runway three two, one thousand eight hundred meters. Landing performance requires a minimum of two thousand meters. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, antiskid inoperative, request longest available runway for precautionary landing, advise runway length available. Second task: ANAC123, unable runway three two, performance requires minimum two thousand meters, antiskid inoperative, request runway one four. If ATC cannot immediately comply, I would advise: ANAC123, prepared to hold for runway one four if required — safety consideration. If unable to obtain runway one four at all, I would evaluate diverting to an alternate airport with longer runways.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

The longest available runway is an asset you should know about before you need it.

Brief destination runway lengths during pre-flight. Know the longest. Know the shortest. Know the approach available for each.

When conditions change — as they do — you will know your options before the pressure is on.

In abnormal conditions, ask early. ATC needs time to sequence traffic, coordinate runways, and prepare the field.

A late request may leave no option but the shorter runway.

Early request. Clear communication. Firm decision. Safe landing.

That sequence works every time.
