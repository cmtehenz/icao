# Cargo Smoke

**Catalog ID:** 0244  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Cargo Smoke — smoke or fire warning associated with a cargo compartment, requiring immediate emergency checklist action, a clear distress call, and priority landing. Even when flying an H130 without a transport-category cargo hold, ICAO exam English still expects you to handle cargo smoke phraseology professionally.

Cargo smoke on airliners is a time-critical fire event: suppress if equipped, land immediately, and communicate nature, location, and intentions. For helicopter academy pilots, treat this as essential Part 2/Part 3 vocabulary, and relate it to more common H130 realities — cabin baggage fire, external load issues, or baggage compartment smoke — using the same radio structure.

Brazilian ops language must stay ICAO-standard: Mayday, cargo smoke, request immediate vectors, souls, fuel.

## 📖 Meaning

Cargo Smoke is the detection of smoke or fire indications in a cargo compartment, indicating possible combustion of cargo or compartment systems, and requiring immediate emergency procedures, possible fire suppression, declaration of distress, and landing at the nearest suitable airport.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Fumaça no porão de carga / Cargo Smoke.

Detecção de indicações de fumaça ou fogo em um compartimento de carga, indicando possível combustão da carga ou de sistemas do compartimento, e exigindo procedimentos de emergência imediatos, possível extinção de fogo, declaração de emergência e pouso no aeroporto adequado mais próximo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When cargo smoke is indicated, the crew should:

- apply the AFM cargo smoke or fire checklist immediately
- discharge cargo fire suppression if the aircraft is so equipped
- declare Mayday — treat as a fire emergency until proven otherwise
- request immediate vectors to the nearest suitable airport and land
- report nature (cargo smoke), location (cargo compartment), intentions; souls/fuel when asked
- for H130 context: apply the same structure to baggage/cabin cargo fire if that is the actual aircraft configuration

Cargo smoke reporting commonly includes:

- cargo smoke warning / smoke in cargo compartment
- fire suppression discharged (if applicable)
- request immediate landing
- souls on board and fuel remaining
- evacuate after landing as required

**Important:** Do not debate whether the warning is real while remaining at cruise. Checklist, Mayday, land. On the ICAO exam, use precise "cargo smoke" language even if your daily H130 has only a small baggage area — then add a helicopter-relevant note if asked about your actual aircraft.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to cargo smoke reports to:

- clear the shortest path to land with absolute priority
- alert airport fire and rescue for a possible aircraft fire on arrival
- request souls, fuel, and dangerous goods information if relevant
- coordinate runway and taxiway clearance for emergency evacuation
- obtain updates on smoke or fire status

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Training scenario for exam English: you are in cruise when a cargo smoke warning triggers. You run the checklist and discharge suppression as equipped.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Control, ANAC123, cargo smoke, request immediate vectors to nearest suitable airport to land.

Control: ANAC123, turn left heading two seven zero, descend, nearest airport Guarulhos, say souls, fuel, and dangerous goods if any.

You provide the data and continue:

ANAC123, cargo fire suppression discharged, continuing Mayday, request shortest approach.

For an H130 with smoke from baggage in the cabin, the structure is identical — change location words, keep the urgency and landing request.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger Mayday cargo smoke, turn left heading two seven zero, descend immediately.
2. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board, fuel remaining, and dangerous goods.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway cleared, fire services standing by for aircraft fire.
4. Approach: ANAC123, report if smoke or fire indications continue.
5. Tower: ANAC123, after landing stop on the runway if required, evacuate when able.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Mayday Mayday Mayday, Control, ANAC123, cargo smoke, request immediate vectors to land.
2. ANAC123, souls one five zero, fuel two hours, no dangerous goods.
3. ANAC123, cargo suppression discharged, smoke warning continues, request shortest approach.
4. ANAC123, cleared to land, will stop on the runway for emergency services.
5. ANAC123, evacuating after landing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Cargo smoke is a fire until you are on the ground and rescue says otherwise.

The exam wants the template: Mayday, cargo smoke, immediate landing, souls and fuel. Learn it cold.

Then translate it to the H130 you actually fly. You may not have a Class C cargo hold. You may have cabin baggage, a baggage compartment, or an external load. If that burns or smokes, the English structure does not change — only the location phrase does: smoke in the cabin baggage area, request immediate landing.

Never sound academic while the aircraft is on fire. Sound clear. Nature. Location. Intentions. Request.

Brazilian controllers will mobilize fire services fast when they hear cargo smoke or aircraft fire. Give them souls and fuel. After landing, follow evacuation discipline.

Master the airliner phrase for the test. Master the cabin/baggage version for the helicopter. Same professionalism either way.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

A cargo smoke warning activates in flight. What do you do and what do you transmit?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would immediately apply the cargo smoke checklist, discharge fire suppression if equipped, and declare Mayday. I would report cargo smoke and request immediate vectors to the nearest suitable airport. When asked, I would provide souls on board, fuel remaining, and dangerous goods information if any. I would land as soon as possible and prepare for emergency evacuation. On a helicopter without a cargo hold, I would use the same structure for baggage or cabin cargo smoke.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating a cargo smoke warning as a possible false alarm at cruise.  
  ✔ Checklist and land — treat as fire until confirmed otherwise on the ground.

- ❌ Omitting location: saying only "we have a warning."  
  ✔ Say cargo smoke / smoke in cargo compartment.

- ❌ Forgetting dangerous goods when ATC asks.  
  ✔ Know if DG is aboard; answer clearly yes/no and details if required.

- ❌ Using only airline cargo language with no helicopter transfer skill.  
  ✔ Also practice cabin baggage smoke calls for H130 relevance.

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, KAR-go SMOHK, reh-KWEST ih-MEE-dee-ut VEK-terz too LAND

**Word Stress**

- Cargo → KAR-go
- Smoke → SMOHK
- Immediate → ih-MEE-dee-ut
- Vectors → VEK-terz

Practice:

Cargo smoke... request immediate vectors... nearest suitable airport.

Together:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, cargo smoke, request immediate vectors to land.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Cargo smoke warning in cruise. Suppression available on the exam scenario aircraft.

**Your first task**

Give your Mayday call.

**Your second task**

ATC asks souls, fuel, and dangerous goods. Warning remains after suppression. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday Mayday Mayday, Control, ANAC123, cargo smoke, request immediate vectors to nearest suitable airport. Second task: ANAC123, souls one five zero, fuel two hours, no dangerous goods, cargo suppression discharged, warning continues, request shortest landing. I would not stay at altitude to troubleshoot beyond the checklist, and on an H130 I would apply the same urgency to cabin baggage smoke with adjusted location wording.
