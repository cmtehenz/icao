# Need Assistance

**Catalog ID:** 0313  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Need Assistance — the pilot statement that help is required from ATC, aerodrome services, or emergency responders, ranging from non-urgent support to emergency handling. Core Part 2 and Part 3 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: declare the need early, state the nature of assistance, and provide fuel and souls when relevant.

Need assistance is Critical priority because delayed requests cost options — runway preference, rescue readiness, and traffic priority.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for assistance requests under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including pan-pan and mayday when the situation meets those definitions.

Understanding how to request assistance in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Need Assistance is a pilot declaration or ATC confirmation that the aircraft requires help — such as priority handling, emergency services, medical support, or technical ground assistance — including a brief description of the nature of the assistance required and any related fuel or persons-on-board information.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Necessito assistência / Need Assistance.

Declaração do piloto ou confirmação do ATC de que a aeronave precisa de ajuda — como prioridade, serviços de emergência, apoio médico ou assistência técnica no solo — incluindo uma breve descrição da natureza da assistência e informações relacionadas de combustível ou pessoas a bordo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When you need assistance, the crew should:

- state need assistance and the nature of the problem briefly
- declare pan-pan or mayday when urgency or distress definitions are met
- report souls on board and fuel remaining when the situation is abnormal or emergency
- request specific help: priority landing, fire services, medical, technical
- for H130: include landing site constraints if diverting to a heliport or confined area
- update ATC if the level of assistance required increases

Assistance exchanges commonly include:

- request assistance / need assistance
- request priority landing
- request emergency services / ambulance / fire
- pan-pan / mayday
- souls on board and fuel remaining

**Important:** "Need assistance" is not a substitute for mayday when distress exists. Match the phrase to the severity.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers handle assistance requests to:

- mobilize the correct ground and emergency services
- provide priority and traffic separation for the affected aircraft
- coordinate diversion aerodromes and medical support
- gather fuel and souls data for rescue planning
- escalate from normal handling to emergency phase as required

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 with a passenger medical problem inbound to Congonhas.

You transmit:

ANAC123, request assistance upon arrival, medical, one passenger ill, souls on board four, fuel remaining zero plus five zero.

If the situation is urgent but not distress:

ANAC123, pan-pan, pan-pan, pan-pan, request priority landing, medical assistance.

Approach clears you with priority and alerts tower and medical services. The key is early, specific, and complete — nature of help, people, fuel.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, confirm you need assistance.
2. Approach: ANAC123, say nature of assistance required.
3. Tower: ANAC123, assistance arranged, confirm souls on board.
4. Control: ANAC123, request confirmed, emergency services advised.
5. Approach: ANAC123, confirm priority required.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, affirmative, need assistance, medical.
2. ANAC123, request assistance, technical problem, request priority.
3. ANAC123, pan-pan, pan-pan, pan-pan, request priority landing.
4. ANAC123, need assistance upon arrival, fire services standby.
5. ANAC123, souls on board four, fuel remaining zero plus four zero, request assistance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Assistance language must be specific.

Need assistance — for what? Medical, technical, fire standby, priority landing. Vague requests waste the first minute of the ground response.

In the H130, cabin and performance problems escalate quickly. Speak early. Add souls and fuel. If distress criteria are met, use mayday. If urgency without immediate danger to the aircraft, pan-pan may fit. Part 3 is about matching words to reality.

Do not wait for a perfect diagnosis before asking for help.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

A passenger becomes seriously ill in flight and you need priority and medical help. How do you request assistance?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare the appropriate urgency phrase if required, request priority landing, and state that I need medical assistance upon arrival. I would report souls on board and fuel remaining, and briefly describe the problem. I would keep updates short as the situation changes and follow ATC instructions for routing and approach.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "problem on board" without the type of help needed.  
  ✔ State medical, technical, fire, or priority as applicable.

- ❌ Delaying the request until short final.  
  ✔ Request early so services can prepare.

- ❌ Using mayday for a minor non-urgent request.  
  ✔ Match mayday, pan-pan, or need assistance to severity.

- ❌ Forgetting souls on board and fuel remaining.  
  ✔ Include both in abnormal and emergency assistance calls.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

A passenger is seriously ill. You need medical help and priority into Congonhas.

**Your first task**

What is your initial assistance request?

**Your second task**

Approach asks you to confirm souls on board and fuel remaining. What do you add?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, request assistance, medical, request priority landing — use pan-pan if urgency criteria are met. Second task: ANAC123, souls on board four, fuel remaining zero plus five zero — or the actual figures. Be specific about medical assistance and complete the standard emergency information.
