# Loose Animal on Board

**Catalog ID:** 0335  
**Category:** Passenger  
**Priority:** Low  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Loose Animal on Board — a live animal that is free in the cabin or cockpit area instead of secured in a carrier, crate, or approved restraint, creating distraction and possible interference with flight controls.

Loose Animal on Board is Low catalog priority but real operational risk in small aircraft. ICAO exam English may reference a loose dog or other animal. Use the general phrase loose animal on board, then specify if needed.

For H130 operations, a loose animal is especially dangerous: confined cabin, exposed controls, and no cabin crew to catch the animal. Secure pets before start. If one gets loose in flight, fly first, contain second, tell ATC only if the situation affects safety or requires assistance on landing.

Understanding how to communicate a loose animal event in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Loose Animal on Board means a live animal is unrestrained in the aircraft, posing a risk of distraction, injury, or interference with flight controls, and may require crew action and ATC notification if safety is affected.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When an animal becomes loose on board, the crew should:

- maintain aircraft control as the absolute priority
- avoid sudden maneuvers that worsen the situation unless safety requires
- have a passenger attempt to secure the animal if it can be done without entering the control area
- land as soon as practical if the animal threatens the controls
- advise ATC if the situation affects safety or if assistance is needed after landing

Loose Animal communication commonly includes:

- loose animal on board, request priority landing if controls threatened
- dog loose in cabin, requesting assistance on arrival
- animal secured, continuing approach
- security or handling assistance for animal on arrival if needed

**Important:** Low priority means many events are handled without Pan Pan — if the animal is quickly secured. On the H130, a loose animal near the cyclic is not low risk. Prevent it with carriers and briefing. If prevention fails and controls are threatened, treat it as a safety urgency.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to loose animal reports to:

- understand an unusual safety distraction on board
- provide priority handling if the pilot requests it
- arrange assistance or isolation on the apron if needed
- avoid surprise when an animal exits the aircraft after landing
- coordinate with handling if the animal affects disembarkation

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising toward Florianópolis in the H130 with a passenger and a dog that was supposed to remain in a carrier.

The carrier latch fails. The dog runs forward toward the front seats.

You keep a stable cruise, tell the passenger to secure the dog immediately, and prevent anyone from blocking your controls.

If the dog is secured quickly, you may continue and advise Ground on arrival if needed. If the dog keeps interfering, you transmit:

Approach, ANAC123, loose animal on board interfering with crew, request priority landing.

You land, shut down, and contain the animal before passenger disembarkation.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger, confirm if priority landing is required.
2. Approach: ANAC123, say nature of problem with the animal.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, advise if assistance needed on the apron.
4. Ground: ANAC123, roger loose animal, handling can assist after shutdown.
5. Approach: ANAC123, priority approach approved if required.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, loose animal on board, request priority landing.
2. ANAC123, dog loose in cabin, animal now being secured.
3. ANAC123, loose animal secured, continuing approach, no priority required.
4. Pan Pan Pan, ANAC123, loose animal interfering with controls, request immediate landing.
5. ANAC123, loose animal on board, request assistance on apron after landing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Loose animal is preventable English — and emergency English when prevention fails.

Exam sources may say loose dog on board. The general professional label is loose animal on board. Add species only if useful.

Most of the time, the lesson is CRM and cabin discipline: carriers closed, briefed, verified before lift-off.

In the H130, there is nowhere for a panicked animal to go except toward your hands and feet. That is why Low catalog priority can become High real risk in seconds.

Do not chase the animal while hand-flying alone. Stabilize. Assign the passenger. Divert if needed.

Tell ATC when safety is affected. Otherwise secure it and continue.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

When should you advise ATC about a loose animal on board?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I should advise ATC if the animal affects flight safety, interferes with the crew or controls, or if I need priority landing or assistance on arrival. If the animal is quickly secured and safety is not affected, I may continue without an urgency call and handle it after landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Declaring Mayday for a briefly loose pet that is immediately secured.  
  ✔ Match urgency to actual safety impact.

- ❌ Saying only "dog problem" with no clear status.  
  ✔ Say loose animal on board, then secured or request priority.

- ❌ Pilot leaving the controls to catch the animal in single-pilot flight.  
  ✔ Fly first; use a passenger to secure the animal.

- ❌ Opening doors to release an animal with rotors turning.  
  ✔ Shut down or establish a safe condition before any apron release.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

A dog escapes its carrier in cruise and runs near the front seats. Your passenger is trying to catch it. Controls are not yet touched.

**Your first task**

What do you do first, and what might you say if it continues toward the controls?

**Your second task**

The dog is secured one minute later. What do you tell ATC if you already called for priority?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: I maintain control of the helicopter and direct the passenger to secure the animal without entering the control area. If it threatens the controls: ANAC123, loose animal on board interfering with crew, request priority landing. Second task: ANAC123, loose animal secured, cancel priority if no longer required, continuing approach — or continue priority if still unsettled and I prefer to land promptly.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Loose Animal on Board is a small phrase for a big distraction hazard.

Prevent it. Brief it. Secure the carrier.

If it happens in the H130, fly first, contain second, speak if safety needs ATC.

Low priority on the catalog. High respect in the cockpit.
