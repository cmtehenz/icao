# Steep Turn

**Catalog ID:** 0431  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Steep Turn — a level turn at a high bank angle used for maneuvering, traffic avoidance, or training, requiring precise altitude, speed, and coordination control.

Steep turns appear in H130 training, confined-area repositioning, and traffic avoidance near busy Brazilian heliports and airports.

You must describe the maneuver and related traffic calls in ICAO English for Parts 2 and 4.

## 📖 Meaning

Steep Turn is a helicopter turn flown at a bank angle significantly greater than a normal turn — commonly around thirty degrees or more in training standards — while maintaining altitude and airspeed through coordinated cyclic, collective, and anti-torque inputs.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Curva íngreme / steep turn.

Curva de helicóptero voada com ângulo de inclinação significativamente maior que o de uma curva normal — comumente cerca de trinta graus ou mais nos padrões de treinamento — mantendo altitude e velocidade por meio de entradas coordenadas de cíclico, coletivo e antitorque.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When performing a steep turn, the pilot should:

- clear the area visually before rolling in
- set bank, maintain altitude with collective as required, and keep balanced flight
- avoid excessive bank that risks loss of control or overstress
- roll out on heading with altitude and speed under control
- use steep turns for traffic avoidance only when necessary and safe

Steep turn related reporting commonly includes:

- turning left / right to avoid traffic
- maneuvering, remaining clear
- unable to accept heading due to traffic, turning to avoid
- resuming previous heading / routing

**Important:** Steep turns increase load factor and power required. In the H130, brief training limits. In operations, prefer standard rate or moderate bank unless traffic or terrain demands more.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers interact with steep-turn situations when:

- issuing traffic advisories that require immediate maneuvering
- sequencing helicopters in tight visual patterns
- understanding why a helicopter turns off an assigned heading
- coordinating visual separation near the field
- assessing pilot reports after avoidance maneuvers

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on a visual approach in your H130. Tower calls traffic opposite direction.

You see the traffic late and need a prompt turn to remain clear.

You transmit:

Turning left to avoid traffic, remaining clear, will resume approach when clear.

After the traffic passes, you roll out and continue as cleared.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, traffic twelve o'clock opposite direction, advise in sight.
2. Tower: ANAC123, turn left immediately, traffic alert.
3. Tower: ANAC123, roger turning to avoid, report clear of traffic.
4. Tower: ANAC123, resume own navigation, report field in sight.
5. Tower: ANAC123, continue approach, traffic no longer a factor.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, traffic in sight — or looking.
2. ANAC123, turning left immediately, traffic in sight.
3. ANAC123, clear of traffic, resuming approach.
4. ANAC123, resuming own navigation, will report field in sight.
5. ANAC123, continuing approach, traffic no factor.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Steep turn is a tool. Traffic avoidance is the reason.

Clear first. Bank second. Altitude always. In the H130, a steep turn done poorly loses height into obstacles — especially near city helipads.

Part 4 loves: traffic alert, what do you do? Answer: look, turn as required to remain clear, report turning to avoid traffic, then resume when clear.

Do not overbank into a training demonstration when a moderate turn would do. Save steep bank for when geometry demands it — and when you have altitude and power to spare.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Tower issues a traffic alert. You must turn promptly. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maneuver as required to remain clear and transmit: turning left to avoid traffic, remaining clear. When clear, I would report: clear of traffic, resuming approach — or request further instructions. I would not delay the turn to make a long radio call first.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Talking on the radio before starting the avoidance turn.  
  ✔ Aviate first. Call while or after maneuvering safely.

- ❌ Losing altitude in the steep turn near obstacles.  
  ✔ Hold altitude; know your surroundings.

- ❌ Not reporting when clear of traffic.  
  ✔ Advise clear so ATC can resume sequencing.

- ❌ Using extreme bank in the circuit without need.  
  ✔ Match bank to the requirement.

Steep turns are for geometry and survival — not for style.

See traffic. Turn clean. Hold height. Report clear.
