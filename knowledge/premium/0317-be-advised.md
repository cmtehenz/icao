# Be Advised

**Catalog ID:** 0317  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Be Advised — the standard phrase used to pass important information that is not a clearance or instruction, alerting the other station to traffic, weather, airport conditions, or operational status. Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: recognize advisories, acknowledge them, and do not confuse them with clearances.

Be advised is Medium priority as a catalog item and High importance in daily radio work — missed advisories create surprises; misread advisories create unauthorized actions.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for giving and receiving advisories under DECEA/ICAO procedures.

Understanding "be advised" in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Be Advised is a radiotelephony phrase introducing information that the receiving station should know — such as traffic, weather, runway conditions, or status — without by itself constituting a clearance or instruction to act, though it may lead to a subsequent clearance or pilot decision.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Seja informado / Be Advised.

Frase radiotelefônica que introduz informação que a estação receptora deve conhecer — como tráfego, meteorologia, condições de pista ou status — sem por si só constituir uma autorização ou instrução para agir, embora possa levar a uma autorização subsequente ou a uma decisão do piloto.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When you hear or use be advised, the crew should:

- treat the content as information, not automatic authorization to change path or altitude
- acknowledge with roger or a short confirmation when appropriate
- request a clearance if the advisory requires an action that needs ATC approval
- for H130: pay special attention to advisories about rotor wash, pad occupancy, and low-level traffic
- use be advised yourself when passing non-clearance information to ATC
- read back only if the advisory includes or is followed by a clearance element

Be-advised exchanges commonly include:

- be advised, traffic...
- be advised, weather...
- be advised, runway/helipad occupied / wet / closed
- roger, advised
- request vectors / clearance based on advisory

**Important:** "Be advised" informs. "Cleared" and "climb/descend/turn" instruct. Know which one you heard.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use be advised to:

- pass traffic and weather information quickly
- warn of airport or heliport conditions
- share status that affects planning without changing the clearance yet
- prompt pilots to request an amended clearance if needed
- keep situational awareness high on busy Brazilian frequencies

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 inbound to Campo de Marte.

Approach: ANAC123, be advised, traffic helicopter at your twelve o'clock, two miles, opposite direction.

You reply:

ANAC123, roger, looking / traffic in sight.

Later Tower says:

ANAC123, be advised, helipad wet, braking action not reported.

That is information for your landing decision — not a landing clearance by itself. When Tower clears you to land, that is the clearance. The advisory prepared you; it did not replace the clearance.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, be advised, traffic twelve o'clock, two miles.
2. Tower: ANAC123, be advised, helipad occupied, expect delay.
3. Control: ANAC123, be advised, moderate turbulence reported.
4. Approach: ANAC123, be advised, destination weather deteriorating.
5. Ground: ANAC123, be advised, taxiway Alpha closed.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, roger, looking for traffic.
2. ANAC123, roger, advised, helipad occupied.
3. ANAC123, roger, will advise if unable due turbulence.
4. ANAC123, roger, request latest weather.
5. ANAC123, roger, request alternate taxi route.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Be advised is the radio's information channel.

Listen for the facts. Acknowledge. Decide whether you need a new clearance. Do not turn, climb, or land merely because you were advised of something.

In the H130, pad and low-level advisories matter as much as airway weather. Part 2 wants you to show you know the difference between information and instruction.

When you pass information to ATC, be advised is clean and professional — short fact, no speech.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC says "be advised" about traffic and later about a wet helipad. What do those calls mean, and what do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Those calls are information, not clearances. For traffic, I would acknowledge and look for the traffic or request vectors if needed. For a wet helipad, I would factor the condition into the landing decision and still wait for a landing clearance. If the advisory means I need a different route or delay, I would request the appropriate clearance or state intentions.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Treating "be advised" as permission to deviate or land.  
  ✔ Advisories inform; clearances authorize.

- ❌ Giving no acknowledgment to an important advisory.  
  ✔ Roger or a short confirmation keeps ATC in the loop.

- ❌ Reading back an advisory as if it were a full clearance.  
  ✔ Read back clearances; acknowledge information.

- ❌ Ignoring destination weather advisories until too late.  
  ✔ Use the information to plan diversion or delay early.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**ADVISE** — **A**lert with facts, **D**o not treat as clearance, **V**erify if action needs approval, **I**nform ATC with roger, **S**eek amended clearance if required, **E**xecute only when cleared.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Approach advises opposite-direction traffic. Tower later advises the helipad is wet.

**Your first task**

What do you say after the traffic advisory?

**Your second task**

Does the wet-helipad advisory clear you to land? What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, roger, looking — or traffic in sight when acquired. Second task: No — be advised is information only. Acknowledge, for example ANAC123, roger, advised, and wait for landing clearance before landing. Request a different pad or delay if the condition makes landing unsafe.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Be advised is core Part 2 information discipline for Brazilian H130 operations.

Hear the fact. Acknowledge it. Do not invent a clearance inside an advisory.

Information prepares the decision. Clearance authorizes the action. Keep them separate on every frequency.

That is the be advised lesson — know what you were told, and what you were not cleared to do.
