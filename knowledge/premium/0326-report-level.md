# Report Level

**Catalog ID:** 0326  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Report Level — the pilot report that advises ATC that the aircraft is maintaining a specified altitude or flight level, often after reaching it or when confirming current level.

Report Level is altitude state language. Controllers use it to verify you are stable at an assigned level, to confirm level after a frequency change, or to check altitude when traffic or coordination requires absolute clarity.

For H130 operations in Brazilian controlled airspace, level reports appear after climbs and descents, during radar identification, and when ATC says "report level" or "say level." A precise level report prevents altitude busts from becoming communication failures.

Understanding how to report level in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Report Level instructs the pilot to advise ATC of the altitude or flight level currently being maintained, or to confirm when the aircraft is level at an assigned altitude after climb or descent.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Reporte nivelado / Report Level.

Instrução para o piloto informar ao controle a altitude ou o nível de voo que está sendo mantido, ou confirmar quando a aeronave estiver nivelada na altitude atribuída após subida ou descida.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Report Level, the crew should:

- verify the altimeter setting and indicated altitude or flight level
- report the exact level being maintained
- use flight levels above the transition altitude and altitudes below as applicable
- report promptly after leveling if "report level" was part of the clearance
- correct immediately if the reported level does not match the assigned level

Report Level commonly includes:

- level at flight level nine zero
- maintaining four thousand
- level flight level six zero
- say your level / report level for confirmation

**Important:** Level means maintaining — not climbing through, not descending through. If ATC asks for your level while you are still climbing, say climbing through the altitude, not level. Honesty protects separation. In the H130, capture the assigned level, then report level.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers work with Report Level to:

- confirm the aircraft is stable at the cleared altitude
- verify Mode C or surveillance altitude readouts
- coordinate traffic separation vertically
- confirm level after handoff or frequency change
- detect altitude deviations early

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You have just been transferred to Florianópolis Approach in the H130 after descending to four thousand feet.

Approach says:

Approach: ANAC123, radar contact, report level.

You verify the altimeter and reply:

ANAC123, level at four thousand.

Approach now trusts the altitude picture and continues vectors for the approach. A wrong or vague level call would have forced extra questions and delayed sequencing.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, report level.
2. Radar: ANAC123, descend and maintain flight level six zero, report level.
3. Control: ANAC123, say your level.
4. Approach: ANAC123, maintain four thousand, report level.
5. Radar: ANAC123, identify, report level and heading.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC says "report level." You are still descending through five thousand to four thousand. What should you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I should not say I am level at four thousand yet. I should report that I am descending through five thousand for four thousand, or that I will report level when I reach four thousand. I only report level when I am maintaining the assigned altitude.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "level" while still climbing or descending.  
  ✔ Report level only when maintaining the altitude.

- ❌ Mixing flight level and altitude formats incorrectly.  
  ✔ Use flight levels above transition; altitudes below as published locally.

- ❌ Giving an approximate altitude.  
  ✔ Report the exact assigned or indicated level ATC expects.

- ❌ Ignoring a "say your level" request during busy radio.  
  ✔ Answer promptly — altitude confirmation is safety-critical.
