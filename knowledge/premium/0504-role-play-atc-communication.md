# Role Play ATC Communication

**Catalog ID:** 0504  
**Category:** ICAO Speaking  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Role Play ATC Communication — the ICAO oral exam task in which you act as the pilot in a simulated radiotelephony exchange with the examiner playing ATC, demonstrating Part 2 phraseology at Level 4 or above.

Role play is not casual conversation. It is exam-standard radio discipline.

In Part 2, the examiner issues clearances, requests reports, and introduces complications — weather, traffic, frequency changes, unable clearances. You must read back correctly, ask for clarification, and use standard phraseology while remaining intelligible under pressure.

For H130 ICAO Delta crews, this is the critical bridge between classroom English and live Brazilian ATC. ANAC assessment Part 2 lives or dies on role-play performance.

Understanding how to perform ATC role play in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Role Play ATC Communication is the ICAO speaking task type in which the candidate assumes the pilot role in a simulated air traffic control exchange — receiving instructions, reading back clearances, making requests, and handling unexpected changes — so that radiotelephony proficiency can be assessed at Operational Level 4 or higher.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When performing ATC role play, the candidate should:

- use call sign on first contact and as required by local procedure
- read back all safety-critical items: altitude, heading, runway, frequency, squawk
- request clarification immediately if any element is unclear
- keep transmissions short, standard, and paced
- handle "unable" professionally with a reason when needed
- for H130: include helicopter-specific requests when the scenario requires — hover, helipad, confined area
- recover from mistakes with a clean correction, not a long apology

Role-play success depends on:

- correct phraseology over creative English
- complete readbacks
- listening for the full clearance before keying the mic
- managing examiner curveballs: hold, delay, weather deviation, frequency change
- maintaining composure when the scenario escalates

**Important:** In role play, plain English is allowed when standard phraseology does not cover the idea — but do not abandon standard forms for routine clearances. Level 4 means you can do both.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

ATC role play allows examiners to:

- test real radiotelephony under controlled pressure
- verify readback discipline for critical clearances
- introduce non-routine elements safely
- assess interaction when the plan changes
- confirm the candidate can operate in English with ATC, not only describe aviation topics

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Part 2 begins. The examiner is Approach.

Approach: Descend and maintain three thousand, expect ILS runway one four.

You read back fully. Then the examiner adds traffic and a hold. You copy the hold as published, report entering, and stay calm.

Later: weather ahead, request deviation. You make a clear request, accept the new heading, and read back the altitude you must maintain.

The role play ends when the examiner stops — not when you invent an ending. Discipline throughout.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, descend and maintain three thousand, expect ILS runway one four.
2. Radar: ANAC123, turn left heading two seven zero, vector for sequencing.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land runway one four, wind one two zero at eight.
4. Approach: ANAC123, hold as published, expect further clearance at time four five.
5. Radar: ANAC123, advise intentions due weather ahead.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Role play is Part 2. Treat it like a real frequency.

Key the imaginary mic only when you have the message ready. Read back the numbers. Never invent a clearance you were not given.

The most common failure is incomplete readback — missing the runway or the altitude. Examiners notice every miss.

When you do not understand: "Say again" or "Confirm altitude" is Level 4. Silence or guessing is not.

If the examiner speaks faster than you can copy, slow your own rate on the readback. Clarity scores higher than speed.

H130 scenarios may include helipad clearances, hover taxi, or offshore pads. Use the same radio discipline you use on a Congonhas frequency. The aircraft type changes; the standard does not.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

During role play, ATC clears you to descend to an altitude you believe is below your minimum safe altitude. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would not accept an unsafe clearance. I would reply unable with a reason and request an alternative — for example: unable to descend to that altitude due minimum safe altitude, request higher or present position hold. In role play this shows both phraseology and safety judgment. Level 4 includes saying unable clearly when required.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reading back only part of a multi-element clearance.  
  ✔ Read back altitude, heading, runway, and frequency as issued.

- ❌ Using long conversational English for routine clearances.  
  ✔ Prefer standard ICAO phraseology first.

- ❌ Saying "OK" or "copy" instead of a proper readback.  
  ✔ Use full readback with call sign as required.

- ❌ Freezing when the examiner changes the plan.  
  ✔ Acknowledge, clarify if needed, then read back the new instruction.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**ROLEPLAY** — **R**ead back every critical item, **O**wn your call sign, **L**isten to the full clearance, **E**scalate with say again if unclear, **P**hraseology before chat, **L**eave unsafe clearances with unable, **A**dapt when the plan changes, **Y**ield to composure over speed.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Role play: You are inbound to Florianópolis in the H130. Approach issues a descent, then a hold, then asks your intentions due weather.

**Your first task**

Approach says: "ANAC123, descend and maintain four thousand, expect ILS runway one four." What is your readback?

**Your second task**

Approach then says weather is deteriorating and asks intentions. Give a Level 4 role-play response.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, descending four thousand, expect ILS runway one four. Second task: ANAC123, intentions are to continue for ILS one four if weather remains above minima; if not, request hold or diversion — stated clearly, then wait for ATC. Role play rewards complete readbacks and clear intentions without panic speech.
