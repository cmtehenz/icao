# Maintain Own Separation

**Catalog ID:** 0020  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson introduces a phrase that places additional responsibility on the pilot.

Normally, ATC is responsible for separating aircraft operating under its control.

However, in certain situations, ATC may instruct a pilot to:

"Maintain Own Separation."

This means the pilot accepts responsibility for maintaining safe separation from specific traffic while continuing to comply with other ATC instructions.

Understanding exactly when and why this instruction is used is an important operational skill.

## 📖 Meaning

Maintain Own Separation instructs the pilot to assume responsibility for maintaining safe separation from specified traffic or obstacles while continuing the flight under ATC clearance.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Mantenha sua própria separação.

O piloto assume a responsabilidade por manter separação segura em relação ao tráfego ou à aeronave especificada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When instructed to Maintain Own Separation, the pilot becomes responsible for visually maintaining safe separation from the identified aircraft or traffic.

This instruction is commonly associated with:

- visual approaches
- helicopter operations
- formation or special flights
- VFR operations in controlled airspace
- traffic sequencing under visual meteorological conditions (VMC)

Although separation responsibility changes, the pilot must still comply with:

- assigned altitude
- assigned heading
- ATC clearances
- airspace restrictions

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Maintain Own Separation to:

- improve traffic flow
- reduce unnecessary delays
- allow visual sequencing
- increase airport capacity in VMC
- simplify helicopter traffic management
- permit visual operations while maintaining safety

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're approaching Florianópolis Airport in your H130.

Weather conditions are excellent.

You have another helicopter clearly in sight ahead of you.

Approach says:

Approach: ANAC123, traffic twelve o'clock, one mile, same altitude. Maintain own separation and follow the traffic.

You report the traffic in sight.

From that moment, you maintain a safe distance while continuing the approach.

Tower later clears you to land.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, maintain own separation from the preceding helicopter.
2. Approach: ANAC123, traffic in sight, maintain own separation.
3. Tower: ANAC123, maintain own separation and follow the traffic.
4. Approach: ANAC123, maintain own separation during the visual approach.
5. Approach: ANAC123, maintain own separation until established on final.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Traffic in sight, maintaining own separation, ANAC123.
2. Maintaining own separation from the preceding helicopter, ANAC123.
3. Wilco, maintaining own separation, ANAC123.
4. Following the traffic, maintaining own separation, ANAC123.
5. Maintaining own separation until final, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine you're walking through a busy airport terminal.

Normally, security barriers organize everyone's movement.

Now imagine those barriers are removed.

You can still walk safely, but you must pay much closer attention to the people around you.

That's what Maintain Own Separation means.

ATC is no longer providing separation from that specific traffic.

Instead, the controller knows you have visual contact and trusts you to maintain a safe distance.

Professional pilots never become complacent during visual operations.

When separation becomes your responsibility, your situational awareness must increase—not decrease.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

When might ATC ask a pilot to maintain their own separation?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

ATC may issue this instruction during visual operations when the pilot has the other aircraft in sight. It allows the pilot to maintain safe spacing while ATC continues managing the overall traffic flow.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Accepting the instruction without seeing the traffic.  
  ✔ If you do not have the traffic in sight, advise ATC immediately.

- ❌ Assuming ATC is still providing visual separation.  
  ✔ Once accepted, you are responsible for maintaining separation from the specified traffic.

- ❌ Focusing only on the aircraft ahead.  
  ✔ Continue monitoring altitude, heading, airspeed, and other traffic.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Traffic in sight, maintaining own separation, ANAC123.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Imagine riding a bicycle behind a friend.

There are no traffic lights telling you how far to stay back.

You naturally keep a safe distance by watching the rider ahead.

That's exactly what Maintain Own Separation means.

ATC is no longer measuring the distance for you.

You are.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

Approach: ANAC123, traffic eleven o'clock, one mile. Maintain own separation and follow the helicopter.

**Your first task**

Read back the instruction exactly as a professional pilot.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

What should you do if ATC instructs you to maintain your own separation but you cannot see the reported traffic?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

If I cannot see the traffic, I should immediately advise ATC that the traffic is not in sight. I cannot accept responsibility for visual separation until I have positive visual contact with the aircraft.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Today's lesson highlights an important transition of responsibility.

Most of the time, ATC provides separation.

Sometimes, under the right weather conditions and with the traffic clearly in sight, you become responsible for maintaining safe spacing.

Remember these three principles:

- Never accept visual separation unless you have the traffic in sight.
- Continue complying with all other ATC clearances.
- If you lose sight of the traffic, inform ATC immediately.

Great pilots know not only how to fly the aircraft, but also when responsibility shifts from the controller to the cockpit.
