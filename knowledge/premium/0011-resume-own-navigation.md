# Resume Own Navigation

**Catalog ID:** 0011  
**Category:** Navigation  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson explains one of the most important transitions between ATC guidance and pilot navigation.

During radar vectoring, ATC temporarily tells you where to fly.

Eventually, the controller will say:

"Resume Own Navigation."

At that moment, responsibility for lateral navigation returns to the pilot.

Understanding exactly when and how this happens is essential for both real-world operations and the ICAO English test.

## 📖 Meaning

Resume Own Navigation instructs the pilot to stop following ATC vectors and continue navigating according to the flight plan, cleared route, or published procedure.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Retome sua própria navegação.

Volte a seguir sua rota, procedimento ou plano de voo utilizando seus próprios meios de navegação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC says Resume Own Navigation, the aircraft is no longer being radar vectored.

From that moment, the pilot resumes navigating using:

- GPS
- RNAV
- VOR
- Flight Management System (FMS)
- Published SID, STAR or airway
- Cleared route

The pilot must not continue flying the last assigned heading unless it remains consistent with the intended route.

All previously assigned altitude, speed, and frequency restrictions remain in effect unless specifically changed.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Resume Own Navigation to:

- end radar vectoring
- return navigation responsibility to the pilot
- rejoin the planned route
- simplify ATC workload
- position aircraft after weather deviations
- transition from vectoring to published procedures

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're flying your H130 toward Curitiba Airport.

Because of heavy traffic, Approach has been providing radar vectors for the last ten minutes.

You have flown several assigned headings.

Once spacing has been achieved, the controller says:

"ANAC123, resume own navigation direct REKPA."

You immediately stop following the previous heading.

Your GPS already displays REKPA.

You turn toward the waypoint and continue the flight along your cleared route.

Navigation is now your responsibility again.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, resume own navigation.
2. Approach: ANAC123, resume own navigation direct REKPA.
3. Approach: ANAC123, resume own navigation and proceed to AFA VOR.
4. Approach: ANAC123, resume own navigation, cleared direct NITUX.
5. Approach: ANAC123, resume own navigation after the weather deviation.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine you're driving with a police escort through heavy traffic.

For a while, you simply follow the police vehicle.

You don't choose your own turns.

Then the officer reaches the highway entrance and says:

"From here, you're on your own."

You thank the officer and continue using your GPS.

That's exactly what Resume Own Navigation means.

During radar vectors, ATC is your temporary navigator.

Once the controller says Resume Own Navigation, the responsibility returns to you.

Professional pilots immediately understand that the last assigned heading is no longer the primary reference.

The flight plan becomes the reference again.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

When would ATC instruct an aircraft to resume own navigation?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

ATC normally issues Resume Own Navigation after radar vectoring is no longer necessary. The pilot then resumes following the cleared route or flight plan using the aircraft's navigation systems.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing to fly the last assigned heading.  
  Once instructed to resume own navigation, return to the cleared route.

- ❌ Thinking altitude restrictions are cancelled.  
  Only navigation responsibility changes. Altitude and speed restrictions remain unless ATC changes them.

- ❌ Waiting for another heading.  
  Resume navigation immediately unless clarification is required.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Resuming own navigation, ANAC123.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Imagine someone borrowing the steering wheel of your car for a few minutes.

When they give it back, you're driving again.

Radar Vectors are ATC holding the steering wheel.

Resume Own Navigation is ATC handing it back to you.
