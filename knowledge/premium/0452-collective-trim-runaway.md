# Collective Trim Runaway

**Catalog ID:** 0452  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Collective Trim Runaway — uncommanded collective trim motor movement that drives the collective up or down without pilot input, causing sudden power and altitude changes and possible rotor RPM excursions.

Collective trim runaway can produce an uncommanded climb or descent and rapid changes in torque and Nr. The pilot must oppose the motion, isolate the trim, and restore rotor and power control immediately.

For H130 helicopter operations, this is a critical emergency. Declare Mayday, protect Nr, and land as soon as possible with minimal further maneuvering.

Understanding how to report collective trim runaway in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Collective Trim Runaway is an uncommanded operation of the collective trim system that moves the collective lever without pilot input, causing unintended changes in power, altitude, and potentially rotor RPM.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Runaway do trim do coletivo.

Operação não comandada do sistema de trim do coletivo que move a alavanca do coletivo sem entrada do piloto, causando mudanças não intencionais de potência, altitude e, potencialmente, RPM do rotor.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When collective trim runaway occurs, the pilot should:

- physically hold and oppose the collective movement
- isolate collective trim per AFM memory items
- restore Nr and torque to the normal range immediately
- land as soon as possible at a suitable site
- declare Mayday as soon as control actions allow

Collective trim runaway reporting commonly includes:

- collective trim runaway
- uncommanded collective movement, request immediate landing
- rotor RPM fluctuation if relevant
- position and souls on board

**Important:** Protect rotor RPM first. An uncommanded collective can drive Nr out of limits faster than you can explain the failure on frequency.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to collective trim runaway to:

- clear traffic for an immediate landing
- avoid altitude or speed assignments that fight the emergency
- coordinate emergency services
- provide a simple, short path to land
- keep the frequency available for further Mayday updates

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are at one thousand five hundred feet in your H130 near Belo Horizonte.

The collective begins rising uncommanded. Torque increases and the helicopter climbs. You push against the runaway, cut out collective trim, and recover a stable power setting. Nr has fluctuated but is returning to green.

You declare Mayday, report collective trim runaway, and request immediate landing. Approach clears the area. You fly a stable approach with firm collective control and land.

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Mayday, collective trim runaway, say souls on board.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Collective trim runaway attacks power and Nr at the same time.

If the collective runs up, torque and climb appear. If it runs down, you sink and Nr may rise. Your hand on the collective is the primary defense.

On the H130, know the collective trim cut-out by feel. Stop the motor. Then set a known power. Then talk.

Mayday is correct. Even after isolation, treat the collective as suspect and land ASAP. Do not accept a long transit with a trim system that has already runaway.

Tell ATC you need immediate landing and no altitude assignments that require fine collective work you cannot trust.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Collective trim runs away upward. You stop it and recover Nr. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, collective trim runaway, trim isolated, rotor RPM normal, request immediate landing nearest suitable. I would state position and souls on board when able.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Fixating on the radio while Nr is out of limits.  
  ✔ Control collective and Nr first; then Mayday.

- ❌ Calling it only "power problem" without saying collective trim runaway.  
  ✔ Name the failure so the emergency is understood.

- ❌ Continuing the mission after a successful cut-out.  
  ✔ Land as soon as possible for inspection.

- ❌ Accepting a busy visual circuit with many power changes.  
  ✔ Request a simple straight-in approach.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Collective trim runaway is a rotor and power emergency wrapped in a trim failure.

Hold the lever. Cut out. Restore Nr. Mayday. Land.

On the H130, never give a failed collective trim another chance at altitude. End the flight while Nr is still yours to command.
