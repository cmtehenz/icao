# Fire in the Hold

**Catalog ID:** 0276  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Fire in the Hold — fire or confirmed combustion in a cargo hold or baggage compartment, requiring immediate emergency checklist action, distress communication, and priority landing. On transport-category aircraft this is a classic ICAO exam scenario; on the H130 you adapt the same English to baggage or cabin cargo fire.

Fire in the hold is time-critical. Suppress if equipped, land immediately, and tell ATC nature, location, and intentions. Do not stay at cruise debating whether the warning is real.

For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, master the exam phraseology — fire in the hold, cargo fire, request immediate landing — then relate it to the helicopter's actual baggage or cabin cargo configuration when asked about your type.

## 📖 Meaning

Fire in the Hold is an active fire or confirmed fire indication in a cargo hold or baggage compartment, requiring immediate emergency procedures, possible fire suppression, declaration of distress, and landing at the nearest suitable airport with fire services alerted.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Fogo no porão / Fire in the Hold.

Fogo ativo ou indicação confirmada de fogo em um porão de carga ou compartimento de bagagem, exigindo procedimentos de emergência imediatos, possível extinção, declaração de emergência e pouso no aeroporto adequado mais próximo com serviços de combate a incêndio alertados.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When fire in the hold is indicated or confirmed, the crew should:

- apply the AFM cargo / hold fire checklist immediately
- discharge hold or cargo fire suppression if the aircraft is so equipped
- declare Mayday — treat as fire until proven otherwise
- request immediate vectors to the nearest suitable airport and land
- report nature (fire in the hold / cargo fire), intentions, souls and fuel when asked
- for H130: use the same radio structure for baggage or cabin cargo fire; do not invent a jet cargo hold the type does not have

Fire in the hold reporting commonly includes:

- fire in the hold / cargo fire / smoke in cargo compartment
- fire suppression discharged (if applicable)
- request immediate landing and fire services
- souls on board and fuel remaining
- evacuate after landing as required

**Important:** Specify location — hold, cargo, cabin baggage — so ATC alerts the correct ground response. On the ICAO exam, use precise "fire in the hold" language even if your daily H130 has only a small baggage area.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to fire in the hold reports to:

- clear the shortest path to land with absolute priority
- alert airport fire and rescue for a possible aircraft fire on arrival
- request souls, fuel, and dangerous goods information if relevant
- coordinate runway clearance for emergency evacuation
- obtain updates on fire or smoke status

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Training scenario for exam English: you are in cruise when a hold fire warning triggers. You run the checklist and discharge suppression as equipped.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Control, ANAC123, fire in the hold, request immediate vectors to nearest suitable airport to land.

Control: ANAC123, turn left heading two seven zero, descend, nearest airport Guarulhos, say souls, fuel, and dangerous goods if any.

You provide the data and continue:

ANAC123, hold fire suppression discharged, continuing Mayday, request shortest approach.

For an H130 with fire in cabin baggage, keep the same urgency and landing request — change only the location words.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger Mayday fire in the hold, turn left heading two seven zero, descend immediately.
2. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board, fuel remaining, and dangerous goods.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway cleared, fire services standing by for aircraft fire.
4. Approach: ANAC123, report if fire or smoke indications continue.
5. Tower: ANAC123, after landing stop on the runway if required, evacuate when able.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, fire in the hold, request immediate landing.
2. ANAC123, turning left two seven zero, descending, fire in the hold.
3. ANAC123, souls six, fuel one hour thirty, no dangerous goods.
4. ANAC123, fire suppression discharged, continuing Mayday.
5. ANAC123, will stop on the runway, prepare to evacuate, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Fire in the hold is a landing emergency with a systems name.

Checklist. Suppress if equipped. Mayday. Land. Evacuate if needed.

Brazilian pilots often say "fogo no porão." Prefer fire in the hold or cargo fire in ICAO English — then the action: request immediate landing, fire services standing by.

In the H130 you may not have a Class C cargo hold with bottles. That does not excuse weak exam English. Know the calls. Know that ATC needs location, intentions, souls, and fuel.

Never continue to destination with an unresolved hold fire indication. Nearest suitable airport. Now.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

A cargo hold fire warning activates in cruise. What do you do and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would apply the fire checklist immediately, discharge suppression if equipped, declare Mayday stating fire in the hold, and request immediate vectors to the nearest suitable airport. When able I would report souls and fuel, whether suppression was discharged, and whether smoke or fire indications continue. After landing I would stop as required and evacuate if the fire is not confirmed extinguished.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "fogo no porão" without Mayday or landing request.  
  ✔ Mayday, fire in the hold, request immediate landing.

- ❌ Staying at cruise to "confirm" the warning for too long.  
  ✔ Checklist and land — treat as fire until proven otherwise.

- ❌ Not specifying hold versus cabin versus engine.  
  ✔ State location clearly so ATC alerts the right services.

- ❌ Inventing H130 hold-suppression drills the type does not have.  
  ✔ Teach exam English; keep type systems factual.
