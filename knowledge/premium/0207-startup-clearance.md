# Startup Clearance

**Catalog ID:** 0207  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Startup Clearance — ATC authorization to start engines on the apron or parking stand before taxi or hover taxi.

Startup clearance is not a takeoff clearance. It is permission to bring the aircraft to life on the ground under controlled conditions. At many Brazilian airports, especially busy training fields, you must request and receive startup before turning the engine.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, startup clearance is highly relevant on every sortie. The H130 starts on the ramp, builds rotor RPM, completes checks, then requests hover taxi or air taxi to the departure point. Starting without clearance can create jet blast or rotor wash hazards, radio congestion, and apron conflicts.

Understanding how to request, receive, and read back startup clearance in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency and daily academy operations under DECEA procedures.

## 📖 Meaning

Startup clearance is ATC authorization for an aircraft to start engines at the parking position or designated start area, usually issued by Ground or Apron Control before taxi clearance.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Autorização de partida dos motores / startup clearance.

Autorização do controle para a aeronave ligar os motores na posição de estacionamento ou área designada, geralmente emitida pelo Solo ou Apron antes da autorização de táxi.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When requesting and executing startup clearance, the crew should:

- complete pre-start checks and confirm the area is clear of personnel, vehicles, and equipment
- contact Ground or Apron with position, ATIS, and request for startup
- read back the startup clearance and any restrictions exactly
- start engines only after clearance is received
- advise ready for taxi or hover taxi when start checks are complete
- report unable if startup must be delayed for technical or safety reasons

Startup clearance commonly includes:

- cleared for startup
- startup approved, expect departure runway
- startup approved, hold for release
- startup delayed due to traffic or flow control
- contact Ground when ready for taxi after start

**Important:** Startup clearance does not authorize movement. After start, you still need taxi, hover taxi, or air taxi clearance. Do not leave the parking spot until movement clearance is issued. In the H130, rotor wash begins as soon as RPM rises — protect people and light aircraft nearby.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use startup clearance to:

- control when aircraft become active on a congested apron
- sequence departures with available slots and runway capacity
- protect ground staff and other aircraft from blast and rotor wash
- manage flow control and CTOT constraints before engines burn fuel
- coordinate between Apron, Ground, and Tower before movement begins

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are in the H130 at the ICAO Delta training stand at Florianópolis. Pre-start checklist is complete. Blades are clear. Students are briefed.

You call Ground with ATIS information and request startup.

Ground approves startup and tells you to expect runway three one, hover taxi via Alpha when ready.

You start the engine, bring rotors to flight RPM, complete after-start checks, then call again ready for hover taxi.

Ground clears you to hover taxi to the holding point. Only then do you leave the stand.

If Ground had said startup delayed due to inbound traffic, you would keep the engine shut down, save fuel, and stand by. That is why startup is a controlled step — not an automatic habit.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, startup approved, runway three one, report ready for taxi.
2. Ground: ANAC123, cleared for startup, expect hover taxi via Alpha.
3. Apron: ANAC123, startup approved, hold position, contact Ground on one two one decimal niner.
4. Ground: ANAC123, startup delayed due to inbound traffic, stand by.
5. Ground: ANAC123, startup approved, QNH one zero one niner, advise ready for departure.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, startup approved, runway three one, wilco, report ready for taxi.
2. ANAC123, cleared for startup, expect hover taxi via Alpha.
3. ANAC123, startup approved, holding position, contacting Ground one two one decimal niner.
4. ANAC123, startup delayed, standing by.
5. ANAC123, startup approved, QNH one zero one niner, will advise ready.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Startup is the first radio contract of the flight.

You ask. ATC decides. You read back. Then you start.

In training academies, students sometimes treat startup as automatic — checklist done, engine on. That habit fails on a controlled Brazilian aerodrome. Ground may delay you for traffic, flow, or apron congestion.

In the H130, startup also means rotor wash. People walking behind the tail, open hangar doors, light trainers nearby — all are reasons ATC and the academy want controlled starts.

Request startup with position and information. Read back the runway or restrictions. Start only when cleared. When ready to move, request taxi or hover taxi as a separate clearance.

Two clearances. Two readbacks. Zero assumptions.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You have completed the pre-start checklist at the parking stand. Explain how you obtain startup clearance and what you do if Ground delays the start.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would contact Ground, state my parking position, confirm ATIS information received, and request startup. If startup is approved, I would read back the clearance and any runway or taxi expectations, then start engines. If Ground delays startup due to traffic or flow control, I would acknowledge, keep the engine shut down, and stand by until approved. I would not start without clearance, and after start I would still request a separate taxi or hover taxi clearance before leaving the stand.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Starting engines before receiving startup clearance.  
  ✔ Request and receive clearance first, then start.

- ❌ Treating startup clearance as permission to taxi.  
  ✔ Startup only authorizes engine start. Request taxi or hover taxi separately.

- ❌ Incomplete readback: saying only "startup" without runway or restrictions.  
  ✔ Read back full clearance: startup approved, runway, and any hold instructions.

- ❌ Calling Tower for startup when Ground or Apron owns the frequency.  
  ✔ Use the correct agency — usually Ground or Apron on Brazilian aerodromes.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Request Startup, Information Charlie

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, reh-KWEST START-up, in-for-MAY-shun CHAR-lee

**Word Stress**

- Request → reh-KWEST (stress on second)
- Startup → START-up
- Information → in-for-MAY-shun (stress on third)
- Charlie → CHAR-lee

Practice:

Request startup... information Charlie.

Together:

ANAC123, at training stand two, information Charlie, request startup.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**START** — **S**ay position and ATIS, **T**ell Ground you request startup, **A**wait clearance before ignition, **R**ead back runway and restrictions, **T**axi only after a second clearance.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Startup clearance is the gate between silent aircraft and live aircraft.

Ask for it. Wait for it. Read it back. Then start.

In the H130 academy, every lesson begins here. Rotor wash, apron traffic, and Brazilian Ground procedures all depend on that first clearance being correct.

Do not merge startup with taxi in your mind. They are separate authorizations.

Request. Clearance. Start. Then request movement.

That is how professional helicopter operations begin — calm, legal, and clear on the radio.
