# Report Reaching Altitude

**Catalog ID:** 0322  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Report Reaching Altitude — the pilot report that advises ATC when the aircraft has arrived at an assigned altitude or flight level after climb or descent.

Report Reaching Altitude closes the vertical clearance loop. Controllers need confirmation that you are level at the cleared altitude before they can plan the next climb, descent, or crossing traffic through the levels you vacated.

For H130 operations in Brazil, reaching reports are common in IFR cruise, terminal climbs out of Congonhas or Navegantes, and when ATC has limited radar altitude information. A clean reaching report keeps the controller's altitude picture accurate.

Understanding how to report reaching altitude in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Report Reaching Altitude instructs the pilot to advise ATC when the aircraft has arrived at and is maintaining the assigned altitude or flight level following a climb or descent clearance.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Reporte alcançando a altitude / Report Reaching Altitude.

Instrução para o piloto informar ao controle quando a aeronave chegou e está mantendo a altitude ou o nível de voo atribuído após uma autorização de subida ou descida.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Report Reaching Altitude, or when the clearance requires it, the crew should:

- climb or descend to the assigned altitude as cleared
- level off accurately at the cleared altitude
- report promptly when reaching and maintaining that altitude
- include the altitude or flight level reached
- remain ready for further climb, descent, or level instructions

Report Reaching Altitude commonly includes:

- reaching flight level nine zero
- level at four thousand
- reaching three thousand, maintaining
- report reaching assigned altitude when requested for traffic sequencing

**Important:** Reaching means you are at the assigned altitude and under control — not still climbing through it. In the H130, vertical speed can be high; capture the level cleanly, then report. Do not report reaching while still five hundred feet below.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers work with Report Reaching Altitude to:

- confirm the aircraft is established at the cleared level
- authorize subsequent climb or descent clearances
- plan crossing traffic through vacated altitudes
- maintain accurate altitude awareness without relying only on Mode C
- coordinate handoffs between sectors at known levels

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are climbing out of Navegantes in the H130, cleared to flight level nine zero.

Approach says:

Approach: ANAC123, climb and maintain flight level nine zero, report reaching.

You climb through five thousand, then seven thousand, and capture flight level nine zero.

You report:

ANAC123, reaching flight level nine zero.

Approach acknowledges and issues the next routing or frequency change. Your altitude picture and theirs now match.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, climb and maintain flight level nine zero, report reaching.
2. Radar: ANAC123, descend and maintain four thousand, report reaching altitude.
3. Control: ANAC123, climb flight level one one zero, advise when reaching.
4. Approach: ANAC123, maintain three thousand, report reaching.
5. Radar: ANAC123, descend two thousand, report level.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

3. ANAC123, level at four thousand.
4. ANAC123, reaching three thousand, maintaining.
5. ANAC123, descending two thousand, will report level.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Reaching altitude is the opposite of leaving altitude — and both matter.

Leaving frees the old level. Reaching confirms the new one.

If you never report reaching, ATC may hesitate to clear the next step. They do not know whether you are still climbing, still descending, or already level.

Brazilian controllers often pair the climb clearance with "report reaching." Treat that as part of the clearance, not an optional extra.

In the H130 academy environment, students sometimes report reaching early because the altimeter is close. Close is not level. Level is level.

Capture. Stabilize. Report.

That habit looks professional on every frequency from Florianópolis to Congonhas.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between reporting leaving altitude and reporting reaching altitude?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Reporting leaving altitude means I advise ATC when I start vacating a previous altitude or flight level. Reporting reaching altitude means I advise ATC when I have arrived at and am maintaining the newly assigned altitude. Leaving frees the old level; reaching confirms the new level.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting reaching while still climbing or descending through the altitude.  
  ✔ Report only when level at the assigned altitude.

- ❌ Saying "reaching" without stating the altitude.  
  ✔ Say: reaching flight level nine zero.

- ❌ Forgetting the report after ATC specifically requested it.  
  ✔ Treat "report reaching" as a mandatory part of the clearance.

- ❌ Confusing "reaching" with "leaving."  
  ✔ Leaving = starting the change. Reaching = completing the change.

What do you transmit?

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Report Reaching Altitude tells ATC the vertical clearance is complete.

You are where they cleared you. The next plan can begin.

In H130 Brazilian operations, make the report after a clean level-off — not during the capture.

Leave when you leave. Reach when you reach. Maintain until cleared again.

That is disciplined altitude communication.
