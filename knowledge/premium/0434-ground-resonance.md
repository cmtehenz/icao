# Ground Resonance

**Catalog ID:** 0434  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Ground Resonance — a potentially destructive self-excited vibration that can occur when a helicopter is on the ground with rotors turning, typically involving landing gear and rotor system interaction.

Ground resonance awareness is critical for H130 crews during start, shutdown, and on-ground operations with rotors turning at Brazilian helipads and airports.

You must describe recognition, immediate actions, and emergency communication in ICAO English for Parts 2 and 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Ground Resonance is a severe, rapidly increasing vibration that can develop when a helicopter is in contact with the ground with the rotor system turning, caused by a coupling between rotor blade lead-lag motion and landing gear oscillation, capable of destroying the aircraft within seconds if not corrected.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Ressonância no solo / ground resonance.

Vibração severa e de aumento rápido que pode se desenvolver quando o helicóptero está em contato com o solo com o sistema de rotor girando, causada pelo acoplamento entre o movimento de avanço-atraso das pás e a oscilação do trem de pouso, capaz de destruir a aeronave em segundos se não for corrigida.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ground resonance is suspected, the pilot should:

- recognize rapidly increasing rhythmic vibration on the ground
- follow AFM immediate actions — typically lift into a hover to uncouple from the ground, or shut down per procedure if that is the prescribed action
- never ignore building vibration during start or shutdown
- inspect landing gear and rotor system after any event
- declare emergency if structural damage or fire risk exists

Ground resonance reporting commonly includes:

- experiencing severe vibration on ground, lifting to hover
- emergency, ground resonance, request assistance
- shutting down, request fire services
- unable to continue, aircraft damaged, request emergency services

**Important:** Seconds matter. Know the H130 AFM immediate action for ground resonance cold. If the procedure is to lift off, do it immediately. If it is to shut down, do that. Do not freeze on the controls while the airframe tears itself apart.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers support ground resonance emergencies by:

- clearing the area around the helicopter
- dispatching fire and rescue immediately
- holding other traffic
- coordinating tow or evacuation after shutdown
- obtaining souls on board and nature of the emergency

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on the pad in your H130 with rotors turning after landing. A rhythmic vibration begins and builds rapidly.

You apply immediate AFM actions — lift to a hover to break the ground coupling — then land carefully when stable or divert to a suitable spot.

You transmit:

Emergency, severe vibration on ground, lifting to hover — or shutting down per procedure — request fire services.

Ground crew and ATC clear the area.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger emergency, fire services rolling, say souls on board.
2. Tower: ANAC123, area cleared, advise intentions.
3. Tower: ANAC123, roger shutting down, remain on frequency if able.
4. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services on scene, report when safe.
5. Ground: ANAC123, request evacuation status when able.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

3. ANAC123, shutting down on pad, request fire services.
4. ANAC123, on ground, rotors stopping, will report when safe.
5. ANAC123, evacuation not required — or evacuating now.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Ground resonance is an on-ground killer.

It does not wait for a perfect radio call. Immediate actions first. Communication second.

Know whether your H130 procedure is lift off or shut down. Part 3 loves that distinction — answer from the AFM, not from memory of another type.

Soft or uneven surfaces, damaged dampers, and improper touchdown can contribute. After any hard landing or gear event, treat start and shutdown with extra caution.

Brazilian pads with uneven surfaces deserve respect. If vibration builds — act.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Severe rhythmic vibration begins on the ground with rotors turning. What do you do and say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would immediately apply the AFM ground resonance procedure — typically lift into a hover to uncouple from the ground, or shut down if that is prescribed. Then I would transmit: emergency, severe vibration on ground, ground resonance, request fire services. I would not delay action to make a long explanation. After the aircraft is safe, I would report souls and status.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Waiting to "see if the vibration settles."  
  ✔ Act immediately per AFM.

- ❌ Applying the wrong type's procedure from memory.  
  ✔ Use the H130 checklist only.

- ❌ Not calling fire services after a serious event.  
  ✔ Request emergency services early.

- ❌ Restarting without inspection after resonance.  
  ✔ Inspect gear and rotor system before further flight.
