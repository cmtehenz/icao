# Vortex Ring State

**Catalog ID:** 0077  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers vortex ring state — a critical aerodynamic condition every helicopter pilot must recognize.

Vortex Ring State occurs when the helicopter descends into its own downwash, causing a sudden loss of lift and a rapid rate of descent.

In the H130, vortex ring state is most likely during steep approaches with low airspeed and high rate of descent.

You must recognize the buffet and sink, apply recovery technique immediately, then communicate if control is threatened.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Vortex Ring State is an aerodynamic condition in which the helicopter descends into its own rotor downwash, resulting in loss of lift and a high rate of descent.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Estado de anel de vórtice.

Condição aerodinâmica em que o helicóptero desce dentro da própria ressaca do rotor, resultando em perda de sustentação e alta taxa de descida.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Vortex Ring State occurs, the pilot should:

- recognize buffet, vibration, and increasing rate of descent
- apply forward cyclic to increase airspeed and exit the downwash
- reduce collective if altitude permits during recovery
- avoid raising collective alone — that deepens the vortex
- declare Mayday if altitude cannot be recovered

Vortex Ring State reporting commonly includes:

- vortex ring state or settling with power
- recovering or unable to arrest descent
- request immediate landing
- souls on board and position

**Important:** Raising collective alone worsens vortex ring state. Recovery requires forward cyclic and airspeed. Aviate first — then communicate.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Vortex Ring State declarations to:

- clear traffic from the aircraft's route immediately
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable landing area
- coordinate emergency services if altitude is critical
- request souls on board and position
- prepare for possible hard landing or go-around

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on a steep approach to a confined helipad near Florianópolis in your H130.

Airspeed decays below translational lift and rate of descent increases rapidly.

The aircraft buffets and sinks despite collective input.

You apply forward cyclic, reduce collective slightly, and accelerate out of the vortex.

When stable, you transmit:

Pan Pan Pan Pan, Florianópolis Tower, ANAC123, vortex ring state, recovering, going around.

Tower clears traffic and you reattempt a shallower approach.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say altitude and intentions.
2. Tower: ANAC123, cleared go-around, report when established.
3. Approach: ANAC123, radar contact, say souls on board.
4. Tower: ANAC123, wind calm, cleared to land when ready.
5. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services standing by if required.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Vortex ring state is the helicopter falling into its own wake.

Steep. Slow. High descent rate.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Cyclic forward. Collective down. Airspeed.

Do not pull collective alone — that feeds the vortex.

Push the nose forward.

Exit the downwash.

Regain airspeed.

Then climb if needed.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "caiu no vórtice" or "settling" on frequency.

Use vortex ring state or settling with power — ICAO standard terms.

In the H130, avoid steep approaches with low airspeed and high descent rate.

Train recognition until buffet means cyclic forward — not more collective.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is vortex ring state and how do you recover?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Vortex ring state occurs when the helicopter descends into its own downwash, causing loss of lift and a high rate of descent. I would apply forward cyclic to increase airspeed, reduce collective if altitude permits, and exit the downwash. I would declare Pan Pan or Mayday depending on altitude remaining, report vortex ring state, and request go-around or immediate landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Raising collective alone when the aircraft sinks.  
  ✔ Forward cyclic first — more collective deepens the vortex.

- ❌ Saying "vórtice" without standard English.  
  ✔ Use vortex ring state or settling with power.

- ❌ Continuing a steep slow approach into confined areas.  
  ✔ Maintain airspeed and a manageable descent rate.

- ❌ Confusing vortex ring state with engine failure.  
  ✔ Power is available — the problem is aerodynamic recirculation.
