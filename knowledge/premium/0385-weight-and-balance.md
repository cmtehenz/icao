# Weight and Balance

**Catalog ID:** 0385  
**Category:** Performance  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Weight and Balance — the calculation and control of aircraft total weight and the distribution of that weight so the center of gravity remains within approved limits for safe flight.

Weight and Balance is not a ground formality. Incorrect loading changes performance, stability, and controllability — and may make takeoff or landing illegal and unsafe.

For H130 operations in Brazil — passenger shifts, cargo, fuel burn, and offshore loads — weight and balance must be verified before departure and respected when load changes. Discussing load-related delays or limitations with ATC requires clear ICAO English.

This lesson prepares you for Part 2, Part 3, and Part 4 communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Weight and Balance is the process of determining the aircraft's total weight and center of gravity location, ensuring both remain within the approved envelope for the intended flight phase.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Peso e balanceamento / Weight and Balance.

Processo de determinar o peso total da aeronave e a localização do centro de gravidade, garantindo que ambos permaneçam dentro do envelope aprovado para a fase de voo pretendida.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When managing weight and balance, the crew should:

- calculate zero-fuel weight, fuel, and takeoff weight before departure
- verify CG is within forward and aft limits
- refuse loading that exceeds weight or CG limits
- recalculate after last-minute passenger or cargo changes
- advise ATC if loading delay or offload is required for balance

Weight and Balance control includes:

- maximum structural and performance-limited weights
- passenger and baggage distribution
- fuel quantity and burn effect on CG where applicable
- cargo restraint and station arms
- landing weight after fuel burn

**Important:** A last-minute passenger move or extra bag can put CG out of limits. Recalculate before accepting the load. Do not depart "close enough."

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers encounter weight and balance issues when:

- aircraft request delay at the gate or pad for loading adjustment
- crews report unable to depart due to overweight or balance
- offload or fuel reduction is needed before taxi
- performance-limited departures follow weight reduction
- diversion or return is required after a loading discrepancy is found

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

At Congonhas, ground loads an extra passenger at the last minute. Your H130 takeoff weight would exceed the performance limit for the hot afternoon.

You call before taxi:

São Paulo Congonhas Ground, ANAC123, request delay, weight and balance adjustment, unable to depart at current load.

Ground: ANAC123, roger, hold position, advise when ready.

After offloading baggage:

ANAC123, ready to taxi, weight and balance within limits.

You protected the flight with a clear delay request — not a rushed departure.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, advise ready to taxi.
2. Ground: ANAC123, roger delay for loading, hold position.
3. Tower: ANAC123, say reason for delay.
4. Ground: ANAC123, taxi when ready, no rush.
5. Tower: ANAC123, unable departure due weight — confirm intentions.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, negative ready, request delay for weight and balance.
2. ANAC123, holding position, adjusting load.
3. ANAC123, delay for weight reduction, will advise ready.
4. ANAC123, ready to taxi, weight and balance within limits.
5. ANAC123, unable departure overweight, requesting offload time.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Weight and balance is the first performance calculation — before V-speeds, before takeoff charts, before "ready."

If the load is wrong, nothing else matters. Offload. Redistribute. Delay. Then go.

In the H130, a few passengers or bags shifting aft can move CG quickly. Brief the load. Watch last-minute changes. Recalculate.

When you need time on the ramp, tell ATC: request delay, weight and balance adjustment. Do not invent a radio problem to buy time. Honest operational language is respected.

Never taxi while "finishing the numbers." Either you are within limits or you are not ready.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Ground asks if you are ready to taxi. Your weight and balance is not yet within limits after a last-minute load change. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would say: ANAC123, negative ready, request delay for weight and balance adjustment. I would advise when within limits and ready to taxi. I would not accept a taxi clearance while overweight or out of CG. Clear delay language keeps ATC informed and keeps the aircraft safe.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Departing after a last-minute passenger without recalculating.  
  ✔ Recalculate weight and CG after every load change.

- ❌ Saying "not ready" without a reason when delay is operational.  
  ✔ State: delay for weight and balance / load adjustment.

- ❌ Confusing maximum structural weight with performance-limited weight.  
  ✔ Use the more restrictive limit for the conditions.

- ❌ Ignoring CG when total weight is under maximum.  
  ✔ Weight and CG are both required — either can stop the flight.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Ground calls ready for push or taxi. An extra passenger boarded. Your calculation shows takeoff weight above the performance limit.

**Your first task**

What do you say to Ground?

**Your second task**

After offloading two bags, you are within limits. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, negative ready, request delay for weight and balance, unable to depart at current load. Second task: ANAC123, ready to taxi, weight and balance within limits. I would not minimize the issue or depart overweight. Clear status updates let ATC plan the sequence.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Weight and balance is the foundation of every performance number that follows.

Calculate. Verify CG. Recalculate after changes.

If you need time — request delay for weight and balance. If you cannot depart — say unable, overweight or out of limits.

In the H130, small load changes matter. Treat them seriously.

Within the envelope — then taxi. Outside — fix it first.

That is professional weight and balance discipline.
