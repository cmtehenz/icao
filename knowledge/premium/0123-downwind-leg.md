# Downwind Leg

**Catalog ID:** 0123  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the Downwind Leg — the segment of the traffic circuit that runs parallel to the runway in the direction opposite to landing.

Every VFR circuit includes a downwind leg.

At Joinville, Navegantes, and smaller aerodromes near São Paulo, circuit traffic is common in the H130.

Understanding the downwind leg — its meaning, its position in the circuit, and how to report it — is a foundational ICAO communication skill.

ATC uses downwind leg instructions to sequence circuit traffic and manage separation efficiently.

## 📖 Meaning

The Downwind Leg is the portion of the traffic circuit flown parallel to the runway and in the opposite direction of landing, connecting the crosswind leg to the base leg of the approach.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Trecho de través (ou leg de través).

A parte do circuito de tráfego voada paralelamente à pista e no sentido oposto ao pouso, conectando o trecho cruzado ao trecho base da aproximação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

During the Downwind Leg, the pilot should:

- maintain pattern altitude as prescribed or as directed by ATC
- keep the runway visible and within reach for a base turn
- complete the before-landing checks or appropriate checklists
- monitor for traffic entering the circuit
- report downwind when instructed by ATC

Downwind Leg operations commonly include:

- report downwind for sequencing
- extend downwind to provide spacing behind arrivals
- number in sequence issued by ATC
- speed adjustment if requested

**Important:** The downwind leg is not just a transit. It is the last opportunity to complete all approach preparations before descending on base and final. Use the downwind to configure, check, and brief.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers use the Downwind Leg to:

- sequence circuit traffic efficiently
- provide spacing between aircraft on final
- issue number-in-sequence information
- extend downwind to accommodate IFR arrivals
- manage simultaneous circuit and straight-in traffic

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are flying circuit training in your H130 at Joinville.

Tower instructed you to join the circuit for runway one five.

You complete the crosswind turn and establish on the downwind leg.

The runway is on your left.

You transmit:

Tower, ANAC123, downwind, runway one five.

Tower responds:

ANAC123, number two, follow Cessna on base, extend downwind, I'll call your base.

You extend the downwind leg, maintaining altitude and visual separation from the Cessna.

Tower calls:

ANAC123, turn base.

You turn, descend on base, and roll out on final aligned with the runway.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, report downwind runway one five.
2. Tower: ANAC123, number two, extend downwind, I'll call your base.
3. Tower: ANAC123, downwind, number one, cleared touch-and-go.
4. Tower: ANAC123, extend downwind, IFR traffic on final.
5. Tower: ANAC123, turn base, number one.

5. ANAC123, turning base, number one.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

The downwind leg is your preparation window.

Everything that needs to happen before landing should happen on the downwind.

Gear down. Flaps set. Speed adjusted. Landing checklist complete. Fuel checked.

When you turn base, you should already be configured.

When you turn final, you should already be stabilized.

Many Brazilian pilots rush the downwind because the runway looks close.

The runway is not going anywhere.

Use the full leg.

In the H130, the circuit at Joinville is tight. The downwind feels short.

Fly it deliberately. Not fast, not slow — but prepared.

If ATC extends you, accept it gracefully. The extra distance is a gift for your checklist.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What reports and tasks should be completed during the downwind leg?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

During the downwind leg, I would report downwind to ATC when instructed, complete the before-landing checks or landing checklist, set the appropriate configuration for the approach, and maintain pattern altitude while keeping the runway in sight. I would also monitor for other circuit traffic and comply with any ATC instructions to extend downwind, adjust speed, or report my sequence. By the time I turned base, the aircraft should be fully configured and ready for the approach.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Rushing through the downwind without completing the landing checklist.  
  ✔ Complete all before-landing checks on the downwind leg.

- ❌ Turning base without ATC clearance when instructed to report downwind.  
  ✔ Wait for ATC to call your base when under circuit control instructions.

- ❌ Not reporting downwind when instructed.  
  ✔ Transmit: Tower, ANAC123, downwind, runway [number].

- ❌ Forgetting to look for traffic when extending downwind.  
  ✔ Maintain visual separation from all circuit traffic during extensions.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Downwind Leg

**Pronunciation:** DOWN-wind leg

**Word Stress**

- Downwind → DOWN-wind (stress on first syllable)
- Leg → leg (short, clear)

Practice:

Downwind... Leg... Downwind Leg.

Together:

Tower, ANAC123, downwind, runway one five.

Avoid: *downuind* — the W in downwind is pronounced. Say DOWN-wind clearly.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**PREP** — **P**arallel to runway in opposite direction, **R**eport when instructed, **E**xtend when told, **P**repare landing checklist on this leg.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are on the downwind leg at Joinville in your H130. Tower transmits: "ANAC123, number two, extend downwind, I'll call your base, traffic Cessna on base."

**Your first task**

Read back the instruction and report your position.

**Your second task**

What tasks do you perform while extending the downwind?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would respond: ANAC123, number two, extending downwind, traffic in sight. While extending the downwind, I would maintain pattern altitude, complete the landing checklist, confirm aircraft configuration, monitor the Cessna on base for spacing, and wait for Tower to call my base turn. I would not turn base until instructed.
