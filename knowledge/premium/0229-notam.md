# NOTAM

**Catalog ID:** 0229  
**Category:** Navigation  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers NOTAM — Notice to Airmen (Notice to Air Missions) — a time-critical aeronautical information notice that alerts crews to temporary or permanent changes affecting flight operations, including airspace, aerodromes, navaids, procedures, and hazards.

NOTAMs are not optional reading. They close runways, activate restricted areas, remove navaids from service, and warn of obstacles and laser activity. A crew that skips NOTAMs flies yesterday's airport into today's reality.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations in Brazilian airspace under DECEA, NOTAMs shape VFR training routes, IFR approaches, helipad availability, and temporary restricted areas near cities and coastal zones. Preflight briefing must include a current NOTAM review for departure, destination, alternates, and route.

Understanding how to discuss NOTAMs, confirm status with ATC, and integrate NOTAM information into ICAO English supports Part 2 radiotelephony and Part 4 interaction skills.

## 📖 Meaning

A NOTAM is an official notice distributed by means of telecommunication containing information concerning the establishment, condition, or change in any aeronautical facility, service, procedure, or hazard, the timely knowledge of which is essential to personnel concerned with flight operations.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When using NOTAMs in flight planning and operations, the crew should:

- obtain current NOTAMs for departure, destination, alternates, and FIR route before flight
- identify items that change runway, taxiway, approach, airspace, or navaid availability
- brief operationally relevant NOTAMs — not every line item with equal weight
- cross-check ATIS and ATC for real-time confirmation of critical NOTAM items
- request clarification from ATC if a NOTAM status is unclear in flight
- update the plan if a new NOTAM or ATC advisory supersedes the brief

NOTAMs commonly cover:

- runway and taxiway closures or partial unserviceability
- navaid and lighting unserviceability
- temporary restricted or danger areas
- crane and obstacle notifications
- approach procedure changes or suspended minima

**Important:** NOTAM language is abbreviated and dense. Decode carefully. In the H130, low-level VFR routes are sensitive to temporary obstacles, tethered balloons, and temporary restricted areas that may not affect high jet airways the same way. Brief what affects your altitude and route.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

ATC and AIS use NOTAMs to:

- publish time-critical changes faster than AIP amendment cycles
- warn crews of temporary hazards and unserviceabilities
- activate and deactivate special-use airspace
- support ATIS content and controller advisories
- maintain a common information baseline across operators

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You plan a training flight from Congonhas to Florianópolis in the H130. During briefing you pull NOTAMs.

One NOTAM: ILS runway one four Florianópolis unserviceable. Another: temporary restricted area active along the coastal route below three thousand feet until 2200 UTC.

You rebrief: expect VOR or RNAV approach at destination. Plan the coastal leg above the temporary restriction or laterally clear. You note both items on the pad.

En route, Approach confirms the ILS remains out of service. Your NOTAM brief matches live ATC. No surprise on the approach chart at the last minute.

That is NOTAM discipline: read, decode, brief what matters, confirm with ATC when it affects the clearance.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, ILS runway one four unserviceable as published by NOTAM, expect VOR runway one four.
2. Radar: ANAC123, temporary restricted area active as NOTAMed, remain clear, vector for deviation.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway one seven Left closed, NOTAM in force, expect runway one seven Right.
4. Clearance: ANAC123, note NOTAM, navaid Alpha unserviceable, cleared as filed via the alternate route.
5. Approach: ANAC123, confirm you have current NOTAM for crane three miles final runway two eight.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

NOTAMs are the difference between the published world and the real world today.

Do not print them and ignore them. Decode the ones that change your runway, approach, route, or altitude. Brief those. Leave trivia for the package, not for the critical brief.

In Brazilian ops, temporary restricted areas and aerodrome works appear constantly. The H130 at low level meets obstacles and temporary airspace that high cruise traffic never sees. Your NOTAM filter must match your profile.

If ATC says something conflicts with your NOTAM brief — for example, a navaid you thought was out is in use — clarify. Information can be updated. Ask.

NOTAM review is preflight professionalism. It is also Part 4 interaction: explaining how information affected your plan.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Describe how you use NOTAMs when planning a flight and what you do if a NOTAM affects your destination approach.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

During preflight I would obtain current NOTAMs for departure, destination, alternates, and the route. I would identify items that affect runways, taxiways, navaids, airspace, and approach procedures, and brief those to the crew. If a NOTAM makes the planned approach unserviceable, I would select and brief an alternate procedure or runway, adjust fuel if needed, and confirm the expectation with ATC on first contact. I would not rely only on memory of yesterday's NOTAMs.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Skipping NOTAM review because "the route is familiar."  
  ✔ Familiar routes still get temporary closures and restrictions. Check every flight.

- ❌ Reading all NOTAMs without highlighting operationally critical items.  
  ✔ Filter for runway, approach, airspace, and navaid items that change the plan.

- ❌ Assuming ATIS replaces NOTAM review.  
  ✔ ATIS is complementary. NOTAMs cover route and destination items ATIS may not list in full.

- ❌ Not confirming an unclear NOTAM status with ATC when it affects the clearance.  
  ✔ Ask: confirm ILS status, restricted area activity, or runway availability.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Confirm Current NOTAM, ILS Unserviceable

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, kun-FERM KER-ent NO-tam, EYE-el-es un-SER-vis-uh-bul

**Word Stress**

- NOTAM → NO-tam ( comms: NO-TAM )
- Unserviceable → un-SER-vis-uh-bul
- Confirm → kun-FERM
- Current → KER-ent

Practice:

Current NOTAM... ILS unserviceable... expect VOR.

Together:

ANAC123, confirm current NOTAM, ILS runway one four unserviceable, expect VOR.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**NOTAM** — **N**ote time-critical changes, **O**btain for route and airports, **T**riage what affects your flight, **A**pply to brief and fuel plan, **M**atch with ATIS and ATC in flight.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Your destination NOTAM shows the ILS unserviceable and a temporary crane on final. ATIS confirms ILS out of service.

**Your first task**

On first contact with Approach, what do you say to confirm your plan?

**Your second task**

Approach asks if you have the crane NOTAM. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, information Charlie received, expect VOR runway one four, ILS unserviceable. Second task: ANAC123, affirmative, crane NOTAM copied, will remain alert on final. I would brief the alternate approach and the obstacle, and continue only with a procedure that does not depend on the unserviceable ILS.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

NOTAMs tell you what changed since the AIP was printed.

Read them. Decode them. Brief what matters for your altitude and route.

In the H130, temporary obstacles and low-level restrictions are not background noise — they are the mission environment.

Confirm critical items with ATIS and ATC. Fly today's airport, not yesterday's memory.

That is NOTAM discipline.
