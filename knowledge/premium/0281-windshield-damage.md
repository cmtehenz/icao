# Windshield Damage

**Catalog ID:** 0281  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Windshield Damage — cracking, shattering, or impact damage to the windshield, requiring immediate assessment of visibility and structural integrity, possible speed reduction, urgency or distress communication, and diversion or priority landing as required.

Bird strike, hail, foreign object impact, or thermal cracking can all produce windshield damage. For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, this is highly relevant — helicopter windshields and chin bubbles are exposed in low-level and approach environments. Master ICAO English: windshield damage, cracked windshield, limited visibility, request diversion.

Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 speaking both expect clear description of visibility and intentions.

## 📖 Meaning

Windshield Damage is any cracking, delamination, shattering, or impact damage to the aircraft windshield that may reduce visibility, compromise structural integrity, or both, requiring abnormal or emergency procedures, ATC notification, and often diversion or priority landing.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Dano no para-brisa / Windshield Damage.

Qualquer rachadura, delaminação, estilhaçamento ou dano por impacto no para-brisa da aeronave que possa reduzir a visibilidade, comprometer a integridade estrutural, ou ambos, exigindo procedimentos anormais ou de emergência, notificação ao ATC e frequentemente desvio ou pouso prioritário.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When windshield damage occurs, the crew should:

- maintain control; reduce speed if required by AFM / structural guidance
- assess visibility for the flying pilot; transfer control if the other side is clear
- declare Pan Pan for manageable cracked windshield with adequate visibility; Mayday if shattered, structural failure, or unable to see to continue safely
- request diversion or priority landing as needed
- report nature (windshield damage / cracked windshield), visibility status, intentions
- for H130: same structure — highly applicable to helicopter operations

Windshield damage reporting commonly includes:

- windshield damage / cracked windshield / shattered windshield
- limited visibility / visibility OK on left/right
- request diversion / priority landing
- bird strike (if that was the cause)
- souls and fuel when asked

**Important:** Do not continue into IMC or a complex approach if the flying pilot cannot see. Transfer control or divert. Say limited visibility clearly.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to windshield damage reports to:

- provide priority handling and simpler approaches when possible
- offer vectors to VMC or the nearest suitable airport
- avoid assigning complex visual maneuvers the crew cannot see to fly
- alert emergency services if structural failure is suspected
- request visibility and intentions updates

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are approaching Florianópolis in the H130 when a bird strike cracks the right windshield. Visibility on the left is still good. You transfer control to the left seat.

You transmit:

Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, windshield damage after bird strike, limited visibility right side, request priority landing runway one four.

Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, cleared present position for runway one four, say if you require assistance on landing.

You reply:

ANAC123, left seat flying, visibility OK left side, request straight-in, no emergency services required at this time.

If the windshield shatters and you cannot see, upgrade to Mayday and request immediate landing.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan windshield damage, expect straight-in runway one four.
2. Approach: ANAC123, say visibility and which pilot is flying.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, wind one four zero at eight, say if services required.
4. Center: ANAC123, roger cracked windshield, say diversion request.
5. Approach: ANAC123, vectors for longest runway, report field in sight when able.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Windshield damage is a visibility and structure problem.

Slow if required. Fly from the clear side. Tell ATC what you can and cannot see.

Brazilian pilots often say "rachou o para-brisa" or "quebra do windshield." Prefer windshield damage, cracked windshield, or shattered windshield in ICAO English — then limited visibility and intentions.

In the H130 this is real operational risk, not only exam theory. Bird strikes and FOD happen low. Brief who flies if one side is blinded.

Pan Pan when manageable. Mayday when you cannot see or structure is failing. Do not pride your way through an approach you cannot see.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

A bird strike cracks the windshield. You still have usable visibility on one side. What do you do and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain control, reduce speed if required, transfer flying duties to the pilot with clear visibility, and declare Pan Pan stating windshield damage after bird strike with limited visibility on the damaged side. I would request priority landing or diversion and a simple approach. If the windshield shattered and I could not see, I would declare Mayday and request immediate landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "bird strike" without windshield/visibility status.  
  ✔ State windshield damage and whether visibility is limited.

- ❌ Continuing a visual approach from the damaged side.  
  ✔ Transfer control to the clear side or divert.

- ❌ Using Mayday for a small crack with full visibility — or Pan Pan when blind.  
  ✔ Match urgency to visibility and structural risk.

- ❌ Using only Portuguese "para-brisa" on the ICAO exam.  
  ✔ Prefer windshield damage / cracked windshield.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Windshield damage is about what you can still see — and what you must tell ATC.

Slow. Transfer. Declare. Land simple.

In the H130 this is daily-risk English, not only exam theory. Bird strikes happen where we fly.

Pan Pan when you can still fly safely. Mayday when you cannot see. Never pretend visibility is fine when it is not.

That is how windshield damage ends as a normal landing from the clear side — not as a visual approach flown through a spiderweb of cracks.
