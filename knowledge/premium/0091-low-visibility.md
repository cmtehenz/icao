# Low Visibility

**Catalog ID:** 0091  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers low visibility — when you cannot see far enough to continue VFR safely.

Low Visibility means meteorological visibility is reduced by fog, mist, rain, smoke, or haze below the values required for the intended operation.

In the H130, low visibility along the Brazilian coast and in valleys can appear quickly and trap VFR flights.

You must recognize the reduction early, divert or request IFR, and communicate clearly with ATC.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 operational communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Low Visibility is a meteorological condition in which visibility is reduced below the requirements for safe continuation of the planned VFR or visual operation.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Baixa visibilidade.

Condição meteorológica em que a visibilidade fica abaixo do necessário para continuar com segurança a operação VFR ou visual planejada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Low Visibility occurs, the pilot should:

- assess whether VFR minima can still be maintained
- divert, return, or request an IFR clearance early
- inform ATC of low visibility and intentions
- avoid continuing into uncontrolled IMC
- request special VFR only when legal and safe

Low Visibility reporting commonly includes:

- visibility reducing or low visibility
- unable to continue VFR
- request diversion or IFR clearance
- request special VFR if applicable

**Important:** Low visibility is a decision trigger. Communicate before you lose options.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Low Visibility reports to:

- provide diversion routing
- issue IFR clearances when requested and available
- update visibility information for the aerodrome
- sequence special VFR or IFR traffic
- assist aircraft that can no longer maintain VFR

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are VFR along the coast toward Florianópolis in your H130.

Visibility drops rapidly in mist and haze.

You decide early and transmit:

Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, low visibility, unable to continue VFR, request diversion to Navegantes.

Approach provides vectors and you land safely while conditions are still workable.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger low visibility, say intentions.
2. Approach: ANAC123, cleared diversion Navegantes, descend at pilot's discretion.
3. Tower: ANAC123, visibility one thousand meters, runway two nine in use.
4. Approach: ANAC123, advise if able to maintain VFR.
5. Tower: ANAC123, special VFR approved, remain clear of cloud.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, low visibility, unable to continue VFR, request diversion.
2. ANAC123, visibility reducing, request IFR clearance.
3. ANAC123, low visibility, returning to departure aerodrome.
4. Approach, ANAC123, request special VFR to the airport.
5. ANAC123, low visibility on final, going around, request vectors.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Low visibility is not a challenge to push through.

It is a signal to change the plan.

The sequence:

Recognize. Decide. Communicate. Divert or IFR.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "visibilidade ruim" or "não dá para ver" on frequency.

Use low visibility or unable to continue VFR — ICAO standard terms.

In the H130, coastal mist can erase the shoreline in minutes.

Have an alternate before visibility collapses.

Train early diversion language until it is automatic.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if visibility dropped below VFR minima in cruise?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would inform ATC of low visibility and that I am unable to continue VFR. I would request diversion to the nearest suitable airport or an IFR clearance. I would state my intentions clearly and early, before entering uncontrolled IMC.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing VFR into deteriorating visibility.  
  ✔ Divert or request IFR before options disappear.

- ❌ Saying "não enxergo" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use low visibility or unable to continue VFR.

- ❌ Not stating intentions after reporting visibility.  
  ✔ Always say divert, return, or request IFR.

- ❌ Entering cloud without a clearance.  
  ✔ Remain clear of cloud unless IFR or legal special VFR.

Visibility drops quickly on a VFR coastal flight to Florianópolis.

**Your first task**

**Your second task**

Approach asks if you can continue. What do you report?
