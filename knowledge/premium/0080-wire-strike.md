# Wire Strike

**Catalog ID:** 0080  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers wire strike — one of the leading causes of fatal helicopter accidents.

Wire Strike means the helicopter contacts power lines, cables, or wires during low-level flight.

In the H130, wire strike risk is high during confined area approaches, pipeline patrol, and operations near rural Brazilian power lines.

You must see and avoid wires, use wire-strike protection if installed, and declare Mayday immediately if contact occurs.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Wire Strike is contact between the helicopter and power lines, cables, or wires, often resulting in catastrophic damage or loss of control.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Colisão com fios (wire strike).

Contato do helicóptero com linhas de energia, cabos ou fios, frequentemente resultando em dano catastrófico ou perda de controle.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Wire Strike occurs or is imminent, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the absolute first priority
- assess damage to main rotor, tail rotor, and airframe
- declare Mayday immediately
- land as soon as practicable at the nearest suitable area
- request emergency services and report souls on board

Wire Strike reporting commonly includes:

- wire strike or struck power lines
- nature of damage if known
- request immediate landing
- souls on board and position

**Important:** Wires are often invisible against terrain and sky. Prevention through reconnaissance is the primary defense. If strike occurs — Mayday and land.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Wire Strike declarations to:

- clear all traffic from the aircraft's route immediately
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable landing area
- coordinate emergency services and power company notification
- request souls on board and nature of damage
- prepare for possible crash landing or autorotation

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are approaching a confined construction site near Joinville in your H130.

On short final, you see unmarked power lines too late.

The wire cutter engages and the aircraft yaws violently.

You maintain control and transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Joinville Tower, ANAC123, wire strike, request immediate landing field ahead.

Tower clears traffic and emergency services respond.

You land in the field and shut down for inspection.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, Mayday acknowledged, say souls on board and nature of damage.
2. Tower: ANAC123, all traffic cleared, emergency services standing by.
3. Approach: ANAC123, radar contact, say position and intentions.
4. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land any area, wind calm.
5. Tower: ANAC123, fire rescue en route to your position.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Wires kill helicopter pilots.

They are thin. They blend with the background. They appear late.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

See. Avoid. Reconnoiter.

Orbit confined areas before committing.

Look for poles, towers, and shadows of wires.

Assume wires exist near roads, rivers, and buildings.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "bateu no fio" or "linha de alta tensão" on frequency.

Use wire strike or struck power lines — ICAO standard terms.

In the H130, wire-strike protection helps — but it is not a license to fly blind.

If you strike — Mayday immediately. Assess control. Land now.

Train your eyes to find poles before you find wires.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if you struck power lines during approach?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain aircraft control and declare Mayday Mayday Mayday. I would state my callsign, report wire strike or struck power lines, describe any known damage, state my position and souls on board, and request immediate landing at the nearest suitable area. I would report safe on the ground after landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Descending into confined areas without a reconnaissance orbit.  
  ✔ Orbit and look for poles, towers, and wires before committing.

- ❌ Saying "bateu no fio" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use wire strike or struck power lines.

- ❌ Continuing to destination after a wire strike.  
  ✔ Land immediately — structural damage may be critical.

- ❌ Not declaring Mayday after contact.  
  ✔ Wire strike is always distress — Mayday.
