# Emergency Landing

**Catalog ID:** 0041  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers the phraseology for one of aviation's most serious decisions.

Emergency Landing means landing when the safety of the aircraft or occupants is at serious and immediate risk.

This is not a normal diversion. It is a declaration of intent to land immediately because continuing flight is unsafe.

Your radio call must pair emergency landing with the underlying problem — fire, engine failure, gear malfunction.

In your H130, emergency landing may mean a field, a highway, or the nearest airport — but ATC must know your intention.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Emergency Landing is a landing made when the safety of the aircraft or its occupants is at serious and immediate risk.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pouso de emergência.

Pouso realizado quando a segurança da aeronave ou dos ocupantes está em risco sério e imediato.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Emergency Landing is required, the pilot should:

- declare Mayday or Pan Pan as appropriate for the underlying emergency
- state the intention to make an emergency landing
- request the nearest suitable airport or longest available runway
- inform ATC of the underlying problem: fire, engine, gear, medical
- coordinate with emergency services through ATC

Emergency Landing reporting commonly includes:

- executing emergency landing
- performing emergency landing
- we need an emergency landing at the nearest airport
- requesting the longest available runway for emergency landing

**Important:** Always pair emergency landing with the underlying event. Do not say emergency landing alone without context.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Emergency Landing intentions to:

- clear the nearest runway immediately
- alert fire, rescue, and medical services
- provide straight-in approaches and priority handling
- advise on longest available runway when requested
- clear all traffic from the approach path

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You declared Mayday after engine failure in your H130 near Salvador.

Autorotation is not required — you have partial power but safe flight is in doubt.

You are five miles from Salvador International and transmit:

ANAC123, one five miles final, performing emergency landing, engine failure.

Tower clears all traffic and alerts emergency services.

You request the longest available runway:

ANAC123, requesting the longest available runway for emergency landing.

Tower clears you to land runway one zero with fire services standing by.

You complete a safe emergency landing and report all souls safe on the ground.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, cleared straight-in approach runway one eight.
2. Tower: ANAC123, runway three six cleared to land, fire services alerted.
3. Approach: ANAC123, say intentions for landing.
4. Tower: ANAC123, longest runway available is runway two seven.
5. Tower: ANAC123, emergency services standing by.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Emergency landing is an execution phrase — it tells ATC you are landing now, not later.

Brazilian pilots often say emergency landing without stating why.

Always pair it with the underlying event: emergency landing due to engine failure, emergency landing due to fire in the cabin.

The ICAO Delta Students material uses executing emergency landing and performing emergency landing.

Both are correct. Speak with confidence.

For fixed-wing aircraft, longest available runway matters when gear status is uncertain.

In the H130, you may land on any suitable area — but still inform ATC of your intention clearly.

Do not confuse emergency landing with go-around. Go-around means you are climbing away. Emergency landing means you are landing now.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you request from ATC when you need an emergency landing?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare Mayday or Pan Pan for the underlying emergency, state that I am performing an emergency landing, request the nearest suitable airport, and request the longest available runway if gear status is uncertain. I would provide position, souls on board, and fuel remaining when requested.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying emergency landing without stating the underlying problem.  
  ✔ Pair with the event: emergency landing due to engine failure.

- ❌ Confusing emergency landing with go-around.  
  ✔ Go-around means climbing away; emergency landing means landing now.

- ❌ Not requesting longest runway when gear status is uncertain.  
  ✔ Request the longest available runway for emergency landing.

- ❌ Using pouso de emergência in English transmission.  
  ✔ Use emergency landing or performing emergency landing.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Performing Emergency Landing

**Pronunciation:** per-FORM-ing e-MER-gen-cy LAN-ding

**Word Stress**

- Emergency → e-MER-gen-cy
- Landing → LAN-ding
- Longest available runway → speak slowly, word by word

Practice:

Performing... emergency landing...

Together:

ANAC123, one five miles final, performing emergency landing, engine failure.

Speak with authority — ATC must sequence traffic and services immediately.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**LAND SAFE** — **L**ocate airport, **A**nnounce emergency, **N**otify services, **D**escend, **S**ecure approach, **A**pproach brief, **F**inal call, **E**vacuate if needed.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You have declared Mayday for fire in the cabin in your H130 and are ten miles from Salvador.

**Your first task**

Transmit your emergency landing intention to Tower with the underlying reason.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

Tower offers runway one zero, but runway two seven is longer. What do you request?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would request the longest available runway for emergency landing. I would state that we have fire in the cabin and need the longest runway with fire services standing by. I would report position and souls on board, ANAC123.
