# Cleared to Divert

**Catalog ID:** 0104  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers cleared to divert — either an ATC instruction or a pilot decision to proceed to an alternate airport when the destination is unavailable.

Cleared to Divert involves rerouting, new clearances, and sometimes emergency communication when the divert is due to technical or weather reasons.

In the H130, diversions are real events at Brazilian coastal airports during rapid weather deterioration at Florianópolis, Navegantes, and Joinville.

You must know how to request a diversion clearly, readback a diversion clearance, and communicate your intentions and fuel state to ATC.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 operational communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Cleared to Divert is an ATC clearance authorizing the aircraft to proceed to an alternate aerodrome or landing area instead of the original destination, due to weather, technical, medical, or operational reasons.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Autorizado desvio / alternado (cleared to divert).

Autorização do ATC para que a aeronave prossiga para um aeródromo alternativo em vez do destino original, por razões meteorológicas, técnicas, médicas ou operacionais.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Cleared to Divert is issued or requested, the pilot should:

- acknowledge with the alternate aerodrome name or identifier
- confirm fuel state is sufficient for the divert
- advise ATC of the reason for diversion if not already stated
- update flight plan and ETA if ATC requests
- declare emergency if the divert is due to a critical situation

Cleared to Divert reporting commonly includes:

- request divert to alternate due weather at destination
- fuel state given with divert request
- technical problem requiring divert
- medical emergency, request divert to nearest suitable airport
- cleared to divert, readback with destination

**Important:** When diverting, always state your fuel state and reason. ATC needs this to prioritize your diversion and alert the alternate airport.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC issues Cleared to Divert to:

- authorize rerouting to an alternate when destination is unavailable
- expedite diversions due to emergency or fuel critical situations
- coordinate with the alternate airport for arrival
- notify rescue coordination if the divert is due to emergency
- ensure proper sequencing at the alternate aerodrome

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are approaching Florianópolis in your H130.

Visibility has dropped below minimums due to dense fog.

You have been holding for twenty minutes. Fuel is now limiting.

You transmit:

Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, fuel state forty-five minutes, unable to continue holding, request divert to Navegantes.

Approach replies:

ANAC123, cleared to divert Navegantes, maintain four thousand, advise ETA.

You readback and proceed to Navegantes.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, cleared to divert Navegantes, fly heading three four zero, climb to four thousand.
2. Approach: ANAC123, destination below minimums, say fuel state and intentions.
3. Approach: ANAC123, cleared to divert, confirm alternate aerodrome.
4. Approach: ANAC123, roger divert request, cleared direct Navegantes, maintain VFR.
5. Approach: ANAC123, Florianópolis closed, cleared to divert, say alternate.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Cleared to divert Navegantes, maintaining four thousand, ANAC123.
2. Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, requesting divert to Navegantes, fuel state four five minutes.
3. ANAC123, cleared to divert, proceeding to Navegantes, wilco.
4. Approach, ANAC123, technical problem, request divert to nearest suitable airport.
5. ANAC123, diverting Curitiba, estimating zero three five zero local, fuel state two hours.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Divert early. Not when the fuel light is on.

Weather. Technical. Medical. Fuel. All valid reasons to divert.

The sequence:

Identify the problem. Calculate fuel. Choose the alternate. Request the diversion. State the reason and fuel state.

Brazilian pilots sometimes hold too long at Florianópolis or Navegantes waiting for weather to lift.

Time and fuel are the limits.

Know your alternate before departure. Know the route. Know the distance.

When you decide to divert — transmit clearly. State the reason. State the fuel.

ATC will help you. But only if you communicate early.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How would you request a diversion from ATC when your destination weather is below minimums?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would advise ATC that the destination is below minimums, state my current fuel state, name my intended alternate, and request clearance to divert. For example: Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, visibility below minimums, fuel state forty-five minutes, request divert to Navegantes. I would readback the clearance with the alternate name and assigned altitude.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Waiting until fuel is critically low before requesting a diversion.  
  ✔ Request divert early when holding time is consuming reserve fuel.

- ❌ Not stating the reason for the divert request.  
  ✔ Always state weather, technical problem, or medical emergency as the reason.

- ❌ Not including fuel state in the divert request.  
  ✔ Fuel state is essential — ATC needs it to prioritize your clearance.

- ❌ Readback only the clearance without the alternate name.  
  ✔ Readback: cleared to divert Navegantes, and confirm the altitude or route assigned.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are holding over Florianópolis in your H130. Destination visibility is below minimums. You have been holding for twenty-five minutes. Fuel state is now fifty minutes remaining.

**Your first task**

What is your radio call to ATC?

**Your second task**

ATC clears you to divert to Navegantes and asks for your ETA. How do you respond?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would transmit: Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, destination below minimums, fuel state five zero minutes, request divert to Navegantes. On receiving clearance, I would calculate ETA and respond: ANAC123, cleared divert Navegantes, estimating one eight minutes, maintaining four thousand.
