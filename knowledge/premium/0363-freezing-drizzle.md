# Freezing Drizzle

**Catalog ID:** 0363  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Freezing Drizzle — light intensity drizzle that freezes on contact with cold surfaces, producing ice accretion that may look gradual but can still become operationally dangerous, especially for aircraft with limited ice protection.

Freezing drizzle is often underestimated because the precipitation looks light. Professional crews still exit, report, and refuse prolonged exposure. ICAO English must distinguish freezing drizzle from freezing rain and from ordinary drizzle.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, cold-front drizzle in the South can create freezing drizzle near freezing levels. Rotor systems do not tolerate "light" ice for long.

Understanding freezing drizzle phraseology is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Freezing Drizzle is drizzle — fine water droplets — that freezes upon contact with aircraft surfaces at or below freezing temperature, causing ice accretion that requires exit from the conditions, use of ice protection if available, and timely reports to ATC.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Garoa congelante / Freezing Drizzle.

Garoa — finas gotículas de água — que congela ao contato com superfícies da aeronave em temperatura igual ou inferior a zero, causando acúmulo de gelo que exige saída das condições, uso de proteção contra gelo se disponível, e informes oportunos ao ATC.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When freezing drizzle is encountered or forecast, the crew should:

- recognize freezing drizzle as icing precipitation, not harmless light rain
- activate ice protection within approved envelopes if equipped
- request climb or descent to leave the freezing drizzle layer
- report freezing drizzle and icing intensity
- divert if ice continues to accrete or performance degrades
- avoid holds in freezing drizzle

Freezing drizzle reporting commonly includes:

- freezing drizzle at altitude
- light or moderate icing in freezing drizzle
- request climb / descent / diversion
- clear of freezing drizzle

**Important:** Drizzle droplets are small, but freezing drizzle can still produce significant ice — and may be associated with SLD conditions in some environments. Do not minimize the report because the rain looks light.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use freezing drizzle reports to:

- distinguish intensity and type of freezing precipitation for other flights
- offer altitude changes out of the drizzle layer
- keep holding patterns clear of icing precipitation
- update ATIS and approach information
- support diversion coordination when multiple aircraft exit the layer

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Cruising at five thousand feet in southern Brazil in the H130, you enter light drizzle and notice ice beginning on the windscreen. Temperature is below freezing.

You report freezing drizzle with light to moderate icing and request descent to warmer air. Approach clears descent. Ice stops accreting as you leave the layer. You continue with a clear PIREP for following traffic.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, freezing drizzle reported at five thousand, advise.
2. Approach: ANAC123, descend and maintain three thousand, warmer temperatures reported.
3. Center: ANAC123, say icing conditions in the drizzle.
4. Approach: ANAC123, previous aircraft reported freezing drizzle, expect icing.
5. Approach: ANAC123, divert approved if icing continues.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

2. ANAC123, moderate icing in freezing drizzle, request diversion.
3. ANAC123, descending three thousand, exiting freezing drizzle.

5. ANAC123, unable hold, freezing drizzle, request vectors.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Freezing drizzle fools people because it looks gentle.

Gentle precipitation. Serious ice.

Say freezing drizzle — not only "garoa" or "drizzle." The freezing word is what changes the decision.

In the H130, treat any freezing precipitation as exit weather unless you are certified and equipped for the encounter — and even then, respect intensity growth.

High priority: under-reporting creates traps for the next aircraft in the same layer.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is freezing drizzle and how would you report it?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Freezing drizzle is fine drizzle that freezes on contact with cold aircraft surfaces and causes ice accretion. I would report freezing drizzle with my altitude and icing intensity, request climb or descent to exit the layer, and divert if ice continues to build.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Calling it only "garoa" on an ICAO frequency.  
  ✔ Use freezing drizzle.

- ❌ Staying in the layer because accretion seems slow.  
  ✔ Exit — slow ice still becomes dangerous.

- ❌ Not stating icing intensity with the precipitation type.  
  ✔ Report freezing drizzle and light/moderate/severe icing.

- ❌ Accepting holds in freezing drizzle.  
  ✔ Request vectors clear of freezing precipitation.
