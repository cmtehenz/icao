# Cross Runway

**Catalog ID:** 0025  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the highest-risk instructions on the airport surface.

Cross Runway authorizes an aircraft or vehicle to proceed across an active or inactive runway at a specified point.

This instruction exists because runway incursions remain one of the most serious safety risks in aviation.

You must never cross a runway without explicit authorization.

## 📖 Meaning

Cross Runway instructs the pilot to taxi across the designated runway using the assigned route, after receiving explicit ATC authorization.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Cruze a pista.

Autorização explícita para atravessar a pista indicada durante o táxi.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Cross Runway, the pilot should:

- confirm which runway to cross
- approach the runway holding point with caution
- scan the full runway length before entering
- cross expeditiously without delay unless instructed otherwise
- continue on the assigned taxi route after crossing
- read back the instruction when part of a taxi clearance

A pilot must not cross a runway when:

- only given "taxi via" without crossing authority
- instructed to hold short of that runway
- uncertain about the clearance — ask ATC before proceeding

**FAA rule:** A clearance must be obtained prior to crossing any runway.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Cross Runway to:

- allow aircraft to reach departure runways or parking areas
- coordinate ground movement at complex airports
- protect active landing and departing traffic
- prevent runway incursions
- maintain orderly surface operations

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are taxiing your H130 at Navegantes after landing.

Ground instructs:

Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Bravo, cross runway one eight, hold short of runway zero seven.

You stop at runway one eight, scan left and right, confirm no landing or departing traffic, and cross when safe.

After crossing, you continue on Bravo and hold short of runway zero seven as instructed.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, cross runway one eight, taxi via Bravo.
2. Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Alpha, cross runway zero nine, hold short of runway one eight.
3. Ground: ANAC123, cross runway two seven left at Bravo.
4. Ground: ANAC123, hold short runway one eight. (No crossing — contrast instruction.)
5. Ground: ANAC123, cross runway three six left, contact tower on the other side.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Cross runway one eight, taxi via Bravo, ANAC123.
2. Taxi via Alpha, cross runway zero nine, hold short of runway one eight, ANAC123.
3. Cross runway two seven left at Bravo, ANAC123.
4. Holding short runway one eight, ANAC123.
5. Cross runway three six left, contact tower, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

A runway is not a taxiway.

It is a high-speed corridor for aircraft on takeoff and landing.

When Ground says Cross Runway, they are opening a controlled gate for you — for a moment.

Before you enter that gate:

- Stop at the hold short line.
- Look both directions.
- Confirm you have the correct runway.
- Cross without unnecessary delay.

If anything is unclear, stop and ask.

The worst phrase on the ground is "I thought I was cleared."

Professional pilots never cross on assumption.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

When is a pilot allowed to cross a runway during taxi?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A pilot may cross a runway only after receiving explicit ATC authorization as part of a taxi instruction. If the clearance is unclear or hold short is issued, the pilot must stop and request clarification before crossing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Crossing because the runway looks empty.  
  ✔ Cross only with explicit ATC authorization.

- ❌ Confusing hold short with cross runway.  
  ✔ Hold short means stop before the runway. Cross runway means authorized to proceed across.

- ❌ Slow unnecessary stop on the runway after crossing is authorized.  
  ✔ Cross expeditiously — do not loiter on the runway.

- ❌ Incomplete readback omitting the runway to cross.  
  ✔ Read back: Cross runway one eight, ANAC123.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Cross Runway

**Pronunciation:** kross RUN-way

**Word Stress**

- Cross → one syllable, crisp "kross"
- Runway → RUN-way

Practice:

Cross... Runway...

Together:

Cross Runway.

Then inside a complete transmission:

Taxi via Alpha, cross runway zero nine, hold short of runway one eight, ANAC123.

Say the runway number distinctly — it is safety-critical.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**Cross** only when the controller **opens the door**.

Hold short means the door is closed.

No explicit cross = door stays closed.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Bravo, cross runway one eight, hold short of runway zero seven.

**Your first task**

Read back exactly as a professional pilot.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

You are holding short of runway one eight but Ground has not said "cross." What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I must remain holding short and not enter or cross the runway. I should contact Ground and request clarification if I believe I should cross but have not received explicit authorization.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Cross Runway is a permission, not a suggestion.

It is one of the last lines of defense against runway incursions.

Read back clearly.

Stop when required.

Look before you cross.

The best pilots protect the runway even when they are still on the ground.
