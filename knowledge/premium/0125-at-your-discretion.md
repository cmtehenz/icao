# At Your Discretion

**Catalog ID:** 0125  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers At Your Discretion — an ATC phrase that returns decision-making authority to the pilot for a specific action.

At Your Discretion means: the controller is not dictating the timing — you are.

It is an invitation for pilot judgment.

In the H130, flying into Congonhas, Navegantes, or Curitiba, you may receive At Your Discretion when ATC wishes to give you flexibility on when to descend, depart, or execute a maneuver.

Understanding this phrase — and knowing how to respond, exercise, and acknowledge discretion — is an important ICAO proficiency skill.

## 📖 Meaning

At Your Discretion is an ATC phrase indicating that the pilot may execute the specified action when ready, applying their own judgment on timing, without requiring a specific ATC instruction to commence.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

A seu critério.

Frase do controle de tráfego aéreo indicando que o piloto pode executar a ação especificada quando estiver pronto, aplicando seu próprio julgamento sobre o momento, sem necessitar de uma instrução específica do controle para iniciá-la.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC uses At Your Discretion, the pilot should:

- acknowledge the instruction
- apply professional judgment on when to execute the action
- consider aircraft performance, passenger comfort, traffic, and operational factors
- begin the action when operationally ready — not necessarily immediately
- advise ATC when the action is commenced if required

At Your Discretion operations commonly include:

- descend at your discretion to a cleared level
- depart at your discretion when traffic permits
- taxi at your discretion when the ramp is clear
- turn at your discretion to reach a routing or waypoint

**Important:** At Your Discretion does not mean do it whenever you feel like it. It means exercise professional judgment within the operational context. Undue delay or ignoring the instruction entirely is not appropriate. Act when you are ready — promptly and professionally.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers use At Your Discretion to:

- reduce radio congestion by delegating timing to the pilot
- accommodate aircraft performance differences
- allow pilots to optimize descent or departure timing
- provide flexibility when traffic allows but sequencing is not critical
- reduce the number of individual control instructions needed

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at flight level one two zero inbound to Congonhas.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, descend to altitude four thousand, at your discretion.

This means: you may begin your descent to four thousand feet whenever you judge it appropriate.

You are currently twenty minutes out.

You assess: passenger comfort — a gradual descent is preferable. Performance — optimal descent begins in five minutes.

You wait five minutes, then begin:

ANAC123, leaving flight level one two zero, descending altitude four thousand.

Approach acknowledges.

The descent is smooth. Fuel is optimized. Passengers notice nothing.

At Your Discretion gave you the flexibility to make that decision professionally.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, descend to altitude four thousand, at your discretion.
2. Tower: ANAC123, depart at your discretion, no delay required.
3. Approach: ANAC123, proceed direct XINGU, at your discretion.
4. Ground: ANAC123, taxi to holding point runway one zero, at your discretion.
5. Approach: ANAC123, when ready descend to flight level one zero zero, at your discretion.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Descend altitude four thousand at our discretion, ANAC123.
2. ANAC123, depart at our discretion, wilco.
3. ANAC123, direct XINGU at our discretion, wilco.
4. ANAC123, taxi holding point runway one zero at our discretion.
5. ANAC123, descend flight level one zero zero at our discretion, wilco.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

At Your Discretion is a phrase of trust.

The controller is saying: I trust your judgment. You decide the timing.

That trust requires a professional response.

Not immediate action if the aircraft is not ready.

Not indefinite delay because "discretion" sounds like a vacation.

Professional discretion means: I will act when it is right to act.

In the H130, descending at your discretion into Congonhas might mean waiting until after the turbulence layer, or until the passengers have finished their meal service on a longer rotation.

It might mean delaying the turn to give yourself better routing.

The key word is professional.

When you exercise discretion, you are the pilot in command.

Own that responsibility.

Act on it promptly.

Advise ATC when you commence.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How would you respond if ATC said "descend at your discretion" and you needed two minutes before beginning the descent?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would acknowledge the clearance and advise ATC when I began the descent. I would say: Descend at our discretion, ANAC123. When ready to descend, I would transmit: ANAC123, leaving flight level one two zero, descending altitude four thousand. I would begin the descent as soon as operationally appropriate — not after an unreasonable delay. Discretion means professional judgment on timing, not indefinite flexibility.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Interpreting At Your Discretion as permission to delay indefinitely.  
  ✔ Act when ready, promptly and professionally. Inform ATC when you commence.

- ❌ Not reading back the cleared level or action when acknowledging.  
  ✔ Include the cleared action in the readback: Descend altitude four thousand at our discretion, ANAC123.

- ❌ Commencing the action without advising ATC when a position report is expected.  
  ✔ Advise ATC when you begin: ANAC123, leaving flight level one two zero.

- ❌ Confusing At Your Discretion with no restriction, and violating traffic sequencing.  
  ✔ Discretion applies to timing, not to ignoring clearance limits or separation requirements.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** At Your Discretion

**Pronunciation:** at yor dis-KREH-shun

**Word Stress**

- At → at (short)
- Your → yor (short, unstressed in natural speech)
- Discretion → dis-KREH-shun (stress on second syllable)

Practice:

At... Your... Discretion... At Your Discretion.

Together:

Descend altitude four thousand at our discretion, ANAC123.

Note: in readback, pilots say "our discretion" (speaking as crew) — "at our discretion, ANAC123."

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Curitiba Approach transmits: "ANAC123, descend to altitude five thousand, at your discretion."

**Your first task**

You need ninety seconds before starting the descent due to a configuration change. What do you say, and what do you transmit when you begin?

**Your second task**

ATC then asks: "ANAC123, confirm you are leaving flight level?" You have not yet started. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would acknowledge: Descend altitude five thousand at our discretion, ANAC123. After ninety seconds I would transmit: ANAC123, leaving flight level one one zero, descending altitude five thousand. If ATC queried before I had started, I would advise: ANAC123, not yet descending, commencing in one minute. At Your Discretion requires professional judgment — but if ATC queries, that signals the timing has become operationally significant and I should act promptly.
