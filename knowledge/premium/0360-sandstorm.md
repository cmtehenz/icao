# Sandstorm

**Catalog ID:** 0360  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Sandstorm — a strong wind event that lifts sand into the air, severely reducing visibility and creating abrasive conditions for engines, blades, and windscreens, often requiring delay, diversion, or holding until the storm passes.

Sandstorms are high-impact low-visibility events. ICAO English must state sandstorm, visibility if known, and clear intentions: hold, divert, or unable approach.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, true desert sandstorms are uncommon in most training areas, but the concept appears in ICAO exams and in operations over dry interior regions and international deployments. Treat it as a visibility and engine-ingestion hazard.

Understanding sandstorm phraseology is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Sandstorm is a meteorological event in which strong winds lift sand particles into the atmosphere, causing very low visibility and abrasive conditions hazardous to flight, typically requiring avoidance, delay, or diversion rather than penetration of the storm.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Tempestade de areia / Sandstorm.

Evento meteorológico em que ventos fortes levantam partículas de areia na atmosfera, causando visibilidade muito baixa e condições abrasivas perigosas ao voo, tipicamente exigindo desvio, atraso ou espera em vez de penetração na tempestade.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a sandstorm affects operations, the crew should:

- obtain visibility and weather trend from ATIS, Tower, or SIGMET/METAR
- do not attempt approach or low-level cruise into zero or near-zero visibility sand
- request hold, delay, or divert to a clear alternate
- protect engines and rotors — avoid unnecessary ground operations in blowing sand
- report sandstorm observations to ATC for other traffic
- after the storm, inspect for abrasion and contamination before next flight as required

Sandstorm reporting commonly includes:

- sandstorm over the field / ahead on route
- visibility meters or runway visual range if known
- request hold / divert
- unable approach due to sandstorm

**Important:** Sandstorm and dust storm are related but not identical — sand particles are generally heavier and stay closer to the surface; still, both destroy visibility. Use the term that matches the report or what you observe.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers manage sandstorm information to:

- close or restrict approaches when visibility collapses
- pass RVR/visibility and storm movement
- sequence holds and diversions
- protect ground operations and departing traffic
- relay PIREPs of blowing sand along routes

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Diverting toward an interior Brazilian alternate in the H130, METAR shows visibility dropping in a sandstorm. Approach advises sandstorm over the field, visibility five hundred meters.

You request diversion to a coastal alternate still reporting VMC. You do not attempt a visual approach into the sand wall. After landing at the alternate you report the diversion reason as sandstorm at destination.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, sandstorm over the field, visibility five hundred meters, advise intentions.
2. Tower: ANAC123, runway visual range below minima, expect delay.
3. Approach: ANAC123, divert approved, contact Center on...
4. Center: ANAC123, sandstorm reported along your route, advise.
5. Approach: ANAC123, sandstorm moving east, expect improvement in one hour.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, sandstorm over the field, request divert.
2. ANAC123, unable approach due to sandstorm, request hold.
3. ANAC123, diverting for sandstorm, request routing to alternate.
4. ANAC123, sandstorm ahead, request weather deviation.
5. ANAC123, field not in sight, sandstorm, request vectors for delay.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Sandstorm is a wall you do not fly through for schedule.

Visibility dies. Engines ingest. Blades erode.

Brazilian students may never see a classic desert sandstorm in São Paulo — still master the English. Examiners use it.

In the H130, low-level flight into blowing sand is a trap. Divert or wait. Say sandstorm, not only "baixa visibilidade," so the hazard type is clear.

High priority because penetration decisions are often irreversible at low altitude.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Destination is reporting a sandstorm with very low visibility. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report unable to approach due to sandstorm and request hold if improvement is expected soon, or request diversion to a suitable alternate. I would not attempt a visual approach into the sandstorm.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Pushing a visual approach into a sand wall.  
  ✔ Divert or hold — do not penetrate.

- ❌ Saying only "não dá para ver" without the hazard name.  
  ✔ Use sandstorm and request hold or divert.

- ❌ Confusing sandstorm with ordinary rain shower language.  
  ✔ Name the phenomenon correctly for ATC and meteorology.

- ❌ Starting engines for repositioning in heavy blowing sand without need.  
  ✔ Protect the aircraft on the ground when possible.
