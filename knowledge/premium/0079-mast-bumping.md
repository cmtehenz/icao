# Mast Bumping

**Catalog ID:** 0079  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers mast bumping — a catastrophic risk in teetering-rotor helicopters.

Mast Bumping occurs when the rotor hub contacts the mast during low-G or extreme flapping conditions, which can sever the mast.

In the H130, the rotor system design differs from classic teetering systems, but low-G awareness and smooth control remain essential for all helicopter pilots.

You must avoid low-G maneuvers, recover smoothly if low-G is encountered, and communicate if structural damage is suspected.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Mast Bumping is contact between the rotor hub and the mast, typically during low-G flight in teetering-rotor helicopters, which can cause catastrophic mast failure.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Batida no mastro (mast bumping).

Contato entre o cubo do rotor e o mastro, tipicamente em voo de baixo G em helicópteros de rotor teetering, podendo causar falha catastrófica do mastro.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Mast Bumping risk exists, the pilot should:

- avoid abrupt forward cyclic that produces low-G conditions
- recover from low-G by gently applying aft cyclic — never abrupt lateral cyclic
- avoid steep pushovers and abrupt control inputs in turbulence
- declare Mayday immediately if mast bumping or structural damage is suspected
- land as soon as practicable for inspection after any suspected event

Mast Bumping reporting commonly includes:

- suspected mast bumping or low-G event
- abnormal vibration after recovery
- request immediate landing
- souls on board and position

**Important:** Mast bumping can be fatal within seconds. Prevention is the only reliable strategy. If suspected, Mayday and land immediately.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Mast Bumping declarations to:

- clear all traffic from the aircraft's route immediately
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable landing area
- coordinate emergency services on the ground
- request souls on board and nature of the problem
- prepare for possible catastrophic structural failure

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are descending through light turbulence near Florianópolis in your H130.

An abrupt forward cyclic input produces a brief low-G sensation.

You gently apply aft cyclic and restore positive G without lateral inputs.

You feel unusual vibration afterward.

You transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, suspected mast bumping after low-G event, request immediate landing.

Approach clears traffic and vectors you direct to the airport.

You land for immediate inspection.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Mast bumping is a teetering-rotor nightmare.

Low G. Hub hits mast. Mast can fail.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Avoid low G. Recover gently. Land and inspect.

Never push over abruptly.

Never apply abrupt lateral cyclic in low G.

Gentle aft cyclic restores positive load.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "bateu no mastro" or "low G" on frequency.

Use suspected mast bumping or low-G event — ICAO-compatible operational English.

Even if your H130 rotor design differs from classic teetering systems, low-G discipline still matters.

Train smooth control inputs until abrupt pushovers never happen.

If you suspect contact — Mayday and land. Do not continue the flight.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is mast bumping and what would you tell ATC if you suspected it?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Mast bumping is contact between the rotor hub and the mast, usually during low-G flight in teetering-rotor helicopters. If I suspected mast bumping, I would declare Mayday Mayday Mayday, report suspected mast bumping or low-G event with abnormal vibration, state my position and souls on board, and request immediate landing for inspection.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing flight after a low-G event with vibration.  
  ✔ Land immediately for inspection — structural damage may be developing.

- ❌ Saying "bateu no mastro" without standard English.  
  ✔ Use suspected mast bumping or low-G event.

- ❌ Applying abrupt lateral cyclic during low G.  
  ✔ Recover with gentle aft cyclic only.

- ❌ Treating mast bumping as Pan Pan.  
  ✔ Suspected mast bumping is distress — Mayday.
