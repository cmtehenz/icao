# Drone Near Airport

**Catalog ID:** 0223  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Drone Near Airport — the advisory or report that an unmanned aircraft system (UAS/drone) is operating or sighted near the aerodrome, creating a collision and disruption hazard for manned aircraft.

Drone near airport is high-priority traffic conflict language. ATC may suspend arrivals and departures, issue traffic advisories, or ask pilots to report drone sightings. Pilots must know how to report position, altitude estimate, and direction of flight without cluttering the frequency.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations at Congonhas, Santos Dumont, Guarulhos, Curitiba, and Florianópolis, drones are a real urban risk — especially at low level where helicopters train and transit. Rotorcraft often share the same altitude band as unauthorized drones. Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 speaking both test calm, precise reporting.

Understanding how to acknowledge drone advisories, report sightings, and change approach or departure plans is essential for ICAO proficiency and collision avoidance.

## 📖 Meaning

Drone Near Airport means an unmanned aircraft is reported or observed in the vicinity of the aerodrome or its approach and departure paths, creating a potential collision hazard and often requiring traffic advisories, delays, missed approaches, or temporary suspension of operations.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Drone near airport / Drone / VANT próximo ao aeroporto.

Significa que uma aeronave não tripulada foi reportada ou observada nas proximidades do aeródromo ou das trajetórias de aproximação e decolagem, criando risco de colisão e frequentemente exigindo avisos de tráfego, atrasos, arremetidas ou suspensão temporária das operações.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When a drone is reported near the airport, the pilot should:

- acknowledge the traffic advisory and increase visual scan
- be prepared for go-around, hold, or delay without argument
- report any drone you see: position relative to the field, estimated altitude, color/size if useful, direction of movement
- avoid maneuvering aggressively toward the drone to "get a better look"
- for helicopters, remember you may be at the same height as the drone — prioritize separation
- expect possible temporary closure of a runway or circuit

Drone near airport situations commonly include:

- unauthorized recreational drones in the circuit
- sightings on final or upwind
- multiple conflicting pilot reports
- police or authority coordination while operations pause
- false alarms that still require a cautious response until cleared

**Important:** A drone report is treated as real until proven otherwise. In the H130, do not climb or turn into unknown airspace without ATC. If you lose sight of the drone, say so. If you still see it, keep concise updates.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers advise drone near airport to:

- protect manned aircraft from mid-air collision with UAS
- share real-time sightings across all traffic
- justify temporary holds, go-arounds, or runway suspension
- coordinate with airport authority and security responders
- collect pilot reports that help locate the drone

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on downwind for Santos Dumont in the H130 on a VFR training circuit.

Tower: ANAC123, traffic information, drone reported northeast of the field at low altitude, advise if in sight.

You scan. You see a small quadcopter off the northeast approach path.

You report the drone in sight, northeast, estimated below circuit altitude, moving west. Tower stops departures and extends your downwind. The drone descends behind buildings. Operations resume only after reports go cold.

Your short report gave tower the picture they needed.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, drone reported north of the field, advise if in sight.
2. Approach: ANAC123, delay vectors, drone near the airport, expect holding.
3. Tower: ANAC123, go around, drone reported on final.
4. Tower: ANAC123, report any drone activity on downwind.
5. Ground: ANAC123, hold position, departures suspended, drone near airport.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Drones turned low-level flying into a new see-and-avoid problem.

Helicopters live in the drone band. That means your scan is not optional on Brazilian city circuits — Santos Dumont and Congonhas especially.

When tower says drone reported, answer with eyes, not opinions. In sight or not in sight. If in sight: where, how high roughly, which way it moves. Then fly the clearance — go around, extend, hold.

Part 3 loves this scenario because it mixes traffic English with decision English. Keep the answer ordered: receive advisory, scan, report, comply with go-around or delay, avoid chasing the drone.

Never assume a drone will give way. Assume you must separate.

Unauthorized drones are illegal; your job is not enforcement. Your job is survival and clear radio.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Tower reports a drone near the airport while you are on final. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would be prepared to go around immediately if instructed or if I assess a collision risk. I would acknowledge the drone advisory and increase my visual scan along the approach path. If I see the drone, I would report its position, approximate altitude, and direction of movement in a short transmission. I would not continue the approach into a conflict and I would not maneuver toward the drone to observe it more closely. After the go-around or delay, I would follow ATC vectors and continue reports if the drone remains in sight.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing final after a drone-on-final go-around instruction.  
  ✔ Go around immediately; argue later if at all — never on short final.

- ❌ Long emotional reports about "irresponsible people flying drones."  
  ✔ Give position, height estimate, and movement only.

- ❌ Climbing or turning toward the drone without ATC.  
  ✔ Maintain assigned path or cleared missed approach; prioritize separation.

- ❌ Saying "negative contact" once and stopping the scan.  
  ✔ Keep looking; drones are small and intermittent visually.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**DRONE** — **D**etect with a deliberate scan, **R**eport position and movement briefly, **O**bey go-around or hold, **N**ever chase it, **E**xtend separation — especially in the helicopter band.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Drone near airport is a see-and-avoid emergency with modern packaging.

Scan. Report briefly. Go around when told — or when required.

Do not chase. Do not lecture on frequency. Do not assume the drone will miss you.

In the H130 you share the drone's altitude. That makes your discipline the difference.

High-priority English. High-priority eyes.
