# Persons on Board

**Catalog ID:** 0334  
**Category:** Passenger  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Persons on Board — the standard report of how many people are in the aircraft, including crew and passengers, used in emergencies, urgency situations, and whenever ATC requests the count.

Persons on Board is High priority because rescue, fire, and medical services plan from that number. ICAO exams also use "souls on board." Both appear in professional English; know them and answer immediately.

For H130 operations, the number is usually small — often two to six — but accuracy still matters. Count everyone before engine start and update if the load changes. In emergencies, ATC will ask; hesitation looks unprepared.

Understanding how to report persons on board in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Persons on Board is the total number of people in the aircraft, including flight crew, cabin crew if any, and passengers, reported to ATC for emergency coordination, search and rescue planning, and operational confirmation.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Pessoas a bordo / Persons on Board.

Número total de pessoas na aeronave, incluindo tripulação de voo, tripulação de cabine se houver, e passageiros, informado ao controle para coordenação de emergência, planejamento de busca e salvamento e confirmação operacional.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC requests persons on board, or when declaring an emergency, the crew should:

- report the total number of people currently on the aircraft
- include crew and passengers — do not report passengers only unless asked specifically
- know the number before departure and update after any change
- provide the figure immediately during Mayday or Pan Pan exchanges
- pair with fuel remaining when ATC asks for both

Persons on Board communication commonly includes:

- souls on board four
- persons on board six
- say souls on board
- confirm number of persons on board

**Important:** Souls on board and persons on board mean the same operational count in ATC emergency coordination. On the H130 with no cabin crew, count pilot(s) plus passengers. Do not forget an infant. Do not give an old company schedule number if someone deplaned.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers request Persons on Board to:

- inform fire, rescue, and medical services how many people to expect
- plan evacuation and search effort if needed
- complete emergency documentation and coordination
- confirm load context during diversions and priority landings
- support SAR if radio contact is later lost

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You declare a medical urgency inbound to Congonhas in the H130.

Approach responds:

Approach: ANAC123, roger Pan Pan, say souls on board and fuel remaining.

You answer without delay:

ANAC123, souls on board four, fuel remaining one hour.

Approach relays the number to emergency services. Ambulance and fire know exactly how many people are arriving. Your immediate, accurate count made their plan possible.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board.
2. Approach: ANAC123, roger Mayday, say persons on board and fuel remaining.
3. Tower: ANAC123, confirm number of persons on board.
4. Control: ANAC123, say souls on board for rescue services.
5. Approach: ANAC123, medical emergency copied, souls on board?

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, souls on board four.
2. ANAC123, persons on board six, fuel remaining one hour.
3. ANAC123, souls on board three, two passengers and one pilot.
4. ANAC123, souls on board five, request medical assistance.
5. ANAC123, persons on board four, all conscious.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Persons on board is a number you must own before you need it.

In every serious radio event, ATC will ask. The professional pilot answers in one breath.

Souls on board is traditional emergency English. Persons on board is equally understood. Use either; just be consistent and fast.

Brazilian H130 flights change passenger counts often between legs. Recount after each boarding. A wrong number sends the wrong number of rescuers.

Do not philosophize on frequency about who counts as crew. Total living people on the aircraft. That is the answer.

Know it cold.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What does "say souls on board" mean, and who is included in the number?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

It means ATC wants the total number of people in the aircraft. I include all crew and all passengers — everyone on board. I should answer immediately, especially during an emergency or urgency call.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reporting only passengers and forgetting the pilot.  
  ✔ Include all persons on board.

- ❌ Hesitating or guessing during a Mayday.  
  ✔ Know the number before departure; update when it changes.

- ❌ Confusing souls on board with passenger count only.  
  ✔ Souls on board = everyone.

- ❌ Giving an outdated number after a passenger deplaned.  
  ✔ Recount after any boarding change.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You have declared Pan Pan for a medical emergency. ATC says: "ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining."

You have one pilot and three passengers. Fuel for one hour twenty minutes.

**Your first task**

What do you reply?

**Your second task**

Why is this information critical for emergency services?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, souls on board four, fuel remaining one hour two zero minutes. Second task: Rescue and medical services need the exact number of people to plan response, equipment, and search if required. An accurate count saves time and prevents incomplete rescue effort.
