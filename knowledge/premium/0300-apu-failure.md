# APU Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0300  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers APU Failure — loss of auxiliary power unit function affecting electrical or pneumatic support on the ground or in flight, requiring checklist actions and clear ATC communication when the failure changes start capability, systems availability, or dispatch plans. The H130 may have limited or no APU compared with transport aircraft — still teach ICAO exam English for APU failure, then transfer the reporting structure to loss of auxiliary power or ground-power dependence.

APU failure is High priority because it can strand you on the ground, complicate engine start, or reduce electrical/bleed options before departure or after landing.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for APU failure, then map the same clarity to helicopter systems limitations under Brazilian ops.

Understanding APU failure in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

APU Failure is the loss of normal auxiliary power unit operation, which may remove electrical and/or pneumatic support used for engine start, air conditioning, or backup power, requiring checklist actions and clear notification to ATC or ground services when the failure affects start, taxi, departure delay, or need for external power.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha do APU / APU Failure.

Perda do funcionamento normal da unidade auxiliar de potência, o que pode remover suporte elétrico e/ou pneumático usado para partida de motor, ar-condicionado ou energia de reserva, exigindo ações de checklist e notificação clara ao ATC ou serviços de solo quando a falha afetar a partida, o táxi, o atraso na decolagem ou a necessidade de energia externa.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the APU fails, the crew should:

- confirm APU indications and apply AFM/QRH APU failure procedures
- assess impact on electrical power, bleed air, and engine start capability
- request ground power or air start cart if required before departure
- advise ATC of delay, inability to start, or need to return to stand if already taxiing
- declare Pan Pan only if the failure creates an in-flight systems urgency — most APU failures are operational, not distress
- for H130: transfer the same clear English to loss of auxiliary power or dependence on ground support

APU failure reporting commonly includes:

- APU failure / APU inoperative
- request ground power / air start / return to stand
- expect delay / unable to depart without external power
- souls and fuel when asked if returning or delaying on frequency
- advise if electrical load is limited after landing

**Important:** On the ICAO exam, distinguish APU failure from APU fire. Failure is a systems loss; fire is Critical. In H130 ops, APU may be limited — retain exam vocabulary and transfer "need external power / delayed departure" English.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers and ground agencies use APU failure information to:

- plan gate or stand return for external power
- sequence delayed departures without blocking taxiways
- coordinate ground support equipment
- understand reduced systems capability after landing
- avoid treating every APU call as a fire emergency

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Exam-style scenario: at Congonhas, APU fails before engine start. You need ground power.

You transmit:

Ground, ANAC123, APU failure, request ground power and expect delay.

If already taxiing and unable to continue without APU support for systems:

ANAC123, APU failure, request return to stand for ground power.

If in flight the APU was providing backup electrical support and load shedding becomes necessary, advise ATC of the systems limitation and intentions — without inventing a fire.

For H130 reality, use the same clarity when you need external power or cannot complete a start as planned.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, roger APU failure, hold position, ground power arranged.
2. Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Alpha, return to stand two four approved.
3. Tower: ANAC123, roger delay for ground power, report ready for departure.
4. Approach: ANAC123, roger APU inoperative, say if any systems limitation for arrival.
5. Tower: ANAC123, after landing expect ground power on stand, advise if electrical limited.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

4. ANAC123, APU inoperative, no systems limitation for approach, will need ground power after landing.
5. ANAC123, ready for departure after ground power connected.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

APU failure is usually an operational problem, not a Mayday — unless it cascades into a real in-flight emergency.

Say "APU failure" or "APU inoperative." Then say what you need: ground power, air start, return to stand, or delay.

Do not dramatize a failed APU into a fire call. Do not hide a failed APU when it blocks departure and occupies a taxiway.

In the H130, classic APU dependence may be rare. ICAO still expects the vocabulary. Learn it. Transfer the communication pattern: name the system loss, state the operational effect, ask for support.

Brazilian examiners separate APU failure from APU fire. You must too.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

The APU fails before engine start and you need external power. What do you do, and what do you say to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would apply the APU failure checklist and determine whether ground power or an air start unit is required. I would advise ground control of APU failure, request ground power, and report an expected delay. If already taxiing, I would request return to stand. I would not declare Mayday for a simple APU failure unless it creates a genuine in-flight emergency. On a helicopter with limited or no APU, I would use the same clear structure when external power or start support is required.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Declaring Mayday for a routine APU failure on the ground.  
  ✔ Use plain operational English unless fire or real distress exists.

- ❌ Confusing APU failure with APU fire on the oral.  
  ✔ Failure = systems loss; fire = Mayday emergency.

- ❌ Occupying the taxiway without telling ATC you cannot depart.  
  ✔ Request return to stand or hold and state APU failure early.

- ❌ Skipping APU failure study because H130 APU is limited.  
  ✔ Learn exam English; transfer external-power and delay language.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit on ground frequency before start?
