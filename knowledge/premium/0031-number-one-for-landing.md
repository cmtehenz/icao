# Number One for Landing

**Catalog ID:** 0031  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers sequence position — a simple phrase that tells you where you stand in the landing queue.

Number One for Landing means you are first in line to land.

You may report this to Tower, or Tower may confirm it to you.

Either way, it is an acknowledgment of your position in the sequence — not a landing clearance by itself.

Professional pilots understand the difference between being number one and being cleared to land.

## 📖 Meaning

Number One for Landing indicates that the aircraft is first in the landing sequence for the specified runway.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Número um para pouso.

A aeronave é a primeira na sequência de pouso na pista indicada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Number One for Landing applies, the pilot should:

- confirm the runway designator
- monitor for traffic still on the runway or on short final ahead
- prepare for landing clearance
- not assume landing clearance until Tower issues cleared to land
- advise Tower if sequence position is unclear

Number One for Landing is used when:

- the pilot reports position in the pattern
- Tower confirms sequence before landing clearance
- traffic ahead has landed or vacated the runway
- sequencing in VFR or visual conditions

**Important:** Number one is sequence information. Cleared to Land is the authorization to land.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Tower uses Number One for Landing to:

- confirm landing sequence position
- reduce uncertainty in busy traffic patterns
- coordinate landing clearances efficiently
- prepare pilots for imminent landing authorization
- manage traffic flow at airports like Congonhas and Florianópolis

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on final approach to runway two one at Navegantes in your H130.

The preceding aircraft has vacated the runway.

You report to Tower:

Tower, ANAC123 number one for landing runway two one.

Tower replies:

ANAC123, number one, runway two one, cleared to land.

You read back the landing clearance and complete the approach.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, number one, runway two one, cleared to land.
2. Tower: ANAC123, confirm you are number one for landing.
3. Tower: ANAC123, number two, follow the traffic on final.
4. Tower: ANAC123, extend downwind, traffic on a two-mile final.
5. Tower: ANAC123, wind two one zero at one two, runway two one.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Tower, ANAC123 number one for landing runway two one.
2. ANAC123, number one for landing runway two one.
3. ANAC123, traffic in sight, number one.
4. ANAC123, confirm number one for landing.
5. ANAC123, not number one, traffic ahead in sight.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Think of a bakery queue.

Being first in line does not mean you have your bread yet.

You still wait for the baker to hand it to you.

Number one for landing is your place in line.

Cleared to land is the baker handing you the bread.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "first to land" or "primeiro da fila" on frequency.

Use standard ICAO phraseology: number one for landing runway two one.

Always include the runway designator.

And never assume number one without Tower confirmation — they may have traffic you cannot see.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How would you tell Tower you are number one for landing?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report: Tower, ANAC123 number one for landing runway two one. I include my callsign, number one for landing, and the runway designator.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "first to land" instead of number one for landing.  
  ✔ Use standard phraseology: number one for landing runway XX.

- ❌ Assuming number one without Tower confirmation.  
  ✔ Wait for Tower to confirm or correct your sequence position.

- ❌ Omitting the runway number in the report.  
  ✔ Always include the runway designator.

- ❌ Treating number one as landing clearance.  
  ✔ Wait for cleared to land before landing.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

Tower: ANAC123, confirm you are number one for landing.

**Your first task**

Respond appropriately if you are indeed number one for runway two one.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

Tower says you are number one but has not issued cleared to land. May you land?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

No. Number one confirms my sequence position only. I must receive cleared to land from Tower before landing. I continue the approach prepared to land when clearance is issued.
