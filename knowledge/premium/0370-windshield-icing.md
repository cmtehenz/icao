# Windshield Icing

**Catalog ID:** 0370  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Windshield Icing — ice accretion on the windshield that reduces or destroys forward vision during flight, approach, or hover.

Windshield icing is a vision emergency. Instruments can bring you to minima; they cannot replace a clear view of the runway, helipad, or hover references. In the H130, degraded forward vision threatens the visual segment and any VFR portion of the flight.

For Brazilian operations — freezing mist into Curitiba, winter drizzle over the plateau, cold-soak arrivals after cruise — windshield ice demands heat if available, exit from icing, go-around if vision is inadequate, and clear ATC communication.

Understanding how to report windshield icing and request diversion or priority in ICAO English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Windshield Icing is the formation of ice on the aircraft windshield due to flight in supercooled moisture or freezing precipitation, which obscures forward visibility and may require windshield heat, exit from icing conditions, go-around, or diversion when visual references for landing or hover cannot be maintained.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Gelo no para-brisa / Windshield Icing.

Formação de gelo no para-brisa da aeronave devido ao voo em umidade super-resfriada ou precipitação congelante, o que obscurece a visibilidade frontal e pode exigir aquecimento do para-brisa, saída das condições de gelo, arremetida ou desvio quando as referências visuais para pouso ou pairado não puderem ser mantidas.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When windshield icing occurs, the crew should:

- select windshield heat / anti-ice per AFM if installed and operative
- exit icing conditions to stop further accretion
- assess whether forward vision is adequate for approach and landing
- go around or divert if the windshield remains obscured at minima
- advise ATC of windshield icing and reduced forward visibility
- avoid forcing a landing without visual references

Windshield icing scenarios commonly include:

- ice sheet or rime on the windshield in freezing cloud
- windshield heat failure combined with icing
- loss of runway or helipad visual cues on short final
- request for diversion to better conditions

**Important:** Side-window vision is not a substitute for a clear windshield on a helicopter approach to a confined pad or runway. If you cannot see what you must land on, go around and tell ATC why.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use windshield icing reports to:

- approve go-arounds and missed approaches without delay
- clear diversions to airports with better weather
- provide priority approaches when vision is marginal but still workable
- understand sudden missed approaches that are not traffic-related
- warn following aircraft of icing on the approach

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on the ILS into Curitiba. Freezing drizzle. Windshield heat is on, but ice is still building at the edges and reducing forward view. At minima you cannot see the runway lights clearly.

You transmit:

Curitiba Tower, ANAC123, going around, windshield icing, reduced forward visibility, request vectors for diversion.

Tower: ANAC123, roger go-around, climb runway heading three thousand, contact approach.

You clean up, climb, and coordinate the diversion with Approach.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Windshield icing is not "a little frost." It is the difference between a landing and a press conference.

In the H130, hover and landing are visual tasks. Obscured glass means you stop the approach.

Use heat early. Exit the freezing moisture. If the windshield is still opaque at minima — go around. Say windshield icing. That phrase explains the missed approach in three words.

Brazilian winter approaches into Curitiba and Porto Alegre see this. Crews who push below minima with a frozen windshield are gambling with terrain and obstacles.

Do not gamble. Go around. Divert. Report.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Ice on the windshield prevents you from seeing the runway at minima. What do you do and what do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would execute a go-around immediately because forward visual references are inadequate for landing. I would advise ATC that I am going around due to windshield icing and reduced forward visibility, then request vectors for another approach only if conditions improve, or request diversion to an alternate with better weather. I would keep windshield heat selected if available and avoid remaining in icing that continues to obscure the windshield. In the H130 I will not attempt to land without a usable forward view.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying only "going around" without the reason.  
  ✔ Add: windshield icing, reduced forward visibility.

- ❌ Trying to peek through a clear corner and continue below minima.  
  ✔ If required visual references are not in sight, go around.

- ❌ Forgetting to request diversion after repeated icing on final.  
  ✔ One failed visual segment due ice is enough — divert.

- ❌ Confusing windshield fogging with icing on the radio.  
  ✔ Use the correct term; icing implies freezing moisture and may need different handling.
