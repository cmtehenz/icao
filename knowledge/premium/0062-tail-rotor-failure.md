# Tail Rotor Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0062  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the most critical helicopter emergencies.

Tail Rotor Failure means the tail rotor has lost effectiveness — yaw control is degraded or lost entirely.

In the H130, the tail rotor counteracts main rotor torque and provides directional control.

Without tail rotor authority, the helicopter may enter an uncontrolled yaw or spin.

You must aviate first, apply emergency procedures, then communicate with Mayday.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Tail Rotor Failure is the loss of tail rotor effectiveness or drive, resulting in degraded or lost yaw control.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha do rotor de cauda.

Perda de efetividade ou acionamento do rotor de cauda, resultando em controle de guinada degradado ou perdido.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Tail Rotor Failure occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the absolute first priority
- apply tail rotor failure emergency procedures from the flight manual
- reduce power and airspeed as appropriate for the failure type
- declare Mayday immediately
- request vectors to nearest suitable landing area

Tail Rotor Failure reporting commonly includes:

- tail rotor failure or loss of tail rotor effectiveness
- unable to control yaw
- request immediate landing
- souls on board and position

**Important:** Tail rotor failure is always a distress situation — Mayday. Yaw control is critical for safe helicopter flight. Aviate first — then communicate clearly with standard phraseology.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Tail Rotor Failure declarations to:

- clear all traffic from the aircraft's route immediately
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable landing area
- coordinate emergency services on the ground
- request souls on board and exact position
- prepare for possible crash landing or autorotation

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are at five hundred feet on approach to Navegantes helipad in your H130.

Suddenly the helicopter yaws uncontrollably to the right — tail rotor drive has failed.

You lower collective, apply anti-torque procedures, and fight to maintain heading.

When partially stable, you transmit:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, Navegantes Tower, ANAC123, tail rotor failure, unable to control yaw, request immediate landing helipad.

Tower clears all traffic and emergency services respond.

You execute a running landing on the helipad and secure the aircraft.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, Mayday acknowledged, all traffic cleared, say souls on board and position.
2. Tower: ANAC123, cleared direct helipad, emergency services standing by.
3. Approach: ANAC123, radar contact, say intentions and souls on board.
4. Tower: ANAC123, wind two seven zero at one zero knots, cleared to land any area.
5. Tower: ANAC123, emergency vehicles dispatched to helipad.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Tail rotor failure is one of the most dangerous helicopter emergencies.

Two types matter:

Loss of tail rotor effectiveness — LTE — often at low speed and high power.

Complete drive failure — total loss of yaw control.

The sequence every professional pilot learns:

Aviate. Procedure. Mayday.

Fly the aircraft first.

Apply the correct emergency procedure from the flight manual.

Do not chase the radio while spinning.

When you have partial control — Mayday immediately.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "perdeu o rotor de cauda" or "girando" on frequency.

Use tail rotor failure or loss of tail rotor effectiveness — ICAO standard terms.

In the H130, train the tail rotor failure procedure until it is muscle memory.

You may have seconds to act.

The radio call comes after you start flying the procedure — not before.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if you experienced tail rotor failure in flight?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would maintain aircraft control and apply tail rotor failure emergency procedures. I would declare Mayday Mayday Mayday, state my callsign, report tail rotor failure or loss of tail rotor effectiveness, state my position and souls on board, and request immediate landing at the nearest suitable area. I would provide updates as the situation develops.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Trying to radio Mayday before applying emergency procedures.  
  ✔ Aviate first — apply tail rotor failure procedure, then transmit.

- ❌ Saying "rotor de cauda parou" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use tail rotor failure or loss of tail rotor effectiveness.

- ❌ Increasing power when yaw becomes uncontrollable.  
  ✔ Follow flight manual — often reduce power and airspeed.

- ❌ Not declaring Mayday — treating it as urgency.  
  ✔ Tail rotor failure is always distress — Mayday.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are at three hundred feet on final approach to Congonhas helipad when the helicopter yaws uncontrollably in your H130.

**Your first task**

After applying emergency procedures and gaining partial control, transmit your distress declaration.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

Tower asks for your souls on board. What do you report?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report clearly: tail rotor failure, ANAC123, souls on board two. I would state my position, confirm I am attempting immediate landing at Congonhas helipad, and describe whether yaw control is partial or lost.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Tail Rotor Failure is a fight for yaw control.

Aviate first.

Apply the procedure.

Mayday when you can speak.

Land immediately.

Never treat this as Pan Pan.

The radio call does not restore tail rotor authority — but a clear call helps ATC clear the landing area.

Train the procedure until it is reflex. Fly the aircraft. Speak like a professional.
