# Helideck Winching

**Catalog ID:** 0410  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Helideck Winching — transferring personnel or equipment between helicopter and vessel or platform by hoist when a landing is not possible or not preferred.

Winching is highly relevant H130 capability for Brazilian maritime and offshore support: vessel transfers in higher sea states, emergency evacuation, and operations where the deck is obstructed or unavailable for landing.

You must communicate winch on station, hover, transfer progress, and emergencies in clear ICAO Part 2 and Part 3 English.

## 📖 Meaning

Helideck Winching is a helicopter hoist operation conducted over a ship or offshore installation to transfer people or equipment without landing, requiring a stable hover, clear crew coordination, and continuous communication of winch status to the vessel and ATC as required.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Içamento / winching em helideck ou embarcação.

Operação de guincho de helicóptero sobre navio ou instalação offshore para transferir pessoas ou equipamento sem pousar, exigindo pairado estável, coordenação clara de tripulação e comunicação contínua do status do guincho com a embarcação e o ATC conforme necessário.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When conducting Helideck Winching, the pilot should:

- confirm the vessel or platform is ready for winch operations before descending to hover
- report on station and establishing hover for winch
- maintain power margin and position references throughout the transfer
- advise when winch complete and lifting away
- declare urgency immediately if the cable fouls, a person is in difficulty, or hover performance is lost

Winching reporting commonly includes:

- request winch operations, landing not available
- on station, establishing hover for winch
- winch in progress / winch complete
- lifting away, request departure

**Important:** Winching extends time in the hover over water or steel. Fuel, power, and sea state limits matter more than schedule. If the hover becomes unsafe, abort the winch and climb away — then tell everyone.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers support Helideck Winching by:

- protecting the hover area from conflicting traffic
- noting extended on-station times over vessels
- coordinating SAR assets if a winch emergency develops
- sequencing the helicopter back into coastal airspace after transfer
- maintaining awareness of overwater operations without a landing

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

A vessel deck off Rio is obstructed. Your H130 is tasked to winch a technician aboard.

You transmit:

Rio Approach, ANAC123, ten miles south, descending for winch operations on support vessel, landing not available, will report on station.

On station:

ANAC123, on station, establishing hover for winch, expect one zero minutes.

When complete:

ANAC123, winch complete, lifting away, request coastal routing.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger winch operations, report on station, no conflicting traffic.
2. Control: ANAC123, advise when winch complete, remain clear of airway.
3. Approach: ANAC123, say fuel state and expected hover time.
4. Control: ANAC123, roger winch emergency, SAR alerted, say intentions.
5. Approach: ANAC123, roger lifting, cleared coastal transit, report field in sight.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, winch operations, will report on station.
2. ANAC123, on station, hover for winch, expect one zero minutes, fuel one hour four zero.
3. ANAC123, winch complete, lifting away, request coastal routing.
4. ANAC123, pan-pan, winch difficulty, climbing away, request remain clear.
5. ANAC123, field in sight, descending for landing.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Winching English is time and status: on station, winch in progress, winch complete, lifting.

ATC needs to know you will hover for minutes over one geographic point. That is a traffic hazard if unspoken.

In the H130, hoist work demands a rock-steady power picture. If torque or references degrade, abort. A partial transfer is better than a ditching.

Brazilian vessels may offer a dirty deck or high sea state where winch is the only option. Brief abort criteria with the crew before you descend: cable foul, person spin, power limit, deck spray.

Never leave the frequency mute for the entire hover. Give a progress call if the transfer runs long.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You must transfer a person by winch because the deck is obstructed. What do you tell ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would state that landing is not available and that I will conduct winch operations: ANAC123, descending for winch operations on support vessel, landing not available, will report on station. On station I would report establishing hover and expected time. When finished: ANAC123, winch complete, lifting away, request coastal routing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Hovering for a long winch without an on-station call.  
  ✔ Report on station and expected duration.

- ❌ Saying nothing when aborting a winch.  
  ✔ Call: aborting winch, climbing away, say reason if able.

- ❌ Omitting fuel state when asked during long hovers.  
  ✔ Know fuel and hover time before you commit.

- ❌ Treating winch as informal and using only Portuguese on ATC.  
  ✔ Use ICAO English for ATC; crew hoist calls on intercom.

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, on STAY-shun, eh-STAB-lish-ing HOV-er for WINCH

**Word Stress**

- Station → STAY-shun
- Establishing → eh-STAB-lish-ing
- Winch → WINCH

Practice:

Together:

ANAC123, on station, establishing hover for winch, expect one zero minutes.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**WINCH** — **W**ait for vessel ready, **I**nform on station, **N**ote fuel and time, **C**omplete or abort clearly, **H**ead away with lifting call.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Deck obstructed on a vessel south of Rio. You set up for winch transfer.

**Your first task**

Initial call to Approach.

**Your second task**

Cable issues force an abort mid-transfer. Person is back on deck, aircraft safe. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, descending for winch operations on support vessel, landing not available, will report on station. Second task: ANAC123, aborting winch, person on deck, climbing away, aircraft under control, request coastal routing — or pan-pan if assistance or priority is still required.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Helideck winching trades a landing for a precise hover and honest status calls.

On station. Hover time. Winch complete. Lifting.

In the H130 on Brazilian maritime tasks, abort criteria keep you alive when the cable or the sea misbehaves.

Ready vessel. Stable hover. Clear English. Climb away clean when the transfer is done — or when it must stop.
