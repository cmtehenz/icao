# Descend and Maintain

**Catalog ID:** 0015  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers one of the most common descent clearances issued by Air Traffic Control.

Just like Climb and Maintain, this clearance contains two actions:

Begin the descent immediately.  
Stop exactly at the assigned altitude.

Professional pilots understand that descending below the assigned altitude is just as dangerous as climbing above a cleared altitude.

## 📖 Meaning

Descend and Maintain instructs the pilot to descend immediately and level off at the assigned altitude.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Desça e mantenha.

Inicie a descida imediatamente e nivele exatamente na altitude autorizada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Descend and Maintain, the pilot must:

- begin descending promptly
- level off precisely at the assigned altitude
- maintain that altitude until another clearance is received
- continue complying with assigned headings and speeds

This instruction changes only the altitude.

It does not cancel headings, vectors, speed restrictions, or navigation clearances.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Descend and Maintain to:

- sequence arriving aircraft
- establish vertical separation
- position aircraft for approach
- reduce arrival delays
- coordinate crossing traffic
- prepare aircraft for instrument or visual approaches

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You're approaching Curitiba Airport at 8,000 feet.

Approach instructs:

Approach: ANAC123, descend and maintain four thousand feet.

You immediately begin descending.

Your assigned heading remains unchanged.

As you approach 4,000 feet, you smoothly level off and continue waiting for the next clearance.

A few minutes later:

Approach: ANAC123, cleared ILS approach runway one five.

The descent clearance positioned you perfectly for the approach.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, descend and maintain four thousand feet.
2. Approach: ANAC123, descend and maintain two thousand five hundred feet.
3. Center: ANAC123, descend and maintain flight level one two zero.
4. Approach: ANAC123, descend and maintain three thousand, expect ILS runway zero seven.
5. Approach: ANAC123, descend and maintain five thousand due crossing traffic.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Descending to four thousand, ANAC123.
2. Descend and maintain two thousand five hundred, ANAC123.
3. Leaving flight level one eight zero for flight level one two zero, ANAC123.
4. Descending three thousand, ANAC123.
5. Descending five thousand, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine you're taking an elevator down.

You press the button for the fourth floor.

You don't stop at the fifth.

You don't continue to the third.

You stop exactly where you were instructed.

Altitude works exactly the same way.

Professional pilots don't "approximate" an altitude.

They capture it precisely.

Every altitude assignment protects another aircraft somewhere nearby.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Why is it important to stop exactly at the assigned altitude?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Because ATC uses altitude assignments to maintain safe vertical separation between aircraft. Descending below the assigned altitude may create a conflict with other traffic and reduce safety.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Descending below the assigned altitude.  
  ✔ Level off precisely.

- ❌ Delaying the descent unnecessarily.  
  ✔ Begin descending promptly.

- ❌ Assuming another altitude is expected.  
  ✔ Fly the clearance you have—not the one you expect.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Descending to four thousand, ANAC123.

Descending to four thousand, ANAC123.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Think of parking on the third floor of a building.

You don't stop on the fourth.

You don't continue to the second.

The assigned altitude is your parking floor.
