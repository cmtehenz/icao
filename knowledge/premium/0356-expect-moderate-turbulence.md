# Expect Moderate Turbulence

**Catalog ID:** 0356  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Expect Moderate Turbulence — an ATC or ATIS advisory that moderate turbulence is forecast or reported ahead, so the crew can secure the cabin, adjust speed if required, and prepare to report actual conditions.

"Expect" is forecast language. It is not a clearance and not a confirmed PIREP of what you will feel — it is a warning to prepare and then report what you actually encounter.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, moderate turbulence advisories are common near coastal gradients, ridges, and convective weather. Part 2 radiotelephony must show you understand the advisory and can respond with a correct PIREP.

Understanding expect moderate turbulence in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Expect Moderate Turbulence is an advisory from ATC or meteorological information indicating that moderate turbulence is forecast or has been reported in an area ahead, prompting crews to prepare the aircraft and occupants and to report actual turbulence intensity when encountered.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Expectativa de turbulência moderada / Expect Moderate Turbulence.

Aviso do ATC ou de informação meteorológica indicando que turbulência moderada é prevista ou foi reportada em uma área à frente, levando as tripulações a preparar a aeronave e os ocupantes e a reportar a intensidade real da turbulência quando encontrada.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC says expect moderate turbulence, the crew should:

- acknowledge the advisory
- secure loose objects and brief occupants as needed
- adjust to recommended turbulence speed if published
- fly attitude with smooth inputs when turbulence begins
- report actual intensity: light, moderate, or severe as experienced
- request climb, descent, or deviation if the ride is unacceptable

Expect-advisory handling commonly includes:

- roger, expect moderate turbulence
- moderate turbulence encountered at altitude
- request climb / descent / weather deviation
- ride improving / clear of turbulence

**Important:** "Expect" does not mean you must already be in moderate turbulence. Prepare first. Report what you feel with the correct intensity word.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers issue expect moderate turbulence to:

- warn aircraft before they enter known or forecast rough air
- prompt timely PIREPs that refine the picture for others
- offer altitude or route options early
- reduce surprise workload on frequency when bumps begin
- support safe sequencing through weather-affected sectors

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Cruising toward Florianópolis at five thousand feet in the H130, Approach advises expect moderate turbulence near the coastal ridge in two zero miles.

You secure the cabin, brief the passenger, and continue. Entering the area you feel definite strain and altitude changes — true moderate turbulence. You report it and request climb to seven thousand. Ride improves.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, expect moderate turbulence near the coast in two zero miles.
2. Approach: ANAC123, previous aircraft reported moderate turbulence at five thousand.
3. Center: ANAC123, expect moderate turbulence FL090 to FL070, advise.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say ride conditions.
5. Approach: ANAC123, climb and maintain seven thousand, report ride.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, roger, expect moderate turbulence.
2. ANAC123, moderate turbulence at five thousand, request climb seven thousand.
3. ANAC123, moderate turbulence occasional, ride improving at seven thousand.
4. ANAC123, request weather deviation left of course, moderate turbulence.
5. ANAC123, clear of moderate turbulence, light turbulence now.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Expect is preparation language. Encountered is PIREP language.

When ATC says expect moderate turbulence, do not argue — prepare. When you hit it, say moderate turbulence with altitude.

Brazilian pilots sometimes answer "ok, turbulento" without intensity. Use the ICAO scale: light, moderate, severe.

In the H130, moderate means definite strain but controllable. If control becomes difficult, it is severe — upgrade the report.

Part 2 loves this distinction. Show the examiner you heard expect, prepared, then reported accurately.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC says expect moderate turbulence ahead. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would acknowledge the advisory, secure the cabin, and adjust speed if required. When I encounter turbulence I would report the actual intensity with altitude — for example moderate turbulence at five thousand — and request climb, descent, or deviation if needed. I would update ATC when the ride improves.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Ignoring "expect" until the aircraft is already bouncing hard.  
  ✔ Prepare as soon as the advisory is received.

- ❌ Reporting "turbulência" without intensity.  
  ✔ Say light, moderate, or severe.

- ❌ Calling forecast "expect" the same as a PIREP.  
  ✔ Expect is advisory; your PIREP confirms what you feel.

- ❌ Not updating when clear of turbulence.  
  ✔ Report ride improving or clear of moderate turbulence.
