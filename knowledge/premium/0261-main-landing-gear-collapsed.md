# Main Landing Gear Collapsed

**Catalog ID:** 0261  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Main Landing Gear Collapsed — an emergency in which one or both main landing gear legs collapse or fail structurally during landing, taxi, or ground operations, requiring immediate aircraft control, evacuation decisions, and critical ATC and RFF coordination.

Main landing gear collapsed is a critical emergency. The aircraft may settle onto a wing, nacelle, or fuselage side. Fire risk rises. Evacuation may be required. The radio must deliver essentials: what collapsed, aircraft status, fire or smoke, evacuation, souls, and fuel.

For H130 helicopter ICAO Delta academy operations in Brazil, many H130s use skids; a classic main-gear collapse is primarily fixed-wing exam and multi-type English. Still learn it — and understand the airport emergency language when a jet collapses a main gear on your shared runway at Congonhas, Guarulhos, or Santos Dumont.

Understanding this emergency phraseology is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 under pressure.

## 📖 Meaning

Main Landing Gear Collapsed is an emergency condition in which one or both main landing gear assemblies fail structurally or fold during ground contact or taxi, causing the aircraft to settle abnormally, often with fire risk, runway blockage, and possible evacuation.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Colapso do trem de pouso principal / Main Landing Gear Collapsed.

Condição de emergência em que um ou ambos os conjuntos do trem de pouso principal falham estruturalmente ou se dobram durante o contato com o solo ou o táxi, fazendo a aeronave assentar de forma anormal, frequentemente com risco de fogo, bloqueio da pista e possível evacuação.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When main landing gear collapses, the crew should:

- maintain directional control as able and stop the aircraft
- shut down engines and secure critical systems per emergency checklist
- assess fire, smoke, and evacuation need immediately
- declare Mayday if not already in emergency handling with ATC
- report gear collapsed, aircraft position, fire status, and evacuation
- for other traffic: expect runway closed, go around, hold, or divert

Main gear collapse scenarios commonly include:

- collapse on touchdown after hard landing or structural failure
- collapse during rollout or high-speed taxi
- one main gear collapse causing wing-low attitude
- subsequent fuel leak, fire, or runway excursion
- H130 note: skid aircraft differ; still master exam English and airport response language

**Important:** After collapse, survival and fire prevention come first. Then short radio essentials. Do not give a long story while fire risk is rising.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to main gear collapse to:

- stop further runway operations immediately
- launch RFF to the aircraft
- vector go-arounds and organize diversions
- obtain fire, evacuation, souls, and fuel information
- protect the accident site and emergency routes

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Wheeled-aircraft emergency: after touchdown at Congonhas, the right main landing gear collapses. The wing settles. You stop. No fire yet. Evacuation is ordered as a precaution.

You transmit:

ANAC123, Mayday, main landing gear collapsed, aircraft on runway, evacuating, no fire at this time.

Tower: ANAC123, firefighters on the way, confirm souls and fuel when able.

You evacuate. RFF arrives. The runway is closed. Behind you, an H130 on approach goes around and diverts — correct shared-airport discipline.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, roger gear collapsed, firefighters rolling, say if fire or evacuation.
2. Tower: ANAC123, confirm runway blocked, all traffic go around.
3. Approach: ANAC456, go around, runway closed, main gear collapse on the field.
4. Tower: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.
5. Tower: ANAC123, remain on frequency if able, assistance on scene.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, Mayday, main landing gear collapsed, evacuating, no fire observed.
2. ANAC123, runway blocked, evacuating now.
3. ANAC456, going around, runway closed copied.
4. ANAC123, souls one hundred twenty, fuel remaining two hours — or as applicable.
5. ANAC123, remaining on frequency as able.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Gear collapse is a ground emergency that starts in the last seconds of flight.

If you are the crew: stop, secure, decide evacuate, then speak essentials. Fire status matters more than perfect grammar — but ICAO Level 4 still wants clear structure under stress.

If you are other traffic — including H130 academy flights — the phrase "main landing gear collapsed" means the runway is an emergency scene. Go around. Divert. Do not request a look.

Brazilian short runways and dense traffic make rapid closure language critical. Congonhas does not have spare concrete. Your clean go-around helps RFF work.

Critical. Short. Ordered. That is the radio standard.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

After landing, a main landing gear collapses and you stop on the runway. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would stop and secure the aircraft, assess fire and the need to evacuate, and order evacuation if required. I would declare Mayday if needed and advise ATC that the main landing gear has collapsed, state fire status, evacuation status, and runway blockage, then provide souls and fuel when able. Survival and fire prevention come before long explanations.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Giving a long narrative before securing and assessing fire.  
  ✔ Secure, assess evacuate/fire, then short essential radio calls.

- ❌ Other traffic continuing approach to "see what happened."  
  ✔ Go around immediately; runway is closed for emergency response.

- ❌ Forgetting to state whether fire is present or evacuation is underway.  
  ✔ Fire status and evacuation status are critical for RFF.

- ❌ Treating this as irrelevant because the H130 has skids.  
  ✔ Learn exam English; in real ops, react correctly as shared-airport traffic.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** ANAC123, Main Landing Gear Collapsed, Evacuating

**Pronunciation:** AY-nak WUN TOO THREE, MAYN LAN-ding GEER kuh-LAPST, ih-VAK-yoo-ay-ting

**Word Stress**

- Main → MAYN
- Landing → LAN-ding
- Collapsed → kuh-LAPST (stress on LAP)
- Evacuating → ih-VAK-yoo-ay-ting (stress on VAK)

Practice:

Main landing gear collapsed... evacuating... no fire.

Together:

ANAC123, Mayday, main landing gear collapsed, evacuating, no fire at this time.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Main landing gear collapsed is survival language.

Stop. Secure. Evacuate if needed. Tell ATC fire and evacuation status in short ICAO English.

For the exam, speak it fluently. For the H130 on skids, respect it as the phrase that closes the airport in front of you.

Critical events need empty sky and clear radio — not spectators and long stories.
