# Priority Landing

**Catalog ID:** 0033  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers a request that signals urgency to ATC — without always being a full distress call.

Priority Landing asks ATC to sequence your aircraft ahead of other traffic because of an urgent situation.

This may involve low fuel, a medical emergency, a system malfunction, or other time-critical conditions.

It is different from Mayday, but it still requires clear communication, a stated reason, and professional cooperation with ATC.

Understanding when and how to request priority landing is essential for ICAO Part 2 and Part 3.

## 📖 Meaning

Priority Landing is a pilot request for preferential ATC handling to land before other traffic due to an urgent or emergency situation.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When requesting Priority Landing, the pilot should:

- state the nature of the urgency clearly
- use Pan Pan for urgency or Mayday for distress as appropriate
- request priority landing or priority handling
- comply with ATC vectors and sequencing instructions
- provide souls on board and fuel remaining when requested

Priority Landing is used when:

- medical emergency on board
- low fuel or minimum fuel situation
- system malfunction requiring expedited landing
- any urgent situation requiring landing ahead of traffic

**Important:** Priority landing is a request. ATC will sequence traffic accordingly, but the pilot must still follow all instructions and may need to declare Pan Pan or Mayday depending on severity.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC coordinates Priority Landing to:

- protect aircraft in urgent or emergency situations
- clear traffic from the landing sequence
- provide vectors to the nearest suitable airport
- alert emergency services when required
- minimize delay for time-critical landings

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Congonhas in your H130 with a passenger experiencing chest pain.

You declare urgency and request priority handling.

Approach: ANAC123, say nature of emergency.

You reply:

Pan Pan Pan, Approach, ANAC123, medical emergency on board. Request priority landing runway one seven right.

Approach: ANAC123, cleared priority approach runway one seven right, number one, all traffic hold.

You read back and proceed directly while ATC holds other traffic.

Emergency services are standing by on arrival.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Think of priority landing as asking to jump the queue because something serious is happening.

You must explain why.

A controller cannot prioritize you if they do not understand the situation.

Pan Pan signals urgency.

Mayday signals distress.

Priority landing is the operational request that follows.

In Brazil, pilots sometimes say "I need to land first" on frequency.

That is not standard phraseology.

Say: request priority landing, and state the reason.

Pair the request with Pan Pan or Mayday when the situation warrants it.

ATC will do their part — but clear communication starts in your cockpit.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between priority landing and Mayday?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Mayday declares a distress situation where immediate assistance is required because the aircraft or occupants are in grave and imminent danger. Priority landing is a request for preferential sequencing to land ahead of traffic, often used with Pan Pan for urgency or Mayday for distress. Priority landing describes what you need; Mayday describes the severity of the emergency.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "I need to land first" instead of request priority landing.  
  ✔ Use standard phraseology and state the reason.

- ❌ Requesting priority without explaining the nature of the urgency.  
  ✔ State medical emergency, low fuel, or the specific problem clearly.

- ❌ Confusing priority landing with the emergency declaration itself.  
  ✔ Declare Pan Pan or Mayday first, then request priority handling.

- ❌ Expecting landing clearance without following ATC vectors.  
  ✔ Comply with all ATC instructions during priority handling.
