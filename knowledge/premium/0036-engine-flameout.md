# Engine Flameout

**Catalog ID:** 0036  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers a sudden and specific engine event.

Engine Flameout means combustion has stopped in the engine — the flame has gone out.

Unlike a complete mechanical failure, a flameout may be recoverable with a relight procedure.

In your H130, a flameout at altitude still demands immediate action and clear communication.

You must aviate first, attempt relight if appropriate, then inform ATC with precise phraseology.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 vocabulary and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Engine Flameout is the sudden loss of engine combustion, where the flame extinguishes inside the engine.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Apagão de motor.

Perda súbita da combustão no motor — a chama se apaga no interior do motor.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Engine Flameout occurs, the pilot should:

- maintain aircraft control as the first priority
- attempt engine relight procedures if conditions permit
- monitor whether power is restored or altitude is being lost
- declare Pan Pan for urgency or Mayday for distress as appropriate
- inform ATC of flameout, restart status, and intentions

Engine Flameout reporting commonly includes:

- engine flameout
- attempting relight
- negative restart, request vectors to nearest airport
- engine relit, maintaining altitude

**Important:** Flameout is not the same as engine failure. Tell ATC whether you are attempting a restart and whether altitude can be maintained.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Engine Flameout reports to:

- provide vectors to the nearest suitable airport if restart fails
- assign altitude blocks during relight attempts
- clear traffic from the aircraft's route if altitude cannot be maintained
- request intentions and souls on board when distress is declared
- coordinate priority landing if the situation escalates

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are cruising at four thousand feet in your H130 over the coast near Florianópolis.

Without warning, the engine flames out.

You lower the collective slightly, maintain rotor RPM, and begin the relight procedure.

When stable, you transmit:

Pan Pan Pan, Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, engine flameout, attempting relight, maintaining altitude at four thousand feet.

Approach acknowledges and asks you to report restart status.

Thirty seconds later, the engine relights. You report positive restart and continue with caution.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger flameout, report if able to restart.
2. Approach: ANAC123, descend to flight level zero eight zero, report restart status.
3. Approach: ANAC123, turn right heading two seven zero, vectors to the field.
4. Approach: ANAC123, say intentions.
5. Approach: ANAC123, nearest airport is one five miles at your twelve o'clock.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Pan Pan Pan, Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, engine flameout, attempting relight.
2. ANAC123, negative restart, request vectors to the nearest airport.
3. ANAC123, engine relit, maintaining altitude.
4. Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, ANAC123, engine flameout, unable to maintain altitude, descending.
5. ANAC123, flameout, relight unsuccessful, request immediate landing at Navegantes.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Flameout is a word many Brazilian pilots never use on frequency.

They say motor apagou or engine stopped.

Use engine flameout — it tells ATC combustion stopped, not that the engine mechanically failed.

The key difference from engine failure: flameout may be transient.

Keep ATC updated every thirty seconds during a relight attempt.

If you cannot maintain altitude, upgrade from Pan Pan to Mayday immediately.

In the H130, a flameout at low altitude leaves little time for restart — prioritize rotor RPM and landing area selection.

At altitude, you have more options — but never delay the first radio call while the aircraft is unstable.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

How is an engine flameout different from engine failure in your report to ATC?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Engine flameout means combustion stopped and I may attempt a relight. Engine failure means the engine is not producing usable power. I would report flameout if I am attempting restart, and engine failure if power cannot be restored. I tell ATC my restart status and whether I can maintain altitude.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Confusing flameout with engine stall — different mechanisms, same urgency to report.  
  ✔ Use engine flameout and state whether relight is in progress.

- ❌ Not telling ATC whether restart is being attempted.  
  ✔ Report attempting relight or negative restart on every update.

- ❌ Using flame out as one vague word without clear engine identification.  
  ✔ Say engine flameout clearly — two words, standard phraseology.

- ❌ Waiting until restart fails before calling ATC.  
  ✔ Inform ATC immediately after securing the aircraft, even during relight.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Engine Flameout, Attempting Relight

**Pronunciation:** EN-jin FLAME-out, a-TEMPT-ing RE-light

**Word Stress**

- Flameout → FLAME-out (two words)
- Relight → RE-light (not re-lite)
- Attempting → a-TEMPT-ing

Practice:

Engine flameout... attempting relight...

Together:

Pan Pan Pan, Florianópolis Approach, ANAC123, engine flameout, attempting relight.

Speak clearly — controllers need to know if you are recovering or descending.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are at three thousand five hundred feet over Guarulhos in your H130 when the engine flames out.

**Your first task**

After securing the aircraft and beginning relight, transmit your urgency declaration to Approach.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

Approach asks if you are able to restart. The relight fails. What do you report?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report negative restart, state that I am unable to maintain altitude, declare Mayday if required, and request vectors to the nearest suitable airport. I would provide my position and intentions for an immediate landing, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Engine Flameout demands precision in your radio call.

It is not engine failure — it is combustion stopped, and you may be recovering.

Aviate first. Attempt relight if appropriate. Then speak.

Keep ATC updated every thirty seconds during restart.

If altitude is lost, escalate to Mayday without hesitation.

Train the distinction. Fly the aircraft. Speak like a professional.
