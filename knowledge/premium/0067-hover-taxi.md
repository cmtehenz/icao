# Hover Taxi

**Catalog ID:** 0067  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers hover taxi — one of the most common helicopter ground movement procedures.

Hover Taxi means movement of a helicopter above the surface at slow speed and low altitude, generally below one hundred feet AGL, and normally above translational lift airspeed.

In the H130, hover taxi is used to move between helipads, parking areas, and departure points at airports and heliports.

You must receive clearance, read back correctly, and maintain awareness of obstacles and other traffic.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 radiotelephony.

## 📖 Meaning

Hover Taxi is ATC authorization or pilot movement of a helicopter in ground effect at low altitude and slow speed between points on an airport or heliport.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Táxi em sustentação.

Movimento do helicóptero acima da superfície em baixa altitude e baixa velocidade entre pontos no aeródromo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Hover Taxi is used, the pilot should:

- obtain clearance from Ground or Tower before hover taxiing
- read back the route, hold points, and any restrictions
- maintain safe altitude and avoid obstacles
- yield to aircraft on the movement area
- report reaching the assigned parking or departure point

Hover Taxi clearances commonly include:

- hover taxi to the helipad
- hover taxi via a specific route
- hold short of a runway or active area
- contact tower when ready for departure

**Important:** Hover taxi is not free movement on the airport. You need clearance and must read back hold short instructions like any surface movement.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC uses Hover Taxi to:

- route helicopters safely on the movement area
- separate helicopter traffic from fixed-wing taxi routes
- prevent conflicts at helipads and parking areas
- coordinate departures and arrivals at busy heliports
- maintain orderly traffic flow at congested airports

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You have just shut down at the general aviation ramp at Congonhas.

After startup, you contact Ground:

Ground: ANAC123, hover taxi to helipad two via the north taxi route, hold short of runway one seven.

You read back the clearance and hover taxi at fifty feet along the assigned route.

At the hold short point, you stop and contact Tower for departure clearance.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, hover taxi to helipad one.
2. Ground: ANAC123, hover taxi via north ramp, hold short of runway three five.
3. Tower: ANAC123, hover taxi to the departure helipad, report ready.
4. Ground: ANAC123, hover taxi to parking via Bravo helipad route.
5. Tower: ANAC123, hover taxi behind the departing helicopter, hold short of the active helipad.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Hover taxi to helipad one, ANAC123.
2. Hover taxi via north ramp, hold short of runway three five, ANAC123.
3. Hover taxi to departure helipad, will report ready, ANAC123.
4. Hover taxi to parking via Bravo helipad route, ANAC123.
5. Hover taxi behind departing traffic, hold short active helipad, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Hover taxi is not flying — it is controlled surface movement in the air.

The discipline is the same as taxi in an airplane:

Clearance. Route. Hold short.

You do not hover across an active runway without explicit clearance.

You do not shortcut across ramps because it looks faster.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "taxi aéreo" or "hover" without the full phrase.

Use hover taxi to helipad — ICAO standard phraseology.

In the H130 at Congonhas or Navegantes, know your airport diagram before you hover.

Low and slow does not mean low discipline.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is hover taxi and how would you request it from Ground?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Hover taxi is helicopter movement above the surface at low altitude and slow speed between points on the aerodrome. I would contact Ground, state my position and request hover taxi to my destination. I would read back the assigned route and any hold short instructions, comply with the clearance, and report when ready at the assigned point.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Hover taxiing without clearance.  
  ✔ Obtain hover taxi clearance from Ground or Tower first.

- ❌ Saying "vou de hover" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use hover taxi to helipad or hover taxi via route.

- ❌ Crossing active areas without hold short compliance.  
  ✔ Hold short as instructed — same discipline as fixed-wing taxi.

- ❌ Omitting readback of hold short instructions.  
  ✔ Read back full route and hold short points.

Hover taxi is surface movement with rotors turning.

Get clearance.

Read back hold shorts.

Fly the route on the diagram.

Professional helicopter pilots treat hover taxi with the same precision as a runway clearance.
