# NVG Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0412  
**Category:** Helicopter Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers NVG Failure — total or partial loss of night vision goggle capability during night helicopter operations, requiring immediate transition to unaided vision or IFR procedures and clear urgency communication.

NVG failure is a night emergency of vision, not of the airframe. The aircraft may be fine while your primary night reference disappears.

For H130 operations in Brazil — HEMS corridors, coastal SAR, and remote night transfers — you must declare the failure, state remaining visual capability, and request climb, vectors, or landing without delay.

Understanding how to report NVG failure in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

NVG Failure is the partial or complete loss of night vision goggle function during flight, leaving the pilot with unaided night vision or instrument references only, and requiring immediate assessment of visual conditions and communication of intentions to ATC.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha de óculos de visão noturna (NVG).

Perda parcial ou total da função dos óculos de visão noturna em voo, deixando o piloto apenas com visão noturna sem auxílio ou com referências de instrumentos, exigindo avaliação imediata das condições visuais e comunicação de intenções ao ATC.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When NVG failure occurs, the crew should:

- transition immediately to unaided vision or instruments as trained
- assess whether VFR can be maintained without NVGs
- declare urgency or distress if safe flight is threatened
- request climb, vectors, diversion, or priority landing
- advise ATC of NVG failure and current visual conditions

NVG failure reporting commonly includes:

- NVG failure, requesting climb
- pan-pan, NVG failure, diverting
- unable night VFR without NVGs
- request priority landing, lights full bright

**Important:** Aviate first — establish a safe flight path with the remaining references. Then communicate. Do not troubleshoot goggles at low level over dark terrain.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to NVG failure reports to:

- provide immediate vectors and altitude for recovery
- clear traffic from the helicopter's path
- coordinate priority landing and lighting
- relay weather and nearest suitable fields
- treat the event as a night visual emergency

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are twenty miles south of Florianópolis at eight hundred feet on a night coastal transfer in your H130. Both NVG tubes fail.

You transition to instruments and unaided vision. The coastline is barely visible. You transmit:

Pan-pan pan-pan pan-pan, Florianópolis Approach, NVG failure, unable maintain night VFR safely, request climb to three thousand and vectors to Florianópolis for landing.

Approach clears you, turns on approach lights, and sequences you for a priority arrival.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, pan-pan acknowledged, climb and maintain three thousand, turn left heading three six zero.
2. Approach: ANAC123, Florianópolis weather clear, runway one four lights full bright, expect straight-in.
3. Approach: ANAC123, traffic cleared, report field in sight unaided.
4. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land runway one four, winds one five zero at six.
5. Approach: ANAC123, say souls on board and fuel remaining.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, climbing three thousand, left heading three six zero, NVG failure.
2. ANAC123, expect straight-in one four, lights full bright.
3. ANAC123, field in sight unaided, descending for landing.
4. ANAC123, cleared to land runway one four.
5. ANAC123, souls three, fuel one hour one five.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

NVG failure English is pan-pan English until you know you can continue safely.

If you lose the goggles over dark water or jungle, you have lost your primary night visual system. Say it. Ask for altitude and a field.

In the H130, transition training matters — but ATC only hears what you transmit. "NVG failure" plus "unable night VFR" plus a clear request gets you help.

Do not soft-pedal: "having some goggle issues" wastes seconds. Use standard urgency. State intentions. Fly the instruments if the outside world disappears.

Brazilian coastal nights can look black beyond the shoreline. Climb is often the first request. Landing is the second.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Your NVGs fail at low level on a night coastal flight. What do you transmit?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would declare urgency and state the problem and need: Pan-pan pan-pan pan-pan, NVG failure, unable maintain night VFR safely, request climb to three thousand and vectors to nearest suitable field for landing. I would then report souls and fuel when asked, and advise when the field is in sight unaided.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Continuing low-level without declaring because "the helicopter is fine."  
  ✔ The emergency is visual. Declare and climb or divert.

- ❌ Saying "goggles broken" without a clear request.  
  ✔ State NVG failure and request climb, vectors, or priority landing.

- ❌ Forgetting to ask for lights full bright.  
  ✔ Request maximum lighting for unaided night landing.

- ❌ Delaying the call while troubleshooting tubes at eight hundred feet.  
  ✔ Fly first, communicate second, troubleshoot on the ground.

- Failure → FAYL-yer

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Night coastal flight east of Santos. NVGs fail. Horizon barely visible.

**Your first task**

Compose the initial pan-pan call.

**Your second task**

Approach clears you to two thousand and asks if you can continue VFR. You cannot. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Pan-pan pan-pan pan-pan, Santos Approach, ANAC123, NVG failure, unable night VFR, request climb and vectors to Santos for priority landing. Second task: ANAC123, unable continue VFR unaided, request IFR clearance or radar vectors for approach — I would not accept a VFR continuation without a usable visual reference.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

NVG failure is a vision emergency. Treat it like one on the radio.

Declare. Climb or divert. Request lights and priority. Report souls and fuel.

In the H130 at night over Brazil, unaided vision may not be enough. Instruments and ATC are your partners.

Say NVG failure clearly. Get altitude. Get a runway or pad. Land with a plan — not with hope.
