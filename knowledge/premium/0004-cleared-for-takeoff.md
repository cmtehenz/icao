# Cleared for Takeoff

**Catalog ID:** 0004  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## Mission Brief

Today you'll learn one of the most important phrases in aviation.

There is only one instruction that authorizes an aircraft to begin its takeoff roll:

Cleared for Takeoff

Professional pilots never assume they have takeoff clearance. They wait to hear these exact words.

## Meaning

ATC authorizes the aircraft to begin the takeoff roll on the assigned runway.

## Portuguese Meaning

Autorizado para decolagem.

## Operational Meaning

A Cleared for Takeoff clearance gives the pilot legal and operational authorization to begin the takeoff.

It follows only after the controller has confirmed that:

- the runway is available
- separation requirements are met
- conflicting traffic is clear
- departure can be conducted safely

Until these words are spoken, the aircraft must remain stationary, even if it is already lined up on the runway.

## Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Cleared for Takeoff to:

- authorize departure
- ensure runway separation
- coordinate arrivals and departures
- maintain traffic flow
- guarantee runway safety
- comply with ICAO and FAA separation standards

## Real Operational Context

You're flying your H130 from Navegantes Airport.

Tower previously instructed:

"ANAC123, runway zero seven, line up and wait."

You enter the runway and align with the centerline.

A commercial jet lands ahead of you and vacates the runway.

The controller then says:

"ANAC123, runway zero seven, cleared for takeoff."

Only now do you:

- release the brakes
- increase power
- begin the takeoff roll

The runway became yours only after hearing those words.

## Real ATC Phraseology

1. ANAC123, runway zero seven, cleared for takeoff.
2. ANAC123, wind zero eight zero at six knots, runway one eight, cleared for takeoff.
3. ANAC123, cleared for immediate takeoff.
4. ANAC123, runway two seven, cleared for takeoff, traffic departing parallel runway.
5. ANAC123, after departure fly runway heading, runway one eight, cleared for takeoff.

## Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Runway zero seven, cleared for takeoff, ANAC123.
2. Cleared for takeoff runway one eight, ANAC123.
3. Immediate takeoff approved, ANAC123.
4. Cleared for takeoff, runway two seven, ANAC123.
5. Runway heading after departure, cleared for takeoff, ANAC123.

## Captain Delta Teaching

Gustavo, this is one of the most disciplined moments in every flight.

Imagine you've completed every checklist.

The helicopter is perfectly aligned.

The engine instruments are normal.

The runway is completely empty.

You are ready.

But you still do not move.

Why?

Because in aviation, being ready is not enough.

You need authorization.

Professional pilots never take off because the runway looks clear.

They take off because ATC has officially transferred the runway to them.

That's why you'll hear experienced pilots say:

"No clearance, no takeoff."

## Common ICAO Speaking Question

Why is it dangerous to assume you are cleared for takeoff?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Assuming takeoff clearance can lead to a runway incursion or collision with another aircraft. Pilots should begin the takeoff roll only after hearing the explicit instruction "Cleared for Takeoff."

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Confusing Line Up and Wait with Cleared for Takeoff.  
  Only the second authorizes departure.

- ❌ Starting the takeoff because the runway appears empty.  
  Visual observation never replaces ATC clearance.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Cleared for takeoff runway one eight, ANAC123.

- ❌ Forgetting the runway number.  
  Always include the runway in your readback.

## Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Cleared for Takeoff

**Pronunciation:** kleerd for TAKE-off

**Word Stress**

- Cleared → clear and confident
- Takeoff → stress TAKE

Do not split it unnaturally into: Take... off...

Instead, say it smoothly: Takeoff.

Practice:

Cleared... Cleared for... Cleared for Takeoff.

Then inside a complete transmission:

ANAC123, runway one eight, cleared for takeoff.

## Memory Trick

Imagine an airport runway as a stage.

Many aircraft may be waiting behind the curtain.

Only one actor is invited onto the stage.

The invitation is:

Cleared for Takeoff.

Without the invitation, the show never begins.
