# Expect Circling

**Catalog ID:** 0109  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Expect Circling — an ATC advisory informing the pilot to anticipate a circling approach rather than a straight-in landing.

Expect Circling means the instrument approach will end with a visual maneuver around the airport to align with a different runway than the one the approach was flown to.

In the H130, circling approaches are common at Florianópolis, Joinville, and Navegantes where runway layout and terrain require visual maneuvers.

You must acknowledge the advisory, plan the circling maneuver, and maintain visual contact with the runway throughout.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and circling approach operations.

## 📖 Meaning

Expect Circling is an ATC advisory indicating that the pilot should anticipate flying a visual circling maneuver after completing the instrument portion of an approach, in order to land on a different runway.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Espere procedimento de circuito visual.

Aviso do controle de que após o segmento por instrumentos o piloto realizará uma aproximação visual em circuito para pousar em uma pista diferente da utilizada no procedimento.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Expect Circling is received, the pilot should:

- acknowledge the advisory
- review the circling minima for the approach and aircraft category
- plan the circling direction based on terrain and traffic
- check the circling radius for the aircraft category
- maintain VMC throughout the visual maneuver

Expect Circling planning commonly includes:

- confirm circling MDA and required visibility
- review obstacle environment near circling area
- confirm circuit direction with ATC if not published
- plan go-around if visual contact is lost during circling

**Important:** If visual contact with the runway environment is lost during circling, execute the missed approach immediately. Do not attempt to continue circling in IMC.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC issues Expect Circling to:

- pre-inform the pilot of the intended landing runway
- allow time to review circling minima before the approach
- coordinate the visual maneuver with other traffic
- manage wind-favored or operationally preferred runways
- reduce crew workload at the decision point

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Joinville in your H130 on the RNAV approach.

The landing runway is runway one three, but winds favor runway three one.

Approach advises:

ANAC123, expect circling to runway three one after RNAV approach.

You review the circling minima, note the MDA, and plan a left circuit to runway three one.

You acknowledge:

Expect circling runway three one, ANAC123.

After breaking out visually you execute the circuit and land on three one.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, expect circling approach to runway three one.
2. Approach: ANAC123, expect circling, wind favoring runway zero one.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared circling approach runway three one, report final.
4. Approach: ANAC123, circling approved, maintain visual with runway.
5. Tower: ANAC123, if circling required, circuit to the left, runway zero one.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Circling is the highest workload visual maneuver in IFR operations.

You are flying by instruments until you are not — and then everything is visual.

The sequence:

Receive expect circling. Review minima. Confirm circuit direction. Fly the approach. Break out visual. Fly the circuit. Land.

Brazilian pilots sometimes lose situational awareness during the circuit because the airport looks different from a non-standard angle.

Pre-brief the circuit before starting the approach — not when you break out.

In the H130, know your circling radius for your category. Stay within protected airspace.

If you lose visual contact with the runway at any point during circling: missed approach. No exceptions.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What precautions do you take when expecting a circling approach?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

When I receive expect circling, I review the circling minima including MDA and required visibility for my aircraft category. I pre-brief the circuit direction, identify the obstacle environment, and plan a go-around if visual contact is lost. During the approach I do not descend below MDA until established visually, and if I lose visual contact at any point during the circuit I execute the missed approach immediately.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Using straight-in minima instead of circling minima.  
  ✔ Circling minima are higher — always use the correct values.

- ❌ Starting the circuit before breaking out visually.  
  ✔ Do not descend below MDA without clear visual reference.

- ❌ Not pre-briefing the circuit direction before the approach.  
  ✔ Brief circuit direction on the ground or well before the approach.

- ❌ Continuing to circle after losing visual contact.  
  ✔ Execute missed approach immediately if visual contact is lost.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Inbound to Joinville, Approach advises: ANAC123, expect circling to runway three one after RNAV approach runway one three.

**Your first task**

What do you acknowledge and what do you immediately review?

**Your second task**

During the circuit you enter a cloud layer and lose visual contact with the runway. What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would acknowledge: Expect circling runway three one, ANAC123. I would immediately review the circling MDA, required visibility, and circuit direction. During the circuit, if I lost visual contact with the runway I would execute the missed approach immediately, climbing on the published missed approach track, and report to ATC: ANAC123, going around, lost visual.
