# Fly Direct

**Catalog ID:** 0001  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## Mission Brief

Today you'll learn one of the most common ATC instructions used worldwide.

Fly Direct is a navigation clearance that allows an aircraft to proceed directly to a specified waypoint, navigation aid, airport, or fix instead of following the originally planned route.

Professional pilots hear this instruction every day because it saves time, reduces controller workload, and improves traffic flow.

## Meaning

Fly directly toward a specified waypoint, navigation aid, airport, or fix.

## Portuguese Meaning

Voe diretamente para um ponto específico.

## Operational Meaning

A Fly Direct clearance changes your navigation.

Instead of following the published airway, SID, STAR, or flight plan, ATC authorizes you to fly in a straight line to a designated point.

The clearance does not cancel any previous altitude, speed, frequency, or other ATC restrictions.

## Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Fly Direct to:

- shorten the route
- reduce controller workload
- improve traffic flow
- avoid weather
- reduce airborne delays
- provide efficient sequencing
- reduce fuel consumption

## Real Operational Context

You're flying an H130 from Ituporanga (SSKG) to Balneário Camboriú.

Originally your GPS route contains six waypoints.

ATC sees there is no conflicting traffic and says:

"ANAC123, fly direct Balneário."

Instead of continuing through every waypoint, you simply navigate directly toward Balneário while complying with every other ATC clearance.

Nothing else changes.

Your altitude remains the same.

Your transponder remains the same.

Your frequency remains the same.

Only your navigation changes.

## Real ATC Phraseology

1. ANAC123, fly direct NITUX.
2. ANAC123, cleared direct AFA VOR.
3. ANAC123, fly direct present position to waypoint BRAVO.
4. ANAC123, proceed direct REKPA.
5. ANAC123, fly direct the airport.

## Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Flying direct NITUX, ANAC123.
2. Cleared direct AFA VOR, ANAC123.
3. Direct BRAVO, ANAC123.
4. Proceeding direct REKPA, ANAC123.
5. Flying direct the airport, ANAC123.

## Captain Delta Teaching

Gustavo, imagine you're driving with Google Maps. The GPS originally planned a route through several streets. Suddenly, it finds a faster way and tells you to turn immediately toward your destination. That's exactly what Fly Direct means in aviation.

It is one of the simplest and most common ATC shortcuts. Your responsibility is to acknowledge the clearance, read back the waypoint correctly, and navigate directly to it while continuing to comply with every other clearance already in effect.

Remember: Fly Direct changes your route—not your altitude, speed, or other instructions.

## Common ICAO Speaking Question

When would ATC instruct an aircraft to fly direct?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

ATC may instruct an aircraft to fly direct when they want to shorten the route, improve traffic flow, reduce delays, or help the aircraft avoid weather. It is a common instruction in both IFR and VFR operations.

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying: Go direct.  
  ✔ Prefer: Fly direct. or Proceed direct.

- ❌ Reading back only: Roger.  
  ✔ Correct: Flying direct NITUX, ANAC123.

- ❌ Turning immediately without understanding the destination waypoint.  
  Always identify the correct fix before changing course.

- ❌ Thinking Fly Direct cancels previous clearances.  
  It doesn't. Maintain altitude, speed and frequency unless instructed otherwise.

## Pronunciation Coaching

**Fly Direct** (flai dih-REKT)

**Word Stress**

- Fly → clear and short
- Direct → stress the second syllable: di-RECT (not DI-rect)

Practice slowly:

Fly... Direct...

Now together:

Fly Direct.

Then inside a complete transmission:

ANAC123, fly direct NITUX.

## Memory Trick

Imagine drawing a straight line on your GPS.

No airways. No extra turns. No intermediate fixes.

Just one straight line.

Fly Direct = Straight to the point.
