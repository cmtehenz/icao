# Low Fuel

**Catalog ID:** 0043  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers a fuel urgency that every pilot must communicate clearly.

Low Fuel means your fuel quantity is below planned reserves or minimum required for destination plus reserve.

This is not fuel starvation — fuel is reaching the engine, but you do not have enough to continue safely.

Pan Pan is the standard urgency call. You must state fuel remaining in time, not just quantity.

In your H130, fuel margins on long cross-country flights over Brazil require careful planning and honest reporting.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Low Fuel is a fuel state where quantity is below planned reserves or minimum required for destination plus reserve.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Combustível baixo.

Quantidade de combustível abaixo das reservas planejadas ou do mínimo necessário para destino mais reserva.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Low Fuel occurs, the pilot should:

- declare Pan Pan Pan for urgency
- state fuel remaining in time — hours and minutes
- request priority landing or exit from holding
- state the maximum time available before landing is required
- correct ATC if they misunderstand your landing time requirement

Low Fuel reporting commonly includes:

- Pan Pan, low fuel state
- we are low on fuel and need to land within one five minutes
- we are low on fuel and need priority
- NEGATIVE, I need to land in one five minutes

**Important:** Low fuel means urgency, not empty tanks. Report time remaining. Use NEGATIVE firmly if ATC offers a delay you cannot accept.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Low Fuel declarations to:

- expedite approach and clear holding
- assign direct routing to the nearest suitable airport
- confirm landing time requirements
- clear other traffic for priority landing
- request fuel remaining in hours and minutes

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are holding at four thousand feet near Guarulhos in your H130 due to weather at your destination.

Your fuel calculation shows thirty minutes remaining — not enough for extended holding.

You transmit:

Pan Pan Pan, São Paulo Approach, ANAC123, we are running low on fuel and need to land within one five minutes.

Approach offers to keep you in hold for five zero minutes.

You respond firmly:

NEGATIVE. I need to land in one five minutes. Request exit from hold and vectors to Guarulhos, ANAC123.

Approach clears you direct and sequences you number one for landing.

You land with twenty minutes fuel remaining.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, exit hold now heading one three five, expect vectors to intercept final.
2. Approach: ANAC123, confirm you need to land in five zero minutes.
3. Approach: ANAC123, cleared direct to the airport.
4. Tower: ANAC123, number one for landing.
5. Approach: ANAC123, say fuel remaining in hours.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Low fuel is one of the most tested scenarios in ICAO Delta exams.

The Heathrow 24C scenario is essential: Pan Pan, land in fifteen minutes, NEGATIVE to fifty minutes.

Brazilian pilots say no fuel or sem combustível — that sounds like empty tanks.

Low fuel means urgency, not zero fuel. You still have fuel — just not enough.

Report time remaining: three zero minutes, one five minutes.

Use digits for clarity: one five minutes, not fifteen minutes casually spoken.

If ATC offers a hold extension you cannot accept, say NEGATIVE immediately.

Do not be polite when your fuel clock is running.

In the H130, monitor fuel on every cross-country leg over the Amazon and central Brazil — diversion options are limited.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between low fuel and fuel starvation?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Low fuel means fuel quantity is below reserves but fuel is still reaching the engine. Fuel starvation means fuel may be in the tanks but is not reaching the engine due to a delivery problem. Low fuel uses Pan Pan. Fuel starvation may require Mayday if engines are affected.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying no fuel instead of low fuel — sounds like empty tanks.  
  ✔ Use low fuel state — urgency, not zero quantity.

- ❌ Accepting hold extension when fuel is critical.  
  ✔ Use NEGATIVE firmly and state your maximum landing time.

- ❌ Reporting fuel only in kilograms without time remaining.  
  ✔ Report fuel remaining in hours and minutes.

- ❌ Waiting until minimum fuel before declaring urgency.  
  ✔ Declare Pan Pan when reserves are below comfort level.
