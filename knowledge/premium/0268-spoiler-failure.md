# Spoiler Failure

**Catalog ID:** 0268  
**Category:** Aircraft Systems  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Spoiler Failure — a malfunction of flight or ground spoilers that reduces roll assist, lift dump, or drag capability, requiring abnormal procedures and ATC communication when descent rate, roll control, or landing performance is affected.

Spoilers on many fixed-wing aircraft help roll control in flight and dump lift after touchdown. Failure can mean a slower roll rate, less ability to steepen descent with drag, or reduced braking effectiveness after landing because weight-on-wheels lift dump is incomplete.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, the H130 does not use wing spoilers. Learn the ICAO English for exams and multi-type careers. You may compare "need more drag to descend" to a steeper helicopter descent with collective — without inventing spoiler panels on the H130.

Part 2 and Part 3 expect you to say spoiler failure and the operational effect, not a vague "control problem."

## 📖 Meaning

Spoiler Failure is a malfunction in which flight spoilers, ground spoilers, or both do not operate normally, which may reduce roll authority, in-flight drag for descent, or lift dump after landing, and which requires abnormal procedures and ATC notification when performance or handling is limited.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Falha de spoilers / Spoiler Failure.

Malfuncionamento em que spoilers de voo, spoilers de solo, ou ambos não operam normalmente, o que pode reduzir autoridade de rolagem, arrasto em voo para descida, ou dump de sustentação após o pouso, e que exige procedimentos anormais e notificação ao ATC quando o desempenho ou o controle estiver limitado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When spoilers fail, the crew should:

- identify whether flight spoilers, ground spoilers, or both are affected
- apply the AFM abnormal checklist and note roll or drag limitations
- plan a shallower descent path if in-flight spoilers are unavailable
- expect longer landing roll if ground spoilers or lift dump failed
- advise ATC of spoiler failure and request longer runway or priority if needed
- for H130 context: use the English accurately; do not claim helicopter spoilers

Spoiler failure scenarios commonly include:

- flight spoilers inoperative — reduced descent flexibility and roll rate
- ground spoilers fail to deploy after touchdown — longer landing distance
- asymmetric spoiler causing roll upset
- ATC expedite descent you cannot meet without spoiler drag
- exam call: spoiler failure, request longer runway

**Important:** If ATC says "expedite descent" and you cannot without spoilers, say unable expedite descent, spoiler failure, and request an earlier descent or more track miles. Do not force an unsafe profile to please the frequency.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers use spoiler failure reports to:

- avoid expecting rapid descent rates the aircraft cannot produce
- assign longer runways when landing performance is degraded
- increase spacing if roll rate or go-around performance is limited
- coordinate priority when the crew requests it
- understand that landing roll may be longer than normal

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are in a jet training scenario descending toward Guarulhos. Flight spoilers fail. Approach asks you to expedite through the altitude block.

You respond:

ANAC123, spoiler failure, unable expedite descent, request earlier descent or additional track miles.

Approach: ANAC123, roger spoiler failure, turn left heading two two zero, descend when ready, expect runway two seven Right.

On short final you add:

ANAC123, ground spoilers may be inoperative, request longer runway if available, expect longer landing roll.

You do not fake a spoiler descent. You negotiate a profile you can fly.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger spoiler failure, say descent capability.
2. Approach: ANAC123, unable expedite, expect vectors for longer final.
3. Approach: ANAC123, expect runway two seven Right, advise landing distance concern.
4. Tower: ANAC123, wind two seven zero at eight, runway two seven Right, cleared to land.
5. Tower: ANAC123, vacate when able, be advised roll may be long.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, spoiler failure, unable expedite descent, request additional track miles.
2. ANAC123, vectors for longer final, spoiler failure, ANAC123.
3. ANAC123, runway two seven Right, possible long landing roll.
4. ANAC123, cleared to land runway two seven Right.
5. ANAC123, will vacate when able, long roll expected.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Spoilers are drag and lift-dump tools. When they fail, your vertical path and your landing roll change.

Do not accept an expedite you cannot fly. Unable expedite descent, spoiler failure is professional English — not weakness.

After landing, if ground spoilers fail, expect to use more runway. Tell ATC before touchdown if you need the longest runway.

In the H130, descent rate is largely collective and attitude. That analogy helps you understand "I need more drag" — it does not mean the helicopter has spoilers. Keep the systems story honest.

Examiners listen for the system name and the effect. Give both.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Flight spoilers fail and ATC asks you to expedite descent. What do you say?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would advise ATC of the spoiler failure and that I am unable to expedite descent as requested. I would request an earlier descent clearance or additional track miles to lose altitude safely, then continue with the AFM procedure and advise if landing distance may also be affected.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Accepting "expedite" when spoilers are failed and descent rate is limited.  
  ✔ Say unable expedite descent, spoiler failure, and request alternatives.

- ❌ Reporting only "control problem" without naming spoilers.  
  ✔ Use spoiler failure and state flight or ground effect.

- ❌ Not warning ATC of a possible long landing roll.  
  ✔ Advise longer landing roll / request longest runway when ground spoilers fail.

- ❌ Inventing helicopter spoiler procedures in H130 briefings.  
  ✔ Teach the exam concept; keep H130 systems factual.
