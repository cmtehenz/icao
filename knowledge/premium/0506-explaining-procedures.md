# Explaining Procedures

**Catalog ID:** 0506  
**Category:** ICAO Speaking  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Explaining Procedures — the ICAO oral exam task in which you describe how a flight procedure is carried out, step by step, in clear English at Level 4 or above.

Explaining procedures is not reading a checklist aloud at high speed. It is sequenced instruction.

In Part 1, the examiner may ask how you prepare for an IFR departure, how you fly an approach, or how you handle a non-normal. In Part 2, the same skill appears when you must tell ATC what you need or what you are doing during a procedure.

For H130 ICAO Delta crews, procedure explanation proves you can turn operational knowledge into intelligible English for both examiner and controller.

Understanding how to explain procedures in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 1 and Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Explaining Procedures is the ICAO speaking task type in which the candidate describes the steps of an aviation procedure in logical order — stating purpose, sequence, and critical checks — so that clarity, sequencing language, and operational vocabulary can be assessed at Operational Level 4 or higher.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When explaining a procedure, the candidate should:

- name the procedure and its purpose in one opening sentence
- list steps in chronological order using first, then, next, finally
- highlight critical safety checks
- mention who does what if crew coordination matters
- keep each step short enough to follow
- for H130: include helicopter-specific steps when relevant — hover checks, rotor, landing site
- link to ATC when the procedure requires a clearance or report

A Level 4 procedure explanation typically includes:

- purpose: why the procedure exists
- preparation: what you check before starting
- execution: main steps in order
- completion: how you know it is done / what you report

**Important:** Do not dump the entire AFM. Select the essential sequence. Examiners score organization and intelligibility, not encyclopedic length.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Procedure explanation skills help examiners and controllers to:

- verify that pilots can communicate process, not only outcomes
- elicit sequencing language under assessment conditions
- support Part 2 exchanges when pilots report progress on a procedure
- assess whether safety-critical steps are prioritized correctly
- connect plain English discussion with radiotelephony reports

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

The examiner asks: Explain how you fly a visual approach to a helipad in the H130.

You open: The purpose of a visual approach to a helipad is to land using visual references while complying with ATC clearance.

Then: First, I confirm helipad in sight and receive clearance. Then I brief wind, obstacles, and go-around path. Next I control speed and descent angle toward the aiming point. Finally I transition to hover or landing as required and report on the pad if requested.

Short steps. Clear order. Ready for Part 2 if ATC asks for a progress report.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, report established on the final approach course.
2. Tower: ANAC123, report helipad in sight, cleared visual approach.
3. Radar: ANAC123, advise when ready to commence the approach.
4. Examiner: ANAC123, explain the missed approach procedure step by step.
5. Approach: ANAC123, report leaving the hold, proceeding inbound.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Procedures are stories with numbered beats.

If you explain without order markers, the examiner hears a cloud of actions. Put first/then/next/finally to work.

Critical checks deserve emphasis: "Before descent, I set the altimeter and brief minima." That shows safety thinking, not just memory.

In Part 2, compress the same procedure into radio length: report the state, not the essay. "Established" is the radio form of a longer Part 1 explanation.

H130 procedures often include site reconnaissance and power checks. Name them. Do not assume the examiner knows your company SOP — explain the logic briefly.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Explain the procedure for requesting and flying a weather deviation during cruise.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First, I assess the weather ahead and decide I need to deviate. Then I call ATC with a clear request: deviation left or right of course, and the reason. Next, I wait for clearance and read back the new heading or routing. Finally, I fly the cleared path, monitor weather, and request further deviation or return to course when able. I never deviate from the cleared route without authorization unless safety requires immediate action, and then I advise ATC as soon as possible.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Explaining steps out of chronological order.  
  ✔ Use first / then / next / finally consistently.

- ❌ Reciting every checklist line without purpose.  
  ✔ Summarize essential steps and highlight critical checks.

- ❌ Forgetting the purpose of the procedure.  
  ✔ Open with why the procedure is done.

- ❌ Mixing Part 1 essay length into Part 2 radio calls.  
  ✔ Compress for ATC; expand for the examiner discussion.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** First Then Next Finally

**Pronunciation:** FURST / THEN / NEKST / FYE-nuh-lee

**Word Stress**

- First → FURST
- Then → THEN
- Next → NEKST
- Finally → FYE-nuh-lee

Practice:

First I brief... then I request clearance... next I execute... finally I report.

Together:

First I confirm the clearance, then I fly the procedure, finally I report completion.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**PROCEDURE** — **P**urpose first, **R**ank steps in time order, **O**utline critical checks, **C**rew roles if needed, **E**xecute language with first/then, **D**rop unnecessary detail, **U**se ATC reports when required, **R**educe length for radio, **E**nd with completion criteria.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Explain the procedure for a precautionary landing due to abnormal vibration in the H130, then compress it for ATC.

**Your first task**

Give a Part 1 Level 4 explanation with purpose and ordered steps.

**Your second task**

Give the Part 2 radio call to ATC initiating the procedure.

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: The purpose is to land as soon as practical while the aircraft is still controllable. First I check engine instruments and reduce severity if procedures allow. Then I select a suitable landing area and brief the crew. Next I advise ATC of the problem and intentions. Finally I execute a controlled landing and shut down for inspection. Second task: ANAC123, abnormal vibration, requesting precautionary landing, present position. Procedure explained; radio compressed.
