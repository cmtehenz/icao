# Maintain Own Separation

**Catalog ID:** 0112  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Medium  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Maintain Own Separation — an ATC instruction authorizing a pilot to take responsibility for their own separation from specified traffic while remaining in communication with ATC.

Maintain Own Separation shifts separation responsibility temporarily to the pilot, requiring visual or procedural awareness of surrounding traffic.

In Brazilian operations, this instruction appears primarily in uncontrolled or advisory airspace near Navegantes, Joinville, and coastal VFR transit areas where IFR and VFR traffic mix.

You must understand when this responsibility applies, how to accept or decline it, and what visual contact obligations it creates.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 4 interaction and communication under shared responsibility conditions.

## 📖 Meaning

Maintain Own Separation is an ATC instruction or advisory authorizing a pilot to be responsible for their own separation from specified traffic, typically requiring the pilot to maintain visual contact with that traffic.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Manter separação própria.

Instrução ou aviso do controle autorizando o piloto a assumir a responsabilidade pela própria separação em relação a um tráfego especificado, geralmente exigindo contato visual com esse tráfego.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Maintain Own Separation is received, the pilot should:

- confirm visual contact with the specified traffic before accepting
- acknowledge acceptance or inability to accept
- maintain visual contact throughout the operation
- advise ATC immediately if visual contact is lost
- not descend below or conflict with the traffic referenced

Maintain Own Separation operations commonly include:

- VFR traffic separation in advisory airspace
- pilot responsibility behind preceding traffic during visual approach
- formation operations under pilot responsibility
- mixed IFR and VFR traffic in uncontrolled airspace

**Important:** If you cannot establish or maintain visual contact with the traffic, do not accept the instruction. Advise ATC: Unable, not in sight, ANAC123. ATC must then provide an alternative separation service.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC issues Maintain Own Separation to:

- reduce workload in advisory airspace where full ATC separation is not available
- allow visual approaches when the preceding traffic is in sight
- manage mixed traffic in Class G or uncontrolled airspace
- authorize sequencing when visual contact is confirmed
- facilitate formation operations

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on a visual approach to Navegantes in your H130.

An Embraer 120 is on a two mile final ahead.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, traffic Embraer one two zero on final, maintain own separation.

You acquire the traffic visually and respond:

Traffic in sight, maintain own separation, ANAC123.

You maintain visual contact and sequence behind the Embraer with appropriate spacing, landing after it clears the runway.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, traffic on final ahead, maintain own separation.
2. Approach: ANAC123, Embraer one two zero, two miles ahead on final, report traffic in sight.
3. Advisory: ANAC123, uncontrolled airspace, suggest own separation with reported traffic at nine o'clock.
4. Approach: ANAC123, maintain own separation from preceding traffic, report established final.
5. Approach: ANAC123, maintain own separation, resume own navigation.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Own separation is pilot responsibility — and responsibility requires certainty.

You accept it only when you have positive visual contact and can maintain it.

The sequence:

Receive instruction. Acquire traffic visually. Confirm you can maintain visual. Accept or decline. Keep eyes on traffic.

Brazilian pilots sometimes accept own separation without a clear visual acquisition — and then lose the traffic.

Never say traffic in sight until you are actually looking at it.

In the H130, with a wide field of view, visual acquisition is usually achievable — but clouds, haze, and sun angle can defeat it quickly.

If you lose visual contact at any point: advise ATC immediately and request separation services.

Do not wait to see if visual is recoverable. Advise now.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

When should you decline an instruction to maintain own separation?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I should decline maintain own separation if I cannot establish visual contact with the referenced traffic, if visibility or cloud prevents reliable visual contact, or if I am not confident I can maintain that visual contact throughout the procedure. I would advise ATC: Unable, traffic not in sight, ANAC123. ATC is then responsible for providing an alternative separation service and I must not compromise separation by accepting a responsibility I cannot fulfill.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "traffic in sight" without actually seeing it.  
  ✔ Only accept when you have positive, maintained visual contact.

- ❌ Not advising ATC when visual contact is lost.  
  ✔ Immediately report: Traffic no longer in sight, ANAC123.

- ❌ Accepting own separation in poor visibility or haze.  
  ✔ If conditions prevent reliable visual, decline the instruction.

- ❌ Treating maintain own separation as a permanent clearance.  
  ✔ It applies to the specific traffic referenced and ends when that traffic is no longer a factor.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

On visual approach to Navegantes, Approach transmits: ANAC123, Cessna on final ahead, maintain own separation.

**Your first task**

You cannot see the Cessna due to sun glare. What do you transmit?

**Your second task**

You acquire visual. Later the Cessna turns base and you lose sight. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

If I cannot see the Cessna I would transmit: Unable, traffic not in sight, ANAC123 — and wait for ATC to provide separation. If I later acquired visual and then lost sight of the Cessna, I would immediately report: Traffic no longer in sight, ANAC123. I would not proceed under own separation responsibility without continuous visual contact.
