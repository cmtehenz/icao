# FOD Reported

**Catalog ID:** 0222  
**Category:** Airport Operations  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers FOD Reported — the advisory or pilot report that foreign object debris has been observed on a runway, taxiway, or apron, creating a damage and safety hazard until inspected and removed.

FOD Reported is the formal hazard language of the movement area. It may come from ATC, airport operations, or from you as the first aircraft to see it. The response is the same: avoid, report, inspect, clear — not ignore.

For H130 helicopter training academy operations, FOD is especially dangerous. Rotor downwash can loft objects into the main or tail rotor, into engines of nearby aircraft, or toward personnel. Congonhas, Guarulhos, Santos Dumont, Curitiba, and Florianópolis all demand strict FOD discipline during taxi and hover. Part 2 and Part 3 both test this vocabulary.

Understanding how to report FOD, acknowledge FOD advisories, and change the taxi or takeoff plan is essential for ICAO proficiency and real aircraft protection.

## 📖 Meaning

FOD Reported means foreign object debris has been observed or notified on the aerodrome movement area, indicating a risk of aircraft damage or injury and typically requiring avoidance, inspection, removal, and possible surface closure or restriction.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

FOD reported / FOD reportado (Foreign Object Debris).

Significa que detritos de objetos estranhos foram observados ou notificados na área de movimento do aeródromo, indicando risco de dano à aeronave ou lesão e normalmente exigindo desvio, inspeção, remoção e possível interdição ou restrição da superfície.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When FOD is reported, the pilot should:

- acknowledge the affected surface and any closure or caution
- avoid the area; do not taxi or take off through reported FOD
- report additional FOD you observe with position and description
- for helicopters, avoid hover taxi that could redistribute FOD
- accept delayed departure or revised routing without pressure
- after FOD is cleared, still maintain lookout on the roll or taxi

FOD Reported commonly involves:

- runway or taxiway metal, plastic, or pavement fragments
- tools or equipment left after maintenance
- wildlife remains or nesting materials
- cargo straps, tags, or wrapping near aprons
- ice chunks or other seasonal debris in relevant climates

**Important:** "FOD reported" is not background chatter. It is a reason to change the plan. In the H130, a plastic bag is not harmless — it can become a rotor or engine event. If you generate FOD (for example, a panel or cover), stop and report immediately.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers say FOD reported to:

- alert all traffic to a shared foreign-object hazard
- justify runway or taxiway inspection and temporary closure
- prompt pilot confirmation from different vantage points
- prevent multiple aircraft from hitting the same object
- coordinate airport authority and operations vehicles for removal

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are lining up at Curitiba in the H130 for a training departure.

Tower: ANAC123, hold position, FOD reported on the runway, inspection in progress.

You hold. You do not ask to "depart from the intersection beyond the FOD" unless ATC offers a assessed-safe option. A vehicle inspects and removes a metal piece. Tower reopens the runway and clears you for takeoff.

Five minutes of patience protected the aircraft and the next student sortie.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, hold position, FOD reported on the runway, inspection in progress.
2. Ground: ANAC123, Bravo closed, FOD reported, taxi via Alpha.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cancel takeoff clearance, FOD reported ahead on the runway.
4. Ground: ANAC123, taxi with caution, FOD reported near stand four, vehicle en route.
5. Tower: ANAC123, confirm runway clear of FOD from your position.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

FOD is the invisible exam you take every taxi.

You pass by looking. You pass by reporting. You fail by assuming someone else already did.

When ATC says FOD reported, freeze the takeoff or the taxi into that surface. When you see FOD first, you become the reporter — short, specific, located.

Part 3 answers should show decision logic: stop, avoid hover over the area, report, wait for clearance. Examiners listen for whether you understand helicopter-specific risk, not only tire damage.

Brazilian busy aprons collect FOD from works, freighters, and wind. Guarulhos and Congonhas are not empty deserts. Eyes outside. Collective disciplined.

If cleared after inspection, still look. Inspection reduces risk; it does not invent perfection.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

ATC reports FOD on the runway as you are about to take off. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would reject the takeoff plan immediately, hold position, and acknowledge that FOD is reported and inspection is in progress. I would not attempt to take off over or around the reported debris. For a helicopter, I would also avoid any hover that could move the FOD. I would wait for ATC to confirm the runway is clear or to issue a new plan such as runway change or delay. If I saw the object myself, I would add a short position report to help the inspection team.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Asking to depart anyway because the FOD "is on the far end."  
  ✔ Hold until the runway is assessed clear or a safe alternative is issued.

- ❌ Using hover downwash to move FOD off the centerline.  
  ✔ Never relocate FOD with the rotor; report and wait for removal.

- ❌ Vague reports: "there is FOD somewhere."  
  ✔ Give surface name and a precise position reference.

- ❌ Cancelling lookout after "runway clear of FOD" is announced.  
  ✔ Continue visual scan during lineup and takeoff roll or lift-off.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are at the holding point at Guarulhos in the H130, ready for departure.

**Your first task**

Tower says: "ANAC123, hold position, FOD reported on the runway, inspection in progress." What do you say?

**Your second task**

From the holding point you see a plastic bag near the edge of the runway. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, holding position, FOD on the runway. Second task: ANAC123, additional FOD in sight, plastic bag near the right edge abeam the holding point — to help inspection and to keep the departure suspended until the surface is truly clear.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

FOD reported means the surface is guilty until proven clean.

Hold. Report what you see. Do not invent a takeoff path around it.

In the H130, downwash makes you part of the FOD problem if you are careless — or part of the solution if you are disciplined.

Eyes outside. Radio short. Rotors patient.

That is high-priority airport English that saves aircraft.
