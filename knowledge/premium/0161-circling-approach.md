# Circling Approach

**Catalog ID:** 0161  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Circling Approach — the instrument approach procedure in which the instrument approach is flown to one runway but the aircraft circles to land on a different runway.

This lesson distinguishes itself from Expect Circling (0109).

Expect Circling is an advisory — ATC tells you in advance to anticipate circling. It is information, not a clearance.

Cleared Circling Approach is the clearance — ATC explicitly authorizes the approach and the circling maneuver. That is what this lesson covers.

At Brazilian airports including Florianópolis, Navegantes, and Joinville, circling approaches are required when the active landing runway is not aligned with the instrument approach that provides the best minima or terrain clearance.

Circling requires precise maneuvering at low altitude and is one of the most demanding instrument approach phases.

## 📖 Meaning

A Circling Approach is an ATC-cleared instrument approach procedure in which the published approach is flown to the minimum descent altitude or decision altitude on one runway alignment, followed by a visual circle-to-land maneuver to land on a different runway, remaining within the published circling area at or above the circling minimum descent altitude.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the crew receives a circling approach clearance, they should:

- confirm the approach type and the runway to which the circling will be made
- review the published circling minima on the approach chart — these are higher than straight-in minima
- brief the circling maneuver: direction of turn, proximity to the airfield, visual reference to maintain
- execute the instrument approach to the circling MDA
- at the circling MDA, fly the visual circling maneuver in sight of the runway environment
- keep the airfield and the landing runway in sight throughout the circle
- do not descend below circling MDA until in a position to make a normal descent to the runway
- if visual contact is lost during the circle, execute the missed approach immediately

Circling approach operations commonly include:

- cleared ILS runway one four, circle to runway three two
- cleared VOR/DME runway two seven, circle to land runway zero nine
- circling minima: MDA eight hundred, visibility three kilometers
- circling protected area defined by radius from runway threshold

**Important:** Circling minima are always higher than straight-in minima. The circling environment requires visual contact with the airfield throughout. If you lose sight of the runway at any point during the circle, execute the missed approach immediately — not when you feel ready, not after one more attempt.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers clear circling approaches when:

- the active runway is not aligned with the best available instrument approach
- terrain or obstacles prevent a straight-in approach to the active runway
- wind conditions require landing on a runway not served by an available instrument approach
- the published circling minima are achievable and the circling area is clear

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are inbound to Florianópolis in your H130. The active runway is three two. The available instrument approach is the ILS runway one four.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, cleared ILS runway one four, circle to runway three two, report runway in sight.

You brief the circling approach: fly the ILS to circling MDA, then execute a right-hand circle to runway three two. Circling MDA is five hundred and eighty feet, visibility three kilometers minimum.

You respond:

Cleared ILS runway one four, circle to runway three two, ANAC123.

You fly the ILS to circling MDA. The runway environment comes into sight.

You report:

ANAC123, runway in sight.

Approach: ANAC123, cleared to land runway three two.

You execute the circle — maintaining the airfield in sight, staying within the circling area, descending to land on runway three two.

Smooth. Controlled. Professional.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Circling approaches are the demanding end of instrument flying.

You are flying a precision or non-precision approach on one alignment, then breaking off and flying a visual maneuver close to terrain and obstacles in marginal weather.

Three rules govern the circle:

First — never go below circling MDA until you are in a position for a normal descent to the runway.

Second — if you lose sight of the runway environment at any point, execute the missed approach immediately.

Third — stay within the published circling radius. The protected area is defined precisely. Outside it, obstacle clearance is not guaranteed.

The difference between Expect Circling and Cleared Circling Approach: expect circling is an advisory. It tells you to prepare. Cleared circling approach is the authorization. It says fly it now.

Do not begin the circle until you have the cleared circling approach. Expect Circling alone is not permission to execute.

In the H130, brief the circling direction, the MDA, the missed approach point, and the runway to land before starting the approach. In marginal weather, have your crew resource management sharp — one pilot flies the approach, the other monitors visual references throughout the circle.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Explain the difference between Expect Circling and a Cleared Circling Approach, and describe when you would execute a missed approach during circling.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Expect Circling is an advisory phrase from ATC informing the crew to anticipate a circling maneuver to a specific runway. It is preparatory information — not a clearance. The crew should brief the circling procedure but may not begin the approach or execute the circle until a formal clearance is issued. A Cleared Circling Approach is the explicit ATC authorization to fly the instrument approach and execute the circling maneuver to land on the specified runway. During the circling maneuver, if the runway environment is lost from sight at any point, the crew must execute the missed approach immediately, without hesitation, from the current position using the published missed approach procedure for the approach that was flown.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Descending below circling MDA during the circle before reaching a position for a normal landing.  
  ✔ Maintain circling MDA until established for the final descent to the runway.

- ❌ Continuing the circle after losing sight of the runway environment.  
  ✔ Lost sight = immediate missed approach. No exceptions.

- ❌ Confusing Expect Circling with clearance to execute the circle.  
  ✔ Expect Circling is advisory only. Wait for the cleared circling approach before executing.

- ❌ Not reading back both runways — the approach runway and the landing runway.  
  ✔ Read back both: cleared ILS runway one four, circle to runway three two, ANAC123.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Florianópolis Approach transmits: "ANAC123, cleared ILS runway one four, circle to runway three two. Circling MDA five hundred and eighty feet, visibility three kilometers."

**Your first task**

Read back the complete clearance.

**Your second task**

During the left-hand circle to runway three two, you pass through a patch of low cloud and lose visual contact with the runway for approximately five seconds. Cloud base is broken at five hundred feet. You are at five hundred and eighty feet. What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would read back: Cleared ILS runway one four, circle to runway three two, ANAC123. If I lost visual contact with the runway during the circling maneuver — even for five seconds — I would immediately execute the missed approach. Five seconds of lost visual at low altitude in a circling environment is not acceptable. I would transmit: ANAC123, going missed, lost visual during circle. I would fly the ILS runway one four missed approach procedure, climb to the published missed approach altitude, and advise ATC. I would not attempt to resume the circle after regaining visual. The approach must be abandoned.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Circling approaches are among the highest-workload maneuvers in instrument flying.

Visual reference is not optional — it is your safety net. Lose it, and the net is gone.

Brief the approach completely: MDA, visibility minima, circling direction, missed approach.

Fly precisely. Maintain altitude. Stay in the protected area.

If visual is lost — missed approach. No debate, no delay.

The missed approach is not failure. It is the system working correctly.
