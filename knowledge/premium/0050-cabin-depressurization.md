# Cabin Depressurization

**Catalog ID:** 0050  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers cabin depressurization — a classic ICAO exam topic.

Cabin Depressurization means the pressurized cabin loses altitude equivalent pressure — rapidly or gradually.

Pressurized helicopters are rare, but ICAO Part 2 and Part 3 test this phraseology extensively.

You must don oxygen, descend immediately, and declare the correct urgency call.

Rapid decompression is distress — Mayday.

Gradual decompression may be urgency — Pan Pan.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Cabin Depressurization is the loss of cabin pressure, exposing occupants to altitude-related hypoxia and requiring immediate descent.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Despressurização da cabine.

Perda de pressão na cabine, expondo ocupantes à hipóxia por altitude.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Cabin Depressurization occurs, the pilot should:

- don oxygen immediately
- initiate emergency descent to ten thousand feet or lower
- declare Mayday for rapid decompression or Pan Pan for gradual loss
- inform ATC of depressurization, intentions, and souls on board
- request lower altitude and priority handling

Cabin Depressurization reporting commonly includes:

- rapid decompression
- sudden decompression
- requesting lower altitude due to pressurization problem
- souls on board and medical assistance when needed

**Important:** Rapid decompression is always Mayday. Time is critical — hypoxia can incapacitate within minutes. Even in an unpressurized H130, know this phraseology for the ICAO exam and for pressurized aircraft operations.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Cabin Depressurization declarations to:

- clear all traffic from the aircraft's altitude and route
- approve immediate descent without delay
- coordinate priority landing and medical services
- request souls on board and nature of injuries
- alert other aircraft in the area

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are simulating a pressurized helicopter exam scenario, callsign ANAC123, cruising at flight level two five zero over Brasília.

The cabin altitude warning sounds and masks deploy.

You transmit:

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, roger Mayday, cleared immediate descent, say souls on board.
2. Control: ANAC123, turn left heading one eight zero, descend unrestricted, Brasília in sight.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway one one cleared to land, emergency services standing by.
4. Approach: ANAC123, all traffic cleared from your altitude, say medical assistance required.
5. Control: ANAC123, confirm you have donned oxygen and are descending.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC immediately after a rapid decompression?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would don oxygen immediately, initiate emergency descent, and declare Mayday Mayday Mayday. I would state my callsign, report rapid decompression, state that I am descending to ten thousand feet, and request vectors to the nearest suitable airport. I would provide souls on board when ATC requests.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Requesting lower altitude before donning oxygen.  
  ✔ Don oxygen first — hypoxia impairs judgment within seconds.

- ❌ Saying "cabine perdeu pressão" instead of standard English.  
  ✔ Use rapid decompression or sudden decompression.

- ❌ Using Pan Pan for rapid decompression.  
  ✔ Rapid decompression is distress — declare Mayday.

- ❌ Delaying descent to complete radio calls.  
  ✔ Descend first — communicate while descending if able.
