# Brace Position

**Catalog ID:** 0352  
**Category:** Emergency  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Brace Position — the protective body posture commanded before an imminent impact, ditching, or crash landing so occupants reduce injury risk during the landing sequence.

Brace position is cabin and cockpit language as much as radio language. On the ICAO exam you must explain it; in operations you must command it and, when needed, tell ATC that a forced landing or ditching is imminent.

For H130 helicopter operations under ICAO Delta academy training in Brazil, brace applies before autorotation touchdown, forced landing, or ditching — with rotor and door considerations, not airliner slide choreography.

Understanding brace position in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Brace Position is the protective seating posture adopted by crew and passengers immediately before an expected impact, forced landing, or ditching, designed to reduce injury from deceleration and debris, commanded by the crew when landing is imminent and survival depends on correct body position.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Posição de proteção / Brace Position.

Postura protetora adotada pela tripulação e passageiros imediatamente antes de um impacto esperado, pouso forçado ou amerissagem, destinada a reduzir lesões por desaceleração e debris, comandada pela tripulação quando o pouso é iminente e a sobrevivência depende da posição corporal correta.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When brace position is required, the crew should:

- fly the aircraft to the best available landing site
- brief occupants early when time allows: brace on command
- command "brace brace brace" or equivalent when impact is imminent
- declare Mayday for forced landing or ditching with position and intentions
- after touchdown, command evacuate or remain as conditions require
- advise ATC of landing complete and evacuation status when able

Brace-related reporting commonly includes:

- forced landing / ditching imminent
- brace for landing (cabin command — not normally to ATC)
- landing complete, evacuating / all souls clear
- request rescue / fire services / SAR

**Important:** Brace is a cabin command. ATC needs Mayday, nature of emergency, and landing intention — not a long explanation of brace technique on frequency.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers respond to imminent forced-landing or ditching calls to:

- clear traffic and protect the landing area when possible
- alert rescue, fire, and SAR services immediately
- obtain last known position and souls on board
- coordinate surface and maritime response after impact
- keep frequency clear for the distressed aircraft

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

En route along the Santa Catarina coast in the H130, engine failure forces an autorotation toward a clear field near Navegantes.

You declare Mayday, state engine failure and forced landing. With seconds left, you command brace to occupants. After touchdown you shut down, open doors, and get clear.

Tower and Approach already have rescue moving because the Mayday was early and clear — not because you described brace technique on the radio.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger Mayday, say souls on board and position.
2. Approach: ANAC123, emergency services alerted, report landing complete if able.
3. Tower: ANAC123, runway vacated for you if able to reach field, wind calm.
4. Approach: ANAC123, SAR and fire services rolling, say if ditching or landing on field.
5. Tower: ANAC123, confirm all souls clear after landing.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, ANAC123, engine failure, forced landing imminent, two miles south of Navegantes.
2. ANAC123, souls on board four, forced landing on open field, request rescue.
3. ANAC123, ditching imminent, request SAR.
4. ANAC123, landing complete, evacuating, request fire services.
5. ANAC123, all souls clear of aircraft, awaiting rescue.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Brace position saves bodies. Mayday saves the response.

Do not confuse the two audiences. Occupants hear brace. ATC hears forced landing or ditching imminent.

Brazilian crews sometimes translate awkwardly — "posição de impacto" on frequency. Keep brace in the cabin. On the radio: Mayday, malfunction, landing now, position, souls.

In the H130, brief early when the autorotation allows. Command late and loud when the ground is close. After landing, evacuate clear of rotors and fire risk.

Examiners want you to know what brace means and when you command it — then switch to clean distress phraseology for ATC.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is brace position, and what do you tell ATC before a forced landing?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Brace position is the protective posture occupants take before an expected impact. I would command brace to the cabin when landing is imminent. To ATC I would declare Mayday, state the emergency — for example engine failure — report forced landing imminent with position, and request rescue. After landing I would report landing complete and whether we are evacuating.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Explaining brace technique to ATC on a congested frequency.  
  ✔ Cabin gets the brace command; ATC gets Mayday and landing intention.

- ❌ Delaying Mayday until after the brace call.  
  ✔ Declare early so rescue is already moving.

- ❌ Forgetting souls and position in the distress call.  
  ✔ Include position and souls when able.

- ❌ Using only Portuguese cabin commands in an ICAO oral answer.  
  ✔ Explain brace in English; demonstrate standard Mayday phraseology.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Pronunciation:** BRAYSS BRAYSS BRAYSS — FORST LAND-ing IH-muh-nunt

**Word Stress**

- Brace → BRAYSS
- Forced → FORST
- Landing → LAND-ing
- Imminent → IH-muh-nunt

Practice:

Brace brace brace... forced landing imminent...

Together:

Mayday Mayday Mayday, ANAC123, forced landing imminent, request rescue.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**BRACE** — **B**rief early if time, **R**adio Mayday first to ATC, **A**ttitude and site selection, **C**ommand brace to occupants, **E**vacuate clear after touchdown.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Engine failure at eight hundred feet near the coast. You select a field. Occupants are briefed.

**Your first task**

What do you transmit to ATC?

**Your second task**

After touchdown you evacuate. What do you say when able?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: Mayday Mayday Mayday, Approach, ANAC123, engine failure, forced landing imminent, eight hundred feet, south of Navegantes, souls four, request rescue. Second task: ANAC123, landing complete, all souls clear of aircraft, awaiting rescue. Brace stays in the cabin — not on the ATC frequency.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Brace is for the body. Mayday is for the system around you.

Command brace when impact is close. Tell ATC the emergency and the landing now.

In the H130, autorotation time is short — practice the words so they come without thinking.

Protect the occupants. Alert the rescue. Speak both languages to the right audience.
