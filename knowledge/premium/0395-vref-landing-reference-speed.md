# Vref Landing Reference Speed

**Catalog ID:** 0395  
**Category:** Performance  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Vref Landing Reference Speed — the landing reference speed used in aeroplane performance: typically 1.3 times the stall speed in the landing configuration, corrected for weight, and used as the basis for approach speed additives.

Vref is fixed-wing exam language central to ICAO Part 2 and Part 4 landing performance discussions.

For H130 operations: the helicopter does not use Vref. Approach and landing speeds follow the AFM — normal approach speed, steep approach, or running landing profiles as applicable. Learn Vref for ICAO English; fly and communicate helicopter speeds in Brazilian H130 operations.

## 📖 Meaning

Vref is the landing reference speed for an aeroplane: a calibrated airspeed, not less than 1.3 VS in the landing configuration at the landing weight, used as the reference for final approach speed before wind and configuration additives.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Velocidade de referência de pouso Vref / Vref Landing Reference Speed.

Velocidade calibrada de referência para pouso em aviões, não inferior a 1,3 VS na configuração de pouso no peso de pouso, usada como base para a velocidade de aproximação final antes dos acréscimos de vento e configuração. O H130 não utiliza Vref.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When discussing Vref, the crew should:

- calculate Vref from landing weight and configuration (aeroplane)
- apply company additives for wind, ice, and gusts to obtain approach speed
- brief Vref and target approach speed before descent (aeroplane SOP)
- for H130: brief AFM approach speed for the landing type — not Vref
- explain Vref correctly in exams without using it on H130 frequency

Vref-related concepts include:

- Vref ≈ 1.3 VS0 (landing configuration stall speed) at weight
- additives: steady headwind, gust factor per SOP
- flap configuration changes affecting Vref
- landing performance charts based on Vref crossings
- H130: approach speed from AFM; running landing may use higher speed — still not called Vref

**Important:** Saying "approaching at Vref" in an H130 implies fixed-wing technique. Say the actual indicated speed or "on profile / on approach speed" as appropriate for helicopter ops.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

ATC does not assign Vref, but landing speed issues appear when:

- crews request longer runway for landing performance
- tailwind or wet runway makes landing marginal
- go-around follows an unstable approach (speed high or low)
- overweight or performance-limited landings need coordination
- speed control on final conflicts with required approach speed

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Exam context: Vref 120 knots, add half the headwind — fly final at calculated approach speed.

H130 into Santos Dumont: you fly the AFM approach speed for a normal or steep approach. If ATC asks you to keep speed up for spacing beyond what is safe:

ANAC123, unable higher speed on final, performance / approach profile limited, request adequate spacing.

You protect the helicopter profile without inventing a Vref call.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, maintain one eight zero knots until final approach fix.
2. Approach: ANAC123, reduce to final approach speed.
3. Tower: ANAC123, wind one eight zero at one two, cleared to land.
4. Tower: ANAC123, caution tailwind, advise able to land.
5. Approach: ANAC123, go around, fly runway heading, climb two thousand.

Vref is the aeroplane landing reference speed, not less than 1.3 times stall speed in the landing configuration at landing weight, used as the basis for final approach speed with wind additives. ## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Using Vref terminology on H130 approaches.  
  ✔ Use AFM approach speed / on profile language.

- ❌ Defining Vref as any approach speed without the 1.3 VS basis.  
  ✔ State the regulatory/performance definition with landing configuration.

- ❌ Ignoring wind additives in exam answers about approach speed.  
  ✔ Mention headwind and gust additives per typical SOP concepts.

- ❌ Accepting ATC speed control that destabilizes the helicopter approach.  
  ✔ State unable and request spacing when necessary.
