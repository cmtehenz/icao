# Taxi Via

**Catalog ID:** 0024  
**Category:** Ground Operations  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson moves from the air to the ground — and it is one of the most important surface instructions in aviation.

When Ground says Taxi Via, they are giving you a specific route to follow on the airport movement area.

You must understand the route, read it back correctly, and comply with any hold short instructions along the way.

Runway incursions often begin with misunderstood taxi clearances.

Professional pilots treat Taxi Via with the same discipline as a takeoff clearance.

## 📖 Meaning

Taxi Via instructs the pilot to taxi to a destination using the specific taxiways or route stated by ATC.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Taxi via.

Siga a rota de táxi indicada pelo controle até o ponto autorizado.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC issues Taxi Via, the pilot should:

- taxi only on the assigned route
- read back the runway assignment if given for departure
- read back any hold short instruction
- stop at hold short points unless explicitly cleared to cross
- request clarification if the route is not understood

Taxi Via clearances commonly include:

- departure runway assignment
- one or more taxiway designators (Alpha, Bravo, Charlie…)
- hold short of a runway or taxiway
- instructions to contact tower at a holding point

**Important:** ATC does not use the word "cleared" for taxi authorization. Taxi Via is an instruction to move on the surface — not clearance to enter a runway for takeoff.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Ground Control uses Taxi Via to:

- guide aircraft safely on the movement area
- prevent runway incursions
- sequence departures and arrivals on the ground
- route traffic around construction or closed taxiways
- coordinate traffic at complex airports

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You have just landed at Congonhas Airport in your H130.

After vacating the runway, Ground contacts you:

Ground: ANAC123, taxi to the general aviation ramp via Alpha, Bravo, hold short of runway one seven left.

You read back the full route and hold short instruction, then taxi carefully using the airport diagram.

At the hold short point, you stop and wait for further clearance before crossing the active runway.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Ground: ANAC123, runway one eight, taxi via Alpha, hold short of runway two seven.
2. Ground: ANAC123, taxi to parking via Bravo, Charlie.
3. Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Alpha, cross runway zero nine, hold short of runway one eight.
4. Ground: ANAC123, taxi via Charlie to the helipad.
5. Ground: ANAC123, runway three six left, taxi via Alpha, Delta, contact tower at holding point Delta two.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Imagine a hospital with colored lines on the floor.

You are not free to walk anywhere.

You must follow the line Ground assigns.

Taxi Via is that line.

If Ground says via Alpha, Bravo, you do not shortcut across an open ramp unless cleared.

And if they say hold short of a runway, that is a hard stop — even if the runway looks empty.

The FAA requires controllers to obtain a readback of runway hold short instructions.

That means your readback is not optional politeness.

It is part of the safety system.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What should a pilot include in a taxi clearance readback?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

The pilot should read back the runway assignment if given, the taxi route, and any hold short or runway crossing instructions. The readback should include the aircraft identification and use standard phraseology.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Reading back only "Roger" or "Wilco" without the route.  
  ✔ Read back route and hold short points completely.

- ❌ Omitting hold short from the readback.  
  ✔ Always repeat hold short of runway XX when issued.

- ❌ Crossing a runway without explicit clearance.  
  ✔ Wait for cross runway or taxi instructions that authorize crossing.

- ❌ Confusing taxi instruction with takeoff clearance.  
  ✔ Taxi Via does not authorize entering the departure runway for takeoff.

Speak taxiway letters with ICAO phonetics when needed in busy frequency environments.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**Via** = **V**ector on the **ground**.

Ground gives you the vector (route). You follow it exactly — no improvisation.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

ATC says:

Ground: ANAC123, runway one eight, taxi via Alpha, cross runway zero nine, hold short of runway one eight.

**Your first task**

Read back the clearance exactly as a professional pilot.

**Your second task**

Answer this question:

You have reached runway zero nine. May you cross without another instruction?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Only if the taxi clearance explicitly authorizes crossing runway zero nine. In this example, yes — the clearance includes cross runway zero nine. I must still hold short of runway one eight unless further cleared.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Taxi Via is where many flights feel routine — and where safety can fail if pilots relax.

Know your airport diagram.

Read back hold short instructions.

Stop when required.

The best pilots are disciplined on the ground long before they are impressive in the air.
