# Exit Hold

**Catalog ID:** 0107  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Exit Hold — the ATC clearance that releases an aircraft from a holding pattern and restores its onward routing.

Exit Hold ends the waiting and resumes the flight toward the destination or next waypoint.

In the H130, holding at Brazilian airports like Florianópolis, Curitiba, and Navegantes is common during sequencing, weather, and traffic delays.

You must recognize the instruction, read it back correctly, and transition smoothly from hold to cleared routing.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and holding pattern operations.

## 📖 Meaning

Exit Hold is an ATC instruction releasing an aircraft from a previously assigned holding pattern, clearing it to continue on the assigned route or a new clearance.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Sair da espera.

Instrução do controle liberando a aeronave da espera e autorizando o prosseguimento da rota ou do novo rumo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Exit Hold is received, the pilot should:

- note the current position in the holding pattern
- plan the exit at the inbound fix at the end of the current orbit
- confirm the onward routing or new clearance
- contact approach or the next sector as instructed
- note any revised routing, altitude, or speed given with the exit clearance

Exit Hold operations commonly include:

- resume own navigation on departure from hold
- cleared approach after exiting hold
- new altitude assigned with exit
- resume filed route after exiting hold

**Important:** Do not exit the holding pattern until you receive the explicit clearance. Continue orbiting until ATC says exit hold.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC issues Exit Hold to:

- release a held aircraft when traffic permits
- sequence the aircraft for approach after a delay
- resume routing after weather or traffic clears
- coordinate an aircraft to the next sector
- provide a new clearance after the hold reason is resolved

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are holding at IDAMO fix near Florianópolis in your H130, waiting in a holding stack.

Traffic ahead clears and Approach transmits:

ANAC123, exit hold, expect ILS runway one four, report leaving hold.

You plan the exit at the next inbound fix and transmit:

Exit hold, expect ILS runway one four, ANAC123.

On departing the fix outbound, you report:

Leaving hold, ANAC123.

Approach sequences you for the approach and the delay is behind you.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, exit hold, resume own navigation, proceed direct IDAMO.
2. Approach: ANAC123, exit hold, cleared ILS runway one four, report leaving hold.
3. Approach: ANAC123, exit hold, descend and maintain three thousand, report vacating hold.
4. Radar: ANAC123, exit hold, contact Florianópolis Approach on one two zero decimal niner.
5. Approach: ANAC123, exit hold, speed two two zero knots, vectors to final.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Exit hold, direct IDAMO, ANAC123.
2. Exit hold, cleared ILS runway one four, ANAC123. Leaving hold, ANAC123.
3. Exit hold, descend and maintain three thousand, ANAC123.
4. Exit hold, Florianópolis Approach one two zero decimal niner, ANAC123.
5. Exit hold, speed two two zero, ANAC123.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Exit hold ends the waiting. Now the work begins.

The moment you hear exit hold, two things happen: you plan your exit and you read back the onward clearance.

The sequence:

Hear exit hold. Identify position in the hold. Plan exit at inbound fix. Read back clearance. Fly the route.

Brazilian pilots sometimes rush the exit before completing the current orbit.

Always exit at the inbound fix — not mid-orbit, not on outbound.

In the H130, confirm your altitude and routing before leaving the hold.

If you receive a new clearance with exit hold, read back every element.

Train the transition from holding to cleared routing as a distinct mental phase.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What do you do when ATC instructs exit hold and how do you confirm?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

When ATC instructs exit hold, I acknowledge and read back the complete clearance including the onward routing or approach. I plan the exit at the inbound fix at the end of the current orbit, not mid-pattern. I report leaving the hold when I depart the fix, and confirm my altitude and routing before continuing toward the destination.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Exiting the hold mid-orbit without completing the pattern.  
  ✔ Always exit at the inbound fix.

- ❌ Saying only "Roger" without reading back the onward clearance.  
  ✔ Read back the full clearance: exit hold, route, altitude, ANAC123.

- ❌ Forgetting to report leaving the hold when asked.  
  ✔ Report: Leaving hold, ANAC123.

- ❌ Entering a new hold instead of the assigned route after exit.  
  ✔ Confirm onward routing clearly before departing the fix.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are holding at IDAMO near Florianópolis. Approach transmits: ANAC123, exit hold, descend and maintain two thousand, expect ILS runway one four.

**Your first task**

What do you read back?

**Your second task**

Approach asks you to report leaving hold. What do you say when you depart the fix?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would read back: Exit hold, descend and maintain two thousand, expect ILS runway one four, ANAC123. When I depart the inbound fix I would report: Leaving hold, ANAC123. I would then manage the descent and configure for the ILS.
