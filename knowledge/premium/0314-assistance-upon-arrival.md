# Assistance upon Arrival

**Catalog ID:** 0314  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Assistance upon Arrival — the pilot request for specific ground help to be ready when the aircraft lands or arrives at the stand or helipad. Core Part 2 and Part 3 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: request the right services early enough for the aerodrome to prepare.

Assistance upon arrival is High priority because fire, medical, and technical teams need lead time before you touch down.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for arrival assistance under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including medical, fire standby, and engineering support.

Understanding how to request assistance upon arrival in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 and Part 3 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Assistance upon Arrival is a pilot request that specified ground or emergency services be available when the aircraft arrives — for example medical personnel, fire services on standby, technical support, or passenger assistance — usually coordinated by ATC with the destination or diversion aerodrome.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Assistência na chegada / Assistance upon Arrival.

Solicitação do piloto para que serviços de solo ou de emergência especificados estejam disponíveis quando a aeronave chegar — por exemplo equipe médica, bombeiros em standby, apoio técnico ou assistência a passageiros — geralmente coordenada pelo ATC com o aeródromo de destino ou de desvio.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When requesting assistance upon arrival, the crew should:

- say assistance upon arrival and name the service required
- give ETA or distance so the aerodrome can time the response
- report souls on board and nature of the problem
- for H130: specify helipad or runway arrival if that changes vehicle access
- update ATC if the required assistance changes before landing
- after landing, follow ATC and ground instructions to the assistance point

Arrival-assistance exchanges commonly include:

- request assistance upon arrival
- medical / fire services / technical assistance upon arrival
- ambulance standing by
- request follow-me / marshaller
- souls on board... assistance upon arrival

**Important:** Requesting assistance upon arrival is proactive coordination. It does not automatically mean mayday — but it often accompanies abnormal or urgency situations.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers coordinate assistance upon arrival to:

- alert ARFF, medical, and technical teams before landing
- position vehicles without blocking other traffic unnecessarily
- pass ETA, souls, and problem type to the aerodrome
- sequence you to the most suitable runway, pad, or stand
- keep the frequency informed when services are in place

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 with a caution light and a passenger feeling unwell, diverting to Campo de Marte.

You call:

ANAC123, request assistance upon arrival, medical and technical, souls on board three, ETA two zero minutes.

Approach coordinates with the tower. Later:

Tower: ANAC123, assistance arranged, medical and technical standing by.

You continue the approach knowing the right teams will meet you. That is the purpose of the call — prepare the ground before the skids touch.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, confirm assistance required upon arrival.
2. Tower: ANAC123, say type of assistance upon arrival.
3. Approach: ANAC123, assistance arranged, medical standing by.
4. Tower: ANAC123, fire services on standby for your arrival.
5. Ground: ANAC123, assistance vehicles in position, taxi as directed.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Assistance upon arrival is a timing request as much as a help request.

Say what you need and when you will be there. Medical. Fire standby. Technical. Not "some help."

In Brazilian city heliports and airports, vehicle access and pad space matter for the H130. Give ATC enough notice. Part 3 scenarios often hinge on whether you prepared the ground or only announced the problem on short final.

Request early. Update if the need changes. Land to a prepared response.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You have a technical caution and one unwell passenger. You will land in twenty minutes. How do you request assistance upon arrival?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would request assistance upon arrival, specifying medical and technical support, report souls on board, and give ETA or time to run. I would keep the reason brief and update ATC if the condition changes. After landing, I would follow instructions to the point where the assistance teams are waiting.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Requesting "assistance" with no service type.  
  ✔ Name medical, fire, technical, or other specific help.

- ❌ Calling only on short final when teams cannot position in time.  
  ✔ Request with enough lead time and an ETA.

- ❌ Forgetting souls on board with medical requests.  
  ✔ Include the total persons for response planning.

- ❌ Assuming the request remains valid after a diversion change.  
  ✔ Reconfirm assistance at the new arrival aerodrome.

## 🧠 Memory Trick

**ARRIVE** — **A**nnounce the service type, **R**eport souls, **R**elay ETA, **I**nform if needs change, **V**erify services arranged, **E**xit to the assistance point as directed.
