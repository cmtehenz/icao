# Hail

**Catalog ID:** 0094  
**Category:** Weather  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 3

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers hail — frozen precipitation that can damage rotors, windscreens, and airframe surfaces.

Hail is almost always associated with thunderstorms and must be avoided.

In the H130, even small hail can damage rotor blades and force an immediate precautionary landing.

You must deviate around hail shafts, report hail to ATC, and land for inspection if hail is encountered.

This lesson prepares you for ICAO Part 2 phraseology and Part 3 emergency communication.

## 📖 Meaning

Hail is precipitation in the form of ice pellets produced by thunderstorms, capable of causing significant aircraft damage.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Granizo.

Precipitação em forma de pedras de gelo produzida por tempestades, capaz de causar dano significativo à aeronave.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Hail is present or encountered, the pilot should:

- avoid the area completely — never penetrate a hail shaft
- request weather deviation or diversion immediately
- report hail to ATC with location and size if known
- land as soon as practicable for inspection if hail is struck
- declare urgency if damage affects safe flight

Hail reporting commonly includes:

- hail encountered or hail shaft ahead
- request weather deviation
- possible airframe or rotor damage
- request precautionary landing

**Important:** Hail means thunderstorm. Avoidance is mandatory. If struck — inspect before further flight.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

ATC responds to Hail reports to:

- warn all aircraft in the area immediately
- approve weather deviations and diversions
- relay hail PIREPs with location
- assist damaged aircraft with priority landing
- coordinate emergency services if structural damage is reported

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are IFR toward Curitiba in your H130 when ATC advises a thunderstorm on your route.

You request deviation, but briefly encounter small hail at the edge of the cell.

You transmit:

Curitiba Approach, ANAC123, hail encountered, request weather deviation and precautionary landing Curitiba.

Approach clears you direct and you land for rotor and windscreen inspection.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Approach: ANAC123, roger hail, deviation approved, say intentions.
2. Approach: ANAC123, hail reported by previous aircraft, advise.
3. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land, emergency services notified if required.
4. Center: ANAC123, say hail size and altitude if able.
5. Approach: ANAC123, all traffic advised of hail on your PIREP.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. Curitiba Approach, ANAC123, hail encountered, request weather deviation.
2. ANAC123, hail shaft ahead, unable to continue present routing, request diversion.
3. ANAC123, hail encountered, possible rotor damage, request precautionary landing.
4. Pan Pan Pan Pan, ANAC123, hail damage, request priority landing.
5. Approach, ANAC123, clear of hail, proceeding direct Curitiba for inspection.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Hail is frozen damage.

It comes from thunderstorms. It hits hard. It can ruin blades.

The sequence:

Avoid. Deviate. Report. Land and inspect if struck.

Never aim for the dark rain shaft under a CB.

Brazilian pilots sometimes say "granizo" on frequency.

Use hail — ICAO standard term.

In the H130, rotor blades and the windscreen are vulnerable.

If you hear or feel hail — exit and inspect.

Train thunderstorm avoidance until hail is something you only hear about in PIREPs — not in your cockpit.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What would you tell ATC if you encountered hail in flight?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would report hail encountered with my altitude and position, request weather deviation or diversion, and request a precautionary landing for inspection. If damage affected safe flight, I would declare Pan Pan or Mayday and request priority landing.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Flying near thunderstorm cores where hail is likely.  
  ✔ Deviate early — hail shafts are no-go areas.

- ❌ Saying only "granizo" without intentions.  
  ✔ Use hail encountered, request deviation or precautionary landing.

- ❌ Continuing the flight after hail without inspection.  
  ✔ Land and inspect rotors and windscreen before further flight.

- ❌ Not warning ATC for other traffic.  
  ✔ Report immediately — hail PIREPs save aircraft.
