# Monitor

**Catalog ID:** 0136  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Monitor — an ATC instruction directing the pilot to listen on a specified frequency without the requirement to establish two-way contact.

Monitor is a passive listening instruction.

It tells you to tune a frequency and pay attention — but it does not require you to call the station.

In the H130, operating around Navegantes, Florianópolis, or Joinville, you may be asked to monitor ATIS, a guard frequency, or an advisory frequency while remaining in contact with your current controller.

Understanding the difference between Monitor and Contact is essential for professional radio management.

## 📖 Meaning

Monitor is an ATC instruction directing the pilot to listen on a specified frequency without initiating two-way communications unless the broadcast contains information affecting the flight.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Monitore.

Instrução do controle para o piloto sintonizar e escutar uma frequência específica sem a necessidade de estabelecer contato bidirecional, a menos que a transmissão contenha informação relevante para o voo.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When Monitor is received, the pilot should:

- tune the specified frequency on the secondary radio
- listen continuously to that frequency
- not transmit on that frequency unless required by a broadcast
- maintain primary communication on the current frequency
- note any relevant information received on the monitored frequency

Monitor instructions commonly apply to:

- ATIS broadcasts before arrival
- Guard frequencies during oceanic or remote segments
- Advisory frequencies in uncontrolled airspace
- Discrete frequencies awaiting handoff
- VOLMET or meteorological broadcast frequencies

**Important:** Monitor does not require acknowledgment on the monitored frequency. Unlike Contact, there is no expectation of a two-way exchange. If the broadcast requires a response, use the frequency accordingly — otherwise, simply listen.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Monitor to:

- ensure pilots receive ATIS or advisory broadcasts independently
- reduce radio congestion on primary frequencies
- maintain pilot awareness on guard or emergency frequencies
- prepare pilots for an imminent frequency transfer
- allow passive monitoring without tying up the frequency

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are descending toward Florianópolis in your H130, currently with Approach on 119.0.

Approach transmits:

ANAC123, monitor ATIS on one two seven decimal seven.

You tune 127.7 on your second radio and begin listening.

The ATIS gives you the current weather, runway in use, and QNH.

You note runway three two is in use, QNH 1018, visibility ten kilometres.

You do not call the ATIS station — you simply absorb the information.

When Approach asks: ANAC123, confirm you have information Bravo?

You reply: Affirm, information Bravo, ANAC123.

Monitor gave you the data before ATC needed to read it to you. Efficient and professional.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Monitor is a one-word instruction with one clear expectation: listen.

Not call. Not check in. Not confirm you are there.

Just listen.

Many Brazilian pilots confuse Monitor with Contact and immediately call the monitored frequency.

This clutters a broadcast channel or wakes up a station that did not expect a call.

If ATC wants you to call someone, they will say Contact.

If they say Monitor, tune in and wait.

The practical skill is dual-channel management: maintain your primary frequency for ATC communications while silently monitoring the second frequency for information.

In the H130, your audio panel allows you to monitor a secondary frequency with the intercom split. Use that capability.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

What is the difference between Monitor and Contact in ATC radio procedures?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

Monitor means I tune a frequency and listen without transmitting, unless required by a broadcast. Contact means I am expected to initiate two-way communication with a specified unit. If ATC says monitor ATIS, I listen to the recorded broadcast. If ATC says contact Approach, I call Approach and establish a two-way radio exchange. The two instructions have different expectations and require different pilot actions.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Calling the monitored frequency to confirm monitoring.  
  ✔ Monitor means listen only. Do not transmit unless required.

- ❌ Confusing Monitor with Contact and expecting a two-way exchange.  
  ✔ Monitor is passive. Contact is active.

- ❌ Not readback the frequency when acknowledging the monitor instruction.  
  ✔ Include the frequency: Monitoring one two seven decimal seven, ANAC123.

- ❌ Failing to actually tune the frequency after acknowledging.  
  ✔ Tune immediately. Monitoring means active listening, not just acknowledgment.

Curitiba Approach instructs: "ANAC123, monitor ATIS on one two six decimal five, report when you have information."

**Your first task**

**Your second task**

I would acknowledge: Monitoring one two six decimal five, ANAC123 — and tune the ATIS on my secondary radio while maintaining Approach on the primary.
