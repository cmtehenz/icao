# Confirm Deviation

**Catalog ID:** 0309  
**Category:** Radio Communication  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Confirm Deviation — the ATC request for the crew to confirm that they are deviating from the cleared route, track, or procedure, or to state the nature of an off-route deviation. Core Part 2 skill for every H130 crew in Brazilian airspace: announce deviations early, state direction and reason, and return to course when able.

Confirm deviation is High priority because unannounced off-route flight destroys separation assumptions.

For ICAO Delta / Captain Delta training in Brazil, master ICAO phraseology for route and track deviations under DECEA/ICAO procedures, including weather and traffic avoidance.

Understanding how to confirm deviation in ICAO-standard English is essential for Part 2 proficiency.

## 📖 Meaning

Confirm Deviation is an ATC request for the pilot to verify or describe a departure from the cleared route, track, heading, or procedure — typically confirming direction, magnitude, and reason so ATC can maintain separation and issue an amended clearance if required.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Confirmar desvio de rota / Confirm Deviation.

Solicitação do controle para que o piloto verifique ou descreva um afastamento da rota, track, proa ou procedimento liberado — tipicamente confirmando direção, magnitude e motivo para que o ATC mantenha separação e emita uma autorização emendada se necessário.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When ATC asks you to confirm deviation, the crew should:

- state that you are deviating and give direction: left or right of track
- give approximate distance off course if known
- state the reason briefly: weather, traffic, terrain, technical
- request an amended clearance or advise when able to return
- for H130: deviations may be smaller in distance but still require the same radio discipline
- never deviate silently in controlled airspace when communication is available

Deviation exchanges commonly include:

- confirm deviation / deviating left/right of track
- request deviation [direction] due weather
- [number] miles left/right of course
- able to return to course / returning to course
- request amended clearance

**Important:** Deviation is not the same as diversion. Deviation is off the cleared path temporarily; diversion changes the destination. Use the correct word.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers confirm deviation to:

- restore an accurate traffic picture when radar or reports show off-route flight
- issue amended headings or routes that protect separation
- coordinate weather deviations across sector boundaries
- distinguish intentional deviation from navigation error
- clear you back to the original route when the obstruction is past

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

Operational scenario: H130 on an IFR airway toward Belo Horizonte. A build-up sits on the airway centerline.

You request:

ANAC123, request deviation twenty degrees right due weather.

Control approves. Later Control asks:

ANAC123, confirm deviation.

You reply:

ANAC123, affirmative, ten miles right of track due weather, request return when clear.

When the weather is behind you, you advise returning to course and accept the amended clearance. The deviation was intentional, announced, and closed cleanly.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Control: ANAC123, confirm deviation.
2. Radar: ANAC123, confirm left of course.
3. Control: ANAC123, say reason for deviation.
4. Approach: ANAC123, deviation approved, report back on course.
5. Radar: ANAC123, confirm able to return to route.

## 🎙 Professional Pilot Readbacks

1. ANAC123, affirmative, deviating right of track due weather.
2. ANAC123, ten miles left of course, request return.
3. ANAC123, request deviation fifteen degrees right.
4. ANAC123, returning to course now.
5. ANAC123, back on course, resume own navigation.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Deviation radio work has three beats: request or advise, confirm when asked, close the loop when back on course.

If weather forces you off track, speak before you drift far. In controlled Brazilian airspace, silence looks like a navigation error. In the H130 you can turn tightly — that does not remove the duty to tell ATC where you are going.

Part 2 examiners listen for direction, reason, and the return. "Deviating" alone is incomplete.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

You must leave the airway centerline to avoid weather. ATC later asks you to confirm deviation. What do you report?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would confirm the deviation, state left or right of track, give approximate distance off course if known, and state the reason, usually weather. I would request an amended clearance if needed and advise when able to return to course. When clear of the weather, I would report returning to course or back on course as instructed.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Saying "diversion" when you only mean off-track weather avoidance.  
  ✔ Use deviation for temporary off-route flight; diversion for a new destination.

- ❌ Deviating first and calling much later.  
  ✔ Request or advise as early as practical.

- ❌ Omitting left/right when confirming.  
  ✔ Always give direction of deviation.

- ❌ Never reporting back on course.  
  ✔ Close the loop so ATC can restore the original route plan.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

You are right of the airway for weather. Control asks you to confirm deviation, then later asks if you can return.

**Your first task**

What do you say to confirm the deviation?

**Your second task**

Weather is clear. What do you say?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: ANAC123, affirmative, ten miles right of track due weather. Second task: ANAC123, clear of weather, returning to course — or request return to route, then comply with the amended clearance. Direction, reason, and closure are the three required pieces.
