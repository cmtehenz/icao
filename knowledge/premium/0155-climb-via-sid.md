# Climb Via SID

**Catalog ID:** 0155  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2 · Part 4

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Climb Via SID — the ATC clearance that authorizes a departing aircraft to follow both the lateral routing and the published altitude restrictions of a Standard Instrument Departure procedure simultaneously.

Climb Via SID is more powerful than a standard SID clearance.

A standard SID clearance gives you the lateral routing. ATC still controls your altitude separately.

Climb Via SID gives you both: fly the SID route and climb through the SID's published altitude restrictions without requiring individual altitude clearances for each step.

This clearance is increasingly used at busy Brazilian departure airports including São Paulo and Congonhas, where SID altitude profiles are designed to optimize departure flow.

Understanding the precise difference between SID Clearance and Climb Via SID is critical ICAO knowledge — and a common source of deviation when pilots confuse the two.

## 📖 Meaning

Climb Via SID is an ATC clearance authorizing the departing aircraft to follow the lateral routing of the Standard Instrument Departure and to comply with all published altitude restrictions of that procedure, climbing through the SID altitude profile without requiring individual altitude instructions from ATC for each step.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Suba via SID.

Autorização do controle de tráfego aéreo para a aeronave em partida seguir o roteamento lateral da saída por instrumentos padrão e cumprir todas as restrições de altitude publicadas desse procedimento, subindo pelo perfil de altitude do SID sem necessitar de instruções individuais de altitude do controle para cada etapa.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When the crew receives Climb Via SID, they should:

- confirm the SID name is loaded and active in the FMS
- comply with every published altitude restriction on the SID from the beginning of the departure
- comply with all published speed constraints on the SID
- do not level off at intermediate altitudes unless a published restriction or an ATC amendment requires it
- climb continuously through the SID altitude profile to the final SID altitude or the top altitude assigned in the clearance
- advise ATC of any inability to comply with a restriction before or during the departure

Climb Via SID operations commonly include:

- an assigned top altitude that caps the SID climb: "climb via SID, except maintain flight level one zero zero"
- complex SIDs with step-climb altitude restrictions at multiple fixes
- speed restrictions published at specific waypoints for noise abatement or separation
- transition from SID guidance to en-route climb after the SID ends

**Important:** The key distinction — Climb Via SID authorizes the crew to climb through published altitude restrictions. A standard SID clearance does not. If ATC says "fly the SID" without "climb via," you climb to your assigned altitude only. If ATC says "climb via SID," the SID altitude profile is your authorized climb profile.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses It

Controllers issue Climb Via SID to:

- reduce radio communication during busy departure flows by using published profiles
- allow the crew to manage their own altitude within the coded SID profile
- optimize departure flow through complex terminal airspace
- coordinate departure traffic with en-route sectors using pre-defined profiles
- streamline clearances when the SID altitude profile is consistent with the separation plan

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are departing Congonhas in your H130 on an IFR flight to Florianópolis.

Clearance Delivery transmits:

ANAC123, cleared to Florianópolis, BRASA THREE departure, runway one seven, climb via SID except maintain flight level one zero zero, squawk zero two three five.

You load BRASA THREE in the GPS.

You brief with your co-pilot: the SID routing, and every published altitude restriction — three thousand at ARCOS, six thousand at BRASA, nine thousand at NEXUS. Top altitude: flight level one zero zero.

You respond:

Cleared to Florianópolis, BRASA THREE departure, runway one seven, climb via SID except maintain flight level one zero zero, squawk zero two three five, ANAC123.

After takeoff, you climb through the SID altitude profile — three thousand at ARCOS, six thousand at BRASA, nine thousand at NEXUS, level at flight level one zero zero.

No intermediate altitude calls to ATC. The SID profile was your clearance.

Clean. Efficient. Professional.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Climb Via SID is the complete departure package.

Route: the SID.

Altitude: the SID restrictions, plus the assigned top altitude.

Speed: the SID constraints.

All three. One clearance.

When you hear "climb via SID" — every altitude restriction on that SID plate is now an active clearance.

You do not need to call ATC for each step. You climb through them.

The trap that catches pilots: hearing "climb via" and still waiting for individual altitude clearances.

Or worse: hearing a standard SID clearance and climbing through published restrictions as if Climb Via was issued.

In Brazilian airspace, controllers increasingly use Climb Via SID at the larger airports. Know the phrase. Know the responsibility it carries.

In the H130, brief every altitude restriction on the SID before takeoff.

They are not optional reference points. With Climb Via clearance active, they are your authorized altitude targets.

If you cannot comply with any restriction — advise ATC before departure.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

Explain what Climb Via SID authorizes and how it differs from a standard SID clearance with an assigned altitude.

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

A standard SID clearance authorizes the lateral routing of the Standard Instrument Departure. The crew climbs to the specific altitude assigned by ATC and does not follow the SID published altitude restrictions unless separately cleared. Climb Via SID is a combined clearance authorizing both the lateral routing and the published altitude profile of the SID. The crew climbs through the SID's altitude restrictions at each fix without requiring individual altitude clearances. Often a top altitude is assigned: for example, climb via SID except maintain flight level one zero zero means the crew follows the SID altitude profile up to flight level one zero zero and levels off there. The crew is responsible for complying with every published restriction between the runway and that top altitude.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Waiting for individual altitude clearances after receiving Climb Via SID.  
  ✔ Climb Via SID authorizes the entire published altitude profile. Climb through it.

- ❌ Climbing through SID restrictions after receiving a standard SID clearance.  
  ✔ Standard SID clearance: climb to ATC-assigned altitude only. Climb Via is required for the profile.

- ❌ Not reading back the top altitude exception in the Climb Via clearance.  
  ✔ Always read back the exception: climb via SID except maintain flight level one zero zero.

- ❌ Not briefing all SID altitude restrictions before departure.  
  ✔ With Climb Via active, every published restriction is a target. Brief them all before takeoff.

## 🗣 Pronunciation Coaching

**Target Phrase:** Climb Via SID Except Maintain Flight Level One Zero Zero

**Pronunciation:** klym VY-uh SID eks-EPT men-TAYN flyt LEV-ul WUN ZE-ro ZE-ro

**Word Stress**

- Climb → klym
- Via → VY-uh (two syllables)
- SID → SID (one word, not spelled out)
- Except → eks-EPT
- Maintain → men-TAYN
- Flight Level → flyt LEV-ul
- One Zero Zero → WUN ZE-ro ZE-ro

Practice:

Climb via SID... except maintain... flight level one zero zero.

Together:

Climb via SID except maintain flight level one zero zero, BRASA THREE departure, runway one seven, ANAC123.

SID is spoken as one word — not S-I-D — in operational phraseology.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

Congonhas Clearance transmits: "ANAC123, cleared to Florianópolis, BRASA THREE departure, runway one seven, climb via SID except maintain flight level one zero zero, squawk zero two three five."

The BRASA THREE SID has altitude restrictions: three thousand at ARCOS, six thousand at BRASA, nine thousand at NEXUS.

**Your first task**

Read back the complete clearance.

**Your second task**

After ARCOS, your engine performance limits your climb to five thousand feet. You cannot reach six thousand at BRASA. What do you do?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would respond: Cleared to Florianópolis, BRASA THREE departure, runway one seven, climb via SID except maintain flight level one zero zero, squawk zero two three five, ANAC123. If I could not meet the six thousand restriction at BRASA due to performance, I would advise ATC before or immediately at the point of non-compliance: ANAC123, unable to meet BRASA restriction, maximum performance five thousand, request amended clearance. I must not continue climbing via SID while knowingly unable to comply with a published restriction.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Climb Via SID gives you both the road and the altitude profile in one clearance.

That is more authority — and more responsibility.

Brief every restriction before departure. Know each target altitude at each fix.

But if you cannot meet a restriction — say so. Immediately.

That honest communication is what keeps separation intact.

One clearance. Full responsibility. Professional execution.
