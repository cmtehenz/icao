# Sterile Cockpit

**Catalog ID:** 0464  
**Category:** CRM  
**Priority:** High  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 1 · Part 2

## ✈ Mission Brief

Today's lesson covers Sterile Cockpit — the rule that during critical phases of flight, crew conversation is limited to duties essential for safe operation of the aircraft.

Sterile cockpit protects attention when the aircraft is closest to the ground and the workload is highest: taxi, takeoff, approach, and landing. Non-essential talk — company gossip, passenger stories, meal plans — belongs elsewhere.

For H130 operations, sterile cockpit applies to low-level flight, confined-area work, and IFR approaches. Part 1 vocabulary and Part 2 radio discipline both depend on protecting attention for ATC and flying tasks during critical phases.

## 📖 Meaning

Sterile Cockpit is an operational rule requiring that, during critical phases of flight — typically taxi, takeoff, climb below a defined altitude, approach, and landing — flight crew members refrain from non-essential activities and conversation, limiting communication to that required for the safe operation of the aircraft.

## 🇧🇷 Portuguese Meaning

Cabine estéril / Sterile Cockpit.

Regra operacional que exige que, durante fases críticas do voo — tipicamente táxi, decolagem, subida abaixo de uma altitude definida, aproximação e pouso — os membros da tripulação evitem atividades e conversas não essenciais, limitando a comunicação ao necessário para a operação segura da aeronave.

## 🛩 Operational Meaning

When sterile cockpit is in effect, the crew should:

- limit talk to checklists, callouts, traffic, weather, and ATC
- defer cabin and company non-essential calls until after the critical phase
- keep PF focused on flying; PM handles radios and configuration
- challenge sterile-cockpit violations professionally and immediately
- resume normal conversation only when above the sterile altitude or after landing/taxi clear

Sterile cockpit typically includes:

- engine start to climb above sterile altitude
- descent below sterile altitude through landing and taxi
- any low-level or high-workload special operation defined by SOP

**Important:** Sterile does not mean silent to ATC. Essential radio calls continue. Non-essential chatter stops.

## 🎯 Why ATC Uses This Concept

Controllers rely on sterile-cockpit discipline when:

- issuing time-critical clearances on short final or during takeoff roll
- expecting immediate readbacks during go-around
- coordinating runway crossings and hold-short instructions
- managing frequency congestion in terminal areas
- needing crews to hear traffic advisories without distraction

## 🌍 Real Operational Context

You are on short final to Congonhas in the H130. A cabin call comes about passenger seating.

PM responds briefly that sterile cockpit is in effect and will call back after landing.

Tower: ANAC123, wind three two zero at one five, runway three five left cleared to land.

You reply:

ANAC123, cleared to land runway three five left.

Attention stayed on the landing. The non-essential call waited.

## 📡 Real ATC Phraseology

1. Tower: ANAC123, line up and wait runway one seven right.
2. Tower: ANAC123, cleared for takeoff runway one seven right, wind one eight zero at eight.
3. Tower: ANAC123, go around, aircraft on runway, climb two thousand.
4. Ground: ANAC123, hold short runway two eight, traffic landing.
5. Tower: ANAC123, cleared to land runway three five left, caution wake turbulence.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Teaching

Sterile cockpit is attention hygiene.

Below the sterile altitude, every word should earn its place. If it does not help fly, navigate, or communicate for safety, it waits.

In the H130, "critical phase" often includes more than airline climb-to-ten-thousand logic — confined areas, pinnacles, offshore decks, and low VFR corridors demand sterile discipline even when not IFR.

Protect the PM's ears for ATC. A missed hold-short instruction is how runway incursions begin.

If someone breaks sterile cockpit, say: "Sterile." Then continue the task. Correct the habit on the ground, not with a speech on short final.

## ❓ Common ICAO Speaking Question

On short final, company radio asks for a long commercial message. What do you do?

**Good ICAO Level 4 Answer**

I would not engage the long message. The PM might say we are sterile and will call after landing, or simply ignore until clear of the runway. ATC clearances and flying remain priority. After landing and taxi clear, we return the company call.

## 🇧🇷 Common Brazilian Mistakes

- ❌ Chatting about non-operational topics on final.  
  ✔ Keep only checklist, callouts, and ATC.

- ❌ Answering long company calls during taxi for takeoff.  
  ✔ Defer until after climb above sterile altitude.

- ❌ Thinking sterile cockpit means no ATC readbacks.  
  ✔ Essential radio continues; non-essential talk stops.

- ❌ Punishing a sterile reminder as disrespect.  
  ✔ Treat "sterile" as a safety call, not a personal attack.

## 🎯 Captain Challenge

While lining up for takeoff in the H130, a passenger interphone call begins a long non-operational conversation. Tower issues takeoff clearance.

**Your first task**

What does the crew do about the interphone?

**Your second task**

What do you say to ATC?

**Expected ICAO Level 4 Answer**

First task: End or defer the interphone — sterile cockpit — PF focuses on takeoff. Second task: ANAC123, cleared for takeoff runway one seven right — or, if distracted and not ready: ANAC123, unable, request delay. Never accept takeoff clearance while attention is captured by non-essential talk.

## 👨‍✈️ Captain Delta Final Debrief

Sterile cockpit keeps the crew's bandwidth for the runway.

Essential words only. ATC included. Gossip excluded.

In the H130, low-level Brazilian operations make sterile discipline a daily habit, not an airline formality.

Protect the critical phase. Read back the clearance. Land the aircraft. Talk later.
