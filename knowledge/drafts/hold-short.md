# Hold Short

**Catalog ID:** 0003  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2  
**Draft status:** Working document — not consumed by Captain yet  
**Confidence:** high

## Simple English meaning

Stop before the runway and wait.

## Portuguese meaning

Parar antes da pista e aguardar autorização.

## Operational meaning

Hold short prevents runway incursions — you must stop at the holding point.

## Who normally uses it

Ground and Tower controllers; pilots read back and stop at the line.

## When it is used

During taxi when crossing a runway is not yet authorized.

## Why it is used

Runway incursions are a top safety risk — hold short is a critical barrier.

## 5 realistic aviation examples

1. Taxi via Romeo, hold short of runway one eight.
2. Cross runway three six, hold short of runway one eight.
3. Taxi via Romeo, hold short runway one eight, ANAC 123.
4. Taxi via Romeo and hold short of runway one eight.
5. N789EF holding short of runway 27L.

## 3 realistic pilot responses

1. Hold short runway one eight, ANAC 123.
2. Holding short runway three six, ANAC 123.
3. RESEARCH REQUIRED

## Common pronunciation mistakes

- HOLD SHORT — equal stress, crisp stop between words.
- Always include the runway designation in the readback.

## Common Brazilian mistakes

- Rolling past the hold line without a crossing clearance.
- Reading back hold short but not stopping.
- Confusing hold short with line up and wait.

## Compare with similar concepts

- **line up and wait:** Line up and wait is on the runway; hold short is before entering.
- **holding short:** Holding short is your report; hold short is the instruction.

## One ICAO speaking question

Why must you read back 'hold short' on every taxi clearance?

## Captain teaching notes

Teach calm readback first. Ground at Guarulhos issued a complex taxi with two hold shorts — the FO read each runway back; Tower thanked us for the crisp calls.

## Memory tip

Many runway incursions start with an incomplete hold short readback.
