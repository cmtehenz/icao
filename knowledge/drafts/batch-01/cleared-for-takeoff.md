# Cleared for Takeoff

**Catalog ID:** 0004  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Source level:** researched  
**Draft:** Premium batch-01 — working document (not consumed by Captain)

## Meaning

ATC authorizes the aircraft to begin the takeoff roll.

*Portuguese: Autorizado para decolagem.*

## Operational Meaning

This is the explicit clearance required before an aircraft may take off from a controlled runway. It follows line up and wait when applicable — the pilot must not enter the takeoff roll until this clearance is clearly received and read back.

## When Used

After line up and wait or when holding at the runway threshold, when Tower determines the runway is clear and separation is assured for departure.

## Who Uses It

Tower controllers issue cleared for takeoff, often with runway, wind, and departure instructions; pilots read back runway and clearance before adding takeoff power.

## Real ATC Phraseology

- PT-ABC, runway one eight, cleared for takeoff.
- PT-ABC, wind zero niner zero at eight, runway two seven, cleared for takeoff.
- PT-ABC, after departure maintain runway heading, cleared for takeoff.
- PT-ABC, cleared for immediate takeoff runway one eight.
- PT-ABC, traffic on final, no delay, cleared for takeoff.

## Real Pilot Readbacks

- Runway one eight, cleared for takeoff, PT-ABC.
- Cleared for takeoff runway two seven, PT-ABC.
- Maintain runway heading, cleared for takeoff, PT-ABC.
- Cleared for immediate takeoff runway one eight, PT-ABC.
- No delay, cleared for takeoff, PT-ABC.

## Common ICAO Speaking Questions

- Why is it dangerous to confuse line up and wait with cleared for takeoff?
- What must you read back after receiving takeoff clearance?
- When would Tower issue cleared for immediate takeoff?

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- Confusing line up and wait with takeoff clearance.
- Saying authorized takeoff instead of cleared for takeoff.
- Missing runway number in readback.

## Pronunciation Coaching

- CLEARED for TAKE-off — stress cleared and takeoff clearly.
- Do not pronounce takeoff as two slow separate words in radio speech.
- Read wind and runway numbers digit by digit when included.

## Captain Teaching Notes

Teach this as a trigger phrase. The student must not move from line-up into takeoff roll until this clearance is clearly received. Takeoff clearance follows line up and wait — they are not the same instruction.

## Memory Trick

No cleared for takeoff, no takeoff.
