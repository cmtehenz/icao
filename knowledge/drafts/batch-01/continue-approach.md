# Continue Approach

**Catalog ID:** 0006  
**Category:** Approach  
**ICAO parts:** 2, 3  
**Source level:** researched  
**Draft:** Premium batch-01 — working document (not consumed by Captain)

## Meaning

Continue flying the published or instructed approach. Landing clearance has not yet been issued.

*Portuguese: Continue a aproximação.*

## Operational Meaning

Tower issues this instruction when the runway is not yet available but expects to clear the aircraft to land later. The aircraft continues the approach while awaiting further instructions. Continue approach does not authorize landing.

## When Used

When traffic, runway occupancy, or sequencing requires the aircraft to maintain a stable approach without landing clearance yet — often with expect late landing clearance or number two in sequence.

## Who Uses It

Approach and Tower controllers issue continue approach; pilots read back and maintain the approach while prepared for cleared to land or go around.

## Real ATC Phraseology

- PT-ABC, continue approach.
- PT-ABC, continue approach, traffic will depart prior to your arrival.
- PT-ABC, continue approach, expect late landing clearance.
- PT-ABC, continue approach, number two behind the Boeing 737.
- PT-ABC, continue approach, runway occupied.

## Real Pilot Readbacks

- Continuing approach, PT-ABC.
- Continue approach, number two, PT-ABC.
- Continuing approach behind traffic, PT-ABC.
- Approach continued, PT-ABC.
- Continuing, PT-ABC.

## Common ICAO Speaking Questions

- What is the difference between continue approach and cleared to land?
- What should a pilot do after receiving continue approach?
- When must a pilot go around instead of continuing the approach?

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- Confusing continue approach with cleared to land.
- Landing without receiving landing clearance.
- Reading back only roger.

## Pronunciation Coaching

- con-TIN-ue ap-PROACH — three clear syllables on continue.
- Runway numbers as individual digits when included.
- Do not clip approach at the end of the readback.

## Captain Teaching Notes

This is probably one of the most misunderstood ATC instructions. Continue approach does not authorize landing. The pilot must continue the approach while waiting for either cleared to land or go around. Students material uses Tower, N890KL continuing approach runway 18 as the pilot report form.

## Memory Trick

Continue flying. Do not continue landing.
