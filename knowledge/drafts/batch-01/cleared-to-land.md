# Cleared to Land

**Catalog ID:** 0005  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Source level:** researched  
**Draft:** Premium batch-01 — working document (not consumed by Captain)

## Meaning

ATC authorizes the aircraft to land on the specified runway or landing area.

*Portuguese: Autorizado para pouso.*

## Operational Meaning

Issued by Tower when the runway or landing area is available and landing is authorized. Continue approach is not the same as cleared to land — the pilot should wait for an explicit landing clearance before landing at a controlled airport.

## When Used

On final approach or downwind when Tower has confirmed runway availability, separation from traffic, and landing conditions including wind and any hold-short instructions after landing.

## Who Uses It

Tower controllers issue cleared to land, often with runway, wind, and sequence information; pilots read back runway and clearance before continuing to land.

## Real ATC Phraseology

- PT-ABC, runway one eight, cleared to land.
- PT-ABC, wind one two zero at six, runway two seven, cleared to land.
- PT-ABC, number one, runway one eight, cleared to land.
- PT-ABC, continue approach, expect late landing clearance.
- PT-ABC, runway one eight, cleared to land, hold short runway one four.

## Real Pilot Readbacks

- Runway one eight, cleared to land, PT-ABC.
- Cleared to land runway two seven, PT-ABC.
- Number one, cleared to land runway one eight, PT-ABC.
- Continuing approach, PT-ABC.
- Cleared to land runway one eight, hold short runway one four, PT-ABC.

## Common ICAO Speaking Questions

- What should a pilot do if he is on final but has not received landing clearance?
- What is the difference between continue approach and cleared to land?
- When would Tower issue cleared to land with hold short instructions?

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- Thinking continue approach means cleared to land.
- Reading back only roger.
- Forgetting runway number.

## Pronunciation Coaching

- CLEARED to LAND — stress cleared and make land strong and clear.
- Read wind and runway numbers digit by digit when included.
- Do not rush the readback on combined clearances with hold short.

## Captain Teaching Notes

Teach that continue approach is not the same as cleared to land. The student should wait for an explicit landing clearance before landing at a controlled airport. If on final without clearance, continue the approach and be prepared to go around.

## Memory Trick

Continue approach = keep coming. Cleared to land = landing authorized.
