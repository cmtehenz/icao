# Go Around

**Catalog ID:** 0007  
**Category:** Approach  
**ICAO parts:** 2, 3  
**Source level:** researched  
**Draft:** Premium batch-01 — working document (not consumed by Captain)

## Meaning

Discontinue the landing and climb away for another approach.

*Portuguese: Arremeter.*

## Operational Meaning

Used whenever landing is no longer safe. It may be instructed by ATC or initiated by the pilot. A go around is never considered a failure — professional pilots initiate one whenever the approach becomes unstable or safety margins are reduced.

## When Used

When traffic is on the runway, the approach is unstable, runway is occupied, windshear is reported, landing clearance was not received, or any condition makes landing unsafe.

## Who Uses It

Tower may instruct go around; pilots announce going around and execute the published or assigned missed approach procedure.

## Real ATC Phraseology

- PT-ABC, go around.
- PT-ABC, go around, traffic on the runway.
- PT-ABC, go around, unstable approach.
- PT-ABC, go around, runway occupied.
- PT-ABC, go around, windshear reported.

## Real Pilot Readbacks

- Going around, PT-ABC.
- Go around acknowledged, PT-ABC.
- Going around due traffic, PT-ABC.
- Going around, climbing runway heading.
- Executing go around, PT-ABC.

## Common ICAO Speaking Questions

- When would you decide to perform a go around?
- What is the difference between go around and missed approach?
- When may a pilot initiate a go around without ATC instruction?

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- Waiting too long to go around.
- Trying to save an unstable approach.
- Saying abort landing instead of going around.

## Pronunciation Coaching

- GO a-ROUND — stress go clearly, round as one syllable.
- Students material uses Tower, N456UV going around from runway 09.
- Announce going around promptly before maneuvering.

## Captain Teaching Notes
