# Missed Approach

**Catalog ID:** 0008  
**Category:** Instrument Procedures  
**ICAO parts:** 2, 3  
**Source level:** researched  
**Draft:** Premium batch-01 — working document (not consumed by Captain)

## Meaning

A published IFR procedure followed when the required visual references are not obtained or landing cannot be completed.

*Portuguese: Procedimento de aproximação perdida.*

## Operational Meaning

Every instrument approach includes a missed approach procedure. It protects terrain clearance and separates aircraft. A missed approach is a published IFR procedure; a go around is simply discontinuing the landing — sometimes both happen together.

## When Used

When the pilot cannot continue to land at the missed approach point, required visual references are not in sight, ATC instructs a go around, or landing cannot be completed safely.

## Who Uses It

Pilots execute the published or ATC-assigned missed approach; Tower or Approach may issue additional instructions such as runway heading, climb, hold, or contact Departure.

## Real ATC Phraseology

- PT-ABC, execute published missed approach.
- PT-ABC, fly runway heading, climb three thousand.
- PT-ABC, contact Departure.
- PT-ABC, report established in the hold.
- PT-ABC, execute missed approach.

## Real Pilot Readbacks

- Executing published missed approach.
- Runway heading, climb three thousand.
- Missed approach, PT-ABC.
- Contacting Departure.
- Executing missed approach procedure.

## Common ICAO Speaking Questions

- What is the difference between a go around and a missed approach?
- When must you fly the published missed approach procedure?
- What would you report to ATC after initiating a missed approach?

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- Using missed approach and go around as synonyms.
- Forgetting that the missed procedure is already published on the approach chart.
- Not briefing the missed approach before every IFR approach.

## Pronunciation Coaching

- MISSED ap-PROACH — stress missed clearly.
- Students material uses N789WX following published missed approach procedure.
- Read climb altitudes digit by digit.

## Captain Teaching Notes

A missed approach is a published IFR procedure. A go around is simply discontinuing the landing. Sometimes both happen together — announce going around, then fly the published missed approach unless ATC assigns otherwise.

## Memory Trick

Every IFR approach already has an escape plan.
