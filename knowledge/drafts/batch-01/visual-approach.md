# Visual Approach

**Catalog ID:** 0009  
**Category:** Approach  
**ICAO parts:** 2, 3  
**Source level:** researched  
**Draft:** Premium batch-01 — working document (not consumed by Captain)

## Meaning

An ATC-authorized approach conducted with visual reference to the airport or preceding aircraft.

*Portuguese: Aproximação visual.*

## Operational Meaning

Used when weather conditions permit visual navigation instead of flying the complete instrument procedure. A visual approach still requires ATC clearance — seeing the runway or airport alone does not authorize landing.

## When Used

When the pilot has the airport or preceding traffic in sight, weather is at or above visual approach minimums, and ATC can provide separation by visual means.

## Who Uses It

Approach and Tower issue cleared visual approach; pilots report airport or traffic in sight and maintain visual separation when assigned.

## Real ATC Phraseology

- PT-ABC, cleared visual approach runway one eight.
- PT-ABC, report airport in sight.
- PT-ABC, maintain visual separation.
- PT-ABC, follow the traffic ahead.
- PT-ABC, cleared visual approach.

## Real Pilot Readbacks

- Cleared visual approach runway one eight.
- Airport in sight.
- Maintaining visual separation.
- Following traffic.
- Visual approach approved.

## Common ICAO Speaking Questions

- Under what conditions may ATC clear an aircraft for a visual approach?
- What is the difference between a visual approach and cleared to land?
- What are the pilot responsibilities when maintaining visual separation?

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- Confusing visual approach with visual flight rules.
- Forgetting visual separation responsibilities when assigned.
- Landing without cleared to land after receiving visual approach clearance.

## Pronunciation Coaching

- VIS-ual ap-PROACH — stress visual on the first syllable.
- Students material uses Tower, N234KL on the visual for runway 17.
- Report airport in sight clearly before accepting visual clearance if asked.

## Captain Teaching Notes

A visual approach still requires ATC clearance. Seeing the runway alone does not authorize landing. Cleared visual approach is followed by cleared to land when Tower is ready. Part 2 vocabulary uses Cleared visual approach runway two four.

## Memory Trick

Visual navigation. ATC clearance still required.
