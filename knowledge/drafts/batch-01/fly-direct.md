# Fly Direct

**Catalog ID:** 0001  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Source level:** researched  
**Draft:** Premium batch-01 — working document (not consumed by Captain)

## Meaning

Fly straight to a specified waypoint, fix, or navigation aid.

*Portuguese: Voar direto para um ponto.*

## Operational Meaning

ATC uses this to shorten the route, simplify navigation, or reposition traffic. FAA defines “direct” as straight-line flight between navigation aids, fixes, or points. The pilot must read back the point and navigate directly to it while complying with the rest of the clearance.

## When Used

When traffic and airspace allow a shortcut, for re-routing around weather, to improve flow, or to reposition aircraft to a fix, VOR, waypoint, or aerodrome.

## Who Uses It

Center and Approach controllers issue fly direct or proceed direct clearances; pilots read back the fix and navigate direct while maintaining altitude, speed, and separation requirements.

## Real ATC Phraseology

- Helicopter PT-ABC, fly direct NITUX.
- PT-ABC, cleared direct AFA VOR.
- PT-ABC, fly direct present position to waypoint CHARLIE.
- PT-ABC, proceed direct NITUX, resume own navigation.
- PT-ABC, fly direct the airport.

## Real Pilot Readbacks

- Flying direct NITUX, PT-ABC.
- Cleared direct AFA VOR, PT-ABC.
- Direct CHARLIE, PT-ABC.
- Proceeding direct NITUX, PT-ABC.
- Flying direct the airport, PT-ABC.

## Common ICAO Speaking Questions

- When would ATC ask a pilot to fly direct?
- What navigation action do you take after proceed direct?
- How is fly direct different from resume own navigation?

## Common Brazilian Mistakes

- Saying go direct when phraseology should be fly direct or proceed direct.
- Forgetting to read back the waypoint.
- Confusing fly direct with resume own navigation.

## Pronunciation Coaching

- Fly di-RECT — stress the second syllable.
- Keep direct strong on the second syllable.
- Spell unusual fix names clearly if asked.

## Captain Teaching Notes

Teach that fly direct is not just go there. It is an ATC navigation instruction. The pilot must read back the point and then navigate directly to it while maintaining the rest of the clearance.

## Memory Trick

Fly direct = shortcut to a point.
