# Fly Direct

**Catalog ID:** 0011  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2  
**Draft status:** Working document — not consumed by Captain yet  
**Confidence:** high

## Simple English meaning

Fly straight to a point.

## Portuguese meaning

Voar diretamente para um ponto ou fixo.

## Operational meaning

ATC shortens your route when traffic and airspace allow a direct track.

## Who normally uses it

Center and Approach controllers; pilots acknowledge and navigate direct.

## When it is used

When traffic permits a shortcut, for re-routing around weather, or to improve flow.

## Why it is used

Saves time and fuel while maintaining radar separation from other traffic.

## 5 realistic aviation examples

1. Helicopter PT-ABC, fly direct NITUX.
2. Proceed direct Galeao, descend flight level one four zero.
3. Direct NITUX, PT-ABC.
4. Fly direct Galeao.
5. N567ST flying direct to KXYZ.

## 3 realistic pilot responses

1. Fly direct NITUX, PT-ABC.
2. Direct Galeao, ANAC 123.
3. RESEARCH REQUIRED

## Common pronunciation mistakes

- Fly di-RECT — stress the second syllable.
- Spell unusual fix names clearly if asked.

## Common Brazilian mistakes

- Starting the turn before confirming the correct fix name.
- Saying 'OK direct' instead of reading back the fix.
- Confusing 'direct to' with 'resume own navigation'.

## Related concepts

- vectors
- resume own navigation
- proceed direct

## Compare with similar concepts

- **vectors:** Vectors are heading assignments; direct is navigation to a fix.
- **resume own navigation:** Resume own nav returns routing responsibility to the pilot.

## One ICAO speaking question

When would ATC ask a pilot to fly direct?

## Captain teaching notes

Teach calm readback first. Cruise over the interior, Center offered direct Brasília when the sector was quiet — saved twelve minutes without a single vector.

## Memory tip

Direct clearances are common in cruise when IFR traffic is radar-identified.
