# Line Up and Wait

**Catalog ID:** 0004  
**Category:** ATC Phraseology  
**Priority:** Critical  
**ICAO Parts:** Part 2  
**Draft status:** Working document — not consumed by Captain yet  
**Confidence:** high

## Simple English meaning

Enter the runway, line up, and wait for takeoff clearance.

## Portuguese meaning

Entrar na pista, alinhar e aguardar autorização de decolagem.

## Operational meaning

You are on the runway but not cleared for takeoff — position for imminent departure.

## Who normally uses it

Tower controllers; pilots read back and comply exactly.

## When it is used

When departure is imminent but landing traffic or wake turbulence requires a short wait.

## Why it is used

Reduces taxi time while keeping the runway protected until takeoff is safe.

## 5 realistic aviation examples

1. ANAC 123, runway three six, line up and wait.
2. Behind the landing Airbus, line up and wait runway one eight.
3. Line up and wait runway three six, ANAC 123.
4. Cleared to line up and wait runway three six.
5. Tower, N234GH lined up and waiting, runway 36R.

## 3 realistic pilot responses

1. Line up and wait runway three six, ANAC 123.
2. Holding in position runway three six, line up and wait, ANAC 123.
3. Holding in position runway three six, line up and wait, ANAC 123.

## Common pronunciation mistakes

- LINE UP AND WAIT — four distinct words, steady pace.
- Do not clip 'and' — it matters legally.

## Common Brazilian mistakes

- Taking off without hearing 'cleared for takeoff'.
- Confusing line up and wait with position and hold.
- Incomplete readback missing the runway number.

## Related concepts

- hold short
- cleared for takeoff
- cancel takeoff

## Compare with similar concepts

- **cleared for takeoff:** Takeoff clearance follows line up and wait — they are not the same.
- **hold short:** Hold short keeps you off the runway; line up and wait puts you on it.

## One ICAO speaking question

Why is a correct readback critical after 'line up and wait'?

## Captain teaching notes

Teach calm readback first. Morning rush at Congonhas, Tower sequenced three departures with line up and wait — each crew waited for takeoff clearance before adding power.

## Memory tip

ICAO phraseology replaced 'position and hold' with 'line up and wait'.
