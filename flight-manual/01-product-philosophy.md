# ICAO Delta Flight Manual

## Section 1 — Product Philosophy

Version 1.0

---

## 1. Mission

ICAO Delta exists to help pilots pass the ICAO/SDEA exam by training the exact skills required in the test:

- speaking clearly;
- understanding aviation communication;
- answering naturally;
- interacting with ATC;
- improving pronunciation;
- building confidence under pressure.

ICAO Delta is not a generic English app.

ICAO Delta is an **AI Flight Instructor** — a Digital Flight Academy whose first training program is ICAO/SDEA exam preparation.

> **Educational architecture:** [educational-systems/](./educational-systems/) defines *how Captain teaches*. [Mission Engine](./03-mission-engine.md) defines *what the student flies*. [Training Programs](./21-training-programs.md) define *what each course teaches*. See [RFC-001](./architecture/rfc/RFC-001-educational-intelligence-architecture.md) · [RFC-002](./architecture/rfc/RFC-002-training-programs-architecture.md).

---

## 2. Core Objective

The main objective is simple:

> Help the student reach ICAO Level 4 or higher.

Every feature must answer one question:

> Does this increase the student’s chance of passing the ICAO/SDEA exam?

If the answer is no, the feature goes to the backlog.

---

## 3. Learning Philosophy

The student should not spend most of the time reading.

The student should spend most of the time:

- speaking;
- listening;
- remembering;
- repeating;
- being corrected;
- trying again.

The app must avoid passive study.

Reading is support.  
Speaking is the goal.

---

## 4. Training Method

ICAO Delta uses a progressive method:

1. Listen
2. Understand
3. Practice with support
4. Practice with less support
5. Speak without help
6. Receive debrief
7. Try again

The student should gradually move from:

Full answer  
↓  
Sentence blocks  
↓  
Keywords  
↓  
Symbols  
↓  
No help

This prevents memorization and builds real speaking ability.

---

## 5. Daily Mission Philosophy

The student should not waste energy deciding what to study.

Captain Delta creates a daily mission.

Each mission is like a training flight:

1. Briefing
2. Pronunciation
3. Vocabulary
4. Part 1
5. Part 2
6. Mission Recall
7. Mock Exam
8. Debrief
9. Optional Audio Mission

The student simply follows the mission.

---

## 6. Captain Delta

Captain Delta is the identity of ICAO Delta.

Captain Delta is not a chatbot.

Captain Delta is a personal ICAO flight instructor.

He must behave like:

- a senior helicopter captain;
- a calm flight instructor;
- a supportive examiner;
- a professional aviation coach.

Captain Delta should guide, correct and motivate the student.

He must never sound like a generic AI assistant.

---

## 7. Product Rules

### Rule 1 — One goal first

The current priority is helping the student pass the ICAO exam.

Future features must not distract from this goal.

---

### Rule 2 — Speak before seeing the answer

The student should try to answer before reading the model answer.

Active recall is more important than passive review.

---

### Rule 3 — One correction at a time

Captain Delta should not overwhelm the student.

After each answer, he should give:

- one positive point;
- one main improvement;
- one next action.

---

### Rule 4 — Train like the exam

The app should simulate the real SDEA experience.

The closer the training feels to the exam, the better.

---

### Rule 5 — Every session ends with a debrief

The student should always know:

- what improved;
- what was weak;
- what to study tomorrow.

---

## 8. Product Identity

ICAO Delta should feel like:

> A personal ICAO instructor available every day.

Not:

> A PDF with questions.

Not:

> A generic English course.

Not:

> A chatbot.

The student should feel guided from the first screen to the final debrief.

---

## 9. Success Definition

ICAO Delta is successful when the student can:

- answer Part 1 naturally;
- understand and interact in Part 2;
- describe situations clearly;
- pronounce aviation terms clearly;
- maintain fluency under pressure;
- finish a full mock exam with confidence.

The final success is not completing lessons.

The final success is passing the ICAO/SDEA exam.
