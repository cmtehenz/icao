# Philosophy

Every study session is a flight.

The student should never feel like they are opening different modules.

Instead,

they are progressing through one complete mission.

The Mission Timeline provides orientation.

Captain Delta guides the student through every leg of today's flight.

---

# Purpose

The Mission Timeline visually represents today's complete ICAO mission.

It always answers three questions:

Where am I?

What have I already completed?

What comes next?

The student should never need to decide what to study next.

Captain already knows.

# Timeline States

Every mission block has one state.

Not Started

The mission has not begun.

---

Current

The student is currently working on this mission.

Captain always highlights the current block.

---

Completed

Mission finished successfully.

The timeline becomes green.

---

Review Recommended

Mission completed,

but Captain suggests another pass.

The block becomes amber.

---

Skipped

Mission intentionally skipped.

Captain may recommend returning later.

---

Locked

Future missions.

Cannot begin until prerequisites are completed.

---

# Mission Navigation

Students may revisit completed missions.

Captain recommends,

but never forces,

the ideal sequence.

The timeline is flexible.

The learning path is guided.

---

# Timeline Persistence

Mission progress is automatically saved.

If the student leaves,

Captain remembers.

When returning,

the student resumes exactly where they stopped.

The timeline never resets accidentally.

# Timeline Philosophy

The Mission Timeline is not a checklist.

It is a flight plan.

The objective is creating flow.

Not collecting completed tasks.

Mission Timeline succeeds when:
