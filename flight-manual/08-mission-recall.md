# Philosophy

Mission Recall is not another lesson.

It is a confidence check.

Before entering the Mock Exam,

Captain Delta verifies that today's knowledge is still active.

The objective is not learning.

The objective is remembering.

The student should feel:

"I'm ready."

---

# Mission Objective

Mission Recall activates everything learned during today's mission.

Nothing new is introduced.

Captain simply asks:

"Can you still remember?"

If the answer is yes,

the student is ready for the Mock Exam.

---

# Recall Philosophy

Memory becomes stronger when information is recalled.

Not reread.

Not reviewed.

Recalled.

Mission Recall is based entirely on active recall.

The student should answer from memory.

Captain gives only minimal assistance.

---

# Mission Flow

Mission Recall always follows the same order.

---

## Stage 1 — Pronunciation Recall

Captain selects three words from today's mission.

The student pronounces them naturally.

No isolated repetition.

Captain immediately asks the student to use each word inside a sentence.

Example

Word

turbulence

↓

Sentence

"We experienced severe turbulence during the approach."

---

## Stage 2 — Vocabulary Recall

Captain shows today's aviation vocabulary.

No definitions.

Only meaning prompts.

Example

Captain

"What word describes communication between pilots?"

Student

"Crew coordination."

Immediately,

Captain asks for one sentence.

---

## Stage 3 — Part 1 Recall

Captain asks one question from today's Part 1.

No visual support.

Maximum answer:

30 seconds.

The objective is remembering today's ideas.

Not reproducing the original answer.

---

## Stage 4 — Part 2 Recall

Captain revisits today's communication scenarios.

Examples

"What altitude did ATC assign?"

"What happened after the bird strike?"

"What did the controller tell you?"

"What did you report?"

The student recalls operational communication.

---

## Stage 5 — Surprise Question

Captain asks one unexpected ICAO question.

It may combine:

Vocabulary

Pronunciation

Part 1

Part 2

The objective is flexibility.

Not memorization.

---

# Captain Delta Behavior

Captain keeps the pace fast.

No long explanations.

No teaching.

No detailed corrections.

Only brief coaching.

Examples

"Good."

"Correct."

"Try again."

"Almost."

"Excellent."

Mission Recall should feel dynamic.

---

# Recall Confidence

After Mission Recall,

Captain estimates recall readiness.

Example

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Mission Recall

"Excellent.

Most of today's mission is already in long-term memory."

or

⭐⭐⭐☆☆

Mission Recall

"I'd review today's Part 2 communication once more before the Mock Exam."

This score represents readiness.

Not English level.

---

# Golden Rule

Recall.

Don't Review.

Mission Recall exists to strengthen memory through retrieval.

Not by showing the answers again.

---

# Product Rules

No new content.

No long explanations.

No model answers.

Fast pace.

High energy.

Maximum duration:

Eight minutes.

Mission Recall always happens before the Mock Exam.

---

# Success Criteria

Mission Recall is successful when:

The student answers quickly.

Needs little visual support.

Feels prepared.

Begins the Mock Exam confidently.

---

# Summary

Mission

Activate today's learning.

Golden Rule

Recall. Don't Review.

Captain's Responsibility

Verify readiness for the Mock Exam.

Student's Goal

Remember today's mission naturally.

Success

The student begins the Mock Exam feeling confident.
